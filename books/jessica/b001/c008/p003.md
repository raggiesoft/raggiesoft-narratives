The Saturday morning light felt different. Kelly sat in front of the family's computer, the dial-up modem blessedly silent after making its usual screeching handshake with the server. She was rereading the emails that had come in overnight, a cascade of support that felt like a dream.

- *Kelly — we’ve got you.* — from the Quantum War Council.

- *Send me your resume.* — from a senior manager at MagnaByte.

- *Texas here. We’ll vouch for you anywhere.* — from a collective of developers in Austin, Dallas, and Houston.

- *You’re one of us now. And you’re Not For Sale.* — from Alex, her former Aurora colleague.

Her twin brother, Ryan, came into the room with a bowl of cereal. "Still reading them?" he asked gently.

"I just... can't believe it's real," she said, pointing to the email from Quantum inviting her to their Redmond campus. "They want to meet me on Monday. You're still coming with me, right?"

"Wouldn't miss it," he said, his loyalty to his sister being absolute.

She knew the developers had all walked out yesterday morning. Alex, the kind senior dev, had called to warn her. But the company itself... what did that mean? Was it just Evan now, alone in that fourth-floor loft? The uncertainty left a knot in her stomach.

Just then, the pager clipped to her belt buzzed sharply. She unclipped it, her blood running cold as she read the message. It was a stark, capitalized demand.

BALLROOM. GRAND HYATT. NOW. -ER

"What is it?" Ryan asked, seeing the color drain from her face.

She showed him the pager. "He wants me to go to a hotel. Now."

"Why?" Ryan's voice was sharp with concern. "The devs are gone. You don't owe him anything, Kel."

"I know," she said, a conditioned knot of fear tightening in her chest. Part of her was terrified, but another part was morbidly curious. This had to be the final, bizarre act of the whole disaster. "I think I have to go," she said, her voice steadier than she felt. "I need to see what happens. To close the book on it for good." She looked at her brother, her expression pleading. "Will you come with me? You don't have to go in the room, but... just in case. I don't want to be there alone."

"Of course, Kel," he said immediately. "I'll be right outside the door."

Their father, David Madsen, who had overheard their conversation from the kitchen, stepped into the doorway. "A hotel downtown? I'll drive you both," he said, his tone leaving no room for argument. "If that guy pulls anything and you need to bail, I don't want you waiting for a bus."
