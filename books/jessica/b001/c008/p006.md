Later after arriving home, a courier envelope arrived at the Madsen's home, addressed to Kelly. She opened it with trembling hands, her brother Ryan watching from the doorway of their shared living room. Inside were two documents: a crisp, formal letter on Aurora Systems Board of Directors letterhead and a paper paycheck.

She read the letter first. It was a clear, unequivocal apology. The board explained their unanimous vote to shut down the company, condemned Evan's behavior, and confirmed that the enclosed check was for her entire three months of service—including the first month that was meant to be unpaid.

Then she looked at the check. It was a real paycheck, for a real, substantial amount of money. The validation, the apology, the sheer, overwhelming relief that the entire ordeal was truly over—it was too much. The world narrowed to a pinpoint, and a wave of cathartic sobs shook her.

Ryan was there in an instant, wrapping his arms around her in a firm, grounding hug, just as he had done so many times before. "It's okay," he murmured, holding her tight. "It's over. You're safe."

When her tears finally subsided, she leaned against him, clutching the letter and the check. "That's not all," she said, her voice shaky but filled with a new kind of hope. "The people from Quantum... the ones on DevNet? They want to meet with me. This Monday, after my classes."

She looked up at him, her eyes pleading. "I want you to come with me."

Ryan didn't hesitate. He pulled her into another tight, grounding squeeze hug. "Of course I'll come with you," he said, his voice firm and unwavering. "We're in this together. Always."

With that simple, heartfelt "yes," Ryan wasn't just agreeing to go to a meeting; he was agreeing to the first day of the rest of their lives—the beginning of a new internship at Quantum, one the company was already drawing up with incredible speed.
