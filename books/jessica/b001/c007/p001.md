The Quantum lobby was a controlled storm of arriving developers. The War Council was waiting—Jessica at the center, flanked by Quantineers, independents, and even a few allies who’d flown in overnight from Cupertino and Texas. The “NOT FOR SALE” pins were everywhere.

Halfway through the arrivals, two Aurora devs wheeled in a battered rolling suitcase and a milk crate full of binders.

“What’s that?” a Quantineer asked.

The taller of the two grinned. “Internal build of AuroraOS. And the source code.” He explained that Evan had never filed for copyright or trademark protections. “Legally, it’s just… code. If we wanted to, we could fork it tomorrow and make it ours.”

When the last of the visitors had arrived, the doors closed. A Quantineer stepped to the front. “This started as a fight to protect Jessica. Now it’s bigger—it’s about how we treat each other in this industry.”

Jessica spoke next. “I didn’t ask for this fight. But I’m glad I’m not fighting it alone. Whatever happens to AuroraOS, whatever happens to Rothwell—we’ve already won something he can’t touch. We’ve got each other.”

Applause filled the lobby, a sound of relief and solidarity. As the group began to break into smaller conversations, grabbing coffee and pastries, Alex found a quieter corner by the window. He pulled out his cell phone and dialed Kelly's number. She had to be out of class by now.

"Hey, Kelly, it's Alex," he said when she answered. "Just wanted to let you know we're all here at Quantum, safe and sound." He paused, then continued. "Also, one more thing. When we were all leaving, I swung by your desk and packed up your things—your books, the picture of you and your brother, the stuff from your drawer. I didn't think it was a good idea to leave it there."

He could hear the surprise and gratitude in her voice. "I'll be heading home later this evening," he added. "I can stop by your house and drop the box off if that's okay. Saves you from ever having to go back to that place." He listened for a moment, then smiled. "It's no trouble at all. We take care of our own. I'll see you later."
