# Part 2: Aurora's Rebellion

That same morning, the loft at Aurora Systems was unusually still. At the far end of the room, a cluster of devs leaned over a whiteboard that, officially, was for sprint planning. Unofficially, it was the Aurora War Council. A Not For Sale button was pinned to every chest.

“Alright,” one said quietly. “We know the offer’s coming. Evan’s been talking like it’s already a done deal.”

Another nodded. “The second it’s made, we tip off the Quantineers. They’ll make sure it dies before it gets anywhere near her.”

They mapped out the chain of communication: an internal lookout would spot the offer being prepped, a trusted contact from the 174 club would relay the message to Jessica’s allies at Quantum, and they would stall the offer internally if possible. They kept the code moving just enough to keep Evan distracted, but their real work was happening in whispers and encrypted messages. The two councils—one in Redmond, one in Pioneer Square—were ready to move as one.
