# Part 1: Quantum's Alliance

The next day, on the \#253 eastbound bus across the SR 520 Bridge, the Quantum engineers who shared Jessica’s transfer leaned in. They’d heard every word of yesterday’s warning from the Aurora crew.

“Jess, we’ve been talking,” one said. “If Rothwell’s really sending you an offer, we’re not letting it get past the front desk.”

Another, in a worn Quantum hoodie, nodded. “We’ll intercept it. Or bury it. Or make it so radioactive no one in HR will touch it.”

The bus became a rolling council chamber, voices low but urgent, hashing out contingencies. By the time they reached the Quantum campus, the plan was already taking shape. Ten minutes later, they regrouped in a break room where someone had scrawled “WAR COUNCIL” on the whiteboard above the coffee machine.

Jessica wheeled in, eyebrow raised. “Subtle,” she said.

“We’re not hiding it,” the hoodie-wearer grinned. “We’re making it official.”

They laid out the plan: keep ears open for recruiter chatter, intercept any offer package, and use subtle signals like buttons and code comments to show solidarity. They would also quietly remind anyone who asked that Aurora’s HQ was inaccessible and that Rothwell’s management style was a morale killer.

Jessica listened, half-amused, half-moved. “You know I can handle myself,” she said.

“We know,” one replied. “But you shouldn’t have to handle *this* alone.”

The War Council had been born—on a city bus, over a lake, and now in the heart of Quantum’s own halls.
