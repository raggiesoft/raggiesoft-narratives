# Part 1: The Leak

The next morning, a sharp gasp cut through the mid-morning lull in the Aurora loft. On a developer’s screen, the front page of *TechWire Daily* was displayed. The headline blazed: THE TAYLOR MEMO: CEO’S HANDWRITTEN PLAN TO POACH QUANTUM ENGINEER LEAKS. Below it were scans of Evan’s blue-ink scrawls, every damning word and underline visible.

The room went still. Then someone’s gaze flicked toward Kelly Madsen's desk near the stairwell entrance, where the outbound mail tray sat. “The offer letter,” a voice said, his tone urgent. “It’s sitting right there on Kelly’s desk.”

The developers exchanged a look of quiet panic. None of them could just walk over and take it without raising suspicion. One of the senior developers quickly typed a single word into his terminal and sent it to Kelly's machine: Urgent.

Kelly saw the message pop up on her screen. She looked up, her eyes wide, and met the developer's gaze. He gave a subtle, almost imperceptible nod toward the mail tray on her desk. She understood immediately. Her heart pounded, but she kept her expression neutral. Pretending to organize the afternoon's outgoing mail, she casually shuffled through the envelopes. Her fingers found the heavy, cream-colored one addressed to Jessica Taylor. With movements that she prayed looked routine, she slid it into a manila folder filled with old invoices.

A few minutes later, on the pretense of asking a question about a supply order, she walked the folder over to the developer's side of the loft. She placed it on the senior developer's desk, who gave her a single, grateful nod. As soon as she was back at her desk, he took the envelope from the folder and buried it in the bottom drawer of a battered filing cabinet, under a stack of old bug printouts. "Gone," he murmured to the developer next to him.

At the Quantum campus, the scene repeated. The War Council gathered around a laptop, staring at the same headline. Then a phone buzzed. An engineer from Aurora was pinging them. “We saw the article too,” the message read. “And guess what was supposed to go out in the mail today... We handled it.”

Jessica, leaning in the doorway, folded her arms. “So the offer’s dead?”

“Dead and buried,” one of the Quantineers said. “And now Rothwell’s got bigger problems than you.”
