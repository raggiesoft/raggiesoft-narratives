After getting Kelly and Ryan set up with their logins, Maria led them to the break room for coffee and the promised tour of the best snacks. The room was bright and buzzed with the low murmur of a few engineers grabbing drinks between code sprints. A former Aurora developer, the one who had welcomed Kelly earlier, was pulling a soda from the machine and smiled as they came in.

"First day going okay?" he asked.

"It's great," Kelly said, her voice still holding a note of disbelief. "It’s just… a lot to take in. In a good way."

Maria handed Kelly a Quantum-branded mug. "We're all glad to have you here. And on that note, we wanted to talk to you about something. Your resume."

Kelly frowned slightly. "My resume?"

The ex-Aurora dev leaned against the counter. "Yeah. A few of us were talking, and we think you should just leave Aurora Systems off it completely," he said, his tone gentle but firm. "Pretend it never happened. Let this internship be the first thing on there after your high school jobs."

Kelly looked taken aback. "But… it was three months of experience."

"It was three months of you being taken advantage of," Maria corrected softly. "And right now, the name 'Aurora' is toxic. If a hiring manager sees that on your resume, the first thing they'll ask about isn't your work; it'll be about Evan Rothwell and the scandal. You'll spend every interview for the next year having to explain that mess. You don't deserve that."

"He's right," the other developer added. "Your real career starts here. With a real paycheck, real work, and a team that has your back. Don't let Evan's shadow follow you. Just wipe the slate clean."

Kelly looked down at the Quantum logo on her new mug, then back at the faces of the engineers who had supported her since her first anonymous post on DevNet. They weren't just giving her career advice; they were protecting her. They were officially welcoming her into the fold by helping her erase the chapter that had almost soured her on the industry entirely.

"Okay," she said, a small, genuine smile finally reaching her eyes. "Okay. Aurora who?"

The developer grinned. "That's the spirit. Now, the chocolate chip cookies are in the third jar from the left. Don't let anyone tell you otherwise."
