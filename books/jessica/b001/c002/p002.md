# Part 2: Unwelcome Currents

Jessica had slept late, the kind of deep, dreamless sleep that only comes after weeks of running on adrenaline. Even so, she woke with the Expo still clinging to her — the glare of stage lights, the hum of the crowd, the weight of being *seen*.

Her laptop sat open on the desk by the window, the unread email still at the top of her inbox:

**From:** Quantum Communications\
**Subject:** Press Inquiries Protocol\
Please redirect all media requests to the Quantum press office. Do not engage directly.

She’d read it twice last night, the words both a relief and a reminder that her life had shifted in ways she hadn’t yet processed.

The kitchen was already alive when she rolled in. The *Providence Standard* — the *ProStan*, as everyone called it — was spread wide across the table, its pages curling at the edges. The big headline in the business section blared about William Keswick’s appearance at MagnaWorld Expo, complete with a grainy photo of him at the podium. There was no mention of Jessica; this was a business headline, not a tech one, and she was fine with that.

Tall glasses of Coffee Milk stood beside bowls of cereal, spoons clinking softly. Aubrie was perched sideways in her chair, scanning the arts section while absently stirring her flakes. Amanda had already abandoned her own bowl to sketch something on the back of an envelope, muttering about a new idea. Elise sat nearest the door, her spoon untouched, eyes flicking toward the driveway every so often.

The knock came mid‑morning. Not the easy tap of a neighbor, but a brisk, deliberate rhythm.

Elise was on her feet instantly. “Stay here,” she said, already moving toward the front hall.

Jessica set her spoon down and followed, the hum of her chair blending with the muffled voices outside. Through the glass of the front doors, she saw them: three people, cameras and notepads in hand, their posture already leaning forward like they could smell a story.

Elise opened the door just enough to step into the frame, her shoulders squared.

“Can I help you?”

The man with the microphone smiled like it was a formality.

“We’re with TechLine Weekly. We’d like to speak with Jessica Taylor about her appearance at MagnaWorld Expo.”

Jessica’s stomach tightened.

Elise didn’t blink.

“Any press inquiries go through Quantum’s communications office. You can find their contact information online.”

The woman with the notepad tried again.

“We just have a few questions—”

“And I just gave you the answer,” Elise said, her tone still even but edged with steel. “This is a private residence. You need to leave.”

She closed the door firmly, the latch clicking into place.

From the kitchen, Amanda called, “Who was that?”

Elise turned the deadbolt. “Nobody we’re letting in.”

Jessica lingered in the hallway, the weight of it settling in. The Expo had followed her home.

Elise’s gaze softened as she looked at her.

“You’re safe here. They don’t get past me.”

Back at the table, the *ProStan* still lay open, the Coffee Milk still cold, the cereal bowls waiting. Outside, the driveway was empty again, but Jessica knew it wouldn’t be the last knock.
