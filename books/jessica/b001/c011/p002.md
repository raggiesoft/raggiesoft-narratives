# Part 2: The Gate and the Extended Family

They found their gate with plenty of time to spare. The waiting area was a sea of gray upholstery and muted conversations. Jessica guided them to a spot near the window with a clear view of their plane being prepped on the tarmac. Kelly and Ryan took the seats on either side of her, their boarding passes clutched in their hands.

"So, what's it really like there?" Ryan asked, breaking the silence. "At MagnaByte?"

Jessica watched as a baggage cart zipped past their plane. "If Quantum is my home, my immediate family, then MagnaByte... they're like my cousins. The ones you don't see every day, but when you do, you pick up right where you left off. We used to be the biggest rivals, but the engineers in the trenches? We always respected each other's work."

She turned to them, her expression becoming more animated. "That's what this partnership is really about. It's not just two CEOs on a screen. It's about the people who build things, working together. And that's what you're about to see."

She leaned in, her voice filled with a quiet excitement that was contagious. "That 'About' box you both wrote? The code you've been checking in for the past few weeks? You're about to walk into a MagnaByte development lab, on their campus, and see the code *you wrote* running live on their machines, on MagnaOS. You're going to see your work living in their world."

The full weight of the trip seemed to land on Kelly and Ryan at that moment. This wasn't just a field trip. They were contributors. The lines of code they had so carefully crafted in their shared corner of Jessica's office had already crossed the country and were waiting for them in a lab in Cupertino.

"Seriously?" Kelly breathed, her eyes wide. "Our code is already there?"

"It's all part of the same build now," Jessica confirmed with a nod. "You two are part of the bridge we're building. Today, you get to walk across it and see what's on the other side."

Just then, the gate agent's voice crackled over the PA system. "We would now like to begin pre-boarding for our flight to San Jose. We invite any passengers needing extra time or assistance to board at this time."

Kelly and Ryan exchanged a quick, uncertain glance. They knew this was Jessica's cue, but were they supposed to go with her or wait for their boarding group? They hesitated, not wanting to overstep.

Jessica caught their look and understood immediately. "You two are with me," she said, her tone leaving no room for doubt. She gave them a reassuring smile. "Traveling companions. That means we all go together."

She gathered her bag. For her, it was a familiar call. But for Kelly and Ryan, walking with her toward the jet bridge, it was another small, significant moment—a confirmation that they weren't just interns; they were part of her team.
