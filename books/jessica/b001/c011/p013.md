# Part 13: Morning in Cupertino

The California sun, already bright and warm, streamed into the hotel room. Ryan was the first one awake, a grin already on his face as he looked out the window at the sprawling, unfamiliar landscape of Silicon Valley. He gently shook his sister's shoulder.

"Kelly, wake up," he whispered. "We're still in Cupertino."

Kelly stirred, blinking in the bright light. A slow, happy smile spread across her face as the memories of the previous day returned—the dev lab, the bug bash, the feeling of genuine accomplishment. For the first time, a new place felt exciting, not overwhelming.

Jessica was already awake, checking her email from her bed. "Morning, you two," she said, her voice still a little rough with sleep. "Sleep well?"

They both nodded enthusiastically. The three of them fell into an easy, practiced rhythm of getting ready. Ryan, ever-watchful, stayed close by Kelly's side, his calm presence a familiar anchor that helped keep her grounded amidst the newness of the hotel room and the anticipation of the day ahead.

An hour later, they were packed and ready. Jessica, with the efficiency of a seasoned traveler, handled the checkout process at the front desk in a matter of minutes. As they stepped out of the hotel lobby and into the warm morning air, the same polished black, accessible van from the day before was already waiting at the curb, the driver greeting them with a familiar, professional smile.

They boarded the van, the twins buzzing with a quiet, happy energy. Their time in Cupertino was almost over, but the experience had already changed them. They weren't just the kids from Seattle anymore; they were part of a team, part of a story that was bigger than they had ever imagined.
