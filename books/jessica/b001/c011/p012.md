The city lights of Silicon Valley spread out like a glittering circuit board below their hotel room window. Ryan was already asleep, a day of excitement and intense focus having finally caught up with him. Kelly was reading a book on the other side of the bed, her eyelids heavy. The room was peaceful, wrapped in the quiet hum of the hotel.

Jessica’s cell phone rang, the sharp, digital sound cutting through the stillness. She answered it, her voice low. "Jessica Taylor."

"Hey, Jess, it's Mark," came the voice of her manager from Redmond. "Sorry to call so late, but I just got off the phone with David over at MagnaByte." He paused, and Jessica could hear the smile in his voice. "He wanted to know if the Madsen twins were interested in ending their internship at Quantum early to accept a full-time, entry-level engineering position with MagnaByte, effective immediately."

Jessica laughed softly, glancing over at the sleeping twins. "He's not subtle, is he?"

"Not in the slightest," Mark chuckled. "I told him, 'No way, we found them first. Finders keepers.' We had a good laugh about it." His tone then shifted, becoming more serious. "But he wasn't really kidding, and neither was I. You were right to call, Jess. I've already fired off emails to HR and the director of our division. I made it clear that we need to formalize a long-term plan for them. We're not letting talent like that walk out the door."

"Good," Jessica said, a wave of relief washing over her. "Thank you, Mark."

"Thank *you*," he replied. "Now, get some sleep. Sounds like you've all earned it. We'll see you tomorrow."

She ended the call and looked over at the other bed. Kelly had finally drifted off, her book resting on her chest. In sleep, she had instinctively curled up next to her brother, their arms loosely draped over each other—a familiar, comforting position they had shared since childhood.

Jessica smiled. She switched off her bedside lamp, and the room was cast into the soft glow of the city lights outside. Tomorrow, they would have a big breakfast with her "cousins" at MagnaByte, the brilliant engineers who were now the twins' colleagues. Then they would fly home. For the first time in a long while, Jessica fell asleep feeling a profound and peaceful sense of a job well done.
