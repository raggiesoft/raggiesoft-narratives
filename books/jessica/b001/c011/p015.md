# Part 15: Homeward Bound

The morning sun was higher in the sky when the accessible van returned to take them back to the airport. David and a few of the other MagnaByte engineers walked them out, the farewells feeling less like a corporate send-off and more like seeing family off after a holiday visit.

"You've got my email," David said, clapping Ryan on the shoulder. "If you find any more kernel panics, you know who to call."

"And you have a standing invitation to our next bug bash," another engineer added with a grin to Kelly. "We could use the help."

The ride back to SJC was quieter than the trip in. The initial, overwhelming awe had been replaced by a deep, buzzing sense of accomplishment. Kelly and Ryan sat together, talking in low, excited voices, not about the glamour of the campus, but about the code. They discussed how they could have optimized their bug fix, what they had learned from the code review, and what they hoped to work on next.

Jessica listened from her seat, a quiet smile on her face. The transformation was remarkable. In just two days, they had gone from timid, first-time flyers to confident young engineers discussing race conditions and memory leaks.

Checking in at the airport was a completely different experience this time. There was no hesitation, no nervous fumbling. Ryan handled their bags at the counter while Kelly confidently held onto their boarding passes. They navigated the security checkpoint like seasoned travelers. Jessica still guided them, but they no longer needed the constant reassurance.

As they settled in at the gate, waiting for their flight back to Seattle, Kelly looked out at the planes on the tarmac. "It feels different now," she said thoughtfully. "The airport, the planes... it all feels a little less scary."

"That's because you know where you're going," Jessica replied.

And she was right. They weren't just going back to Seattle. They were going back to their new desks at Quantum, to a project they were a part of, and to a future that, for the first time, felt bright, clear, and full of possibility.
