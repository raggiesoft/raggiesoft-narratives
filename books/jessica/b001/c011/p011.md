# Part 11: A Room with a View

The hotel MagnaByte had booked for them was a sleek, modern tower of glass and steel in the heart of Silicon Valley. For Jessica, it was a familiar stop, a comfortable and convenient home base for her trips to Cupertino. For Kelly and Ryan, it was another breathtaking new experience. The most they had ever stayed in were budget roadside motels on family trips years ago; the hotel's soaring, art-filled lobby felt like a palace.

Their room was a spacious, corner suite, fully ADA compliant. Wide doorways, a roll-in shower, and ample space around the furniture allowed Jessica to navigate her power wheelchair with ease. The room was dominated by two queen-sized beds and a massive window that offered a glittering panorama of the valley at dusk.

"Okay," Jessica said, wheeling over to the window. "Twins, you get the bed by the window. I'll take this one."

Kelly and Ryan stood in the middle of the plush carpet, their overnight bags still in their hands, simply taking it all in. The beds had more pillows than they owned collectively. There was a minibar stocked with tiny, expensive bottles of juice. The television was wider than their kitchen table back home.

"This is..." Ryan started, trailing off as he ran a hand over the smooth, dark wood of the dresser.

"Not a roadside motel," Kelly finished for him, a quiet wonder in her voice.

This was their first time ever staying in a hotel on a company's dime, and the sheer luxury of it was almost as overwhelming as the day's events. They weren't just being paid for their work; they were being cared for, treated as valued professionals whose comfort mattered.

As Jessica unpacked her own small bag, she watched them. They were no longer the nervous kids from the airport that morning. They were two young engineers who had debugged code in a world-class lab, impressed a team of senior developers, and were now quietly processing the sheer magnitude of how much their lives had changed in just a few short weeks.

Ryan eventually claimed the spot by the window, while Kelly bounced lightly on the thick, comfortable mattress. "I could get used to this," she said with a grin, the day's exhaustion finally giving way to a simple, happy fatigue.

Jessica just smiled. "Good," she said. "You've earned it."
