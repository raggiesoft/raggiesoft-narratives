# Part 5: Inside Infinite Loop

Ryan spun in a slow circle on the manicured walkway, his head tilted back to take in the full sweep of the curved glass building. "We're here," he said, his voice a mix of reverence and disbelief. "We are actually standing on Infinite Loop."

"It's... quieter than I expected," Kelly added, her eyes fixed on the iconic MagnaByte logo.

"All the chaos is on the inside," Jessica said with a laugh, leading them toward the main entrance. "Come on."

The lobby of Building 1 was bright, minimalist, and buzzed with a focused, creative energy. As they approached the reception desk, the woman behind the counter looked up and broke into a wide, genuine smile.

"Jessica! It's been too long," she said, already tapping on her keyboard to prepare their visitor badges. "We heard you were coming."

"Hey, Maria," Jessica replied warmly. "Good to see you. I brought some new talent with me this time." She gestured to the twins. "This is Kelly and Ryan Madsen, two of our best new interns. They're helping us make sure Aurora Presenter feels right at home on MagnaOS."

The receptionist handed them their badges, her eyes kind as she looked at Kelly. "Welcome to MagnaByte, both of you. We've all been so impressed by your story, Kelly. We're glad you landed on your feet."

Kelly felt a blush rise to her cheeks, but it was one of warmth, not embarrassment. They knew. And they didn't see her as a victim; they saw her as a peer who had navigated a disaster and come out the other side.

Just as they clipped on their badges, a senior MagnaByte engineer approached them from the security gate, his own badge swinging from a lanyard. "Jess! Heard you were inbound," he said, giving her a friendly nod before turning to the twins. "Kelly, Ryan, I'm David. Jessica sent a message ahead. She said someone was anxious to see their code in the wild."

He winked at Kelly. "You want to see your 'About' box running on a real Magna machine?"

Kelly's jaw dropped slightly. She had expected a tour, maybe a meet-and-greet, but not this. Not right away. "You... you have it ready?"

"Of course," David said, gesturing for them to follow him through the gates. "We pulled the latest build this morning. Your work is officially part of the project now. Let's go take a look."

He led them down a pristine white corridor, the air humming with the quiet power of innovation. For Kelly and Ryan, this was the moment it all became real. They weren't just visitors; they were collaborators, walking into the heart of MagnaByte to see the tangible proof of their own small, but significant, contribution to the bridge being built between two worlds.
