# Part 16: The Flight Home

The SJC departure gate was bright and airy, and this time, the twins navigated it with the quiet confidence of seasoned travelers. When the gate agent called for pre-boarding, they knew the drill. Kelly and Ryan simply fell into place on either side of Jessica, ready to board as her traveling companions.

At the aircraft door, the now-familiar routine played out smoothly. Jessica transferred to the aisle chair, her power wheelchair was tagged for gate-check, and she led the way onto the plane. They settled into the same configuration as before: Jessica on the aisle, and the twins in the middle and window seats.

As the plane climbed above the golden California landscape, the adrenaline of the past two days finally began to fade, replaced by a deep and happy exhaustion. Kelly leaned her head against the cool plastic of the window, watching the world shrink below. Ryan, feeling the familiar pull of sleep, simply snuggled into his sister's side, his head resting on her shoulder as he drifted off. Kelly wrapped a protective arm around him, the two of them a small, peaceful island in the humming cabin.

Jessica watched them for a moment, a profound sense of pride swelling in her chest. The transformation was astonishing. The nervous, wide-eyed kids she had met at SEA yesterday morning were gone. In their place were two young, confident engineers who had debugged code on a foreign OS, impressed a team of senior developers at a rival company, and found a system-crashing bug on their first day. They weren't just interns anymore; they were a part of the team, a part of the bridge she had spent her career building.

She looked away, giving them their privacy, and turned her gaze toward the clouds. The conversation with Mark replayed in her mind. A long-term plan. It wasn't enough. These two were special, and MagnaByte's interest, however joking, was a clear signal of their value.

Jessica made a quiet promise to herself. As soon as they were back in Redmond, she wasn't just going to talk to her higher-ups; she was going to build a case. She would outline a multi-year development path, propose mentorship programs, and secure a commitment from Quantum to see the Madsen twins not just through their internship, but through their college degrees and into full-time roles. They had taken care of Kelly when she was in trouble. Now, it was time to make sure her future, and her brother's, was secure. They had found a home at Quantum, and Jessica would make sure they never had to leave.
