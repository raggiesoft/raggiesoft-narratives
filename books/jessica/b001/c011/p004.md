# Part 4: The California Sun

The plane touched down at San Jose International Airport (SJC) with a gentle bump, rolling past palm trees that looked impossibly exotic to Kelly and Ryan. The California sun was a stark, brilliant contrast to the familiar gray skies of Seattle. As they deplaned, Jessica's power wheelchair was already waiting for her at the end of the jet bridge.

"Okay, now what?" Ryan asked, looking around the bustling terminal. "Do we find a bus?"

"Not this time," Jessica said with a smile. "MagnaByte takes care of their guests. Follow me."

She led them through the arrivals hall and out to the curb, where a polished black, high-end accessible van was waiting. The driver, a man in a crisp suit, stepped out and greeted Jessica by name. With a quiet click, the van's side door slid open and a discreet ramp unfolded smoothly to the curb.

While the driver handled their luggage, Jessica rolled her power wheelchair directly into the spacious, leather-appointed interior, locking her chair into place. Kelly and Ryan followed, settling into the comfortable seats behind her. For the twins, who were used to city buses, the quiet luxury of a private, chauffeured vehicle felt like another world.

The drive from SJC to Cupertino was a tour of the heart of Silicon Valley. Jessica pointed out the low, sprawling campuses of famous tech companies as they passed them on the freeway. The landscape was a sea of beige office parks, manicured lawns, and the occasional, iconic logo on a sign that made Ryan crane his neck to see. It was the world they had only ever read about in magazines, and now they were in the middle of it.

After a short drive, the van turned off the main road and onto a street that felt different. The landscaping was more deliberate, the buildings more cohesive.

"Here we are," Jessica said, her voice holding a note of fondness. "Infinite Loop."

The van glided to a smooth stop in front of the main building of the MagnaByte campus. The iconic, multi-colored MagnaByte logo gleamed in the afternoon sun. The driver opened their door, and as Kelly and Ryan stepped out, they stared at the sleek, curved glass of the building, the place where the other half of their code now lived.
