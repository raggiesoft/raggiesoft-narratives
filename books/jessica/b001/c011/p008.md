# Part 8: Lunch at Caffe Magna

The MagnaByte cafeteria, affectionately known as Caffe Magna, was less like a corporate lunchroom and more like a high-end international food court. Stations for wood-fired pizza, fresh-made sushi, and authentic street tacos buzzed with activity. The cavernous, sunlit room was filled with the murmur of a dozen languages and the clatter of trays, all against a backdrop of ambient electronic music. For Kelly and Ryan, who were used to brown-bagging it at community college, it was a dazzling spectacle.

David led them through the crowd to a large round table where Jessica was already talking with Kenji, the senior architect from her morning meeting. As they approached, a ripple of recognition moved through the nearby tables. Several MagnaByte developers—some of whom Kelly recognized from the DevNet threads—looked over, caught her eye, and gave her a small, supportive nod or a thumbs-up. They all knew her story, and the silent welcome was universal: *You're safe here. We've got your back.*

"I was just telling Kenji you two are naturals," David said as they sat down, placing his tray on the table. "They tracked down a subtle rendering bug in the MagnaOS beta in under two hours. Clean fix, too. No hard-coded nonsense."

Jessica’s face beamed with pride. "I'm not surprised," she said. "They're fast learners."

David took a bite of his pizza and looked directly at Jessica, a playful but sincere glint in his eye. "You should probably talk to your bosses about making them a full-time offer when their internship is up," he said, loud enough for the twins to hear. "Because if Quantum doesn't snatch them up, we just might. A good debugging team is hard to find."

Kelly and Ryan exchanged a wide-eyed, ecstatic look. It was a joke, mostly, but the compliment underneath was real and powerful. A senior MagnaByte engineer, a legend in the field, was not only praising their work but was suggesting they were valuable enough for two of the biggest tech companies in the world to compete over.

As they ate their lunch—Kelly with a slice of pizza, Ryan with a burrito bowl that was bigger than his head—they listened to the casual, brilliant conversation flowing around them. It was a mix of shop talk about compiler flags, gossip about a rival's delayed product launch, and a fierce debate about the best science fiction movie of the decade.

This was it. They were in the heart of Silicon Valley, eating a famous free corporate lunch, and being treated not as interns, but as peers. For Kelly, the last vestiges of the Aurora nightmare finally melted away, replaced by the bright, warm reality of her future.
