# Part 15: The Fallout

The moment she cleared the stage, the noise hit her from a different angle — not the roar of the crowd, but the sharper, more focused hum of the press.

Down one side of the service corridor, a cluster of reporters had already gathered, badges swinging, recorders and cameras at the ready. Security kept them at a polite distance, but their voices carried.

“Keswick at MagnaWorld — unprecedented!”\
“Quantum–Magna alliance confirmed!”\
“Jessica Taylor on stage — what does it mean?”

It was like listening to two entirely different conversations. The business press was locked on William Keswick’s satellite appearance, already framing it as the corporate story of the year. But the tech press — the ones with worn‑in laptop bags and camera straps digging into their shoulders — were buzzing about her.

*Jessica Taylor at MagnaWorld Expo.*

She caught fragments as she rolled past:

“She’s the Aurora Presenter lead…”\
“First time she’s been public for Quantum…”\
“If she’s here, this is bigger than we thought.”

A microphone was suddenly in front of her, its foam windscreen branded with a tech blog’s logo.

**Reporter:** “Jessica, can you tell us what your role is in Quantum Office Suite 98?”

She froze for half a beat. Before today, she had never been the public face of Quantum. She’d been in conference rooms, code reviews, late‑night bug hunts — not under stage lights, not in front of cameras. And she had no prepared answer.

**Jessica:** “…No comment.”

It was the safest thing she could think to say.

Security moved her along, the questions trailing after her like static.

“Were you involved in the Keswick deal?”\
“Will Aurora Presenter ship on MagnaOS?”\
“Is this a permanent role for you?”

She didn’t look back.

In the green room, the door closed behind her, muting the noise. She exhaled slowly, feeling the adrenaline ebb. Somewhere out there, headlines were already being written — some with Keswick’s name in bold, others with hers.

She wasn’t sure which unsettled her more: that she’d just been part of tech history… or that, for the first time, she was in it as a name people would remember.
