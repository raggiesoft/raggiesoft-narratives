# Part 13: Zero Tolerance

## 21:15 PM (Third Quarter)

The halftime break didn't reset the game. It just let the concrete freeze harder.

As the third quarter began, the temperature inside **The Crucible** dipped into the low 30s. A biting wind swirled down from the mountains, cutting through jackets and turning breath into clouds of steam.

For the CPI Forgers, this was standard operating procedure. They stood on the sideline in their heavy capes, breathing calmly.

For the Tallahassee State Renegades, it was a biological crisis. They huddled around the few portable heaters on their sideline, shivering, looking miserable.

"They are thermal-crashing," Freddie observed, tucking his hands deeper into the pocket of his orange hoodie. "Their operating temperature is too low. Their reaction times are lagging."

"They're soft!" Jessica yelled, cup of hot chocolate in hand. "They're used to Florida sunshine! Welcome to the mountains, boys!"

## 10:00 Left in 3rd Quarter

The Renegades received the kickoff. They needed a spark. They needed a touchdown to prove they were the number four team in the nation.

They tried to throw deep. The quarterback dropped back, his breath visible in the stadium lights. He wound up and launched a pass toward their star wide receiver.

But the ball wasn't a soft leather oval anymore. In the freezing air, it was a rock.

The receiver reached up. The ball hit his frozen hands. *CLUNK.*

It bounced off his chest and fell harmlessly to the turf.

"Incomplete!" the crowd roared.

"Friction coefficient," Freddie noted. "Cold hands. Hard surface. High probability of drop."

## 07:30 Left in 3rd Quarter

Tallahassee punted. Again.

CPI took over on their own 30. And the "Steamroller" started up again.

*Handoff. Five yards.* *Handoff. Four yards.* *Handoff. First down.*

It was demoralizing. The CPI offensive line, big corn-fed engineers who understood leverage better than anyone, simply pushed the freezing, exhausted Tallahassee defenders backward.

"Why are they ranked fourth?" Paige asked, watching a Renegade linebacker fail to wrap up a tackle. "They look terrible."

"They're ranked on stats," Jessica explained, her voice hoarse from cheering. "They play fast teams on grass fields. They put up 50 points a game against weak defenses. But they've never played in a factory before. They can't handle the physics of this place."

## 03:00 Left in 3rd Quarter

The drive consumed seven minutes of the clock. It was a slow, deliberate suffocation.

On the 5-yard line, the CPI quarterback didn't even fake a pass. He turned and handed the ball to their fullback—a human wrecking ball with a neck roll and dirty uniform.

He hit the line. The pile didn't move. So he kept his legs churning.

*Drive. Drive. Drive.*

The entire offensive line surged forward. The Renegades slid backward on the cold turf.

**TOUCHDOWN FORGERS.**

The cannon fired. *BOOM.*

The student section began a new chant, low and rhythmic, pointing at the opposing sideline.

*O-VER-RA-TED!* *clap-clap-clapclapclap* *O-VER-RA-TED!* *clap-clap-clapclapclap*

**CPI FORGERS: 27** **TALLAHASSEE ST: 0**

## 00:00 End of 3rd Quarter

As the teams switched sides for the final quarter, the stadium was in a frenzy. The shutout was intact. The giant wasn't just sleeping; it was in a coma.

Freddie looked at the scoreboard. 27 to 0.

"The data is conclusive," Freddie said, adjusting his glasses. "The ranking algorithm is flawed. It failed to account for environmental variables."

"That's exactly right, Freddie," Jessica beamed, putting her arm around him. "They forgot to factor in the Ironheads. One more quarter. Let's finish them."
