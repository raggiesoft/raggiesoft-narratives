# Part 9: The Forger's Call

## 19:00 PM

The countdown clock on the scoreboard hit **00:00**.

The world ended.

Every light in the stadium—the massive banks of floodlights, the concourse fluorescent strips, even the scoreboard advertisements—died instantly. The Crucible was plunged into a heavy, suffocating darkness, lit only by the faint, ghostly glow of thousands of digital watches and the stars above the mountain rim.

For three seconds, there was absolute silence. Sixty thousand people held their breath. The air pressure seemed to drop, sucking the oxygen out of the bowl.

Freddie stopped breathing. His hand was crushed in Paige’s grip. He felt the panic rising in his throat—the darkness was too sudden, the silence too heavy.

Then, a sound cut through the cold mountain air like a knife.

*TING...*

It was the recorded sound of a hammer striking steel—sharp, high-pitched, and clear.

And then, the response.

*THUD.*

Jessica brought her boot down on the aluminum bleacher with all her strength. Sixty thousand other people did the same.

The impact was physical. The entire concrete structure of the North End Zone lurched. It wasn't a sound; it was a seismic event. Freddie felt the vibration travel up through the soles of his shoes, rattling his teeth.

*TING...*

*THUD.*

The second stomp was louder. The sheer mass of the sound hit Freddie in the chest. It was terrifying. It was the sound of a giant walking.

"Ride it!" Jessica screamed into the darkness, her face illuminated by a sudden, blood-red glow.

On the massive Jumbotron, a single image appeared: **A GLOWING, MOLTEN ANVIL.**

*TING...*

*THUD.*

Freddie squeezed his eyes shut. His heart was hammering against his ribs, trying to escape. *Too loud. Too big. Too much.*

But then, he felt it. The pattern.

It wasn't random. It wasn't chaotic. It was a perfect, binary grid.

*Signal. Response. Signal. Response.*

He took a breath. He waited for the *TING*. He anticipated the *THUD*.

*TING...*

Freddie stomped his foot. *THUD.*

He felt the connection. He was one pixel in a sixty-thousand-pixel image. He wasn't an error code here; he was part of the operating system.

*TING...*

*THUD.*

On the field, the Tallahassee State Renegades were huddled near their sideline. They were looking up at the stands, shifting nervously. They were used to booing. They were used to cheering. They weren't used to *this*. They weren't used to a machine.

The rhythm sped up.

*TING-THUD. TING-THUD. TING-THUD.*

The anvil on the screen began to spark. The sound was deafening now, a rolling thunder that shook the very foundations of the earth.

"HERE WE GO!" Jessica roared, grabbing Freddie’s shoulder.

The music exploded.

A massive, distorted industrial guitar riff ripped through the stadium speakers, shaking the air. The drums kicked in—a thunderous, driving beat that matched the frantic pounding of Freddie’s heart.

*"The lights go dark... the tension builds..."* Ryan O’Connell’s voice roared from the towers, gritty and aggressive. *"A single, pounding, heavy sound!"*

Pyrotechnics blasted from the top of the scoreboard—jets of flame shooting into the night sky, turning the smoke maroon.

**"IGNITION!"** the crowd screamed along with the track.

Down in the tunnel, the smoke poured out. And then, they emerged.

The **CPI Forgers**.

They didn't dance out. They sprinted. A wave of maroon jerseys poured onto the field, holding a massive sledgehammer high in the air.

The noise in the stadium went from deafening to impossible. It wasn't a cheer anymore. It was a roar—a primal, unified sound of intimidation that drowned out thought, drowned out fear, and drowned out the rejection letter waiting in Freddie’s future.

*"THIS IS THE FORGER'S CALL!"* the speakers blasted. *"IGNITION!"*

Freddie was screaming. He didn't even realize it. He was on his feet, his orange hoodie vibrating with the noise, his earplugs barely holding back the tide. He was stomping. He was clapping. He was part of the machine.

"LOOK AT THEM!" Jessica yelled, pointing at the terrified Renegades on the sideline. "THEY ARE SCARED OF THE NOISE!"

Freddie looked. The opposing team looked small. They looked disorganized. They looked like they had walked into a factory and gotten caught in the gears.

"It is... effective!" Freddie yelled back, his voice lost in the roar.

He looked at Paige. She was grinning at him, her face flushed with adrenaline. She squeezed his hand.

"You okay?" she mouthed.

Freddie looked at the field. He looked at the anvil on the screen. He looked at the thousands of people screaming in unison.

"I am online!" Freddie shouted. "The system is online!"
