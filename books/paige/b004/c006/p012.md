# Part 12: The Return Vector

## Wednesday, May 12, 2004 — 08:30 AM (PST) Cabin 4, The Iron Creek Lodge

The packing sequence was silent and efficient. The "Bear's Den" had served its purpose—it had been a sanctuary, a bunker, and a hospital—but now the mission was over.

Freddie folded his orange sweatshirt and placed it into his duffel bag. He checked the drawers. Empty. He checked under the Alaska King bed. Clear.

"Perimeter check complete," Freddie announced. "No artifacts left behind."

"Good," Jessica said, zipping up her bag. "Let's load the Senator. It's a straight shot to the airport."

They walked out to the white sedan. The sun was shining, but Freddie didn't look back at the cabin. He didn't get sentimental about buildings once they were empty. He simply logged the data: *Safe location identified for future reference.*

## 11:00 AM — Vanguard Rental Return (Pacific Hwy South)

The drive back was the reverse of the arrival, but the feeling was different. As the towering fir trees of Ashford gave way to the strip malls of Spanaway, and then the concrete ribbons of Interstate 5, Freddie didn't panic.

He sat in the back, sunglasses on, watching the world speed by. He wasn't trapped in the noise anymore; he was just passing through it.

Jessica spotted the sign—**VANGUARD RENT-A-CAR**—and pulled off International Boulevard into the sprawling open-air lot. It wasn't a fancy garage; it was acres of asphalt surrounded by chain-link fencing, smelling of exhaust and car wash soap.

She pulled into the return lane under the metal awning. A guy with a thick jacket and a handheld computer walked up.

"Mileage?" the attendant asked.

"Enough," Jessica smiled.

She handed over the keys. The receipt printed with a buzzing sound from the device on his belt.

"Vehicle surrendered," Jessica said, patting the white roof of the car. "Goodbye, slushbox. You did okay."

"It was adequate," Paige admitted, slinging her bag over her shoulder. "But I still missed the clutch."

"Bus is loading!" the driver of the Vanguard shuttle yelled.

It was a standard cutaway van—the kind with the folding hydraulic door and the fiberglass body. They hauled their bags up the steps.

The interior smelled of vinyl seats and diesel fumes. The seating was two-by-two with a narrow aisle down the center.

Freddie slid into a window seat on the right side. Paige immediately slid in next to him, blocking the aisle. Jessica took the seat directly in front of them.

"Perimeter secure," Paige whispered, buckling the lap belt.

Freddie leaned his head against the window, watching the rental lot disappear as the bus lurched forward, merging back onto the highway toward the terminal.

## 11:30 AM — SeaTac Airport (Arrivals Drive)

The shuttle dropped them at the curb on the lower level of the terminal. The noise was louder here—buses idling, whistles blowing, the announcements over the PA system.

"Hold the line," Freddie whispered to himself.

They walked to the island marked **COURTESY VANS**.

They didn't have to wait long. A white van with **THE AIRPORT GRAND HOTEL** stenciled on the side pulled up to the curb. The driver, an older man with a friendly nod, hopped out to grab their bags.

"Heading to the Grand?" he asked.

"Affirmative," Freddie said, climbing into the back row of the van. He liked the back row. It was defensible.

## 11:45 AM — The Lobby

The hotel was a stark contrast to the lodge. It was glass, steel, and beige marble. It smelled of cleaning chemicals and conditioned air.

Jessica walked to the front desk while Freddie and Paige stood near a potted palm tree, guarding the luggage.

"Checking in under Avery," Jessica told the clerk. "We have an Early Check-In add-on."

The clerk typed for a moment. "I see it here. Housekeeping is just putting the finishing touches on the room. It should be ready by noon. If you’d like to have a seat in the lobby, I’ll wave you over the second it’s clear."

"Understood," Jessica said.

She walked back to the siblings. "Fifteen minutes. Holding pattern."

They sat on a leather sofa near the elevators. Freddie watched the business travelers rolling their suitcases across the polished floor. He watched the automatic doors slide open and shut.

"It is busy," Freddie noted. "But organized."

"It's a transit point," Paige said. "Nobody stays here. They just pause here."

At exactly 12:00 PM, the clerk caught Jessica’s eye and held up a key card packet.

"Green light," Jessica announced.

## 12:05 PM — Room 512

They entered the room. It was a standard "Executive King." A large bed, a desk, a TV in an armoire, and a window that looked out over the airport tarmac.

Freddie dropped his bag and walked straight to the window. He inspected the glass immediately.

"Triple-pane," Freddie noted, pressing his finger against the cold surface. "There is a four-inch air gap. Soundproofing is active."

He watched a massive 747 taxiing slowly toward the runway, its engines shimmering with heat. He could feel the low rumble in the floor, but the high-pitched whine was gone.

"Machines," Freddie whispered. "Going home."

"How are you holding up?" Jessica asked, sitting on the edge of the King bed to test the mattress. "It's not the Alaska King, but we'll fit."

"I am functional," Freddie said, turning away from the window.

He looked at his sisters. They looked tired. Not the "good tired" of the river, but the deep, bone-weary exhaustion of adrenaline wearing off.

"I am ready," Freddie stated clearly.

"Ready for what?" Paige asked, kicking off her shoes.

"Ready to go home," Freddie said. "To Christiansburg. To the basement. To the static."

He adjusted his glasses.

"The adventure was... necessary. The data was valid. But the mission is complete."

"Agreed," Jessica sighed, flopping backward onto the white duvet. "I miss my own bed. I miss the Silo."

"I miss driving a car that listens to me," Paige added, sitting next to her.

Freddie walked over and sat between them. The bed dipped under his weight. It was tighter than the log cabin bed, but that just meant the pressure would be better.

"Tomorrow we fly," Freddie said. "Tomorrow we execute the final leg."

"Tomorrow," Paige confirmed, leaning her head on his shoulder.

They sat there in the generic hotel room, listening to the muffled roar of jet engines outside, ready to close the file on the Pacific Northwest and return to the safety of the known world.
