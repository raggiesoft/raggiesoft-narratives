# Part 10: The Refueling

## Tuesday, May 11, 2004 — 12:15 PM (PST) The Junction Grill, Elbe, WA

They walked across the two-lane highway from the train depot. The air smelled of grilled onions and beef—a distinct, heavy scent that signaled "food" to the primitive part of the brain.

"Target identified," Jessica said, pointing to the small wooden building with the line of people out front. "**The Junction Grill**."

"It is... small," Freddie noted.

"It's a shack," Paige corrected. "But the smell is correct. That is the smell of grease and victory."

They joined the line. Freddie stood between his sisters, sandwiched safely. He watched the operation through the serving window. It was an assembly line. One person grilling. One person dressing buns. One person taking money.

"Efficiency," Freddie whispered, admiring the workflow. "High throughput. No wasted motion."

When they got to the window, the order was simple.

"Three cheeseburgers," Jessica ordered. "Fries. And..." She looked at Freddie.

"Chocolate shake," Freddie said. "Viscosity check."

"Three shakes," Jessica finished.

They took their brown paper bag and the carrier of styrofoam cups to a wooden picnic table near the road. The sun was fully out now, warming the damp wood.

Freddie unwrapped his burger. It wasn't gourmet. It was a smashed patty, melted American cheese, pickle, and onion on a soft bun. It was wrapped in plain white paper that was already turning translucent.

He took a bite.

Grease. Salt. Protein.

"Status?" Paige asked, dipping a fry into her chocolate shake.

"Optimal," Freddie mumbled, mouth full. "This is better than the fancy dinner."

"Way better," Jessica agreed, wiping a drip of ketchup from her thumb.

"Vanessa said we would eat at the top of the **Sky-Spire**," Freddie said, chewing thoughtfully. "She said the floor rotates. She said I would see the whole city."

He looked at the burger in his hands.

"I do not want the floor to move while I am eating," Freddie stated firmly. "And I do not want to see the city. I want to see the trees. And I want the burger to taste like... this."

"Like 100% real American cholesterol," Paige joked.

"Like fuel," Freddie corrected. "Simple fuel."

They ate watching the cars drive by on SR-7. Every time a logging truck rumbled past, Freddie felt the vibration in the picnic table. It was a heavy, low frequency.

He didn't flinch. He just chewed his burger, grounded by the heavy food and the heavy trains across the street.

"What's the plan for the afternoon?" Jessica asked, crumpling her wrapper into a ball.

Freddie looked at the empty shake cup. He looked at the mountain looming above the trees. He felt the "good" fatigue of a day spent processing new, safe data.

"Return to Base," Freddie decided. "Nap protocol. Then... maybe we sit by the river."

"River watch," Paige nodded. "I like it."

"Then let's go," Jessica said, standing up. "The Bear's Den is waiting."

They walked back to the *Vanguard Senator*, full, tired, and content, leaving the iron giants and the burger shack behind for the quiet sanctuary of the woods.
