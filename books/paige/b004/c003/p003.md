# Part 3: The Disconnect

## Spring 2004 Tuesday, May 4, 2004 — 18:00 PM

The ride from CPI to **Pepper Plaza** usually took thirty minutes. Today, to Freddie, it felt like re-entry burn.

He sat next to Paige. She was buzzing with post-presentation energy, talking about Haldeman’s face when the screen went black.

"Did you see the guy in the third row?" Paige laughed, squeezing Freddie’s knee. "His jaw literally dropped. We are legends, Captain. Legends."

Freddie forced a smile. *Legends.* In his mind, he saw a different image. He saw Paige in Italy, drinking wine in a plaza, laughing with people who didn't need her to hold their hand on a bus. He saw Jessica in a high-rise in New York, commanding a boardroom.

*They are legends,* Freddie thought. *I am the anchor weight.*

## 18:45 PM — 105 Pepper Street

The apartment smelled of victory and pepperoni. Jessica had ordered from **Pizza Palace** on the way home.

They sat in the living room. The TV was tuned to **WNRV-TV**, the volume set to Freddie’s preferred level (14).

On the screen, the **GBS Evening Report** was winding down. The anchor was signing off with a story about a cat stuck in a tree.

"So," Jessica said, grabbing a slice. "Summer plans. We need to figure out the schedule. I was thinking we could do a beach trip in July. Maybe the Outer Banks?"

"Yes!" Paige agreed, kicking her feet up on the coffee table. "Freddie loves the aquarium. We could rent that cottage again. The one with the quiet porch."

Freddie chewed his pizza. It tasted like cardboard. *The Outer Banks.* Another trip where they would have to watch him. Another summer where they wouldn't go to Europe or New York because he needed a "quiet porch."

*It has to be tonight,* the logic bomb whispered. *Before the gravity resets.*

## 19:00 PM

The TV jingle for **Phrase Spin** blared through the speakers. The audience clapped. The colorful wheel began to spin.

"Oh, I love this puzzle," Paige said, leaning forward. "Things found in a... kitchen!"

Freddie stood up.

"I require... the restroom," Freddie lied. His heart was hammering against his ribs like a trapped bird.

"Okay," Paige said, not looking away from the screen. "Yell if you need backup."

Freddie walked down the hall. He didn't go to the bathroom. He went to his bedroom.

He had packed the bag that morning. Just the essentials.

- Three pairs of underwear.

- Two t-shirts.

- His spare glasses.

- His medication (enough for three days).

- The **Iron-Logic** laptop (he couldn't leave the architecture).

He put on his orange hoodie. He pulled the hood up.

He took a piece of notebook paper from his desk. He had written it during the bus ride, shielding it from Paige.

*I am setting you free. - M*

He placed it on the kitchen counter next to the house keys he was leaving behind.

He looked at the back of Paige’s head over the sofa. She was shouting, "Spatula! It's Spatula!"

He looked at Jessica, who was laughing.

*Goodbye, Lieutenants,* Freddie thought. *End of watch.*

He slipped out the front door.

## 19:15 PM — Pepper Plaza Transfer Pont

The sun was setting. The air was cooling.

Freddie stood at the shelter. His phone buzzed in his pocket. A text from Vanessa.

On the transport. ETA 2 mins.

The **Number 1** bus roared around the corner. The brakes squealed. The doors hissed open.

Freddie stepped up. He swiped his pass for the last time.

He looked down the aisle.

Vanessa was sitting in the back. She wasn't wearing her "student" clothes. She was wearing jeans and a jacket. She looked... older. Harder.

She patted the empty seat next to her.

Freddie walked down the aisle. His legs felt heavy. The bus lurched forward before he sat down, and he stumbled. Vanessa caught his arm.

"Gotcha," she said softly.

Freddie sat. He looked out the window as the bus passed the entrance to his neighborhood.

"Did you leave the note?" Vanessa asked.

"Affirmative," Freddie whispered.

"Good," Vanessa said. She didn't let go of his arm. "You did the right thing, Freddie. They're going to be so proud of you for being strong."

## 19:40 PM — Falling Branch Park & Ride

The Number 1 bus terminated at the Falling Branch shopping center. It was a concrete island in a sea of parking lots.

They stepped off. The wind was picking up.

"Transfer required," Freddie muttered, checking the schedule on the signpost. "Roanoke Express. Departure 19:45."

"It's right there," Vanessa pointed.

The **Roanoke Express** was a large, coach-style bus. It was the commuter line that connected the university town to the city of Roanoke.

They boarded. The driver didn't look at them. The bus was mostly empty—just a few tired workers heading home.

Vanessa led him to a seat in the middle.

"Phone," Vanessa said, holding out her hand.

Freddie pulled his Nokia from his pocket. It was on.

"If you leave it on," Vanessa explained, her voice reasonable and calm, "they will track the signal. Jessica will call the police. They'll drag you back before you even get to see Seattle. Do you want them to drag you back to the cage?"

Freddie shook his head. "Negative."

"Turn it off," Vanessa commanded gently. "Go dark."

Freddie pressed the power button. The screen faded. The connection to the network—to Paige—was severed.

He handed the brick of plastic to Vanessa. She put it in her bag.

"Safe mode engaged," she smiled.

## 20:15 PM — Campbell Court, Roanoke

The bus descended into the city. It turned into **Campbell Court**.

It wasn't a terminal in the romantic sense. It was the ground floor of a concrete parking garage. The ceiling was low and stained with soot. The lighting was sickly yellow sodium-vapor.

The air was thick. Heavy diesel fumes hung in the stagnant space, trapped by the concrete beams. The noise was deafening—engines idling, air brakes hissing, people shouting.

Freddie coughed. He pulled his hoodie tight around his face.

*Sensory Overload: Critical.*

"It smells like poison," Freddie choked out.

"It's just the city," Vanessa said, steering him toward the ticket window. "Focus on me, Freddie. Look at my back."

They approached the **Pathfinder** window. The glass was scratched and bulletproof.

"Will Call," Vanessa told the agent. "Two for Seattle. Transfer in Nashville."

The agent slid two envelopes under the glass.

"Bus 492," the agent grunted. "Lane 4. Departs 9:10 PM. Don't wander off. Security goes off duty at 9."

Vanessa took the tickets. She led Freddie to a metal bench near Lane 4.

A bus was idling right in front of them, its exhaust pipe spewing gray smoke directly into the waiting area.

Freddie sat down. He curled in on himself, knees to chest, rocking slightly.

"It is too loud," Freddie whispered. "The air quality is hazardous."

"I know," Vanessa said. She sat next to him, but she didn't hug him like Paige. She put a hand on his knee—a controlling grip, not a comforting one.

"This is the hardest part," Vanessa said. "But you have to endure it. For them. Think of Paige in Italy. Think of her eating gelato. She can't do that if you're calling her to pick you up because it smells bad."

Freddie squeezed his eyes shut. He visualized Paige in Italy. He visualized the Colosseum.

*Endure,* he told himself. *Endure the smog. Endure the noise.*

He checked his mental clock. **20:30 PM.**

Back at the house, *Stellar Voyage: The New Era* was starting on WNRV. Paige and Jessica would be settling in on the couch.

They would look for him. They would find the note.

Freddie let out a small, broken sound that was lost in the roar of a diesel engine.

"Shh," Vanessa soothed, patting his knee. "Almost there. The bus is coming."

They sat in the gray smog, waiting for the ride that would take him 2,600 miles away from his anchor.
