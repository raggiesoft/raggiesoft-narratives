# Part 6: The Voltage Spike

## Monday, June 21, 2004 — 20:15 PM 105 Pepper Street SE

The cake was decimated. The blue numbers were smoking ruins on the platter.

Jessica and Aunt Linda, wise to the ways of glucose, had carefully sliced thin, reasonable pieces for Amanda and Elara. They were happily coloring on the floor, content and stable.

But the Birthday Girls (Sarah and Jenna) and the opportunist (Harper) had gone rogue. They had consumed massive corner pieces laden with thick buttercream roses.

The result was immediate and terrifying.

At the center of the room, Freddie was in his safe mode. He was docked on the sofa, with Paige heavily leaned against his left side. Her head was on his shoulder, her arm looped through his, her leg pressed against his leg. She was warm, heavy, and still. She was the anchor that kept the room from spinning.

Then, the anomaly occurred.

Harper was vibrating. Literally vibrating.

She was standing in the middle of the living room, bouncing on the balls of her feet. Her pupils were dilated. She had a smear of blue icing on her chin that looked like war paint.

"I’m not tired," Harper announced, her words coming out like bullets from a machine gun. "I could run to the bowling alley. I bet I could run there right now. Do we have a jump rope? I need to jump."

"Harper, sit down," Aunt Linda suggested from the kitchen.

"I can't sit!" Harper yelled happily. "My legs are full of bees!"

She swiveled her head. She locked eyes with Freddie.

To Harper, Freddie was the Core. He was the designated Cuddle Station.

"Cuddle!" Harper shouted.

She launched herself at the sofa.

"Incoming!" Paige warned, bracing herself.

Harper scrambled onto Freddie’s lap. Usually, this was a docking maneuver he welcomed. He liked the weight of her. He liked the quiet way she would rest her head on his chest.

But this wasn't a dock. It was a collision.

Harper tried to settle in, but her body wouldn't cooperate. She was a live wire. She twitched. She kicked her legs. She drummed her fingers on his chest in a manic, irregular rhythm. She buried her face in his neck, breathing fast and hot, then immediately popped up to look around the room.

Freddie flinched. His hands hovered over her waist, unsure of where to grab.

The sensation was wrong. It wasn't the deep, steady pressure he needed. It was erratic. It was sharp. It was like holding a bag of popcorn while it was popping.

"Negative," Freddie said, his voice tight. He pressed back into the sofa, trying to retreat into Paige’s stillness.

"Harper," Freddie said, trying to hold her still, but she slipped through his grip like a ferret. "You are oscillating. I cannot ground you."

"I know!" Harper giggled, squirming. She poked his nose. She poked his glasses. "I'm buzzing. Can you feel it? It feels like sparkles!"

"Input is too high," Freddie said, squeezing his eyes shut. The erratic movement was disrupting his equilibrium. "Paige, assist."

"She's a hazard," Paige laughed, but she saw the tension in Freddie’s jaw. "Harper, eject. You're too hot for the Captain right now. You're scrambling his sensors."

"I have too much voltage!" Harper agreed happily.

She realized she couldn't stay still long enough to enjoy the hug anyway. Her skin felt too tight for her body.

"Okay, gotta go!" Harper declared.

She slid off Freddie’s lap, leaving his shirt rumpled and his glasses crooked.

Freddie immediately grabbed Paige’s hand and pulled it tight against his chest, needing to reset the baseline.

"Status?" Paige asked, smoothing his shirt.

"Frequency mismatch," Freddie exhaled, leaning his head back on Paige’s shoulder. "She is operating on AC current. I require DC."

Harper began doing laps around the coffee table, making race car noises.

Meanwhile, the twins were experiencing their own manic episode.

Jenna was on the floor, wrestling with a sofa cushion. She was laughing hysterically at nothing. "The pillow is fighting back! It’s got me! Send reinforcements!"

Sarah, usually the calm Lieutenant, was pacing the room, gesturing wildly with a napkin she was shredding into confetti.

"Did you know magnesium burns at 3,100 degrees?" Sarah lectured rapidly to no one in particular, walking in tight circles. "That's hot enough to melt iron. We ate cake next to melting iron. That is statistically unsafe but awesome. Do we have any iron? We should test it."

"Status check?" Jessica asked, leaning in the kitchen doorway with a trash bag, watching the asylum.

"System overload," Freddie reported, watching Harper blur past him for the fifth time. "Meltdown imminent."

"Ten minutes," Jessica predicted, checking her watch. "Then we crash."
