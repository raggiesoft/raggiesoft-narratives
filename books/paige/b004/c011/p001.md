# Part 1: Trajectory Calculations

## Monday, June 21, 2004 — 14:00 PM 105 Pepper Street SE

The house was buzzing. The twins were officially fifteen.

In the kitchen, Sarah was demonstrating her "Suffocation Protocol" on a lit match over the sink, just to show off to Harper. She licked her fingers, pinched the flame, and grinned as the smoke curled up.

"See?" Sarah said. "Physics. The moisture absorbs the thermal energy."

Freddie and Paige, sitting at the kitchen table, recoiled in unison. They both pulled their hands into their sleeves.

"Negative," Freddie said, shaking his head. "That is thermal insanity. I require my nerve endings."

"Agreed," Paige shivered, leaning against him. "I prefer blowing. Or water. Touching fire is a glitch."

"It’s efficient!" Sarah argued.

"It’s terrifying," Jenna countered, walking in. "Okay, enough science class. Jessica said we’re going out. Where are we going?"

Jessica walked in, twirling the keys to the sedan. Aunt Linda jingled the keys to the minivan.

"Since you are now fifteen," Jessica announced, "and technically adults-in-training, we decided against the kiddie pizza palace. We are going to Galaxy Lanes."

"Bowling?" Jenna pumped her fist. "Yes! I’m gonna crush you, Sarah."

"We shall see," Sarah said coolly, adjusting her glasses. "Bowling is just geometry with heavy spheres."

Freddie hesitated. He looked down at his wheelchair. He looked at his hands, which weren't strong enough to swing a ten-pound ball.

"I will... observe," Freddie suggested. "I can keep score."

"Nope," Jessica grinned. "You’re playing, Captain. They have technology."
