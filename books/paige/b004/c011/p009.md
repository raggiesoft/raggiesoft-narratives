# Part 9: The Platoon Deployment

## Monday, June 21, 2004 — 21:45 PM The Living Room

The photo session was over. The evidence was secured in Aunt Linda's camera. Now, the logistics began.

Aunt Linda clapped her hands. "Alright, listen up. Tomorrow morning, the minivan leaves for Virginia Beach at 0900 hours. The invasion force is retreating."

A collective groan went up from the younger siblings. They didn't want to leave.

"But," Linda continued, smiling. "For tonight, we are not separating. No one is going to the basement. We are doing a full-scale camp-out right here."

Jessica took charge. This was their house—hers, Paige’s, and Freddie’s. They had bought it from Great Aunt Marge before she passed, using the settlement money. It was their fortress, and tonight, they were lowering the drawbridge.

"Operation Mega-Bed is a go," Jessica announced. "Sarah, Jenna—haul the air mattresses up from the Underworld. Harper, help me move the coffee table."

## The Heavy Lifting

The heavy oak coffee table was slid all the way against the TV stand, opening up a vast rectangle of beige carpet.

Then, Jessica turned to the sofa.

"Evacuate the cushions," Jessica commanded.

She and Paige pulled the cushions off. Jessica gripped the metal handle hidden inside the frame and pulled.

CREAAAK-CLUNK.

The mechanism unfolded. The beige sofa transformed into a Queen-sized sleeper bed with a thin but acceptable mattress.

"The Command Module," Freddie noted, looking at the bed. "Capacity: Three."

"Exactly," Paige said, smoothing a fitted sheet over the mattress. "You're in the middle, Captain. We're the bumpers."

## The Barracks

Sarah and Jenna returned from the basement, dragging bundles of vinyl. The electric pumps roared to life—VRRRRRRRRR—filling the living room with the sound of inflation.

One by one, the "Platoon" took shape on the floor.

## The Bedding Assignments:

- Command Module (Sofa Bed):

  - Freddie transferred from his wheelchair to the center of the Queen bed. He lay back against the pillows, finally taking the weight off his spine.

  - Paige climbed in on his left (Port Side).

  - Jessica climbed in on his right (Starboard Side). It was a tight squeeze for three adults, but they fit like puzzle pieces—a solid wall of Averys.

- The Perimeter (The Floor):

  - Sarah and Jenna took two twin air mattresses on the far side of the room, near the window.

  - Amanda and Elara shared a double air mattress near the kitchen entrance.

  - Aunt Linda claimed a twin mattress near the armchair, acting as the overseer.

That left one spot.

Harper stood holding her pillow, looking at the layout. There was one twin air mattress left.

"I want that one," Harper pointed.

She dragged her mattress until it was jammed right up against the metal frame of the sofa bed, directly below where Freddie was lying.

"Harper, you're going to be underfoot," Sarah warned.

"I don't care," Harper said, dropping her pillow. "I need to be near the Captain. In case of... turbulence."

Freddie looked over the edge of the sofa bed. He could see Harper looking up at him, her eyes wide and tired.

"Permission granted," Freddie whispered.

## 22:15 PM

"Lights out," Aunt Linda whispered from her corner.

She clicked the lamp off. The room plunged into darkness, lit only by the faint green glow of the VCR clock: 10:15.

The room settled. The rustle of sleeping bags and the creak of the vinyl mattresses slowly faded.

It was a full house. A house that Freddie, Paige, and Jessica owned. A house that was usually quiet, now filled with the breathing of nine people.

"Freddie?" Harper’s whisper came from the floor, inches from the bed frame.

"I am here, Harper," Freddie murmured into the dark.

"Are you okay?" Harper asked. "Are you squished?"

Freddie felt Paige’s arm draped over his chest. He felt Jessica’s shoulder pressed against his. He felt the solid presence of his sisters on all sides.

"I am contained," Freddie whispered back. "It is optimal."

"Okay," Harper yawned. "Goodnight, Captain."

"Goodnight, Harper."

Freddie closed his eyes. The nightmares couldn't get in here. There was no room for them. The bed was full. The floor was full. The house was full.

For the first time since the bus ride, Freddie Avery slept without dreaming.
