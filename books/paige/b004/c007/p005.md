# Part 5: The Soundtrack Protocol

## Thursday, May 13, 2004 — 10:20 AM (PST) Concourse C, SeaTac Airport

They were rolling past a duty-free shop when the vibration hit.

Through his foam earplugs, Freddie felt a rhythmic thumping. It wasn't the chaotic noise of the terminal; it was a beat. A specific, high-energy, synthesized bassline. *Thump-thump-thump-thump.*

He recognized the frequency.

"Halt," Freddie said, gripping the armrests.

"What's wrong?" Jessica asked, stopping the chair instantly. "Too crowded?"

"The audio," Freddie whispered.

He reached up and pulled the yellow foam plug out of his left ear.

The sound rushed in. It was coming from the overhead speakers of a trendy clothing store called *Urban Pulse*.

It was **"Strut Like a Man."**

But it wasn't the original 1960s version by **Vinnie & The Valiants**, with its high-pitched falsetto and doo-wop snapping.

It was the 1980s Hi-NRG cover by the drag superstar **Delight**.

The beat was relentless. The synthesizers were aggressive. And Delight’s voice wasn't asking for permission—it was barking orders with a gravelly, powerful growl. The perspective wasn't first-person; she was telling a story about a man breaking free.

Freddie listened to the verse thundering through the store entrance:

*“Oh how she tried to cut him down to size,* *Telling dirty lies to his friends.* *But he stood up taller, didn't let her bother,* *Said 'The world isn't coming to an end.'”*

Freddie sat frozen. The data matched perfectly. Vanessa had tried to cut him down. She had tried to minimize him, to make him small, to isolate him from his "friends" (his sisters).

Then the chorus hit, an explosion of synthesized brass and drums:

*“Strut like a man, talk like a man,* *Strut like a man, my son.* *No woman's worth crawling on the earth,* *So strut like a man, my son.”*

"She's singing about me," Freddie whispered.

"Who?" Paige asked, leaning in.

"Delight," Freddie said, nodding toward the speaker. "The lyrics are in the third person. She is observing the subject. The subject is leaving a toxic variable."

He listened as the bridge kicked in:

*“Bye bye baby, I don't mean maybe,* *Gonna get along somehow.* *Soon she'll be crying on account of all her lying,* *Oh yeah just look who's laughing now.* *He's gonna strut like a man,* *Fast as he can,* *Strut like a man from you.”*

"Fast as he can," Freddie repeated, gripping the wheels of his chair. "He is escaping. He is walking away from 'Her'."

"That's exactly what you did," Jessica said, tapping her hand on the handle of the wheelchair in time with the beat. "You didn't crawl. You walked."

"I am rolling," Freddie corrected. "But the trajectory is the same."

A random file from Freddie's "Pop Culture Database" popped open as he watched the music video playing on a monitor in the window.

"Trivia," Freddie murmured, his eyes losing focus as he accessed the fact. "The character design for **Oceana the Sea Witch** in the movie *The Sea Princess* was based on Delight. The arch of the eyebrows. The body type. The confidence."

He paused, a shadow of sadness crossing his face.

"Delight never saw it," Freddie added softly. "He died of heart failure three weeks before the premiere. He never knew he became an animation icon."

He looked at the store one last time. The song was fading out.

*“He'll tell the world 'Forget about it girl, I'd strut like a man from you.'”*

"Forget about it, girl," Freddie whispered to the memory of Vanessa.

He took the yellow foam plug and rolled it between his fingers, compressing it into a tight cylinder. He jammed it back into his ear canal. He held it there for ten seconds until it expanded, sealing the world out again.

"Protocol complete," Freddie said, his voice muffled in his own head. "Let's go to the gate."

Jessica pushed the chair forward. Freddie sat a little taller, the bassline still thumping in his chest. He wasn't crawling on the earth for anyone. He was going home.
