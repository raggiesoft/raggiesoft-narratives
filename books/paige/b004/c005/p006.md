# Part 6: The Scar

## Sunday, May 9, 2004 — 15:45 PM (PST) The Hill Climb

They walked from Pier 59 back toward the **Hill Climb** elevator. The waterfront was noisy, smelling of diesel from the ferries and frying dough from the tourist stands.

Above them, a massive, double-decked concrete structure loomed over the street, blocking out the sky. It roared with the sound of thousands of cars thundering overhead.

The **Alaskan Way Viaduct**.

Freddie stopped. He looked up at the grey, stained concrete underbelly of the highway. It was loud. It dripped water. It cut the city off from the sea like a prison wall.

"Architecture check," Freddie muttered, wincing at the noise of a truck hitting an expansion joint above. "That structure is... hostile."

"It's ugly," Jessica agreed. "It’s like a scar."

"It is a barrier," Freddie corrected. "It severs the connection between the urban grid and the hydrological system. It is inefficient. It is loud. It should be removed."

"Maybe they'll tear it down one day," Paige said, guiding him toward the elevator.

"They should put it underground," Freddie decided. "Like the bus tunnel. Hide the noise. Open the view."

They rode the elevator up to the market level, leaving the roar of the Viaduct behind.

## 16:00 PM — 1st Avenue

They walked to the **TAPS** bus stop on 1st Avenue. A teal-and-white trolleybus was approaching, its electric poles sparking slightly against the overhead wires.

"Electric trolley," Freddie noted, pleased. "Route 10. It goes near the hotel."

They boarded. The bus was quiet, humming with electric power. Freddie took a window seat. Paige sat next to him, re-establishing the anchor. Jessica sat across the aisle.

Freddie looked out the window as they rolled through the city. He saw the buildings. He saw the people.

He didn't see a prison anymore. He saw a city that had problems (the Viaduct) and solutions (the Tunnel). Just like him.

He looked at his hand, still laced with Paige's.

*We move as a unit,* he thought. *Italy. New York. Or just back to the hotel.*

"Status?" Paige whispered.

"Mobility verified," Freddie said. "The chair is... a valid option. I will consider acquisition."

"We'll get you a fast one," Jessica promised from across the aisle. "Titanium. Matte black. Like a stealth fighter."

Freddie smiled. "Stealth mode," he whispered. "I like that."

The electric bus hummed up the street, carrying the Unit back to base, ready for the next day's mission.
