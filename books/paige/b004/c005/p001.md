# Part 1: Fueling the Expedition

## Sunday, May 9, 2004 — 08:30 AM (PST) The Cascade Suites, Room 412

The blackout curtains held the line against the morning, maintaining the protective twilight of the "Silo," but the digital clock on the microwave insisted it was time to start the day.

Freddie woke up in the center of the King bed. He was warm. He was compressed. He was safe.

To his left, Jessica was already stirring, checking her watch with the efficiency of a shift commander. To his right, Paige was still deeply asleep, her arm thrown over his chest like a seatbelt, anchoring him to the mattress.

"Morning, Captain," Jessica whispered, sitting up and stretching.

"Morning," Freddie rasped. He ran a diagnostic. His back was stiff from the injury, throbbing with a dull ache where the skin was healing, but the "static" in his head was low. The anxiety that usually greeted him upon waking—Where am I? Is she here?—was absent. He knew exactly where he was. He was with the Unit.

"Weather check," Jessica announced, sliding out of bed. She walked to the window and cracked the heavy curtains just an inch.

A beam of bright, clean sunlight sliced into the room, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air.

"Visual confirmation," Jessica smiled, looking back at them. "Blue skies. High of 65 degrees. Perfect visibility for the Spire."

"65 degrees," Freddie noted, sitting up carefully. "Low humidity. Optimal."

## 09:00 AM — The Kitchenette

They moved with the easy rhythm of a team that had lived in close quarters for years. There was no scrambling for modesty, no hiding behind doors. They simply moved from the bed to the bathroom to the kitchenette, getting dressed in comfortable layers for a day of walking.

Freddie put on fresh jeans, wincing slightly as the waistband settled near the bandage on his lower back, and a soft grey t-shirt. He tied his orange hoodie around his waist—just in case the temperature dropped or he needed the armor.

Jessica took command of the stove. The kitchenette was small, but she treated it like a restaurant line.

"Order up," Jessica said, cracking eggs into a bowl. "The Captain needs protein for the climb."

She whisked the eggs with a fork. She added diced ham, cheddar cheese, and mushrooms she had prepped the night before from their Market Street run.

"Omelet," Freddie said, watching the pan sizzle. "Structural integrity looks good."

"Wheat toast," Paige added, popping two slices into the toaster. "Grape jelly. And..."

She held up the canister.

"White Hot Chocolate."

"Essential," Freddie agreed.

For themselves, the sisters opted for something lighter but equally fueling.

"Yogurt parfaits," Jessica said, setting out bowls of vanilla yogurt, granola, and sliced strawberries. "And coffee. Lots of coffee."

## 09:30 AM — The Dining Table

They sat at the small round table. The map of Seattle was spread out under their plates like a tablecloth.

Freddie cut into his omelet. It was fluffy, hot, and savory. He took a bite of toast with grape jelly—sweet and crunchy. He took a sip of the white hot chocolate—creamy and soothing.

"Fuel status?" Paige asked, crunching on granola.

"Tanks filling," Freddie said. He pointed to the map with his fork.

"Vector confirmed," he stated. "We exit the hotel. We enter the Transit Tunnel at Convention Place. We board the bus. We transfer to the Metro-Rail at Westlake Center. We proceed to the Sky-Spire."

"And the bus ride?" Jessica asked gently, watching him over her coffee mug.

Freddie paused. He thought about the Pathfinder. He thought about the vibration, the smell, the fear.

Then he looked at the map. He saw the "Ride Free Area" box. He saw the tunnel line.

"The bus is electric," Freddie reminded himself. "It is a dual-mode. It runs on overhead wires in the tunnel. It is silent. It is clean."

He looked at Paige.

"And the crew is correct," he added.

Paige smiled, reaching over to squeeze his hand. "The crew is ready."

"Then we launch," Freddie said, finishing his chocolate. "Tourist Protocol is active."

They cleared the table. They grabbed their bags (water, cameras, jackets). They walked out the door of Room 412, not as refugees fleeing a crime scene, but as explorers stepping out into a brand new world.
