# Part 1: The Invasion Force

## Saturday, June 19, 2004 — 18:45 PM 105 Pepper Street SE

The sun was beginning to dip behind the Blue Ridge Mountains, casting long shadows across the driveway.

Freddie sat on the front porch in The Rover, acting as Air Traffic Control. He held a walkie-talkie (a set they used to communicate between the house and the porch without yelling).

"Target is visual," Freddie spoke into the device. "Maroon minivan turning onto Pepper Street."

"Copy that," Jessica’s voice crackled back. "Pizza is in the oven. The landing zone is clear."

The minivan—a bruised and battered voyager of the highways—pulled into the driveway. It looked heavy, riding low on its suspension from the weight of five children, one adult, and luggage for a weekend.

The engine died. For a split second, there was silence.

Then, the doors slid open.

"We’re here!" Harper (11) yelled, tumbling out first. She stretched her legs, grimacing at the stiffness, her headphones around her neck blasting tinny pop music.

The twins, Amanda and Elara (9), scrambled out next, moving like a single entity. They blinked at the new house, processing the data.

Sarah and Jenna (turning 15 in two days) emerged from the back row. They looked exhausted.

And finally, Aunt Linda stepped out of the driver’s seat. She looked like a woman who had survived five hours of "Are we there yet?" and "She’s touching me!"

"Welcome to the Base," Freddie said, rolling down the ramp to meet them.

"Freddie!" Elara screamed. She ran up and hugged his knees. Amanda followed silently, placing her hand on his arm to verify he was real.

"Hey, Captain," Sarah said, adjusting her glasses. She looked at the house. "So this is the fortress?"

"It is fortified," Freddie confirmed. "Come. Jessica has carbohydrates."
