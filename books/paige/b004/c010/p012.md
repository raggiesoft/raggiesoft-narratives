# Part 12: Structural Integrity Test

## Sunday, June 20, 2004 — 12:30 PM The Living Room (Surface Level)

While the Laundry Brigade conquered the mountains of denim in the basement, the living room had been transformed into a high-density affection zone.

Freddie sat in the center of the beige sofa. To an outside observer, he might have looked like a hostage. To Freddie, he felt like a tree that had been colonized by very aggressive, very loving wildlife.

Paige was the root system. She hadn't moved in an hour. She was pressed tight against his left side, her arm locked through his, her head resting heavily on his shoulder. She was reading a paperback with one hand, her thumb rhythmically stroking the side of his arm. She was the static variable—the unmoving anchor that kept him from floating away.

The twins, Amanda and Elara, were the dynamic variables.

"Scaling the north face!" Elara announced.

Elara (Age 9) had climbed up the back of the sofa. She draped herself over Freddie’s shoulders like a human scarf, her chin resting on top of his head, her arms dangling loosely over his chest.

"Elara," Freddie noted, his voice vibrating through her chest. "You are obstructing my visual field."

"I'm a hat," Elara giggled, swinging her legs which were dangling behind his head. "I'm keeping your brain warm."

Amanda (Age 9) occupied the southern territory. She was kneeling on the sofa cushions on Freddie’s right side. She wasn't content to just sit next to him; she treated him like a piece of playground equipment.

She climbed carefully onto his lap, avoiding his sensitive knees with practiced precision. She straddled his waist, facing him, and wrapped her arms around his neck. She pressed her forehead against his nose—her signature "Mind Meld."

Input Warning: Restriction of movement. Temperature critical. Oxygen intake reduced by 15%.

Freddie’s logic brain flashed the red lights. He was completely immobilized. He had a sister on his left, a sister on his head, and a sister on his chest. He couldn't scratch his nose if he wanted to.

He closed his eyes. He took a breath, smelling the vanilla shampoo Elara used and the faint scent of crayons on Amanda’s hands.

He compared this to the Pathfinder bus. He compared it to the empty apartment in Norfolk. He compared it to the cold, clinical way Vanessa had touched him—like he was a project to be fixed, not a person to be held.

This wasn't fixing. This was fusing.

"Status?" Paige asked softly, feeling his breathing shift under the weight of the twins. "Are they crushing you?"

"Structural integrity is holding," Freddie whispered. "The pressure is... acceptable."

Amanda pulled back from the Mind Meld. She looked at Freddie with wide, dark eyes. She took his glasses off his face and put them on her own. They were way too big; they slid down her nose instantly.

She smiled—a silent, gummy grin.

"She says you look like a bug with them on," Elara translated from the top of his head. "And she says you are squishy."

"I am not squishy," Freddie corrected, gently taking his glasses back from Amanda and sliding them back on. "I am resilient."

Amanda hummed a low note—Mmm-hmm—and collapsed forward, burying her face in the crook of his neck. She went boneless, turning into dead weight.

"Docking complete," Freddie murmured, wrapping his free arm around Amanda’s back to keep her from sliding off.

"I want in," Elara decided.

She slid down from the top of his head, tumbling over his shoulder like a slinky, and wedged herself into the tiny gap between Freddie and the armrest on the right. She grabbed Freddie’s right hand and started playing with his fingers, counting them one by one.

"One, two, three, four, thumb," Elara counted.

Freddie sat there. He was a lattice of limbs. He was the center of their universe.

"You know," Paige said, not looking up from her book, but squeezing his arm tighter. "Vanessa would hate this."

"She would categorize it as chaotic," Freddie agreed. "She would say it lacks boundaries."

"Do we have boundaries?" Elara asked, interlocking her fingers with Freddie’s.

"Negative," Freddie said. "We have connections."

He rested his chin on top of Amanda’s head. He leaned his cheek against Elara’s hair. He felt Paige breathing against his ribs.

The sensory overload was massive. It was loud, hot, and heavy.

And Freddie wouldn't have traded it for all the silence in the world.

"Hold position," Freddie ordered softly.

"Aye aye, Captain," Elara whispered.

Amanda just squeezed him tighter.

The Jungle Gym was open, and it was never closing down.
