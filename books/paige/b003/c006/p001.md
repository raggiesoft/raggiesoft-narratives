# Part 1: The Cool Down

## Wednesday, July 9, 2003 15:40 PM

The **Blue Ridge Transit** bus hissed to a halt in the sawtooth bay of **Pepper Plaza**. The doors folded open, and the Avery unit stepped out onto the concrete.

The heat in Christiansburg was just as aggressive as it had been in Blacksburg. The air was heavy, thick with moisture and the smell of asphalt baking in the sun.

"Home," Jessica announced, pointing diagonally across the street.

They waited for a break in the traffic on First Street, then crossed quickly to the sanctuary of **105 Pepper Street**.

Jessica unlocked the front door. They stumbled inside, the air conditioning hitting them like a blessing. The hum of the window unit was the best sound Freddie had heard all day.

"Locking," Jessica said, throwing the deadbolt and engaging the security chain.

"Decontamination," Paige gasped, pulling her sticky t-shirt away from her skin. "I feel gross. The humidity is... excessive."

"Agreed," Freddie said, leaning against the wall to kick off his sneakers. "Thermal regulation is required immediately."

They moved as a unit down the hallway to the Master Bedroom—the **Silo**.

There was no hesitation. The "Modesty Protocol" had been deleted days ago. In this heat, and after the stress of the encounter at Newman Hall, clothes were just another layer of friction.

Freddie peeled off his orange hoodie, which felt heavy and suffocating. He dropped it into the hamper. His jeans followed.

Paige and Jessica did the same, shedding their sweat-dampened clothes with sighs of relief.

"Tub," Jessica commanded. "Cool water. Not cold. Just cool."

They walked into the bathroom. The clear curtain was pulled back. Jessica reached in and turned the tap. She adjusted the mix until the water running over her wrist was a refreshing, tepid temperature—perfect for bringing down a core body temperature without shocking the system.

"Ready, Freddie?" Paige asked.

"Ready," Freddie said.

His legs were trembling from the exertion of the day. The spasticity was flaring up due to the heat. He couldn't lift his leg high enough to clear the porcelain rim on his own.

He didn't have to ask. Jessica took his left arm; Paige took his right. They braced themselves, their skin slick against his.

"Up," Jessica counted. "And over."

They managed the transfer with the efficiency of a pit crew. Freddie slid down into the water. He let out a long, groaning exhale as the cool liquid surrounded him, sapping the heat from his tired muscles.

"Better?" Paige asked, kneeling on the bathmat.

"System cooling," Freddie murmured, closing his eyes. "Operating temperature normalizing."

Jessica stepped into the tub behind him, sitting on the edge to let her feet soak, grabbing the sponge. Paige knelt beside the tub, taking the soap.

They began to wash him. It wasn't a frantic scrub; it was a rhythmic, calming maintenance routine. Paige soaped his arms, her hands firm and sure. Jessica squeezed the sponge over his shoulders, letting the water cascade down his back.

Freddie opened his eyes. He looked at his sisters. He saw them clearly—no clothes, no barriers, just skin and care.

And suddenly, he started to laugh.

It wasn't a nervous laugh. It was a genuine, bubbling chuckle that shook his shoulders and made the water ripple.

Paige paused, the soap hovering over his elbow. "What? What's funny?"

"Krystal," Freddie wheezed, a smile breaking across his face.

"What about her?" Jessica asked, wringing out the sponge.

"I am running a simulation," Freddie said, looking from Jessica to Paige. "I am imagining Krystal standing in this doorway right now."

He gestured to the scene: him, naked in the tub; his sisters, naked and washing him.

"She believed you were aides," Freddie explained. "Hired staff. Employees."

"Right," Jessica scoffed. "The 'Help'."

"If you were 'The Help'," Freddie continued, his logic engine finding the humor in the absurdity, "this scenario would be a violation of Occupational Safety and Health Administration protocols. Aides remain clothed. Aides wear gloves. Aides wash with clinical detachment."

He looked at Paige’s hand resting gently on his chest.

"Aides do not provide... this. This is not clinical. This is structural."

Paige giggled. She imagined the look on Krystal's face—the absolute, brain-melting confusion she would experience if she saw them right now.

"She would explode," Paige laughed. "Her brain wouldn't be able to process it. She’d probably call the police."

"Or she would think it was an orgy," Jessica added dryly, pouring water over Freddie’s hair. "Because her database only has two folders: 'Professional' and 'Sexual.' She doesn't have a folder for 'Family.'"

"She lacks the source code," Freddie agreed. "She cannot compile the file."

The three of them laughed together, the sound bouncing off the tiled walls. It was a victory laugh. They had taken the thing Krystal tried to make weird—their closeness, their intimacy—and they had reclaimed it.

"Well," Jessica said, wiping a bubble of soap from Freddie’s nose. "She is never going to find out. Because she is never setting foot in this house."

"Banned," Freddie stated firmly. "Blacklisted."

"Added to the firewall," Paige agreed. "List of Banned Entities: 1. Robert. 2. Krystal."

"Security level: Maximum," Jessica confirmed. "No unauthorized packets allowed."

She rinsed Freddie’s back one last time. "Okay, Navigator. You're clean. And you're cool. Let's get dried off and get in the bed. I think we earned a nap."

"Affirmative," Freddie said, reaching for the towel.

He felt clean. Not just from the soap, but from the laughter. The static was gone. The heat was gone.

He was in the Base, and the perimeter was secure.
