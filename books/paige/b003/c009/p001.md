# Part 1: The Purge

## Wednesday, July 16, 2003 13:00 PM

The atmosphere in **Room 204** of Archer Hall was tense. The humidity of the Virginia summer pressed against the windows, but inside, the air conditioning kept the room at a crisp, clinical temperature.

Dr. Aris Thorne stood at the front of the room, her posture rigid. She did not look like a professor who was about to lecture; she looked like a general inspecting the troops before a battle.

On the whiteboard behind her, she had written a single word in stark, black letters:

**ANATOMY**

Freddie sat in his usual spot in the second row, flanked by the Avery Phalanx. Jessica was on his left, Paige on his right. His notebook was open. His pen was uncapped.

He was ready. The "Manual" had been reviewed. The data had been verified.

"Today," Dr. Thorne announced, her voice cutting through the murmurs of the class, "we begin our detailed study of the reproductive systems. We start with the female anatomy."

A ripple of nervous energy went through the room.

"As I stated on day one," Dr. Thorne continued, pacing slowly, "this is a science class. We will use proper terminology. We will view clinical diagrams. We will discuss function and physiology without euphemism."

She clicked a button on her remote. The overhead projector whirred to life.

A detailed, cross-section diagram appeared on the screen. It was clinical, labeled with numbers corresponding to structures like the uterus, fallopian tubes, and ovaries.

From the back row—the row Freddie had internally labeled "The Noise Sector"—came the sound.

It started as a snicker. Then a whisper. Then a distinct, frat-boy voice said something that was definitely not a medical term.

It was followed by a chorus of stifled laughter from three other guys sitting nearby. They were leaning back in their chairs, hats backward, clearly treating the lecture like a locker room joke.

Dr. Thorne didn't flinch. She didn't even look at the screen. She looked directly at the back row.

"Mr. Peterson," she said.

The laughter died instantly.

"Dr. Thorne?" the boy asked, trying to look innocent.

"Pack your bag," Dr. Thorne said. "And take Mr. Davies and Mr. Miller with you."

"Whoa, wait," Peterson protested, sitting up. "We didn't do anything. We were just—"

"You were demonstrating that you lack the maturity required to be in this room," Dr. Thorne interrupted, her voice ice cold. "I warned you on Monday. Zero tolerance."

She walked to her desk and picked up a red folder.

"Furthermore," she added, enjoying the moment, "today is July 16th. The deadline to drop a class with a refund passed on Friday. The deadline to withdraw with a 'W' grade passed yesterday at 5:00 PM."

The color drained from the boys' faces.

"That means," Dr. Thorne continued, "that when I administratively remove you from this roster for disruptive conduct, the Registrar will record a grade of **F**."

"You can't fail us!" one of the other boys shouted, standing up. "My GPA—"

"Your GPA reflects your performance," Dr. Thorne stated. "And your performance here is a failure. Get out."

The silence that followed was heavy. The three boys stood up, grabbing their bags with a mix of fury and humiliation. They stomped out of the room, muttering curses, slamming the door behind them so hard the frame rattled.

Dr. Thorne watched the door close. Then she turned back to the class.

She looked at the remaining students. The room felt different now. It was lighter. The "static" was gone.

The only people left were the ones who were writing notes. The nursing students. The psychology majors. And the Avery Unit.

"Now," Dr. Thorne said, her voice returning to a calm, lecture tone. "That concludes the filtration process. Let us proceed with the science."

She pointed to the diagram on the screen.

"Who can identify Structure Number 4?"

Freddie looked at the screen. He looked at the diagram. It matched the one in his textbook perfectly.

He didn't giggle. He didn't blush. He raised his hand.

"Yes, Mr. Avery?" Dr. Thorne asked.

"Structure Number 4 is the Fallopian Tube," Freddie stated clearly. "It serves as the transport vessel for the ova."

"Correct," Dr. Thorne nodded. "Excellent terminology."

Freddie lowered his hand. He felt Paige squeeze his arm. He felt Jessica bump his shoulder.

He looked around the room. The creeps were gone. The noise was gone.

"Stable," Freddie whispered to himself. "The class is stable."
