# Part 5: The Uplink

## Monday, July 14, 2003 12:15 PM

The walk from **105 Pepper Street** to the **Pepper Plaza Transit Center** took exactly three minutes. It was a diagonal line across the street, a vector Freddie had already memorized.

The summer heat was oppressive, shimmering off the asphalt, but Freddie didn't mind. He had the letter. He had the "Source Code" folded neatly in his back pocket, right next to his wallet.

"Bus inbound," Jessica announced, shielding her eyes against the sun.

Turning the corner from North Franklin Street was a white bus with maroon and orange stripes. It was a heavy-duty, 40-foot **Stalwart** city bus, the workhorse of the **Blue Ridge Transit** fleet.

The LED destination sign above the windshield glowed amber in the bright light: **1 CPI**

"On time," Freddie noted, checking his watch. "The grid is active."

They stood in the shelter of the sawtooth bay. As the bus hissed to a halt, the air brakes purged with a sharp *psshhh*—a sound Freddie associated with the start of a journey. The doors folded open.

"Badges out," Jessica said, pulling her **PolyPass** from her purse.

She stepped up first. *Beep.* The card reader chirped a valid read. Paige followed. *Beep.* Freddie stepped up last. He pulled his card from his pocket—the warm plastic rectangle that identified him as **FREDDIE AVERY**. He tapped it against the target.

*Beep.*

The driver nodded. "Welcome aboard."

Freddie walked down the aisle. It was the summer session, so the bus wasn't at "crush load." There were plenty of seats. He chose a row near the back window. He slid in next to the glass. Paige sat next to him. Jessica took the seat in front, turning sideways to face them.

"Next stop," the automated voice announced as the bus rumbled out of the plaza. *"Business 460."*

The bus merged onto the highway, the diesel engine shifting gears smoothly. Freddie pressed his forehead against the cool glass. For the next thirty minutes, he didn't have to make decisions. He didn't have to interpret facial expressions. He just had to sit.

He felt the vibration of the road through the seat. He felt Paige’s shoulder against his. He felt the paper in his pocket.

"We are delivering the patch," Freddie whispered, watching the trees blur by.

"We are," Paige agreed. "Straight to the SSD office. Once they have that letter, the accommodations are locked in. Extended time. Quiet rooms. No more surprises."

"No more surprises," Freddie repeated.

The bus rolled past the strip malls of Christiansburg, climbing the hill toward Blacksburg. The landscape shifted from commercial sprawl to the green, rolling hills of the New River Valley.

Usually, this transition made Freddie anxious—leaving the known for the unknown. But today, he wasn't just a passenger. He was an Admin. He had his credentials.

"Automated Voice," the speaker crackled overhead. *"Approaching CPI. End of the line."*

The stone towers of **Commonwealth Polytechnic** rose in the distance, looking less like a fortress to be conquered and more like a server farm waiting to be programmed.

"Ready to upload?" Jessica asked, standing up as the bus slowed.

Freddie stood up. He adjusted his glasses. He checked his pocket one last time to make sure the letter was still there.

"Upload sequence initiated," Freddie said. "Let's go."
