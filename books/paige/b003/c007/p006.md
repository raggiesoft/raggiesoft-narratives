# Part 7: The Thunderhead

## Monday, July 14, 2003 15:00 PM

The sky over the **New River Valley** had turned a bruised, angry purple. The summer heat had broken, but it had broken violently.

Inside **Archer Hall**, the world was shaking.

A massive thunderstorm was parking itself directly over the campus. Rain lashed against the plate-glass windows of the student lounge area with such force that it sounded like gravel. Outside, visibility was zero; the building across the quad had vanished behind a curtain of grey water.

*BOOM.*

Thunder cracked directly overhead, vibrating through the terrazzo floor.

Freddie flinched violently. He was lying on a vinyl bench in the corner of the lounge, his legs curled up, his head pressed hard into Paige’s lap. He had his hands clamped over his ears, eyes squeezed shut.

"Sensory overload," Freddie gasped. "Audio input exceeds safety limits."

"I know," Paige whispered, leaning over him to create a human shelter. She pressed her hands over his hands, adding another layer of pressure. "It’s just noise, Freddie. It’s just electricity discharging. It can’t get in here."

*FLASH.*

Lightning lit up the lounge like a strobe light, followed instantly by another earth-shaking *CRACK*.

Freddie whimpered, burying his face in Paige’s stomach. To him, the thunder wasn't just sound; it was a physical assault. It was unpredictable. It was chaotic. It was the opposite of structure.

Jessica walked quickly across the lounge, balancing a cardboard tray from the vending machines. She looked wet; she had sprinted from the snack bar down the hall.

"Fuel secured," Jessica announced, sliding onto the bench at Freddie’s feet. "I got pretzels and water. The main cafeteria is a no-go. We are trapped until this cell passes."

"Status of the storm?" Paige asked, looking up.

"Radar is red," Jessica reported grimly. "It's a heavy cell. Probably another twenty minutes of the heavy stuff."

Freddie groaned into Paige’s shirt. "Twenty minutes is 1,200 seconds. That is too many seconds."

"Focus on the count," Paige instructed gently. "Breathe in... one, two, three..."

They were in their formation. The Avery Unit. Shield up. Anchor down. Waiting out the chaos.

But the chaos had an avatar.

Krystal emerged from the hallway. She was bored. She was stuck in the building, her flip-phone had no signal in the storm, and she was agitated.

She spotted them in the corner. She saw Freddie curled up in a fetal position, hiding in his sister's lap.

To anyone else, it looked like a medical situation. To Krystal, it looked like an opportunity.

*He’s scared,* she thought. *He’s vulnerable.*

She walked over, her heels clicking on the hard floor. She stopped right in front of the bench, looming over them.

"Wow," Krystal said, her voice cutting through the hum of the rain. "He really doesn't like the thunder, huh?"

Jessica looked up. Her eyes were hard. "Go away, Krystal. Now is not the time."

Krystal ignored her. She stepped closer to Freddie’s head. She leaned down, putting her face uncomfortably close to his ear.

"Hey, Freddie," she purred. "It's pretty loud out here, isn't it? All that banging? It’s scary."

Freddie stiffened. He could smell her perfume—too sweet, too chemical. It mixed with the ozone smell of the storm to create a nauseating cocktail.

"I have an idea," Krystal whispered, glancing at the sisters with a smirk. "I know a place where you can't hear the thunder. There's a janitor's closet down the hall. In the interior corridor. It’s thick concrete. Totally soundproof."

She paused, letting the implication hang in the air like humidity.

"It's dark in there," she added. "And it's private. Just you and me. I could... distract you. Make you forget all about the storm. We could have some real fun while we wait."

Paige froze. Her hands stopped rubbing Freddie’s back.

Freddie peeled one eye open. He looked at Krystal.

"Janitor's closet," Freddie repeated, his voice shaking. "Storage for chemical solvents. Mops. Buckets. High risk of contamination."

"No, silly," Krystal giggled, reaching out to touch his arm. "It's cozy. Come on. Leave your sisters here. Let me take care of you. I promise, I can make you feel *so* much better than a hug."

That was the line.

Jessica didn't stand up. She *launched* herself.

She slammed the tray of pretzels onto the table and rounded the bench in a single, fluid motion. She stepped directly into Krystal’s personal space, forcing the girl to stumble back in her high-heeled sandals.

"THAT IS ENOUGH!" Jessica screamed.

The sound was louder than the thunder. The few other students in the lounge turned to look. Jessica didn't care.

"Are you deaf?" Jessica shouted, pointing a finger inches from Krystal’s nose. "Or are you just stupid? He doesn't want you! He is in distress! He is overwhelmed! And you are trying to *seduce* him into a broom closet?"

"I was just trying to help!" Krystal stammered, shrinking back. "He needs—"

"He needs safety!" Jessica roared. "He needs stability! He does not need a quickie in a room that smells like bleach with a girl who thinks 'no' is just a suggestion!"

Krystal flushed red. "You're just jealous because he—"

"Jealous?" Jessica laughed, a harsh, terrifying sound. "I am his sister! I am his protector! You are a predator, Krystal. You see a boy who struggles to walk, a boy who is sensitive to noise, and you think, 'Easy target.' You think you can just wear him down."

Jessica took another step forward. Krystal hit the wall.

"You are done," Jessica hissed, her voice dropping to a lethal, low register. "If you come near him again—if you offer him sex, cigarettes, or a 'good time'—I will file a harassment complaint with the Dean so fast your head will spin. I will get you expelled. Do you understand me?"

Krystal looked at Jessica. She looked at the fury in her eyes. Then she looked past her at the bench.

Freddie had sat up. He wasn't looking at Krystal. He was looking at Paige.

Paige had her arms wrapped tight around his shoulders. She was rocking him gently.

"She is a virus," Freddie stated clearly, his voice cutting through the silence of the room. "Quarantine her."

"You heard him," Jessica snarled. "Get. Out."

Krystal scrambled. She didn't have a comeback. The fantasy of being the savior, the seductress, crumbled under the weight of Jessica’s rage. She turned and practically ran down the hallway, disappearing toward the restrooms.

Jessica stood there for a moment, her chest heaving. She smoothed her shirt. She took a deep breath.

She turned back to the bench.

"Threat neutralized," Jessica announced, her voice returning to normal.

She sat back down. She picked up a pretzel and handed it to Freddie.

"Eat," she commanded. "Salt helps the shock."

Freddie took the pretzel. He took a bite. The crunch was grounding.

"That was... loud," Freddie said, looking at Jessica.

"Sometimes you have to be louder than the storm," Jessica shrugged.

*BOOM.*

Another clap of thunder shook the room. Freddie flinched, dropping the pretzel and diving back into Paige’s lap.

"It is still here," he whimpered.

"I know," Paige whispered, resuming her stroking of his hair. "But Krystal isn't. It's just us, Freddie. Just the Unit."

"Hug required," Freddie mumbled. "Duration: Infinite."

"Accepted," Paige promised, leaning her cheek on top of his head as the rain battered the glass. "I will hug you for the rest of your life if you want, Freddie. I'm not going anywhere."

"Good," Freddie sighed, closing his eyes. "The closet... was an illogical proposal."

"Very illogical," Paige agreed.

Outside, the storm raged on. But inside the circle of the Avery Unit, the perimeter was finally, completely secure.
