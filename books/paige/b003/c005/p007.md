# Part 7: The Incorrect Variable

## Wednesday, July 9, 2003 14:00 PM

**Newman Hall** was the living room of the university. While the library was for silence and the labs were for code, Newman was for noise, food, and decompression.

Freddie sat on a low, plush sofa in the main atrium. He physically could not sit at the high-top tables near the café; the bar-stool height required a climb his spastic diplegia made dangerous, and his balance was too precarious for a seat without a back. The sofa was the only viable option.

He had the emergency brake engaged and was currently in "Recharge Mode." His legs were stretched out on the coffee table (a minor violation of etiquette he ignored). Paige sat next to him, reading a paperback. Freddie was lying with his head in her lap.

It was a public display of intimacy that most siblings abandoned in middle school. But for the Avery Unit, it was functional. The weight of her hand resting on his chest provided the necessary pressure to keep his heart rate steady in the bustling student center.

"Page turn," Freddie murmured, hearing the rustle of paper above him.

"Chapter four," Paige replied softly, tracing a pattern on his shoulder with her thumb.

A shadow fell across them.

Freddie opened his eyes. He saw a pair of designer jeans, a tight pink top, and a face that was trying very hard to look friendly. It was the girl from Monday. The one Dr. Thorne had ejected.

"Hey," she said. She didn't sound aggressive today. She sounded... rehearsed.

Freddie tensed. He felt Paige’s hand stop moving on his chest.

"I didn't catch your name the other day," the girl said, shifting her weight to one hip. "I'm Krystal. With a K."

Freddie sat up slowly. He didn't want to engage, but the social contract required an acknowledgment of a direct address. He swung his legs off the table.

"Freddie," he stated.

"Freddie," Krystal repeated, testing the word like it was a flavor. "That’s a nice name. Look, Freddie, I wanted to apologize. For Monday. I came on a little strong in the lobby."

She glanced at Paige, then back to Freddie. She offered a smile that didn't quite reach her eyes.

"I figured maybe we got off on the wrong foot. I wanted to see if I could buy you a coffee? There’s a place right over there." She pointed to the **Espresso Bar** in the corner. "My treat. No strings attached. Just... getting to know you."

Freddie processed the offer. *Input:* Coffee. *Variable:* Caffeine causes tremors. *Variable:* Bitter taste profile. *Conclusion:* Incompatible.

"I do not drink coffee," Freddie said flatly. "It induces kinetic instability."

Krystal blinked. "Oh. Okay. Well, they have tea? Or soda?"

"I drink hot chocolate," Freddie corrected. "With whole milk. But I do not require a beverage at this time."

He turned his body away from her, rotating toward Paige. He lifted his arm and wrapped it around Paige’s waist, pulling himself into her side. He buried his face in her shoulder, further engaging the "emergency brake" protocol.

It was a clear, non-verbal signal: *I am occupied. I am safe. Go away.*

Krystal watched the hug. She saw the way Freddie clung to the girl in the navy t-shirt. She saw the way the girl instinctively wrapped her arms around him, resting her chin on his head. To Krystal, this wasn't a signal of rejection. It was a signal of *dependency*.

*She's good,* Krystal thought, assessing Paige. *Very hands-on. Probably one of those specialized aides for the severe cases.*

"That's sweet," Krystal said, her voice dripping with a condescending kind of sympathy. "She takes good care of you, huh?"

Freddie pulled back slightly. "She is essential," he said into Paige’s shirt.

"I bet," Krystal nodded. She stepped closer, lowering her voice so "the help" wouldn't hear (even though Paige was literally holding him). "But hey... does she have to be there *all* the time? I mean, surely you get some free time? When you aren't... doing therapy?"

Paige looked up. Her expression was unreadable. She tightened her grip on Freddie.

"He isn't in therapy," Paige said calmly. "He's resting."

"Right, right," Krystal waved a hand dismissively. "Resting. Look, Freddie... I'm just saying. If you ever want a break... from the routine..."

She pulled a small piece of paper from her pocket—a slip of notebook paper with a phone number written in purple gel ink. She tucked it boldly into the pocket of Freddie’s orange hoodie.

"Call me," Krystal whispered. "I bet I can show you some things that aren't in your textbook."

She straightened up, waiting for a reaction. She expected a blush, a smile, or for him to push the girl away now that a "better option" was available.

Instead, Freddie stared at the slip of paper in his pocket like it was a contaminant. He looked at Paige.

"She thinks you are an employee," Freddie whispered.

"She thinks you're a project," Paige countered.

Freddie shoved the paper deeper into his pocket to hide it, then leaned back into Paige, closing his eyes again. He assumed the interaction was over.

But Krystal didn't leave.

She stood over them, hands on her hips, her posture aggressive. She watched the way Paige resumed stroking Freddie’s hair—a gentle, rhythmic motion that seemed to melt the tension right out of his body. She watched the way Freddie settled deeper into her lap, turning his face inward toward her stomach, completely ignoring the "hot girl" standing right there.

It infuriated her.

"You know," Krystal said, her voice losing its sugary coating and becoming tight with genuine annoyance. "You're really possessive. It’s not cute. He’s a grown man, not a pet."

Paige stopped stroking Freddie’s hair. She slowly lifted her gaze. She didn't look angry anymore; she looked genuinely baffled.

She studied Krystal’s face—the heavy eyeliner, the plucked brows, the sheer determination to ignore reality. Then she looked down at Freddie.

They had the same dark, thick hair. They wore identical black-rimmed glasses. They had the same nose—a slightly upturned Avery trait inherited from their mother. They even had the same jawline.

To anyone paying attention, they looked like two versions of the same person.

"Are you blind?" Paige asked quietly.

Krystal blinked. "Excuse me?"

"Look at his face," Paige commanded, pointing a finger at Freddie. "Then look at mine. Really look."

Krystal frowned. She looked down at Freddie, who opened his eyes to peer up at her through his lenses. Then she looked at Paige.

She saw the eyes. Dark brown. Identical. She saw the nose. She saw the set of the mouth.

The realization hit her.

"Wait," Krystal said, taking a step back. "You guys look... alike."

"We're twins," Paige stated flatly. "Fraternal. Born twelve minutes apart."

Krystal froze. Her mind raced, processing the new data. *Twins.*

She looked at Freddie, lying comfortably across Paige’s thighs. She looked at Paige’s hand resting intimately on his chest. She looked at the way their bodies were pressed together on the sofa.

And then, she looked at herself. She looked at her own tight pink top, her designer jeans, the offer she had just put on the table.

She realized what had just happened. He hadn't rejected her because he had a girlfriend. He hadn't rejected her because he was busy.

He had rejected her for his *sister*.

The confusion on Krystal’s face curdled into something ugly. It was the look of a merchant who realizes someone is burning money in front of them.

"You're his *sister*?" Krystal repeated, her voice rising in disbelief.

"Yes," Paige said.

Krystal gestured wildly between herself and Paige. "So... you can't... you know. You can't *do* anything with him."

"No," Paige said, her voice dropping to zero degrees Kelvin. "We can't."

"Then why did he pick *you*?" Krystal burst out, the frustration boiling over. She pointed at Freddie. "I offered you a sure thing! I offered you my room! And you're choosing to sit here and cuddle with the one girl on campus you *can't* have?"

She shook her head, looking at Freddie like he was a broken appliance.

"That is such a waste," Krystal hissed. "You're trading sex for... what? A hug? That doesn't make any sense!"

In Krystal's world, sex was the ultimate currency. To see someone trading it for mere safety felt like an insult to her entire economy.

"It makes perfect sense," Freddie said. He sat up, sliding his hand into Paige’s. "I require stability. You offer transaction."

"It's gross!" Krystal snapped, pivoting to shame because logic had failed her. "It's creepy! Look at you two! Brothers and sisters don't touch like that. It looks like you're together!"

Paige stood up. She moved so fast that Krystal flinched.

Paige stepped between Freddie and Krystal. She didn't shout. She didn't scream. She spoke with the absolute, terrifying calm of a woman defending her home.

"You listen to me," Paige said softly. "You see 'gross' because your mind is in the gutter. You see 'creepy' because you can't imagine a man touching a woman without wanting something from her."

She took a step forward, forcing Krystal to retreat.

"He touches me because I am safe," Paige hissed. "He touches me because the world is loud and I am quiet. There is nothing 'sick' about it. The only thing 'sick' here is that you can't handle the fact that he picked a Safe Person over a cheap hookup."

"I am not cheap!" Krystal sputtered, her face flushing red. "And people are staring! It looks bad!"

"Let them stare," Freddie said.

He stood up behind Paige. He placed his hand on her shoulder—not for support this time, but for solidarity. He squeezed.

"Your definition of 'value' is flawed," Freddie stated. "You are dismissed."

Krystal looked at them—the united front, the identical glasses, the unbreakable wall of the Avery Unit. She realized she couldn't win. She couldn't shame them because they refused to be ashamed.

"Freaks," she muttered one last time, turning on her heel. "You deserve each other."

She stormed off toward the exit, her heels clicking angrily on the tile.

Paige watched her go until she pushed through the doors. Then, she let out a long breath and slumped against Freddie.

"Status?" she asked.

"Status: Validated," Freddie said. He looked down at her. "She called us freaks."

"Yeah," Paige shrugged, turning to hug him. "But she left. And we're still here."

"And we have the sofa," Freddie added.

"And we have the sofa," Paige agreed.

She sat back down. Freddie lay back down, putting his head right back in her lap. He closed his eyes.

"Resume regulation," Freddie murmured.

"Resuming," Paige whispered, running her fingers through his hair.

The noise of the student center continued around them, but in their little bubble on the sofa, the signal was clear. They were weird. They were different. But they were right.
