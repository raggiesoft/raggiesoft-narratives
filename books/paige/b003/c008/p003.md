# Part 3: The Dark Closet

## Tuesday, July 15, 2003 02:45 AM

*WHOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO-OOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOOO.*

The sound was everywhere. It wasn't just in the air; it was vibrating through the floor tiles of **Archer Hall**.

The PA system crackled, the voice distorted by static and fear. *"Tornado Warning. Seek shelter immediately. This is not a drill."*

"We have to move!" Krystal screamed, her fingers digging into Freddie’s bicep like claws.

"My sisters," Freddie gasped, trying to pull back toward the restrooms. "They are in the lavatory. I cannot leave the Unit."

"There's no time!" Krystal yelled over the roar of the wind. "The roof is coming off! We have to go now!"

She yanked him hard. Freddie stumbled, his legs heavy and unresponsive, like they were moving through wet concrete. He tried to shout for Paige, but his voice was a dry rattle.

Krystal dragged him down the hallway. It stretched out endlessly, the fluorescent lights flickering and buzzing like angry insects. He saw other students running, their faces blurred and panicked, disappearing into classrooms.

"Not there," Krystal hissed, pulling him past the open doors. "Too crowded. We need privacy. We need to be *safe*."

The roar outside grew deafening—a freight train grinding against the brick walls. The windows at the end of the hall shattered, glass exploding inward.

"Here!" Krystal shouted.

She wrenched open a heavy steel door marked **CUSTODIAL**. She shoved Freddie inside. He stumbled into the darkness, tripping over a mop bucket.

Krystal slammed the door shut.

*CLICK.*

The sound of the lock engaging was louder than the storm. The roar of the tornado muffled instantly, replaced by a heavy, suffocating silence. The air smelled of bleach and aggressive perfume.

"Safe," Krystal whispered.

A beam of light cut through the blackness. Krystal had clicked on a flashlight. She shone it right in Freddie’s face, blinding him. He threw his hands up to shield his eyes.

"Put your hands down," Krystal commanded.

She lowered the beam until it illuminated her own face. She was smiling. It wasn't a nice smile. It was the smile of a hunter who had finally cornered the prey.

"See?" she purred, stepping closer. "No sisters. No noise. Just us."

"I require... evacuation," Freddie stammered, backing up until he hit the shelves of cleaning supplies. "This environment is confined."

"It's cozy," Krystal corrected. She stepped right up to him, pressing her body against his. He could feel the heat radiating off her.

"Now is the time, Freddie," she whispered, reaching out to grab the zipper of his orange hoodie. "No one can hear us in here. No one can stop us. You're finally going to learn what you really need."

She pulled the zipper down. Her hand slid inside the hoodie, touching his chest. Her fingers were cold.

"Please," Freddie begged, freezing in terror. "Stop."

"Don't be shy," Krystal giggled, moving her face closer to his. "You're going to thank me."
