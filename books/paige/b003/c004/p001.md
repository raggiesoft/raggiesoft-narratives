# Part 1: The Primary Node

## Wednesday, June 25, 2003 09:30 AM

The silver **Horizon Avalon** idled in the driveway of 105 Pepper Street. The morning was already hot, the air shimmering above the asphalt of Pepper Plaza across the street.

Paige sat in the driver’s seat. She adjusted the rearview mirror, checking the sightlines. "Clutch in," she whispered. She shifted into Reverse. The gear stick clicked into place with mechanical precision. "We are mobile," Freddie confirmed from the passenger seat. He had his clipboard ready—a literal clipboard he had found in the Ops Room supplies. It held their insurance cards, the FERPA copies, and a list of objectives.

"Destination: **Montgomery General Internal Medicine**," Jessica announced from the back seat. "Dr. Vance. He’s the primary node. He handles my physicals, and he’s the gateway to the specialists."

Paige eased the car out of the driveway. Her clutch control was becoming muscle memory now; the car didn't even shudder. They turned onto the main road, heading toward the hospital district on the south side of Christiansburg.

## 10:00 AM Dr. Vance’s Office

The waiting room smelled of antiseptic and old magazines. It was quiet, save for the low murmur of the receptionists behind the sliding glass window. They approached the desk as a unit.

"Checking in," Jessica said. "Three appointments. Avery, Avery, and Avery." The receptionist blinked, looking at the three siblings who stood shoulder-to-shoulder. "New patients?" "I'm established," Jessica said. "They are new. Transfers from Virginia Beach."

The receptionist handed over three heavy clipboards stacked with forms. "Update yours, and fill out the new patient packets for them. Insurance cards?" Freddie handed over the cards from his clipboard. He watched her scan them. *Beep.* "Okay," she said. "Have a seat. Bring the forms up when you're done."

They sat in a row of chairs. Freddie looked at the stack of paper. *Medical History. Family History. Surgical History.* He uncapped his pen. He liked forms. Forms were binary. *Yes/No. Check Box.*

Then he reached the last page: **HIPAA Authorization & Release of Information**. *I hereby authorize this practice to release my protected health information to...*

"Hold," Freddie said. Jessica and Paige looked up from their own clipboards. "We need a policy decision," Freddie stated. "Standard protocol is privacy. My records are sealed." He looked at Jessica. "But you are the Admin. You need to know if the hardware fails." He looked at Paige. "You are the Anchor. You need to know if the chemicals are imbalanced."

"And you need to know about us," Jessica countered. "If Paige has a reaction to the shot, or if I get sick during finals, you need to be able to talk to the doctor."

"Redundancy," Freddie nodded. "Total transparency." He wrote in the blank lines on his form: *1. Jessica Avery (Sister)* *2. Paige Avery (Sister)* *3. Ellen Avery (Mother)*

He checked the box for **\[X\] ALL RECORDS**.

Paige watched him, then copied the list onto her form. Jessica did the same on her update form.

"This is the Tri-Lateral Agreement," Freddie whispered, signing his name at the bottom. "If one unit goes down, the others have the schematic."

## 10:30 AM Exam Room 2

Dr. Vance was a tall man with wire-rimmed glasses and a demeanor that was efficient but kind. He didn't blink when all three siblings crowded into the exam room. "Okay," Dr. Vance said, looking at the charts. "We’re doing intake for Freddie and Paige today?"

"Affirmative," Freddie said from the exam table. He was sitting on the crinkly paper, legs dangling. "I require a maintenance check and referrals."

"Let's start with the history," Dr. Vance said. "I see Cerebral Palsy and ADHD on the transfer file." "Spastic Diplegia," Freddie corrected. "Mild. Mostly affects the lower extremities. I require Baclofen for the spasticity." "And the ADHD?" "Ritalin," Freddie said. "Standard dosage. It maintains focus."

Dr. Vance nodded, making notes. Then he looked at Jessica, then back to Freddie. "Anything else?"

Freddie took a breath. He looked at Paige. She gave him a small nod. *Use the manual.* "There is a suspected discrepancy in the operating system," Freddie stated clearly. "Suspected?" Dr. Vance asked. "Autism," Freddie said. "My sisters and I have reviewed the diagnostic criteria. My sensory processing, social deficits, and reliance on routine match the profile. However, I lack a formal diagnosis code."

Dr. Vance stopped writing. He looked at Freddie—really looked at him. He saw the lack of eye contact, the slight rocking, the precise, mechanical speech patterns. He looked at Jessica and Paige, who were standing guard like Secret Service agents. "I see," Dr. Vance said softly. "Well, you're in the right place. We can get that looked at."

"We have a lead," Jessica interjected. "The Dean of Students recommended **Dr. Aris Thorne** in Blacksburg. But we need a referral for insurance." "Dr. Thorne is excellent," Dr. Vance agreed. "I'll write the referral today. 'Evaluation for Autism Spectrum Disorder.' That should get you in the door."

"Thank you," Freddie said.

"Now," Dr. Vance turned to Paige. "And for you?" Paige sat up straighter. "I need a referral too. To **Dr. Halloway** in Riner." "OB/GYN?" Dr. Vance asked. "Yes," Paige said. "I want to start the **Cycle-Stop** injection. Jessica is on it. We... we live in close quarters. We want to stabilize the variables."

Dr. Vance didn't ask why siblings living together needed birth control. He was a doctor; he cared about health, not living arrangements. "Dr. Halloway is great," he said. "I'll put that referral in as well. Anything else?"

"Just maintenance," Jessica said. "We want to make sure everyone is running at 100% before the semester starts."

Dr. Vance finished his notes. "You guys have a good system here," he observed, tearing off the prescription slips for the referrals. He handed them to Freddie. "You're the keeper of the papers?" "I am the Archivist," Freddie said, taking them.

## 11:15 AM The Parking Lot

They walked back to the Avalon. The sun was high overhead. Freddie held the referrals in his hand. *Referral: Dr. Aris Thorne (Psychiatry/Neurology).* *Referral: Dr. Halloway (Gynecology).*

He looked at the papers. They weren't just slips of paper. They were patch notes. They were upgrades waiting to be installed. "We have the keys," Freddie said. "We have the keys," Paige agreed, unlocking the car. "Now let's go get some lunch. I'm starving."

"Route 8 next?" Jessica asked. "Negative," Freddie said, checking the schedule in his head. "Today is Wednesday. We need to call Dr. Halloway first. But the bus run... that is a tactical necessity. We need to verify the route."

"Next week," Jessica promised. "Next week, we ride to Riner."
