# Part 6: The Midnight Threshold

## Friday, November 14, 2008 — 08:30 AM WWRC Dining Hall

It was the final breakfast. The "Exit Protocol."

Harper sat at her usual defensive table near the door, her noise-canceling headphones around her neck. She wasn't eating. She was watching the clock on the wall.

08:32 AM. The family would be here in four and a half hours.

Across the room, the source of the static was holding court.

Travis was sitting on a table—not a chair, the table—surrounded by his sycophants. He was wearing a new t-shirt that said LEGALLY LETHAL.

He was louder than usual today. He was vibrating with a specific, arrogant energy.

"Tomorrow," Travis shouted, pointing a fork at a terrified freshman. "Tomorrow is the day, little man. Midnight tonight. The clock strikes twelve, and I am a free agent."

"Happy birthday, Travis," one of the girls simpered.

"It ain't just a birthday," Travis sneered, looking around the room until his eyes locked on Harper in the corner. "It’s a license. I turn eighteen tomorrow. That means I’m an adult. I can do what I want. I can sign myself out. I can go where I want."

He grinned at Harper. It was a wolfish, hungry grin.

"No more kiddie rules," Travis said, his voice dropping just enough to be terrifying. "Tomorrow, I’m a grown man. And grown men take what they want."

Harper gripped her tray. The math hit her like a physical blow.

Today is Friday the 14th. Tomorrow is Saturday the 15th. He turns 18 tomorrow.

She realized with a jolt of cold terror that she was escaping just in time. If she stayed for the weekend... if she was still here when he became a legal adult... the dynamic would shift. He wouldn't just be a bully. He would be a man targeting a child.

He thinks turning 18 gives him permission, Harper realized. He doesn't know it just gives him a prison sentence.

She stood up. She dumped her tray. She rolled out of the cafeteria without looking back.

Four hours, she told herself. Just four hours until the Unit gets here.
