# Part 5: The Countdown

## Thursday, November 13, 2008 — 21:00 PM Suite 200A

The week had been a gauntlet, but the "Freddie Protocol" had worked. Harper had worn her headphones like armor. She had eaten quickly. She had hidden in the library during free time.

Now, it was the final night.

Harper sat on her bed. Her suitcase was already packed. Every shirt was folded. Every sock was matched.

Her roommate, Kayla, was crying on the other bed.

"He isn't calling me back!" Kayla wailed, throwing her phone at the pillow. "He's probably with Jessica!"

Harper looked at Kayla. She ran a diagnostic.

Variable: Boyfriend. Status: Unreliable. Reaction: Hysteria.

Harper stood up. She walked over to Kayla’s bed. She didn't offer a hug (she reserved those for the Unit). She offered data.

"Kayla," Harper said, her voice steady.

Kayla looked up, mascara running. "What?"

"We leave in fourteen hours," Harper stated. "If he isn't calling you, he is a bad variable. You are wasting processing power on a glitch."

Kayla blinked. "A... glitch?"

"You passed your evaluation," Harper reminded her. "You got a recommendation for the Culinary program. That is real. The boy is just static."

Kayla sniffled. She wiped her eyes. "Static?"

"Noise," Harper said. "Turn it off."

She handed Kayla a tissue.

"We're going home tomorrow," Harper said. "Focus on the exit strategy."

Kayla took the tissue. She blew her nose. "You're weird, Harper."

"I know," Harper smiled, thinking of the Mega-Bed waiting for her. "I'm a Avery."

She walked back to her desk. She opened her notebook.

She drew a large square. She wrote a number inside it.

14 HOURS.

"System standby," Harper whispered. "Prepare for extraction."
