# Part 2: The Debrief

## Friday, November 14, 2008 — 13:45 PM Interstate 81 South

The car was quiet. The hum of the tires on the highway was the only sound for twenty miles.

Finally, Harper spoke.

"It was a war zone," she said.

"The vocational assessment?" Freddie asked. "Did the soldering fail?"

"No," Harper shook her head. "The work was fine. I liked the electronics. I liked the drafting. It was the people. The downtime."

She looked at her siblings.

"There was a fight," Harper said. "A big one. On Tuesday."

She described the scene in the PERT lounge. The argument over the TV. The metal chair hitting the wall.

"Violence," Freddie noted, flinching. "Uncontrolled kinetic energy."

"But it wasn't just the fight," Harper whispered. She hugged her arms to her chest. "It was the guy who started it. Travis."

Jessica glanced in the rearview mirror. "Did he hurt you?"

"He tried," Harper said, her voice trembling. "He cornered me. Before the fight started. He grabbed my chair. He... he wouldn't let me leave."

Freddie’s hand tightened on her knee. "He breached your perimeter?"

"He's a Senior," Harper said. "He's huge. And he's mean."

She looked out the window at the passing trees.

"He turns eighteen tomorrow," she added softly. "November 15th."

The car went silent.

"He was bragging about it at breakfast," Harper continued, tears leaking out of her eyes. "He said that at midnight tonight, he becomes a 'free agent.' He said grown men can take what they want. He looked right at me when he said it."

Freddie processed the data. His face hardened behind his glasses.

"He is operating on a false algorithm," Freddie stated coldly. "He believes age grants immunity. In reality, age grants liability."

"Liability?" Harper asked, wiping her nose.

"If he touches you today," Freddie explained, his voice devoid of emotion but heavy with threat, "it is a juvenile offense. A misdemeanor. He gets counseling."

He adjusted his glasses.

"If he touches you tomorrow... after 00:00 hours... he is an adult male assaulting a minor female. That is a felony. That is the registry. That is prison."

"He doesn't know that," Harper whispered. "He thinks he's Romeo. He thinks I'm playing hard to get."

"He is not Romeo," Jessica hissed from the front seat, gripping the steering wheel. "He is a target for Mr. Sterling. If he ever comes near you again, we bury him."

"You got out just in time, Harp," Paige said, rubbing Harper's back. "You beat the clock. He wakes up tomorrow a man, and his victim is gone."

"I know," Harper exhaled. "That's why I had to leave. I couldn't stay for the weekend."

She looked at Freddie.

"I just want to be boring," Harper said. "I want to sit in the basement and sort laundry. I want to watch The Local. I don't want to be hunted."

"Boring is underrated," Freddie said. "Boring is stable."

He squeezed her knee.

"You are a Avery," Freddie stated. "We do not engage in chaotic social friction. We withdraw to the bunker."

"Exactly," Harper nodded. "Take me to the bunker."
