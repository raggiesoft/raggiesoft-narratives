# Part 2: Barnett Hall

## Sunday, November 9, 2008 — 15:15 PM Woodrow Wilson Rehabilitation Center

The drive from the restaurant to the campus was short. They took Exit 222, winding past the hospital and up the hill to the rehabilitation center.

They pulled up to Barnett Hall. It was a large, brick dormitory building, functional and institutional, but softened by the autumn leaves blowing across the lawn.

"Target acquired," Freddie whispered from the back seat. "Dormitory Office."

They unloaded the car. Harper transferred into her purple chair. She gripped her bag in her lap.

"Let's go," Jessica said.

They walked into the lobby. The air smelled of floor wax and industrial cleaner. A sign on the wall pointed to the Dormitory Office.

Inside, the office was buzzing with activity. Staff members in polo shirts were checking names off lists.

"Avery, Harper," Jessica announced at the desk.

The housing coordinator looked up. "Ah, yes. PERT Program. You're in the West Wing. All-female suite."

She handed Harper a key card and a packet of rules.

"You are in Suite 200," the coordinator explained. "It’s a three-room suite. Rooms A, B, and C. You are in Room A. There are two students per room, so you'll have one direct roommate, and four suitemates. All high school age."

"Security check," Freddie asked. "Are the adult clients isolated?"

"Strictly," the coordinator nodded to Freddie. "The adult vocational students are in a different wing. The PERT students have their own staff and their own curfew. It's a controlled environment."

"Acceptable," Freddie noted.

"We have an escort ready to take you up," the coordinator said, signaling a staff member—a young woman named Sarah (not Avery) with a lanyard.

"Right this way," the escort said. "Elevators are down the hall."
