# Part 3: Suite 200

## 15:30 PM

They rode the elevator to the second floor. The escort led them down a wide hallway to a door marked 200.

She swiped a master key. The door clicked open.

They stepped into a small common area. It had standard dorm furniture—a vinyl couch, a sturdy table. Three doors branched off it.

"Room A," the escort pointed to the left.

Harper rolled in.

The room was simple. Two twin beds. Two desks. Two wardrobes. The floor was tiled linoleum—easy to roll on.

"Bed 1 is yours," the escort said. "Your roommate, Kayla, hasn't arrived yet."

"Okay," Jessica said, dropping the duffel bags. "Setup protocol. We have thirty minutes before we have to clear out."

The Avery Unit sprang into action.

Jessica unpacked the clothes, hanging them in the wardrobe with military precision. Paige made the bed. She put on the sheets Harper had brought from home—flannel, purple, smelling of the laundry detergent from Pepper Street. She arranged the pillows to create a "nest." Freddie rolled around the room, checking the outlets and the clearance.

"Desk height is adjustable," Freddie noted. "Good ergonomics. The turning radius between the beds is tight, but sufficient."

He looked at the bathroom attached to the suite.

"Shared facility," Freddie observed. "You must coordinate shower times with five other females. I suggest a schedule."

"I'll make a chart," Harper muttered, putting her laptop on the desk.

They set up her station. Laptop. Headphones. A picture frame with the photo Aunt Linda had taken on the sofa—the "Heap."

Within twenty minutes, the sterile dorm room looked like a forward operating base.
