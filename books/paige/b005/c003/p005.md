# Part 6: The Supply Line

## Saturday, July 21, 2007 — 14:30 PM 105 Pepper Street SE, Christiansburg, VA

The garlic bread was decimated. The spaghetti bowl was empty. The "Mess Hall" protocol had been a resounding success.

Now, the table was cleared, and Freddie had deployed the maps.

He smoothed out a laminated chart on the dining table: The New River Valley Transit Schematic (2007 Edition).

"Mission planning," Freddie announced, looking at Harper. "If you are to execute a weekly visitation schedule, we must establish the logistics of the supply line. The 'Rover-to-Base' link."

Harper rolled her chair closer, her chin resting on her hands. "Lay it on me, Captain. How do I get here without Mom knowing?"

"Public transit," Freddie stated. "It is the only secure channel."

He pointed to a spot on the map in Radford.

"Origin Point: Highland Village Apartments. Aunt Linda's sector."

He traced a line with his finger to a large blue dot near the university.

"Waypoint Alpha: McConnell Transit Center. The Hub. Located directly across from Valley University on Main Street."

He traced a thick red line moving east along US-11.

"The Artery: Blue Ridge Transit (BRT) Route 3. The Connector. It runs every thirty minutes."

He tapped a spot in downtown Christiansburg.

"Destination: Pepper Plaza. Located at 9 Roanoke Street. It is the transit depot for the county seat."

He pointed out the window to the parking lot diagonally across the street.

"That is Pepper Plaza. You disembark there. You cross the street. You are at Base."

"It's that close?" Harper asked, looking out the window. "I can see the bus stop from here."

"Visual contact confirmed," Freddie nodded. "However, the tariff structure is complex. We are currently in the 'Summer Window'."

He pulled up a notepad and began writing the fee structure.

## Phase 1: Summer Protocol (Now - August 31, 2007)

"Until school starts," Freddie explained, "you are a civilian passenger. You must pay cash."

- **Leg 1 (Radford Transit):** You board the bus near your apartment. Cost: \$0.50. Exact change required. No transfers issued.

- **Leg 2 (Blue Ridge Transit Route 3):** You board at McConnell Transit Center. Cost: \$1.00. You must request a Transfer Slip upon boarding if you need to switch to another BRT bus, though for this route, it is a direct shot.

"Total cost, one way: \$1.50," Harper calculated. "Round trip: Three bucks."

"Affirmative," Freddie said. "Do you have funding?"

"I have an allowance," Harper grinned at Aunt Linda.

"She does," Linda confirmed from the kitchen, where she was helping Jessica dry dishes. "But for the first few runs? I'm riding with you. I need to make sure those bus drivers know how to work the wheelchair lifts. I don't want you stranded on Route 11."

"Agreed," Freddie said. "The lift mechanism can be temperamental. Supervision is required for the beta test."

## Phase 2: The Student Protocol (September 2007 - Forward)

Freddie drew a line on the paper.

"However," Freddie said, his eyes gleaming behind his glasses. "Once the academic cycle begins, the rules change. You will be a freshman at Radford High School."

"Ugh, don't remind me," Harper groaned.

"No," Freddie corrected. "This is a tactical advantage. Your Student ID is a 'Skeleton Key'."

He tapped the map.

"Starting in September, showing a valid Radford High School ID grants 100% Fare Exemption on both Radford Transit and Blue Ridge Transit."

Harper sat up straight. "Free rides?"

"Unlimited," Freddie confirmed. "You flash the badge. You board the bus. No cash. No friction. You can transit between Radford and Christiansburg as often as the schedule allows."

"That is power," Harper whispered. "I can come here every day after school."

"Negative," Linda called out. "Homework first. Weekends only."

"Fine," Harper rolled her eyes. "Free rides on weekends."

## The Twins Protocol (Restricted)

Amanda and Elara crowded around the map.

"What about us?" Elara asked. "Do we get free rides?"

Freddie looked at the twins. He looked at Aunt Linda.

"You are twelve," Freddie said gently. "Middle School Protocol is... restrictive."

"You guys are cargo," Harper teased. "You don't have clearance."

"Harper is correct," Linda said, walking over and putting her hands on the twins' shoulders. "You two are too young to ride the inter-city bus alone. Not yet. When you hit High School? You get the badge. Until then, if you want to visit the Base, you ride with me in the van, or you ride the bus with Harper and an adult."

Amanda looked at the map. She traced the red line of Route 3. She looked at Freddie.

"Bus," Amanda hummed.

"One day," Freddie promised her. "Upgrade pending."

## Phase 3: The Future (January 2008 Forecast)

"There is one more variable," Freddie added, looking at his notes. "Intel suggests a system update in January."

"What update?" Jessica asked, leaning against the doorframe.

"Integration," Freddie said. "Regional Passes. Currently, the agencies are siloed. But in 2008, they are launching a unified fare card. One pass for Radford, Blue Ridge, Bluewater, and Roanoke Valley. Total freedom of movement across the entire New River Valley."

"The Grid expands," Paige smiled.

"Exactly," Freddie said. "But for now... we focus on Route 3."

He handed the map to Harper.

"Study the timetable," Freddie ordered. "Memorize the stops. Next Saturday, we execute the first run."

Harper took the map. She folded it carefully and tucked it into the side pocket of her wheelchair.

"Mission accepted," Harper said. "See you next Saturday, Captain."

"See you next Saturday, Satellite," Freddie replied.

The supply line was established. The Base was no longer an island; it was a Hub.
