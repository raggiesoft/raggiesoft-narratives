# Part 3: The Mega-Bed

## Monday, June 25, 2007 — 11:15 AM 105 Pepper Street SE, Christiansburg, VA

The "Thru-Floor Lift" was found. The TFL-3000 was in the virtual shopping cart. The method of descent was secured.

Now, they had to secure the destination.

"Phase Two," Jessica announced, opening a new browser tab. "The Landing Pad. We need a bed for the basement that actually fits five adults without someone waking up with a foot in their mouth."

"The King is 76 inches wide," Freddie recited, staring at the ceiling as he ran the numbers. "Divided by five occupants, that creates a lateral allotment of 15.2 inches per person."

"That is... coffin width," Sarah noted with a shudder. "It restricts circulation. I woke up with a numb arm."

"I woke up on the floor," Jenna added, though she didn't look upset about it.

"We need width," Paige said, sitting on the floor and leaning against *The Rover*. "But not *too* much width. Freddie likes the squeeze."

Freddie nodded. It was a delicate variable.

He thought about the night before—the weight of Jenna on his legs, the pressure of Sarah’s grip on his arm, the warmth of Paige and Jessica flanking him. The sensation of being *buried* in his sisters was the most secure he had ever felt.

"I require... contact," Freddie clarified. "I do not want isolation. I want the tangle. But I require the option to rotate my spine without elbowing a ribcage."

"So, bigger than a King," Jessica summarized. "But we have to get it down those stairs."

She typed: biggest mattress size

The search results populated.

**Alaskan King:** 108" x 108". "Whoa," Jenna gasped. "Nine feet? That’s an arena!"

Jessica clicked the price tag. **\$7,500 (Mattress Only). Custom Freight Shipping Required.**

"Negative," Sarah said instantly. "That is the price of a used car. And we cannot get a nine-foot mattress down those narrow stairs. It physically won't fit through the door. It is a logistical impossibility."

"Agreed," Freddie said. "We need a modular solution."

"Modular," Jessica mused. "Like... building blocks."

She opened a new tab. combine mattresses to make bigger bed

The results were full of "family bed" forums and DIY hacks.

- **Option A:** Two Full Beds side-by-side. (54" + 54" = 108"). Same width as an Alaskan King.

- **Option B:** King + Twin XL. (76" + 38" = 114").

- **Option C:** Two Queen Beds. (60" + 60" = 120").

"Two Queens," Freddie calculated instantly. "Total width: 10 feet. Depth: 80 inches. That provides 24 inches of lateral space per person."

"That’s double what we have now," Paige noted. "That’s luxury. We could all lay flat on our backs and barely touch."

"But," Sarah interrupted, her brow furrowing as she visualized the schematic. "The seam. The crack between the mattresses. If you sleep in the middle—which is Freddie's position—you fall into the void. It collects debris. It pinches. It is unsanitary."

"There is a patch for that," Jessica said, scrolling down. She pointed to a product image: a T-shaped piece of foam called a **Bed Bridge**.

"You put this wedge in the crack," Jessica explained. "Then you use this..." She pointed to a heavy-duty nylon strap system. "...a connector belt. It wraps around the perimeter of both mattresses and ratchets tight so they don't slide apart."

"Structural integrity," Freddie noted. "But the texture... the bridge creates a bump."

"Layering," Jessica said. "We put the bridge in. We strap the beds together. And then..."

She clicked on a listing for a **Memory Foam Topper**.

"We buy two massive Queen-sized memory foam toppers. We lay them over the mattresses *and* the bridge. It unifies the surface. You won't even feel the seam."

Freddie closed his eyes. He pictured it.

Two Queen beds locked together in the center of the basement. A ten-foot-wide island of soft foam. No edges to fall off of. No fighting for covers.

He could lie in the center. Paige could be on his left, Jessica on his right. Sarah and Jenna could take the flanks, or they could all pile in the middle. The extra space meant they *could* spread out if it was hot, but the Bed Bridge meant they could still roll to the center and form the "Heap" without a gap stopping them.

"Two Queens," Freddie decided. "The geometry is sound. The modularity allows for transport down the narrow stairs."

"And the frame?" Jenna asked. "We can't just put them on the concrete. Spiders. Moisture."

Freddie looked at the specs for the *Access-Pro TFL* they had just found. He looked at his sisters.

"We are Forgers," Freddie stated, his voice firm. "We do not buy a frame. We build a platform."

"Wood?" Jessica asked.

"Lumber," Freddie confirmed. "2x4s and plywood. We build a riser in the basement. We carpet it. We drop the mattresses in. A custom install."

"I can build that," Jenna said, flexing her fingers. "I built stage sets in drama club. Give me a drill and a saw."

"Then it is settled," Jessica announced, closing the laptop. "Project: Mega-Bed is a go."

She looked at Freddie.

"We get the lift. We get the bed. And then... we never have to leave the pile."

"Never," Freddie whispered. "Systems... optimal."
