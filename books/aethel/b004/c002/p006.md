# Chapter 6: The Starlight Check-In

## Part 1: The Shadow of the Archivist

Theme: trauma-healing

Location: The Garden Bath

POV: Kaelan

=

The water in the stone tub was calm, reflecting the Milky Way—a galaxy Kaelan had hand-crafted billions of years ago when he was just Solas, a being of pure fusion.

But tonight, looking at the stars didn't bring him peace. It brought him a name.

*High Archivist Malakor.*

Kaelan sat up in the water, the steam curling around his chest. He turned his head and spat onto the grass outside the tub.

"Filth," he hissed.

Kaela, who was floating on her back with her eyes closed, didn't flinch. She just drifted closer until her arm brushed his.

"You're thinking about him," she said softly. "The Traitor."

"I'm thinking about the Wedge," Kaelan corrected. His voice was dark, vibrating with a heat that threatened to boil the bathwater. "I'm thinking about how he drove a spike of ice into my heart the day Elara died."

He looked at his hands—hands that had once held the tools of creation, but for two years, from age sixteen to eighteen, had refused to hold his own sister.

"Two years, Kaela," Kaelan rasped. "Two years of flinching away from you. Two years of acting like a moody, broken teenager because *he* thought love was a weakness to be excised."

He remembered those years in the cottage. He remembered Kaela crying silently in the bed they shared, reaching out for him, and the Wedge forcing him to roll away, to turn his back, to feel nothing but cold irritation.

Malakor the High Sage was dead. Lord Malakor was dead. But High Archivist Malakor—the divine entity who had betrayed their parents, Lyra and Aelion, and destroyed the Silver Temple—was the one who had stolen those hugs.

"He's dead, Sun," Kaela whispered, standing up in the water. She moved to the bench beside him. She wrapped her wet arms around his neck, pulling his head down to her shoulder. "His soul is scattered in the void. He can't hurt us."

Kaelan let out a shuddering breath. He wrapped his arms around her waist, pulling her tight. He buried his face in her wet hair.

"I still owe you," he mumbled into her neck. "I owe you two years of holding."

"You've been paying it back since we turned nineteen," Kaela smiled, stroking the back of his head. "With interest."

"Compound interest," Kaelan corrected fiercely, squeezing her tighter. "I am never letting go again. Not for a Wedge. Not for a King. Not for a God."

He held her there in the steam, letting the contact burn away the memory of the Archivist's cold. They were twenty-one now. They were survivors. They were the last of the Silver Temple, and they were finally warm.

## Part 2: The Connection

Theme: divine-contact

Location: The Garden

POV: Kaela

=

"We should check in," Kaela said after a long silence.

Kaelan pulled back slightly to look at her. "With who? The Council? Horg is probably asleep."

"With the Others," Kaela said, tilting her head toward the sky. "The Silver Temple is gone. Mom and Dad are gone. But the pantheon isn't empty. There are others out there. Survivors. Watchers."

"They haven't spoken to us since we were infants," Kaelan noted.

"Because we were hiding," Kaela reasoned. "We were playing human. But we just flared our power to fix the Core. We just blinded four pilgrims with divine light. They know we're active, Sun. If we don't say hello, they might think we're going down Malakor's path."

Kaelan sighed. He looked up at the stars.

"Fine," he agreed. "But we do it from here. I'm not astral projecting to the Ether. The water is too nice."

"Agreed."

They didn't need a temple. They didn't need an altar. They were the Solas and the Lunara. They *were* the altar.

They sat facing each other on the stone benches. They held hands under the water.

"Open the frequency," Kaela whispered.

She closed her eyes. She dropped the mental shields she kept up to pass as a human woman. She let her consciousness expand. She felt the tides of the oceans. She felt the pull of the moon.

Kaelan did the same. He felt the magma in the mantle. He felt the solar wind hitting the atmosphere.

They projected a thought, a harmonic signal beamed straight into the fabric of the universe:

*We are here. We are Lyra and Aelion's children. We are standing.*

The response was instantaneous.

It didn't come as a voice. It came as a shift in the starlight. The air in the garden shimmered. The reality of the physical world peeled back slightly, overlaying the garden with the vast, shimmering darkness of the Astral Plane.

Three lights appeared above the garden wall. Not physical lights, but presences. Old, vast, and powerful.

## Part 3: The Verdict of the Remnant

Theme: divine-approval

Location: The Garden / The Astral Plane

POV: Kaelan

=

Kaelan didn't bow. He stayed seated in his bath.

The three lights coalesced into vague forms—the Remnant of the Old Pantheon.

The first felt like stone and roots—Thalor, the World-Shaper. The second felt like wind and currents—Aria, the Breath. The third felt like pure mathematics and history—Oren, the Keeper.

*Solas,* a voice echoed in Kaelan's mind. It was Oren. *Lunara.*

"We go by Kaelan and Kaela now," Kaelan said out loud. "We're local."

*We see you,* Thalor’s presence rumbled, vibrating the water in the tub. *We felt the Core stabilize. You stitched the wound the Archivist left. You saved the sphere.*

"We live here," Kaela said simply. "We fixed our roof."

*You have done more than that,* Aria whispered, a breeze rustling the mint leaves. *You have survived the Wedge. The Archivist sought to make you cold, Solas. He sought to turn you into a weapon like himself. But you are warm.*

Kaelan squeezed Kaela's hand. "I had help."

*We were worried,* Oren admitted. *When the Silver Temple fell, when Lyra and Aelion were murdered... we feared their children would grow up vengeful. We feared you would become tyrants to the mortals.*

The lights seemed to scan the garden. They looked at the fortified cottage. They looked at the "Peep" hole in the gate. They looked at the twins, naked and vulnerable, yet terrifyingly powerful.

*But you are not tyrants,* Thalor noted with what felt like amusement. *You are... neighbors?*

"We are citizens," Kaelan corrected. "We have a Council. We vote."

*You refused the crown,* Oren observed. *Twice. You rejected the worship of the pilgrims. You punished the voyeurs not with death, but with light.*

"They violated our privacy," Kaelan muttered. "They got off easy."

*It pleases us,* Aria sang. *The Divine Realms are shattered. The era of Temples is over. Perhaps... perhaps the era of the Cottage is better.*

The three lights pulsed in unison. It was a feeling of pride. It was the feeling of distant relatives realizing the kids turned out okay.

*Do you seek to return?* Oren asked. *There are fragments of the Silver Realm remaining. You could rebuild. You could rule the stars as you were born to do.*

Kaelan looked at Kaela.

Kaela looked at the cottage. She looked at the chimney where the smoke from their hearth curled up. She looked at the root cellar door where the potatoes were sleeping.

"No," Kaela said. "We're good."

"We have a garden here," Kaelan added. "And the bakery makes really good cinnamon buns on Freeday. Does the Silver Realm have cinnamon buns?"

*It does not,* Oren conceded. *It mostly has dust and echoes.*

"Then we stay," Kaelan said firmly. "We are the Guardians of Sunstead. That is title enough."

*So be it,* Thalor rumbled. *Live your lives, Little Scions. Hug your sister, Solas. Pay your debt. The stars are watching, and for the first time in an eon... they are smiling.*

The presence faded. The Astral Plane receded. The garden returned to normal. The crickets started chirping again.

## Part 4: Regular Townsfolk

Theme: sunstead-peace

Location: The Garden Bath

POV: Kaela

=

The silence that followed was heavy, but good.

"Well," Kaela said, leaning back against the stone rim. "That went well. They didn't try to smite us."

"They sounded relieved," Kaelan noted. "I think they were afraid we were going to ask for money."

He splashed a little water on his face.

"They're gone. The Archivist is gone. The Temple is gone."

He looked at her. His golden eyes were soft, the anger about the Wedge finally cooling into something manageable.

"It's just us, Moon. Just us and the Council."

"And Horg," Kaela added. "Don't forget Horg."

"Never."

Kaelan stood up. He offered her his hand.

"Come on. The water's getting cold, and I'm starving. I think there's some leftover stew in the pot."

Kaela took his hand. She let him pull her up.

They stepped out of the bath, two ancient gods in human skin, dripping wet on the grass of their backyard. They dried each other off with the soft linen towels, taking their time, enjoying the friction, enjoying the fact that they could touch without fear.

They walked back into the cottage.

The Ward of Intent on the door felt them coming. It felt their exhaustion, their hunger, and their overwhelming love for each other.

The latch clicked open before they even touched it.

"Show off," Kaelan muttered to the door.

They went inside and locked the world out, ready to sleep in their shared bed, just two regular townsfolk who happened to have the blessing of the stars.
