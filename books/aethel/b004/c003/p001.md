# Prologue: The Silence Between Stars

Theme: cosmic-horror

Location: The Void Beyond the Rim

POV: The Unseen

=

The universe was too loud.

It clicked. It whirred. It spun. Atoms vibrated. Planets orbited. Hearts beat. It was a cacophony of motion that disgusted Him.

The Unseen sat on a throne made of dead starlight, located in the pockets of absolute zero between the galaxies. He was not a man, though He could wear the shape of one. He was the Embodiment of Stasis.

He looked at the small, annoying blue sphere called Earth.

"Malakor failed," The Unseen whispered. His voice was not a sound; it was the absence of sound, like a vacuum sucking the air out of a room. "He tried to break the gear physically. Crude."

He shifted his gaze to the Voidlands.

"Seraphina failed," He noted. "She tried to trap the Light. But the Light burned her."

He zoomed his vision in. Past the clouds. Past the continents. Down to a dusty ridge in a town called Sunstead.

He saw the cottage. He saw the stone walls. He saw the shimmering heat of the traps. And inside, sleeping in a tangled knot of limbs and warmth, he saw the Anomalies.

Solas and Lunara. The Children of the Temple.

They were glowing. Even in sleep, they radiated energy. They were warming the sheets. They were pulling the tides. They were keeping the core spinning.

They were the battery that kept this noisy little planet alive.

"You exist," The Unseen mused, "because I allowed your parents to believe they were safe. I allowed Malakor to kill them, but I missed the seedlings."

He raised a hand. It was pale, translucent, and cold enough to freeze time.

"I will not send another servant," The Unseen decided. "Servants are noisy. Servants have egos."

He pointed a finger at the twin suns orbiting the planet.

"If I cannot break the machine from the inside... I will simply unplug it."

He began to chant. It was a spell of dampening. A spell of cosmic mute.

He didn't target the Earth. He targeted the Sky.
