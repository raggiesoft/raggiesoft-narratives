# Chapter 2: The Ribcage of the Gods

## Part 1: The Ossuary of Stars

Theme: space-gloom

Location: The Outer Ribcage (The Approaches)

POV: Kaela

=

The Celestial Keel didn't just sail; it drifted like a speck of dust into the mouth of a giant.

They were passing between the first two ribs of the Star-Eater.

From a distance, the ribs had looked like fossilized bone. Up close, Kaela realized they were something far more complex. They were made of a calcified, organic metal—white as marble, pitted with the impacts of meteors, and vast enough that the Keel could have flown a thousand loops around a single bone without touching the surface.

"It's not a skeleton," Kaelan whispered, his face pressed against the glass canopy. "It's a chassis."

"It's both," Kaela said, her hands dancing over the navigational controls. "It's a biomechanical shell. It grew this to protect the Core."

She looked ahead. The interior of the ribcage was a vast, hollow cathedral of space, lit by the pulsating violet light of the entity at the center—a star trapped in a web of dark matter and silver chains.

The Star-Eater.

It wasn't asleep. It was idling.

"PROXIMITY ALERT," the ship's computer chimed. "SECURITY GRID DETECTED."

A wall of red laser-light materialized across the gap between the ribs. It was a net tight enough to slice an atom in half.

"Seraphina went through this?" Kaelan asked, tensing.

"She broke through it," Kaela said, pointing to a jagged, torn section of the grid miles to their port side. "She burned a hole with the Void. But we don't need to break in."

She reached out with the Circuit. She pushed her mind into the security grid. She didn't attack it; she introduced herself.

Identify, she projected. Scion Unit Alpha. Scion Unit Beta.

The red grid flickered. A massive, scanning eye opened on the surface of the nearest rib. It was a lens of blue crystal the size of a lake.

It swept over the Celestial Keel.

Kaelan flinched instinctively, his hand going to his sword. "It feels heavy. Like it's weighing my soul."

"It's weighing your clearance," Kaela corrected.

"BIOSIGNATURE CONFIRMED," a voice boomed—not from the ship, but from the bones themselves, vibrating the hull of the boat. "LINEAGE: ZENITH. WELCOME, MAINTENANCE CREW."

The red grid turned a soothing, welcoming green. It parted, creating a wide, safe tunnel for the boat.

"Maintenance crew," Kaelan muttered, shaking his head. "We're basically cosmic janitors."

"Janitors with the keys to the universe," Kaela reminded him. "Take us in."

They drifted past the threshold. Inside the ribcage, the physics changed. There was gravity here—artificial and steady. Atmosphere clung to the bones in thick, misty layers.

And there was a voice.

The Star-Eater wasn't just a machine; it was semi-sentient. And it was bored.

"DIAGNOSTIC REPORT," the entity rumbled, its voice echoing in their minds. "SYSTEM STATUS: OPTIMAL. CORE TEMPERATURE: NOMINAL. WHY ARE YOU HERE, SCIONS? NO SCHEDULED MAINTENANCE FOR 4,000 CYCLES."

Kaela stepped up to the glass. "We aren't here to fix the engine," she projected back to the god. "We're here to report a pest infestation."

She visualized Seraphina. She projected the image of the corrupted Queen tearing through the outer net.

"PROCESSING..." the entity hummed. The violet light at the core pulsed angrily. "THREAT IDENTIFIED. DESIGNATION: VIRUS. CORRUPTION LEVEL: CRITICAL."

"She wants to wake you up," Kaelan added. "She wants to use you to eat the suns."

"INEFFICIENT," the Star-Eater dismissed. "I AM IN SLEEP MODE. AWAKENING PROTOCOLS REQUIRE UNANIMOUS VOTE OF THE HIGH COUNCIL. THE VIRUS IS UNAUTHORIZED."

"She doesn't care about authorization," Kaela said. "She has an army. She's heading for the brain stem."

"DEFENSIVE SYSTEMS COMPROMISED BY VIRAL MAGIC," the entity admitted, sounding almost embarrassed. "INTERNAL PARASITES DETECTED IN SECTOR 7. REQUESTING MANUAL DEBUGGING."

"That's us," Kaelan grinned, cracking his knuckles. "We're the debuggers."

"ACCESS GRANTED TO INTERIOR," the Star-Eater boomed. "REMOVE THE PARASITE. DO NOT DAMAGE THE UPHOLSTERY."

A pathway lit up in the dark—a series of floating rune-stones leading deeper into the skeletal structure, toward a massive, suspended fortress that served as the entity's control center.

"Did a god just tell me not to scratch the furniture?" Kaelan asked.

"It likes its nap," Kaela smiled. "And it hates Seraphina."

She pointed to the fortress ahead. The Obsidian Queen was docked there, looking like a splinter in a giant's thumb.

"She's parked at the brain stem," Kaela said. "She's trying to hack the mind directly."

"Then let's go pull the plug," Kaelan said.

He pushed the tiller. The Celestial Keel shot forward, authorized and welcomed by the very monster the villain was trying to tame. They weren't sneaking in; they were being invited to take out the trash.

## Part 2: The Golden Exodus

Theme: space-gloom

Location: The Inner Ribcage (Approaching the Brain Stem)

POV: Kaela

=

The fortress suspended in the center of the ribcage—the Brain Stem—was a terrifying fusion of biology and architecture. It was a massive, pulsating node of black crystal and silver piping, where the Star-Eater's nervous system connected to the physical controls.

Docked against it was the Obsidian Queen, looking like a tick burrowed into skin.

"Shields up," Kaelan said, his hands hovering over the control spheres. "If she has an army left, this is where they make their stand."

"They're moving," Kaela said, watching the tactical display.

A swarm of small heat signatures erupted from the hangar bay of Seraphina's flagship. There were dozens of them—dropships, escape pods, and retrofitted void-skiffs.

They weren't forming a defensive phalanx. They were scattering.

"They're launching fighters," Kaelan growled, the solar array humming as it charged. "She's throwing everything at us."

"Wait," Kaela said. She reached out with the Circuit.

She expected to feel the sharp, disciplined aggression of soldiers. Instead, she felt a wave of frantic, greedy panic. She felt the heavy weight of gold in their pockets and the desperate desire to be anywhere but here.

"They aren't attacking," Kaela realized. "They're looting. They're running."

The lead skiff—a jagged, ugly vessel piloted by the scar-faced mercenary Krell—broke formation and burned hard toward the Celestial Keel.

Kaelan tracked it with the solar lance. "He's coming right at us."

"Don't fire," Kaela commanded.

The mercenary skiff didn't open its weapon ports. Instead, a mechanical arm extended from the hull. It was waving a piece of white cloth—a bedsheet tied to a spear.

A hail crackled over the comms.

"Scion Vessel! Do not fire! Repeat, do not fire!"

Kaelan blinked. "Is that... Jareth?"

"We surrender!" the mercenary shouted over the static. "We are combat ineffective! We are leaving the theater!"

Kaela stepped to the comms panel. "Identify. Are you flanking?"

"We are retiring!" Jareth yelled. "She's insane! She paid us, and we are taking the severance package! We want no part of the God! We just want to spend the gold!"

Kaela looked out the glass canopy. She could see the other ships streaming past them, headed back toward the ribcage exit. They were weighed down, their engines straining. They were loaded with the transmuted gold bars Seraphina had created.

"They think they're rich," Kaelan muttered. "They don't know the gold is going to turn back into radioactive sludge in three days."

"That's not our problem," Kaela said. "Let them go."

She keyed the comms.

"This is the Celestial Keel," she announced, her voice calm and authoritative. "Your surrender is accepted. You have safe passage. Do not return."

"Bless you, Scions!" Jareth cried. "She's all yours! She's in the Node Room! She's trying to hotwire the brain!"

The mercenary fleet burned their engines, accelerating past the twins. They were a pathetic parade of greed, fleeing the monster they had helped feed, clutching fool's gold to their chests.

"UNAUTHORIZED ENTITIES DEPARTING," the Star-Eater’s voice boomed in the cockpit. "SHOULD I ENGAGE ANTI-BODY DEFENSES?"

"No," Kaela told the god. "Let the parasites flush themselves out. We're here for the infection."

"ACKNOWLEDGED. PATH TO BRAIN STEM CLEAR. GOOD LUCK, JANITORS."

Kaelan steered the boat toward the silent, abandoned flagship.

"Just her," Kaelan said, watching the mercenary taillights fade into the distance. "No army. No shields. Just the Queen."

"And the silence," Kaela added.

They docked the Celestial Keel next to the Obsidian Queen. The airlock hissed open.

There were no guards. There were no traps. The corridors of the enemy ship were empty, littered with discarded armor and the debris of a hasty evacuation.

They walked through the ghost ship, crossing the gantry into the Star-Eater’s Brain Stem.

They were walking into the mind of a god, and the only thing waiting for them was a woman who thought she could outsmart the universe.
