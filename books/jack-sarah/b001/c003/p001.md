# Part 1: The Tuesday, August 15th, 2006 Meeting 🗓️

The air in Meeting Room 3 at the Fairlawn Recreation Center was stale with the lingering scent of coffee and the quiet hum of the fluorescent lights above . It was mid-August, and the core planning team for the center's annual Halloween event was gathered around a long, beige table.

Jennifer, the front desk's Office Supervisor, took her seat at the head of the table.

Scott, the Aquatics Supervisor, sat to her right, already looking through some notes .

Donald and Mandy, representing the front desk, found their spots, chatting quietly.

A moment later, Jack and Sarah Bennett walked in together and took two empty chairs side-by-side. Jennifer gave a small, knowing nod. Normally, Sarah would sit with Scott and the aquatics team, and Jack with the front desk crew. But it was an unspoken but universally accepted arrangement: in large, potentially overwhelming meetings, the Bennett siblings sat together. It was a simple, reasonable accommodation that kept them grounded, allowing them to offer each other quiet support if needed.

Jennifer tapped a pen on her notepad, calling the meeting to order. "Alright everyone, thank you for coming. As you know, our annual Halloween Spooktacular is our biggest building-wide event, and it's time to lock down this year's theme so I can get the purchase orders submitted".

"Last year's 'Pirate Adventure' was a big hit," Scott offered, "especially having kids 'Walk the Plank' off the diving board".

"It was," Jennifer agreed. "Which means we've set a high bar. Let's start brainstorming this year's new theme".

An idea that had been brewing among the staff for weeks finally surfaced. It was Mandy who spoke first, a mischievous glint in her eye. "What if, for one night... we're not city employees?" she ventured. "What if the whole rec center is being run by a... temporary staffing agency?".

Jack looked at her, his expression curious. "What kind of temp agency?" he asked, his literal nature seeking clarity.

Donald leaned forward, a slow, wicked grin spreading across his face. "The best kind," he said. "One with a CEO who's been in business for a very, *very* long time. A temp agency from downstairs".

A wave of delighted, subversive energy filled the room. The idea was perfect. "Underworld Staffing Solutions," Sarah said, the name landing with a perfect thud. "I love it".

"The devil runs a temp agency, and we're all his employees for the night," Jennifer mused, the concept clicking into place. "It's hilarious. It's creative. And it's something every department can get in on."

The ideas began to flow immediately. "Okay, if the pool is the River Styx," Scott jumped in, "then my lifeguards are the damned crew of Charon's ferry, the 'Styx Ferrymen & Co.'". "Perfect!" Jennifer clapped. "And the front desk staff are the gatekeepers. The Booking Agents collecting the toll for passage!".

Jack's face lit up. A specific, defined role with a clear script. It was a perfect fit. He could already picture it: the construction paper ferry, the 'toll' of a membership card—it was a system, a game with rules that made sense. Under the table, Sarah gave his hand a quick, proud squeeze.

"This is diabolically good," Jennifer said, her pen flying across her notepad. "I can write this up for the city in a way they won't even question. 'A facility-wide interactive theatrical experience.' I'll get the requisitions submitted this week so all the supplies arrive by early October".

She looked around the table at her smiling, enthusiastic team. "Alright, minions," she said with a grin. "Let's plan a hell of a party."
