# Part 2: The War is Over

The evening air in the Bennett living room was still and quiet, a profound calm that felt foreign after months of legal-induced anxiety. On the sofa, Jack and Sarah were curled up together under a blanket, their hug tight and protective. The recent, ugly court battle for guardianship had left them both shaken, and the fear of what their mother, Lorraine, might try next was a constant, unspoken presence between them.

Their father, Jermaine, sat in a nearby armchair, a thin folder of legal documents resting in his lap. He read the final page, his shoulders visibly relaxing as he let out a long, slow breath. He closed the folder with a soft, final thud.

"Well," he said, his voice filled with a weary relief that cut through the silence. "It's over."

Jack and Sarah looked up from the sofa, their anxious expressions not yet changing. They didn't dare to feel hopeful until they understood.

"I just got off the phone with the lawyer," Jermaine continued. "The judge's ruling from last week is filed. Your mother has officially been declared a Vexatious Litigant by the court".

Jack looked at Sarah, his brow furrowed in confusion. "A what?" he asked. "What does 'vexatious litigant' mean?" Sarah added, her voice wary.

Jermaine leaned forward, his expression gentle. "It's a legal term," he explained. "It means the judge saw that she was using the court system just to hurt and harass you. Think of it like a bully on a playground. The judge is the principal who just told that bully they're not allowed to even talk to you anymore without getting permission first. She can't just file lawsuits to scare you. The court has put up a permanent wall to protect you".

The meaning finally sank in. A visible wave of relief washed over the siblings on the sofa. The tension in Jack's shoulders eased, and the fearful tightness of their hug softened into one of pure, exhausted relief.

Sarah shook her head, a bitter, incredulous smile on her face. "After all that. After ignoring our disabilities our entire lives, she suddenly tries to use them to get a guardianship over us".

"She can't use that tactic, or any other, against you in court ever again," Jermaine promised. "You can finally, truly relax. The fight is over". He looked at his two children, his heart aching for what they had endured. "The final divorce hearing is just a formality now. It's scheduled for the week before Thanksgiving. Under Virginia law, we'll have been legally separated for a full year, and then it will all be finalized".

He stood up and came over to the sofa, sitting on the coffee table in front of them and placing a hand on each of their knees. "We got through it. Now we can focus on the future—on the move, on bringing our whole family together under one roof."

Sarah leaned into her father's touch, her own relief palpable. Jack released his hold on Sarah and reached for his dad, and Jermaine pulled both of his children into a tight, protective group hug. The war with Lorraine was over. Their new life could finally begin.
