In the quiet darkness of Devin's living room, the four cousins lay squeezed together on the queen-sized sofa bed. The weight and warmth of their bodies was a profound comfort, a silent fortress against the ugliness of the world outside. For a long moment, the only sound was their shared breathing.

"Okay," Clare whispered again from the far right, her voice cutting through the silence. "What really happened?"

"We know she was always a jerk to you guys," Molly's voice added from the far left. "To all of us, really. She never believed a word we said about... anything".

The unspoken word hung in the air: *disabilities*. It was the core of their shared experience with their aunt, the constant, dismissive cruelty that had united them long before this night.

Sarah took a shaky breath, feeling Jack shift beside her. "It's more than that this time," she said, her voice a low murmur. "Dad... he found out she's been cheating on him. He has proof. A video".

A stunned silence fell over the bed. This was the one thing Molly and Clare hadn't expected. Being a jerk was one thing; this was a betrayal of a different magnitude.

"She *what*?" Molly finally whispered, her voice a mixture of shock and disgust.

"He's filing for a legal separation tomorrow," Jack added, his own voice quiet but steady now that he was here, safe, with them. "It's... it's finally happening."

"Good," Clare said, the single word sharp and fierce in the darkness. "Good for your dad. You guys don't have to deal with her anymore."

"We're glad," Sarah admitted. "It's just... a lot."

"Well, you're not dealing with it alone," Molly stated, her voice a firm, unwavering promise from the other side of Jack. "You're here with us now."

Jack felt a hand find his in the darkness—Molly's. A moment later, he felt Sarah's hand find his other one. He was the link in a chain, connecting them all. They lay there for a long time, not speaking, four parts of a single, unbreakable whole. They hated Lorraine for the pain she had caused, but in that shared, quiet darkness, they were profoundly grateful. Her cruelty had forged their alliance, and now that the final battle had begun, they were all exactly where they needed to be: together.
