The quiet in Jack's bedroom was a comfortable, healing thing. Molly sat on the edge of the full-sized bed by Jack's side, with Clare in the matching spot on Sarah's side. The two siblings were propped up against their pillows, still tired, but the simple, quiet presence of their cousins was a profound comfort.

Jack shifted slightly, a wistful sigh escaping him. "I wish this bed was bigger," he said, his voice soft. "It's not fair that you guys are just perched on the edge. It would be nice if we could all just... fit. Properly. I guess a King is the biggest they make, though".

Clare's eyes lit up with a familiar, proactive spark. "Maybe we could push two beds together in the new house! Like in a hotel!"

"The seam in the middle would be awful," Sarah pointed out, her practical nature kicking in.

Molly, who had been listening quietly, reached for her laptop on the nightstand. "Hold on," she said, her analytical mind already engaged. "There has to be something bigger than a standard King. It's a logistical problem. Someone must have solved it".

As she typed, the others watched, intrigued. A few moments of clicking and scrolling passed.

"Whoa," Molly breathed, her eyes widening at what she saw on the screen. "Okay, you guys are not going to believe this. There are beds called Wyoming Kings, Texas Kings... and... get this... an Alaskan King".

"An Alaskan King?" Jack repeated, the name sounding impossibly grand.

"It's a perfect square," Molly reported, reading from the screen. "Nine feet by nine feet. It's massive. It says it's designed for families that co-sleep." She turned the laptop so they could all see the picture—a vast, inviting expanse of mattress that could easily accommodate all four of them with room to spare.

A stunned, hopeful silence fell over the room as they stared at the image.

"We just need a giant bed, then," Clare declared, her voice full of awe. "A ridiculously huge one, for the new house. For all of us."

"The Quad," Sarah murmured, the name for their future sanctuary spoken aloud for the first time as she looked at the picture of the massive bed.

They all looked at each other, and in that moment, the future was clear. It wasn't just a house they were moving into; it was a promise. A promise of a shared space, of a ridiculously large Alaskan King bed, and of a life built on the unwavering foundation of their love for one another.
