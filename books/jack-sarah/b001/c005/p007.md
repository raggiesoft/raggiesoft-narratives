# Part 7: A Father's Understanding 👨‍👧‍👦

A flurry of movement at the entrance to the ER bay announced the arrival of Jermaine Bennett. He rushed in, his face pale with a father's terror. "Jack? Sarah? Oh, my God. I came as soon as I got the call. What happened?"

He went straight to Sarah's other side, his hand gently touching her forehead, his relief at seeing her conscious warring with his fear. Just as Jack opened his mouth to explain, the curtain was pulled back further, and a doctor entered, followed by a respiratory therapist.

"Mr. Bennett? I'm Dr. Evans," the doctor said, turning his attention to Sarah. "We're just going to run a few quick tests, get a baseline."

As the medical team began their work, efficiently checking Sarah's breathing and drawing a blood sample to test her oxygen levels, Jack recounted the chaotic series of events to his father in a low, urgent voice.

"It was a Code Purple," he started, the words tumbling out. "For Sarah, from Aquatics. But when I got to the pump room, the smell... Dad, it was awful. I had to call a Code Green. She was inside, having a meltdown". He explained the agonizing decision to prioritize her evacuation over providing immediate comfort. "Then HazMat said the fumes were in the main vents. They pulled the fire alarm to get everyone out—called a Code Orange even though there was no fire, just to make sure people evacuated immediately".

Jermaine listened, his expression a mixture of horror and immense pride in his son's actions. When the medical team finished their initial assessment and left to await test results, the bay fell quiet again, save for the rhythmic beep of the monitor.

Jermaine looked from Sarah, pale and small on the gurney with the oxygen tube in her nose, to Jack, still wearing the heavy rubber boots, his face smudged with grime and exhaustion. The full weight of what they had been through, and what they had become to each other, seemed to settle over him.

"Jack," he said, his voice soft. "I know... since everything with your mother... I know you two have been sharing a bed".

Jack looked up, surprised, a defensive explanation already forming on his lips. "Dad, it's not—"

"I know it's not," Jermaine cut him off gently, his voice full of a deep, unwavering understanding. "I know exactly what it is. It's two siblings who have been through hell, finding safety and comfort in each other. It's innocent, and it's what you both need. I see it, and I approve".

He reached over and squeezed Jack's shoulder. "When Sarah is all better, and we're home and past this," he continued, "I'll help you. We can officially set up one room as your shared bedroom, and we'll turn the other into a study space for you both. If you'd like".

Jack looked at his father, a wave of profound gratitude washing over him, moved by the depth of his dad's acceptance. "Yeah, Dad," he said, his voice thick with emotion. "I'd like that. A lot".

From the gurney, Sarah, who had been listening to the entire exchange, turned her head on the pillow. She couldn't speak, but a weak, yet radiant, smile touched her lips as she looked at her father. It was her non-verbal, heartfelt "yes".
