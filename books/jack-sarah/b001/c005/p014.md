# Part 14: The Confirmation レントゲン

The nurse, Karen, finished her urgent call. "They're on their way up now," she said to Jack, her voice calm but firm. "Try to keep her as upright as she's comfortable with."

Jack helped adjust Sarah's pillows, propping her up slightly. Her breathing seemed more labored now, and the wet rattle in her chest was more pronounced with every painful cough.

A few minutes later, a young man in scrubs arrived, wheeling a large, beige portable X-ray machine into the room. Its mechanical presence made the situation feel alarmingly real.

"Okay, folks, just need to get one quick image of her lungs," the technician said efficiently. He turned to Jack. "Sir, for your safety, I'm going to have to ask you to step out into the hallway for just a minute while I take the shot".

Jack's first instinct was to refuse. The thought of leaving Sarah's side, even for a second, felt like a betrayal. But he saw the professional resolve on the technician's face and knew it wasn't a request. He looked at Sarah, whose eyes were wide with a new wave of fear.

"I'll be right outside the door," he promised, giving her hand one last, firm squeeze. "Right here."

He stepped out into the quiet, dimly lit hallway of the respiratory floor. The door clicked shut behind him, muffling the sounds from within. He stood there, his bare feet cold against the polished linoleum, the silence of the hospital at 4 AM pressing in on him. He could hear the faint, muffled instructions from the technician, the whir of the machine being positioned. The brief separation was agonizing.

Less than two minutes later, the door opened again. "All done," the technician said. "You can come back in."

Jack rushed back inside. The technician was already wheeling the large machine out of the room. The nurse was helping Sarah get comfortable again. Sarah looked at him, her expression a mixture of relief that he was back and terror of the unknown. She coughed again, that awful, gurgling sound that seemed to steal her breath.

"The doctor will have the results in just a few minutes," the nurse said, her voice soft with sympathy. "The image is sent to them digitally. We'll know what we're dealing with very soon."

Jack sat back down, taking his sister's hand again. They waited in a new, heavy silence, filled with the cold dread of knowing that the X-ray would almost certainly confirm the hospital's worst fears.
