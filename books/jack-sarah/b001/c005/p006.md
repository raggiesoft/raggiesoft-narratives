# Part 6: A Safe Harbor ❤️‍🩹

The walk from his car to the emergency room entrance of Crossroads Memorial was a blur for Jack. He was still wearing the heavy, out-of-place rubber boots, a surreal reminder of the chaos he'd just left. He quickly checked in at the front desk, the friendly but efficient triage nurse handing him a visitor's badge after he explained who he was there to see.

"She's in Bay 4, just through those doors and to your left," the nurse directed.

Jack pushed through the doors, the bright lights and antiseptic smell of the ER a stark contrast to the familiar chaos of the rec center. He found Bay 4 and pulled back the curtain.

The sight of his sister made his heart clench. Sarah was lying on a gurney, looking small and pale in a hospital gown. A clear plastic tube, a nasal cannula, was looped over her ears, feeding her a steady stream of oxygen. An IV line was taped to the back of her hand, and the steady, rhythmic beep of a heart monitor filled the small space. Her eyes were red-rimmed and watery, and she looked utterly, profoundly exhausted.

She turned her head as he entered, and the weariness in her expression was instantly replaced by a wave of pure, overwhelming relief. She couldn't speak, but her eyes said everything. She weakly lifted her free arm from the gurney in a clear, desperate invitation.

Jack was at her side in an instant. "I'm here, Sarah," he whispered. "I'm here."

He leaned over carefully, mindful of the tubes and wires, and wrapped his arms around her. It wasn't the tight, grounding hug of a meltdown, but a gentle, encompassing embrace of pure comfort and reassurance. He could feel a slight tremor running through her as she clung to him, her head resting against his chest.

When he finally drew back, he pulled the visitor's chair close to the gurney and took her hand, lacing his fingers through hers. She squeezed his hand weakly, her gaze never leaving his face. She was still scared, still hurt, but her anchor was here. Her brother was with her. They sat together in the sterile quiet of the bay, a two-person fortress, waiting for the doctor, ready to face whatever came next, together.
