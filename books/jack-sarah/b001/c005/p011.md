About an hour later, Jermaine returned to the quiet hospital room, a duffel bag slung over his shoulder. "Reinforcements have arrived," he announced softly, his presence a comforting balm.

He placed the bag on the pull-out sofa and began to unload its contents: a fresh change of clothes for Jack, their phone chargers, and both of their laptops. As he started to place Sarah's discarded work uniform into a plastic bag, Sarah shifted in the bed, her eyes widening with a sudden thought.

Her voice was still a raw croak, but the words were clearer now. "Dad... my pants... pocket...".

Jack understood immediately. He went over to the counter where her sweatpants were folded. He reached into the pocket and pulled out her phone, her wallet, and her keys. "Got 'em right here, Sarah," he said, placing them safely on her bedside table where she could see them. A look of immense relief washed over her face.

The sight of his sister's belongings made Jack acutely aware of his own. His shoes were still locked away in the contaminated second floor of Fairlawn. He looked down at the heavy, black PPE boots he was still wearing. With a deep sigh, he sat on the edge of the sofa and unlaced them, pulling them off with a grunt. The relief was instantaneous. He peeled off his damp socks, finally letting his feet breathe after hours of being encased in rubber.

As he was stretching his toes, he heard a small gasp from the bed. Sarah had tried to shift her position to get more comfortable, and in doing so, the flimsy ties on the back of her hospital gown had come completely undone. The gown gaped open and fell off, exposing her front to the cool air of the room. A deep blush crept up her neck.

"Whoa, hang on," Jermaine said, noticing immediately. Both he and Jack moved toward the bed, but Jermaine waved him off gently. "Let me, son. After all the practice I got with these stupid things when your Uncle Devin had his bypass surgery, I'm practically an expert".

His hands were quick, gentle, and practiced. He deftly gathered the back of the gown and secured the ties with a firm knot, covering his daughter back up completely. Sarah let out a shuddering breath of relief. "Thanks... Dad," she rasped.

Just then, the nurse, Karen, came back in for a routine check of Sarah's vitals. The brief moment of family normalcy was replaced by the quiet beeps and hums of the medical equipment.

Once the nurse was finished and had left the room, Jermaine could see the deep exhaustion settling on both his children. "Alright, kids," he said softly. "You're in good hands. Try to get some rest. I'll be back first thing in the morning."

He gave Jack a hug, then leaned over and kissed Sarah's forehead one last time before heading out for the night, leaving the two siblings alone in the quiet sanctuary of the hospital room.
