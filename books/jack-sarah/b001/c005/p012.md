# Part 12: The Long Night 😠

The quiet in Sarah's hospital room was a fragile thing. Jack had finally started to drift off on the pull-out sofa, the profound exhaustion of the day weighing him down. He could hear the soft, rhythmic beep of Sarah's heart monitor and her restless shifting in the hospital bed. The long, terrible day was finally over, and all he wanted was the oblivion of sleep.

He was just on the edge of it when the door to the room swished open without warning. A fraction of a second later, the main overhead fluorescent lights snapped on, flooding the dark room with a harsh, sterile, and utterly unwelcome glare.

Jack groaned, squeezing his eyes shut against the sudden, painful brightness. "Time for our midnight check!" a cheerful voice announced. It was a new nurse, the one for the night shift.

He pushed himself up on his elbows, blinking. The fragile peace was shattered. He looked over at Sarah's bed. She had an arm thrown over her eyes to block the light, her face a clear mask of irritation.

The nurse was efficient, already wrapping the blood pressure cuff around Sarah's arm and placing a thermometer in her ear. Sarah let out an audible, frustrated sigh. Her voice, still a raw croak but stronger now, was filled with annoyance.

"How much longer?" she rasped, pointing to her mouth. "NPO? I'm really hungry".

The nurse's expression was sympathetic. "I know, honey. It's the worst part. The doctor wants to keep you off food and drink until his morning assessment. He needs to be absolutely sure your breathing is stable before we introduce any risk of aspiration. It's for your safety".

Sarah slumped back against her pillows, another frustrated sound escaping her. That was not the answer she wanted.

The nurse finished her checks, recorded the numbers, and with another cheerful, "You two try to get some sleep now!" she dimmed the lights and slipped out of the room.

Jack and Sarah were left once again in the dim quiet, but now they were both wide awake, annoyed, and, in Sarah's case, acutely aware of her empty stomach. The long night stretched before them, promising more interruptions and little real rest.
