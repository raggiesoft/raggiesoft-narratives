# Part 16: The Conti-Vac 💧

The first few hours of the morning were a miserable, exhausting blur. The diuretic medication was working with a vengeance. Twice, Sarah had to frantically press the call button, and the nurse, Karen, had to help her make the slow, painful trip from the bed to the bathroom and back. The effort left Sarah pale, breathless, and trembling with exhaustion.

After the second trip, Karen's expression was sympathetic but firm. "Sarah," she said gently, "this is no good. You're not getting any rest, and you're using up energy you need to heal your lungs. I've spoken with the doctor, and we're going to get you set up with the Conti-Vac System".

Jack, who had been dozing fitfully on the sofa, sat up. Sarah stared at the nurse, her brow furrowed in confusion.

"It's an external catheter," Karen explained, already retrieving the equipment from a supply cart. "It's not invasive at all. It's just a soft, comfortable wick that we place externally between your legs. It's connected to a tube with very gentle suction, and it just pulls the urine away into a canister by the bed. It means you can just relax and let your body do its thing without having to get out of bed every half hour".

The color drained from Sarah's face, replaced by a deep, mortified blush. Jack could see the disbelief, then the horror, in her eyes. She was a strong, capable Lifeguard used to being in control. The idea of being hooked up to a machine like this was a profound indignity.

She shook her head, her voice a raw, pleading croak. "No... please..."

"I know it feels strange, honey," the nurse said kindly but without wavering. "But I promise you, it's going to make you so much more comfortable. It's a standard tool we use for patients on strong diuretics. It will let you rest."

Jack saw the tears of frustration welling in his sister's eyes. He crossed the room and took her hand. "Sarah, it's okay," he said softly. "It's just a machine. To help you get some sleep. It's temporary."

Trapped between medical necessity and her own exhaustion, Sarah finally gave a small, defeated nod, turning her face away in embarrassment. The nurse began to discreetly set up the system. Jack, knowing this was a moment of profound vulnerability for his sister, started to turn away to give her privacy, but he felt her grip tighten on his hand. He looked back. She shook her head slightly, her eyes silently pleading with him to stay.

He stayed, turning his gaze toward the window, giving her the illusion of privacy while still being the grounding presence she needed. He listened to the quiet, professional movements of the nurse, his heart aching for his sister's loss of dignity, but knowing this was another necessary, humbling step on her long road back to health.
