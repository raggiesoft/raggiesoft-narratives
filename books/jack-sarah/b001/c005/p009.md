The transport team arrived with a gurney, their movements quiet and efficient. As they prepared to move Sarah, she looked at Jack, her red, irritated eyes pleading. She couldn't speak, but she held her hand out to him, a clear, desperate need for connection. Jack took it instantly, his grip firm and reassuring.

He walked alongside the gurney as they wheeled her out of the bustling ER and through the long, sterile corridors of Crossroads Memorial. The time was now 8:42 PM, long after the sun had set. They should have been in the final hour of their closing shift at Fairlawn. Instead, they were here, his hand a constant anchor for her in a world that had become frightening and unstable.

They arrived at a quiet room on the respiratory floor. As the transport team helped settle Sarah into the hospital bed, a nurse confirmed the hospital's policy. "She is permitted one overnight guest," she said kindly to Jermaine and Jack.

Jermaine looked at his two children—Sarah, small and vulnerable in the large bed, and Jack, standing steadfastly by her side. He smiled a gentle, knowing smile. "Jack," he said softly, "she's going to want you to be by her side".

Hearing her father's words, Sarah turned her head on the pillow, her gaze locking with Jack's. She took a ragged breath, a painful-sounding cough shaking her frame. She was visibly struggling, trying to force her voice to work. A raw, hoarse whisper rasped out.

"Jack... Tonight... Stay".

"Of course, Sarah," Jack said immediately, squeezing her hand. "I'm not going anywhere."

"It's settled then," Jermaine said, his voice warm with approval. "I'll go home and get you a change of clothes, Jack. You stay here with your sister."

A weak but radiant smile spread across Sarah's face. It was a silent, heartfelt thank you to them both.

Just then, Jack's phone buzzed in his pocket. He pulled it out to see a text from Kaitlyn.

Kaitlyn: Hey Jack, it's Kaitlyn. Just checking in. How are you and Sarah holding up? Everyone here is sending their best for a speedy recovery for her.

Before he could reply, a second text came through.

Kaitlyn: FYI, Joe closed the center for the night since it was almost 9 anyway. I put in a time edit for you for 9:15, so you're covered. Sarah's time will be handled by the city's emergency leave policy. Someone from HR or Jennifer will be in touch tomorrow. Just focus on Sarah.

Jack felt a wave of gratitude for his coworkers. He quickly typed back a reply.

Jack: Thanks so much, Kaitlyn. We're doing okay. They're keeping Sarah overnight for observation. I'm staying with her. She won't be in tomorrow. I'm off anyway. Appreciate you all.

He put the phone away and turned his full attention back to his sister, his role as her overnight protector now official. He was right where he needed to be.
