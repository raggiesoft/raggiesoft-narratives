# Part 5: Going Home

The morning of the fourth day arrived with the blessed news they had been waiting for. After a final check, Dr. Evans officially signed Sarah's discharge papers.

"You responded remarkably well, Sarah," he said with a warm smile. "But you need to take it easy for the next week. No strenuous activity, and get plenty of rest." He handed Jermaine a thick envelope. "This has all her prescriptions and the return-to-work forms you'll need."

Jermaine took the envelope, his expression one of profound relief. He looked at Sarah. "Okay, sweetie, I'll drop this off at the Occupational Health office on my way to work. You know the drill, it'll take them a few days to process it. Once they give the all-clear, they'll call Scott, and he'll call you to get you back on the schedule. So don't even think about work for a while. Just focus on resting."

A nurse came in and, with a few practiced, painless movements, removed the IV from Sarah's hand. It was the last tube, the final tether connecting her to the hospital. A shudder of pure relief went through her.

She looked at Jack, her eyes shining. Her voice was stronger now, clearer. "Home," she said, the single word filled with a deep, weary longing. "I just want to go home and sleep".

Jack helped her gather the few belongings they had accumulated—laptops, chargers, the cards from their coworkers. He was still moving with a slight stiffness from his own CMT flare-up, but his entire focus was on his sister's freedom.

Jermaine went to get a wheelchair for the trip to the car. As he pushed a very tired but very happy Sarah out of the room, Jack followed right behind them. The long, frightening ordeal was finally over. They were going home.
