# Part 4: The Last Night 😴

The second full day in the hospital ended with a quiet, hopeful energy. With the promise of being discharged in the morning, Sarah seemed more like herself than she had in days. But first, they had to get through one last night.

One last night of interruptions.

Jack had just managed to drift into a shallow, fitful sleep on the pull-out sofa when the familiar swish of the door and the merciless snap of the overhead lights jolted him awake. Midnight. The nurse was cheerful and efficient as she took Sarah's vitals. When she left, the room was plunged back into a darkness that felt loud with their shared, wide-awake frustration.

It took another hour for Jack to fall back asleep. He was awoken again at 2 AM. And again at 4 AM. Each time, the routine was the same: the bright lights, the beeping machines, the quiet questions, and the shattering of their fragile attempts at rest.

After the 4 AM check, Jack didn't even bother trying to go back to sleep. He just lay on his back, staring at the acoustic tiles of the ceiling, feeling gritty and exhausted. He was done.

A voice, rough with sleep and annoyance, came from the hospital bed. "I am never," Sarah declared into the darkness, "going to take a full, uninterrupted night of sleep for granted ever again."

"You and me both," Jack murmured, turning his head on the lumpy pillow to look in her direction.

He heard her sigh, a long, weary sound. "Just a few more hours," she said, more to herself than to him. "Then we go home."

Jack closed his eyes, not to sleep, but to picture it. Home. A comfortable bed. Quiet. And a long, deep sleep with no one coming in to wake them up. The thought was the only thing getting him through the last few hours of this long, final night.
