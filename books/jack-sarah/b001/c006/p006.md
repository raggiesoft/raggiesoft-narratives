The epic, synthesized score of "The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel" swelled to a triumphant finish, and the credits began to roll on Jack's laptop. For a blissful two hours, the four cousins had been lost in their favorite cult classic, the sterile reality of the hospital room momentarily forgotten.

As Jack closed the laptop, the quiet of the room rushed back in, punctuated by the steady beeping of the monitors. The distraction was over. Sarah's stomach immediately chose that moment to let out a long, loud, and angry gurgle of protest.

She glared at her abdomen as if it had betrayed her, then looked at her siblings and cousins, her face a mask of pure, grumpy frustration.

"I'm... so... hungry," she rasped, her voice still a raw croak. "I don't even care. They could bring me a tray of... of prison slop right now, and I would eat every last bite".

She paused, a flicker of her old, dry wit surfacing through the misery. A small, wry smile touched her lips.

"...Wait," she croaked, her eyes widening in mock seriousness. "Don't call it slop. Mrs. DeMarco might hear me".

The unexpected and deeply familiar reference to their legendary and terrifyingly strict high school cafeteria supervisor landed with perfect comedic timing. The absurdity of it, of being more afraid of Mrs. DeMarco's judgment than a hospital's dietary offerings, struck Sarah as hilarious. A raw, croaking laugh escaped her, which quickly turned into a fit of genuine, body-shaking laughter.

The sudden exertion was too much for her strained system. The heart monitor beside her bed suddenly let out a series of loud, insistent, high-pitched beeps, the display flashing red. Her laughter had triggered the alarm.

For a split second, a jolt of pure, Pavlovian terror went through Sarah. *The alarm! I'm in trouble! I'm going to get trash duty!* Then she blinked, the reality of the IV in her hand and the hospital gown rushing back. Wait... hospital. Not Northwood High.

She was profoundly relieved to remember that she had, in fact, never been a recipient of Mrs. DeMarco's "Advanced Sanitation Studies".

Jack, Molly, and Clare, who had also been laughing, jumped at the sound of the alarm. A moment later, the nurse rushed in. "What's going on? Is she okay?"

Jack, still chuckling, pointed at his sister. "She's fine! She... she just made a really funny joke."

The nurse glanced at the monitor, saw Sarah's heart rate already settling, and shook her head with a smile of disbelief as she silenced the alarm. "Well, try not to laugh yourselves into cardiac arrest. You're going to give me one."

The alarm was off, but the four cousins were still giggling, the moment of pure, shared levity so powerful it had literally required medical intervention.
