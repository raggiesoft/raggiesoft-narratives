# Part 2: The Longest Morning 🥞

The phone in the room buzzed, a much softer sound than the jarring alarms of the night. Jack picked it up and immediately pressed the speakerphone button so Sarah could hear their dad's voice.

"Hey, son. I'm on my way over now," Jermaine's voice filled the small room, a welcome sound of normalcy. "How's your sister doing? What are they bringing her for breakfast?".

Jack looked over at Sarah, who had her eyes closed, her face a mask of grumpy exhaustion. "She's doing okay, Dad," he said quietly. "But she's NPO. No food for her."

Hearing her father's voice, Sarah's eyes opened. She glared at Jack, then made a gesture toward her own mouth before pointing firmly at him. Her voice, a raw and insistent croak, was loud enough for the speakerphone to pick up.

"You... eat," she commanded.

There was a pause on the other end, then Jermaine's voice came back, full of warmth and amusement. "Good for her," he said. "You heard your sister, Jack. I'll stop by the hospital cafeteria on my way up and get breakfast for both of us. See you in a few".

True to his word, Jermaine arrived a short while later, balancing two trays laden with pancakes and coffee for himself, and scrambled eggs for Jack. The aroma filled the small room, and Jack's stomach rumbled painfully. He looked at Sarah, feeling a pang of guilt.

"Eat," she insisted again, her voice a little stronger. "I want you to".

Knowing it was what she needed him to do, Jack reluctantly began to eat, as did his father. They were halfway through their meal when Dr. Evans came back in. He smiled warmly at Jermaine. "Mr. Bennett, I'm Dr. Evans. I'm overseeing Sarah's care. I'm glad I caught you". He gave them a quick, positive update. "She's responding very well to the diuretics. Her lungs are already sounding much clearer than they did a few hours ago."

As if on cue, Sarah shifted uncomfortably in the bed, a look of profound annoyance on her face. "Hate this," she rasped, gesturing vaguely at her lower body. "Peeing... lying down. It feels... so unnatural".

The doctor nodded sympathetically. "I know it does. But it's the most effective way for us to get that fluid off while letting your body rest. You're doing great."

Sarah looked from the doctor to her father, her eyes pleading. "Want... to see them," she struggled to say. "Molly... Clare... Uncle Devin... Grandpa Sam...".

"Of course, sweetie," Jermaine said immediately, his heart aching for her. "As soon as I leave here, I'll call them all. I promise they'll come visit you today".

After the doctor left, Jermaine pulled his chair closer to the bed. "I'm not going anywhere for a while," he said, settling in to keep his children company. The room fell into a comfortable, if tired, silence. There wasn't much to do.

"Dad?" Jack asked, an idea forming. "Can you get my laptop from the bag you brought?"

Jermaine handed it over. Jack opened it and pulled a DVD from one of the side pockets. "Want to watch a movie?" he asked his sister.

Sarah's eyes lit up with a spark of her old self. She gave a grateful nod. Jack slid the disc into the laptop's drive, and the opening titles of a beloved 1980s fantasy-adventure, "The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel," filled the screen, its cheesy synthesizer score a welcome distraction. The three of them settled in to watch, a small island of family normalcy in the sterile, beeping world of the hospital.
