The small, shared glimmer of hope seemed to lighten the heavy atmosphere in the hospital room. Jack, wanting to hold onto the fragile moment of normalcy, reached for his laptop. "Ready to get back to Aethel?" he asked Sarah. She gave a weak nod, her eyes already fixed on the screen.

He unpaused the movie, and the cheesy 80s synthesizer score of "The Silver Gauntlet of Aethel" once again filled the room, a welcome distraction for them all.

They were only a few minutes back into the film when a soft knock on the door announced new arrivals. Molly and Clare stepped inside, their faces etched with worry. The moment Jack saw his cousins, he felt another wave of profound relief. He got up from the sofa and met them with immediate, tight hugs.

The twins went straight to Sarah's bedside, their expressions softening with concern. "Sarah, oh my gosh, how are you feeling?" Molly asked, her voice low. "We came as soon as your dad called," Clare added, gently touching Sarah's arm.

Sarah gave them a small but genuine smile, clearly happy to see them. Her gaze then flickered to the laptop screen, where the hero, Kaelan, was brandishing his sword.

"No way," Molly said, her eyes lighting up with recognition despite her worry. "Is that 'The Silver Gauntlet'?"

"You can't just be watching this from the middle," Clare stated with mock seriousness. "That's a crime against cult classics."

"Restart it, Jack," Molly insisted. "From the beginning. We're watching it properly".

Jack was all too happy to comply. He clicked the mouse, and the movie's opening credits began to roll. The four cousins, with Jermaine watching from his chair with a fond smile, were now a single, focused unit, finding a precious moment of shared joy in the familiar, beloved film.

They were about halfway through when the nurse, Karen, came back in for another vitals check. Jack paused the movie again. After the check, Sarah, looking embarrassed but knowing it was necessary, used her raw voice to speak to the nurse. "Can you... help me... to the bathroom?" she rasped. "I need... to go".

The nurse nodded kindly and began to help Sarah out of the bed. The movie remained paused as the cousins waited patiently, a silent, supportive vigil. When Sarah was safely back in bed a few minutes later, she looked at Jack and gave a small nod.

He pressed the spacebar, and the adventure in Aethel resumed. Huddled around the small laptop screen, the four of them were completely absorbed, a tight-knit fellowship finding comfort and strength in their shared ritual, the beeps and hums of the hospital fading into the background.
