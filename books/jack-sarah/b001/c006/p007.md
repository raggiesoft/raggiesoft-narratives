The four cousins shared another small laugh as the nurse left, the absurdity of Sarah's joke setting off the heart monitor still sinking in. The reference to their formidable high school cafeteria supervisor, Mrs. DeMarco, hung in the air, a shared and potent memory.

"You know," Clare said with a grin, "I'm pretty sure half our graduating class still has nightmares about getting 'Advanced Sanitation Studies' from her."

Jack's eyes lit up with a mischievous spark. "Speaking of trash," he said, reaching for the laptop Jermaine had brought earlier. "Hang on, I almost forgot I had this. I have to show you guys".

He opened the laptop, and after a moment of clicking, he turned the screen so Sarah, Molly, and Clare could all see. On the screen was a grainy, flash-lit photo from their senior prom. It showed a horrified-looking Josh Riley standing stiffly in his rented tux. Beside him, in the center of the gymnasium floor, was his date: Brenda Binn, a 50-gallon, industrial-grey trash can with a cheap plastic tiara taped crookedly to its lid.

The sight of the picture, the perfect visual punchline to their shared memory, sent a fresh wave of helpless, cathartic laughter through the three girls.

"I still can't believe Mark and his friends hijacked the napkin vote like that," Molly said, shaking her head as she chuckled. "Everyone was voting honestly, and they just didn't care".

"I know!" Clare added, wiping a tear of laughter from her eye. "They collected all the napkins, pretended to count them, and then Mark just got up and announced Josh had won anyway".

"Just so they could wheel out Brenda Binn as his queen," Jack finished, a real, happy laugh escaping him. "The look on his face was legendary".

The four of them looked at the picture again, a perfect, frozen moment of high school justice.

"So epically fitting," Sarah managed to rasp from the bed, a genuine smile on her face. "The only queen the King of Trash Duty ever deserved".

Jack looked at his sister and his cousins, all of them laughing together in this sterile hospital room. It was a miserable memory for Josh Riley, but here, together, it was a legendary story that belonged to them, another reason they were an unbreakable unit.
