The afternoon flew by in a blur of happy chaos. Jack found he enjoyed his role, gravely accepting the "toll" of a membership scan or a day pass before granting patrons passage into the Spooktacular. He watched his coworkers, all in their infernal costumes—Mandy the fellow warlock, Bob the "Accountant from the Abyss," Susan the "Oracle"—and felt a sense of camaraderie in their shared, silly secret.

At 7:00 PM, his shift was over. Tanya, the "Nocturnal Operations Manager," took over his station for the late shift. But Sarah wasn't done until 9:15 PM, and the thought of waiting in the quiet break room seemed dull compared to the party raging outside. He decided to stay.

He took off his warlock robes, leaving just his themed ID badge clipped to his t-shirt, and set off to explore. He wandered into the Youth Lounge, now the "Monster Mash Arcade," and watched kids trying to set high scores on the free-play machines. The energy was infectious.

After a while, he felt a pull to see how his sister was doing. He bypassed the ramp and took the staff-keyed elevator up to the second floor, the quiet ride a brief respite from the noise. The doors opened to the familiar, humid 84-degree air of the natatorium hallway.

He walked to the entrance of the pool deck and stopped, a wide grin spreading across his face. The pool was glowing orange, filled with dozens of floating mini-pumpkins and splashing, laughing children. It was the "Great Pumpkin Float," and it was a massive success. He scanned the deck and spotted Sarah. She was in her element, standing with a confident posture as she directed another lifeguard, every inch the "Ferry Captain" of her chaotic, happy domain.

He didn't want to interrupt her while she was on duty, so he just watched from the doorway. After a moment, she must have sensed him. She turned, her eyes finding his across the deck, and her professional focus melted into a huge, radiant smile. She gave him a happy, enthusiastic wave.

Jack waved back, his heart swelling with a fierce, quiet pride. He was tired, but profoundly happy. He found a spot on the observation bleachers on the third floor, a perfect vantage point to watch the festivities, feeling completely content to wait in their second home until his sister was ready to go.
