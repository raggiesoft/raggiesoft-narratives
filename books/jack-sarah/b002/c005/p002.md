# Part 2: A Rec Center Sanctuary

“I have your swim bag in the back seat along with my things,” Clare said as they pulled into the familiar Fairlawn Recreation Center parking lot. “I’m sure Sarah will be happy to see us.”

The two of them checked in at the front desk, the familiar beep of the scanner a comforting, normal sound. They made their way to their respective locker rooms. “Meet you on the pool deck,” Jack said, already feeling more like himself in the beloved, quirky building.

“Yeah, same. Meet you on deck,” Clare replied as she hugged her cousin again. He got changed as quickly as he reasonably could, looking forward to washing the strange, unsettling afternoon away in the cool water.

As promised, Clare was waiting for Jack on the pool deck. The humid, 84-degree air of the natatorium was a familiar embrace. “Where’s Sarah?” Jack asked, scanning the lifeguard stands.

As the Lead Lifeguard on duty, Sarah was not in one of the high stands. Jack and Clare spotted her just as she was finishing her rounds on the pool deck, heading towards the aquatics office with a clipboard and a small tray of water samples. Just as she was about to step inside to conduct her routine water tests, she saw her brother and her cousin, and a wide, happy smile broke across her face. She quickly ducked into the office to drop off the clipboard and samples, then immediately popped back out onto the deck to greet them.

“There she is!” Jack exclaimed happily, quickly approaching his sister and offering her a hug.

He gave his sister a hug, and Clare said to Sarah, "I think your brother had a weird afternoon. He'll tell you about it later.”

Jack’s cheeks flushed slightly. He had already decided that tonight, once they were all home, he would tell them everything—the whole bizarre, creepy story about Delores and the empty dorm room. He needed their collective wisdom and support.

Sarah gave him a questioning, concerned look but just squeezed his arm in support. "Okay. Well, I'm glad you're here now. I just have to run these tests really quick, then I'll be right back out."

She disappeared into the office for a few minutes. Inside, she methodically tested the water samples for chlorine and pH levels. Everything came back within the expected, safe parameters. With her duties complete, she emerged back onto the pool deck, her professional focus shifting to relief and happiness at seeing her family.

"Alright, all good," she announced. She looked at the main lifeguard stand. "It's about time for rotation anyway. I'll go relieve Jen up there. You two have a good swim!"

She gave Jack's arm another squeeze before heading over to the main stand to relieve the other guard.

After a relaxing swim that did wonders to clear his head, it was time to get going. Jack offered both Sarah and Clare one last hug before he and Clare went their separate ways to the locker rooms.

Jack came out to the hallway where Clare was waiting. They made their way to the front desk where Jennifer, his boss, was standing, looking concerned. "Jack? Are you available this evening by chance? We just had a call-out while you were in the pool."

Jack stopped and asked, “What hours did you need?”

“Now until close,” Jennifer said.

The idea of grounding himself in a familiar routine was incredibly appealing after the chaos of the afternoon. He turned to Clare. "Do I have a city shirt in the car by chance?"

“Yes, I think so,” Clare replied.

“We can check,” Jack said to Jennifer. “I have my badge because it also has my rec membership.”

“Oh, we have spare shirts if that’s all you need,” Jennifer replied with a relieved smile.

Clare smiled. “Then let me go get your water bottle and I will let Sarah know that she needs to bring you home.”

Jack did have his city shirt in the car. He clocked in and got settled in for the day, taking care of the scan station. The rec center had about an hour to go before close, and Sarah, having finished her shift, found a seat in the front lobby to do homework, perched where she could keep a discrete eye on Jack, a silent, supportive presence while he worked.
