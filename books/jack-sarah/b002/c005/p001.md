# Part 1: A Cousin's Rescue

Jack stepped out of the quiet apartment building and into the bright, late afternoon air of the VDU campus, taking a deep, cleansing breath. The text to his cousin Clare had been sent just moments before, and he looked around, hoping her car would be the next one he saw. The relief of being out of that room was immense, but it was followed by a wave of confusion and embarrassment.

A few minutes later, a familiar small Stark Astra pulled up to the curb, and Clare got out, her brow furrowed with concern. "Jack? Are you okay? You look like you've seen a ghost."

He was pale, and she could see the tension in his shoulders. This was not the happy, excited cousin she had expected to find.

"I'm fine," he said, the words coming out a little too quickly. He opened the passenger door and slid in, grateful for the familiar comfort of her car. He immediately pulled his cousin into a hug, a gesture that was less about greeting and more about seeking a safe anchor after the deeply unsettling encounter.

Clare hugged him back, her concern growing. "Okay, you're definitely not fine," she said gently as they pulled apart. "What happened? I thought you were on a tour."

"I was," Jack said, his voice muffled as he stared out the window. "It just... got weird. The tour guide, Delores… she was really intense." He trusted Clare completely, but the full story was too mortifying to share just yet. He was still processing the whiplash of going from a seemingly normal conversation to a deeply uncomfortable, predatory situation in a matter of minutes. "She gave me this private tour, and it was just... a lot. Too fast. I don't think I'm going to see her again."

Clare, sensing his deep distress and his reluctance to share the details, didn't push. She knew from his tone and the way he was avoiding her gaze that something had genuinely scared him. "Okay," she said softly, putting the car in gear. "I'm sorry that happened. Well, I'm glad you're out of there now. Let's go swimming. It'll clear your head. We can talk about it later if you want."

"That sounds like a great idea," Jack replied, finally letting out a long breath as she got on the main road to head to the rec center. The simple, uncomplicated plan was exactly what he needed.
