Later that evening, after the family had finished one of their last dinners at Jermaine's house, the men—Jermaine, Devin, and Sam—settled in the living room to watch a basketball game. The low murmur of the television provided the perfect cover. Jack, his expression a mask of anxiety, caught Sarah’s eye and gave a slight nod towards his bedroom. She understood instantly.

Molly and Clare, who had been waiting for this conversation since the phone call in the car, followed them without a word. The four of them gathered in Jack’s room, the door closed, creating a private, safe space away from the rest of the family. Jack was too scared and embarrassed to tell his dad, his uncle, or his grandfather the full, ugly details of his afternoon. But here, with his three women, he knew he could say anything.

He sat on the edge of his bed, and they gathered around him, their presence a silent, supportive circle.

“Okay, Jack,” Sarah said gently. “We’re all here. What really happened?”

He took a shaky breath and began to tell them everything. He told them about the private tour, the dark hallway, and the long lunch where Delores had so expertly lowered his guard. And then he got to the hardest part, his voice dropping, his gaze fixed on the floor.

“She took me to this empty apartment-style dorm,” he stammered. “And she… she led me straight to the bedroom. She said we could… you know… do things there.” He couldn’t bring himself to say the word sex. “I think… I think she was going to let me have a highly inappropriate encounter with her.”

He recounted the kiss, the disorienting speed of it all, and the final, horrifying moment of clarity. “I looked in her eyes,” he whispered, “and there was nothing there. It was like she was looking through me. Like I wasn’t even a person. I was just… a thing she could use.”

Sarah, Molly, and Clare exchanged horrified, angry glances over his head.

“So I stopped it,” Jack finished, his voice trembling slightly. “I told her I had to go and I left.”

“Jack, that’s not just ‘intense’,” Sarah said, her voice tight with a protective fury. “That’s predatory.”

“You absolutely did the right thing by leaving,” Clare added, her own voice shaking with anger on his behalf.

He finally looked up at them, his eyes full of confusion and self-doubt. “I know. But then she showed up at the rec center, with that story about her ex, and she was crying… and I just… I couldn’t be a jerk,” he explained, the words tumbling out. “It’s my character flaw. I wouldn’t know how to hurt someone, how to just tell her to go away, even when my gut was screaming at me.”

“That’s not a flaw, Jack,” Molly said softly, putting a hand on his knee. “That’s your kindness. And she used it against you.”

He had told them everything, and they didn’t judge him. They understood. Their faces weren’t filled with disappointment, but with a fierce, unified resolve. They knew the danger was real, and they knew it was up to them to be the wall he needed.

Seeing his vulnerability and the shame warring with his relief, Sarah knew what he needed most. “Oh, Jack. Come here.”

She moved to sit beside him on the bed, and Molly and Clare closed in, their own arms wrapping around him and Sarah. They enveloped him in a gentle, protective group hug, a circle of unwavering, unconditional love. They didn’t care that he had been manipulated or that he felt flawed; they just cared that he was safe. In that moment, surrounded by the three most important women in his life, Jack finally let the last of his tension go. He was loved, no matter what.
