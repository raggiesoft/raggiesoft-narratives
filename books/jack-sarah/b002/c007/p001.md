# Part 1: A Mission for the Sister

A week or so after the disastrous dinner date, on a Saturday afternoon, Jack, Molly, and Clare made a special trip to the Dominion Tower Mall in Norfolk. The official mission: find a perfect 23rd birthday present for Sarah. Her 23rd was a big deal, not just because it was her birthday, but because she had also just been officially promoted to Head Lifeguard at Fairlawn, and they wanted to get her something that celebrated both milestones. They had chosen this specific mall because it had a specialty outdoor gear store that wasn't in any of the malls closer to their Virginia Beach home, and they were on the hunt for a specific high-end pair of polarized sunglasses Sarah had been wanting.

They walked through the bustling mall concourse as a single, cohesive unit. Jack was in the middle, his arms draped comfortably over his cousins' shoulders. Molly and Clare, in turn, each had an arm wrapped around his waist, their linked arms and easy strides a testament to their deep, platonic affection. They were a small island of familial comfort in the sea of weekend shoppers.

"Okay, so what are we thinking?" Clare asked, her voice bright with the energy of the mission. "She's been dropping hints about needing a new, really good pair of sunglasses for when she's on the main stand."

"That's a good idea," Molly added, her analytical mind already working. "But we should also get her something fun. Something just for her, not for work. She loves that author who writes those cozy mystery novels."

"Both are great ideas," Jack said, feeling more relaxed and himself than he had in weeks, safely buffered by his two favorite cousins. "We can hit the sunglass store first, then the bookstore."

Their shopping trip was a success. At the outdoor gear store, they found the exact stylish but durable pair of polarized sunglasses they knew Sarah wanted. At the bookstore a little while later, they found the latest novel by her favorite mystery author. With their mission accomplished and their stomachs starting to rumble, a new consensus was quickly reached.

"I'm starving," Jack announced. "Food court?"

"Definitely," Molly and Clare said in unison. "It's on the third floor, right?"

"Yep," Jack confirmed. "Let's go."

They made their way towards the escalators, still walking in their familiar, linked formation.
