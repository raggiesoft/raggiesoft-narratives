# Part 2: A Taste of Twin Magic

“Okay,” Molly said, “it’s going to be impossible for all three of us to navigate that line and find a table. You two go scout for a place to sit. I’ll be the food warrior and get our order.”

“Sounds like a plan,” Jack said. “Usual for everyone?”

“You know it,” Molly confirmed with a smile before heading into the fray at the popular burger counter.

Jack and Clare, meanwhile, began to navigate the crowded seating area. Jack put his arm around his cousin’s shoulders, leaning on her for a bit of support in the noisy crowd. Clare responded in kind, wrapping her arm around his waist.

They were just about to claim a newly vacated table when a voice, dripping with false sweetness, cut through the food court chatter.

“Well, hello, Molly.”

Jack and Clare froze. It was Delores. She stood directly in front of them, her eyes fixed on Clare with a look of pure disdain.

*Unbelievable,* Delores thought, a surge of resentment rising in her. *Still attached at his hip, I see.*

The young woman with Jack looked at Delores, her expression one of polite, genuine confusion. Clare knew exactly who this was from Jack's stories and the family debriefs, but this was her first time laying eyes on the infamous Delores. A spark of mischief and protective anger lit within her; she decided to play dumb. “I’m sorry, have we met?” Clare asked, her tone open and friendly.

Delores’s smile faltered. She looked from Clare's face to Jack's. This was a trick. It had to be. "Very funny," she scoffed. "You know we met. At my apartment. You're Jack's cousin, Molly."

Clare just tilted her head, her expression still one of complete, innocent bewilderment. She was enjoying this immensely. “Molly?” she repeated, her brow furrowed in feigned thought. “I’m sorry, I don’t think I know a Molly. Is she a friend of yours?”

“Stop it,” Delores hissed, her frustration growing. “You look exactly like her.”

“I do?” Clare said, her eyes wide with feigned surprise. “Wow, that’s so weird! I’ll have to tell her you said that, if I ever meet her. Small world!”

Delores stared, her mind unable to process the situation. Was this girl really this dumb? Or was she an incredible actor? Her manipulative plan was dissolving into a puddle of pure, flustered confusion. She was about to say something truly nasty when a new voice came from behind her.

"Hey guys, sorry that took forever. The line was insane."

Delores froze. She turned around slowly. Standing there, holding a tray laden with burgers and drinks, was Molly. She took in the scene in a split second: Delores, furious and bewildered; her identical twin sister, Clare, looking the picture of wide-eyed innocence; and Jack, standing by, a barely suppressed grin on his face. Molly immediately understood what was happening. *Oh, I see,* she thought, a spark of amusement in her eyes. *Clare is playing Twin Magic. Perfect. Let's help her out.*

She looked at Delores with the same cool, analytical gaze Delores remembered from the dinner. "Oh," Molly said, her voice perfectly neutral. "Hello again." Then, she deliberately shifted her energy, mimicking her sister's more open and proactive demeanor. She turned to Clare, her expression bright and engaging. "Clare, you will not *believe* who I just saw over by the pretzel stand!"

Delores’s brain seemed to short-circuit. She looked from Molly to Clare, then back to Molly. They were identical. Their voices were identical. And now, the one she thought was the quiet, analytical one was acting just as bubbly as the one pretending to be a complete airhead. She couldn't tell them apart at all.

Jack, who could easily see the subtle differences in his cousins' base mannerisms, was thoroughly enjoying watching them get one over on Delores. He decided to play along.

"So, which one of you is driving us home?" Jack asked, looking from one twin to the other with a playful, questioning look, as if he himself couldn't tell.

"I am!" they both said in perfect, cheerful unison.

Delores stared, completely adrift in a sea of identical faces, voices, and now, mannerisms. The ground beneath her feet felt like it had vanished. She had come here to assert control, to manipulate, and instead, she had been rendered utterly powerless by the simple, baffling existence of two people who looked and sounded exactly the same.

"I... you..." she sputtered, her face flushing a deep, embarrassed red.

Clare took the tray from Molly. "Well," she said brightly, turning back to Delores, her act of innocent confusion dropped now that the prank was complete. "It was... interesting to meet you. We should get our table before our food gets cold."

With that, the three of them—Jack flanked by his two identical, smiling cousins—simply walked away, leaving Delores standing alone in the middle of the food court, completely disarmed, utterly humiliated, and finally, blessedly, defeated.
