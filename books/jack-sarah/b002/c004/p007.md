The apartment was stunning. Jack had expected a typical dorm room—cinderblock walls, a communal bathroom down the hall. Instead, he found himself in a fully equipped, modern apartment with a small kitchen, a living area, and a separate bedroom. He was genuinely wowed.

“This is part of the university?” Jack asked, impressed.

“Yeah. Nice, isn’t it?” Delores said. She didn’t show him the kitchen or the living room. Instead, she led him straight to the bedroom and patted the neatly made bed. “We could do things in here together, since no one is here.”

The suggestion was so direct it took Jack’s breath away. A current of heat went through him, a simple, primal response. This was the kind of thing that happened to other people, not to him. But here was this pretty, intense girl, offering him an escape from the overwhelming day. He was caught in the momentum, a passenger on a ride she was driving.

He let her pull him down to sit on the bed. “I guess I don’t want to push you too far,” he said, but the protest was weak, and he knew it.

“You’re not pushing me,” Delores said, her eyes locked on his. “I’m pulling you.”

She leaned in and kissed him. It wasn’t gentle; it was deep and immediate, a kiss that was trying to stake a claim. For a moment, Jack was lost in it, kissing her back as she guided him to lie back on the unfamiliar mattress.

Things moved quickly then. With a practiced speed that made his head spin, Delores straddled his hips and pulled her VDU polo shirt over her head in one smooth motion, tossing it aside. She was topless now, leaning over him, her hair falling around her face. She was breathing heavily, a triumphant little smile on her lips as she waited, expecting him to admire her, to be overcome with desire.

And in that split second, with her leaning over him, Jack’s eyes focused on hers. He was expecting to see passion, or excitement, or maybe even the same nervous curiosity he felt.

He saw none of it.

What he saw was a terrifying, hollow intensity. He saw a hunger that had nothing to do with him. Her eyes weren't looking at *him*, but *through* him, as if he were just a solution to a problem she had. The realization hit him with the force of a physical blow: *She doesn’t even know my last name.*

The heat vanished, replaced by an ice-cold dread. This wasn’t connection. This was a transaction. He pulled her closer for one more kiss, but it was different this time. He felt the aggressive pressure of her tongue, and it confirmed everything. The intimacy was a performance.

“Wait,” Jack said, his voice coming out hoarse. He put his hands on her shoulders, gently but firmly stopping her.

Delores froze, her smile faltering. “What? What’s wrong?”

“Stop. Just… stop,” he said, sitting up and swinging his legs off the bed. The air in the room suddenly felt thick and suffocating. He reached for his jacket.

“I don’t understand,” Delores said, her voice shifting from seductive to sharp and pouty. “I thought you wanted this.”

“I was wrong. This is… this is too fast,” he said. He pulled out his phone, turning away from her and quickly sending a text to Clare: Ready to be picked up.

“Too fast?” Delores let out a short, bitter laugh. “There’s no such thing.”

“For me, there is,” he said, finally turning to face her. She was still sitting on the bed, her arms crossed over her chest, watching him with a look of cold, profound injury. “I have to go,” he said softly. “My ride is almost here.”

He didn't wait for her reply. He walked out of the dorm, leaving her alone in the silent apartment. As Delores, now completely alone, reached for her shirt, she realized her mistake. She had pushed too hard, too fast. But it was too late. He was already gone.
