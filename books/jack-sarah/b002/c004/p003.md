# Part 3: An Unexpected Guide

Jack found an empty bench outside the Sinclair Center and collapsed onto it, the exhaustion from the tour washing over him. He just wanted to sit quietly and wait for Clare.

“You look like you’ve been through the wringer,” a voice said.

He looked up. A young woman in a VDU polo shirt and khaki pants was standing in front of him, a look of friendly concern on her face. She looked like one of the official tour guides.

“Is there anything I can help you with? Any questions I can answer?” Delores asked, sitting down beside him without waiting for an invitation. She launched into a series of well-rehearsed, generic questions about his interests, seeming to answer his unspoken questions about the university with an easy confidence. Jack, still overwhelmed, was just grateful for the quiet, one-on-one attention after the chaos of the group tour.

After talking for a few minutes, Jack asked, “Where is the nearest restroom?”

A small, predatory glint appeared in Delores’s eyes, so quick that Jack missed it. “This building right next to us. Chemistry. Here, I’ll show you,” she said, her voice full of helpfulness. She led him to the doors, and as they stepped inside, the bright January sunlight was replaced by a deep, disorienting gloom. She escorted him down a hallway that was almost completely dark. “They’ve been trying to fix the lights in here for a while now,” she said, her voice a low murmur in the darkness.

“I can’t see very well,” Jack said, instinctively slowing down.

This was her plan. She knew this hallway was unlit, a pocket of manufactured privacy in the middle of a busy campus. She had thought, in the anonymous dark, she could press him against a wall, let things happen, see how far he would go. It was her first attempt to test his boundaries. But she felt him tense up beside her, his discomfort a palpable thing in the enclosed space. She immediately backed off. It was too soon to show her true colors.

“Here, just hold on to my arm,” she said, her voice all business. “The men’s room is just down here on the left.”

She legitimately guided him through the darkness to the restroom door and waited for him in the hallway while he was inside. When he came back out, she led him back towards the exit. They stepped out of the building and back into the bright, sunny afternoon. Jack had to stop and blink for a moment, his eyes struggling to adjust from the profound darkness to the brilliant light, feeling disoriented and even more exhausted than before.
