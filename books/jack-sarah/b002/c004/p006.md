# Part 6: A Private Tour

"Come on," Delores said, a conspiratorial smile on her face as she stood up from the cafeteria table. "I want to show you one of the best-kept secrets on campus. It's much quieter than this."

Lulled into a sense of comfort after their long lunch conversation, Jack followed her without question. They left the noise of the Sinclair Center behind, walking down a path that led away from the main academic quad and towards a quieter, more residential part of the university. The crowds thinned out, and the feeling of being at a massive open house event began to fade, replaced by a sense of quiet intimacy.

Delores kept up a steady, charming stream of conversation, pointing out different buildings, but her focus remained entirely on him. She walked a little closer than a normal tour guide would, her arm occasionally brushing against his.

They stopped in front of a handsome brick building that looked more like a modern apartment complex than a traditional dorm. It was quiet, with no students coming or going.

"This is one of the newer residential halls," she explained. "Apartment-style. It's mostly for upperclassmen."

She led him to a side door, pulling a student ID card from her pocket. Jack watched, a little impressed. He didn't realize the tour guides had that kind of access.

She swiped the card. The lock beeped and clicked open.

"The university keeps a few of the rooms unassigned, as model apartments to show on tours like this," she said smoothly. "Come on, I'll show you."

She pushed the door open and gestured for him to go inside, a triumphant little smile on her lips. Jack stepped out of the bright afternoon sunlight and into the quiet, still air of the empty building, following her lead.
