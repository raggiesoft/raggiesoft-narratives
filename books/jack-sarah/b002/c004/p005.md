# Part 5: Lunch in the Lion's Den

Feeling disoriented from the sudden shift from darkness to bright sunlight, Jack was about to suggest they head back to the Sinclair Center when Delores put a light hand on his arm.

“I don’t know about you, but all that walking made me starving,” she said with an easy smile. “The open house is providing lunch for everyone in the main cafeteria. My treat. Come on, it’ll give us a chance to talk more.”

Before Jack could protest, she was gently guiding him toward another large building. His defenses were down, and the idea of a simple, public lunch felt safe enough. Inside, the cafeteria was a loud, bustling space. Delores navigated the lines with a practiced ease, and soon they were sitting at a small table in a slightly quieter corner, trays of surprisingly decent food in front of them.

Here, in the bright, open space, Delores turned the full force of her attention on him. The tour guide persona was completely gone, replaced by a captivating intensity. She asked him about his family, his friends, his favorite classes at the community college. She leaned in when he spoke, her eyes locked on his, making him feel like he was the only person in the crowded room.

She was a master at creating a bubble of intimacy. When he mentioned his love for astronomy, she didn’t just nod; she asked him to explain the difference between a nebula and a galaxy. When he talked about the comfort of working with his sister at Fairlawn, she didn’t belittle it; she sighed and said how lucky he was to have a family that close, her voice tinged with a carefully crafted hint of sadness that made him feel a pang of sympathy for her.

For the first time all day, Jack felt his exhaustion and anxiety begin to melt away. He was used to being the quiet one, the observer. He wasn’t used to being the subject of such focused, intelligent interest. He found himself opening up, talking more than he had all day, lulled into a sense of comfort by her seemingly genuine fascination with him. He was so engrossed in their conversation that he didn’t notice she was subtly isolating him, creating an "us against the world" feeling right there in the middle of a crowded cafeteria.

“This has been… amazing,” Delores said as they were finishing, her voice a low, intimate murmur. “I feel like I could talk to you for hours. It’s so loud in here, though.” She leaned in a little closer. “I know a place we could go that’s a little more private, where we could actually hear each other think.”

Jack, caught in the web of the connection she had so expertly woven, heard himself agree before he’d even had a chance to think about it. The guard he had up just an hour ago was now completely, foolishly, down.
