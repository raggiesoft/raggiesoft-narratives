# Part 1: A Day of Dread and Duty

The alarm clock on the nightstand blared at 6:15 AM, a harsh, unwelcome sound in the quiet darkness of the bedroom. A groan came from the middle of the queen bed. Jack blindly fumbled for the snooze button, but another hand shot out from beside him and slapped it into silence.

“Time to get up,” Sarah murmured, her voice thick with sleep.

Jack just groaned again, pulling the covers a little tighter. For the past few weeks, ever since the disastrous date, Sarah had been sleeping in bed with him. Her presence was a profound comfort, a solid, safe anchor against the anxiety that had been his constant companion. But this morning, even her closeness couldn’t completely ward off the specific dread he felt for the evening to come: the family dinner at the Peking Duck Inn, where Delores would meet everyone for the first time.

“Come on,” Sarah said, sitting up. “Big day for both of us.”

She was right. They got up and began the quiet, practiced routine of getting ready. Jack pulled on a pair of blue jeans and a fresh, city-issued rec center t-shirt, while Sarah layered comfortable sweatpants and a matching t-shirt over her city-issued swimsuit. She slipped on a pair of flip-flops, her standard work footwear that allowed for a quick transition in case of a water rescue.

As they headed downstairs for breakfast, Sarah, walking a step behind her brother, noticed him pause for a moment at the top of the stairs, his hand gripping the railing a little tighter than usual before he carefully made his way down. It was a subtle sign of his ongoing struggle, a flicker of the physical challenges he faced every day.

They took turns using the bathroom, then Sarah quickly poured two bowls of cereal and two glasses of milk, a simple breakfast for a complicated day. They ate in the quiet of the new kitchen.

“Nervous?” Jack asked.

“A little,” Sarah admitted, swirling the milk in her cereal bowl. “Excited, too. It’s my first time as the acting Aquatics Supervisor for a full day. I just want everything to go right.” She looked at him. “What about you? You’re shadowing Donald for opening and closing procedures today, right?”

Jack nodded, pushing a spoonful of cereal around his bowl. “Yeah. I’m a little nervous about that. It’s a lot of responsibility.” But that wasn’t what was really bothering him. The thought of the dinner that night was a heavy weight in his stomach.

Sarah, ever observant, saw the look on his face. “It’ll be okay, Jack. The dinner, I mean. We’ll all be there. We’re your wall.”

He gave her a small, grateful smile. They finished their breakfast and grabbed their things. At the door leading to the garage, Jack paused and turned to his sister, pulling her into a hug.

“You’re going to be a great boss today,” he said.

“And you’re going to be a great supervisor-in-training,” she replied, hugging him back tightly. “Now let’s go. Don’t want to be late.”

They got into the car, and Jack, as the designated driver for the morning, started the engine and pulled out of the driveway, heading for another fateful day at Fairlawn.
