# Part 8: A Dinner on Thin Ice

Simon returned from the kitchen a few minutes later, the wonderful aroma of the food preceding him. He placed a steaming bowl of wonton soup and a plate of crispy shrimp egg rolls in front of Jack, a familiar, comforting sight. He then served the other Bennetts their own "usuals" before setting a plate of what Delores had ordered in front of her.

"Enjoy, everyone," Simon said with a warm, genuine smile for the family and a polite, professional nod for their guest. He then engaged in a bit of quick, easy banter with Jermaine and Sam, a comfortable back-and-forth that was full of shared history and in-jokes that Delores couldn't possibly follow. She just watched, a tight, forced smile on her face, feeling more like an outsider than ever.

As the family began to eat, the conversation flowed freely again, but Delores barely touched her food. She was on high alert, acutely aware that she was on very thin ice after her earlier outburst. Her mind was a chaotic jumble. She had to be polite, had to be charming, but the sight of Molly and Clare, sitting side-by-side in their identical outfits, was deeply unsettling. She remembered the mall, the dizzying confusion of the "Twin Magic" prank, and a fresh wave of resentment and frustration washed over her.

She struggled to keep her composure, forcing herself to take small, meaningless bites of her food, pecking at it like a bird. But it wasn't just the social tension. Every few minutes, a subtle, sharp grimace would cross her face, and she would shift her weight in the chair, looking physically uncomfortable. At one point, she stopped eating entirely, her hand going instinctively to her lower abdomen, pressing firmly against her waistband as she squeezed her eyes shut for a second.

Molly, ever the analyst, noticed. Seated directly across from Delores, she had a perfect view of the other woman's tense posture and untouched plate. She watched Delores wince again, her knuckles white as she gripped her napkin under the table.

She's not eating, Molly thought, logging another mental note. And she’s in pain.

Molly observed the sudden pallor in Delores's cheeks and the way her irritability seemed to spike every time she shifted in her seat. The pieces clicked into place—the short fuse earlier, the snapping at Jermaine, the physical discomfort now.

Mental Note \#6: She’s starting her period. And judging by the look on her face, it was a bad one.

Molly took a sip of her tea, keeping her expression neutral. It wasn't exactly polite dinner conversation to point out that their guest was dealing with menstrual cramps, especially with her dad and uncle right there. She kept the observation to herself, but it added a layer of context to the evening. Delores wasn't just mean; she was miserable.

Delores was caught in a trap of her own making. She had to be the perfect, polite girlfriend in front of Jack's father, uncle, and grandfather, but the very presence of his happy, close-knit family, especially the identical twin cousins who had so recently humiliated her, was a constant, grating irritant. The festive, happy atmosphere of the restaurant only served to highlight her own profound sense of isolation and anger.
