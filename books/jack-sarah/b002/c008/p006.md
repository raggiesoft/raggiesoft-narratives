With the public cleared from the natatorium, Sarah and her team spent the next two hours on the grueling decontamination process. They skimmed, treated, and shocked the pool with high levels of chlorine, following the city's health and safety protocols to the letter. By around 4:00 PM, they had done everything they could. Now, all that was left was to let the powerful filtration system cycle the treated water overnight.

Having managed her staff—dismissing those who wanted to go home early and finding shifts at other centers for those who needed the hours—Sarah had one last supervisory duty. She headed downstairs to the administration wing to debrief the acting Facility Supervisor, Rebecca, a friendly Event Coordinator from Rec Services who had been assigned the role for the day. Together, they meticulously filled out the incident report for Sarah's boss, Scott, detailing the time, the nature of the contamination, and the precise steps taken for remediation.

At 4:30 PM, the report was finished. With a half-hour left on her own shift, Sarah felt a powerful pull to be near her brother. She walked out to the front lobby and pulled up a spare stool behind the counter.

"Pool's all treated," she announced to Donald as she settled in. "Thought I'd come hang out down here until close. Be an extra set of hands."

"Happy to have you," Donald said with a smile.

She spent the rest of the afternoon there, a quiet, supportive presence. She watched Jack as he greeted patrons, his face a pale mask of polite professionalism. She could see the deep weariness in his eyes, the same exhaustion from their lunch break, and her own anxiety simmered just below the surface. She was glad she was here, close by, keeping a watchful, loving eye on the brother she knew was still struggling with a sense of doom, an anxiety she hoped would vanish the moment they finally dealt with Delores for good.

At 4:45 PM, Jean’s voice came over the PA system, her tone clear and professional: "May I have your attention please: the time is now 4:45 PM and Fairlawn Recreation Center will be closing in 15 minutes. If you need to make arrangements for a ride, please do so at this time. Again, the time is now 4:45 PM and Fairlawn Recreation Center will be closing in 15 minutes. If you need to make arrangements for a ride, please do so at this time.”

As the last few patrons began to gather their things, Donald started the initial closing procedures for the registers. At 5:00 PM on the dot, Jack, as part of his training, made the final closing announcement over the PA system. While Donald locked the main doors, Jean, her shift now over, was dismissed for the day.

“Alright, Jack,” Donald said, “time for the final lesson of the day: closing report.”

He showed Jack how to count down his own drawer, then how to consolidate the day's total cash and check intake. Together, they filled out the closing report, and Donald had Jack accompany him to the safe to watch as he signed the logbook, a final act of passing on the responsibility.

At 5:06 PM, the report was filed and the safe was locked. “That’s us,” Donald said. “You’re all set, Jack. Go on home.”

He met Sarah, who had been waiting patiently after being officially dismissed by Rebecca a few moments earlier. They walked to the time clock together, and they both clocked out at 5:12 PM. As far as the city's payroll system was concerned, with its rounding to the nearest quarter-hour, they had both finished at 5:15 PM.

They walked out to the car together, the chilly February air a welcome change. True to her word, Sarah got into the driver's seat. She drove them away from the rec center and towards the Peking Duck Inn, where the rest of their family was already waiting. As they pulled into the bustling parking lot, they saw Delores getting out of her mother's car, her face a mask of polite neutrality. The stage was set for the first, and final, meeting between Delores and the Bennett clan.
