# Part 10: A Safe Harbor at Home

The drive back from the Peking Duck Inn was quiet. The festive atmosphere of the restaurant, a place that usually felt like a second home, had been soured by Delores’s tense, resentful presence. Back at the house, the three of them—Jermaine, Sarah, and Jack—retreated to the living room, the unspoken weight of the evening settling around them.

Jack sank into the corner of the sofa, looking pale and utterly drained. Sarah immediately sat beside him, pulling him into a tight, protective hug. He leaned into her, his head resting on her shoulder, a shudder running through him.

"I'm scared of her, Sarah," he finally admitted, his voice a muffled whisper against her shirt. "The way she looked at me… at all of us. I’m really scared."

"I know, Jack. We all saw it," she said, her arms tightening around him. "But you're not alone in this. We won't let her hurt you."

Jermaine, who had been watching them from his armchair, leaned forward, his expression full of a father's fierce, gentle love. "Son, listen to me," he said, his voice a comforting rumble. "I know tonight was hard. But I want you to remember what we're building. In just a couple of weeks, we'll be in the new house. You'll have the Quad, that whole amazing space you helped design. You, Sarah, Molly, and Clare, all together."

Jack considered the plan. The Quad. A sanctuary built for them, by them. It would give him all the safe, non-sexual female contact he could ever want, with his three most favorite women in the world. He thought of their easy hugs, their shared laughter, the uncomplicated comfort of their presence. That was safety. That was home.

And yet, Delores represented something different, something he thought he was supposed to want, something he could never receive from his sister or his cousins: romantic affection. But after tonight, after seeing the coldness in her eyes, the possessiveness, the barely concealed rage… was this what he really wanted? The contrast between Delores's toxic intensity and the pure, unconditional love he felt from his family was a chasm.

A wave of profound exhaustion, deeper than anything he'd felt before, washed over him, making it hard to even think. "I… I think I need to go to bed," he mumbled, looking at Sarah. It was only 7:22 PM, far earlier than his usual 9:30 bedtime, but the emotional and physical toll of the day had completely spent him.

"Okay, Jack. Let's get you to bed," Sarah said immediately, her voice soft with concern.

She helped him up from the sofa and followed him into the bedroom that had, over the past few weeks, become their shared space. As Jack began to get ready for bed, Sarah noticed he was so exhausted that his movements were clumsy, his hands struggling with the buttons on his shirt. She made a quiet mental note of his profound fatigue as she stepped in to help him get changed into his pajamas, her worry for him a sharp, persistent ache.

A few minutes later, they were both in the queen bed. Jack rolled onto his side, his back to her, a silent indication of his vulnerability. Sarah moved in behind him, wrapping her arm around his waist and pulling him into a secure, comforting embrace, spooning him just as she had on other difficult nights.

"Try to rest," she whispered into his ear. "And remember the promise of the Quad. A place with all of us. A place where you're safe. You could never, ever have that with Delores."

Jack didn't respond, but she felt the tension in his shoulders begin to release as he leaned back into her embrace. She held him, her own heart heavy with worry, and together, in the quiet darkness of the room, they both slowly, finally, drifted off to sleep.
