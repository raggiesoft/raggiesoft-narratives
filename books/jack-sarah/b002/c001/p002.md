# Part 2: The First Night

The hotel room was simple, clean, and dominated by two large queen beds. Jermaine dropped his bag by the bed closest to the window, a silent claim. “Alright, I’m going to grab a shower,” he announced, his voice weary from the drive and the emotional weight of the last few months. “You two get settled.”

As their father disappeared into the bathroom, Jack and Sarah began to unpack their own small bags by the other bed. The unspoken arrangement hung in the air: this would be their bed for the trip. Jack was quiet, seemingly lost in his own world as he arranged his things, but Sarah was acutely aware of him. The memory of the hug in the lobby was still a warm, solid presence in her mind. It was a small, simple moment, but it had changed something for her. She was genuinely looking forward to climbing into bed with him, to the uncomplicated comfort and safety of his presence beside her.

A little while later, all three of them were in their pajamas. Jermaine was already in his bed, reading a book under the dim light of the bedside lamp.

“Okay, dork,” Sarah said to Jack with a playful smile, pulling back the covers on their bed. “Time to hit the hay. We’ve got a big day tomorrow.”

Jack climbed in, and Sarah slid in beside him, clicking off the main lights so only their father’s small lamp remained. The room fell into a comfortable, quiet darkness. As she settled under the covers, she felt Jack shift. He rolled onto his side, his back to her, but she could feel the quiet tension in his shoulders. Without a word, she moved a little closer, draping an arm gently over his side and pulling him into a soft, comforting cuddle. She felt him relax instantly, his body melting into her embrace with a quiet sigh of relief.

He was her brother, her rock through the storm of their parents’ divorce. And as she held him in the quiet darkness of the hotel room, she knew she was his rock, too.
