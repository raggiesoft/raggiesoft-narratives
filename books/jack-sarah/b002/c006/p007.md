# Part 7: A Theory is Formed

They retreated to the privacy of Jack’s bedroom, the four of them creating a familiar, safe circle. Jack recounted the final, manipulative moments at the apartment door, and the girls listened, their expressions growing grimmer with every word.

“Okay,” Sarah said, pacing the floor after he finished. “So, what is her deal?”

“Well,” Clare chimed in, looking thoughtful, “maybe she *did* have a really bad breakup, and she’s just so messed up from it that she’s become super possessive herself. It’s sad, but it also makes her dangerous for you to be around, Jack. She clearly needs therapy, not a new boyfriend.”

“I don’t think she’s just messed up,” Sarah countered, her voice sharp with a protective fury honed by what she had witnessed at the rec center. “I think she’s a user. She saw you, Jack, saw how kind and maybe a little overwhelmed you were, and decided you were an easy target. That whole story about the ‘controlling ex’ is a line, a script she uses to make her targets feel sorry for her. She doesn't want a boyfriend; she wants someone she can control. She’s mad at us because she knows a strong family is the one thing that can see through her act.”

This theory felt chillingly accurate, a close second place to the real truth. It explained Delores's behavior perfectly based on what Sarah had seen. But Molly, who had been quiet, processing all her mental notes from the dinner, had one more piece to add.

“Okay, this is going to sound crazy,” she began, “but what if you’re both right? What if she’s a user, like you said, Sarah… but what if her story about the controlling ex is true, she just got the roles mixed up?”

The other three looked at her, confused.

“Think about it,” Molly continued, the theory taking shape. “What if *she* was the one who was super controlling? What if she was the abuser, and this guy—whoever he was—finally managed to get away from her?”

A stunned silence fell over the room as the pieces clicked into place.

“And now,” Molly finished, her voice barely a whisper, “she’s freaking out because she lost control. So she’s trying to find someone new—you, Jack—to be a replacement. She’s trying to do it all over again, only this time she thinks she can get it ‘right.’ It would explain everything—why she’s so intense, why she lies about everything, and why her mom is so angry. The last one ended in a huge disaster, and she doesn't want a repeat.”

Jack stared at his cousin, a chill running down his spine. It was a horrifying theory, but it made perfect, terrible sense. It explained the hollow, hungry look in Delores’s eyes. It explained the way her mother used him as a weapon. He wasn’t a person to them; he was a project. A do-over. The thought was sickening.
