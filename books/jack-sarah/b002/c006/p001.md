# Part 1: An Unassuming Dinner

The drive to Delores’s apartment was quiet, with Jack and his cousin Molly sharing the front seat of her car. As Molly pulled into a parking spot, she glanced at Jack. His face was a mask of polite neutrality, but she could see the tension in his shoulders. “You sure about this?” she asked softly.

Jack just shrugged, his eyes not meeting hers. “It’s just dinner. I told her I would.”

As they got out and walked towards the apartment building’s main door, Molly instinctively started to loop her arm through Jack’s, a familiar gesture of comfort and closeness between them. But she stopped herself, letting her hand drop to her side. Jack’s unease about this girl was palpable, and Molly needed to be an impartial set of eyes, taking mental notes without the distraction of their usual closeness. Her guard was already up on his behalf.

When they arrived, Delores met them at the door, her smile a little too bright. Inside, the apartment was meticulously clean but felt impersonal. An older woman with Delores’s intense eyes emerged from the kitchen.

“You must be Jack,” she said. “I’m Robyn, Delores’s mother. And you are?”

“My cousin, Molly,” Jack said. “She drove me.”

“Well, come in, both of you!” Robyn said with a practiced politeness that felt brittle. Molly was the only one who noticed it—a subtle, sharp tension emanating from Robyn that was directed not at her guests, but at her own daughter. She kept the observation to herself, another mental note in a growing list.

“You’re just in time. Delores, set another place for Molly. You have to stay and try my world-famous lasagna.”

“Oh, you don’t have to—” Molly started to protest.

“Nonsense!” Delores insisted. “Mom’s lasagna is legendary. You have to stay.”

Molly, not wanting to make a scene, reluctantly agreed. They ate in the small dining area. As Robyn served the main course, Molly noticed the foil tray it came from—a family-sized portion from the freezer aisle at ValueMart. She kept a neutral expression, but made a mental note. *Mental Note \#1: "World-famous" lasagna comes from the freezer aisle.* Jack, oblivious, simply accepted the food.
