# Part 6: The Debrief

In the quiet cocoon of Molly’s car, the tension from the apartment finally began to dissipate, replaced by a shared, grim understanding. Molly waited until they were a few blocks away, the building's entrance no longer visible in the rearview mirror, before she spoke.

“So,” she said, her voice low. “That was… something.”

Jack let out a long, shuddering breath he didn’t realize he’d been holding. “Something is one word for it. Her mom is really weird, Molly. The way she was asking about Dad… the look on her face… it was so creepy.”

“I saw it,” Molly said, her knuckles white on the steering wheel. “The whole thing felt like a performance. Let’s call the others. They need to hear this.”

Molly gestured to the flip phone in the center console. Jack picked it up, held down the side button, and waited for the beep. “Call Sarah,” he said. The phone chirped and began dialing, the call immediately coming through the car’s speaker system.

“Hey, you guys on your way?” Sarah’s voice came through first. “We’re on speaker,” Clare added.

“Yeah, we’re heading home now,” Molly said. “And listen, the date was a total disaster. I was taking mental notes the whole time.”

“Mental notes?” Clare asked, intrigued.

“Yeah,” Molly said, her voice grim. “Note one: the ‘world-famous’ lasagna was from the ValueMart freezer aisle. They lie about small, pointless things. Note two: Robyn, the mom, is still furious with Delores over something big, and she kept taking passive-aggressive shots at her all through dinner. Note three: Robyn is using Jack to punish Delores. She kept praising him for being responsible and then giving Delores a look like, ‘Why can’t you be like him?’ Note four: Robyn completely controls the conversation and has zero interest in anything Delores has to say.”

“Wow,” Sarah breathed over the speaker. “What a mess.”

“It gets worse,” Jack said, his voice quiet. “I have a mental note of my own. When Molly hugged me goodbye, Delores had this look on her face… like she was furious. Like she hated that Molly was even touching me.”

“I saw that too!” Molly confirmed. “Her eyes were like ice. Which brings me to Mental Note number five: Delores is possessive and she actively belittles your family. She made some snide comment about you needing your own life and not just being tied to us.”

A cold silence fell on the speakerphone.

“Okay, that’s it. You’re done with her,” Sarah said, her voice tight with a protective fury.

“I know,” Jack said, his voice hardening with resolve. “When I end it, I’m going to need backup. I’m going to need your help, all three of you, to tell her it’s over. She’ll just start crying or twist it around like she did tonight. I need her to see a wall, so she knows there’s no way back in.”

“Jack, you just say the word,” Clare said, her voice fierce. “We will be your wall.”

“Always,” Sarah and Molly said in unison.

The conversation shifted to Jack’s request for comfort that night and Sarah’s immediate, loving agreement to stay with him. He felt the tension he’d been holding since walking into that apartment finally begin to release. He had told them everything, and now, he had a plan, and an army at his back.

Jack stared out the passenger window as Molly drove, the streetlights blurring past. The quiet cocoon of her car was a sanctuary, but the creepy feeling from the apartment still clung to him like a film.

“Hey, Mol?” he said, his voice quiet. “Tonight was… really bad. It’s one of those nights. I… I’m going to need someone with me tonight. I know we don't do it all the time, but..."

Molly’s expression softened with immediate understanding. This wasn't a routine request; this was a signal of deep distress. “Of course, Jack,” she said gently. “Don’t even worry about it. Here.” She gestured to the flip phone in the center console. “Call her.”

Jack picked up the phone, held down the side button, and waited for the beep. “Call Sarah,” he said, his voice steady despite the tremor he felt inside. The phone chirped and began dialing, the call immediately coming through the car’s speaker system.

“Hey, you guys on your way?” Sarah’s voice came through. “We’re on speaker,” Clare added, her voice just as clear.

“Yeah, we’re heading home now,” Molly said. “Listen, you two, the date was a total disaster. I was taking mental notes the whole time.”

For the next ten minutes, they recounted the entire evening for Sarah and Clare—the freezer-aisle lasagna, Delores’s possessive comments, and the deeply unsettling interrogation from Robyn about their father.

“I knew it,” Sarah said, her voice tight with anger. “I knew she was trouble. I’m so sorry you had to go through that, Jack.”

“He’s pretty creeped out,” Molly confirmed. “It’s one of those nights, Sarah. He’s going to need you.”

There was no hesitation on the other end of the line. Sarah’s response was immediate and warm. “Understood. Poor guy. Don’t worry about a thing. I'll get his full-sized bed ready for us. I’ve got him.”

A wave of profound relief washed over Jack. He wasn't just being granted a favor; his need for safety was being met without question. A quiet, sobering thought settled in his mind: after tonight, he didn’t think he could face sleeping alone again for a long, long time. This was no longer going to be an occasional thing; from now on, he knew he would always need one of his three favorite women by his side.

“Thanks, Sarah,” he said, his voice thick with emotion.

“Always, Jack,” her voice came back, a warm promise through the car’s speakers. “See you in a few.”
