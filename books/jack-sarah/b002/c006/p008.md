The weight of the day's events, from the unsettling encounter with Delores to Jack's emotional confession, settled over the four of them in the quiet of his bedroom. The horrifying truth of Molly's theory hung in the air, solidifying their collective resolve.

"Well," Molly said, finally breaking the silence as she stood up, "it's getting late, and we should probably head home."

"Are you going to be okay?" Clare asked Jack, her expression full of concern.

"I will be now," Jack said, and he meant it. He felt seen, understood, and protected.

Molly came to the side of the bed and leaned down, giving him a gentle but firm hug. "You call us for anything," she whispered. "We're your wall, remember?"

Clare hugged him next, just as fiercely. "We love you, Jack."

"Love you guys too," he murmured. "Thanks for... everything."

Sarah walked them to the door, and the sisters exchanged quiet, knowing looks of support before Molly and Clare headed home. When Sarah returned, the room felt even more like a sanctuary.

"Ready to call it a night?" she asked softly.

Jack just nodded, the exhaustion of the day finally catching up to him. They went through the simple, quiet motions of getting ready for bed. Sarah made sure he had his water on the nightstand and that his pillows were comfortable. She changed into her own pajamas, the easy, matter-of-fact lack of privacy a new and comforting normal for them.

She slid into the queen bed beside him. From this night forward, this would be their routine. One of his three trusted females would always be there, a steady presence in the dark. Tonight, it was Sarah.

He rolled onto his side, and she immediately moved in behind him, spooning him in a protective, comforting embrace. She felt the tension in his shoulders begin to melt away as he leaned back into her.

"Try to get some sleep," she whispered into his hair.

"Okay," he murmured, his eyes already drifting shut. For the first time since he'd walked into that dorm room at VDU, he felt completely and utterly safe.
