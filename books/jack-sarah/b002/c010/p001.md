# Part 1: Off to Work

Jack woke up slowly, the grogginess of sleep giving way to a dull, anxious weight in his chest. For a moment, he didn't know where he was. Then he felt a gentle, steady breathing beside him and remembered. He was in his own bed, and Sarah was there, a solid, comforting presence beside him. The memory of the the creepy interrogation, Delores’s manipulative tears, and the horrifying accuracy of Molly’s theory—came flooding back. He was grateful he hadn't been alone.

They got ready for work in a quiet, shared rhythm. The new normal of their shared space was unspoken but understood. Sarah drove them to Fairlawn, the Stark Paladin a familiar bubble of safety against the world.

As she pulled into a far-off parking spot, she glanced at her brother. He had been quiet the whole ride, staring out the window.

“Hey,” she said softly. “You with me?”

Jack took a deep breath. “The other night… hearing what you guys thought… it made it all real. I want to end it, Sarah. I really do. But I’m scared of what would happen. I'm scared of *her*.”

“I know,” Sarah said, her voice full of a grim understanding. “After what you and Molly told us, we’re all scared for you. She's not just a creep, Jack. She's a predator. We saw how she reacted to Molly just hugging you. She has a huge problem with anyone being close to you.”

“So what do I do?” he asked, his voice small.

“For today, we just get through work,” Sarah said, her tone firm and reassuring as they got out of the car. “Tonight, we'll make a solid plan with Molly and Clare. All of us together. We’re your wall, remember?”

“Agreed,” Jack said, a little of the tension leaving his shoulders. “Just us. No more her.” He offered his sister a hug before they headed for the door, a gesture of gratitude and solidarity.

Jack had about fifteen minutes before his 9:00 AM shift. He put his lunch in the break room fridge after greeting his boss. Sarah, with a long wait until her 10:30 AM shift, headed to the aquatics office to stow her things and check the pool’s chemical balance logs. Jack made his way to the restroom, then filled his water bottle. The usual things.

He clocked in and made his way to the front desk to begin his day. He was assigned the scan station for now, with plans to be put on a register later when another worker arrived. Just another typical day at the front desk.

He started greeting patrons as he always did, his voice polite and steady, soon settling into the familiar routine that had worked well for him for the past year and a half. Nothing out of the ordinary on the surface, but underneath, he was counting the hours until he could be with his family again, safe and away from the threat that now had a name and a face.
