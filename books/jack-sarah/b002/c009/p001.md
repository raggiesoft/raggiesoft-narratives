# Part 1: A Morning Shrouded in Fog 🥣

The morning of Monday, February 12th, arrived not with the usual cheerful chaos of their shared home, but with a quiet, heavy stillness. Sarah woke first, a habit born from years of being her brother's protector. She turned her head on the pillow in the

full-sized bed they now shared and looked at Jack. He was still asleep, but it wasn't a restful sleep. His breathing was shallow, and his brow was furrowed, as if he were wrestling with something even in his dreams. The restless, anxious night had taken its toll on both of them.

"Jack," she said softly, gently shaking his shoulder. "Time to get up. We have that stats class this morning."

He groaned, the sound muffled by his pillow. It took him a long, weary moment to push himself into a sitting position. As he reached for the jeans and t-shirt laid out for him, Sarah noticed his movements were clumsy and slow. He managed the jeans, but when he picked up his shirt, his fingers fumbled with the buttons, unable to guide them through the holes. His mild cerebral palsy always seemed more pronounced when he was exhausted, but this was different. His hands were trembling slightly.

"Here, let me," Sarah said, her voice even and matter-of-fact. She moved in front of him and quickly buttoned his shirt, her own anxiety simmering just below the surface.

This whole thing with Delores is tearing him apart, she thought, her worry solidifying.

Downstairs in the kitchen, their father, Jermaine, was already at the table, sipping a cup of coffee and looking through the morning paper. He looked up as they entered, his smile fading slightly as he took in his son's appearance.

Jack walked over to the refrigerator and reached for the gallon of milk, but his grip was unsteady. The heavy container wobbled precariously in his hand.

"I've got it," Sarah said, quickly taking it from him before he could drop it. She placed two bowls on the counter and grabbed his favorite cereal from the pantry. She poured a bowl for him and one for herself, then added the milk, performing the simple task he’d done for himself a thousand times before.

"You alright there, son?" Jermaine asked, his voice laced with concern. "You look completely wiped out".

Jack just shrugged, sinking into a chair at the table. "Just tired," he mumbled.

He stared down at his favorite cereal, picked up his spoon, and began pushing the colorful loops around in the milk in slow, swirling patterns. He didn't take a single bite.

Jermaine caught Sarah's eye, his expression grim. "That dinner last night really did a number on him," he said in a low voice, certain he was identifying the source of his son's distress.

Sarah nodded, her own face tight with worry. "I know, Dad. He barely slept. The stress of that girl... it's just eating him alive". They both watched Jack poke at his untouched breakfast, their shared worry settling between them like a heavy weight. For them, the conclusion was as logical as it was obvious: the ugly confrontation with Delores was taking a severe toll on him, and they would have to help him through it.
