# Part 2: A Day of Confusion (Monday, February 12<sup>th</sup>, 2007)

The day after the disastrous dinner at the Peking Duck Inn, the entire family was on high alert. The morning routine was quiet, a stark contrast to the usual cheerful chaos. Jack was withdrawn, the profound exhaustion from the weekend clinging to him like a shroud. He picked at his breakfast, his appetite gone, a symptom Molly and Sarah exchanged a worried look over. They both attributed his lack of hunger to the immense stress of the Delores situation .

At the rec center, the day was a struggle. It was a short-staffed Monday, with just Jack on a register and Jean at the Scan Station, while Kaitlyn, Jennifer’s assistant, stayed in the back office, observing how Jack handled the responsibility of running the desk largely on his own. Jack, usually so patient and steady, found himself easily irritated by the normal, everyday demands of the front desk. The constant phone ringing, the cheerful chatter of patrons, the bright fluorescent lights—it was all a low-grade sensory assault that left his nerves feeling raw and exposed. He was clumsy, dropping a stack of registration forms and fumbling with the cash drawer, his fine motor skills seemingly betraying him. Everyone assumed he was just reeling from the awful family dinner.

That evening, the family gathered, their conversation circling around how to best support Jack in the upcoming, inevitable breakup. In the middle of their discussion, Jack's phone rang. His face went pale when he saw the caller ID. It was Delores.

He retreated to his and Sarah's shared bedroom for a semblance of privacy. "Hello?" he answered, his voice barely a whisper.

"Hey," Delores's voice was a purr on the other end, completely ignoring the tension from the previous night. "I was just thinking... with Valentine's Day coming up on Wednesday, we should do something really special. Just us. No family." Her words were carefully chosen, a gentle poison. "I found this romantic little Italian place. We could have a proper date, just the two of us, and really connect, you know?"

The suggestion caught Jack completely off guard. A fancy Italian dinner? This was the girl who seemed to subsist on VDU cafeteria food and whose financial situation was, to put it mildly, precarious. The gesture was so out of character that it could only be a performance, a grand, manipulative attempt to love-bomb him.

His stomach churned. It was a test. A clear attempt to isolate him from the protective "wall" of his family. But he was trapped. He hadn't executed the breakup plan yet, and to refuse a Valentine's Day date would surely provoke a fight he didn't have the energy for.

"Okay," he heard himself say, the word tasting like ash. "Yeah, that sounds... nice."

He hung up, the feeling of impending doom now a suffocating certainty. He had just agreed to walk into the lion's den, alone. When he told his sister and cousins, their faces were a mixture of fury and concern, but they understood his predicament. The plan shifted: he would endure the Valentine's dinner, and they would be his immediate backup, ready for his call the second it was over .

That night, sleep offered no escape. He was plagued by restless anxiety, waking up several times in a cold sweat, the feeling that something terrible was about to happen so potent it felt like a physical presence in the room. Sarah, sleeping beside him, would murmur his name, and he would focus on the sound of her steady breathing, the only thing that could anchor him in the terrifying darkness.
