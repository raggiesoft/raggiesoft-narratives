The very next day, Monday, Jack felt strong enough for another important outing. The visit to Fairlawn had bolstered his spirits, and he knew he had to take care of his college paperwork. He, Sarah, and Grandpa Sam drove to the Community College of Hampton Roads campus in Virginia Beach.

The walk from the parking lot was long, and Jack, still easily exhausted, draped his arm over Sarah’s shoulders, leaning on her for support. Sarah walked steadily, letting him set the pace, her presence a solid anchor.

As they approached the main entrance of the building, a familiar figure exited, nearly colliding with them.

It was Delores.

She stopped short, a flicker of surprise followed by a familiar look of cold disdain crossing her face. Jack, Sarah, and Sam all tensed. But this time was different. They were not just facing a manipulative ex-girlfriend; they were facing a known predator, the abuser of an unknown boy named Leo, and their stance was no longer just protective—it was a silent, unified wall of absolute condemnation.

Delores didn't try to win Jack back. She just looked at Jack, pale and weak, with his arm draped over his sister’s shoulders, and a cruel, snide smirk twisted her lips.

“Well, look at this,” she said, her voice dripping with venom. “Still can’t stand on your own two feet without your big sister holding you up? Some things never change.”

Before Jack or Sarah could even react, Grandpa Sam stepped forward, his calm, steady presence suddenly radiating an unshakeable authority. He didn't raise his voice. He didn't need to. He was looking at the person who had tormented his grandson and who had admitted to tormenting another vulnerable boy before him. His words were quiet, but they carried the weight of that horrifying knowledge.

“And some things do, young lady,” Sam said, his voice quiet but cutting. He looked her directly in the eye. “He is standing on his own two feet, surrounded by a family that loves him. That is something you clearly know nothing about. Now, I suggest you walk away and do not look back.”

The words were a final, absolute dismissal. Delores stared at the three of them—Jack, silent and resolute; Sarah, protectively supporting her brother; Sam, an immovable patriarch whose eyes held a cold fury she hadn't seen before. There was no weakness to exploit. A cold dread, a feeling she remembered all too well, began to creep up her spine. This felt familiar. This felt like the Porters. This was another family that would close ranks, another family that would protect their own. And she knew what came next.

*They’ll get a restraining order,* she thought, the fear sharp and immediate. *Just like that psycho Stella Porter threatened to. They’ll do it, too.* The fight was over before it began. As much as she wanted a boyfriend, she knew better than to mess with Jack again.

For the first time, she truly admitted defeat. In her mind, a switch flipped. *Fine. It's over. He's old news anyway.* Her obsession snapped back with a vengeance to its original target. *It's time to get Leo back.*

Without another word, Delores turned on her heel and stormed away, finally and forever out of their lives. Jack watched her go, a profound sense of relief washing over him. He leaned a little more heavily on Sarah, and together, the three of them turned and walked into the administration building, leaving the past behind them.
