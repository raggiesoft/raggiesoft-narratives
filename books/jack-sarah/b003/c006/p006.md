The days following the final encounter with Delores were filled with a profound and healing peace. With the threat gone for good, the family could finally focus on the future they had planned. While Jermaine, Devin, and Sam handled the heavy lifting of moving furniture into the main house, the four cousins turned their attention to their own special project: transforming the converted garage into The Quad.

Jack, under strict doctor's orders for bed rest, was already installed in his designated spot on the magnificent, nine-foot-by-nine-foot Alaskan King bed. Propped up on a mountain of pillows, he was the undisputed project manager, a role he took on with a quiet, happy seriousness.

"Okay," he directed, pointing a slightly trembling finger. "That box has all my astronomy books. They go on the bottom two shelves of that bookcase, please. Heaviest ones on the bottom."

Sarah, Molly, and Clare worked as a seamless, efficient team. They unpacked boxes, assembled flat-pack furniture, and began to transform the large, empty room into a warm, lived-in space that reflected all four of their personalities. And at every step, they included Jack.

"Jack, where do you want the lamp?" Clare would ask, holding up two different options.

"Which poster should go over the main desk?" Molly would inquire, showing him the choices.

It was a slow, happy process. From time to time, one of them would notice Jack starting to look a little tired or withdrawn, a spectator rather than a participant. In those moments, they would stop whatever they were doing.

"Okay," Sarah announced during one such lull. "Huddle up. Mandatory grounding session."

The three of them would climb onto the massive bed, carefully navigating around Jack's tender incision, and envelop him in a gentle, warm group hug. They'd rest there for a moment, a silent transfer of love and reassurance, making sure he felt not just wanted, but essential to the very fabric of their new home.

He lay there on the bed, unable to lift a single box, yet feeling more a part of something than he ever had in his life. He watched his three most favorite women in the world bustle around him, their easy laughter and cheerful chatter filling the air. They weren't just unpacking boxes; they were building their fortress, their sanctuary, their future. And he was right in the very heart of it.
