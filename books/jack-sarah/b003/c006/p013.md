# Part 13: The Biological Deadline

The room was quiet, save for the hum of the mini-fridge and the soft, rhythmic breathing of the four cousins settled in the massive bed.

Jack was safe, warm, and surrounded by his wall. But across the mattress, on the far left, Molly was staring at the ceiling, her mind whirring like a computer processor working through a complex algorithm.

She couldn't sleep. The "mental notes" she had collected—the dinner, the confrontation at the hospital, Delores's scream about "precious sisters"—were swirling, trying to form a cohesive picture. There was one variable that still didn't fit: the speed.

"Jack?" Molly’s voice cut through the darkness, soft but sharp with intent.

Jack stirred, turning his head on the pillow. "Yeah, Mol?"

"That day at the university," she said, propping herself up on one elbow. "The 'Private Tour.' You said she took you to the apartment and tried to initiate things... immediately. Like, within hours of meeting you."

"It was sooner than that," Jack whispered, the memory surfacing with a cold clarity he hadn't fully processed until now.

Molly paused. "What do you mean?"

"Before the apartment," Jack corrected her, his voice trembling slightly. "Before lunch, even. We were in the Chemistry building. She took me down this hallway that was completely pitch black. She said the lights were broken."

He swallowed hard, remembering the suffocating darkness and the sudden, predatory shift in the air.

"I felt her stop," he continued. "She got really close. I think... I think she wanted to do something right there, against the wall. But I tensed up because I couldn't see, so she backed off and took me to the apartment instead."

Molly sat up fully, her eyes adjusting to the dim light. This new data point changed the equation. It made it worse.

"She didn't even wait for a bed," Molly murmured, horrified. "She tried to initiate sex in a hallway minutes after meeting you?"

"Yeah," Jack said. "It felt... desperate."

"That's because it was," Molly said, her voice clinical but grim. "Manipulators usually play the long game. They groom you. But this? This was a blitz. And I think I know why."

Sarah sat up, adjusting her glasses. "What are you thinking?"

"I'm thinking about deadlines," Molly said. "Why risk scaring him off in a dark hallway with zero buildup unless she had to? Unless there was a clock ticking that we couldn't see."

Clare sat up on the far right. "What kind of clock?"

"A biological one," Molly stated. "I’ve been counting the days backward. The open house was late January. The dinner where she was so irritable and snapping at everyone was mid-February... about two weeks later."

She looked at Sarah. "You know how miserable she looked at the Peking Duck Inn? Cramping, shifting in her chair?"

"Yeah," Sarah realized, her eyes widening. "She was starting her period."

"Exactly," Molly said. "Which means two weeks before that... during the hallway incident and the apartment... she wasn't just 'horny,' Jack. She was ovulating. I’d bet my life she was in her fertile window that exact weekend."

A heavy silence fell over the bed as the implication landed.

Jack felt a cold chill that had nothing to do with the temperature of the room. "She wanted... a baby?"

"She wanted a receipt," Clare said darkly. "She wanted an anchor. Just like she thought about in the car."

"But why rush?" Jack asked. "Why not wait until we were actually dating?"

"Because of Leo," Molly said, connecting the final dot. "Think about what she yelled at the hospital. She hated his sisters because they 'interfered.' She said she had him 'under her thumb' but she never locked it down. Why?"

"Guardianship," Sarah breathed. "We talked about this. If Leo was under legal guardianship, she couldn't marry him. It wouldn't be legal. She couldn't get her claws into the money that way."

"Right," Molly continued, her blue eyes hard. "So when she met you, Jack... she didn't know. She saw you—kind, disabled, quiet—and she didn't know if you had that same legal protection. She didn't know if Dad or I had papers on you."

"She was racing against a lawyer that didn't exist," Clare realized, disgusted. "She needed to get pregnant that day. If she had a baby, she figured it wouldn't matter what the legal papers said. A baby is a biological trump card. It forces the families to deal with her."

Jack stared at the poster of The Silver Gauntlet above his head. The "Private Tour" wasn't seduction. It wasn't even about him. It was a heist. She was trying to steal his future before his family could lock the door.

"That's it, isn't it?" Jack whispered, the final piece of his trauma clicking into place. "Sex isn't intimacy for people like her. It's a trap. It's how they get around the family."

"It's the Trojan Horse," Sarah agreed, reaching over to take his hand again, squeezing it tight. "Once a partner enters the picture that way, they have rights. If she had gotten pregnant... she would have had a claim on you that rivaled ours. She could have fought Dad. She could have fought us."

"But she failed," Molly said with a fierce satisfaction. "She miscalculated. She didn't get the baby, she got her period, and she lost you."

Jack looked at his sister, then at his cousins. He thought about the "Vestal Virgins" and their vow to protect the flame. He looked at the massive bed they shared, the "no-privacy" safety they had built.

"We keep the door locked," Jack said, his voice finding a new, unshakeable strength. "No sex. No romance. No loopholes. Nobody gets a claim on us but us."

"Agreed," Sarah said.

"Agreed," the twins echoed.

It wasn't a restriction; it was a liberation. They had identified the weapon the enemy used, and they had collectively decided to disarm it forever.

"Okay," Molly said, her analysis complete, her mind finally at rest. She lay back down, pulling the covers up. "Now we can sleep. The puzzle is solved."

Jack lay back, feeling lighter than he had in months. The predator's logic had been exposed, and in doing so, their own resolve was set in stone. They were a closed system. And they were safe.
