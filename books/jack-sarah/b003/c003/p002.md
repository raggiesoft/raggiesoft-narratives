# Part 2: Off to the Operating Room

The morning of the surgery was a quiet, tense vigil in Jack's hospital room. Jermaine, Sarah, Grandpa Sam, and Uncle Devin were all gathered, their conversation soft and subdued, trying to keep a brave face for Jack. The door opened, and Delores swept in, a look of practiced concern on her face.

"I came as soon as I could," she announced, placing her purse on the sofa. "I was worried sick all night. How are you feeling, baby?"

A wave of forced politeness went through the family. They knew what was coming, that Jermaine would be handling this in a few short hours, and they were all determined to respect Jack's wish to avoid a pre-surgery confrontation. Their happiness at seeing her was a carefully constructed mask.

"Hi, Delores," Sarah said, her voice neutral.

"Delores, good morning," Jermaine added, his smile not quite reaching his eyes.

Jack managed a weak smile from his bed. "Hey. I'm okay. Just nervous."

Delores moved to his bedside, placing a proprietary hand on his arm. "Of course, you are. But I'm here now. We'll get through this."

Just then, the door opened again. The stretcher arrived, and the transport techs worked to get Jack from his hospital bed to the gurney. Delores got up forcefully, her arms crossed, as if the entire process were a personal imposition. Sarah, Jermaine, and Grandpa Sam rose quietly to follow.

“Well, this is it,” Delores said, her voice laced with an impatient attitude. “We will probably not see you until tomorrow.”

“Yeah,” Jack said, his gaze avoiding hers. “They say you’ll be allowed to see me after surgery, but I won’t know you’re here.” The hospital staff began to wheel him down the hall. He looked past Delores, his eyes finding his sister’s. “Sarah? Promise you will be here when the hospital says I wake back up?”

“Promise,” Sarah replied, her voice unwavering. “I wouldn’t miss it for the world.”

She took her brother’s hand as they entered the elevator, walking with him and the transport team. Delores, forced to stand close, shot Sarah an angry glare, which both Sarah and Grandpa Sam noticed. Jack, wanting to maintain the peace, gently tried to take Delores’s hand as well, but she snatched it away.

“He wants to hold your hand too,” Sarah whispered. “He wants one last chance before surgery.”

Delores rebuffed him again, and for the first time, Jack felt a profound sense of detachment. He didn’t care.

As they walked the final stretch towards the surgical suite, Delores’s anger boiled over. “Why didn’t you hold my hand in there?” she asked forcefully.

“He offered to hold your hand,” Sarah said calmly.

They arrived at the large double doors of the operating room. "This is as far as family can go," the transporter said.

"One last hug," Sarah said to her brother.

He hugged Delores first; it was stiff and brief. Then he turned to Sarah, and his arms wrapped around her fiercely, lingering because he didn’t like how Delores had snapped at him.

The doors opened, and he was taken into the operating room. As he was wheeled away, he thought he heard a commotion start behind him—Delores’s sharp voice, “What do you mean?” followed by the low, firm, and unmistakable sound of his father’s voice. Then the doors swished shut.

The world inside was cold and smelled of antiseptic. A huge light loomed over a narrow table. A woman with kind eyes leaned over him. “Hi, Jack. I’m from anesthesia. I just need you to count backward from ten for me.”

He stared up at the blinding light, his last conscious thought of his sister’s promise. *Sarah will be there when I wake up.*

“Ten… nine…” he began, his voice muffled by the oxygen mask. The beeping of the monitors seemed to grow distant. “Eight… sev…”

The edges of his vision softened, and the bright light dissolved into a peaceful, welcoming darkness.

The surgical waiting room was a sterile, quiet pocket of anxiety. Jermaine, Sarah, Grandpa Sam, and Uncle Devin sat in tense silence, their eyes fixed on the digital board that read "BENNETT, JACK - IN SURGERY.” Delores sat apart from them, furiously flipping through a magazine, radiating an impatient energy that was starkly out of place.

After letting her stew for a few minutes, Jermaine knew it was time to honor his son's request. He stood and walked over to where she sat.

"Delores," he said, his voice low and firm. "We need to talk."

She looked up, her expression a mixture of impatience and defiance. "What?"

"On Jack's behalf," Jermaine continued, his gaze unwavering, "I need to tell you that your relationship is over. He's made it clear that he does not want to see you again. He has asked that you not return to his room after this.”

Delores stared at him, her face slack with disbelief for a second before it hardened into a mask of pure rage. "You can't be serious," she hissed, getting to her feet. "You can't do that! He's in surgery! He's scared! He needs *me*!" 

Jermaine stepped forward, his voice low and devoid of any warmth, cutting her off before Devin could even speak. "No," he said, his gaze unwavering. "He needs his family. And let me tell you something else, young lady, something everyone in this family knows perfectly well. When my son wakes up from this surgery, if only one person is allowed in that room, it won't be me, his own father. The first face he is going to want to see is Sarah's. His sister."

Delores recoiled as if struck, the statement a direct and undeniable hit against her perceived importance. The thought that she, the girlfriend, would be secondary not only to the family in general but specifically to a mere sister—and that his own father knew and accepted this—was a profound insult that made her seethe with a fresh wave of impotent fury.

"This is you!" she shrieked, her rage now finding its original target, pointing a trembling finger at Sarah. "You and those cousins! You've been poisoning him against me from the start! You can't stand that he has someone else!" 

"Young lady, you will lower your voice," Grandpa Sam said, his tone like granite. "This is a hospital. Our boy is on an operating table.”

His calm authority seemed to enrage her further. "Don't you talk down to me!" she yelled, her control completely shattering. "You have no idea what I've been through! I had everything perfect, and it all got ruined!" In her anger, the carefully constructed victim narrative she'd fed to Jack began to unravel, the truth spilling out in a torrent of rage.

"I had Leo right where I wanted him!" she screamed, the words raw and full of a venom that shocked them. "Completely under my thumb, and his precious sisters ruined everything! I will NOT let you people do it to me again with Jack!"

The Bennetts exchanged confused, alarmed glances. "Who the hell is Leo?" Sarah asked, her voice a sharp whisper. The name, completely unknown, hung in the air. But as she said it, a cold dread washed over her. She remembered the conversation in Jack’s bedroom just a few nights ago, the four of them trying to make sense of Delores's behavior. She remembered Molly's horrifying theory: *What if she was the abuser, and this guy—whoever he was—finally managed to get away from her?* In that moment, hearing the raw, possessive fury in Delores's voice as she spoke that name, Sarah knew. *Oh my God,* she thought, her blood running cold. *Molly was right. She was a monster to him, too.*

Delores froze. She saw the looks on their faces—the confusion hardening into dawning horror. She realized her mistake. She had said his name. She had said too much, her rage betraying her. Her anger dissolved instantly, replaced by a desperate, calculated panic. She crumpled, her face dissolving into tears.

“No, wait,” she sobbed, the tears now flowing freely. “I… I didn’t mean that. I’m just so upset, I don’t know what I’m saying! I’m just so scared for Jack…”

But it was too late. The mask had slipped, and they had all seen the monster underneath.

Grandpa Sam stood up, his tall frame radiating a final, absolute authority. He was completely unmoved by her crocodile tears. “Get out,” he said, his voice dangerously quiet.

“But I… I love him…” she whimpered, trying one last time.

“I am not going to ask you again,” Grandpa Sam stated, his tone like granite. “Either you walk out of this waiting room on your own, or I will pick up that phone and have hospital security escort you out. The choice is yours.”

The tears stopped instantly. Delores’s face, stripped of all pretense, twisted into a mask of pure hatred. With a final, hate-filled glare that promised a bitterness that would never fade, she snatched her purse and stormed out of the waiting room.

The family was left in the ringing silence, shaken but resolute. Their worry for Jack was now compounded by the terrifying knowledge of the monster he had narrowly escaped.

Jermaine was the first to break the silence, his voice low and bewildered. He looked at Sarah. "Who in God's name was she talking about? Who is Leo?"

Sarah shook her head, her expression grim. "We don't know. But we had a theory about her." She looked at Molly and Clare, who both nodded for her to continue. The three of them quickly recounted the conversation they'd had in Jack's room just a few nights before, sharing their theories with the men.

"I thought she was a classic user," Sarah explained. "Someone who targets kind people, tells them a sob story about a 'controlling ex' to get them to lower their guard, and then moves in to take control."

"And I thought maybe she was just really broken from a bad breakup," Clare added. "Someone who was so hurt that she became possessive herself."

Jermaine and Devin listened, their expressions hardening. Then Molly spoke, her voice quiet but firm. "It was both of those things," she said. "But worse. I told them… what if her story was true, but she just got the roles mixed up?"

The three men looked at her, confused.

"Think about what we saw," Molly continued, laying out the evidence. "Her mom is furious with her over some big, 'costly' mistake from her last relationship. Delores is possessive, she lies about small things, and she can't stand seeing Jack show affection to anyone else. And then just now… the way she talked about that boy, Leo. 'Completely under my thumb.' She wasn't the victim in that story. She was the abuser. And when he got away, she went looking for a replacement. She went looking for Jack."

A heavy, horrified silence fell over the waiting room as the full, ugly truth of Molly's theory settled upon them. It wasn't just a theory anymore. They had all just heard the proof spill from Delores's own mouth.

"My God," Jermaine breathed, running a hand over his face. "So she wasn't just a bad girlfriend. She was... hunting."

"She was," Grandpa Sam said, his voice a low rumble of protective fury. "And she's not getting anywhere near our boy ever again."

The family sat together in the sterile waiting room, more united than ever. Their shared worry for Jack on the operating table was now fused with a grim, collective relief. He was free. He was safe. And they were all there to make sure he stayed that way.
