# Part 1: Serious Health Discussion

The non-emergency ambulance dropped Jack off at the sprawling main Heart Hospital for the region. The transfer was a blur of new hallways and new faces, but it wasn’t too long after arriving in his new, private room that his father and sister were there with him again.

Jermaine looked around at the advanced monitoring equipment, his expression grim. “This must be serious if you’re at this hospital,” he said, his voice low. He sat down beside his son’s bed. “Jack, the doctors are saying you’re going to be getting bypass surgery, like Uncle Devin had.”

The words settled heavily in the quiet room. Bypass. Jack stared at the ceiling, the full weight of his situation pressing down on him. In the face of something so real, so life-altering, all the other anxieties of the past few weeks suddenly seemed small and foolish. The stress, the confusion, the dread he felt around Delores… it was a distraction from what truly mattered. His family. His life. The clarity was sharp and absolute. He found his courage.

“Dad,” Jack said, his voice quiet but firm. “There’s something else. I feel like I’ve had it with Delores. I’m not happy anymore.”

He turned his head to look at his sister, who was standing on the other side of his bed. “Sarah, being in the hospital with you yesterday… it made me realize something. The way Delores is, how she acts… it felt like a sick, twisted version of what a family is supposed to be. I felt like I was getting to know my own sister for the first time, and it made me realize just how wrong and unhealthy my relationship with Delores felt.”

Sarah’s eyes filled with relieved tears. She reached out and squeezed his hand. “Oh, Jack. I’m so sorry you went through that with her, but I’m so glad you see it now. We all did.”

Jermaine let out a long breath, a look of profound relief on his face. “Son, I’m glad to hear you say that. I haven’t liked the way she’s treated you from day one. If you want to leave her, you are welcome to come home any time. With the move, the new house will have plenty of space. You are always welcome,” he said, his voice thick with emotion. “I could never leave my child hanging like that.”

“I want to come home – without her – when this hospital discharges me,” Jack said, the decision feeling like a massive weight lifting off his chest. “But Dad… I can’t face her. Not now. I’m scared of what she’ll say, how she’ll react. Can you do me a favor? While I’m in surgery tomorrow… can you be the one to break the news to her? Tell her it’s over, and that she’s not to come back here.”

Jermaine’s expression hardened with protective resolve. “Consider it done, Jack. You don’t worry about her for one more second. You just focus on getting through tomorrow. I will handle Delores.”
