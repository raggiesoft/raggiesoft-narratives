# Part 5: The Transfer

The call came the next morning: a bed had opened up at the Heart Hospital. The news was both a relief and a fresh wave of anxiety for the Bennett family, who had gathered again in Jack’s room.

A nurse brought Jack one last meal at the current hospital—another bland, uninspired breakfast tray. He ate what he could, the conversation around him quiet and subdued. Soon, a transport team arrived with a gurney, their movements efficient and professional. It was time to go.

As they prepared to move him, Jack looked at his family gathered around his bed—his father, his sister, his uncle, his grandfather, and his cousins. He felt a surge of love and fear.

“Wait,” he said, his voice quiet. He pushed himself up slightly. “One last hug.”

One by one, they came to him. Molly and Clare, their hugs quick and fierce. His Uncle Devin, a firm, steadying embrace. His Grandpa Sam, a warm, reassuring hold. His father, Jermaine, hugged him tightly, whispering, “We’ll be right behind you, son. Every step of the way.”

Finally, Sarah. She held him for a long moment, a silent transfer of strength and love. “We’ll be there before you know it,” she promised.

With that, the transport team moved him to the gurney and began wheeling him out. The family followed him down the hallway and to the ambulance bay. They watched as he was carefully loaded into the back of a non-emergency medical transport ambulance—a simple, efficient vehicle, not one for a crisis in progress.

The doors closed, and the ambulance pulled away, its lights off. The family stood on the curb for a moment, watching it disappear into traffic.

“Alright,” Jermaine said, his voice firm, rallying his family. “Let’s go. We’ll meet him there.”

They turned and walked towards their own cars, a small, determined convoy preparing to follow their most vulnerable member to the next stage of his fight. They knew they wouldn't be able to see him right away, that they'd have to wait in another lobby until the hospital staff checked him in and the front desk finally had a room number for them, but they would be there.
