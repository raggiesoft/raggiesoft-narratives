# The Recovery of the Triplets

## The Surgical Intensive Care Unit

The transition from the 140-degree sulfurous hell of the deep-earth cavern to the sterile, climate-controlled Surgical Intensive Care Unit at Fairfax was not a peaceful fade-in. It was a slow, agonizing crawl through a fog of heavy narcotics and surgical trauma.

Drake woke up to the rhythmic, synthetic hiss-click of a ventilator and the steady, urgent beeping of his own heart monitor.

He forced his eyes open, his vision blurry and swimming.

"Ash..." Drake croaked. His throat was incredibly raw, his voice barely a sandpaper whisper. "Jess..."

A shadow moved beside his bed. It was Amanda. She looked completely exhausted, her eyes red-rimmed and her hair pulled back into a messy knot. She was wearing a sterile yellow hospital gown over her clothes, and the second she saw his eyes open, she let out a choked sob.

"Drake," Amanda whispered, gently resting her hand on his shoulder. "I'm here. You're safe. You're in the hospital."

"Where are they?" Drake asked, his breathing picking up. "Where are my sisters?"

"They're alive, Drake," Amanda said quickly. "Jessika is in a step-down recovery room on the fourth floor. Her fever broke last night. Ashleigh... Ashleigh is in the ICU room right next door to you. She just got out of her third surgery."

Drake let his head roll back against the pillows, a profound, heavy relief washing over him. They made it.

But as the initial adrenaline of waking up began to fade, the sheer, crushing reality of his own body crashed down on him. A deep, throbbing fire radiated from his lower half. He tried to shift his weight, but his legs felt completely immobilized, encased in what felt like solid concrete.

He looked back at Amanda. Through his blurry, drug-hazed vision, the crinkling yellow isolation gown she wore looked just like the PPE she wore at her clinic. He saw his LPN. He saw the person who had managed his medical care for the last three years.

"Manda," Drake rasped, his face contorting as a severe wave of pain hit him. "It hurts. The pain is... it's breaking through the meds. Can you check my line? Push something... please."

Amanda’s breath hitched. Her professional instinct—the instinct she had honed for years—screamed at her to reach for his IV pump, to read his charting monitor, to push a bolus of fentanyl to stop his suffering. But her hands stayed firmly planted on the plastic rail of his bed.

"I can't, buddy," Amanda whispered, a tear slipping down her cheek as she reached out to gently stroke his damp hair.

Drake opened his eyes halfway, his brow furrowing in heavy, medicated confusion. "What? You're my nurse..."

"Not today," she said, giving him a sad, watery smile. "I'm not allowed to be your nurse here. I don't have privileges at this hospital, Drake. The doctors know I'm an LPN, but because we're family, there's a strict ethical lockout. I'm just a visitor today. I'm just your big sister."

Drake stared at her, the heavy narcotics making his thoughts move like molasses. "But..."

"It's okay," Amanda reassured him quickly, leaning over him to press the red call button pinned to his sheets. "I can't touch your meds or your machines, but your surgical team is incredible. I'm calling your actual ICU nurse in here right now, okay? They're going to fix the pain."

She grabbed his hand, squeezing it tightly as the call light above his door illuminated.

"I can't be your nurse today," Amanda repeated softly, her voice thick with emotion. "But I'm not going anywhere, Drake. I'm staying right here in this chair until we go home."

## The Prognosis

The heavy glass door to the ICU room slid open, and Dr. Victor Thorne walked in, followed closely by the lead orthopedic surgeon.

Victor looked a decade older. He had spent the last forty-eight hours pacing the surgical waiting room, acting as the family's fierce protector while the federal government completely commandeered the hospital wing. President Sterling had kept his word: Uncle Sam was paying for absolutely everything. The Department of Defense had authorized unlimited funding for their care, flying in top military trauma surgeons and securing the entire intensive care floor with federal agents.

But as the surgeon stepped up to the foot of Drake's bed, Victor’s posture slumped. All the federal resources in the world couldn't rewrite physics.

"Drake, I am Dr. Aris," the surgeon said, his voice gentle but completely unvarnished. "You are a very brave young man. What you and your sisters survived down there... it's a medical miracle you are awake right now."

Drake looked down the length of his bed. For the first time, he realized he couldn't feel the heavy blankets resting over his lower half. There was just a dull, distant numbness from his thighs down, trapped beneath massive, rigid external fixators—heavy metal halos drilled directly into the bone to hold his legs together.

"When that iron valve buckled, it didn't just break your bones, Drake," Dr. Aris explained gently. "The sheer kinetic force of the heavy pipe completely pulverized your tibia and fibula bones. Furthermore, the extreme heat caused severe thermal and hypoxic death to the muscle tissue and nerve clusters. We spent twelve hours in the operating room doing everything medical science can do."

Dr. Aris paused, letting the silence hang in the room.

"The bones are shattered beyond medical repair. The nerve damage is permanent. You and your sister Ashleigh both suffered the exact same trauma. I am so sorry, Drake. Your legs are never going to heal. You will not be able to walk again."

The words hit the room like a physical shockwave. Amanda buried her face in her hands, weeping quietly. Victor stepped forward, placing a heavy, supportive hand on Drake's shoulder.

"How long?" Drake finally asked, staring blankly at the ceiling. "How long do we have to stay here?"

"A long time," Dr. Aris said softly. "You have severe internal tissue damage. You will be in this intensive care unit for at least three weeks. After that, you'll be transferred to an acute inpatient rehabilitation center. Realistically, Drake... you and your sisters will be living in this hospital for the next four to six months."

Four to six months.

Drake turned his head toward the wall that separated him from Ashleigh's room. "When we're off these machines," he said, his voice finding a shred of its former strength. "When we can finally sit up... I want to see them. If we're going to be here for months, we have to be together."

"I promise you," Victor said firmly. "The federal liaisons are already arranging it. The second you are stable enough to leave the ICU, they are locking down an entire rehab wing for the three of you."

## One Month Later: The Reunion

The federal government didn't knock down any walls, but they did completely take over the east wing of the Inpatient Rehabilitation floor in the Specialty Center.

It had been four agonizingly slow weeks of debridement surgeries, skin grafts, and fighting off systemic infections. But finally, the heavy respiratory ventilators were gone, and the triplets had been cleared to leave the ICU.

Federal agents stood guard at the double doors of the wing, having cleared out a massive, sunlit physical therapy lounge overlooking the Virginia skyline. The hospital staff had moved the heavy rehab equipment out, replacing it with comfortable seating, monitors, and three specialized medical beds arranged in a semi-circle.

Two nurses carefully wheeled Ashleigh’s bed into the lounge.

Drake was already there. His legs were still encased in heavy, rigid external fixators resting on elevated pillows, but the soot and grime of the mountain were long gone.

Ashleigh looked over at him, her own legs immobilized beneath pristine white blankets. She looked exhausted, her face pale, but the second her eyes locked onto her brother, a massive, genuine smile broke across her face.

"Hey," Ashleigh whispered, her voice still raspy.

"Hey," Drake smiled back, his eyes watering instantly.

Before either of them could say another word, a wheelchair rolled through the doorway. Jessika pushed herself into the room, wearing standard hospital scrubs. She had fully recovered from the heat stroke weeks ago, but she had outright refused to leave the secure floor until her siblings were brought up from the ICU.

Jessika wheeled herself right between their two beds, immediately reaching out and grabbing both of their hands.

"You're both here," Jessika said, fresh tears spilling down her cheeks as she squeezed their fingers. "We're all here."

Amanda and Erika stood in the doorway, watching the three eighteen-year-olds finally reunite. They had lost so much to the heat and the crushing weight of that mountain, but as Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika held onto each other in the quiet, sunlit room, the bond between the triplets had never been stronger. They had survived the end of the world together. Now, they were going to figure out how to live in it.
