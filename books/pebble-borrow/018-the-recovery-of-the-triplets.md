# The Recovery of The Triplets

## The New Reality

The secured third floor of the Specialty Center didn't look like a standard hospital wing.

When the federal government took over the inpatient rehabilitation floor, they completely customized it for the Wilcox triplets. The heavy, sterile equipment of the ICU was gone. In its place was a massive, brightly lit physical therapy space designed specifically for the grueling realities of spinal cord and catastrophic crush injury recovery.

Drake sat at the edge of a thick, padded mat table. He was wearing a grey t-shirt and loose athletic shorts that hung empty around his immobilized, braced legs. He was sweating profusely, his arms shaking violently as he tried to lift his own body weight using two heavy wooden push-blocks.

"Push, Drake. Lock your triceps," the lead physical therapist encouraged, hovering closely behind him. "You have to build that upper body strength. Your arms are your legs now."

Drake gritted his teeth, letting out a sharp grunt of exertion as he forced his elbows straight, hovering his hips an inch off the mat.

A few feet away, Amanda was watching him like a hawk.

She stood behind Ashleigh's specialized wheelchair, her hands gripping the push handles so tightly her knuckles were white. Amanda had always been the oldest sister, but when their parents died years ago, the dynamic had fundamentally shifted. She had stepped into a fierce, maternal role to keep the triplets together. Seeing Drake and Ashleigh—the two most physically active people she knew—completely paralyzed from the knees down was tearing her apart.

"He's pushing too hard," Amanda said nervously, taking a half-step toward Drake's mat. "He's going to tear a rotator cuff. He just got out of the ICU three weeks ago."

"Amanda, stop," Ashleigh said softly, reaching back to place her hand over her older sister's white-knuckled grip.

Ashleigh was strapped securely into her own chair, her shattered legs resting on elevated, padded leg-rests. She looked exhausted, but her eyes were sharp. "He has to do it. We *both* have to do it. If we can't lift our own weight, we can't transfer from a bed to a chair. You can't carry us forever."

Amanda swallowed hard, her eyes shining with unshed tears. "I would if I had to."

"I know you would," Ashleigh smiled faintly. "But you're our sister, not our nurse."

## The Father Figure

On the other side of the therapy gym, Jessika was sitting on a physical therapy stability ball, doing light core exercises. She had fully recovered from the systemic heatstroke, but she absolutely refused to leave the secured floor while her brother and sister were still inpatient. Where they went, she went.

Dr. Victor Thorne stood quietly near the large windows overlooking the Virginia skyline, watching the four of them interact.

He wasn't wearing his usual bespoke billionaire suits. He was in a simple sweater and slacks, looking every bit the exhausted, deeply devoted father he was. Victor had taken the Wilcox siblings in years ago when their parents passed, raising them as his own. He had given them a home, an education, and a future.

Now, he was watching them rebuild that future from scratch.

Victor walked over to the mat just as Drake's arms finally gave out. Drake collapsed back onto the padding, chest heaving, his face completely flushed. He looked down at his motionless legs, a flash of profound, bitter frustration crossing his face. He slammed his fist into the mat.

"Hey," Victor said gently, stepping up to the edge of the table. He didn't offer empty platitudes or tell Drake it was going to be okay. He just reached out and placed a heavy, grounding hand on his nephew's shoulder. "Breathe."

Drake looked up, his eyes locked onto the man who had raised him. "I can't feel them, Uncle Victor. I look down, and they're just... gone."

Victor knelt down, bringing himself to eye level with Drake, completely ignoring the physical therapist and the federal agents guarding the door.

"I know," Victor said, his voice thick with emotion. "And it is incredibly unfair. But you survived the fire, Drake. Both of you did. You survived losing your parents, and you are going to survive this. And you are not going to do it alone. I am retrofitting the entire B4 apartment. Ramps, widened doors, customized showers. Whatever you and Ashleigh need, it will be there."

Victor looked over at Ashleigh, Jessika, and Amanda, pulling them all into his steady gaze.

"We are a family," Victor promised them. "The mountain didn't break us, and this won't either."
