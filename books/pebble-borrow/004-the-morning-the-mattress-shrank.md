# The Morning The Mattress Shrank

The morning sun pushed through the thin motel blinds in pale, dusty stripes, landing directly across the queen‑sized bed where the fifteen-year-old triplets had spent the night in a tangled, uncomfortable knot. The air conditioner hummed unevenly, rattling every few minutes like it was debating retirement.

Drake woke first. Or rather, he woke because he was being actively crushed from both sides.

Ashleigh was sprawled across his left arm, her knee jammed sharply into his hip. Jessika was curled tightly against his right side, her forehead pressed into his shoulder, her hair tickling his nose. Drake’s legs—already prone to tight, painful morning spasticity—were twisted in opposite directions. One was pinned dead-weight under Ashleigh, the other hanging off the edge of the mattress entirely, the muscle cramping.

He groaned softly, trying to pull his numb arm free. "Ow."

The sound set off a chain reaction.

Ashleigh shifted, mumbling something incoherent, and blindly elbowed him in the stomach. Jessika, startled by the sudden jerking movement, kicked out reflexively and sent the scratchy motel blanket flying to the floor.

Drake winced, rubbing his ribs. "Okay. This is… not working."

Jessika blinked awake, rubbing her eyes and groaning. "Why does my spine feel like it folded in half?"

Ashleigh sat up slowly, her blonde hair sticking out in every direction, rubbing at the stiff muscles in her neck. "Why is my everything sore?"

Drake tried to stretch, but his cramped legs protested violently. "I think we’ve reached the limit of what this bed can handle."

Jessika looked down at the mattress—the rumpled sheets, the missing blanket, the way all three of them, now teenagers with longer limbs and broader shoulders, had drifted into one impossibly cramped pile in the center.

"We don’t fit anymore," she said, her voice dropping, sounding strangely betrayed by the physics of the situation.

For Jessika, it was a simple, frustrating observation of mass and surface area. But for Drake's autistic brain, the words *We don't fit anymore* didn't process as a spatial problem. They processed as a catastrophic severing of his pack.

Drake's breath hitched. The ambient noise of the rattling AC unit suddenly dialed up to a deafening roar in his ears. His chest tightened painfully. He pulled his stiff legs up to his chest, wrapping his arms tightly around his knees, his knuckles turning white. He started to rock, his breathing turning rapid and shallow.

*Don't fit. Not together. Splitting up. Losing them.* Across the room, Amanda stirred at the wobbly desk, lifting her head from her open LPN study guide. She blinked at the three of them, bleary and exhausted. She saw Drake spiraling and immediately shifted into problem-solving mode.

"Hey, it's okay," Amanda mumbled, standing up and stretching her stiff back. "Jess, you can just sleep in the other bed with me from now on. There’s room. And you two—" she nodded gently at Drake and Ashleigh "—clearly sleep better next to each other anyway."

Amanda meant it as a logical, helpful solution. But to Ashleigh, who was already hovering on the edge of sensory overload from the bad sleep, the suggestion shattered her entire baseline.

*Jessika is leaving. The identical twin is moving away. The three are becoming two.* Ashleigh froze. Her hands flew up, clapping hard over her ears to block out Amanda's words. "No," she whimpered, her voice tight and panicked. "No dividing. Three. We are three. We sleep in threes. No dividing!" She squeezed her eyes shut, her breathing syncing with Drake's frantic, hyperventilating rhythm.

The physical reality of the bed didn't matter anymore. Their sanctuary was being torn apart.

Amanda dropped her pen. The exhaustion vanished, instantly replaced by fierce, protective adrenaline.

Jessika didn't even hesitate. She scrambled across the wrinkled sheets and threw her arms around Ashleigh. She didn't say a word; she just applied deep, heavy pressure, wrapping her identical twin in a tight, grounding bear hug, pressing her chin firmly onto Ashleigh's shoulder.

Amanda reached the edge of the bed and climbed right onto the mattress. she moved right into Drake's space, prying his white-knuckled hands away from his knees and pulling his head firmly against her chest. She wrapped her arms around his shaking shoulders, applying the firm, consistent pressure she knew his nervous system desperately needed to reboot.

"I'm here," Amanda said, her voice dropping into a low, steady rumble that vibrated through her chest and into Drake. "I've got you. Nobody is leaving. Nobody is splitting up."

"Three," Ashleigh sobbed into Jessika's shoulder.

"I'm right here, Ash," Jessika whispered, squeezing tighter. "I'm not going anywhere. I'm right here."

They stayed like that for a long time. The only sounds in the room were the hum of the AC and the slow, gradual deceleration of Drake and Ashleigh's breathing. Amanda kept rubbing firm, rhythmic circles into Drake's back, acting as a physical anchor until the rigid tension in his muscles finally began to melt. Ashleigh slowly lowered her hands from her ears, her fingers gripping the fabric of Jessika's t-shirt like a lifeline.

When the panic had finally bled out into exhaustion, Amanda pulled back just enough to look Drake in the eye, then reached over to gently brush Ashleigh's hair out of her face.

"You are not being separated," Amanda said, her voice quiet, absolute, and unshakeable. "You are just moving three feet to the left so Drake's legs don't cramp. But listen to me."

She waited until she had all three of their eyes focused on her.

"If we ever get out of this motel," Amanda vowed, the promise burning with a fierce, iron-clad certainty. "And when I start making real money... I will buy a king-sized bed. The biggest, most ridiculous one they make. And the three of you can go back to sleeping together in a giant pile if you want to."

All three triplets froze.

Drake blinked, the last of the panic fading from his eyes. "Really?"

"Really," Amanda promised. "I swear it."

Ashleigh’s tear-streaked face slowly brightened. "A giant bed?"

Jessika finally smiled—a real one. "Like… a bed we can actually fit in?"

"A bed you can sprawl in," Amanda said, offering a tired but warm smile. "A bed you can grow in. A bed that doesn’t try to break your spine in your sleep."

Drake let out a long, heavy, relieved breath. His shoulders finally dropped. "Okay. That helps."

Jessika nodded, loosening her death grip on her twin. "Yeah. That helps a lot."

Ashleigh leaned her head against Drake’s shoulder, fully regulated again. "I still want the left side."

Jessika grabbed a pillow and lightly smacked Ashleigh in the arm with it. "You always take the left side."

Amanda laughed quietly, climbing off the mattress. "You’re all impossible. But at least eventually, you’ll be impossible with enough room to sleep."

The triplets groaned, stretched, and began untangling their limbs from the sheets, already bickering about who had kicked whom and whose elbow had done the most damage. But underneath the familiar noise, the shift felt right.

They weren’t separating. They were just adapting until Amanda could build them a bigger sanctuary.
