# Federal Escort to Fairfax

## I-66 Eastbound: Drake

The wail of the siren wasn’t just a sound; it was a physical vibration rattling against Drake’s teeth.

The interior of the Advanced Life Support rig was a blinding, sterile white that made his eyes water. Above him, a foil packet of IV fluid swayed violently on its hook as the heavy ambulance banked hard to the left, tearing down Interstate 66 at speeds it was never designed to reach.

He felt like he was suffocating. His lungs still burned with the memory of the 140-degree sulfurous air, and his skin was terrifyingly dry, radiating a heat so intense it felt like he was cooking from the inside out.

"Core temp is sitting at 104.8 and climbing!" a voice shouted from somewhere above his head. A paramedic with intense, focused eyes leaned over him, tearing open another plastic wrapper. "I'm packing his neck and axillary lines with more ice. Push another bag of chilled saline! We need to break this fever before his organs start cooking!"

Freezing, rigid chemical ice packs were shoved under Drake's armpits and against the sides of his neck. The sudden, extreme shock of cold against his boiling skin made his entire body violently convulse.

But the convulsion brought a completely different, blinding kind of hell.

As his torso shivered, the movement traveled downward, and the world dissolved into a wash of pure, white-hot agony. It wasn't just his right leg anymore. The memory of the massive iron pipe bucking under the pressure of the mud flooded his mind—the sheer, crushing weight of the steel grating collapsing over his lower half.

Both of his legs felt like they were packed tightly with crushed glass and set on fire.

"Argh!" Drake choked out a raw, guttural scream, trying to curl inward, but a pair of heavy, gloved hands immediately pinned his shoulders flat against the stretcher.

"Do not move, Drake! Look right at me!" the lead medic ordered, leaning directly into Drake's blurry line of sight. "Your legs are immobilized, but you have severe bilateral fractures. They are shattered, son. If you thrash around, you are going to sever an artery. You have to stay completely still!"

Drake squeezed his eyes shut, his breath coming in shallow, frantic gasps. His heart was hammering so hard it felt like a trapped bird battering against his ribcage. Over the wail of the sirens, he could hear the medic calling out rapid-fire numbers to the driver up front, before hitting the radio pinned to his shoulder.

"Rig One to Escort Lead, patch me through to the LPN!" the medic barked over the radio. A second later, Amanda’s terrified but fiercely focused voice filled the cab. "Amanda, I know you’ve been his primary nurse for three years. We are about to push a massive dose of TXA to stop the internal bleeding and heavy fentanyl for the pain. Does he have any contraindications or adverse reactions to synthetic opioids on his chart?"

*"No allergies!"* Amanda’s voice cracked over the speaker, the sound of the armored SUV roaring in the background. *"His chart is completely clear! But his baseline resting heart rate is normally bradycardic, around fifty-five beats per minute due to athletics! If he's tachycardic at one-sixty, his heart is working three times harder than it's used to! You have to push the fluids faster, or he's going to go into cardiac arrest!"*

"Copy that, Amanda! Pushing meds now!" the medic yelled, ripping open a massive trauma dressing. "Tell that federal escort to push the convoy faster! If we don't get him into a surgical bay in the next ten minutes, he's going to lose his legs, or we're going to lose him to hypovolemic shock!"

Drake felt the floor of the ambulance lurch as the driver slammed the accelerator down. Through the small rear window of the rig, the flashing red and blue strobes of the armored federal SUVs reflected off the highway signs. The escort was physically forcing civilian traffic onto the shoulders, creating a high-speed, unstoppable corridor straight to the Fairfax Trauma Center.

The medic squeezed Drake's shoulder, his voice dropping slightly to a more reassuring, human tone. "Stay with me, Drake. Your sister is watching your vitals. The feds are clearing the road. We are almost there. Just keep breathing."

But the dark edges of Drake's vision were already starting to close in. The roaring sound of the deep-earth drill seemed to echo back into his ears, drowning out the sirens, the hum of the tires, and the frantic shouts of the trauma team as he finally surrendered to the darkness.

## I-66 Eastbound: Ashleigh

The second Advanced Life Support rig was a mirror image of the first—a speeding box of blinding lights, frantic medical personnel, and raw agony.

Ashleigh couldn’t stop shaking. The violent, uncontrollable tremors racking her body weren’t just from the chemical ice packs the paramedics were furiously shoving against her neck and under her arms; it was her nervous system completely short-circuiting. The 140-degree heat of the deep-earth cavern had pushed her to the brink of organ failure, but the pain radiating from her lower half was what threatened to pull her under entirely.

"Her legs are completely rigid, but the swelling is catastrophic!" a paramedic shouted over the wail of the sirens, expertly slicing through the heavy fabric of her soot-stained pants with trauma shears. "I'm feeling severe crepitus just below the knees. Suspected bilateral fractures. The kickback from that mud pipe crushed them both!"

Ashleigh tried to speak, to ask about Drake and Jessika, but her throat was so dry it felt lined with sandpaper. A medic immediately pressed an oxygen mask over her face, the cool, sterile air doing little to ease the burning in her lungs.

"Rig Two to Escort," the lead medic yelled, hitting his radio. "Amanda, Patient Two’s pressure is crashing. We’re reading eighty-five over fifty. I am pushing ten CCs of morphine, but I need her baseline!"

*"Rig Two, this is Amanda,"* the radio hissed back, her clinical training completely overriding her panic. *"Ashleigh runs a naturally low baseline pressure, usually around one-hundred over sixty. If she's dropping below eighty, she's crashing faster than the monitor says! She's actively bleeding out into the muscle tissue. Elevate her upper body slightly to reduce intracranial pressure from the heatstroke and push the morphine wide open!"*

"Secure the splints and push the meds, you heard the nurse!" the lead medic ordered, bracing himself against the bulkhead as the heavy ambulance swerved aggressively onto the shoulder to bypass a line of civilian traffic.

Above the chaotic noise of the trauma bay on wheels, the encrypted federal radio mounted on the ambulance dashboard crackled to life. It was broadcasting across the entire convoy.

"Lead Escort to all medical transports," the FBI tactical commander’s voice filled the cab, tense but rigidly disciplined. "Status check on the casualties."

"Rig Two," Ashleigh's medic keyed his shoulder mic while strapping a rigid brace over her shattered right shin. "Patient is tachycardic, core temp 104.2. Suspected shattered tibia and fibula in both legs. As her LPN confirmed, she is rapidly losing pressure. We are actively fighting off hypovolemic shock. She needs a surgical suite the second we hit the bay doors!"

"Rig One confirms identical status," another voice crackled over the net from Drake's ambulance. "Patient One has severe internal bleeding. We are losing time."

There was a split second of heavy static, and then the FBI commander came back on the line.

"Copy that, Rigs One and Two. Listen up, everyone. I just got off a direct secure uplink with the White House. The Commander-in-Chief has the Situation Room actively monitoring our GPS telemetry."

Even through the suffocating fog of morphine and agony, Ashleigh’s eyes fluttered open. The President.

"President Sterling sends a direct message to the medical teams," the commander continued, his voice amplifying over the roar of the interstate. "You have the absolute, unmitigated backing of the United States Federal Government. He has already ordered the Secret Service and the National Guard to lock down the Fairfax campus. They are clearing the trauma bays and flying in the top orthopedic and burn surgeons on the East Coast via military medevac helicopters right now. The federal government is writing the check for everything. Do whatever it takes to keep those kids alive. Escort units, push it to ninety miles an hour. Let's get these heroes home."

The ambulance engine roared as the driver laid entirely on the horn and the accelerator, the sudden G-force pressing Ashleigh deeper into the stretcher.

"You hear that, Ashleigh?" the medic asked, leaning down, his eyes filled with fierce determination. "The President is clearing the runway for you, and your sister is fighting for you on the radio. You just hold on a little longer."

Ashleigh wanted to nod, but the edges of her vision were fraying into static. The violent shudder of the ambulance, the blinding white light of the halogen bulbs, and the searing, white-hot fire in her shattered legs began to blur together. Knowing that Drake was in the rig ahead of her fighting the exact same battle, she let her eyes slip shut, trusting the wailing convoy to carry them the rest of the way.

## I-66 Eastbound: Jessika

The third Advanced Life Support rig wasn't a scene of shattered bones, but it was still a warzone of heat and panic.

Jessika lay flat on the stretcher, her chest heaving as the oxygen mask fed cool, sterile air into her burning lungs. The heart monitor beside her head was screaming, a rapid, frantic *beep-beep-beep* that tracked her severe tachycardia. She was burning up, her skin flushed and dry, but the paramedics had aggressively packed her with chemical ice and pushed chilled IV fluids the second the doors closed.

Unlike Drake and Ashleigh, she could feel her toes. She could move her legs. The kickback from the rusted iron pipe had thrown her clear of the crushing weight that had destroyed her siblings' lower halves.

But her physical safety offered absolutely no comfort.

Through the haze of the 104-degree fever, she had heard the encrypted federal radio broadcast echo through the cab of her ambulance. She had heard the medics in Rigs One and Two calling out the catastrophic bilateral fractures, the internal bleeding, and the hypovolemic shock.

"Drake... Ash..." Jessika sobbed, her voice muffled by the plastic oxygen mask. A tear slipped down her soot-stained cheek, instantly feeling cold against her flushed skin.

"Rig Three to Amanda," her paramedic said, keying his radio before turning back to check Jessika’s IV line. "Her core temp is hovering at 104.2, but her heart rate isn't coming down. Did she have anything to eat or drink before the descent? We need to know her hydration baseline."

*"They shared a pizza and some sodas about three hours ago down in the B4 living quarters,"* Amanda’s voice replied immediately, the crispness of her LPN training battling the tremor in her voice. *"Nothing since then. She’s critically dehydrated, but she has a slight mitral valve prolapse noted on her chart! It’s mild, but if you push those chilled IV fluids too fast, her heart might flutter. Step the flow rate down by ten percent!"*

"Copy that, stepping the flow rate down," the paramedic said, adjusting the valve on the IV bag, giving Jessika a reassuring nod. "They're in the best hands in the world, Jessika, and your sister is making sure we do this right. You need to focus on breathing. We are almost to Fairfax."

Jessika turned her head slightly, looking out the small, reinforced window of the ambulance's rear door.

They had hit the dense, sprawling suburbs of Northern Virginia. Normally, this stretch of Interstate 66 would be an absolute parking lot, a gridlocked nightmare of commuters and commercial trucks that would bring any emergency vehicle to a crawling, agonizing halt.

But the highway was completely empty.

A massive, rolling blockade of Virginia State Police cruisers and unmarked federal vehicles had joined the convoy, physically forcing thousands of civilian cars onto the shoulders and exit ramps miles in advance. The sheer might of the federal government was on full display, carving an unstoppable, high-speed trench straight through the heart of NoVA traffic to get them to the hospital.

## The Federal Escort

A few hundred yards back, inside the trailing, matte-black FBI SUV, the surreal reality of the empty highway was not lost on the adults.

Amanda sat pressed against the ballistic glass, her eyes wide with disbelief and terror. She knew I-66. Everyone in Virginia knew I-66. Seeing all four eastbound lanes completely devoid of cars, save for the three speeding ambulances and the phalanx of police cruisers, felt like driving through an apocalypse.

"I can't believe this," Amanda whispered, her fingernails digging into her own palms. "It's empty. They actually cleared the entire interstate."

"President Sterling gave the order," the FBI tactical commander said from the driver's seat, his eyes locked on the brake lights of the third ambulance. He didn't take his foot off the accelerator, keeping the heavy armored SUV steadily at ninety miles an hour. "When the White House says clear the road, the road gets cleared."

The encrypted tactical radio mounted on the dashboard suddenly burst through the heavy silence of the cabin.

*"Rig One to Escort Lead,"* a distorted, frantic voice crackled over the speaker. *"Patient One's pressure is tanking. Eighty-five over fifty and dropping. Tachycardic at one-sixty. The crush trauma is releasing massive amounts of myoglobin into his bloodstream. If we don't stabilize this, his kidneys are going to shut down before we hit the bay."*

Amanda’s breath hitched. She wasn't just hearing a panicked paramedic; her LPN training immediately translated the nightmare. Severe rhabdomyolysis. Drake’s pulverized muscle tissue was actively poisoning his own organs.

The FBI commander reached out and flipped a toggle on the console, patching the cabin's microphone directly into the convoy’s frequency. He handed the mic back over his shoulder to Amanda. "They know you're an LPN. Talk to them."

Amanda snatched the microphone, her hands shaking, but her voice sharp and clinical. "Rig One, this is Amanda Wilcox. Push sodium bicarbonate if you have it in the crash cart! You have to alkalinize his blood to buy his kidneys ten more minutes!"

*"Copy that, Amanda, pushing bicarb now,"* the medic shot back over the radio. *"Hold on, Rig Two is trying to break in."*

A new voice cut across the channel, breathless and strained against the wail of the sirens. *"Rig Two to Amanda. Patient Two is in severe neurogenic shock. The bilateral tibia-fibula fractures are completely unstable. The swelling is catastrophic... Amanda, we are losing her pedal pulses."*

Erika sat in the middle seat, her arm wrapped tightly around Amanda's shoulders. She watched her cousin’s face drain of all remaining color.

"What does that mean?" Erika asked quietly, dread sinking like a stone in her stomach.

"It means compartment syndrome," Amanda choked out, lowering the microphone, tears finally spilling over her eyelashes. "The swelling in Ashleigh's legs is so severe it's cutting off the blood supply to her feet. The tissue is dying."

Beside her, Victor Thorne sat in agonizing silence. His ruined dress shirt was still stained with the sulfurous dirt from the cavern and Drake's blood. He leaned forward, his elbows resting on his knees, his hands clasped so tightly his knuckles were stark white. He had just bought a multi-billion dollar corporation, he had just averted a supervolcanic eruption, but in this moment, listening to Amanda translate the horrific reality of his nieces and nephew fighting for their lives, all of his wealth and power meant absolutely nothing.

He couldn't fix shattered bones with a checkbook. He just had to sit in the back of a speeding SUV and pray that the trauma surgeons at Fairfax were as good as the President promised.

"Faster," Victor said, his voice a low, desperate gravel, his eyes burning as he stared at the back of the commander's head. "Please. We have to get there *now*."

"We're two miles out from the Fairfax exit, Dr. Thorne," the commander replied, his knuckles white on the steering wheel as the SUV engine roared. "The Blackhawk surgical teams have already touched down on the roof. They are waiting at the bay doors."
