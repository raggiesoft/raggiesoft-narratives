# The Fingerpoke of Doom

It was the first Monday of January, and the air inside Room 114 was thick with the smell of microwave noodles and the frantic tension of the Monday night ratings war.

Amanda was at the wobbly veneer desk, her hands pressed over her ears as she tried to memorize the cardiovascular system from a borrowed textbook. On the beds behind her, the triplets were heavily invested in the television.

Drake had control of the remote. For the last hour, he and Ashleigh had been stubbornly defending Channel 12, waiting for the massive, highly anticipated main event. Jessika was just watching for the sheer theatrical absurdity of it all.

On the screen, the two biggest stars of the Channel 12 roster finally stood face-to-face in the center of the ring. The crowd was buzzing. The commentators were shouting. The tension was at an absolute boiling point.

Then, the guy in the red and yellow colors raised his hand, extended a single index finger, and gently poked the guy in the black shirt in the chest.

The guy in black dramatically threw himself backward onto the mat. The guy in red covered him. One, two, three. The bell rang.

Silence fell over the motel beds.

Drake stared at the heavy CRT screen, his mouth slightly open. Beside him, Ashleigh just blinked, completely deadpan.

"Are you kidding me?" Drake said, his voice breaking in sheer thirteen-year-old betrayal.

"That was it?" Jessika asked, tossing a noodle back into her bowl. "That was fake even for *them*."

"That's it," Drake said, his face hardening with absolute disgust. "We're done. We're never watching Channel 12 again."

He raised the plastic motel remote like a weapon and aggressively punched the '1' button twice.

The screen blinked black for a fraction of a second before the Channel 11 broadcast snapped into focus. They had switched over at the exact perfect millisecond.

The commentators on the rival show were screaming in pure panic. In the center of the ring, a battered underdog in a flannel shirt was fighting for his life, trying to win the world title against impossible odds.

Suddenly, the unmistakable sound of shattering glass exploded through the cheap television speakers.

The arena crowd on the screen went absolutely, deafeningly nuclear. A bald, unhinged anti-hero marched down the ramp with a steel chair, swinging wildly. Chaos erupted. The underdog hit his finishing move, the referee's hand slammed the mat three times, and the crowd completely lost their minds as the new champion held the belt in the air.

Drake and Ashleigh physically leaped off the mattress, screaming at the top of their lungs, entirely caught up in the sheer, electric adrenaline of the moment. Jessika was laughing, clapping her hands at the sheer chaos on the screen.

At the desk, Amanda finally lowered her hands from her ears. She looked over her shoulder at her brother and sisters, who were currently shadow-wrestling on the threadbare carpet, arguing over who got to be the guy with the steel chair.

She let out a quiet, exhausted laugh and shook her head, turning back to her textbook.

They never turned back to Channel 12. From that Monday on, it was Channel 11 or nothing.
