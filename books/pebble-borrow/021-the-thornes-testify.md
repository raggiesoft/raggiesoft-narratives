# The Thornes Testify

## The Empty Chairs

The flashes from the press cameras created a blinding, strobing wall of light as Dr. Victor Thorne and Professor Erika Thorne walked down the center aisle of the Hart Senate Office Building.

They took their seats at the witness table, raising their right hands as the Chairman of the Joint Select Committee swore them in under penalty of perjury.

The gallery behind them was packed to the absolute fire-code limit. The nation had been waiting for this moment. They wanted to see the billionaire who bought a doomsday device, and the brilliant professor who figured it out. But more than anything, the cameras were searching for the three teenagers who had actually stopped it.

The Chairman leaned into his microphone, adjusting his glasses.

"Dr. Thorne, Professor Thorne... the committee thanks you for your voluntary appearance today," the Chairman began respectfully. He looked at the four empty chairs intentionally left at the witness table. "However, the committee issued subpoenas for Amanda, Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika Wilcox. Can you explain to this Congress why the primary witnesses to the subterranean breach are not present?"

A murmur rippled through the press corps.

Victor leaned forward, pulling his microphone close. He didn't look intimidated. He looked like a man who had drawn a line in the concrete and dared the federal government to cross it.

"Mr. Chairman, Amanda, Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika are my nieces and nephew," Victor stated, his voice a low, unwavering rumble that instantly silenced the murmurs in the room. "When their parents passed away years ago, I became their guardian. They are my family."

Victor looked directly at the bipartisan panel, his "Papa Bear" instincts fully engaged.

"Drake and Ashleigh are currently sitting in a retrofitted living room in Great Falls, Virginia, learning how to exist in wheelchairs because their legs were pulverized doing the job the United States military failed to do," Victor said coldly. "Jessika is recovering from systemic heatstroke. And Amanda is not just their older sister carrying the emotional weight of holding them all together—**she is their Licensed Practical Nurse.**"

Victor leaned closer to the microphone, his eyes locking directly with the Chairman's.

"Amanda is currently executing the strict, non-negotiable orders of their primary care physician and the chief of orthopedic surgery at Fairfax. Those medical orders are absolute: my nieces and nephew need to be at *home*. They are exhausted. They are severely traumatized. And I will not allow them to be wheeled into this room and paraded in front of national television cameras so politicians can applaud them."

Victor squared his shoulders, projecting his voice so it echoed off the marble walls of the hearing room.

"I am their uncle, and I am keeping them home to recover in peace. If this committee feels that a congressional subpoena overrides the medical survival of three teenagers who just saved the Eastern Seaboard, then I invite you to act on it. Send the United States Marshals to my gates in Great Falls. Order them to forcefully rip an LPN away from her paralyzed siblings, and do it on live television. But until you are ready for that headline, if you wish to hold someone in contempt for those empty chairs, you can arrest *me*."

The room was dead silent. The press corps stopped whispering. The Chairman looked at the fiercely protective billionaire, recognizing the absolute political suicide of arresting the kids who just saved millions of lives. He quietly nodded, withdrawing the question.

"Their absence is noted and medically excused, Dr. Thorne. Let us move to the timeline of the acquisition."

## The Geologist's Discovery

The Congresswoman from the left flank took over the questioning, turning her attention to the woman sitting next to Victor.

"Professor Thorne," the Congresswoman said. "Your father purchased Pebble Burrow purely as a real estate and tech investment. When did you realize the facility was not what Marcus Vance claimed it to be?"

Erika adjusted her microphone. She didn't possess her father's billionaire bravado, but she possessed an absolute, razor-sharp scientific authority.

"Almost immediately," Erika testified flatly. "When my father and I took possession of the Institute, we conducted a standard physical audit of the cut-and-cover basement levels—B1 through B4. Everything there was strictly residential and administrative. But then we took the industrial transport elevator down the main shaft to the actual drill cavern, which is located several kilometers below the surface."

Erika gestured to the technical schematics now displayed on the committee monitors.

"The moment the elevator doors opened into that cavern, the ambient temperature was 140 degrees Fahrenheit. Marcus Vance's reports stated the drill was tapping a dormant, stable geothermal rock bed. But I am a geoscientist specializing in volcanology. That entire subterranean cavern is encased in solid basalt rock."

Erika leaned forward, her voice ringing with absolute scientific certainty.

"Basalt is a natural thermal insulator. Even at a depth of several kilometers, the ambient air inside that cavern should not be baking at 140 degrees. The only way solid basalt reaches and sustains that kind of extreme temperature is if it is sitting directly on top of a massive, highly pressurized, active heat source. We weren't looking at a stable geothermal vent. We were standing on the roof of a dormant VEI-8 supervolcano, and the military had built a tungsten-carbide detonator right above it."

## The USGS Intervenes

"And what was your immediate action, Professor?" the Chairman asked, leaning forward, fully captivated by the science.

"I bypassed the military liaisons entirely," Erika stated. "General Vance had spent years using his security clearances to physically ban the United States Geological Survey from the Middletown valley. As the new legal owner of the facility, I personally contacted the Director of the USGS on a secure line. I gave them the raw thermal telemetry, explained the thermal inefficiency of the basalt insulation, and demanded they deploy an emergency deep-earth mapping team to my facility immediately."

Erika looked out at the gallery.

"When the USGS teams finally breached the gates, they were profoundly relieved that the blackout was over," Erika testified, her voice dropping to a somber register. "But that relief lasted exactly as long as it took to process the first seismic scans. The geologists were terrified. They confirmed my hypothesis instantly: the mantle boundary was completely unstable."

Victor took the microphone back, seamlessly backing his daughter up.

"We were simply exploring a facility we had legally purchased," Victor concluded. "My daughter's expertise broke the cover-up. But by the time we fully understood the threat, General Vance had already locked down the grid and forced the drill on. That is when my nieces and nephew had to step into the fire."
