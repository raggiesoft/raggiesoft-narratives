# Great Falls

## The Bubble Bursts

Four months after the 140-degree nightmare under the mountain, the heavy double doors of the Fairfax rehabilitation wing finally swung open for the last time.

For sixteen weeks, the secured hospital floor had been a bubble. It was a sterile, predictable world filled with doctors, physical therapists, and federal agents. It was exhausting and painful, but it was built entirely for them.

Now, they had to go back to the real world.

Drake and Ashleigh sat side-by-side in the hospital lobby in highly customized, ultra-lightweight manual wheelchairs. Their legs were no longer encased in rigid external fixators, but the catastrophic crush injuries had left their lower halves permanently immobilized and severely scarred.

Amanda stood directly behind Ashleigh’s chair, her eyes darting nervously around the lobby. She was gripping the push handles so tightly her knuckles were white. Ever since the surgeon had delivered the news about their legs, Amanda had shifted her "big sister" instincts into absolute overdrive. She was hyper-vigilant, constantly double-checking their transfer boards, their medications, and the slope of every single ramp.

"Okay, the convoy is out front," Dr. Victor Thorne said, walking over with Erika and a stack of discharge paperwork. He looked down at the triplets. "Are we ready?"

Jessika, standing next to Drake with a duffel bag slung over her shoulder, reached down and squeezed his shoulder. "Ready as we'll ever be."

## The Great Falls Estate

They couldn't go home. Pebble Burrow was currently a heavily guarded federal crime scene swarming with FBI auditors and USGS geologists. It would be years before the government handed the keys back.

But Victor Thorne was a billionaire, and he wasn't going to let his family live in a hotel.

The federal SUVs drove them out to Great Falls, Virginia. Victor had purchased a sprawling, completely private, single-story estate tucked away behind heavily wooded security gates. More importantly, he had spent the last three months having construction crews work around the clock to completely gut and retrofit the property.

The SUVs pulled up to the front door, where a seamlessly integrated, low-grade concrete ramp led directly into the house.

Amanda was out of the car before it even fully stopped. She supervised the Secret Service agents helping Drake and Ashleigh transfer from the vehicles to their chairs, hovering inches away just in case they slipped.

When Victor unlocked the front door, the true scale of the modifications became clear. There were no stairs. Not a single step in the entire six-thousand-square-foot house. The hardwood hallways had been widened to comfortably allow two wheelchairs to pass side-by-side. The kitchen featured lowered countertops with cut-outs so Drake and Ashleigh could roll right up to the sink and stove. The bathrooms had been converted into massive, zero-entry wet rooms with fold-down teak benches and reinforced grab bars.

It was a beautiful, luxurious fortress.

"The physical therapists signed off on the blueprints last week," Victor said quietly as Drake and Ashleigh rolled into the massive living room, taking in the space. "It's all yours."

"It's perfect, Uncle Victor," Ashleigh said, letting out a long, exhausted breath as she parked her chair near the large glass windows. "Thank you."

## The Subpoena

While Jessika helped unpack the medical supplies in the bedrooms, Amanda cornered Victor and Erika in the kitchen.

She noticed the thick, heavy manila envelope sticking out of Victor's leather briefcase. It bore the gold seal of the United States Senate.

"What is that?" Amanda asked, her voice instantly dropping into a defensive, razor-sharp tone.

Victor sighed, pulling the envelope out and setting it on the lowered granite island. "It's a formal request from the Joint Select Committee on the Pebble Burrow Incident. They are holding the final round of public hearings next month. They want the kids to testify."

"No," Amanda said instantly. She didn't even blink. "Absolutely not."

"Amanda, it's a congressional committee—" Erika started gently.

"I don't care if it's the Pope," Amanda fired back, her eyes flashing with fierce, protective anger. She pointed down the hall toward the living room where her siblings were resting. "They just spent four months learning how to pull their own body weight out of a bed. They still have nightmares about the heat and the drill. They are traumatized, they are exhausted, and they are *not* being wheeled in front of C-SPAN cameras so politicians can applaud them."

Victor looked at his oldest niece. He didn't see a stubborn twenty-something; he saw a young woman who had almost lost her entire family and was completely unwilling to let them be used for political theater.

"They want the public record, Amanda," Victor said softly. "The nation wants to hear from the people who saved them."

"Then you and Erika can go," Amanda countered, stepping right up to the billionaire. "You bought the facility. Erika found the geological anomalies. You two can sit at the witness table and answer their questions. But Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika are staying right here in this house to heal. Tell Congress no."

Victor looked down at the Senate seal on the envelope, then back up at Amanda's fierce, unwavering glare. A small, immensely proud smile tugged at the corner of his mouth.

"Alright," Victor nodded, sliding the envelope back into his briefcase. "I will handle the committee. The kids stay out of the cameras."
