The triplets exchanged a confused look. Drake stepped forward, gesturing to the silent walls. "Was it a drill? The noise that stopped?"

"Yes. A Mark IV Seismic Penetrator," Victor said, leaning forward and resting his elbows on his knees. "The previous CEO—the man who built this absurd mansion—scheduled a deep-earth penetration test for tomorrow. He assumed the mountain above us was a dead, solid block of basalt. A perfectly safe place to test military hardware."

"It's not dead," Erika added, setting her water bottle down. She looked right at Amanda. "The rock wall down in the main cavern is practically boiling. The kinetic energy readings are off the charts. The previous administration didn't just build a facility on a dormant volcano. They built it directly on top of a highly pressurized magma chamber."

The living room went dead silent. The rippling blue light from the pool suddenly felt a lot less like a luxury submarine and a lot more like a fragile glass bubble.

"Wait," Jessika said, her voice dropping to a whisper. "You're saying if they turned that drill on tomorrow..."

"...It would have acted like a needle popping a pressurized balloon," Erika finished bluntly. "The sudden depressurization would have triggered a localized supervolcano. The eruption would have instantly vaporized this entire facility, cratered Middletown, and leveled everything from Washington, D.C. to the naval base down the coast in a matter of hours."

Drake’s jaw dropped. He looked slowly down at the floor, as if expecting the carpet to suddenly turn to lava. Ashleigh grabbed his arm, her knuckles turning white.

"We stopped it," Victor said firmly, making sure his nieces and nephew looked at him, not the floor. "The power is cut, the console is locked, and the drill is officially dead. You are entirely safe. But the sheer, monumental stupidity required to let it get this far..." His jaw tightened. "The man who ran this place didn't just embezzle funds for a bowling alley and a computer. He nearly wiped out the Eastern Seaboard out of pure negligence."
