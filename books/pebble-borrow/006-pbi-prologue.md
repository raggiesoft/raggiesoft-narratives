# PBI Prologue

Marcus Vance, the Chief Executive Officer of the Pebble Burrow Institute, was currently overseeing the construction of a highly complex, multi-million dollar aqueduct system.

It was going terribly.

He leaned back in his top-of-the-line ergonomic mesh chair, scowling at the custom 22-inch Ultra-Res Strategic Display dominating his desk. Beneath the faux-mahogany veneer of his workstation, the massive black computer tower roared. The dual-linked Vertex-Forge 9800 XL graphics array whined like a miniature jet engine as it rendered the sprawling, ultra-high-resolution city of his Urban Metropolis Builder save file.

Down on Level B2 of the facility, isolated from the noise and rust of the actual military contractors, Vance’s world was perfect. He took a sip of his double espresso. To his left, a reinforced interior window offered a pristine view down into the Level B3 executive gym, though he hadn't used the treadmills in months. To his right, his desk was littered with ignored manila folders—including the overdue maintenance logs for the deep-earth drill currently suspended miles below Level B4.

The drill could wait. His virtual citizens were out of water.

Vance reached for his mouse just as a soft, melodic ping echoed through the suite. He frowned. That was the arrival chime for the private, gold-painted elevator. Nobody was scheduled to come down from the B1 lobby.

The polished elevator doors slid open. Vance expected to see his assistant. Instead, six federal agents in sharp, dark windbreakers stepped out onto his faux-expensive wood floor.

"Marcus Vance?" the lead agent barked, flashing a silver badge. "You are under arrest for federal embezzlement, wire fraud, and misappropriation of classified government funds."

Vance froze. His hand hovered inches from the mouse. "This is a restricted black site. You can't—"

"Hands on the desk. Now."

Panic spiked in Vance’s chest. He lunged toward the bottom-right drawer of his desk—where he kept the encrypted flash drive containing the offshore routing numbers—but an agent was already there, slamming his wrist down onto the faux-mahogany surface. The espresso cup tipped over, sending a dark, hot puddle creeping toward a stack of falsified safety inspection certificates.

The sharp click of handcuffs echoed in the cavernous, quiet office.

"Wait, the buyout!" Vance stammered, his face pale as they hauled him to his feet. "Dr. Thorne is signing the paperwork today! I just need to call my lawyers—"

"Dr. Thorne officially took ownership twenty minutes ago," the lead agent said flatly, shoving Vance toward the gold elevator. "He’s the one who handed us your secondary ledger."

They didn't give him time to grab his coat. They didn't let him lock his workstation. They simply marched the former CEO out of his pristine, isolated fortress. They dragged him into the gold elevator, rode it up to the B1 lobby, hauled him past the massive, heavy-duty blast doors that separated the real facility from his fantasy land, and shoved him into the slow, clunky service elevator.

Minutes later, Marcus Vance was blinking against the humid 2003 Virginia sunlight, shoved into the back of a federal vehicle parked outside a completely unremarkable, rusted maintenance shed.

Far below the earth, the Level B2 executive suite sat in perfect, air-conditioned silence. The espresso continued to drip off the edge of the desk. The cooling fans of the massive black tower hummed steadily. And on the blindingly bright 22-inch monitor, Marcus Vance’s virtual city finally ran out of water, throwing flashing red warning icons across the screen.

The office was a perfectly preserved time capsule of corruption, just waiting for the new owners to step off the elevator.
