# Insert Coin(s)

The air inside the Wizard's Keep arcade on the second floor of the mall was exactly what a sanctuary was supposed to smell like: stale popcorn, floor wax, and the metallic tang of thousands of hot, buzzing glass monitors. It was a chaotic, sensory-overload environment for most people, but for the fifteen-year-old triplets, it was perfectly ordered, predictable noise.

Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika stood in front of the absolute crown jewel of the arcade floor: a massive, four-player linked bank of *Redline Overdrive* sit-down stock car cabinets. The glowing numbers 1 through 4 shone brightly above their heads.

"Expert track?" Jessika asked, cracking her knuckles. "Or are we taking it easy on Drake today?"

Drake scoffed, sliding his long, gangly legs under the steering column of Player 2. He immediately checked the pedals, feeling the heavy, satisfying resistance of the brake and the smooth travel of the accelerator. He gripped the force-feedback wheel, his thumbs resting perfectly on the rigid spokes. "Mammoth Gorge. Four laps. And I'm not using automatic transmission like you casuals."

Ashleigh slid into the hard plastic bucket seat of Player 1, directly to his left. She didn't say a word, just immediately reached out and aggressively slammed her hand down on the four-speed H-shifter, testing the physical clunk of the gears. She hated the beginner oval track. She wanted the sharp, punishing hairpins.

Jessika took Player 3. The Player 4 seat remained empty, its screen flashing the vibrant, attract-mode demo of a polygonal stock car drifting through a tunnel.

"Alright," Jessika said, reaching into the pocket of her faded jeans and pulling out a fistful of brass tokens Amanda had handed them half an hour ago. "Let's go."

*Clink. Clink. Clink. Clink.*

The heavy thuds of the tokens dropping into the coin mechanisms echoed simultaneously across the three cabinets.

Suddenly, the attract-mode music cut out. A triumphant, synthesized brass fanfare blasted from the dual speakers built right into the headrests behind their ears, followed by a booming, digitized voice that echoed across the carpeted floor:

*"RED-LIIIIINE!"*

The screens flashed to the selection menu.

"Manual transmission, hard tires," Drake muttered to himself, his fingers flying across the glowing dashboard buttons. His brain completely detached from the reality of the arcade. The math of the track—the specific RPMs required for a flawless drift, the exact fraction of a second to downshift before the tunnel—became the only thing that mattered.

The countdown timer flashed in massive block numbers on all three screens.

*3... 2... 1... GO!*

The force-feedback wheels violently jerked to life in their hands. The massive subwoofers under their seats rumbled as the virtual engines revved to the redline.

Ashleigh immediately cut hard to the right, throwing her blue stock car directly in front of Jessika to block the racing line.

"Hey!" Jessika yelled over the roaring engines, slamming her foot on the brake pedal to avoid a catastrophic digital collision, the heavy wheel fighting her grip. "Dirty racing, Ash!"

"It's called defense," Ashleigh shot back, not breaking eye contact with the screen. She slammed the shifter into third gear, taking the first wide turn with clinical precision.

Drake ignored both of them. He was already pulling away from the pack. He knew the physics engine of *Redline Overdrive* better than he knew the layout of their cramped motel room. He approached the first sharp hairpin of Mammoth Gorge. He didn't hit the brakes. He simply dropped the heavy plastic shifter from fourth to second, jerked the wheel sharply to the left, and immediately slammed it back up to fourth.

His digital car entered a flawless, high-speed drift, the polygonal tires screeching through the speakers, the force-feedback wheel vibrating wildly in his hands. He cleared the apex of the turn perfectly, gaining a massive lead.

"He's doing the transmission exploit again," Jessika complained, wrestling her car back onto the track after clipping a concrete barrier. "He's literally breaking the code."

"It's a feature, not a bug," Drake yelled back over the booming soundtrack, a rare, genuine smile breaking across his face.

For the next four laps, they weren't homeless teenagers hiding from the state. They weren't wards of their exhausted older sister. They were just three professional drivers tearing through a digital canyon, completely immersed in the roaring, vibrating magic of the linked cabinets.

When the checkered flag finally waved on Drake's screen, declaring him the undisputed champion, the triplets sat back in their bucket seats, their hands still resting on the steering wheels, the adrenaline slowly bleeding out.

Drake let out a long breath, the tight spasticity in his legs momentarily forgotten. He looked over at Ashleigh, who was glaring at her "GAME OVER" screen in second place, and Jessika, who was laughing in third.

It was loud. It was chaotic. It was perfect.

## Player 4 Enters the Game

"You guys are doing it all wrong."

Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika jumped. Amanda was standing right behind Player 3, her arms crossed, watching the "GAME OVER" screen flash on Jessika's monitor. She was still wearing her faded maroon scrub top from her clinical rotation, her hair pulled back into a messy, exhausted ponytail.

Usually, when Amanda showed up at the Wizard's Keep, it meant the tokens were gone and it was time to march back to the reality of the motel.

Instead, she stepped around Jessika and casually slid into the empty, hard plastic bucket seat of Player 4. She held out her hand.

"Give me four tokens," Amanda ordered.

Jessika blinked, entirely caught off guard. She slowly reached into her pocket and dropped four brass coins into Amanda's palm.

"Amanda, this is Expert difficulty," Drake warned, his hands already gripping his steering wheel. "You don't even have a driver's license yet."

"I drive the narrative of this entire family, Drake," Amanda shot back, leaning forward to inspect the heavy, four-speed shifter. "I think I can handle a plastic steering wheel. Put your money in."

A wave of shocked, chaotic excitement rippled through the triplets. They scrambled to drop their tokens into the slots.

*Clink. Clink. Clink. Clink.*

The screens flashed in unison. The booming, digitized announcer voice shook the plastic housing of the cabinets:

***"RED-LIIIIINE!"***

"Okay, how does this work?" Amanda asked, suddenly looking a little less confident as the massive bass speakers under her seat began to vibrate. "Is the right pedal the gas?"

"Manual transmission, Ash! Block her out!" Jessika yelled, completely abandoning any concept of family loyalty the second the menu locked in.

*3... 2... 1... GO!*

The force-feedback wheels violently jerked. Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika launched off the starting line in a perfect, synchronized roar of digital engines, their tires smoking as they slammed into second gear.

Amanda slammed her foot on the gas. Her bright yellow stock car lurched forward, but she severely underestimated the aggressive kickback of the heavy steering wheel. She overcorrected to the left, panicked, jerked the wheel to the right, and plowed head-first into the starting line's concrete barrier at 120 miles per hour.

A massive, digitized *CRUNCH* echoed from her headrest speakers. Her car's hood crumpled into a polygonal mess.

"Amanda, you have to shift!" Ashleigh yelled, taking the first turn at top speed.

"I am shifting! Why am I going backward?!" Amanda shouted over the noise, frantically wrestling with the heavy wheel as her car slowly reversed into a digital wall. "This wheel is actively fighting me!"

"Because you're in a wall!" Drake yelled, entirely in his element. He dropped his shifter into second, executed his flawless, physics-breaking drift around the Mammoth Gorge hairpin, and rocketed into the lead.

As Drake's car blasted through the first glowing checkpoint banner, the arcade announcer's booming voice echoed over the roaring engines:

***"TIME EXTENSION: Your time has been extended!"***

"I can't get out of first gear!" Amanda laughed, a genuine, unburdened sound that the triplets hadn't heard in months. She was aggressively mashing the pedals, her yellow car spinning out in the middle of the track while digital opponents flew past her. "Jessika, did you hit me?!"

"Welcome to the gorge, Amanda!" Jessika cackled, trading paint with Ashleigh as they fought for second place.

For four chaotic laps, the crushing weight of the motel, the LPN exams, and the state social workers completely vanished. They were just four kids screaming at each other in a dark, neon-lit room. Amanda was hopelessly, comically terrible at the game. She spent more time facing the wrong direction than she did actually racing, but she couldn't stop laughing.

On the final stretch, Drake hit the tunnel perfectly, maxing out his RPMs and crossing the finish line a full four seconds ahead of the pack.

The screens flashed wildly. The speakers erupted with a triumphant, deafening roar:

***"RED-LINE!!!!!!"***

Drake threw his hands up in victory, leaning back in the bucket seat with a massive grin. Ashleigh crossed second, Jessika third.

Ten seconds later, Amanda's smoking, heavily damaged yellow car finally limped across the finish line in dead last.

"Okay," Amanda breathed, letting go of the wheel and wiping a tear of laughter from her eye. "I concede. You are the superior drivers. I will stick to managing the budget."

She turned in her seat, looking at the three of them. They were flushed, smiling, and completely regulated. It was the exact opposite of the cramped, suffocating energy of Room 114.

Amanda's smile softened. She reached into her scrub pocket, her fingers brushing against a folded piece of paper.

"You guys having fun?" she asked quietly.

"I broke the track record," Drake said proudly, pointing at the glowing initials 'DRK' at the top of the leaderboard.

"Good," Amanda said, taking a deep breath. The laughter faded, replaced by something much deeper and more permanent. "Because I got a phone call today at the clinic. From an attorney."

The triplets went perfectly still. The ambient noise of the Wizard's Keep seemed to drop away.

"Uncle Victor found us," Amanda said.
