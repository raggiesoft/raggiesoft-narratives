# Brothers Fall from Grace

## The Alexandria Courthouse: The Fall of the CEO

Inside the Albert V. Bryan United States Courthouse, the air was completely stagnant.

Marcus Vance stood at the defense table, his hands resting on the polished wood. Despite the overwhelming mountain of evidence, the financial ledgers, and the catastrophic injuries to the Wilcox teenagers, his hubris had forced a trial. He had pled Not Guilty, convinced his wealth and intellect could somehow outmaneuver the federal justice system.

It took the jury less than four hours to convict him on all seventy-two counts.

The Federal Judge, a no-nonsense jurist appointed for her absolute intolerance of white-collar crime, looked down from the bench. She didn't look at her notes. She looked directly into Marcus's eyes, and her voice carried the weight of a sledgehammer.

"Marcus Vance, you stood before this court and attempted to argue that your actions were simply corporate overreach," the Judge began, her tone dripping with judicial fury. "You tried to distance yourself from the 140-degree furnace you built. You argued that you did not explicitly force Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika Wilcox into that cavern, and therefore, you were innocent of attempted murder."

The Judge leaned forward.

"The jury saw through your delusion, and so does this court. Your greed built a doomsday device under the soil of this country. Your extreme, depraved recklessness is the sole reason two eighteen-year-old heroes will spend the rest of their lives in wheelchairs."

Marcus stiffened, his jaw locking, but the Judge wasn't finished. She was going to make sure his name became synonymous with absolute ruin.

"The federal sentencing guidelines afford me a measure of discretion," the Judge stated coldly. "I intend to use every single ounce of it to ensure you never draw another breath as a free man. On the charges of wire fraud, embezzlement, and treason, I sentence you to life in prison. On the three counts of attempted murder under the depraved heart doctrine, I sentence you to twenty years per count, to be served consecutively."

The gavel was raised.

"You will serve this sentence at the United States Penitentiary Administrative Maximum Facility in Florence, Colorado. You will be placed in solitary confinement under Special Administrative Measures for the remainder of your natural life. May God have mercy on you, Mr. Vance, because the United States will not."

*Bang.*

United States Marshals immediately seized Marcus by the arms, dragging the disgraced billionaire out of the courtroom.

## Fort Belvoir: The Disgrace of the General

Forty miles south, the military tribunal had reached its own devastating conclusion.

Peter Vance stood at the position of attention before the military judge and the panel of four-star generals. Like his brother, he had arrogantly pled Not Guilty, forcing the military prosecutors to lay bare every single treasonous command, every falsified report, and the horrific reality of the mutiny he had orchestrated.

The Major General presiding over the court-martial looked down at the shackled man. There was no anger in his face, only absolute, frigid contempt.

"Lieutenant General Vance," the military judge said, his voice echoing in the silent, wood-paneled room. "The panel has found you guilty of all charges. In the history of the United States Armed Forces, very few men have worn the rank you hold. It is a rank that demands absolute integrity, loyalty, and the unwavering defense of the Constitution."

The judge paused, looking at the silver stars pinned to Peter's dress uniform.

"You have desecrated that uniform. You weaponized your authority to blind the government, you ordered American soldiers to fire upon federal agents, and you nearly initiated a geological extinction event to protect a stolen fortune. You are a traitor to your country, to your soldiers, and to your oath."

Peter stared straight ahead, his face a completely unreadable mask, but the slight tremor in his jaw betrayed his terror.

"This court-martial sentences you to a total forfeiture of all pay and allowances," the judge decreed, his voice cutting through the room like a blade. "You are sentenced to a permanent Dismissal from the United States Army, stripping you of your rank, your honors, and your status as an officer."

The judge leaned forward, delivering the final blow.

"Furthermore, you are sentenced to life imprisonment without the possibility of parole. You will be transported immediately to the United States Disciplinary Barracks at Fort Leavenworth, Kansas, where you will remain until the end of your days. Court is adjourned."

*Bang.*

The heavy wooden gavel struck the block. The Military Police officers immediately stepped forward, grabbing Peter by the biceps and forcefully turning him toward the heavy wooden doors. He walked out of the courtroom not as a three-star general, but as an inmate.

## The Absolute Bottom

Placing both Marcus and Peter Vance in Administrative Segregation (Ad Seg) is the only way they survive their first week.

At ADX Florence, Marcus will spend twenty-three hours a day in a 7-by-12-foot soundproof concrete cell. His bed, desk, and stool are poured concrete. He will never see another inmate, and his only glimpse of the outside world is a four-inch slit window angled at the sky.

At Fort Leavenworth, Peter's reality is just as bleak. As a disgraced commander who nearly caused a national apocalypse, he is a massive target. For his own protection, the military will lock him in the Special Housing Unit (SHU). He will spend the rest of his natural life isolated from the general population, eating meals alone in his cell, completely erased from the world he once tried to destroy.

They are buried alive.
