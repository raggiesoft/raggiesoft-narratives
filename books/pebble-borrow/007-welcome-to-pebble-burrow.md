# Welcome to Pebble Burrow

## Arrival at B1

“Good morning, I am Dr. Victor Thorne, and I’m here to assume ownership of the facility,” he said to the security officer stationed behind the heavy reinforced desk in the B1 lobby. “This is my daughter, Professor Erika Thorne. We’re moving in, and I was told to bring my family. My three nieces and my nephew.”

The heavy steel door leading from the employee checkpoint clicked open. “Dr. Thorne? Hi, I’m Dr. Peter Fratire, the head of facility management. We’ve been expecting you and the Professor,” he said, stepping out to shake their hands. He paused, his eyes drifting past the two billionaires to the four young adults standing behind them with duffel bags. “Ah. The manifest only listed the two of you.”

“A paperwork error, I assure you,” Dr. Thorne said smoothly. “These are the Wilcox siblings. My oldest niece, Amanda, and the triplets: Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika. Ashleigh and Jessika are identical, and Drake is fraternal. I am their maternal uncle.”

“Of course, of course! Welcome to Pebble Burrow Institute,” Dr. Fratire recovered quickly, dabbing his forehead. He had no idea the previous CEO had just been hauled out in handcuffs, and he certainly wasn't prepared for four extra VIPs. “Right this way, please. I want to show you to your quarters. The previous administration left a rather... extensive living space.”

Dr. Fratire bypassed the massive, imposing blast door that led to the main facility and instead stopped at a small, completely nondescript door that looked like a janitor's closet. He tapped his badge, and the door slid open to reveal a pristine, wood-paneled elevator.

Behind Victor, the physical toll of the long journey was catching up to the siblings. Drake was unusually pale, his jaw locked tight against the deep, radiating ache in his legs. His condition was flaring up fiercely, the exhaustion stripping away whatever resilience he had left. Unable to bear his own weight, he had his right arm slung heavily over Ashleigh’s shoulders and his left arm anchored securely around Jessika’s neck.

His sisters stood flush against his sides, bearing his weight seamlessly. They acted as his living crutches, perfectly synchronized in their movements to keep him upright. It was an instinctual formation; they didn't complain, and they didn't waver.

Dr. Fratire gestured to a set of stairs adjacent to the elevator. “We can take the stairs down to—”

“Elevator, please,” Drake spoke up, his voice tight and strained with effort.

“Sure,” Dr. Fratire replied immediately, his eyes widening slightly as he finally registered the severe wobble in the young man's stance and the way the two girls were physically bracing him. “This takes you straight down to the executive living quarters on levels B3 and B4. It operates entirely independently of the main facility grid.”

## The Submarine Mansion

The elevator bell rang, and the doors opened deep underground on Level B4.

As the wood-paneled doors slid apart, Drake let out a tight, shuddering breath. The descent hadn't magically cured the deep, radiating ache in his legs, and his knees buckled slightly as he tried to step forward.

Instantly, Ashleigh and Jessika adjusted their grip. It was a fluid, deeply practiced motion. Ashleigh hitched his right arm higher over her shoulders, her hand wrapping firmly around his waist, while Jessika mirrored the exact same hold on his left side. They didn't stagger under his weight; they simply locked their stances and moved forward in perfect, synchronized harmony to accommodate his faltering steps. To them, carrying their brother wasn't a burden—it was just what they did.

“Wow. This is a small space,” Drake said, his voice strained but laced with amusement as he let his sisters carry him out into what initially looked like a modest, if richly decorated, entryway.

“This is only the foyer,” Dr. Fratire smiled, waving them through the next set of heavy double doors. “Please, make yourselves at home.”

As Ashleigh and Jessika helped Drake through the doors, the true scale of the embezzled funds revealed itself, completely stealing the breath from the siblings' lungs.

The main living room was massive, but what caught everyone's attention was the rippling, aquatic blue light casting mesmerizing shadows across the plush, high-end furniture. One entire wall of the room was made of thick, heavy-duty acrylic, looking directly into the deep end of the Level B3 natatorium pool. The water gently lapped against the glass, making the room feel exactly like standing inside the observation deck of a luxury submarine.

Drake’s jaw dropped. He leaned heavily against Jessika to keep his balance, his eyes tracing the massive space. “All this is ours?”

Ashleigh shifted her footing to brace him as he leaned forward, her grip on his waist unyielding and warm. He glanced down the adjacent hall, his eyes widening even further. “Is that... a bowling alley?”

“Yep, a private bowling alley,” Victor sighed heavily, pinching the bridge of his nose as his brilliant, analytical mind rapidly calculated just how much taxpayer and investor money had been wasted down here. “There is a lot more for the five of you to explore.”

“Living quarters? This is a mansion shoved into a basement!” Amanda laughed, dropping her heavy duffel bags onto a faux-mahogany side table. She immediately turned her clinical eye toward the plush, oversized sofas bathed in the blue pool light, already calculating which one would be best for Drake to elevate his legs on.

Dr. Fratire’s pager suddenly beeped at his hip, the sharp noise cutting through the quiet hum of the underground air filtration. He glanced down at the digital screen. “Dr. Thorne, the B2 executive office suite is unlocked and ready for you upstairs.”

“Excellent,” Victor nodded, his posture shifting instantly from tired uncle to the ruthless CEO about to clean house. He turned to his daughter and nieces. “You five, feel free to unpack while I head up to B2 and begin sorting out the administrative mess. Erika, make sure your cousins are settled before you join me.”

## Settling In

Erika led Amanda and the triplets further into the sprawling subterranean apartment, eventually finding a cluster of massive bedrooms branching off a central lounge area.

As soon as they reached the massive, U-shaped sectional sofa bathed in the rippling blue light of the natatorium window, Ashleigh and Jessika gently lowered Drake onto the cushions.

The moment the weight was off his legs, Drake let out a sharp, ragged hiss of breath, his head falling back against the upholstery. His legs were visibly trembling, the muscles in his calves and thighs rigid and locked.

Amanda didn't even bother unpacking her clothes. She dropped her duffel bag and immediately unzipped her specialized medical go-bag, her demeanor shifting instantly from older sister to working LPN.

"Pants up, Drake. Let me see," Amanda ordered gently but firmly, kneeling on the thick rug in front of him.

Drake gritted his teeth and pulled the fabric of his sweatpants up past his knees. The muscle fibers in his calves were pulled terrifyingly taut, visibly twitching under the skin. It was a severe flare-up of his neuromuscular spasticity—the residual nerve damage from his childhood tethered spinal cord misfiring wildly from the exhaustion of the trip.

"They're completely locked," Amanda noted, her thumbs expertly palpating the rock-hard muscles to find the epicenter of the cramp. Drake violently flinched, his hands gripping the edge of the sofa cushions.

"I've got him," Ashleigh murmured softly. She climbed onto the sofa beside him, wrapping her arms securely around his chest and pulling his back flush against her own. Jessika immediately sat on his other side, taking both of his hands in hers, providing the firm, grounding deep pressure he needed to keep his autonomic nervous system from panicking under the pain.

Amanda pulled a small orange pill bottle from her kit, shaking out a small, scored white tablet. "Tizanidine. Under the tongue, right now. Let it dissolve, it'll hit the central nervous system faster."

Jessika took the pill from Amanda and carefully guided it under Drake's tongue. The Tizanidine was a potent, fast-acting muscle relaxant specifically designed to interrupt the misfiring nerve signals causing the spasticity.

While they waited for the oral medication to cross the blood-brain barrier, Amanda pulled out a tube of highly concentrated, prescription-grade topical Diclofenac gel.

"This is going to burn for a second before it goes numb. Deep breaths," Amanda warned, her tone softening with practiced empathy.

She pressed the gel deep into the knotted bellies of his calf muscles, her thumbs working with aggressive, clinical precision to physically manually break the spasms. The moment the intense chemical heat hit his misfiring nerves, Drake let out a sharp, ragged sob, burying his face into the crook of Ashleigh’s neck as the tears finally spilled over.

None of his sisters flinched or panicked. The burning sensation of the gel was always agonizing, and his tears were an inevitable, accepted part of the routine. Ashleigh immediately adjusted her hold, wrapping her arms fully around him in a tight, protective cuddle. She rested her cheek against his hair, rocking him gently and murmuring quiet, familiar reassurances to pull him through the worst of the chemical sting, while Jessika steadfastly rubbed her thumbs over his white-knuckled grip.

Within ten minutes, the heavy dose of Tizanidine finally flooded Drake's system. The violent trembling in his legs slowly smoothed out, the rock-hard muscles finally melting back into a resting state. Drake went completely boneless against Ashleigh, his breathing evening out into exhausted, tear-hiccuped sighs.

"Better?" Amanda asked softly, wiping the excess gel off her hands with a sterile wipe.

"Much," Drake whispered, his eyes heavy from the potent muscle relaxant. "Thank you, Boss."

Amanda smiled warmly. Before packing her kit away, she pulled a heavy, tightly bound folder from the bottom compartment of her bag. She flipped to a tab labeled *DRAKE - MAR*, checked her wristwatch, and neatly recorded the exact dosage, route, and time for both the oral Tizanidine and the topical Diclofenac gel. She signed her initials in crisp blue ink. It didn't matter that they were deep underground in a billionaire's subterranean mansion; her clinical discipline never wavered. The Medication Administration Record was perfectly updated before she even thought about her own needs.

Zipping her bag shut, Amanda stood up. "Okay, let’s get the rest of things figured out. First, our sleeping arrangements. The rooms are huge, but some of these beds are only big enough for two. Drake, who do you want to share with: Ashleigh, Jessika, or me?"

Ashleigh, still serving as Drake's physical anchor, immediately scooted her duffel bag closer to his. Jessika grinned and said, "Well, I guess it’s settled then. Amanda, I'll take a room with you if Erika wants her own space."

"Sounds like a plan," Erika smiled, claiming a room near the aquatic glass wall, having watched the medical intervention with quiet, profound respect for how flawlessly the four siblings operated as a unit.

Drake giggled weakly as Ashleigh pulled him into a tighter hug, more than excited to share the space with his twin. "What time will Uncle Victor be back?" Ashleigh asked over his head.

"Hard to say. He’s looking for the maintenance logs for some sort of drill test tomorrow," Erika replied, setting her bags down by her chosen door. "Would you four like something to eat?"

"Yes, please," Jessika replied, collapsing onto the plush sofa next to Drake’s legs. Amanda, Ashleigh, and Drake all nodded in vigorous agreement. The adrenaline crash from managing the pain flare-up was making them all starving.

Erika walked over to the sprawling, stainless-steel kitchenette and opened the massive commercial fridge. "Naturally, it’s completely empty. How about I order us some pizzas?"

"Sure, but they definitely won't deliver past the security gate," Amanda pointed out, washing her hands at the sink. "We’ll probably have to take that closet elevator back up to the B1 lobby to grab it from the guards."

Erika picked up the heavy landline phone on the kitchen counter and dialed the front desk, confirming Amanda's assumption. "You guys want your usual?" she asked, covering the receiver.

"Definitely," the siblings replied in unison.

About forty-five minutes later, Erika stepped out of the private elevator balancing three hot pizza boxes and a cluster of sodas.

"Food!" Jessika squealed in delight. Ashleigh already had plates and napkins set out on the kitchen island as Erika dropped the boxes down.

The siblings each grabbed a slice of their favorite, popping the tabs on their sodas. Drake was finally able to sit up under his own power, the medication having fully relaxed his legs, allowing him to enjoy the meal without grinding his teeth.

"This place is insane," Ashleigh said between bites, leaning against the kitchen island and looking at the blue light rippling through the pool windows. "I hope Uncle Victor can take us on a tour of the rest of the building someday."

## Level B2: The Taxpayer’s Dime

The private gold-painted elevator chimed softly as the doors slid open on Level B2. Dr. Victor Thorne stepped out first, his sharp eyes immediately sweeping the executive office suite. Professor Erika Thorne followed close behind, carrying a sleek leather briefcase.

The suite was dead silent, save for a sound that closely resembled a miniature jet engine preparing for takeoff.

"Faux mahogany," Victor muttered, rapping his knuckles against the doorframe. "Laminate flooring. But I guarantee you he billed the Department of Defense for imported Brazilian hardwood."

"Look at the desk, Dad," Erika said, her voice dropping as she walked toward the center of the room.

Sitting atop the massive executive desk was a dark, monolithic 22-inch Ultra-Res Strategic Display monitor. Beneath the desk, a custom-built, jet-black workstation tower was roaring, pushing out waves of heat. A puddle of cold espresso was slowly drying on the faux-wood surface, right next to an overturned mug and a scattering of dog-eared manila folders.

Victor walked around the desk, his brow furrowing as he stared at the screen. The monitor was pushing a resolution so insanely high that the graphics were crystal clear. Flashing red warning icons completely surrounded a sprawling, intricately designed virtual city.

"Is this... a topographical mapping projection?" Victor asked, leaning in closer to read a blinking text box in the corner of the screen.

Erika reached out and tapped the heavy, mechanical keyboard. The screen shifted, zooming in on a digital neighborhood where tiny, animated citizens were holding up protest signs.

"No," Erika said, her voice tightening with disbelief. "It's *Urban Metropolis Builder*. The city just ran out of water."

Victor froze. He stared at the dual-linked Vertex-Forge graphics array visible through the tower's side panel, then back at the \$8,000 military-grade monitor. He did the math in his head.

"This is a custom-built gaming rig," Victor said, his voice dropping an octave into a dangerous, icy calm. "He used a restricted, black-budget government defense grant to build a supercomputer so he could play a city simulator."

"And he couldn't even keep his virtual city running," Erika added, her eyes flashing with anger as she picked up a stained, ignored folder from the puddle of espresso. "While he was doing this, he left the actual facility to rot."

The anger in the room was palpable. Victor, a man who built his entire empire on precision and accountability, was looking at the ultimate monument to taxpayer theft and criminal negligence. He had bought this facility to turn it into a beacon of modern defense research, and he was currently standing in a taxpayer-funded arcade.

"Shut it off," Victor ordered, his jaw clenched tight. "Shut the whole thing down. Pull the plug on the tower if you have to."

Erika didn't bother finding the power button. She reached under the desk and yanked the heavy power cable straight from the wall. The jet-engine whine of the cooling fans died instantly, and the massive screen faded to a silent, satisfying black.

"Alright," Victor said, taking a deep breath and pulling out his reading glasses. "Let's see just how badly he neglected the real world while he was playing mayor. What’s in that folder?"

## The Paper Trail of Ruin

As Erika pulled the espresso-stained folder from the desk, the soggy cardboard tore. A thick stack of papers slipped through the bottom, fanning out across the faux-mahogany surface in a chaotic, damp mess.

Victor reached for the top sheet, adjusting his glasses. He expected to see classified defense schematics or encrypted communications. Instead, he saw a bright red stamp reading: FINAL NOTICE.

"This is an invoice from an industrial metallurgy firm," Victor said, his brow furrowing as he flipped to the next page. "Ninety days past due."

Erika began sifting through the scattered pile, her disbelief growing with every document she uncovered. "Dad, look at these. Past-due notices from the heavy machinery sub-contractors. Ignored structural integrity assessments from independent auditors. Threatening letters from the local utility grid threatening to sever the high-voltage lines."

She held up a particularly thick packet, shaking her head. "He hasn't paid the primary engineering staff their hazard bonuses in six months. The only thing he was paying on time was the private catering company for this floor."

Victor stared at the sea of red ink covering the desk. The sheer scale of the financial negligence was staggering. The previous CEO hadn't just been playing video games while the facility ran itself—he had been actively starving the operation of its essential funds to pad his own pockets and buy custom computer parts.

If the basic utility bills and contractor invoices were this badly neglected, Victor could only imagine the horrific state of the actual, physical machinery keeping the black site running.

Victor dropped the papers back onto the desk. The icy calm from a few moments ago was gone, replaced by the sharp, authoritative edge of a man who was about to turn the entire facility upside down.

He reached across the desk and picked up the heavy, multi-line office phone, punching the button for the B1 security lobby. It rang exactly once before a flustered voice answered.

"Front desk. Uh, how can I help you, Dr. Thorne?"

"Find Dr. Fratire," Victor ordered, his voice echoing in the cavernous, silent office. "Tell him to cancel whatever he is currently doing and meet Professor Thorne and myself at the primary blast doors immediately."

"Yes, sir! Right away, sir! Is there a specific area you'd like him to prepare for?"

"All of it," Victor said flatly. "I want a full, unrestricted tour of the operational levels. I want to see every rusted pipe, every unpaid contractor, and every piece of heavy machinery this facility is hiding. And I want to see it now."

He slammed the phone back onto the receiver, turning to Erika.

"Grab whatever looks like a maintenance schedule out of that pile," Victor said, already striding toward the gold elevator. "We are going down into the dirt."

## The 1970s Time Capsule

Dr. Peter Fratire practically sprinted out of the B1 lobby, his face flushed and a ring of sweat forming around the collar of his lab coat. He had just left Amanda and the triplets settling into the subterranean mansion, completely unprepared to be summoned by a furious billionaire.

Victor and Erika were waiting at the massive, heavy-duty blast doors that separated the lobby from the operational sectors.

"Dr. Thorne," Fratire panted, swiping his keycard with a trembling hand. "I apologize for the delay. Where would you like to begin?"

"The closest operational laboratory," Victor said, his voice dangerously even. "Open the doors, Peter."

The heavy steel gears groaned in protest, shrieking as the blast doors slowly parted. The air that hit them was immediately different—stale, metallic, and smelling faintly of ozone and mildew.

Victor stepped onto the cut-and-cover level, his polished shoes clicking against cracked, yellowing linoleum. They walked into the first lab on the right, and Victor stopped dead in his tracks. Erika let out a slow, disbelieving breath.

It looked like a museum exhibit of Cold War-era science.

The room was illuminated by flickering, buzzing fluorescent tubes. The workstations were cluttered with heavy, analog oscilloscopes covered in a thick layer of dust. A row of centrifuges sat along the back wall, their casings rusted and one visibly propped up by a wedge of folded cardboard. Thick bundles of exposed, fraying wires ran along the ceiling, secured by nothing more than brittle zip-ties and duct tape.

It hadn't just been neglected for months. It hadn't seen a dime of upgrade money since the late 1970s.

Victor walked over to a primary ventilation hood. He dragged a single finger across the glass, coming away with a thick smear of black grime. He looked at the rusted hinges holding a pressurized gas canister to the wall.

"Dr. Fratire," Victor said, not turning around. "How many personnel work in this specific room?"

"Uh, usually six, sir," Fratire stammered, wringing his hands. "Though we've had rolling brownouts on this sector, so they've been working in shifts to save power."

Victor turned around. There was no yelling, just absolute, terrifying authority.

"Shut it down."

"Sir?"

"Shut it all down," Victor repeated, stepping toward the facility manager. "Kill the power to the primary breakers for this entire sector. Evacuate the staff. Cancel every single project, experiment, and requisition order currently active in this building. I am not running a rusted deathtrap disguised as a defense contractor."

Fratire’s face drained of color. "But—Dr. Thorne, sir! We can't just cut the power! The primary seismic penetrator test is scheduled for 0800 tomorrow morning! The military brass are expecting a status report on the drill!"

Erika stepped forward, holding up the espresso-stained folder. "You are planning to run a high-yield deep-earth drill test tomorrow? In a facility that props up its centrifuges with cardboard?"

"The... the drill is on an isolated sub-grid, Professor Thorne," Fratire squeaked, backing up slightly. "It’s housed deep in the bedrock, far below the cut-and-cover levels. It's ready to go."

Victor stared at the terrified manager for a long, silent moment. The anger in his eyes shifted into something much colder.

"Take me to the drill," Victor ordered. "Take us as deep into the Earth as this facility goes."

## The Belly of the Beast

The industrial freight elevator groaned as it descended far past the concrete safety of the cut-and-cover levels. Level B4 was miles above them now. Dr. Fratire stood rigidly in the corner, clutching his clipboard, while Victor and Erika watched the depth gauge steadily tick downward.

As the elevator sank deeper into the bedrock, the air inside the steel cage began to change. The stale, mildewed chill of the upper labs was entirely gone, replaced by a dense, oppressive heat that seemed to radiate through the elevator’s floorboards.

"Is the ventilation system failing down here as well?" Victor asked, loosening his silk tie as a bead of sweat rolled down his neck.

"No, sir," Dr. Fratire replied, wiping his own brow. "The geothermal gradient in this specific rock formation is just... unusually aggressive. The previous administration authorized high-capacity coolant pumps to keep the ambient temperature manageable for the machinery."

The elevator finally jolted to a halt with a heavy, metallic clang. The grating slid open, revealing a cavernous, floodlit subterranean hangar carved directly into the dense, black basalt.

Suspended in the center of the cavern, held by massive reinforced steel scaffolding, was the deep-earth drill. It was pristine, gleaming under the halogen lights. The bit itself was a terrifying, multi-tiered helix of titanium and tungsten carbide, easily the size of a city bus, pointed directly at a precisely mapped bore-hole in the cavern floor.

"State of the art," Dr. Fratire said, a hint of pride bleeding through his terror. "The Mark IV Seismic Penetrator. The military brass are expecting it to easily pierce the lower mantle boundary tomorrow morning."

But Erika wasn't looking at the drill.

She had walked over to the bare, carved wall of the cavern. She pressed her hand flat against the black basalt. She pulled it back almost immediately, her eyes widening.

The heat in the room wasn't just aggressive. It was suffocating. The air smelled faintly of sulfur and superheated silica.

"Dad," Erika said, her voice tight.

Victor turned. He knew that tone. It was the exact tone she used when an experiment was about to critically fail. "What is it, Erika?"

"Dr. Fratire, the previous administration classified this site as an extinct volcanic plug, correct? Just like Mole Hill further down the valley?" she asked, her breathing growing shallow.

"Yes, Professor. That's why the site was chosen. The basalt is incredibly dense—"

"Basalt is also an insulator," Erika interrupted, stepping away from the wall. "But this rock is baking. Do you have any idea how much thermal energy is required to heat a bedrock layer this thick? The sheer scale of the energy transfer happening right now means there is a massive, highly pressurized system directly beneath us."

Dr. Fratire gave a nervous, dismissive chuckle. "Professor Thorne, with all due respect, our initial geological surveys—"

"Were assumed!" Erika snapped, genuine fear bleeding into her eyes. "You assumed this was a dead twin to Mole Hill! Dad, this isn't an extinct plug. The kinetic and thermal energy radiating through this wall means the magma chamber is active. And it's huge."

Victor stared at the terrified manager for a long, silent moment. The anger in his eyes shifted into something much colder. He didn't need to see a forged geological survey. If his daughter told him the energy readings meant they were standing on a loaded gun, they were standing on a loaded gun.

Victor looked from Erika to the massive, pristine drill bit poised over the bore-hole. If that drill activated tomorrow and punctured the pressure cap holding back that thermal energy... the ensuing eruption wouldn't just destroy the facility. It would wipe the entire Eastern Seaboard off the map.

"Dr. Fratire," Victor said, the absolute authority of a billionaire defense contractor echoing in the cavern. "Disable the drill. Cut the power lines. Lock the primary console. This machine does not power on tomorrow, or ever."

## The Override

The ride up the B2 executive suite was suffocatingly tense. The moment the gold-plated elevator doors chimed and slid open, Professor Erika Thorne didn't wait for her father or the terrified facility manager.

She marched straight past the puddle of cold espresso and the dead, unplugged gaming computer on the CEO's desk, heading directly for the secure, multi-line communications console bolted to the far wall.

"Professor Thorne, please wait," Dr. Fratire stammered, his face pale as he chased after her, his clipboard trembling against his chest. "This is a Level-5 classified defense installation. All external communications must be routed through General Vance’s military liaisons. You cannot just broadcast unencrypted—"

"The military classification of this facility ended twenty minutes ago when I signed the final acquisition papers," Dr. Victor Thorne interrupted. His voice was a low, dangerous rumble that instantly froze the manager in his tracks. Victor stepped into the room, adjusting his cuffs with an icy, terrifying calm. "My daughter is making a phone call, Peter. If you attempt to stop her, I will have the federal agents upstairs arrest you as an accessory to treason."

Dr. Fratire snapped his mouth shut, backing away until his shoulders hit the faux-mahogany wall.

Erika slammed her hand onto the console, bypassing the internal facility network and forcing an external hardline connection. She dialed a direct, unlisted number from memory and hit the speaker button.

The line rang twice.

*"United States Geological Survey, Office of the Director,"* a crisp, professional voice answered through the speaker.

"This is Professor Erika Thorne," she said, her voice sharp and commanding, carrying the absolute authority of one of the most respected volcanologists in the country. "I have a Priority-One geological emergency. Put Dr. Linus on the line. Now."

*"Professor Thorne? One moment, please."*

There was a brief click, followed by the sound of shuffling papers.

*"Erika?"* Dr. Linus’s voice echoed through the B2 office. He sounded surprised, but immediately attentive. When a professor of her caliber called a government director with a Priority-One, bureaucratic pleasantries vanished. *"What is going on? Where are you?"*

"I am currently standing in the B2 control suite of the Pebble Burrow Institute in Middletown, Virginia," Erika stated.

*"Middletown?"* Dr. Linus’s tone shifted instantly from surprise to sharp frustration. *"Erika, that is a restricted black site. General Peter Vance barred my geologists from that valley three years ago under the Espionage Act. He claimed it was a stable, extinct volcanic plug. We've been entirely blind to their operations."*

"General Vance lied to you, Linus, and my father just bought the mountain out from under him," Erika said rapidly, leaning over the console. "The ban is lifted, and I need you to listen to me very carefully. I just came up from the deep-earth cavern at the bottom of the main shaft. The ambient temperature down there is one hundred and forty degrees Fahrenheit."

Dead silence fell over the speakerphone.

As the Director of the USGS, Dr. Linus didn't need a textbook to understand the horrifying implications of that number.

*"Erika..."* Dr. Linus said, his voice dropping to a hollow whisper. *"That cavern is carved entirely out of dense basalt rock. Basalt is a severe thermal insulator. For an insulated cavern at that depth to sustain an ambient temperature of one hundred and forty degrees..."*

"The kinetic vibrations and thermal radiation are off the charts," Erika confirmed, her knuckles turning white as she gripped the edge of the console. "It is not a dead plug. They built this entire facility directly on top of a highly pressurized, active magma chamber."

*"My god,"* Linus breathed. The sound of a chair violently scraping against a floor echoed through the line. *"If that pressure cap is compromised—"*

"It's about to be," Erika cut in, her eyes locked on Dr. Fratire, who looked like he was about to pass out. "The military has a tungsten-carbide deep-earth drill the size of a city bus suspended in that cavern. They are scheduled to initiate a manual pierce of the mantle boundary tomorrow morning at zero-eight-hundred hours."

*"No!"* Linus shouted, the sudden panic in his voice vibrating the speaker module. *"Erika, if they puncture that mantle boundary, the sudden depressurization will trigger a VEI-8 volcanic event! The pyroclastic flow will vaporize Middletown, and the ash cloud will bury the entire Eastern Seaboard!"*

"I know," Erika said grimly. "We've manually severed the power to the lower sector to stall the test. But I need your authority to permanently end this. I need an emergency deep-earth mapping team on site immediately. Bring your own seismometers, bring your thermal imaging, bring everything. We need undeniable, federal civilian data to force the Pentagon to stand down."

*"I am overriding the military grid right now,"* Dr. Linus ordered, the sound of frantic shouting already audible in the background of his office. *"We are scrambling the emergency response teams. I am personally calling the Secretary of Energy and the White House Situation Room to brief them on the thermal data you just provided. We will be at your gates in two hours, Professor. Do whatever you have to do to keep that drill turned off."*

"Understood. Thorne out."

Erika hit the disconnect button. The silence that rushed back into the B2 office was deafening.

Victor slowly turned his gaze from the communications console back to Dr. Fratire. The facility manager was physically shaking, his clipboard clattering to the floor. The reality of the apocalyptic scenario he had blindly helped manage had finally shattered his corporate obedience.

"Well, Dr. Fratire," Victor said, his voice as cold as ice. "It appears the United States Government is coming to inspect your facility. I suggest we go back to the B1 lobby to greet them."

## The Arrival

The flashing red and blue strobes of the FBI vehicles cast harsh shadows against the rusted brick of the surface-level maintenance shed. Professor Erika Thorne stood just inside the glass doors, her arms crossed, watching a convoy of unmarked, heavy-duty utility trucks barrel down the access road.

The lead truck slammed into park, and Dr. Linus, the Director of the United States Geological Survey, practically jumped out. He was trailed by half a dozen geologists hauling heavy, reinforced Pelican cases filled with deep-earth seismometers and thermal imaging gear.

"Erika," Dr. Linus said, his face pale and completely devoid of bureaucratic pleasantries.

"Linus," Erika nodded, stepping back to let them inside. "We don't have much time. Vance's military liaisons are completely locked out of the grid, but they know we've severed the power. Follow me."

## The Descent

Erika led the team of scientists down the concrete stairs from the street level, bypassing the slow surface elevator, and emerged into the B1 lobby. The FBI agents securing the B1 security desk stepped aside as Erika swiped her new overriding credentials.

The heavy, hazard-striped blast doors of the main staff entrance shrieked in protest as they parted.

"The cut-and-cover labs on this level are a rusted disaster," Erika explained over her shoulder, leading the geologists past the Cold War-era Cold Rooms and directly toward the primary personnel elevator. "But the real threat is miles down."

They piled into the lift, the heavy doors sliding shut as Erika hit the button for Level B4.

## The B4 Checkpoint

The elevator deposited them into the sprawling, subterranean labyrinth of B4. But Erika didn't lead them toward the luxury submarine-style apartment where her cousins were staying. Instead, she took them down a long, utilitarian concrete corridor completely stripped of Marcus Vance's faux-mahogany aesthetic.

At the far end of the hall, a massive, reinforced steel barricade completely blocked the path. Red warning lights bathed the concrete. It was a fully operational military checkpoint.

"The tunnel on the other side of that checkpoint runs directly under the property line and into the subterranean levels of the Joint Army-Air Force Base next door," Erika explained, gesturing to the heavy blast doors. "It’s how Lieutenant General Vance moved between his command post and his brother’s shell corporation without ever seeing the sun."

Dr. Linus stared at the military barricade in disgust. "A private back door to the Pentagon's wallet."

"Exactly," Erika nodded. She turned away from the military line and pointed to a massive, heavily rusted set of four-panel sliding doors set into the civilian side of the concrete wall.

"But this is our stop. The deep-earth freight elevator."

## The Confirmation

Erika hit the call button. The four leaves of the heavy metal doors slid open with a violent, metallic groan, revealing the massive industrial cage inside.

The USGS team hauled their Pelican cases into the cab. Erika hit the descent button, and the cage plummeted into the pitch-black basalt shaft.

As they dropped past the concrete foundation and entered the raw bedrock, the temperature immediately began to spike. The air grew thick, suffocating, and reeked of sulfur. By the time the freight elevator slammed into the lowest terminal, the geologists were already drenched in sweat.

The grated doors opened into the 140-degree furnace of the cavern.

"My god," Dr. Linus gasped, instantly wiping his glasses as they fogged over.

Suspended in the center of the cavern was the Mark IV Seismic Penetrator, its massive titanium-tungsten helix gleaming under the halogen lights.

The USGS team didn't hesitate. Ignoring the blistering heat, they cracked open their cases and immediately pressed their high-resolution thermal scanners and seismic sensors against the exposed basalt walls.

For three agonizing minutes, the only sound in the cavern was the hiss of the overworked coolant pumps and the frantic clicking of the geologists' keyboards.

"Director," a senior geologist said, his voice shaking as he stared at the readout on his monitor. He turned the screen so Dr. Linus and Erika could see.

The digital cross-section of the rock beneath their feet wasn't solid black or grey. It was a blinding, angry crimson.

"The Professor is right," Dr. Linus whispered, staring at the sheer scale of the thermal transfer. He looked up at the massive, suspended drill bit, a look of absolute horror crossing his face. "This isn't a geothermal vent. It's a localized, highly pressurized magma chamber. If General Vance forces this drill through the mantle boundary... it will trigger a VEI-8 eruption."

Linus turned to Erika, his eyes wide. "We are standing on a supervolcano. And the military built a detonator right on top of it."

## The Sound of Silence

Down on Level B4, the apocalypse was the furthest thing from anyone's mind.

Amanda was gathering the empty pizza boxes from the stainless-steel kitchen island, tossing crumpled napkins into the trash. The rippling blue light from the massive acrylic pool windows cast a calm, aquatic glow over the subterranean living room. On the plush sectional sofa, Ashleigh and Jessika were debating which bedroom had the best closet space, while Drake was trying to figure out how to hook his portable CD player up to the high-end surround sound system left behind by the previous CEO.

It was cozy. It was safe.

Then, the lights died.

The entire B4 apartment plunged into absolute, pitch-black darkness for three agonizingly long seconds. The only illumination was the faint, eerie blue glow of the pool water pressing against the glass.

"Whoa!" Drake yelped, dropping a handful of audio cables.

"Nobody move!" Amanda barked instantly, her voice cutting through the dark with practiced, older-sister authority. "Stay exactly where you are so nobody trips."

A second later, the lights flickered back on, buzzing slightly before returning to their normal, warm brightness.

"Did we blow a fuse?" Jessika asked, blinking against the sudden light and looking up at the ceiling.

"I don't think so," Ashleigh said, her brow furrowing. She stood up from the sofa, tilting her head. "Listen."

Since they had stepped off the elevator, there had been a sound. It wasn't something they actively noticed—just a deep, low-frequency vibration that rattled at the very edge of their hearing. It was the ambient, collective heartbeat of a massive military-grade black site: the roar of high-capacity ventilation turbines, the hum of industrial centrifuges in the upper labs, and the distant, terrifying thrum of the deep-earth drill's coolant pumps miles below.

Now, that heartbeat was stopping.

A deep, metallic groan echoed through the concrete walls, so low they felt it in their chests more than they heard it. It was followed by the distinct, descending whine of massive turbines spinning down. The vibrations in the floor slowly faded away.

Within thirty seconds, the sprawling subterranean mansion was engulfed in absolute, suffocating silence. The only sound left in the room was the gentle lapping of the pool water against the acrylic glass.

Amanda immediately went into mother-hen mode. She crossed the room, instinctively putting herself between her younger siblings and the double doors leading to the foyer.

"Okay, nobody panic," Amanda said, keeping her voice calm but firm. "Uncle Victor said he was shutting down some operations. They're probably just transferring power grids or doing maintenance."

She walked over to the heavy landline phone on the counter and picked up the receiver, holding it to her ear. A steady, clear dial tone hummed back at her.

"Dial tone is active," Amanda confirmed, letting out a small sigh of relief. She reached over and tapped the spacebar on the high-end workstation sitting on the nearby desk. The monitor sprang to life, displaying a perfectly stable internet connection. "Network is up, too. We aren't cut off."

"It sounded like the whole mountain just turned off, though," Drake muttered, looking nervously toward the glass wall, as if expecting something to come swimming through the pool.

"Well, whatever they turned off, it didn't hit our grid," Amanda reasoned, putting the phone back on the hook. "This is a restricted facility. They probably test the backup generators all the time."

Ashleigh crossed her arms, rubbing her shoulders against a sudden chill that had nothing to do with the room's temperature. "Should we try calling Uncle Victor's office on B2?"

"Let's just give him a few minutes," Amanda decided. "He and Erika are busy sorting out the paperwork. We stay right here until they check in. Understood?"

The triplets nodded in unison. None of them wanted to wander out into the silent, cavernous hallways anyway. They sat back down in the living room, surrounded by working technology and perfect lighting, completely unaware that the sudden, eerie silence outside their door was the sound of the world not ending.

## Returning to the Surface

The heavy steel doors of the private elevator slid open with a soft ding, breaking the tense silence of the Level B4 foyer.

Amanda, Drake, Ashleigh, and Jessika practically jumped off the sectional sofa. They hurried toward the double doors, expecting to see their uncle looking annoyed about a blown fuse.

Instead, Victor and Erika stepped out looking like they had just walked through an oven. Victor had completely discarded his suit jacket and loosened his tie, his usually pristine dress shirt clinging to his shoulders with sweat. Erika looked even worse; her hair was pulled back into a messy knot, her sleeves were rolled up, and she carried a faint, acrid scent of sulfur and superheated rock that immediately clashed with the filtered, air-conditioned air of the luxury apartment.

"Uncle Victor! Are you okay?" Amanda asked, stopping in her tracks. "The power flickered, and the whole facility just went quiet."

"We're fine, Amanda," Victor said, his voice raspy. He walked straight past the high-end workstation and collapsed into one of the plush, faux-leather armchairs near the pool window. He ran a hand over his face, looking exhausted but fiercely alert. "The power flicker was intentional. We just manually severed the main feed to the lowest sector."

"What happened down there?" Ashleigh asked, eyeing the dirt on Erika's shoes. "Did something break?"

Erika walked over to the kitchen island, bypassing the leftover pizza to grab a large bottle of cold water from the stainless-steel fridge. She drank half of it in one go before turning to her cousins.

"Nothing broke," Erika said, her voice dead serious. "But it was about to. Tomorrow morning, at 0800."

### The Apocalyptic Reality

The triplets exchanged a confused look. Drake stepped forward, gesturing to the silent walls. "Was it a drill? The noise that stopped?"

"Yes. A Mark IV Seismic Penetrator," Victor said, leaning forward and resting his elbows on his knees. "The previous CEO—the man who built this absurd mansion—scheduled a deep-earth penetration test for tomorrow. He assumed the mountain above us was a dead, solid block of basalt. A perfectly safe place to test military hardware."

"It's not dead," Erika added, setting her water bottle down. She looked right at Amanda. "The rock wall down in the main cavern is practically boiling. The kinetic energy readings are off the charts. The previous administration didn't just build a facility on a dormant volcano. They built it directly on top of a highly pressurized magma chamber."

The living room went dead silent. The rippling blue light from the pool suddenly felt a lot less like a luxury submarine and a lot more like a fragile glass bubble.

"Wait," Jessika said, her voice dropping to a whisper. "You're saying if they turned that drill on tomorrow..."

"...It would have acted like a needle popping a pressurized balloon," Erika finished bluntly. "The sudden depressurization would have triggered a localized supervolcano. The eruption would have instantly vaporized this entire facility, cratered Middletown, and leveled everything from Washington, D.C. to the naval base down the coast in a matter of hours."

Drake’s jaw dropped. He looked slowly down at the floor, as if expecting the carpet to suddenly turn to lava. Ashleigh grabbed his arm, her knuckles turning white.

"We stopped it," Victor said firmly, making sure his nieces and nephew looked at him, not the floor. "The power is cut, the console is locked, and the drill is officially dead. You are entirely safe. But the sheer, monumental stupidity required to let it get this far..." His jaw tightened. "The man who ran this place didn't just embezzle funds for a bowling alley and a computer. He nearly wiped out the Eastern Seaboard out of pure negligence."

### The Burden of Ownership

Amanda let out a shaky breath, stepping back to lean against the kitchen counter. "So... what now? Do we pack up and leave? Hand it over to the government?"

"No," Victor said, standing back up and straightening his posture. The exhaustion in his eyes was rapidly being replaced by a cold, calculating fury. "Erika and I just spent billions buying this entire corporation and taking it private. We own every rusted pipe, every frayed wire, and every uncashed check in this mountain. We bought it to fix it, and we just averted the worst-case scenario. But if the previous CEO missed a supervolcano because he couldn't be bothered to read a geological survey, I need to know exactly what else he was ignoring."

Erika nodded, picking up her water bottle. "The labs on the upper levels are falling apart. The structural integrity is completely compromised. As the new owners, we need a full, immediate audit of every single room, hallway, and storage closet in this complex."

Victor looked at the five of them. "I need you all to stay here. The B4 and B3 levels are fully independent and structurally secure. Use the computers, watch a movie, or go bowling. Do not leave this suite. Erika and I are going back up."

"You're going back out there?" Drake asked, still looking a little pale.

"We own the Pebble Burrow Institute now," Victor said, buttoning his collar back up. "And we are going to see exactly what we bought."

## Level B1: The Motor Pool

The gold-painted elevator deposited Victor and Erika back on Level B1, just past the heavy blast doors. Instead of heading back to the abandoned B2 executive suite, Victor marched directly down the wide, sloped concrete corridor that led to the facility’s subterranean motor pool.

If the upper labs were a 1970s time capsule of neglected science, the motor pool was a graveyard of vehicular manslaughter waiting to happen.

The cavernous concrete garage smelled of old oil, damp exhaust, and rotting rubber. Fluorescent lights flickered overhead, illuminating a small fleet of vehicles that were supposed to be used for secure transport, site maintenance, and official contractor business.

Victor stopped at the edge of the parking bays, crossing his arms. "Erika, tell me I'm not looking at our official transport fleet."

Erika walked over to a white, windowless 1990s cargo van bearing a faded, peeling Pebble Burrow Institute logo. She ran a finger along the deeply rusted wheel well.

"I wish I could, Dad," she sighed, crouching down to inspect the tires. "The tread on these tires is practically non-existent. You can see the wire mesh poking through the rubber."

She stood up and walked down the line, her disbelief growing with every vehicle she passed:

The Security Patrol Cars: Two late-90s sedans with cracked windshields, sagging suspensions, and duct tape holding one of the side mirrors in place.

The Heavy Transport: A military-surplus flatbed truck that looked like it had survived Desert Storm but lost the war against Virginia humidity. A massive puddle of dark transmission fluid was actively pooling beneath its chassis.

The Inspection Stickers: Erika leaned in to look at the Virginia State Inspection sticker on the windshield of a beat-up utility pickup. "Dad, this inspection sticker is technically valid for 2003, but whoever signed off on it was either blind or bribed. The exhaust pipe is literally held up by a wire coat hanger."

It was a fleet designed for a shell company, not a multi-billion dollar defense contractor.

## The Crown Jewel of Embezzlement

Victor didn't say anything. His eyes were locked on the far end of the garage, where an oversized, brightly lit parking bay had been cordoned off with velvet ropes.

Sitting in the center of the bay was the exact opposite of the rusted fleet.

It was a custom, completely pristine 2003 luxury SUV. It was a massive, blacked-out behemoth—heavily armored, sitting on custom chrome rims, and polished to a mirror shine. It looked like a vehicle designed to transport a head of state, or in this case, a corrupt CEO who wanted to feel like one.

Victor slowly walked over to the SUV, his polished shoes echoing in the quiet garage. He peered through the heavily tinted driver's side window.

"Custom cream leather interior," Victor muttered, taking inventory. "Burl wood dashboard. An integrated satellite phone system. And unless I am very much mistaken, those door panels are lined with Level IV ballistic plating."

Erika walked up beside him, shaking her head. "Why would he need a customized, armor-plated luxury tank to drive from a restricted military base to a maintenance shed?"

"Because he wasn't spending his own money," Victor said, his voice dropping into that icy, dangerous calm again. "He billed the Department of Defense for a 'High-Security VIP Transport Vehicle' and used it to commute to his taxpayer-funded bowling alley."

Victor stepped back from the gleaming SUV and looked back down the line of rusted, dangerous company cars. The contrast was physically nauseating. The staff who actually ran the facility were driving around in rolling deatraps, while Marcus Vance rode in air-conditioned, bulletproof luxury.

"Erika," Victor said, pulling a sleek silver pen from his pocket and making a note on his palm. "Add the motor pool to the absolute demolition list. I want every single one of those fleet vehicles sold for scrap metal by the end of the week. Order a fleet of brand-new, safely inspected trucks for the staff."

"And the armored SUV?" Erika asked, raising an eyebrow.

Victor looked at the pristine reflection of himself in the black paint. "Have the mechanics strip out the luxury interior, rip out the CD player, and sell the chassis back to the military at a steep discount. I refuse to have this monument to his ego sitting in my garage."
