# Part 1: The Expanded Jurisdiction – Thursday, October 10, 2053 – 09:30

The sprawling residential living room of The Sanctuary was bathed in the crisp, golden light of an Eden autumn. The massive hydro-glass windows offered a breathtaking view of the valley below, where the emerald alien pines were just beginning to tip with seasonal shades of amber and rust.

Inside the mountain, the Colton family was operating in a state of perfectly synchronized, beautiful chaos.

Wyatt Colton sat in the center of the plush rug, serving as a human jungle gym. At four months old, Tyler and Evan were officially out of the newborn phase, and their genetics were already making themselves glaringly obvious. They weren't soft, fragile infants; they were absolute bowling balls of dense, solid mass.

Wyatt had one thick arm wrapped around Tyler and the other supporting Evan as the identical twin boys aggressively pushed their heavy little legs against his thighs, trying to force themselves into standing positions.

"I'm telling you, Sarah," Wyatt grunted, chuckling as Tyler let out a deep, booming babble and nearly headbutted his collarbone. "I've never felt infants this solid. They weigh twice what the girls did at this age. Look at the grip strength on this kid."

Evan had reached out, his chubby fingers locking onto Wyatt’s black t-shirt with an iron-clad grip, refusing to let go.

Sarah was sitting nearby on the oversized leather sofa, looking thoroughly rested and entirely radiant. The grueling physical toll of her final pregnancy had completely faded. She smiled, watching her husband wrestle with their youngest sons.

"They're going to be massive, Wyatt," Sarah agreed warmly. "They're already trying to bulldoze their way through tummy time. I think the estate is going to need reinforced walls by the time they're teenagers."

A few feet away on the molecularly printed play mat, the middle tier of the family was actively engaged in an academic summit.

At nearly four years old, Jack was sitting cross-legged, holding a brightly colored set of wooden building blocks. Sixteen-month-old Molly and Claire were sitting perfectly upright in front of him, their identical dark eyes wide with focus. They had moved far beyond simple babbling; their cognitive development was accelerating rapidly under Jack's patient, diplomatic tutelage.

"This one goes on top," Jack explained, demonstrating the structural physics of a wooden tower. He handed a red block to Molly.

Molly took it, her face a picture of absolute concentration. She carefully placed it on the top of the stack. She looked up at her brother, her face breaking into a massive, triumphant grin.

"Jack build!" Molly announced, her articulation delightfully sharp for a toddler.

"Me too!" Claire demanded immediately, reaching her tiny hand out. "Claire block! Put on top!"

"You're both doing a great job," Jack praised seamlessly, handing the next block to Claire.

Sarah watched them, her heart melting at the clear, concise sentences forming in her daughters' mouths.

"Aria," Sarah called out to the ceiling array, her voice entirely relaxed. "Can you log the twins' verbal progression in the developmental archives?"

"Certainly, Sarah," Aria’s synthesized voice chimed with a warm, familiar familiarity. "Molly and Claire are currently tracking in the ninety-eighth percentile for expressive language. Jack's continuous verbal engagement has provided an optimal linguistic framework. I will update their specific profiles alongside Ellie, Nora, Leo, Tyler, and Evan immediately."

Wyatt smiled, bouncing Evan gently on his knee. Hearing Aria seamlessly list off all seven of their children by name—completely devoid of the sterile 'Subject' designations from the early years—was a profound, daily reminder that they weren't running a medical experiment. They were raising a real family.

Suddenly, the heavy, flat-footed thud of canvas boots echoed from the hallway.

Wyatt looked up as the Vanguard entered the living room.

At nearly eight years old, the triplets had grown significantly taller, but their unbreakable formation remained identical. Nora walked smoothly into the room using her custom-fabricated titanium forearm crutches, her weaker left leg perfectly supported by the sleek, shock-absorbing gear. She stayed right at the side of Leo's titanium Daily Driver wheelchair, keeping her physical proximity close so he felt the safety of her presence. Leo was humming a happy, steady note, his ruggedized tablet resting on his lap tray.

And leading the patrol was Ellie.

She wasn't just checking the nursery anymore. As the younger kids had grown, and with Jack naturally stepping up to oversee the toddlers, Ellie had realized her commanding presence was required on a much larger scale. The nursery was too small of a perimeter for her tactical mind.

She had officially graduated. She was no longer the Commanding Officer of the Nursery; she was the Commanding Officer of the Estate.

Ellie marched to the center of the living room, her dark eyes quickly scanning the perimeter. She noted the structural integrity of Jack's block tower. She assessed the grip strength of the heavy twin boys wrestling with Wyatt.

"Perimeter check," Ellie announced, looking at Wyatt with absolute seriousness. "South airlocks are sealed. Hydroponics doors locked. Aegis grid green."

"Excellent work, Commander," Wyatt responded, offering a crisp, respectful nod from the floor. "The base is secure."

Ellie gave a firm nod of satisfaction. She didn't need to micromanage Jack's block-building, and she didn't need to oversee the twins. The inner circle was functioning perfectly. Her job was to make sure the walls holding them all safe never cracked.

She turned to her unit. She tapped the wooden rail of Leo's wheelchair, and then looked at Nora.

"Base is secure," Ellie informed them. "Library next. Reading time."

Nora smiled brightly, leaning over to guide Leo's shaking hand to his tablet. Leo slammed his palm against the bright blue square depicting a book.

"Read," the tablet's automated voice declared into the room.

"Let's go," Ellie ordered, leading her siblings smoothly across the living room and down the hall toward the mahogany-paneled library.

Wyatt watched his three oldest children leave the room, completely awestruck by the sheer, undeniable competence of the family they had built. The timeline they had established years ago was holding perfectly. The Vanguard was growing into brilliant, capable leaders. Jack was already a master diplomat, the infant girls were thriving, and the twin boys were going to be absolute powerhouses.

"They're really doing it," Wyatt murmured, looking up at Sarah from the floor. "They're running the mountain."

"We're just the maintenance crew now, Wyatt," Sarah laughed softly, sipping her tea as she watched her chaotic, beautiful, perfectly balanced family. "The kids are officially in charge."
