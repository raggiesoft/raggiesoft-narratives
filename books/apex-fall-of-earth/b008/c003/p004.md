# Part 4: Arrival Day – Saturday, July 19, 2058 – 06:00 (Local Eden Time)

The cavernous, brilliantly lit staging bay on Sub-Level 6 was silent except for the low, rhythmic hum of ten artificial gestation cylinders.

The entire Colton family was assembled on the polished durasteel floor. Wyatt and Sarah stood at the center, their eyes locked on the amber fluid inside the glass chambers. Behind them, the younger tier of the family—five-year-old Tyler and Evan, and six-year-old Molly and Claire—were watching with wide, breathless anticipation.

At the primary holographic console, the twelve-year-old Vanguard stood at their post. Leo sat in his titanium wheelchair, perfectly anchored by Nora’s reassuring weight against his right shoulder and Ellie’s rigid, protective stance on his left.

"Fluid purge sequence initiating in T-minus ten seconds," Aria’s voice echoed from the overhead acoustic array. The synthesized AI had entirely abandoned her neutral, clinical baseline over the last nine months. Her voice was bright, energetic, and rolled with the distinct, melodic vowels of Sydney, New South Wales. *"Alright, team. Stand by. It’s about to get a bit noisy in here."*

Wyatt wrapped his arm tightly around Sarah’s waist. "Ready, Vanguard?"

"Unit is ready," Ellie confirmed, her dark eyes scanning the ten glass cylinders.

"Initiate the drain, Leo," Sarah whispered, tears already pricking the corners of her eyes.

With Nora steadying his arm, Leo reached down to his AAC tablet and slammed his palm against the bright green execution rune.

*"Drain,"* the tablet commanded.

Instantly, the heavy mechanical valves at the base of the ten towering glass cylinders hissed open. The glowing amber amniotic fluid rapidly began to drain away. Inside the chambers, the ten fully developed, full-term infants slowly descended as the fluid level dropped, their bodies gently settling onto the padded, molecularly printed surgical nets waiting at the bottom.

The moment the fluid cleared their faces, the automated medical armatures moved with blinding, flawless precision.

Micro-suction catheters cleared their tiny airways. Synthetic umbilical tethers were clamped and painlessly severed. Thermal swaddles were rapidly wrapped around their small, slippery bodies to preserve their core temperatures.

For three agonizing seconds, the staging bay was completely silent as the ten newborns experienced the cold, unfiltered air of the outside world for the very first time.

Then, the first baby—a robust little boy in Cylinder 4—opened his mouth and let out a furious, deafening wail.

A split second later, a girl in Cylinder 7 joined him. Then another. And another.

Within ten seconds, the sterile medical bay was completely overwhelmed by the chaotic, beautiful, ear-splitting symphony of ten crying infants.

*"Good on ya, little rippers!"* Aria cheered, her Australian accent practically beaming through the speakers. *"Airways are perfectly clear. Heart rates are strong across the board. Apgar scores are all nines and tens. We have ten incredibly healthy little mates on deck!"*

"Open the cylinders," Wyatt commanded, a massive, tearful grin breaking across his scarred face.

The thick glass walls of the artificial wombs seamlessly retracted into the ceiling. The heavy robotic arms gently lifted the ten swaddled babies, transitioning them smoothly into the row of heated, transparent neonatal bassinets lined up across the bay.

Sarah let out a breathless sob of pure joy, stepping forward to look into the first bassinet.

The Colton family broke formation, clustering around the ten crying infants. Tyler and Evan stood by the first bassinet, their massive five-year-old frames completely dwarfing the tiny baby boy inside. Molly and Claire were practically vibrating with excitement, holding onto Sarah’s tunic as they peered over the plastic edges.

"They need names, Mom," eight-year-old Jack said, his diplomatic voice carrying clearly over the chorus of cries. He looked up at the ceiling. "Aria, they need to fit the cultural matrix. What are good names from Sydney?"

*"I've got a brilliant list of historical favorites from the New South Wales regional archives, Jack,"* Aria chimed warmly, projecting a floating blue list of names above the central console. *"Have a squiz at these."*

Sarah walked slowly down the line of bassinets, her heart overflowing. She looked at the five boys and the five girls, checking their demographic tags on the bio-monitors.

"Okay," Sarah smiled, wiping a tear from her cheek. "Let's make them official."

She stopped at the first five bassinets, looking down at the squalling baby boys.

"Lachlan," Sarah named the first, tapping the edge of his bassinet. She moved down the line. "Harrison. Oliver. Ethan. And Lucas."

*"Names registered to the Genesis database,"* Aria confirmed seamlessly.

Wyatt walked down the second half of the line, looking at the five baby girls. Their dark hair and flushed, crying faces were the absolute realization of everything he had fought to protect.

"Isla," Wyatt rumbled gently, assigning the first name. "Ruby. Mia. Harper."

He stepped up to the very last bassinet. Inside, the tenth baby—a little girl with a shock of dark hair—was kicking her tiny legs furiously against her thermal swaddle, crying with an intense, spirited determination.

Before Wyatt could assign the final name on Aria's list, Jack walked over to the bassinet.

The eight-year-old diplomat didn't flinch at the noise. He possessed the exact same gravitational, soothing empathy that had anchored his own younger siblings years ago. Jack reached into the open access port of the bassinet. He didn't try to pick her up; he just gently rested his index finger against the palm of her tiny, flailing hand.

The baby girl’s tiny fingers instantly snapped shut, gripping Jack's finger with surprising strength.

The physical anchor worked like magic. Her furious crying hitched, settling into a soft, wet hiccup. She slowly opened her dark, unfocused eyes, blinking against the bright lights of the medical bay as she blindly tried to locate the source of the steady, comforting warmth beside her.

Jack smiled, an incredibly gentle, completely innocent expression of pure pioneer hospitality. He leaned over the edge of the bassinet so his face was perfectly within her limited field of vision.

"Welcome to Eden," Jack whispered softly. "I'm Jack."

The baby girl let out a tiny, contented sigh, completely soothed by his voice. She didn't let go of his finger.

Wyatt and Sarah stood a few feet away, watching the profound, quiet interaction. They had built the physical infrastructure of the village, but right here, in this tiny interaction, the actual society of Planet Eden was being born. Jack was already bridging the gap between the Colton family and the Genesis cohort.

"What about her, Jack?" Sarah asked softly, stepping up beside her son. "What's her name?"

Jack looked up at the floating blue holographic list, his finger still securely held by the newborn girl. He scanned the remaining options from the Sydney archives, his eyes settling on one that felt perfectly right for the bright, spirited life holding onto him.

"Willow," Jack decided, looking back down at the baby. "Her name is Willow."

*"Willow registered,"* Aria chimed happily. *"The Genesis Ten are officially on the board. Absolutely brilliant work, everyone."*

Over at the central console, Ellie stood perfectly straight. She looked at the ten bassinets, evaluating the sheer volume of the new logistics. She looked at Lachlan, Harrison, Oliver, Ethan, Lucas, Isla, Ruby, Mia, Harper, and Willow. The perimeter of their world had just expanded massively.

Ellie reached out, placing her hand firmly on Leo's shoulder, and looked at Nora.

"Unit is growing," Ellie announced, a fierce, commanding pride in her dark eyes. "Perimeter holds."

Wyatt walked over, wrapping his massive arms around his twelve-year-old Vanguard. "The perimeter holds perfectly, Commander."

The empty, quiet mountain was suddenly filled with life. The 50,000 embryos were no longer just a theoretical concept sleeping in the ice; the very first generation of the new world was awake, breathing, and already holding hands with the family that had saved them.

The village of Genesis was officially alive.
