# Part 2: The Command Staff Celebration – Friday, January 29, 2058 – 10:30 (Local Eden Time)

The heavy brushed-steel doors of the pneumatic elevator hissed open on the residential level, instantly replacing the frigid, hyper-filtered air of the Genesis Vault with the warm, rich aroma of an absolute feast.

"The dining sector is fully prepped, Wyatt," Aria’s voice chimed from the ceiling array. True to the new cultural protocol, the AI was maintaining the bright, energetic Sydney accent. It was a completely new phonetic rhythm for the mountain, but the family was already adapting to the sunlit cadence. "I’ve dimmed the ambient lighting to reduce sensory glare for Leo, and the thermal regulators are holding at a perfect twenty-one degrees."

"Thank you, Aria," Wyatt smiled, his veteran instincts completely at rest as he led his massive family down the hall and into the sprawling living room.

Sarah had spent the entire morning before the vault trip in the hydroponics bay and the molecular kitchen, bypassing standard survival rations to synthesize a massive, perfectly engineered birthday feast. The reclaimed-wood dining table was completely covered with fresh vegetables, roasted proteins, and in the very center, a massive, multi-tiered molecularly printed chocolate cake.

The Vanguard moved toward the table as a single, indivisible organism.

Twelve-year-old Leo rolled his sleek titanium Daily Driver wheelchair up to the head of the table. His broad shoulders relaxed slightly in the dimmed, sensory-friendly lighting. He let out a low, steady baritone hum of approval at the sight of the food.

As always, his sisters flanked him perfectly. Nora slipped her arms out of her custom titanium forearm crutches, leaning them against the table. She slid into the chair on Leo’s right, immediately pressing her hip and shoulder flush against his arm to provide his grounding pressure. On his left, Ellie took her seat, her dark eyes scanning the dining room perimeter before she rested her hand firmly on Leo’s opposite shoulder.

"Perimeter secure," Ellie announced, offering a sharp nod. "Rations are acceptable."

"It's not just rations today, Commander. It's a celebration," Sarah smiled, taking her seat next to Wyatt.

Down at the other end of the table, the younger tiers were operating with flawless, military-grade efficiency.

Eight-year-old Jack, acting as the ultimate diplomatic overseer, was directing the heavy logistics.

"Tyler, Evan," Jack instructed, his vocabulary crisp and articulate. "Please deploy the gift crate. Remember to control your mass."

"Understood, Jack," Tyler replied in his deep, five-year-old voice.

"We have the payload," Evan confirmed.

The five-year-old identical twins stepped up to a heavy, molecularly printed durasteel crate resting near the sofa. Despite their young age, the boys were absolute physical powerhouses. Moving with synchronized, terrifyingly dense strength, Tyler grabbed the left handle and Evan grabbed the right. They lifted the hundred-pound crate without breaking a single sweat, carrying it smoothly across the room and setting it gently at the foot of the dining table so as not to startle Leo.

Six-year-old Molly and Claire immediately flanked the crate, ready to assist.

"The structural integrity of the cake is excellent, Mom," Claire observed, her dark eyes analyzing the dessert with sharp intelligence.

"It is highly aesthetically pleasing," Molly agreed, sitting up straight in her chair.

"Alright, squad," Wyatt announced, his chest swelling with an immense, unshakeable pride as he looked at the nine members of the Colton family. "Before we eat, we issue the new gear. You three officially initiated the decanting process today. You are twelve years old. You are the command staff now. And your gear needs to reflect that."

Jack stepped forward, pulling the first item from the durasteel crate. He walked over and handed it to Nora.

It was a sleek, silver, heavy-duty metallic case. Nora opened it carefully. Inside was a highly advanced, master-crafted botanical grafting kit, complete with microscopic titanium scalpels, nutrient-splicing tape, and a localized digital soil-analyzer.

Nora’s empathetic eyes widened with pure joy. She had recently taken a massive interest in Sarah’s hydroponics bay, displaying a natural, profound talent for keeping the delicate alien and Earth flora alive.

Nora tapped the AAC device strapped to her forearm.

"This is a beautiful upgrade, Mom and Dad," Nora's AAC chimed perfectly. "I will use it to cross-pollinate the Eden ferns with the Earth ivy. Thank you."

"You're very welcome, sweetheart," Sarah beamed.

Jack returned to the crate and pulled out a heavy, dark canvas garment. He handed it to Ellie.

Ellie inspected the item with absolute, ruthless tactical scrutiny. It was a brand new, custom-tailored tactical vest. But unlike her childhood gear, this one was woven from interwoven Kevlar and lightweight ballistic polymer. It featured advanced, modular magnetic pouches, a reinforced spine-guard, and a built-in, master-crafted titanium multi-tool holstered on the collarbone.

Ellie unzipped her old vest and immediately slipped the new armor over her shoulders. She adjusted the heavy nylon straps, testing her mobility and her center of gravity.

She looked at Wyatt, her rigid posture straightening with fierce, undeniable pride.

"Armor upgrade is flawless," Ellie declared, tapping the titanium multi-tool. "Combat readiness increased by forty percent. Excellent manufacturing, Dad."

"Only the best for the Commander," Wyatt grinned.

Finally, Jack reached into the crate with both hands. He pulled out something incredibly dense and heavy, carrying it carefully over to Leo.

It was a deep-black, highly specialized compression garment. Wyatt had engineered it in the Fab-Foundry specifically for Leo's growing, twelve-year-old frame. It was lined with heavy, dense micro-weights and featured a network of pneumatic, temperature-regulating bladders that could be inflated to provide exact, uniform pressure across his chest and back.

Leo’s eyes locked onto the heavy fabric. He understood its geometric purpose instantly.

With Nora and Ellie steadying his arms, Wyatt helped Leo slip the heavy compression vest over his shirt. Wyatt tapped a small dial on the side, engaging the pneumatic bladders.

The vest tightened smoothly, wrapping Leo in a perfectly distributed, crushing embrace.

Leo let out a massive, shuddering breath. His scissored legs relaxed instantly against his wheelchair footboards. The overwhelming sensory input of his changing teenage body was completely neutralized by the heavy, flawless pressure.

He didn't flap his hands. He just closed his eyes for a long moment, a deep, vibrating baritone purr vibrating in his chest. He reached down to his lap tray and typed rapidly.

"The localized pressure is highly efficient. My nervous system is perfectly regulated," Leo’s deep, synthesized voice announced to the table. He looked at his parents, his dark eyes shining with absolute clarity. "I am secure. We are ready to consume the birthday rations."

"Too right, Leo!" Aria’s bright Sydney accent cheered from the ceiling. "Let's tuck in, everyone!"

Wyatt wrapped his arm around Sarah’s shoulders as the chaotic, beautiful machinery of their family sprang into action. Jack, Tyler, and Evan began passing plates. Molly and Claire debated the physics of slicing the cake, and the Vanguard remained perfectly, unbrokenly anchored at the head of the table.

Down in the dark, ten artificial wombs were glowing with new life. But up here in the sunlight, the Colton family was celebrating exactly who they were: a fortress, a family, and the architects of a brand new world.
