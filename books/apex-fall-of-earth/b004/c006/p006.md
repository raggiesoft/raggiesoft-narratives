# Part 6: The Backlog – Tuesday, November 1, 2044 – 09:30

The 144-hour mark arrived exactly as Aria had predicted.

Wyatt and Sarah stood barefoot in the Operations center, Wyatt's hands resting lightly on the heavy mechanical throttle levers.

*"Slipstream telemetry indicates we have reached the coordinates for System 88-Tango,"* Aria’s synthesized voice echoed smoothly from the bulkhead speakers. *"Ready for manual disengagement, Wyatt."*

Wyatt looked at Sarah. She nodded, her hand resting warmly on his shoulder.

He pulled the heavy iron levers backward, pulling them out of the red zone and snapping them into the neutral position.

The deep, sub-audible vibration of the localized spacetime folding engine smoothly spun down into silence. Outside the massive viewport, the swirling, kaleidoscopic tunnel of blue and purple light collapsed in on itself with a silent, brilliant flash.

Normal space rushed back in to meet them.

The viewport was suddenly filled with the breathtaking sight of Exoplanet 4. It was a massive, vibrant jewel suspended in the dark—swirling white clouds over deep, sapphire oceans and vast, sprawling continents of rich green and brown. It was pristine. It was completely untouched.

Sarah gasped, stepping closer to the thick glass. "Wyatt, look at it. It's beautiful."

Wyatt smiled, looking at the virgin world below them. "That's our new home."

But before they could even celebrate, the physics of normal space violently reasserted themselves.

The electromagnetic block of the FTL slipstream instantly vanished. The station's massive parabolic dish was suddenly exposed to the open void, and six days' worth of delayed, light-speed radio waves from the Sol System finally caught up to them.

The Operations center exploded into absolute chaos.

The heavy, grated bulkhead speakers didn't just hiss; they screamed. A terrifying, overlapping cacophony of panicked military chatter, automated distress beacons, and the grating, dual-tone blare of the Emergency Broadcast System flooded the room all at once.

On the command console, the heavy amber CRT monitor flared violently, lines of frantic, unencrypted data cascading down the screen.

And then, the heavy dot-matrix printer bolted to the side of the desk violently shrieked to life.

*ZZZT-ZZZT-ZZZT-ZZZT!*

It began churning out continuous-feed paper at maximum speed, the pins striking the ribbon so hard it sounded like a machine gun. It was a physical backlog of horror, printing out every single apocalyptic broadcast they had missed while they were safely hidden in the fold.

Wyatt and Sarah froze, the serene beauty of their new planet completely overshadowed by the agonizing, mechanical screams of the world they had left behind.
