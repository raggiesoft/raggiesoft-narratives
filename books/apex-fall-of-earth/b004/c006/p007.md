# Part 7: The Backlog – Tuesday, November 1, 2044 – 09:32

The heavy dot-matrix printer screamed, the mechanical pins striking the ribbon with the frantic, deafening rhythm of a machine gun. *ZZZT-ZZZT-ZZZT-ZZZT!*

Wyatt and Sarah stood frozen in the Operations center, the breathtaking view of their pristine new world suddenly overshadowed by the ghosts of the one they had left behind. The electromagnetic block of the FTL slipstream was gone, and six days of delayed, light-speed radio waves from the Sol System were violently slamming into the station’s massive parabolic dish all at once.

Wyatt broke from his shock. He stepped up to the console, grabbing the long, continuous-feed paper spilling out of the printer and onto the grated floor.

Sarah stepped up beside him, her hands trembling as she braced herself against the edge of the 1970s terminal.

The first page was dated just two days after they had entered the slipstream.

**EBS TRANSCRIPT: INTERCEPT DELTA NINER (BROADCAST 006)** **DATE RECEIVED (DELAYED):** Thursday, October 27, 2044 **SOURCE:** United States Northern Command / FEMA **TARGETING:** Eastern Seaboard / Mid-Atlantic Region **THREAT LEVEL:** Extinction Level Event (East Coast Breach / VEI-8 Eruption) **FORMAT:** Synthesized Text-to-Speech

\<EAS DUAL-TONE ACTIVATION BEEP\>

"THE FOLLOWING MESSAGE IS TRANSMITTED AT THE REQUEST OF THE UNITED STATES NORTHERN COMMAND.

THE SUBTERRANEAN ANOMALY HAS BYPASSED THE CONTINENTAL DIVIDE AND REACHED THE EASTERN SEABOARD. THE ORGANIC MASS HAS COLLIDED WITH A HIGHLY PRESSURIZED MAGMA CHAMBER LOCATED BENEATH MIDDLETOWN, VIRGINIA.

THIS COLLISION HAS TRIGGERED A CATASTROPHIC VEI-8 SUPERVOLCANIC ERUPTION. THE TOWN OF MIDDLETOWN AND THE SURROUNDING SHENANDOAH VALLEY HAVE BEEN VAPORIZED. MOLE HILL, A SECONDARY VOLCANIC PLUG, HAS ALSO ERUPTED.

WASHINGTON D.C. AND THE SURROUNDING NATIONAL CAPITAL REGION ARE CURRENTLY BEING BURIED UNDER TOXIC VOLCANIC ASH. CONTINUITY OF GOVERNMENT PROTOCOLS WERE SUCCESSFULLY INITIATED PRIOR TO THE ERUPTION. THE MOUNT WEATHER EMERGENCY OPERATIONS CENTER WAS EVACUATED PRIOR TO THE BREACH.

DO NOT ATTEMPT TO RESCUE CASUALTIES IN THE SHENANDOAH VALLEY. MAY GOD BE WITH YOU."

\<TRANSMISSION TERMINATED\>

Sarah let out a sharp, ragged breath, her hand covering her mouth. "It crossed the whole country. It set off another volcano..."

"It hit the magma chamber under Virginia," Wyatt said, his voice a hollow rasp as he stared at the blocky text. The continent had been cracked wide open.

But the printer wasn't done. It kept churning, feeding another massive block of text into Wyatt's hands. The timestamp on this one was from just twenty-four hours ago.

**GLOBAL BROADCAST: INTERCEPT DELTA NINER (BROADCAST 007)** **DATE RECEIVED (DELAYED):** Monday, October 31, 2044 **SOURCE:** United Nations Security Council / Global Defense Coalition **TARGETING:** All Surviving Global Frequencies **THREAT LEVEL:** Planetary Collapse (Global Evacuation Initiated) **FORMAT:** Unencrypted Human Voice Transmission (Multiple Languages)

"This is a general broadcast on behalf of the surviving members of the United Nations Security Council and the Global Defense Coalition.

The subterranean anomaly that originated in the North American continent has fractured the Earth's tectonic plates. Seismic shockwaves and subsequent supervolcanic eruptions have rendered the Earth's atmosphere toxic and structurally unstable.

The planet is no longer viable.

To all remaining civilian populations: Major world powers have successfully preserved their continuity of government. A coordinated, mass off-world evacuation of what is left of Earth is currently underway. International atmospheric transports are departing from all surviving launch sites. We are directing all orbital traffic to designated staging flotillas beyond the Lunar orbit.

If you are receiving this broadcast, look to the sky. Help is launching. Humanity will survive this, but we must leave our home behind. May God bless us all."

\<HEAVY STATIC / SIGNAL LOST\>

The printer finally gave a last, violent *clack* and went silent. The heavy amber CRT monitor returned to its steady, blinking command prompt. The bulkhead speakers faded back into the low, ambient hiss of deep space.

Wyatt lowered the perforated paper, his hands shaking slightly.

He looked at Sarah. Tears were silently tracking down her cheeks. Neither of them had been born on Earth; they had both grown up on fading off-world colonies, and they had only known the planet as a terrifying corporate meat-grinder. But it was still the cradle of humanity. The staggering loss of the homeworld, the original oceans, and the ancient continents—it was an irreplaceable wound to the entire galaxy. Earth was dead.

But beneath the profound, crushing grief, a different emotion was taking root.

"They survived," Sarah whispered, her voice cracking as she looked up at Wyatt. She tapped the final paragraph of the printout. "Wyatt, the people on Earth... they're evacuating. They made it off."

Wyatt looked at the paper, then back up to the massive viewport. Suspended in the dark was the brilliant, vibrant jewel of Exoplanet 4—a completely untouched world of swirling white clouds, sapphire oceans, and rich green continents.

"The cradle is gone," Wyatt said, his voice steadying, thick with a complex mixture of sorrow and immense relief. "But our people aren't."

He reached out, pulling Sarah firmly against his chest. He held her tight, anchoring her as the final tether to their old lives was severed. The billions fleeing Earth would scatter into the stars, joining the vast, existing network of frontier colonies and off-world settlements. Humanity would survive the loss of its home.

And millions of lightyears away, completely off the corporate grid, Wyatt and Sarah had already found their sanctuary.

"We let the universe go its way," Wyatt murmured into her hair, looking out at the virgin planet waiting for them below. "And we go ours."
