# Part 11: The Overcompensation – Sunday, May 12, 2045 – 18:00

The simulated violet twilight of the Earth Conservatory wrapped around them, but the massive, vaulted room suddenly felt incredibly small and intimate.

Wyatt held Sarah on the soft bluegrass, his broad hands gently cradling the back of her head as she wept into his shoulder. They weren't tears of fear or grief. They were the physical release of absolute, staggering joy. The meticulously planned timeline had been thrown out the window, but neither of them cared.

They were going to be parents.

Eventually, Sarah’s breathing slowed. She pulled back just enough to look at him, wiping the dampness from her cheeks, leaving faint smears of dark garden soil across her face.

She offered a watery, brilliant smile, but as her hands dropped back down to rest over her lower abdomen, the analytical, deeply pragmatic side of her brain finally caught up with the emotion.

She pressed her fingers against the pronounced, firm swell of her stomach. It was still there. It wasn't a trick of the light.

"I don't understand the biology of this," Sarah murmured, her brow furrowing in genuine confusion. She looked up toward the hidden acoustic arrays in the artificial hills.

"Aria," Sarah called out, her voice steadying. "If I am exactly sixteen days post-conception, the embryo is roughly four weeks along in gestational terms. It should be the size of a poppy seed. It's microscopic. So why did I suddenly grow a visible baby bump in the middle of the afternoon?"

Wyatt looked down at her stomach, the exact same mechanical logic clicking in his head. You couldn't build an engine housing before you had the engine.

*"It is a highly common physical misconception, Sarah,"* Aria’s synthesized voice explained smoothly, maintaining her warm, educational cadence. *"What you are currently experiencing is not the physical mass of the fetus. It is severe, acute hormonal bloating."*

"Bloating?" Sarah asked, incredibly skeptical that mere fluid could feel this rigid.

*"Affirmative,"* Aria confirmed. *"The moment implantation occurred, your body began producing massive quantities of progesterone and human chorionic gonadotropin to halt your natural cycle and build the placental life-support system. Progesterone acts as a smooth-muscle relaxant. It prevents the uterus from contracting, but it also severely relaxes your digestive tract. This dramatically slows digestion and causes significant localized fluid retention in the lower abdomen."*

Wyatt listened intently, his hand coming to rest gently over Sarah's. The tight, firm curve of her stomach felt incredibly real to him, regardless of what was causing it.

*"Furthermore, Sarah,"* Aria continued, providing the final piece of the puzzle, *"we must factor in the sudden absence of the Apex Defense Solutions Omni-Cycle blocker. Your endocrine system was anticipating a heavy dose of synthetic corporate suppressants. When it received a saline placebo instead, followed immediately by natural fertilization, your body was entirely unprepared. Your system is currently aggressively overcompensating to secure the pregnancy, resulting in a physical reaction that is exceptionally rapid and pronounced."*

Sarah let out a long, slow breath, the mechanical logic perfectly settling her fears.

It made complete sense. Apex's cheap, cost-cutting medical fraud had essentially shocked her system into overdrive. The sudden "bump" wasn't the baby itself; it was her body frantically building the fortress to protect it, completely rewriting her internal environment in a matter of hours.

"Overcompensating," Sarah repeated softly, a deep, maternal warmth flooding her chest. She looked down at Wyatt's large, calloused hand resting securely over her stomach. "It's just making sure we're safe."

"It's doing exactly what it's supposed to do," Wyatt agreed, his voice a low, incredibly tender rumble.

He didn't care if it was just fluid and hormones right now. To him, that small, sudden swell was the physical manifestation of their future. It was the absolute, undeniable proof that the nightmare of their past was dead, and the family they had promised each other was already growing.

He leaned down, pressing a soft, lingering kiss directly against her bare stomach.

Sarah’s breath hitched, her fingers tangling in his dark hair. The profound intimacy of the gesture—sitting completely bare in the dirt of their new world while he worshipped the very beginning of their child—was staggering.

Wyatt sat back up, his dark eyes locking onto hers. The fierce, protective S.H.O.P. Marine was entirely focused on a completely new mission.

"Well, Mrs. Colton," Wyatt said, a massive, uncontainable grin breaking across his face. "I think you're officially excused from digging tomato trenches for the foreseeable future."

Sarah laughed, a bright, ringing sound that echoed perfectly over the reflecting pond.

"I am not an invalid, Wyatt," she teased, gently swatting his shoulder. "I can still plant seeds."

"You can point at where you want the seeds to go from the comfort of a lounge chair," Wyatt corrected smoothly, standing up from the bluegrass and offering her both of his hands. "I am the heavy machinery from now on. You are carrying the cargo."

Sarah took his hands, letting his massive strength pull her effortlessly to her feet.

Standing in the simulated twilight, entirely covered in the mud of the Earth Conservatory, they had never looked less like corporate contractors, and they had never felt more like a family.

"Let's go upstairs," Wyatt murmured, wrapping his arm securely around her waist. "I think the mother of my child deserves a hot shower and a very large dinner."

"I am suddenly incredibly hungry," Sarah admitted, leaning her weight happily against his side.

They left the hand trowels and the forged steel spade resting in the dirt of their newly planted garden. The seeds were safely in the ground, and the legacy of Planet Eden had officially begun.
