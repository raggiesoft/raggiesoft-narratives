# Part 2: The Alpine Meadow – Saturday, May 11, 2045 – 17:00

"Take us to the mountains," Sarah decided, her eyes locked onto the jagged, towering peaks piercing the horizon. "I want to see the snow."

Wyatt banked the digital yoke smoothly to the right. The sleek, silver runabout responded with flawless agility, banking over the sprawling emerald plains and beginning a rapid, silent ascent toward the massive mountain range.

As they climbed higher, the geography shifted dramatically. The dense, alien pine forests thinned out, giving way to sweeping, terraced valleys of pale stone and rushing, icy waterfalls. High above them, the jagged peaks were capped with brilliant, blinding white snow that reflected the late afternoon sun.

Wyatt leveled the shuttle out, gliding over a massive, pristine alpine meadow nestled directly at the foot of the highest peak. The plateau was a vibrant carpet of short, soft grass, heavily speckled with brilliant blue and gold alien wildflowers.

"How about right there?" Wyatt asked, looking down through the glass canopy. He tapped the environmental scanner on the dash, a pleasantly surprised look crossing his face. "Aria's telemetry says we don't even need to worry about the altitude chill. This entire meadow is sitting over a shallow, inactive geothermal vent. The ambient air temperature is holding at a perfect twenty-two degrees."

"A warm meadow right under the snow," Sarah smiled, absolutely captivated by the landscape. "Land it."

Wyatt eased the throttles back. The runabout's repulsorlifts hummed softly as the teardrop-shaped hull descended, the landing gear deploying with a solid, mechanical thud as it settled gently into the soft grass. Wyatt powered down the main drives, and the cabin fell perfectly silent.

He tapped the console, and the hydraulic gull-wing doors smoothly lifted open on both sides of the ship.

The air that flooded into the cabin was staggering. It was crisp, incredibly fresh, and carried the scent of melting snow mixed with the sweet, fragrant pollen of the wildflowers.

Sarah unbuckled her harness and stepped out of the shuttle. Her heavy hiking boots crushed the soft grass beneath her feet. She looked around the vast, empty meadow, taking in the sheer, towering walls of the snow-capped mountains bordering them on three sides. It was an amphitheater of pure, untouched nature.

She turned back to the shuttle. Wyatt had just climbed out of the pilot's seat, his heavy leather work boots hitting the turf.

Sarah looked at her boots, then down at her denim jeans. She looked up at Wyatt with a bright, entirely uninhibited smile.

"We're off the ship," Sarah announced, her voice echoing softly in the quiet mountain air. "And there isn't a single other human being within a million lightyears."

Wyatt caught her meaning instantly. A slow, deeply relaxed grin spread across his face.

"The Eden Rule," Wyatt agreed.

Without another word, Sarah reached down and unlaced her heavy hiking boots, kicking them off into the grass. She pulled her gray Henley over her head, unhooked her bra, and shimmied out of her denim jeans.

Wyatt mirrored her, stripping off his heavy black t-shirt, his canvas work pants, and his boots, tossing them unceremoniously into the open cargo hold of the shuttle.

Standing completely bare in the alpine meadow, the gentle, geothermally warmed breeze washing over their skin, the absolute freedom of their new world washed over them. They weren't hiding behind the camouflaged glass of the mountain estate, and they weren't shielded by the massive canopy of the forest. They were completely exposed to the sky, entirely vulnerable, and absolutely perfectly safe.

Wyatt reached into the cargo hold and pulled out the heavy, insulated deployment sleeping bag and the canvas duffel of food.

He walked a few meters away from the silver hull of the runabout and spread the thick sleeping bag completely open over a patch of soft grass and crushed wildflowers.

Sarah sat down in the center of it, crossing her bare legs as Wyatt joined her. They opened the duffel bag, pulling out the massive deli-meat sandwiches they had made back at the estate, along with canteens of ice-cold water and the container of fresh, brilliant red strawberries.

They sat together in their own skin, eating their dinner as the golden sun began its final descent behind the towering, snow-capped peaks. The light shifted from gold to a deep, bruised violet, casting long, beautiful shadows across the alpine grass.

"This tastes even better out here," Sarah murmured, taking a bite of a sweet, juicy strawberry. She leaned her weight against Wyatt's side, her bare shoulder pressing against his.

"Everything is better out here," Wyatt agreed, wrapping his heavy arm around her waist and pulling her close. He looked out at the jagged mountains. "No S.H.O.P. Foremen. No klaxons. Just you, me, and the quiet."

As the sun finally vanished, the sky didn't just turn dark. It exploded with light.

Without the atmospheric pollution of Earth or the harsh glare of a corporate colony, the Milky Way stretched across the sky in a brilliant, breathtaking ribbon of starlight. And then, slowly cresting over the eastern ridge, the first of Eden's newly named moons began to rise.

First came Anchor, massive and cratered, casting a soft, pale light over the meadow. It was followed closely by the smaller, smooth sphere of Nomad.

Sarah rested her head on Wyatt's chest, her fingers lightly tracing the heavy scars on his arm as they watched their very own moons claim the night sky.

"It really is paradise, Wyatt," she whispered, her voice thick with sleep and profound contentment.

Wyatt pulled the heavy top layer of the deployment bag over their bare shoulders, sealing the warmth around them. He kissed her forehead, entirely at peace in the absolute silence of the mountain air.

"Yeah, it is," Wyatt murmured, holding her tightly as the stars rotated above them. "And we have all the time in the world to explore it."
