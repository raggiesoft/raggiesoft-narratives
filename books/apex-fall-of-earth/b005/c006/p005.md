# Part 5: The Reading List – Sunday, May 12, 2045 – 10:30

The sleek, silver runabout glided silently back into the cavernous, illuminated hangar of the mountain estate. Wyatt eased the throttles back, and the repulsorlifts set the ship gently down onto the polished stone floor.

The hydraulic gull-wing doors hissed open, and Wyatt and Sarah stepped out, their heavy boots echoing in the quiet space. The transition from the brisk, 12°C low-gravity environment of Anchor back to the warm, 1.02 Earth-standard gravity of The Sanctuary made their limbs feel wonderfully heavy and pleasantly exhausted.

They walked through the docking umbilical and stepped past the sliding brushed-steel doors into the main living quarters.

Sarah stopped in the entryway, leaning against the wall to unlace her hiking boots. She kicked them off, wiggling her toes against the warm, heated stone floor. Wyatt did the same with his heavy leather work boots.

Sarah stood up, looking down at her denim jeans and gray Henley. She grabbed the hem of her shirt, fully intending to invoke the Eden Rule, but paused. She let out a long, contented sigh, dropping her hands to her sides.

"You know," Sarah murmured, leaning her head back against the stone wall. "I love the Eden Rule. But taking off these jeans right now just feels like entirely too much work."

Wyatt let out a deep, booming laugh, perfectly content in his canvas pants and black t-shirt. "The absolute luxury of having an entire planet to ourselves is that we can be lazy whenever we want. Eden Rule is officially suspended due to pilot fatigue."

"Thank you," Sarah smiled, stepping away from the wall and taking his hand.

They bypassed the master suite and the indoor pool, heading straight for the sunken, mahogany-paneled library. They collapsed together onto the plush leather sofa, perfectly comfortable in their civilian clothes, and Wyatt tapped the edge of the massive holographic glass table.

"Alright, Aria," Wyatt called out. "We promised we'd study. Load up Module One of the pediatric and neonatal curriculum."

"Accessing archival literature now," Aria’s synthesized voice echoed warmly from the ceiling array.

The glass table hummed, projecting a pristine, glowing blue holographic interface. Instead of the cascading physics equations or SQL database syntax they had wrestled with yesterday, the table was suddenly flooded with anatomical diagrams of infant development, complex nutritional charts, and detailed texts on pediatric psychology.

Sarah leaned forward, her dark eyes scanning the floating text.

"Okay, so this module covers the first trimester of pregnancy and basic neonatal prep," Sarah noted, swiping a finger through the hologram to expand a chapter on hormonal shifts. "It says the earliest signs of pregnancy usually present around week four to six. Heightened sense of smell, extreme fatigue, and morning sickness."

"Morning sickness," Wyatt muttered, reading over her shoulder. He frowned slightly. "It says here that's caused by the rapid spike in the hCG hormone. Does it happen to everyone?"

"Statistically, approximately seventy to eighty percent of human females experience some form of nausea or vomiting during the first trimester," Aria provided, pulling up a secondary pie chart. "The severity is often correlated with the baseline levels of the hormone. For instance, multiple-gestation pregnancies, such as twins or triplets, typically produce significantly higher levels of hCG, resulting in an earlier and more severe onset of symptoms."

Sarah let out a soft laugh, shaking her head as she read the data.

"Well, luckily we don't have a family history of twins," Sarah smiled, leaning comfortably back against Wyatt's chest. "And we have plenty of time to prepare. It's only May. The S.H.O.P. Marine blockers won't even leave my system until October."

"Exactly," Wyatt agreed, wrapping his arm around her waist and pulling her close. He looked at the overwhelming amount of medical literature floating above the table. It was a staggering amount of information to absorb, but he felt a profound sense of relief knowing they had a massive buffer.

"By the time January rolls around and we actually start trying, we'll have this entire curriculum memorized," Wyatt added confidently. "We'll have the nursery built, the cribs printed in the foundry, and a brand new garden yielding fresh food. We are going to be perfectly, absolutely prepared for this."

"The most prepared parents in the galaxy," Sarah agreed happily, resting her hand over his.

They sat in the library for hours, completely immersed in the textbooks, making detailed plans for the following year. They studied the nutritional requirements for a healthy first trimester, discussed the logistics of setting up the automated medical wing on Sub-Level 1, and completely revelled in the luxury of having total control over their timeline.

They were entirely, blissfully unaware that deep inside Sarah's body, the rapid, miraculous spark of life had already taken hold. The S.H.O.P. Marine blockers had failed, and the clock wasn't set for next year.
