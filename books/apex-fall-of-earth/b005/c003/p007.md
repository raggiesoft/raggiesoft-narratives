# Part 7: The Master Kitchen – Thursday, May 9, 2045 – 18:30

The pneumatic elevator carried them swiftly up from the icy depths of the Genesis Vault, leaving the sleeping future of humanity safely secured in the bedrock.

When the brushed-steel doors parted to reveal the main living quarters, the stark temperature difference was instantly noticeable. While the vault had been a sterile, shivering deep-freeze, the sprawling luxury estate was actively maintained at a perfectly pleasant, ambient **21.6°C**.

Wyatt let out a contented sigh as the warm air hit his skin. "I could get used to this."

*"The estate's geothermal HVAC system runs on an automated, self-regulating loop,"* Aria chimed in helpfully from the ceiling array, her audio tracking them as they walked down the hall. *"The baseline temperature is permanently set to 21.6 degrees Celsius for optimal energy efficiency and comfort. However, as the primary Administrators, you only need to ask if you would like me to adjust the climate control in any specific zone."*

"It's perfect, Aria. Leave it exactly where it is," Sarah smiled, walking past the massive sectional sofas and the indoor waterfall.

The adrenaline of outrunning a super-cell storm and the profound, life-altering discovery of fifty thousand embryos had completely caught up with them. It was quarter past six in the evening, and their stomachs were aggressively reminding them that a peanut butter sandwich at one o'clock was not going to cut it.

Sarah led the way into the estate's sprawling, state-of-the-art kitchen, and Wyatt stopped in his tracks just to take it all in.

On *The Nomad*, the ship's galley was a masterpiece of compact engineering. It had everything they needed, but it was essentially a narrow hallway. If Sarah was standing at the induction stove, Wyatt had to completely leave the room just so she could open the refrigeration unit.

This kitchen, however, was built for a billionaire who intended to entertain a dynasty.

It featured twin, massive black-marble islands, a massive six-burner induction range, dual convection ovens, and a walk-in climate-controlled pantry that dwarfed the dreadnought's entire cargo storage. Best of all, they didn't have to take turns existing in the space.

"Look at the size of this place," Wyatt laughed, walking over to the nearest marble island and resting his hands on the cool stone. He looked down at his t-shirt and denim shorts, perfectly adhering to the Eden Rule's safety clause. "Plenty of room to avoid spitting grease."

"And plenty of room for a sous-chef," Sarah teased, tossing him a heavy, woven cotton apron she found hanging on a hook near the pantry door.

Wyatt caught it with a grin, slipping it over his head. "What's on the menu, Chef Colton?"

Sarah disappeared into the massive walk-in pantry. A moment later, she emerged carrying an armful of supplies the billionaire's automated preservation systems had kept in pristine condition. She set down a vacuum-sealed package of prime, synthetic-wagyu beef cuts, a box of high-end artisan pasta, and a jar of rich, dark truffle sauce.

"We are celebrating," Sarah declared, firing up the massive induction range.

For the next hour, the sprawling kitchen was filled with the incredible sounds and smells of a real, home-cooked meal. And for the first time since they had met, they actually got to cook *together*.

Wyatt took over chopping the vegetables they found in the automated hydroponic crisper, his massive, calloused hands surprisingly delicate with the chef's knife. Sarah manned the stove, boiling the pasta and searing the synthetic-wagyu steaks to absolute perfection. They moved around each other seamlessly in the massive space, bumping hips, stealing tastes of the truffle sauce, and laughing as the apocalyptic storm raged harmlessly against the camouflaged hydro-glass windows just a few meters away.

It was an incredibly domestic, grounded moment. They weren't fighting for their lives. They weren't running from Apex. They were just a husband and wife making dinner in their own home.

When the food was finally ready, they didn't bother carrying it out to the formal dining room. They just sat side-by-side on the heavy oak barstools at the massive marble island, eating straight from the ceramic plates.

The steak practically melted in their mouths, and the rich truffle pasta was a staggering upgrade from corporate nutrient-paste.

Wyatt took a long drink from a glass of purified glacial water, leaning his elbows on the marble counter. He looked at Sarah, who was happily twirling a forkful of pasta, her eyes reflecting the warm, ambient light of the kitchen.

"You know," Wyatt said softly, a deep, resonant contentment in his voice. "I thought planting that garden in the mud this morning was the best part of the day. But I think this beats it."

Sarah smiled, leaning over to rest her head against his shoulder as they ate. "It's the space, Wyatt. We finally have room to breathe."
