# Part 3: The Blueprint – Thursday, May 9, 2045 – 08:00

Wyatt took the last bite of his bacon, leaning back in the heavy dining chair. The relief of not having to strip-mine the beautiful forest outside their window had settled deep into his chest. *The Nomad* was an indestructible, solar-powered fortress. It was a perfect home.

But he was still a mechanic and a builder. His hands needed something to do.

"Alright," Wyatt said, looking across the table at Sarah. "If we aren't framing a house on that ridge, what exactly are we doing with a 12-by-8-meter grid of neon-orange string?"

Sarah’s eyes lit up with absolute pioneer excitement. She pushed her empty plate aside and leaned forward, resting her elbows on the stainless-steel table.

"A garden," Sarah stated, her voice brimming with purpose. "A real, open-air agricultural garden. We have 96 square meters of incredibly rich, loamy soil already measured out and staked. It gets perfect morning sun, and it's elevated just enough to drain perfectly down toward the river."

Wyatt smiled, tracing the rim of his coffee mug. He had watched her carefully tend to the sterile, artificial hydroponics bays back on the Apex orbital stations. She was a colony farm girl at heart; she had always belonged in the dirt.

"A 12-by-8 garden," Wyatt nodded, visualizing the layout. "That's a massive yield. We could grow enough root vegetables and leafy greens to supplement the ship's dry stores indefinitely. But, since I'm out of a construction job..."

Wyatt paused, a playful, ambitious smirk crossing his face. "What if we split the grid? We use eight meters for the garden, and I use the other four meters to dig out a pool. I could line it with some of the smooth river stones, rig up a basic filtration pump powered by the ship's solar grid, and—"

Sarah let out a bright, ringing laugh, completely interrupting his engineering pitch.

"A pool?" she giggled, shaking her head. "Wyatt, you just agreed five minutes ago that we weren't going to unnecessarily tear up the landscape just because we have the tools to do it."

"A man can dream," Wyatt chuckled, holding his hands up in surrender. "I'm just trying to offer some luxury to the frontier."

"We already have the ultimate luxury," Sarah countered, pointing out the viewport toward the valley below. "Why would you spend days digging a hole in the dirt and running PVC pipes when we have a pristine, crystal-clear river fifty meters from our front ramp? The water is perfect. The current is gentle. Wyatt... the river *is* our pool."

Wyatt looked out the window at the winding, sunlit water they had just been splashing in an hour ago. He thought about the bioluminescent aquatic life that had turned the river into a ribbon of starlight the night before.

She was absolutely right. You couldn't engineer anything better than what Exoplanet 4 had already built.

"Can't argue with that logic," Wyatt conceded smoothly, taking a final sip of his coffee. He stood up from the table, gathering their empty ceramic plates. "A 12-by-8-meter garden it is. No concrete, no plasma saws, and no plumbing required."

Sarah stood up, sliding her bare feet across the cool deck plates to join him at the galley sink. She wrapped her arms around his waist from behind, pressing her cheek against his back as he rinsed the heavy cast-iron skillet.

"Just you, me, and the dirt," Sarah murmured happily. "We can spend the morning tilling the soil. We need to clear the top layer of grass and turn the earth so it can breathe before we start planting."

Wyatt set the clean skillet on the drying rack and turned around in her arms. He looked down at her fresh t-shirt and jean shorts.

"Well, if we're going to spend the next four hours ripping up alien sod by hand, we're going to get filthy," Wyatt grinned, kissing the top of her head. "Let's go to the cargo bay and find some shovels."
