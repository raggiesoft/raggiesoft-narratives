# Part 5: The Bait – Thursday, May 9, 2045 – 10:00 (Standard Galactic Time)

Lightyears away from the crystal-clear river and the warm, muddy banks of Exoplanet 4, High Director Kael was not enjoying the morning.

He stood in the center of the cold, blue-lit command spire on Acheron, staring at the massive holographic projection of the Cygnus Arm. The tracker squadrons had pushed deep into the outer rim, burning through millions of corporate credits in hyper-fuel and sensor drone deployments. They had scanned a thousand dead rocks and obsolete civilian freighters.

They had found absolutely nothing.

The sub-commander in the matte-black armor stood at attention near the pneumatic doors, wisely keeping his mouth shut.

"Efficiency, Commander," Kael said, his voice a low, dangerous hum that cut through the silence of the room. He slowly clasped his hands behind his back. "The foundation of this empire is efficiency. We do not expend infinite resources searching an infinite void for two pieces of missing inventory. The cost-to-benefit ratio has become unacceptable."

"Shall I order the tracker squadrons to stand down, High Director?" the sub-commander asked carefully. "Officially declare the assets destroyed?"

"I did not say they were destroyed," Kael corrected coldly, turning away from the galactic map. "I know they are alive. I know they executed a spacetime fold. But if we cannot find them in the dark, we must compel them to step into the light. We need a lure. Something cheaper than a fleet of scout ships."

Kael walked over to the primary command terminal. He swiped his hand across the glass, dismissing the star charts and pulling up the internal employee registry for Apex Defense Solutions.

He bypassed Wyatt Colton’s file—the deep-sea welder was an orphan, raised in the corporate foster system with absolutely no biological ties. A dead end.

He opened the personnel file for Contractor Hayes, S.

The holographic screen populated with Sarah's data. It showed her transfer from a fading outer-rim agricultural colony directly into the S.H.O.P. Marine infantry program. Kael’s eyes scanned past her physical metrics and marksmanship scores, zeroing in on her emergency contact registry and civilian lineage.

A cruel, calculating smile slowly touched the corners of Kael's mouth.

"There," Kael whispered, tapping the glass. "Commander, look at this. Contractor Hayes was not vat-grown. She has family. Two biological parents still working off their planetary debt on the Ag-Colony in the Perseus Sector. Tom and Ellen Hayes."

The sub-commander stepped forward, looking at the glowing blue text. "Sir, those colonists are fully compliant. They meet their crop quotas every cycle. They have no knowledge of the Earth operation or the theft of Legacy Asset 44-A."

"Their compliance is irrelevant," Kael stated, his tone dripping with corporate malice. "They are leverage."

Kael turned back to the holographic display, his mind rapidly constructing the trap. The stolen dreadnought—*The Nomad*—was originally designed as a deep-space listening post. Even if the runaway Marines had painted over the hull and scrubbed the transponders, the ship's internal architecture was still wired to intercept and decode wide-band galactic broadcasts.

"Dispatch a localized enforcement squad to the Perseus Sector," Kael ordered, his voice echoing with absolute dictatorial authority. "Detain Tom and Ellen Hayes. Charge them with corporate treason by proxy. I want them processed and scheduled for neural-reeducation on Acheron."

"Yes, High Director. And the tracker squadrons?"

"Pull them back to the transit relays," Kael instructed. "Then, I want you to broadcast the arrest warrants and the scheduled reeducation of Tom and Ellen Hayes on every unencrypted, open-air subspace frequency in the outer arms. Do not encrypt it. Make sure the broadcast is loud, clear, and constantly repeating."

The sub-commander immediately understood the brutal elegance of the strategy. It cost nothing to transmit a radio wave.

"You want her to hear it," the sub-commander realized.

"I want them both to hear it," Kael corrected, his eyes gleaming in the blue light. "They are hiding somewhere out there, thinking they have won. But if Contractor Hayes is alive, she will not allow her parents to be mind-wiped and sent to the deep-crust labor camps. That stolen ship has the firepower of a small armada. They will come out of hiding to intercept the prisoner transport."

Kael looked back at the vast, empty projection of the galaxy. He didn't need to scan Exoplanet 4. He just needed to wait for the ghost to show itself.

"Set the trap, Commander. Let's see how much these pioneers really care about their family."
