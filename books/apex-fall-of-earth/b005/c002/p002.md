# Part 2: The Nomad – Thursday, May 9, 2045 – 07:45

The rich, savory smell of sizzling bacon and the warm, buttery aroma of rehydrated eggs completely filled the compact galley. It was a staggering upgrade from the bleak, synthetic nutrient-paste they had survived on inside the Apex staging facility.

Sarah scooped the eggs from the heavy cast-iron skillet onto two ceramic plates, laying the crispy strips of bacon perfectly alongside them. She handed one of the plates to Wyatt, who was leaning against the stainless-steel counter, a steaming mug of black coffee already in his hand.

They sat down together at the small, heavy-duty dining alcove tucked just off the galley.

For a few minutes, the only sound was the clinking of their forks and the low, ambient hum of the ship’s life-support systems. It was a quiet, domestic peace that neither of them had ever been allowed to experience before.

Wyatt swallowed a bite of his breakfast, looking out the viewport at the dense, sunlit forest canopy that concealed them.

"So," Wyatt said, tapping his fork thoughtfully against his plate. "The living room footprint is laid out. I was looking at the tree line yesterday while we were tracking the grid. Those alien pines are massive. If I drop three or four of the older ones near the eastern ridge, I should have enough raw timber to start framing the foundation and the porch by the end of the week."

Sarah stopped eating. She looked down at her plate, and then out the viewport at the massive, ancient trunks of the trees Wyatt was talking about.

She remembered the way the forest had come alive last night. The pulsing cyan moss, the glowing violet ferns, the absolute, breathing perfection of the ecosystem.

"Wyatt," Sarah started, her voice gentle but hesitant. She set her fork down and looked across the table at him. "I've been thinking about the grid. And about what we promised yesterday down by the river."

Wyatt set his coffee mug down, giving her his complete attention. "About living in harmony with the valley."

"Right," Sarah nodded, lacing her fingers together on the tabletop. "We said we wouldn't strip-mine this place. We said we'd only take what the forest could spare. Wyatt... those trees have been growing out there for hundreds of years. They're part of the bioluminescent canopy. If we fire up the heavy plasma saws and start felling them just to build a wooden box... aren't we doing exactly what Apex would do? Just taking what we want because we have the tools to do it?"

Wyatt leaned back in his chair, processing her words. His protective instinct—the deep, driving need to fulfill the promise he had made to her in the dark of Sector 4—was warring with the undeniable truth of what she was saying.

"I promised you a house, Sarah," Wyatt said, his voice a low, earnest rumble. "I promised you a place with a real door and a lock that belonged to us."

"I know," Sarah smiled, reaching across the table to cover his hand with hers. "And you gave me one. You stole a multi-billion-credit dreadnought, flew us across the galaxy, and parked it in a wildlife sanctuary."

Before Wyatt could argue, a deep, resonant mechanical whirring vibrated through the deck plates beneath their bare feet.

*"Good morning, Administrators,"* Aria’s pleasant, synthesized voice echoed softly from the galley's overhead speakers. *"The local sun has cleared the eastern ridge. I am deploying the solar arrays to their maximum span to begin the daily battery recharge cycle."*

Outside the viewport, the massive, armored wings of *The Nomad* smoothly extended, sliding silently through the upper branches of the canopy. The pristine photovoltaic panels angled themselves perfectly, catching the golden morning light. The ship wasn't burning fuel. It wasn't emitting exhaust. It was simply sitting in the forest, drinking in the sun right alongside the trees.

Sarah looked at the solar wings, and then back at Wyatt.

"Look at it," Sarah said softly. "Aria is running the entire ship off the sun. The hull is lined with lead and titanium, so we're completely safe from any orbital radiation or storms. We have a galley, a massive shower, hydroponics bays waiting to be planted, and a climate-controlled bedroom."

She squeezed his hand, her eyes shining with absolute clarity.

"I don't need you to cut down a beautiful forest to build me a house, Wyatt," Sarah told him, her voice filled with profound contentment. "Not when we're already sitting inside of one. For now... *The Nomad* is our house."

Wyatt looked at the woman he loved, completely disarmed by her compassion for the world around them. He looked at the heavy, armored bulkheads of the galley, the 2044-era appliances, and the quiet, sun-drenched forest right outside their window.

They had a fortress that ran on sunlight. Why destroy paradise to build a cabin?

A slow, deeply relaxed smile broke across Wyatt's face. He turned his hand over, tangling his fingers with hers.

"Alright," Wyatt agreed, the tension completely leaving his shoulders. "We leave the trees right where they are. *The Nomad* is home."

Sarah beamed, letting out a happy sigh as she picked up her fork again. "Good. Because I have much better plans for that orange string grid we staked out yesterday."
