# Part 3: The Earth Conservatory – Friday, May 10, 2045 – 12:15

Sarah hit the final execution command on the holographic table, completely dissolving the massive cascade of SQL code. Wyatt closed his digital scratchpad, the complex thermal dynamics of the repulsor-coil fading into the glass.

They had officially conquered their first morning of university classes.

Wyatt stretched his heavy arms above his head, the joints in his shoulders popping softly in the quiet, mahogany-paneled library. He looked over at Sarah, who was leaning back into the plush leather sofa, running a hand through her dark hair.

"I don't know about you," Wyatt said, his stomach giving a low, perfectly timed rumble. "But reverse-engineering spacecraft physics makes me incredibly hungry."

Sarah laughed, a bright, completely relaxed sound that echoed warmly in the room. "I was just thinking the exact same thing. But honestly? I'm almost too curious to go to the kitchen right now."

She stood up from the sofa, entirely bare and entirely comfortable in the estate's flawless **21.6°C** ambient air. She walked over to the massive hydro-glass window. Outside, the torrential downpour of the super-cell storm was still violently lashing the invisible face of the mountain, drowning the valley twenty-one meters below them.

"We've seen the master suite, the kitchen, the indoor pool, and the Genesis Vault," Sarah mused, tracing a finger lightly against the cool glass. She looked up toward the ceiling array. "Aria, this mountain is massive. The original architect planned to host twenty people for multiple generations. What else did he build in here to keep them from going crazy?"

*"The estate is equipped with several high-tier recreational and utility sectors designed to combat the psychological effects of prolonged subterranean isolation, Sarah,"* Aria’s synthesized voice chimed pleasantly. *"There is a fully automated Fabrication Foundry on Sub-Level 2, and the Earth Conservatory on Sub-Level 1. Would you care for a tour before lunch?"*

Wyatt was immediately off the sofa. "A Fabrication Foundry? Like a machine shop?"

*"Precisely, Wyatt,"* Aria confirmed. *"It is an industrial-grade manufacturing center capable of rendering raw elemental ingots into complex machinery, utilizing both 5-axis CNC milling and molecular 3D printing. The original architect required the ability to seamlessly repair or replace any component of this estate or The Nomad without relying on an external supply chain."*

Wyatt looked at Sarah, a massive, kid-in-a-candy-store grin spreading across his scarred face.

"Oh, we are definitely going there first," Wyatt declared.

They left the library, walking hand-in-hand down the polished stone corridor to the pneumatic elevator. The brushed-steel doors sealed, and they dropped smoothly down to Sub-Level 2.

When the doors parted, Wyatt stepped out and immediately stopped dead in his tracks.

The room was a sprawling, brightly lit industrial marvel. It smelled faintly of clean machine oil and warm metal. Along the far wall sat massive, towering stacks of raw material—heavy ingots of titanium, tungsten, steel, copper, and specialized high-tensile polymers, all perfectly organized and waiting to be used.

Lining the center of the room were rows of sleek, automated manufacturing arms enclosed in heavy glass safety cubes.

"This is incredible," Wyatt breathed, walking over to one of the massive molecular printers and resting his hands on the pristine glass. "Sarah, this isn't just a repair shop. With this much raw material and these machines, I could build literally anything. I could build an atmospheric rover to explore the planet. I could build automated tractors for your garden."

He turned to look at her, the gears in his head spinning with absolute joy.

"When the time comes," Wyatt added softly, his eyes dropping to her stomach for a fleeting, affectionate second, "I can build the perfect cribs for the nursery."

Sarah smiled, walking up behind him and wrapping her arms around his bare waist, resting her cheek against his broad back. "I think the Coltons are going to be a very dangerous combination. I'll write the software, and you build the hardware."

"Exactly," Wyatt agreed, turning around to pull her into a tight hug. "But Aria mentioned something else. An Earth Conservatory?"

*"That is correct,"* Aria chimed from the foundry's ceiling speakers. *"It is located one floor up on Sub-Level 1. It was designed as a psychological anchor for the executive board's families, should the Earth's biosphere be compromised."*

"Let's go see it," Sarah said, her curiosity piqued.

They took the pneumatic elevator up one floor. As the doors hissed open, the cool, industrial smell of the machine shop was instantly replaced by a thick, fragrant wave of damp soil, blooming flowers, and fresh air.

They stepped out of the elevator and walked through a short, curved tunnel that opened up into a massive, cavernous biodome.

Sarah actually gasped, her hand flying up to cover her mouth.

It was a perfect, pristine slice of Old Earth.

The vaulted ceiling of the cavern was lined with millions of high-definition micro-LEDs, projecting a flawless, moving simulation of a bright, cloud-swept blue sky. Beneath it lay a sprawling, rolling park of vibrant green Kentucky bluegrass.

A winding cobblestone path led through meticulously maintained gardens of blooming roses, hydrangeas, and wildflowers. But the centerpiece of the room completely stole their breath.

Standing in the exact center of the simulated park was a massive, towering White Oak tree. Its thick, sturdy branches reached toward the artificial sky, its leaves a deep, rich green.

Wyatt and Sarah slowly walked down the cobblestone path, entirely bare, feeling the soft, familiar tickle of Earth grass beneath their feet.

For two kids who had grown up in the rusted, failing agricultural colonies and the dark, deep-sea rigs of the frontier, seeing a perfect, unspoiled piece of Earth's natural beauty was staggering. It was even more profound knowing that the real Earth they had left behind was currently burning under the weight of a Megacorporation's hubris.

Sarah walked up to the massive oak tree, reaching out to press her hand against the rough, textured bark.

"He saved it," Sarah whispered, tears welling up in her eyes as the overwhelming nostalgia and beauty of the room hit her. "He knew what Apex was doing to the planet, so he ripped a piece of it out by the roots and hid it in a mountain."

Wyatt stepped up beside her, looking up into the sprawling canopy of the oak. He wrapped his heavy arm around her shoulders, pulling her firmly against his side.

"It's an ark," Wyatt said softly, his voice echoing gently in the quiet, simulated park. "The Genesis Vault for the people, and this room for the planet. He didn't just build a bunker to hide in, Sarah. He built a museum so humanity wouldn't forget where it came from."

Sarah leaned her head against Wyatt's chest, closing her eyes as she listened to the gentle, simulated breeze rustling through the oak leaves. The storm raging outside the mountain felt millions of miles away.

"I'm glad we found this," Sarah murmured, her hand resting over his heart. "When our kids grow up, they won't just have to read about Earth in the university archives. We can actually bring them here. We can show them what it felt like."

Wyatt kissed the top of her head, completely anchored by her presence in the middle of their secret paradise.

"We'll show them everything," Wyatt promised. "But right now... I think it's time to go raid that massive pantry and make some lunch. We have a lot of studying left to do."
