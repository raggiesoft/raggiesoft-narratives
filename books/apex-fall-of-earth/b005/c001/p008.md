# Part 8: The Blind Sweep – Wednesday, May 8, 2045 – 14:00 (Standard Galactic Time)

Back on the sovereign planet of Acheron, the cold, blue light of the central command spire offered no comfort.

High Director Kael stood before the massive holographic projection of the Cygnus Arm, watching dozens of tiny red icons—Apex long-range tracker squadrons—methodically sweeping through uncharted star systems. They were casting a massive digital net in the dark, hunting for a ghost.

The heavy pneumatic door hissed open. The same sub-commander in matte-black armor stepped through, his boots clicking sharply against the polished metal floor before snapping into a rigid salute.

"High Director," the sub-commander reported. "I have the preliminary sweep data from Tracker Squadron Seven, currently operating in the outer boundaries of the Cygnus Arm."

Kael didn't turn away from the hologram. "Have they located the isotopic trail?"

"The trail dissipated shortly after entering the sector, sir," the sub-commander stated, consulting his metallic datapad. "However, the squadron did execute a standard planetary sweep of System 88-Tango. They detected a metallic mass parked on the surface of Exoplanet 4."

Kael turned slowly, his eyes locking onto the sub-commander, a cold, predatory focus sharpening his features. "A metallic mass. Did they establish a digital handshake?"

"They did, sir. But it was a false alarm," the sub-commander said dismissively, tapping the screen to transfer the scout's telemetry to the primary holographic table.

A wireframe image of a ship hovered in the blue light, accompanied by lines of scrolling identification code.

"The automated transponder identified the vessel as an independent civilian freighter, registered under the designation *The Nomad*," the sub-commander explained. "There were zero matching identifiers for Legacy Asset 44-A. The MAC addresses, the corporate registry, and the engine signatures were completely unaffiliated with Apex Defense Solutions."

Kael stared at the digital readout. The registry was flawlessly generic.

"Did the scout execute a low-orbit visual pass?" Kael demanded, his paranoia refusing to let a single detail slip by untested. "A digital transponder can be spoofed by a competent technician."

"They did an optical and thermal sweep of the landing zone, sir," the sub-commander confirmed, pulling up a grainy, high-altitude optical render of a dense, towering forest canopy. "The physical silhouette does not match our missing asset. Legacy Asset 44-A is characterized by a massive parabolic deep-space intercept dish and a blocky, un-winged fuselage. The vessel on Exoplanet 4 possessed no external dish, and was actively deploying massive solar collection wings. It appears to be an obsolete, solar-powered surveyor ship, heavily overgrown by the local flora."

Kael analyzed the visual data. The ship in the optical render was completely buried under the canopy of massive alien pines. The dark, oxidized armor blended seamlessly into the shadows, and the wide solar panels looked like a natural extension of the forest.

It looked absolutely nothing like a heavily armored Apex listening post.

"And the life signs?" Kael pressed, his voice hard. "If two S.H.O.P. Marines are hiding in a stolen civilian ship, the orbital bio-scanners will detect their mammalian heartbeats."

"The bio-scans came back entirely negative," the sub-commander said with absolute certainty. "Zero human life signs detected within a hundred-kilometer radius of the hull. The scout's sensors couldn't penetrate the vessel's primary casing—it is lined with an extreme density of lead and titanium, typical for a ship carrying unrefined radioactive ore—but the surrounding perimeter was completely devoid of activity. If *The Nomad* ever had a crew, they abandoned it or perished long ago."

Kael stared at the holographic projection of the forested plateau for a long, silent moment.

He was looking directly at the greatest threat to his empire. He was looking directly at Wyatt and Sarah, who were, at that exact moment, sitting safely inside the heavy, radiation-shielded walls of their tech room, entirely invisible to the orbital sensors probing the sky above them.

The billionaire's lead-lined hull, Aria's flawless digital scrub, and Sarah's brilliant visual camouflage had worked in perfect unison.

Kael let out a sharp, irritated breath and waved his hand, dismissing the hologram of Exoplanet 4.

"Irrelevant space junk," Kael concluded coldly. "Our asset is a retrofitted Heavy Hauler running on a raw fission core, not a solar-powered civilian wreck. Tell Squadron Seven to stop wasting time on dead surveyor ships and push deeper into the Cygnus Arm. I want every active fission signature in the sector flagged and investigated."

"Yes, High Director," the sub-commander saluted. "Pushing the search grid outward."

As the sub-commander turned and marched out of the room, Kael looked back at the sprawling, infinite map of the galaxy. The red icons of the Apex trackers moved past System 88-Tango, leaving Exoplanet 4 completely behind in the dark.

The megacorporation had looked right at their sanctuary, and they had blinked.

The hunt would continue, ravaging the outer arms of the galaxy, but Wyatt and Sarah had officially slipped through the cracks. They had bought the one thing Apex couldn't afford: time.
