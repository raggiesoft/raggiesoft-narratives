# Part 2: The First Steps – Tuesday, November 1, 2044 – 10:15

Wyatt and Sarah didn't run to the airlock. They walked.

For the first time since they had met in that terrifying, subterranean intake room, there was no ticking clock. There was no klaxon pushing them forward, and no corporate overseer barking orders through a megaphone.

They walked hand-in-hand down the quiet, well-lit corridors of *The Nomad*, entirely at their own pace. They bypassed the wire-mesh lockers that held the massive, archaic canvas EVA suits Wyatt had used during his terrifying spacewalk to blow the thruster caps and escape the Apex dropship. They didn't need armor anymore. Sarah was wearing her soft denim jeans and heather-gray t-shirt; Wyatt was in his canvas pants and a fitted black tee. And, true to the unspoken rule of their new life, they were both completely barefoot.

They reached the heavy, primary cargo doors at the base of the ship's hub.

Wyatt reached out and grabbed the heavy manual release lever. He didn't have to fight it; the billionaire's pristine hydraulics engaged flawlessly.

With a deep, powerful hiss of equalizing pressure, the massive iron blast doors unsealed and slowly began to lower, forming a wide, heavy-duty ramp down to the surface.

The first thing that hit them wasn't the light. It was the smell.

After weeks of breathing stale, recycled oxygen, burning machine grease, and the metallic tang of ozone, the air that rushed into the cargo bay was staggering. It was thick, sweet, and incredibly fresh. It smelled of sun-baked stone, rich, damp soil, and the sharp, clean scent of alien pine needles.

As the ramp hit the bedrock with a heavy *CLANG*, the golden light of the foreign sun spilled into the ship, warming their skin.

Wyatt and Sarah walked down the grooved metal incline together. As they reached the bottom, they didn't hesitate. They stepped off the edge of the steel ramp.

Their bare feet touched the surface of Exoplanet 4.

The silicate bedrock of the plateau was smooth, heated to a perfect, comfortable warmth by the morning sun. Sarah let out a soft, shuddering breath, her toes curling against the solid, immovable earth. It was the most grounding, profound sensation she had ever experienced.

They stood at the edge of the massive plateau, taking in the sheer scale of their new world.

Below them, the terrain sloped down into a sprawling, vibrant river valley. The water was crystal clear, rushing over polished stones and winding its way through a towering, ancient forest. The trees were massive, their leaves a deep, rich emerald green that swayed gently in the steady, warm breeze.

It wasn't a barren rock. It wasn't a dusty colony. It was a thriving, breathing biosphere.

The air was alive with sound. They could hear the distant, rushing roar of the river, the rustle of the wind through the canopy, and the strange, melodic calls of alien birds echoing from the tree line.

It felt exactly like stepping into an infinite, untouched wildlife sanctuary.

Wyatt unclipped his Level 1 PDA from his belt. He had wired the archaic device to act as a localized comms-link directly to the ship's servers.

"Aria," Wyatt said, his voice quiet, almost afraid to break the serene silence of the valley. "Run a wide-band frequency sweep of the entire hemisphere. Maximum range."

The PDA chimed softly in his hand.

*"Sweep complete, Wyatt,"* Aria’s voice filtered through the small speaker. *"I am detecting zero artificial electromagnetic signatures. There are no radio frequencies, no localized comms chatter, no synthetic light sources, and no orbital infrastructure. Based on the ship's telemetry and my archival sociological modeling, the probability of prior human colonization is zero."*

Sarah looked at Wyatt, her eyes wide as the AI's words washed over her.

*"To be precise, Administrators,"* Aria continued, her synthesized tone carrying a distinct note of absolute finality, *"you are the only two human beings on this planet. You have the entire world to yourselves."*

Wyatt slowly lowered the PDA, clipping it back to his belt.

He looked out over the endless expanse of green forests and blue water. The absolute isolation would have driven the billionaire who built *The Nomad* completely insane. That was why the man had hoarded the entirety of human media on a server rack—he was terrified of the quiet.

But for Wyatt and Sarah, who had been treated like expendable inventory their entire adult lives, the silence was a gift.

There were no Apex dropships coming for them. There were no S.H.O.P. Foremen waiting in the trees. There were no borders, no corporate quotas, and no rent to pay. They owned the dirt beneath their feet, the river in the valley, and the sky above their heads.

Sarah let go of his hand and took a few steps forward, her bare feet moving from the warm bedrock to a patch of thick, vibrant green moss growing near the edge of the plateau.

She dropped to her knees, pressing her hands directly into the earth. It was rich, dark, and loamy. It was real dirt.

She looked back over her shoulder at Wyatt, a brilliant, overwhelmed smile breaking across her face, tears of pure joy shining in her eyes.

"I can grow anything in this," Sarah laughed, her hands covered in the dark soil. "Wyatt, I can plant the seeds right here. We can actually build a garden."

Wyatt walked over to her, dropping to his knees on the moss right beside her. He didn't care about the dirt on her hands; he reached out and cupped her face, his thumbs gently wiping the tears from her cheeks.

"We're going to build a lot more than a garden," Wyatt promised, his voice thick with emotion. He looked out at the edge of the forest, already visualizing the timber, the stone, and the layout. He remembered the promise he had made to her in the dark of the Nevada facility. "I'm going to build you that house, Sarah. Right here on this ridge. With a real door, and a lock that belongs to us."

Sarah threw her arms around his neck, pulling him down into a fierce, passionate kiss.

The nightmare of Earth was millions of miles away. They were the only two humans on a virgin planet, backed by a fully stocked, AI-controlled dreadnought, and they had the rest of their lives to turn this wildlife sanctuary into a home.
