# Part 5: The Trade-Off – Saturday, May 11, 2045 – 10:15

Two hours of intense, theoretical study was enough for one morning. Wyatt closed the holographic module on atmospheric fluid dynamics, and Sarah dismissed her network architecture text with a tired but satisfied sigh.

They stood up from the plush leather sofa in the sunken library. Wyatt stretched his heavy shoulders, the joints popping softly, while Sarah ran a hand through her hair.

"My brain is officially full," Sarah declared. She looked down at the soft gray t-shirt and denim cut-offs she was wearing. They had put them on solely for the sake of frying French toast in the kitchen, and there was absolutely no reason to keep them on any longer.

"Agreed," Wyatt smiled. He reached down, grabbing the hem of his black t-shirt and pulling it over his head. "Eden Rule?"

"Eden Rule," Sarah confirmed happily. She stripped off her t-shirt, unhooked her bra, and stepped out of her denim shorts.

Completely bare once again, the flawless **21.6°C** ambient air of the estate washed over their skin, instantly restoring that profound, liberating sense of freedom they had found since claiming the mountain.

Sarah gathered their discarded clothes into a bundle and walked out into the polished stone corridor. She approached the sleek, brushed-steel panel of the nearest pneumatic laundry chute and pulled it open, dropping the garments inside.

The panel hissed shut. But instead of the usual, quick *whoosh* of the pneumatic tubes whisking the fabric away to be stored, a deep, sustained mechanical hum vibrated through the wall. It was followed immediately by the faint, rushing sound of high-pressure water churning deep within the estate's infrastructure.

Wyatt paused, his mechanic's ear instantly catching the new sound. "Aria? Did something jam in the chute?"

*"Negative, Wyatt,"* Aria’s synthesized voice chimed from the ceiling array, perfectly calm. *"The pneumatic delivery was successful. The sound you are hearing is the primary sanitization cycle engaging. The collective weight of your garments from yesterday and today has finally met the minimum mass threshold required to run a full, water-efficient load."*

"So it's actually doing laundry right now," Sarah smiled, leaning her bare shoulder against the warm stone wall.

*"Correct,"* Aria confirmed. *"The system’s optical sensors have already physically separated the un-washable items. Sarah's structural undergarment has been rejected and routed via secondary pneumatics to the clean collection bin in the master suite. The cotton and denim are currently undergoing a heavy-duty saline wash and thermal-dry cycle. They will be deposited, fully sanitized and neatly folded, in the master dressing room in forty-seven minutes."*

Wyatt let out a low whistle, shaking his head. "I am never going to get tired of this house."

He turned to look at Sarah, but she was staring down the long, brightly lit corridor toward the empty guest suites. Her expression was deeply thoughtful, a slight crease forming between her brows.

Standing there completely bare, feeling the absolute, uninhibited comfort of her own skin, a sudden, fleeting thought had crossed her mind. The Eden Rule was pure paradise. It was the ultimate rejection of the heavy, suffocating corporate uniforms that had defined their miserable pasts.

But last night, lying in the Alaskan King bed, they had agreed that once the children arrived, the Eden Rule would have to be heavily revised. They would have to put clothes back on in the common areas, the kitchen, and the library to establish boundaries and act as responsible parents.

For a brief, selfish second, she actually wondered if she was willing to give this absolute freedom up.

Was she really ready to trade the perfect, carefree peace of this empty mountain for a house full of crying infants, toddlers, and the daily necessity of getting dressed every morning?

Sarah looked away from the hallway and up at Wyatt. He was watching her patiently, entirely attuned to her shifts in mood.

Her analytical mind automatically brought up the staggering reality of the Genesis Vault sitting miles beneath their bare feet. She remembered the exact demographic statistics Aria had projected over the holographic terminal yesterday.

Eighty-four thousand original embryos had been extracted from Earth before the megacorporation's greed consumed the planet. The billionaire's culling process had meticulously discarded the non-viable subjects, leaving exactly fifty thousand perfect, genetically flawless starts.

- *Total Viable Embryos: 50,000*

- *Female (XX): 25,200 (50.4%)*

- *Male (XY): 24,800 (49.6%)*

Fifty thousand lives. Fifty thousand children who would never know the sting of a S.H.O.P. Marine's baton, the suffocating heat of a deep-core drill, or the terror of a burning world.

She looked at Wyatt's broad, heavily scarred chest, and the deep, unwavering love shining in his dark eyes.

The momentary hesitation completely vanished, washed away by a fierce, maternal resolve that she didn't even know she possessed until this exact moment.

Yes. She absolutely wanted kids.

She wanted a family with Wyatt. She wanted to raise their own sons and daughters to run barefoot through the Earth Conservatory and swim in the indoor pool. And when their own children were old enough to help, she wanted to go down into that freezing vault and wake up the rest of them, stage by stage, bringing those fifty thousand sleeping souls back into the light.

If keeping them safe, nurturing them, and building a healthy, structured society meant she had to put her denim shorts back on in the living room? It was the easiest trade she would ever make in her life.

"Sarah?" Wyatt asked softly, stepping closer and resting his hands gently on her bare waist. "You went a million miles away just now. What are you thinking about?"

"Just the future," Sarah smiled, wrapping her arms around his neck and rising slightly onto her tiptoes to press her body flush against his. "I was just thinking about the kids."

"The January timeline?" Wyatt asked, a warm, protective light filling his eyes.

"The January timeline," Sarah confirmed, her voice filled with absolute certainty. "I want them, Wyatt. Our kids first, and then the Genesis Vault. I want all of it. And if that means the Eden Rule gets strictly relegated to behind our closed bedroom doors..."

She leaned in, kissing him with a slow, lingering devotion.

"...I'm more than okay with that."

Wyatt kissed her back, his heavy arms holding her securely against the warm stone wall. He didn't need to hear the internal debate to know that she had just made a profound, permanent choice for their family.

"Behind closed doors is perfectly fine with me," Wyatt murmured against her lips, his thumb tracing the curve of her spine. "But until they get here... the mountain is ours."

"Entirely ours," Sarah breathed, completely at peace with the present, and wildly excited for the future.
