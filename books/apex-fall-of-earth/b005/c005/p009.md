# Part 9: The Runabout – Saturday, May 11, 2045 – 13:15

The cool, rain-washed breeze sweeping across the observation terrace was incredibly refreshing, but the fresh air eventually did exactly what it always did: it made them hungry.

Sarah’s stomach gave a soft, polite rumble that made Wyatt chuckle. He wrapped his arm around her bare shoulders, pulling her gently away from the heavy stone balustrade.

"Alright," Wyatt smiled, looking down at her. "Let's go raid the billionaire's pantry again. But this time, no cooking."

"Cold cuts," Sarah agreed immediately, leaning into his side as they walked back through the sliding brushed-steel doors and into the flawless 21.6°C climate control of the estate. "Since we aren't turning on the stove, the Eden Rule remains fully in effect."

They walked completely bare back to the sprawling master kitchen. Sarah bypassed the heavy induction range and headed straight for the stasis fridge. She pulled out a massive haul: thick, vacuum-sealed packages of smoked turkey, cured salami, sharp white cheddar, and a jar of whole-grain mustard.

Wyatt found a loaf of soft, preserved artisan bread and a wooden cutting board. Working side-by-side in their own skin, they quickly assembled two towering, multi-layered deli sandwiches.

They didn't even bother with plates. They just grabbed their sandwiches and sat cross-legged on the plush sectional sofa in the living room, listening to the gentle crackle of the geothermal fire pit. The rich, savory bite of the deli meat and the sharp tang of the mustard hit the spot perfectly.

Wyatt finished his last bite, brushing the crumbs from his hands. He looked up at the ceiling array, his mechanic's curiosity unable to wait any longer.

"Aria," Wyatt called out. "What's the status of the secondary build in the Fabrication Foundry?"

"The molecular 3D printers and CNC milling arms completed their cycles fourteen minutes ago, Wyatt," Aria chimed warmly. "The atmospheric shuttle is fully assembled, powered, and awaiting your physical inspection."

Wyatt looked over at Sarah, a massive, kid-on-Christmas morning grin breaking across his scarred face. "Our car is ready."

Sarah laughed, wiping her mouth with a napkin and uncurling her legs. "Then we need to go see it. But if we're heading into the machine shop and taking a brand new ship out for a test flight over a flooded valley..."

"The Eden Rule is suspended," Wyatt finished, standing up and offering her his hand. "We need to suit up. Real gear this time."

They walked hand-in-hand back to the master suite and stepped into the massive dressing room.

The freedom of the last few days had been incredible, but they were about to be test pilots. Wyatt pulled on a pair of heavy canvas work pants and a fitted black t-shirt. Sarah opted for a pair of durable, dark denim jeans and a long-sleeved gray Henley.

But the biggest change was the footwear. For the first time since they had claimed the mountain, they opened the bottom drawers of the wardrobe and pulled out thick, white cotton socks. Wyatt laced up a pair of heavy, reinforced leather work boots, while Sarah pulled on a pair of sturdy, comfortable hiking boots.

Standing up, the added weight and structure of the boots felt distinctly foreign after days of walking skin-to-stone, but it also felt grounding. They were ready to work.

They took the pneumatic elevator down to Sub-Level 2. When the brushed-steel doors parted, the blinding plasma sparks and the deafening industrial roar they had witnessed that morning were completely gone. The foundry was quiet, smelling of warm metal and fresh polymer.

Sitting in the center of the secondary hangar bay, completely uncoupled from the massive 3D printers, was their new ship.

It was absolutely beautiful.

Unlike the blocky, utilitarian armor of The Nomad, the runabout was a sleek, aerodynamic teardrop of polished, matte-silver titanium. It featured swept-back stabilizing fins and a massive, tinted canopy of reinforced hydro-glass that offered the pilot and passengers a nearly unobstructed 180-degree view.

Wyatt walked slowly around the hull, his heavy boots echoing on the hangar floor. He ran a reverent hand over the seamless, molecularly printed armor.

"Aria, what are we looking at under the hood?" Wyatt asked, thoroughly impressed.

"The chassis is an amalgamation of advanced civilian aerospace templates," Aria explained, her voice echoing through the foundry. "It utilizes high-capacity solar-graphene batteries paired with four redundant repulsorlift coils for silent atmospheric flight. For orbital velocity, it is equipped with a miniaturized, localized ion-drive."

Sarah walked up to the tinted canopy, peering inside. She could see four plush, ergonomic leather seats and a clean, incredibly streamlined digital control yoke.

"Orbital velocity?" Sarah asked, looking up at the ceiling. "I thought we just commissioned an atmospheric shuttle."

"While it is primarily designed for planetary surveys, Sarah, the hull is fully vacuum-sealed and radiation-shielded," Aria clarified. "The ion-drive is perfectly capable of achieving escape velocity. You can comfortably pilot this vessel through the atmosphere, break orbit, and travel to Anchor, Aegis, Genesis, Haven, Nomad, and Aurora. It is a true interplanetary runabout."

"A family car that can go to the moons," Wyatt laughed, shaking his head. He looked across the silver hull at Sarah. "I think the billionaire's definition of a 'light shuttle' is a little different than ours."

Wyatt reached out and tapped the sleek biometric sensor on the side of the hull. With a soft, pneumatic hiss, the gull-wing door smoothly lifted open, revealing the pristine, new-ship smell of the cabin.

"Well, Mrs. Colton," Wyatt grinned, gesturing toward the co-pilot seat. "The rain has stopped, and we have a brand new, custom-built spaceship sitting in the garage. Do you want to go see what our planet actually looks like?"

Sarah didn't hesitate. She climbed into the sleek cabin, dropping into the plush leather seat and instantly securing her four-point harness.

"Fire it up, Wyatt," Sarah smiled, her eyes shining with pure adventure. "Let's go explore Eden."
