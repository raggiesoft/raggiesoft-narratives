# Part 8: The Petrichor – Saturday, May 11, 2045 – 12:30

The relentless, oceanic drumming against the hydro-glass windows slowly began to soften.

Wyatt and Sarah looked up from the holographic map of their newly named solar system. For nearly twenty hours, the super-cell storm had battered the mountain, turning the sky into an opaque wall of slate-gray water. But now, the heavy, concussive sheets of rain were tapering off into a gentle, misty drizzle.

Through the massive window, the dark, bruised clouds were finally beginning to fracture. Brilliant, golden shafts of light from Helios pierced through the gray canopy, striking the flooded valley miles below them and turning the churning water into a mirror of liquid gold.

"It's stopping," Sarah whispered, untangling her legs from Wyatt's and standing up from the plush sectional. She walked over to the glass, resting her bare hands against the cool surface.

Wyatt stood up right behind her, his chest brushing against her back as he looked out at the breaking storm.

"Aria," Wyatt called out. "Is there a pedestrian access point to the outside? A terrace or an observation deck? Opening the massive hangar doors just to get some fresh air seems like overkill."

"There is a secure, cantilevered observation terrace extending from the western face of the estate, Wyatt," Aria chimed perfectly. "It is accessed via the corridor adjacent to the library. The exterior atmospheric pressure is stable, and there is no lightning detected within a fifty-kilometer radius. It is perfectly safe."

Sarah turned around, her eyes bright with anticipation. She looked down at herself, entirely bare, and then up at Wyatt's equally bare, scarred frame.

"Should we get dressed?" Sarah asked, a playful, questioning lilt in her voice. She remembered the pile of clean clothes sitting neatly in the master dressing room.

Wyatt looked out the window at the absolute, pristine wilderness of their new planet. There were no corporate orbital scanners. There were no tracker squadrons in the atmosphere. There were no neighbors for millions of lightyears.

A slow, deeply relaxed grin spread across his face.

"Are we operating heavy machinery?" Wyatt asked, crossing his arms.

"No," Sarah smiled.

"Are we frying bacon in hot grease?"

"Definitely not."

"Then the Eden Rule stands," Wyatt declared, offering her his hand. "We are the only two human beings on Planet Eden. We absolutely do not need to get dressed just to step onto our own front porch."

Sarah let out a bright, ringing laugh, taking his hand. "I love your logic, Mr. Colton."

They walked past the library, their bare feet silent on the polished stone, until they reached a set of heavy, brushed-steel doors at the end of the corridor. As they approached, the automated sensors engaged, and the doors glided silently into the walls.

The transition from the flawlessly climate-controlled 21.6°C interior to the raw, wild atmosphere of the planet was an absolute shock to the senses.

They stepped out onto a wide, smooth-stone terrace suspended high above the valley.

The air was heavy, incredibly moist, and wonderfully cool. It rushed over their bare skin, carrying the intoxicating, earthy scent of petrichor—the smell of rain-soaked stone, crushed alien pine needles, and rich, damp soil.

Sarah walked to the edge of the heavy stone balustrade, taking a massive, deep breath of the rain-washed air. It felt incredibly pure, completely devoid of the industrial ozone and recycled metallic tang that had choked her lungs for her entire adult life.

She rested her arms on the railing, looking down at the world they had claimed.

The river valley they had explored just yesterday was entirely gone, replaced by a massive, swollen lake of brown, churning water that stretched all the way to the canyon walls. It was a terrifying display of Exoplanet 4's raw natural power, but standing high above it on their indestructible mountain fortress, they were completely, miraculously untouchable.

Wyatt stepped up right beside her, wrapping his heavy arm around her bare waist and pulling her securely against his side.

Above them, the clouds parted further. The golden light of Helios washed over the terrace, instantly warming their rain-misted skin.

Sarah leaned her head against Wyatt's shoulder, closing her eyes as the sunlight hit her face. A gentle, cool breeze swept through the valley, drying the fine mist on her arms and legs. It was a profound, intensely intimate communion with their new home. They weren't just observing nature; entirely bare under the sun and the sky, they were a part of it.

"Look at that," Wyatt murmured, pointing his heavy, calloused finger out toward the eastern ridge.

Sarah opened her eyes. Arching brilliantly across the breaking clouds, perfectly framing the flooded valley and the towering green forests, was a massive, vibrant double rainbow.

It was a cinematic, almost impossible display of beauty.

"I don't think I've ever seen a real rainbow," Sarah breathed, her voice thick with wonder. "On the agricultural colonies, the sky was always choked with atmospheric scrubbers. And on the NTTR... we never even saw the sky."

"Well, get used to it," Wyatt smiled, turning his head to press a warm, lingering kiss against her bare shoulder. "Because we have the best seats in the universe."

They stood there for a long time, completely exposed to the elements, letting the warm sunlight of Helios and the cool, rain-moistened air wash over them. The megacorporation, the tracking ships, and the nightmares of Earth were millions of miles away, lost in the dark.

Here, on the terrace of The Sanctuary, there was only the wind, the water, and each other.
