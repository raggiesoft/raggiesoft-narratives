# Part 4: The Lost Time – Saturday, May 11, 2045 – 09:45

The heavy pneumatic elevator carried them swiftly away from the blinding plasma sparks and deafening industrial roar of the Fabrication Foundry. As the brushed-steel doors parted, depositing them back onto the main level, the quiet, pristine luxury of the mountain estate welcomed them back.

In strict accordance with the kitchen and heavy-machinery safety protocols, they were still dressed—Wyatt in his dark canvas shorts and black t-shirt, and Sarah in her denim cut-offs and soft gray tee. But as they walked hand-in-hand down the polished stone corridor, their bare feet padded softly against the floor, perfectly maintaining their physical connection to their new home.

They bypassed the living room and stepped down into the sunken, mahogany-paneled library. Outside the massive, camouflaged hydro-glass window, the rain had finally begun to slow, reducing the torrential downpour to a steady, misty drizzle over the flooded valley.

They took their places on the plush leather sofa, sinking comfortably into the cushions. Wyatt rested his arm along the back of the sofa, letting Sarah tuck her legs up and lean her weight against his side.

"Alright, Aria," Sarah called out, tapping the edge of the massive holographic glass table to wake the terminal. "We saw the new hull and commissioned the family car. Now, it's time to figure out how to actually raise a family. Pull up the pediatric and obstetric archives."

*"Accessing medical and developmental databanks now,"* Aria chimed, her synthesized voice echoing warmly from the ceiling array.

The glass table hummed, projecting a pristine, glowing blue, three-dimensional model of a human timeline. It broke down the standard forty-week gestation period, complete with nutritional requirements, developmental milestones, and a massive sub-directory on early childhood cognitive pacing.

"Okay, let's run the actual timeline," Sarah murmured, her analytical mind immediately engaging with the data. "If my S.H.O.P. Marine hormone blockers flush out completely by the end of the year, and we successfully conceive in January of 2046..."

*"A January conception would place the estimated date of delivery approximately forty weeks later, in October of 2046,"* Aria calculated instantly, adjusting the holographic timeline to display the exact months. *"During this period, you will have full access to the automated medical wing on Sub-Level 1 for routine non-invasive ultrasonography and prenatal blood panels."*

"October 2046," Wyatt repeated, his dark eyes fixed on the glowing blue projection. The sheer reality of it—that in less than a year and a half, he would be holding his own child in this impenetrable fortress—sent a profound, anchoring warmth straight through his chest.

"We'll be ready," Sarah smiled, reaching over to lace her fingers through his. "We have the space, the food, and plenty of time to study the textbooks."

She looked at the chronological timeline hovering above the table, her eyes tracing the months. Suddenly, she stopped, a slight frown creasing her forehead.

"Wait," Sarah said, sitting up slightly. "Aria, what is today's exact date again?"

*"Today is Saturday, May 11th, 2045,"* Aria answered smoothly.

Sarah looked at Wyatt, a sudden, highly amused realization dawning on her face.

"Wyatt... we changed the ship's calendar down in the river valley. We fast-forwarded from November 2044 to May 2045 to match the planet's seasons."

Wyatt blinked, his S.H.O.P. Marine training momentarily struggling to catch up with the civilian calendar math. "Right. Six months. A rounding error."

"Not just a rounding error," Sarah laughed, reaching up to gently cup his scarred jaw. "Wyatt, when is your birthday?"

Wyatt paused. He had spent his entire adult life working deep-sea welding rigs and subterranean bunkers where birthdays were completely irrelevant. Apex Defense Solutions certainly hadn't handed out cupcakes.

"April 8th," Wyatt answered, the date feeling strange on his tongue after so many years of ignoring it.

Sarah’s smile widened into a massive, delighted beam. "Wyatt! We skipped right over April! You were twenty-three when we boarded this ship. You're twenty-four now!"

Wyatt stared at her, the math finally clicking into place. Because of the localized temporal calibration they had authorized to sync with Exoplanet 4's spring season, he had technically aged a year in the blink of an eye.

A deep, booming laugh rumbled in his chest, echoing warmly off the mahogany panels.

"Well, I'll be damned," Wyatt chuckled, wrapping both arms around her waist and pulling her flush against him. "Happy belated birthday to me. I guess I'm officially an old man now."

"You wear twenty-four very well, Mr. Colton," Sarah giggled, kissing him soundly on the lips.

When she pulled back, Wyatt kept his arms securely around her. "And what about you? When do I have to bake a cake?"

"September 12th," Sarah told him, her eyes shining in the soft holographic light. "So I'm officially still eighteen. But I'll be turning nineteen just a few months before we start trying for the baby."

Wyatt looked down at the brilliant, resilient woman sitting in his arms. In just a few months, they would be celebrating her nineteenth birthday, not on a failing agricultural colony or a rusted staging facility, but in a multi-billion-credit mountain estate that belonged entirely to them.

"September 12th," Wyatt committed the date permanently to memory. "We're going to throw an absolute massive party. Just the two of us. We'll harvest the garden, I'll pull something ridiculous out of the billionaire's stasis freezer, and we'll celebrate."

"I can't wait," Sarah breathed, resting her forehead against his.

The chronological jump had severed their last lingering ties to Earth's timeline. They weren't tracking the days since the NTTR collapsed anymore. They were tracking their own milestones.

Wyatt looked back at the glowing blue timeline of human development floating above the table.

"So, pediatric archives," Wyatt said, shifting his focus back to the massive digital curriculum Aria had curated for them. "Where do we start? Diaper changing protocols, or how to properly burp an infant?"

*"I recommend beginning with Module One: Neonatal Sleep Cycles and Nutritional Foundations,"* Aria suggested helpfully.

"Load it up, Aria," Sarah smiled, settling comfortably back into Wyatt's side. "We have a lot to learn."
