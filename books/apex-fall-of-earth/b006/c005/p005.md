# Part 5: The Bootcamp – Monday, December 20, 2049 – 04:15

The automated medical bay on Sub-Level 1 was an entirely different universe than it had been four years ago.

There were no flashing crimson alarms. There was no terrifying, frantic rush of emergency surgical equipment. There was no agonizing, breathless silence waiting for tiny, underdeveloped lungs to start working.

Instead, the room was bathed in a warm, peaceful amber light. Soft, rhythmic acoustic guitar music played through Aria’s ceiling array. And at exactly 04:15, a loud, robust, furiously healthy wail echoed off the sterilized steel walls.

Wyatt Colton stood beside the primary bio-bed, tears streaming freely down his scarred, bearded face. He wasn't gripping the mattress in terror; he was gently supporting his wife’s shoulders.

Sarah let out a breathless, exhausted laugh, her face glowing with a sheen of sweat.

Resting squarely on her chest, completely free of any tubes, wires, or CPAP masks, was an eight-pound, full-term baby boy. He was pink, he was loud, and his neurological reflexes were firing with absolute, perfect neurotypical precision. He kicked his strong little legs against the thermal blanket, instantly rooting against Sarah’s collarbone.

"Delivery complete and flawless," Aria’s synthesized voice murmured warmly. "Subject D’s APGAR score is a perfect ten. Maternal vitals are completely stabilized. No specialized incubation required."

"He's huge," Wyatt whispered, a wet, booming chuckle escaping his chest as he reached out with a trembling, calloused finger to stroke the newborn's cheek. The baby instantly turned and grasped Wyatt's thick finger, his grip surprisingly strong.

"He's perfect," Sarah breathed, kissing the top of the boy's dark, damp head. She looked up at Wyatt, her dark eyes shining with absolute joy. "We did it, Wyatt. A normal, boring, beautiful delivery."

Wyatt leaned down and kissed her deeply, the heavy, lingering trauma of the triplets' traumatic arrival finally, permanently washing out of his soul.

He pulled back, smiling down at his second son. "So. Are we going with William? Or maybe Thomas? I was thinking about—"

"Wyatt, we both know we have absolutely no say in this," Sarah laughed, wincing slightly as she adjusted her posture against the bio-bed. "The commanding officer already filed the paperwork."

Wyatt groaned, a massive grin splitting his face. "She named him after a fictional dog from a holographic picture book, Sarah."

"It's a good name," Sarah countered playfully. "Besides, when she pointed at my belly and gave the order, Nora giggled, and Leo slammed the 'Good' button on his tablet. The Vanguard voted unanimously. Are you really going to pull rank on Ellie?"

Wyatt looked at the squalling, perfectly healthy newborn on his wife's chest. He thought of the fierce, unshakeable three-year-old waiting upstairs to inspect her new logistical asset.

"Jack it is," Wyatt conceded, a deep rumble of laughter vibrating in his chest. "Welcome to the bunker, Jack Colton."

Two hours later, after Sarah was comfortably transferred to a localized recovery bed, Wyatt securely wrapped Jack in a thick, thermal swaddle. He carried his new son into the pneumatic elevator, riding it up to the residential level.

He walked quietly down the hall and pushed open the heavy timber door of the nursery.

The room was dimly lit by the morning twilight. Over the last week, Wyatt had brought a beautiful, standard sixty-millimeter-slatted wooden crib out of storage. He hadn't placed it across the room. Following the strict, unspoken rules of the unit, he had installed the crib directly flush against the left edge of the massive, floor-level Mega-Bed.

The Vanguard was awake.

They were sitting in a tight, horizontal line on the edge of the memory-foam mattress. Leo was in the center, humming a low, curious note. Nora was leaning heavily against his right shoulder. And Ellie was sitting perfectly straight on the left, her dark eyes locked onto the bundle in Wyatt's arms.

"Alright, squad," Wyatt whispered, dropping to one knee right in front of the Mega-Bed so he was at eye-level with the three toddlers. "I brought the new recruit."

He gently lowered his arms, presenting the swaddled, sleeping newborn to the Vanguard.

Nora let out a soft, breathy gasp. She carefully reached out, lightly grazing her fingertips over Jack’s soft cheek. Her empathetic heart instantly melted. She turned to Leo, guiding his spasming hand so he could gently pat the baby's blanket. Leo's humming pitched up in excitement, his body relaxing against Nora's side.

Then, it was the commander's turn.

Ellie didn't coo. She didn't giggle. She leaned forward, her tactical brain intensely evaluating the new asset. She inspected the tightness of the swaddle. She inspected the rhythmic rise and fall of Jack's chest.

She reached out with a tiny, firm index finger and poked Jack directly in the center of his chest.

Jack stirred slightly, letting out a tiny, high-pitched squeak, but settled right back to sleep.

Ellie narrowed her eyes. She leaned down so her face was only inches from the newborn's ear.

"Mine," Ellie whispered, her tone carrying the absolute, uncompromising authority of a drill sergeant laying down the law to a fresh cadet. She pointed a finger at the floor. "Stay."

Wyatt had to physically bite his lip to keep from laughing out loud. Jack was exactly two hours old, completely oblivious to the world, and Ellie was already running him through boot camp. She was establishing the perimeter, giving him his holding orders, and ensuring he understood who was in charge of the nursery.

Satisfied that the newborn had received his briefing, Ellie gave a firm, sharp nod. She looked up at Wyatt and pointed to the new, standard crib resting flush against her side of the Mega-Bed.

"Bed," Ellie commanded.

"Yes, ma'am," Wyatt whispered, his heart overflowing with a fierce, profound love for his beautifully chaotic family.

He stood up, gently lowering Jack over the wooden rail and placing him safely into the center of the crib's mattress.

The moment Wyatt's hands left the baby, Ellie immediately shifted her position. She scooted to the extreme left edge of the Mega-Bed, pressing her side directly against the wooden slats of Jack's crib. She reached her arm through the sixty-millimeter gap, resting her tiny hand protectively on the edge of Jack's mattress.

With her other hand, she reached back and firmly grabbed Leo's sleeve, ensuring the Vanguard's circuit remained unbroken.

Nora anchored Leo from the other side.

The perimeter was secured. The new recruit was safely logged into the inventory. The unit was complete.

Wyatt stood in the dim light of the nursery, watching his four children. The galaxy outside the Aegis shield was burning, but inside this room, humanity had never been stronger.

"Sleep well, Vanguard," Wyatt murmured softly, stepping backward out of the room and letting the heavy timber door slide shut.
