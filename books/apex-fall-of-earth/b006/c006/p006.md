# Part 6: The Authorized Harvest – Thursday, May 18, 2051 – 13:00

The pneumatic elevator hummed a low, steady note as it descended deep into the bedrock of Planet Eden.

Inside the spacious metal car, Wyatt Colton stood with his arms crossed over his massive chest. Sarah was holding a highly energetic, sixteen-month-old Jack on her hip. And parked perfectly in the center of the floor was the Chariot Mark II, loaded with the four-and-a-half-year-old Vanguard.

The elevator slowed, hissing as it approached Sub-Level 2.

Wyatt looked down at his commanding officer. Ellie was standing at the front handle of the wagon, wearing her miniature tactical vest. She was staring at the heavy steel elevator doors with a distinct, incredibly cocky smirk on her face.

"Alright, squad. Listen up," Wyatt announced, adopting his best briefing voice. "We are entering the primary Hydroponics Bay. This is an authorized, supervised harvesting mission. No one touches the nutrient valves, and no one climbs the trellises. Are we clear?"

Ellie didn't answer. She just puffed her small chest out, completely unbothered by the rules.

The doors slid open, revealing the massive, humid expanse of Sub-Level 2.

The room was bathed in the warm, pinkish-purple glow of high-voltage UV lights. Towering vertical columns of alien ferns, synthesized tomatoes, and heavy leafy greens stretched all the way to the vaulted ceiling. The air was thick with the smell of rich soil and the loud, rhythmic thrum of the deep water-reclamation tanks.

For Jack, it was a brand-new universe. He had never been permitted on this sub-level.

Sarah set him down on the grated steel floor. Jack’s eyes went completely wide. He craned his neck, staring up at the towering columns of glowing green plants.

"Big plant!" Jack shouted, his crisp vocabulary echoing over the sound of the water pumps. He immediately took off at a rapid toddler jog down the center aisle. "Eat it! Eat the big plant!"

"Whoa, slow down, recruit," Sarah laughed, chasing after him before he could launch himself into a water tank.

But for the Vanguard, this wasn't a new frontier. They had been here before.

Ellie marched out of the elevator, pulling the Mark II behind her. Nora limped along at the back, gripping the push-bar tightly. Nora looked around the pink-lit room with a nervous, breathy sigh. She remembered what happened the last time they were down here. She remembered the flashing yellow security alarms, her father's terrified shout, and the immediate reprimand.

Nora shrank back slightly, her empathetic heart urging her to stay close to the safety of the elevator.

Ellie, however, felt absolutely no remorse.

The commanding officer stopped the wagon directly in front of Sector 4. She looked down at the exact spot on the grated floor where Wyatt had caught her holding a squished tomato over two years ago.

Ellie didn't look guilty. She looked like a decorated war hero returning to the site of her greatest tactical triumph.

She turned around, pointing a tiny finger at the towering, seven-foot-high biometric scanner Wyatt had been forced to install on the wall specifically to keep her out. Then, she pointed down at the wagon, and finally at herself.

She looked at Nora and Leo.

"Did that," Ellie stated loudly, her dark eyes flashing with absolute, unadulterated pride. "Me. Brought unit. Beat the door."

She wore the memory of the security breach like a S.H.O.P. Marine medal of honor. She had successfully bypassed a Level-3 magnetic lock, utilized her brother as a sandbag to reach the scanner, and extracted her entire squad to a restricted zone. The fact that she got caught was irrelevant; the execution of the heist had been flawless.

From the center seat of the wagon, Leo let out a loud, joyous vocalization. The massive, rhythmic vibration of the hydroponic water pumps and the warm UV lights provided an incredible, soothing sensory input for his rigid nervous system. He slammed his shaking palm down on his AAC tablet.

*"Good,"* the tablet chimed happily.

"I know you did it, Commander," Wyatt sighed heavily, walking up behind them and dragging a heavy hand down his bearded jaw. He looked at his daughter's smug, intensely proud face. He was trying to be a stern authority figure, but the sheer tactical arrogance of his four-year-old was incredibly difficult not to laugh at. "And you're never doing it unsupervised again. Now, grab a basket. We have tomatoes to pick."

Ellie gave a firm nod. The nostalgic tour was over; it was time to work.

She grabbed a small woven basket Wyatt handed her and marched down the aisle to where Sarah was currently wrangling Jack.

"Alright, watch Mama," Sarah instructed, gently twisting a perfectly ripe, red tomato off a low-hanging vine. She handed it to Jack. "Put it in the basket gently, Jack."

Jack grinned, showing all his teeth. He took the tomato, reeled his arm back, and prepared to fast-ball it directly into the metal water tank.

A heavy, canvas-booted foot stomped against the grating.

"No," Ellie commanded sharply, stepping directly in front of the recruit.

Jack froze, his arm still cocked back. He looked at his commanding officer.

Ellie didn't yell. She reached out, firmly taking the tomato from his hand. She held her basket out, slowly and deliberately placing the fruit inside without bruising it. She looked back at Jack, tapping the side of the basket.

"Here," Ellie instructed. "Gentle. Or latrine duty."

Jack didn't know what latrine duty was, but the absolute authority in his sister's voice was universally understood. He gave a rapid, obedient nod. "Gen-tle."

"Good recruit," Ellie approved, patting his dark hair.

Wyatt stood at the end of the aisle, watching Nora carefully pick a tomato of her own and carry it over to Leo, letting him feel the smooth, waxy skin before dropping it into the basket resting on his lap tray.

The harvest was chaotic, messy, and loud, but the chain of command was holding perfectly. The Colton family was officially cultivating their own future, and the Vanguard was overseeing every single step of the operation.
