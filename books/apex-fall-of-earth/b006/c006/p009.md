# Part 9: The Perimeter Test – Friday, November 3, 2051 – 11:45

Down in the Combat Information Center, the holographic code on the primary monitor glowed a steady, secure green. The loophole was officially closed.

Wyatt Colton leaned back in his heavy command chair, letting out a long, satisfied exhale. But before he logged out of the root-command architecture, a lingering detail caught his attention.

"Aria, one more thing," Wyatt rumbled, tapping the edge of the glass console. "Update your localized audio-response matrix. We are officially five years into this operation. You don't need to use their clinical gestation tags anymore."

*"Specify parameters, Administrator,"* Aria chimed warmly.

"Subject A is Ellie. Subject B is Nora. Subject C is Leo. And Subject D is Jack," Wyatt instructed, an affectionate smile touching his scarred face. "From now on, refer to them by their names. They aren't test subjects; they're the Vanguard."

*"Parameters accepted and integrated, Wyatt,"* Aria’s synthesized voice replied, the tone noticeably softening. *"The Colton family registry is fully updated."*

"Good. Now, let's run a field test," Wyatt said, pushing himself out of the chair.

Ten minutes later, Wyatt stepped out of the pneumatic elevator onto the residential level. He found Sarah in the hallway, carrying Jack on her hip, while the triplets were rolling the Chariot Mark II toward the kitchen for their scheduled rations.

"Hold up, squad," Wyatt called out, adopting a crisp, authoritative tone. "Mandatory security drill. Commander, report to the Sub-Level 4 Fab-Foundry access doors."

Ellie immediately stopped the wagon. She loved drills. She spun around, her dark eyes lighting up with the challenge, and marched her unit directly to the heavy, restricted durasteel doors at the end of the hall.

"Alright, Sarah," Wyatt smiled, holding out his thick arm. "Lift her up."

Sarah grinned, completely understanding what Wyatt had been doing downstairs. She hoisted the five-year-old up so Ellie was perfectly level with the biometric hand-scanner mounted seven feet up the wall.

Ellie confidently pressed her small palm flat against the glowing glass. She expected the heavy thud of the locks disengaging.

Instead, the scanner flashed a harsh, bright crimson.

*"Access Denied,"* Aria’s voice announced from the ceiling. *"Civilian-Minor clearance recognized, Ellie. Requesting Administrator approval."*

Instantly, Wyatt’s wrist-mounted comm-link beeped. He tapped the screen, showing Ellie the digital notification that read: *\[Access Request: Ellie Colton - Fab-Foundry - Deny/Approve\]*.

Ellie frowned, staring at the red light on the scanner. She had been physically capable of reaching the lock with Sarah's help, but the system had still rejected her administrative authority.

"My turn," Jack babbled happily from Sarah's other hip. Sarah shifted him over, letting Jack slap his chubby hand against the glass.

*BZZT.* The scanner flashed red again.

*"Access Denied. Civilian-Minor clearance recognized, Jack. Requesting Administrator approval."* Wyatt’s wrist beeped a second time. He looked down at the commanding officer. "Alright, Ellie. You and Jack are locked out. Now... let's test the override."

Ellie’s eyes narrowed. She knew exactly what Wyatt was doing. She turned to the Chariot, tapping the wooden rail. She looked at Leo, then pointed at his ruggedized AAC tablet resting on his lap tray.

"Do it," Ellie commanded.

Nora leaned over, her empathetic heart beating a little faster during the tense security drill. She wrapped her warm hand over Leo’s shaking wrist, steadying his spasming muscles as she guided his index finger down to the tablet's secondary menu.

Leo found the bright orange square depicting a spark plug—the customized icon for the Fab-Foundry doors. He slammed his palm against the glass.

*"Open Fab-Foundry,"* the tablet announced loudly into the hallway.

Wyatt held his breath.

The heavy durasteel doors didn't hiss. The locks didn't cycle. The physical barrier remained absolutely, completely immovable.

Instead, the screen on Leo's AAC tablet suddenly flashed an amber padlock icon.

*"Access Denied,"* Aria’s voice chimed smoothly through the tablet's localized speaker. *"Civilian-Minor clearance recognized, Leo. Requesting Administrator approval."*

For the third time, Wyatt’s wrist-mounted comm-link beeped. A new notification popped up on his screen: *\[Access Request: Leo Colton - Fab-Foundry - Deny/Approve\]*.

Wyatt let out a massive, booming laugh, throwing his head back in pure triumph. The test was an absolute, flawless success.

Amendment 1 was perfectly intact—Leo hadn't been forced to physically reach the high scanner or use his hands to turn a doorknob. His digital access route worked perfectly. But the *security* of the fortress was equalized. Leo was bound by the exact same administrative rules as his able-bodied siblings.

Sarah let out a sigh of relief, resting her forehead against Jack's dark hair. "You did it, Wyatt. The perimeter is actually secure."

Wyatt looked down at his five-year-old tactical genius.

Ellie stared at the sealed blast doors. She looked at Leo's tablet, noting the amber padlock. She processed the tactical reality: her skeleton key was gone. The constitutional loophole had been officially patched.

She didn't throw a tantrum. She didn't argue. She was a S.H.O.P. Marine commander, and she respected a solid, impenetrable defense.

Ellie looked up at Wyatt, crossing her arms over her miniature tactical vest. She gave a single, slow, deeply respectful nod. Her father had finally outmaneuvered her.

"Good Dada," Ellie conceded, the faintest hint of an impressed smirk tugging at the corner of her mouth. She turned back to the Chariot, grabbing the front handle. "Drill over. Rations now."

"Copy that, Commander," Wyatt grinned, stepping out of her way as she marched the Vanguard toward the kitchen.

The Colton family was safe, the laws of Planet Eden were functioning perfectly, and the children were growing up exactly as they were supposed to: fiercely intelligent, completely unified, and heavily supervised.
