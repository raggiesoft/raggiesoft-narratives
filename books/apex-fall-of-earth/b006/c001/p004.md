# Part 4: The Ghost of Eden – Friday, September 22, 2045 – 13:15

The Combat Information Center on Sub-Level 3 was vibrating.

Wyatt Colton stood at the primary tactical holotable, his heavy boots planted firmly on the grated deck as the buried dreadnought’s twin fusion reactors spooled up to ninety percent capacity. The sheer, terrifying amount of power required to completely mask a terrestrial planet was causing the ambient lighting in the CIC to flicker rapidly between blue and white.

"Capacitors are fully charged, Wyatt," Aria’s synthesized voice reported, cutting through the deep, localized rumble of the engines. "The planetary dampening field and the kinetic deflector grid are standing by for manual authorization."

Wyatt looked at the glowing holographic globe hovering above the glass table. For the last two months, it had been a beacon in the dark—a massive, radiating signature of heat, biological life, and advanced electromagnetic infrastructure. Any corporate scout sweeping the sector would spot them like a flare in the night.

"Authorization granted," Wyatt commanded, his dark eyes locking onto the globe. "Throw the switch, Aria. Take us off the grid."
