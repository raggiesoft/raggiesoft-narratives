# Part 7: The Administrative Override – Friday, November 10, 2045 – 15:00

The automated medical wing on Sub-Level 1 was sterile, brilliantly lit, and completely empty except for Wyatt Colton.

At thirty weeks into the triplet pregnancy, Sarah was resting upstairs in the master suite, her body pushed to the absolute physical limits of human gestation. Down here in the quiet hum of the clinic, Wyatt was preparing for the inevitable.

He stood over the primary diagnostic bio-bed, his massive hands resting on the edge of the memory-foam mattress. Hovering directly above the bed was a high-resolution, three-dimensional hologram of a human circulatory system, simulating the exact biomechanics of a multiple-gestation delivery.

Wyatt was a former S.H.O.P. Marine. He knew how to stop a bleed, how to set a bone, and how to keep a shattered contractor alive long enough for medevac. But delivering three infants safely required precision obstetrics, and he was the only human doctor on the planet.

"Aria," Wyatt stated, his dark eyes locked on the glowing holographic telemetry. "Reset the simulation to stage one. We need to bypass your medical diagnostic restrictions."

"I am bound by my core programming, Wyatt," Aria’s synthesized voice replied, polite but unyielding. "I cannot legally or ethically render a medical diagnosis, prescribe pharmaceutical intervention, or independently initiate surgical procedures."

"I know," Wyatt grunted, his mechanic's brain entirely focused on the structural logic of the AI's code. "Which is why you aren't going to do any of those things. I am."

He tapped the primary glass console, bringing up a massive, blank command registry.

"I am the Chief Medical Officer of Planet Eden," Wyatt dictated, his deep voice leaving zero room for debate. "I am officially pre-authorizing a series of 'If/Then' mechanical macros. When the raw telemetry of the bio-bed reaches a specific, pre-defined numerical threshold, you are not making a medical decision. You are simply executing a pre-approved, automated administrative action authorized by me. Do you understand the distinction?"

There was a microsecond of processing silence.

"The logic structure is sound, Doctor Colton," Aria confirmed, seamlessly accepting the administrative workaround. "I am prepared to record your pre-authorized macros."

"Good," Wyatt nodded, his shoulders squaring as he looked at the three holographic umbilical cords hovering over the bed. "Let's build Macro Alpha: Fetal Distress. If any of the three fetal heart rates drop below one hundred and ten beats per minute for longer than sixty seconds during the extraction phase, what is your automated response?"

"Under Macro Alpha," Aria recited smoothly, her robotic arms descending from the ceiling array to hover over the bio-bed in a flawless pantomime of the simulation, "I will immediately increase the localized ambient oxygen flow to the maternal mask by forty percent. I will deploy the automated, soft-tissue extraction forceps to assist in the immediate physical retrieval of the subject, and I will prepare a neonatal resuscitation station with exactly zero-point-one milligrams of synthesized epinephrine."

"Perfect," Wyatt said, his eyes tracking the flawless, rapid movements of the surgical armature. It was incredibly fast, far faster than his own heavy, calloused hands could ever be. "Log it."

"Macro Alpha logged and secured."

Wyatt moved to the foot of the bed, his mind mapping out the terrifying reality of the timeline. They weren't having one baby; they were having three. That meant three separate deliveries, three separate cords to cut, and three separate airways to clear, all within minutes of each other.

"Macro Beta: The Interval," Wyatt commanded, pointing to the three heated, transparent neonatal bassinets he had lined up against the far wall. "As soon as Baby A is successfully extracted and the cord is clamped, I need my hands completely free to assist Sarah with Baby B. I cannot stop to clear an airway."

"Under Macro Beta," Aria responded, "Once you have physically placed an infant onto the heated neonatal scale, I will assume immediate automated stabilization. My secondary armatures will clear the subject's airway using a low-pressure suction catheter, record their Apgar score based on raw biometric data, and initiate thermal swaddling. If the subject's oxygen saturation drops below ninety percent, I will autonomously trigger a positive-pressure ventilation sequence."

Wyatt watched the holographic simulation play out. The primary bio-bed hummed, and the secondary robotic arms smoothly transferred a glowing, simulated infant to the bassinet, immediately going to work clearing the airway while Wyatt's holographic avatar remained entirely focused on the mother.

It was a flawless, mechanical ballet of life-saving precision.

Wyatt let out a long, heavy exhale, dragging a hand down his bearded face. The S.H.O.P. Marine was entirely used to relying on his own physical strength to protect the people he loved, but right now, he had to trust the machine. The tolerances for a triplet birth were too tight. If he hesitated for even a fraction of a second, he could lose one of them.

"We're going to have three babies, Aria," Wyatt murmured, the staggering weight of the impending event settling heavily into his chest. He looked at the three empty bassinets. "And I don't care what the biometric data says. We are not losing a single one."

"Your pre-authorized macros are mathematically optimized for maximum survivability, Wyatt," Aria assured him, her tone warming slightly. "The medical wing is fully prepped. The Aegis grid is holding the planet secure. When Sarah's labor begins, we will simply execute the protocol."

Wyatt nodded slowly. He had built the cribs. He had locked the planetary shield. He had programmed the automated medical overrides.

He was as ready as a father could possibly be.

"Save the simulation matrix," Wyatt commanded, powering down the holotable. "Keep the bio-bed warmed up. It could be any day now."
