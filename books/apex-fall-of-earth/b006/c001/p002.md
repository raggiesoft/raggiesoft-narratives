# Part 2: The Silent Scout – Tuesday, September 5, 2045 – 14:00

Down on Sub-Level 3, the buried Apex dreadnought functioned as the ultimate, heavily armored nervous system for the entire mountain estate.

Wyatt sat in the center of the Combat Information Center (CIC), the primary tactical holotable illuminating his scarred face in a wash of cool blue light. He had spent the last two months aggressively hardening the planet's defense grid. The absurd visit from the tax collectors had been a wake-up call. The galaxy still knew these coordinates existed, and Wyatt refused to leave his pregnant wife and their three unborn children exposed.

He was in the middle of routing auxiliary power to the localized repulsor-shields when the CIC’s ambient lighting instantly snapped from blue to a harsh, flashing crimson.

A high-pitched, oscillating klaxon cut through the quiet hum of the servers.

"Alert, Administrator," Aria’s synthesized voice echoed sharply, completely devoid of her usual warmth. "Proximity sensors have detected an unauthorized orbital breach. A high-velocity, unmanned telemetry probe has just dropped out of sub-light transit. It is currently establishing a geosynchronous orbit directly above our hemisphere."

Wyatt’s S.H.O.P. Marine instincts flared instantly. He slammed his heavy hand onto the console, bringing up the optical telemetry.

Hovering on the screen was a sleek, matte-black satellite bristling with high-gain sensor arrays and deep-core scanning lasers. It had no identifying insignia, no national flags, and no municipal registry codes.

"Is it from the water-world?" Wyatt demanded, his jaw locking tight. "Did the tax collectors send a drone?"

"Negative, Wyatt," Aria reported, her data streams scrolling rapidly across the glass. "The metallurgical composition and propulsion signature do not match municipal government tech. This is a Tier-1 corporate mass-surveyor. It is currently executing a deep-core geological sweep. It is mapping the valley’s raw timber density, the freshwater aquifers, and the subterranean mineral deposits."

Wyatt’s blood went ice cold.

It wasn't a bureaucratic ledger algorithm. It was a corporate scout. Some massive, nameless conglomerate was dragging the outer sectors looking for fresh planets to strip-mine, harvest, and burn to ash, exactly like they had done to Earth.

"Not this planet," Wyatt growled, his deep voice vibrating with absolute, lethal authority. "Open all standard hailing frequencies. Wideband."

"Frequencies open and broadcasting, Wyatt."

Wyatt leaned over the console, staring at the glowing red icon of the probe.

"Unidentified surveyor," Wyatt barked into the comms, his voice ringing with the iron-clad authority of a planetary sovereign. "You are violating the restricted airspace of the independent world of Eden. Cease your geological scans immediately and withdraw from orbit, or you will be fired upon. Acknowledge."

He waited. The only sound in the CIC was the hum of the dreadnought’s reactors.

"The probe is receiving our transmission, Wyatt," Aria confirmed. "But it is ignoring the handshake protocol. It has increased its scanning resolution. It is currently attempting to ping the mountain's power grid."

They were looking for the source of the heat signature. They were looking for his home.

Wyatt didn't hesitate. He wasn't a diplomat, and he wasn't going to negotiate with a greedy algorithm designed to strip a planet bare. Up on the residential level, Sarah was resting in their bed, carrying the entire future of their bloodline.

"Warning phase is over," Wyatt stated, his dark eyes narrowing into a deadly glare. "Aria, cycle the dreadnought's primary dorsal plasma batteries. Route targeting control to my console."

"Plasma batteries online and charged to maximum yield. Targeting lock acquired," Aria confirmed instantly.

Wyatt looked at the tactical map. The probe was hovering exactly 400 kilometers above the surface, completely unaware that the "empty" rock it was scanning possessed a buried military warship.

"Get off my lawn," Wyatt whispered.

He punched the heavy, glowing red execution rune on the glass table.

Miles above the mountain, the camouflaged peak of The Sanctuary parted slightly. A massive, concentrated beam of superheated blue plasma erupted from the dreadnought’s concealed cannons. It tore through the atmosphere in a fraction of a second, perfectly striking the exact coordinates of the surveyor.

On the holotable, the red icon of the probe didn't even flash. It simply ceased to exist, vaporized into a cloud of harmless, molten slag that immediately began burning up in the upper thermosphere.

"Target neutralized," Aria reported calmly, the CIC lighting shifting smoothly from crimson back to a calm, secure blue. "Orbital airspace is clear."

Wyatt let out a slow, heavy exhale, leaning back in the command chair.

He had successfully swatted the fly, but the reality of the situation settled heavily onto his broad shoulders. If a corporate probe had found them, eventually, a corporate harvesting fleet might follow.

He looked at the planetary grid hovering above the table. He couldn't just rely on shooting them down; he needed to make sure they couldn't see the planet in the first place.

"Aria," Wyatt commanded, his mechanic's brain rapidly shifting from defense to stealth. "I need to completely mask our thermal and electromagnetic footprint. I want to pull the dreadnought's cloaking tech and expand it. We are going to build a planetary-scale dampening field."

"That will require a massive reallocation of the Fab-Foundry's resources and significant subterranean engineering, Wyatt," Aria calculated.

"I don't care," Wyatt replied, pushing himself out of the chair. "I'm not letting a megacorporation anywhere near my family. Lock down the grid, Aria. I have to go check on Sarah, and then I'm getting back to work."
