# Part 2: The Village Square – Monday, June 22, 2071 – 15:00 (Local Eden Time)

The transition from the hyper-militarized, polished-stone corridors of the mountain estate down to the sprawling, sunlit valley of Genesis Village was like stepping onto an entirely different planet.

Jack Colton walked down the wide, packed-dirt central thoroughfare, his broad shoulders relaxed as the warm afternoon breeze ruffled his dark hair. The village was a masterpiece of pioneer engineering and raw, vibrant life. Massive, beautifully constructed timber cabins lined the rushing river, their roofs fitted with sleek solar shingles that powered the town without emitting a single puff of smoke.

But the true energy of Genesis wasn't in the architecture; it was in the noise.

"Oi, Jack! Have a butchers at this!" an eight-year-old boy yelled from the village green, his sharp, rhythmic Cockney accent completely cutting through the ambient hum of the water-wheels. He was dangling upside down from a massive wooden jungle gym, much to the amusement of his cohort.

"Keep your grip tight, Arthur!" Jack called back with a melodic, effortless laugh, offering a friendly wave.

Walking on Jack's right side was Sloane. She wore her newly printed canvas clothes, stepping carefully over the roots and river stones. Outwardly, she wore a mask of wide-eyed, fragile awe, acting like a traumatized refugee entirely overwhelmed by the beauty of her sanctuary.

Inwardly, she was meticulously cataloging power dynamics.

She watched the way the villagers looked at Jack. The eight-year-old Cockney cohort and the thirteen-year-old Sydney cohort completely deferred to him. The automated heavy-lifter drones actively diverted their flight paths to give him a wide berth. Jack wasn't just a liaison; he was the undisputed prince of this valley. Securing him meant securing her absolute safety, a lifetime of luxury, and permanent immunity from the syndicate debt she had fled.

But there was a glaring, incredibly annoying obstacle standing in her way.

Walking on Jack's left side was Willow.

The thirteen-year-old girl was chatting animatedly about the hydroponic crop yields, her bright Australian vowels rolling musically in the air. What infuriated Sloane the most wasn't Willow's constant talking; it was the fact that Jack was holding her hand.

Jack's massive, calloused hand was wrapped entirely around Willow's, their fingers comfortably laced together. It was a completely natural, ingrained habit born from the very first day Willow was decanted. As they walked through the bustling town, Jack's thumb would absentmindedly stroke the back of Willow's hand, keeping her perfectly anchored.

Sloane stared at their joined hands, her jaw clenching so tightly her teeth ground together. *She's a child,* Sloane thought, pure venom burning in her chest. *He's a twenty-one-year-old man, a planetary leader, and he's walking around holding a teenager's hand like a glorified babysitter. She's holding him back.* Sloane wanted to swat the girl's hand away and physically insert herself into Jack's airspace. But she knew she was walking a razor-thin tightrope.

Back in the mountain, the Commander's dark, calculating eyes had stripped Sloane down to her core. Ellie Colton didn't believe the scavenger story for a second. If Sloane showed a single crack in her grateful-refugee facade, or if she made a move that threatened the fiercely protected Colton unit, Ellie would have her dragged right back to the Lunar Embassy and thrown off the planet.

Sloane forced her jaw to relax. She looked over at Jack, projecting a soft, sweet smile.

"It's so full of life down here, Jack," Sloane murmured, pitching her voice to sound breathless and amazed. "After drifting in the dark for so long... I didn't think places like this still existed."

"My parents built the foundation," Jack smiled, looking around the town square, "but the kids brought the life."

He gently squeezed Willow's hand. "Alright, wild sparrow. We need to get Sloane settled into Guest Cabin Four. Can you run ahead and make sure the solar array on the roof is perfectly calibrated? I think Tyler knocked one of the panels loose when he was clearing the gutters yesterday."

"Too right, Jack-Jack," Willow beamed, happily dropping his hand. She shot Sloane a polite, welcoming smile before sprinting off down the dirt path toward the residential sector.

Sloane internally rejoiced as the girl finally vanished. She immediately closed the distance, stepping flush into Jack's side as they walked the remaining fifty yards to a beautiful, freshly cleaned timber cabin sitting near the edge of the river.

Jack pushed the heavy wooden door open, gesturing for her to step inside.

The cabin was spacious and completely furnished. A plush, molecularly printed bed sat in the corner, and the small kitchenette was fully stocked with fresh water and synthesized rations. It was a rustic paradise compared to the cramped, failing scrap-runner she had crashed in.

"This is Guest Cabin Four," Jack explained, leaning against the doorframe. "You have your own localized climate control, fresh plumbing, and an open line to Aria if you need anything from the mountain."

Sloane stepped into the center of the room, wrapping her arms around herself. She turned to face him, letting her lower lip tremble just a fraction of an inch. She dropped her voice into a fragile, pleading whisper.

"It's beautiful, Jack. Truly," Sloane said, taking a slow step toward him. "But... it's so big. And I've been alone for so long. Every time I close my eyes, I just see the pirate cutter firing on my ship."

She reached out, gently resting her hands against the front of his canvas shirt. She looked up through her eyelashes, fully deploying the vulnerable charm that had kept her alive in the Outer Rim.

"Could you stay?" Sloane asked softly, letting her hands slide slightly up his chest. "Just for tonight? I don't want to be alone in the dark. I just need to know I'm safe."

Jack looked down at her. His deep, gravitational empathy immediately registered her fear. He hated the idea of her suffering from post-traumatic stress in an unfamiliar room.

But his boundaries regarding his family were absolute, forged in the iron-clad perimeter of the Colton estate.

Jack gently, but firmly, placed his hands over hers, stopping their upward movement, and slowly stepped back, breaking the physical contact.

"I'm sorry, Slow-anne," Jack said, his melodic voice incredibly kind, but entirely uncompromising. "I can't stay here. I don't live in the guest sector."

Sloane blinked, entirely taken aback by the rejection. "You don't?"

"No," Jack smiled, gesturing back up the path toward the center of the village. "I live in the Main Residence with Molly, Claire, and Willow. We share the house. We always have. If I don't go back, it disrupts the unit, and I promised them I'd be home for dinner."

Sloane's blood ran completely cold. *He lives with them.* He was a grown man, and he was still treating his teenage sisters and that obnoxious Australian stray like they were his primary orbit.

A flash of genuine, toxic fury spiked behind Sloane's eyes. She wanted to scream at him to grow up. She wanted to demand that he prioritize a real woman over his childish attachments.

But up on the mountain, she knew the Commander was watching the optical feeds. Ellie was just waiting for a reason to declare her a threat.

Sloane swallowed the bile rising in her throat. She instantly completely buried the anger, replacing it with a flawless mask of understanding and sweet resignation.

"Of course, Jack," Sloane murmured, taking another step back and offering a brave, self-deprecating little laugh. "I'm sorry. I shouldn't have asked. You have your family to take care of. I'm just... I'm just being silly. I'll be fine."

Jack’s face softened with relief, glad she understood. "You're not being silly, Sloane. You've been through hell. But you are completely safe here. The perimeter is locked. No one can hurt you."

"I know," Sloane smiled softly. "Thank you, Jack. For everything."

"Get some rest," Jack instructed gently. "I'll come check on you in the morning."

Jack offered a warm wave, pulling the heavy timber door shut behind him.

The moment the latch clicked into place, the sweet, vulnerable smile completely vanished from Sloane's face. Her features hardened into a mask of cold, predatory calculation. She walked over to the window, watching Jack's broad, athletic figure jog back up the path to rejoin his precious unit.

*Fine,* Sloane thought, her dark eyes narrowing as she watched him go. *If you won't leave the Main Residence for me... I'll just have to make sure you eventually realize that you don't need them anymore.* She turned away from the glass, beginning to unpack the small canvas bag Aria had provided, ready to play the absolute perfect, long-term game.
