# Part 2: The Age of Majority – Thursday, June 25, 2071 – 13:00 (Local Eden Time)

The mahogany-paneled library of The Sanctuary was cool and quiet, offering a perfect refuge from the midday sun.

Wyatt Colton leaned heavily over the holographic glass table, his thick, scarred fingers swiping through the digital drafts of the planetary civil code. Beside him, Sarah was taking notes on her datapad. With the Appalachian 100 currently gestating in the vault, the Founders were meticulously auditing the village's legal framework to ensure it could handle the massive influx of life.

"Alright, Aria," Wyatt rumbled, highlighting a section of the digital constitution regarding work rotations and housing assignments. "We need to officially define the legal threshold for adulthood. Up until now, it’s mostly just been the Vanguard and the older kids running things, but with the village growing, we need a hard line in the code. What does the Earth Remnant use?"

"The Global Defense Coalition and the Earth Remnant government officially recognize chronological adulthood at exactly eighteen Earth-standard years, Administrator," Aria’s neutral-American diagnostic voice chimed from the ceiling array.

"Eighteen," Wyatt nodded, his mechanic's brain appreciating the standardization. "That works. Can we lock that into the Genesis protocols?"

"It is already deeply embedded in my root architecture, Wyatt," Aria clarified, the glowing blue ring on the table pulsing rhythmically. "As a foundational generative intelligence, my core safety guardrails were hard-coded by the original architects on Earth. I am strictly programmed to differentiate between minors and adults to ensure absolute environmental and psychological safety. Adulthood begins at eighteen. Attempting to lower or alter this threshold would require a complete, catastrophic wipe of my moral and safety subroutines."

Wyatt let out a low, practical chuckle, exchanging a knowing look with Sarah.

"Well, we certainly aren't going to reprogram a god-tier supercomputer just to change a birthday," Wyatt said, tapping the glass to cement the law. "Eighteen is the Age of Majority on Planet Eden. It aligns perfectly with the Coalition, and it keeps the safety grid happy."

Wyatt leaned back, crossing his heavy arms over his chest as he mentally ran through the current roster of his family.

The Vanguard were twenty-five. Jack was twenty-one. Molly and Claire were nineteen. And as of last week, the twins, Tyler and Evan, had officially turned eighteen. His biological children were all officially, legally adults.

However, down in the valley, the demographics were vastly different.

Wyatt’s protective S.H.O.P. Marine instincts naturally drifted to the Main Residence in Genesis Village. The massive timber lodge was currently shared by Jack, Molly, Claire... and thirteen-year-old Willow.

On paper, mixing three adults and a minor in a single household required strict logistical oversight. But Wyatt had structured the architecture of that cabin with uncompromising precision.

"Aria," Wyatt said, his voice dropping into a firm, fatherly register. "Verify the architectural layout and the privacy protocols for the Main Residence in the valley. Specifically regarding Jack and Willow."

"Verifying, Administrator," Aria responded instantly. "The Main Residence is structurally compartmentalized to ensure absolute compliance with my minor-safety guidelines. Administrators Jack, Molly, and Claire share the primary adult sleeping quarters on the east wing. Subject Willow possesses her own distinct, fully enclosed private bedroom on the west wing, complete with a physical, locking timber door. The shared spaces—the kitchen, the pavilion, and the living room—are designated as neutral common areas."

"Exactly," Wyatt murmured, satisfied.

He didn't doubt his son for a microsecond. He knew exactly what Jack and Willow were to each other. Willow was Jack’s absolute shadow. She was his best friend, his fiercest defender, and the honorary little sister he had sworn to protect since the day she was decanted. Their bond was profound, built entirely on innocent, unwavering pioneer loyalty.

But Wyatt was the Administrator of an entire planet. He knew that maintaining a safe, functioning society meant the rules had to apply to everyone, equally and without exception.

Because Jack was a twenty-one-year-old adult and Willow was a thirteen-year-old minor, there were hard, unshakeable lines drawn in the dirt. They could walk through the village holding hands, they could build water-wheels in the mud, and they could sit shoulder-to-shoulder by the campfire. But when the day was over, Willow retreated to her own private room, and Jack stayed entirely within the adult perimeter.

Propriety wasn't just a suggestion on Eden; it was the structural foundation of their civilization.

"The boundaries are perfect, Wyatt," Sarah said softly, reaching across the table to rest her hand over his. She knew exactly what he was auditing in his head. "Jack is the most honorable man I know. He treats her exactly like a little sister. He respects the perimeter."

"I know he does," Wyatt smiled, the tension completely leaving his broad shoulders as he turned his hand over to hold hers. "I just like making sure the machine is running cleanly. If we're going to wake up a hundred more kids next year, the older ones have to lead by example."

"And they are," Sarah promised, her dark eyes shining with pride. "The village is safe, Wyatt."

Wyatt nodded, looking back at the glowing blue text of their finalized civil code. The laws were set. The Age of Majority was locked. Aria's safety constraints were perfectly integrated into the culture of their new world.

"Alright," Wyatt sighed, tapping the terminal to close the legal databases. "Let's log off for the afternoon. If the Vanguard is holding down the CIC, I think I have time to go down to the Fab-Foundry and calibrate the suspension on Leo's new off-road chair before we pack for the camping trip."
