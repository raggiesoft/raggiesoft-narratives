# Part 4: The Stewards – Thursday, June 25, 2071 – 11:00 (Local Eden Time)

The Combat Information Center was quiet, save for the low, 40-hertz hum of the geothermal reactors and the steady pulsing of the massive holographic table.

On the glass, the topographical map of the Shenandoah Valley had faded, replaced by the live, real-time telemetry of Planet Eden. Deep on Sub-Level 6, the automated systems were already adjusting the nutrient flows for the one hundred newly active artificial wombs.

Twenty-five-year-old Ellie Colton stared at the glowing blue data streams.

Her dark eyes traced the numbers. They had just authorized the creation of one hundred human lives with a single push of a button. In less than a year, the population of Genesis Village would more than double. And sitting in the ice beneath their boots were forty-nine thousand more.

Ellie gripped the edge of the glass table, her knuckles turning white. The sheer, terrifying magnitude of the power they held in this room suddenly pressed down on her shoulders like a physical weight.

She thought about High Director Kael. She thought about the way the Apex Megacorporation had looked at the Earth and seen only resources to be extracted and inventory to be managed.

"It's a lot of mass," Ellie said quietly, her voice unusually tight. She didn't look away from the numbers. "We hold the keys to fifty thousand lives. We control the weather deflector. We control the food supply. We decide who wakes up and what they sound like."

She looked up, her dark eyes finding Leo in his powerchair, and Nora standing faithfully by his side.

"Do you know how easy it would be for someone to sit in this room and forget what we actually are?" Ellie asked, her voice dropping into a fierce, gravelly whisper that sounded exactly like Wyatt Colton. "To look down at that valley and start acting like a dictator? To start thinking we own them?"

Nora’s breath hitched. Her deeply empathetic heart instantly recognized the moral terror gripping her sister. Nora leaned heavily on Leo’s push-handles, taking a slow, focused breath to navigate the physical friction of her vocal cords.

"We... never," Nora breathed out, the two syllables fierce and absolute.

She lifted her left hand, tapping her forearm AAC to finish the thought without exhausting herself. *"We are not Apex, Ellie. We do not rule them. We are just keeping the rain off their heads."*

Leo let out a deep, vibrating baritone hum. His scissored legs remained perfectly relaxed against his footboards, completely undisturbed by the philosophical weight of the conversation. To Leo, the math was incredibly simple. The geometry of their existence had already been perfectly defined by their father.

He reached down to his customized MagnaPad, his heavy, spasming hand striking the keys with deliberate precision.

"A throne is a structural liability. It requires constant defense against the people beneath it," Leo’s synthesized voice announced, the flawless, cold logic cutting right through Ellie's anxiety. "The mountain is not a throne. It is a foundational pillar. We are the load-bearing struts. We stand beneath the civilization to hold it up. We do not sit on top of it."

Ellie stared at her brother.

The rigid tension completely drained from her neck and shoulders. She let go of the glass table, letting out a long, shuddering exhale.

Leo was exactly right. They weren't gods, and they weren't kings. They were the Vanguard. In military doctrine, the vanguard didn't sit safely in the rear giving orders; the vanguard stood at the absolute front line, taking the heaviest hits so the people behind them could live in peace.

They were the mechanics. They were the stewards of the perimeter.

Ellie uncrossed her arms. She walked around the edge of the holotable, stepping into Leo’s airspace. She placed her hand firmly over Nora’s where it rested on Leo’s shoulder, completing the physical circuit of the unit.

"Load-bearing struts," Ellie repeated, a profound, fiercely proud smile finally touching her lips. "I like that. We carry the weight."

Leo let out a loud, rumbling purr of approval, leaning his head back against Nora's arm. He looked at the glowing blue hologram of the village, watching the tiny, thermal signatures of the children currently playing in the sun.

"Aria," Leo typed, locking the administrative protocol into the mountain's permanent archives. "Log a primary directive for the CIC. The Colton family holds the keys to the vault, but we do not own the people inside it. We are the maintenance crew. The planet belongs to them."

"Directive logged and permanently secured in the foundational root-code, Overseer," Aria’s neutral-American voice chimed respectfully from the ceiling array.

Ellie gave a single, satisfied nod. The moral perimeter was locked.

"Alright," Ellie said, stepping back into her tactical stance, her mind shifting smoothly from philosophy back to logistics. "We have one hundred Appalachian farmers waking up in nine months. Jack and the construction drones are going to need to break ground on a massive housing expansion in the southern quadrant of the valley. Overseer, I need the soil density reports and the architectural blueprints for a hundred-person footprint."

Leo’s hands flew across his MagnaPad, entirely in his element. He didn't want to be worshipped. He just wanted to do the math, keep his sisters close, and make sure the machine ran perfectly.
