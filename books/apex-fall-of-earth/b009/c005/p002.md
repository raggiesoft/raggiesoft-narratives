# Part 2: The Kinetic Variables – Thursday, June 25, 2071 – 10:30 (Local Eden Time)

The heavy brushed-steel doors of the pneumatic elevator on Sub-Level 3 hissed open.

Leo did not turn his head to look. He didn't need to. He processed the arrival entirely through the floorboards.

*Thud. Thud. Thud.*

The vibrations transmitting through the grated steel deck were perfectly symmetrical, striking the metal with identical, immense kinetic force at exactly one hundred and ten steps per minute. The harmonic resonance was flawless. There were only two biological assets on the entire planet capable of generating that specific, mirrored physical impact.

The Enforcers had arrived.

Eighteen-year-old identical twins Tyler and Evan Colton stepped into the Combat Information Center. They were staggering physical specimens, having inherited the absolute maximum potential of Wyatt's deep-sea welder genetics. They stood six-foot-four, their shoulders impossibly broad beneath their heavy canvas work shirts.

To anyone else, the twins were intimidating. To Leo, they were deeply soothing geometric constants.

Tyler and Evan approached the holotable, but their heavy boots stopped exactly two meters away. They inherently understood the physics of Leo's perimeter. They knew their massive size displaced too much air and created too much kinetic tension if they stood too close. They respected the boundary without ever needing to be told.

"Commander. Overseer," Tyler greeted, his voice a deep, rumbling bass that resonated pleasantly in Leo's chest. "We are prepping the automated harvesters for the eastern agricultural expansion."

"The ground is soft near Sector 4," Evan added, his posture an exact mirror of his brother's. "We came to request the updated topographical soil-density reports before we deploy the heavy machinery."

Ellie, standing at Leo's left shoulder, gave them a firm nod. She tapped the edge of the glass table to wake the terminal, but she didn't access the data herself. She deferred entirely to the core.

"Overseer," Ellie said respectfully, looking down at him. "Do we have the soil metrics?"

Leo’s scissored legs remained perfectly relaxed against his footboards, anchored securely by Nora's gentle weight on his right. He looked at his two massive younger brothers. He liked Tyler and Evan. They were loud in the valley, but up here, they were predictable. They followed the rules.

Leo reached his heavy, shaking hand down to his lap tray. With Nora subtly steadying his wrist, his fingers danced across the customized, high-contrast interface of his MagnaPad.

He didn't just pull up a map. He executed a complex, multi-layered geological algorithm he had spent the last week writing in the quiet of the mountain.

The massive holographic globe above the table shifted, zooming in on the eastern agricultural sectors. The terrain illuminated in a brilliant, color-coded topological web.

"The standard deployment route is compromised," Leo's deep, synthesized voice announced into the CIC. "Soil moisture content in Sector 4 is currently at forty-two percent due to recent precipitation. If you drive the Mark IV Harvesters over grid coordinates 88-Alpha, the mass-to-surface-area ratio will cause the treads to sink exactly 14.5 centimeters. You will become stuck."

Tyler crossed his massive arms, studying the glowing blue and red holographic map. "A mud trap."

"Correct," Leo typed. The hologram animated, tracing a new, glowing green path that perfectly skirted the red zones. "I have calculated a revised vector. By shifting your approach vector fifteen degrees to the north, you will engage the underlying bedrock shelf. Traction will remain at ninety-eight percent efficiency."

Evan leaned forward, his dark eyes tracing the new route. A wide, highly impressed grin broke across his face.

"That is a flawless route, Leo," Evan praised, looking directly at his older brother. "It saves us at least two hours of digging drones out of the mud. Thank you."

Leo let out a soft, vibrating hum of satisfaction. He didn't require long, emotional thank-yous. He required the data to be respected.

Beside him, Nora shifted her weight slightly against his chair. She looked at her younger brothers, her highly empathetic heart warmed by their caution and respect for Leo's intellect.

"S-smart... boys," Nora whispered. The two syllables were incredibly breathy, demanding a deliberate, exhausting push from her vocal cords. To Leo, her vocal friction was a familiar, comforting acoustic anchor, a sign of her direct emotional affection.

But for the mechanical realities of the conversation, she conserved her energy. She lifted her left hand, tapping the sleek AAC device strapped to her forearm.

*"The clay density on the original route would have stripped the harvester's primary gears,"* Nora's digital voice chimed smoothly, flawlessly completing her thought with the technical data. *"You saved the colony significant repair time by checking the telemetry before rolling out."*

"Route downloaded to the transport rover," Tyler confirmed, tapping his comm-cuff to sync the data before looking at Ellie. "We are moving out, Commander. Perimeter sweep of the eastern ridge while we're out there?"

"Standard visual sweep," Ellie authorized, returning her hands to her tactical vest. "If you see anything anomalous, do not engage. Report to the CIC."

"Understood," the twins replied in perfect unison.

They turned on their heels, their heavy boots hitting the deck plates in that exact, soothingly symmetrical rhythm, and disappeared back into the pneumatic elevator.

Leo watched the elevator doors seal. The CIC returned to its perfect 40-hertz geothermal hum. He was severely physically disabled, entirely non-verbal, and reliant on his sisters for his daily care. But as he looked at the glowing green path he had just drawn for the two strongest men on the planet, he felt the absolute, unshakeable truth of his existence.

He wasn't a burden to the Colton family. He was the brain that kept the entire machine running.
