# Part 2: The Diplomat's Detail – Thursday, May 23, 2052 – 09:30

Three days later, the residential living room was operating at peak morning efficiency.

The heavy reclaimed-wood coffee table had been pushed aside to make room for a sprawling, molecularly printed padded mat. The massive hydro-glass windows let in the bright, warm spring sunlight, illuminating the heart of the Colton family.

Wyatt and Sarah sat on the oversized leather sofa, mugs of hot coffee in hand, completely content to simply watch the logistics of their six children unfold.

On the far side of the room, the Vanguard was stationed in their standard formation. Six-and-a-half-year-old Leo sat on the plush rug, his back straight, humming a steady, rhythmic note as he meticulously lined up a set of heavy wooden blocks. Nora was sitting flush against his right side, her shoulder pressing firmly against his to provide the deep, grounding pressure his nervous system required. Ellie stood at attention behind them, her arms crossed over her canvas overalls, perfectly content that her primary charges were secure.

But the center of the room belonged to the new recruits.

Molly and Claire were lying side-by-side on the padded floor mat. They were perfectly swaddled, their tiny eyes blinking against the ambient light, fussing softly as they adjusted to the vastness of the living room.

Sitting cross-legged on the mat, mere inches from the twins' heads, was two-and-a-half-year-old Jack.

Jack didn't share Ellie's rigid, tactical approach to siblings. He was the diplomat. He possessed an advanced, naturally gravitational empathy that completely defied his age.

"Hi, Molly. Hi, Claire," Jack cooed, his voice a soft, melodic, and incredibly clear whisper. He leaned forward, keeping his hands respectfully to himself, but positioning his face so they could easily focus on him. "I'm Jack. I'm your big brother."

On the sofa, Wyatt leaned forward, resting his forearms on his knees. He watched closely as the twins reacted to the sound of Jack's voice.

Molly, who had been letting out a series of short, unhappy grunts, instantly quieted. She slowly rolled her tiny head to the left, her unfocused newborn eyes locking onto the blurry shape of Jack's face. She let out a soft, contented sigh.

Beside her, Claire mirrored the movement perfectly. She turned her head to the right, leaning into the space between her and her sister, her gaze also drifting directly toward the sound of Jack's steady, comforting voice.

Both girls blindly reached their tiny, uncoordinated hands out from beneath their swaddles. They didn't reach for the ceiling. They reached forward, their tiny fingers splaying open, stretching directly toward Jack.

Jack’s face lit up with a brilliant, gap-toothed smile. Very carefully, moving with the gentle precision of someone much older, he reached out his own small hands. He let Molly’s tiny fingers wrap around his left index finger, and Claire’s tiny fingers wrap around his right.

The moment they physically anchored to Jack, both twins let out simultaneous, synchronized sighs of absolute peace. Their eyes fluttered shut, and they drifted seamlessly to sleep.

Sarah’s breath hitched. She reached across the sofa cushions, gripping Wyatt's heavy forearm.

"Look at them, Wyatt," Sarah whispered, her dark eyes shining with absolute awe as she looked from the sleeping twins to Jack, and then over to where Ellie and Nora were anchoring Leo.

"The exact same dynamic," Wyatt breathed, a profound, deeply emotional realization washing over his mechanic's brain.

The generational layers of their family had just perfectly locked into place. Ellie and Nora were the impenetrable shield wall that protected Leo from a universe he couldn't process. And now, Jack had naturally stepped into the role of the core for the twins. Molly and Claire didn't need a tactical commander; they needed a steady, unifying diplomat.

Jack looked up at his parents, his hands held perfectly still by his two new sisters. He beamed with fierce, protective pride. "They're holding me, Daddy. They're safe."

"They're very safe, Jack," Wyatt smiled, leaning back into the sofa with a massive, swelling pride. "You're a great big brother."
