# Part 6: The Handover – Friday, May 5, 2051 – 14:00 (Standard Galactic Time)

Three days after the narrow-beam transmission pierced the vacuum of space, the sky above Planet Eden finally parted.

Standing on the edge of the massive, sunlit silicate plateau, Wyatt and Sarah Colton watched as a perfectly hexagonal iris spiraled open in the invisible hard-light barrier of the Aegis shield.

Descending through the opening was a massive, blocky vessel. It wasn't painted in the abrasive black and hazard-yellow of Apex Defense Solutions. Its hull was a pristine, military gray, proudly bearing the unified, blue-and-white insignia of the Global Defense Coalition and the Earth Remnant.

"They're on time," Wyatt noted, his heavy boots planted firmly on the bedrock. He was dressed in his canvas work pants and a black t-shirt, his arms crossed over his chest.

"Aria, maintain target-lock with the surface-to-space batteries until the handshake is physically verified," Sarah commanded, standing securely at his side. She wasn't taking any chances with her planet's security, even with allies.

*"Batteries are locked and tracking, Sarah,"* Aria confirmed from Wyatt’s comm-link. *"The transport is cleared for final approach."*

The GDC prisoner transport hovered over the plateau before its heavy repulsorlifts set it down gently onto the rock, less than fifty yards from the camouflaged rock face that hid *The Nomad* and the primary hangar.

The ship’s heavy rear ramp hissed and lowered, hitting the stone with a solid *clank*.

A squad of heavily armed Earth Remnant Marines marched down the ramp, their weapons resting at the low ready. They were led by an older, battle-hardened officer wearing the silver bars of a Coalition Commander. He stopped at the bottom of the ramp, his eyes scanning the completely pristine, unblemished alien pine forests, and then looking up at the invisible shield above them. He looked entirely bewildered.

Wyatt stepped forward, closing the distance.

"Commander," Wyatt rumbled, his deep voice projecting easily across the plateau. "Welcome to Eden."

The Commander looked at the towering, heavily scarred former S.H.O.P. Marine, and then at Sarah. "Administrator Colton. I’m Commander Vance. I have to admit, when we received your encrypted burst, Alliance Command thought it was a trap. Your coordinates are listed as a radioactive, shattered husk."

"Camouflage," Wyatt replied simply. He tapped his comm-cuff. "Bring them out."

From the edge of the tree line, the squad of silent, hovering Sentinel drones emerged. Suspended in their localized magnetic grapple fields were the surviving members of the *Tartarus* bridge crew, including the sub-commander.

Leading the procession was High Director Kael.

The Sovereign of Acheron looked completely broken. His uniform was rumpled, his arm was secured in a medical splint, and his face was pale. The absolute isolation of a clean, warm, Geneva-compliant holding cell—devoid of torture, interrogation, or any opportunity to manipulate his captors—had completely unraveled his imperial mind.

As the drones deposited the prisoners at the base of the GDC ramp, Kael looked up. He saw the Earth Remnant insignia on the Marines' armor. His eyes narrowed, a desperate, feral flash of his old arrogance flaring up.

"You fools," Kael spat, glaring at Wyatt and Commander Vance. "You think you've won? You think putting me in chains ends the megacorporation? The second my locator beacon drops off the grid, my loyalist fleets will swarm this sector. They will burn the Earth Remnant to ash, and they will glass this mountain!"

Commander Vance didn't flinch. He didn't even reach for his sidearm. He just looked at the High Director and let out a dry, exhausted laugh.

"What fleets, Kael?" Vance asked, his voice dripping with absolute contempt.

Kael froze. "The armada of Acheron—"

"Acheron surrendered yesterday," Vance interrupted, stepping forward so he was mere inches from the dictator's face. "The second Administrator Colton's transmission hit our staging grounds, we broadcast the telemetry of the *Tartarus* being vaporized to the entire Cygnus Arm. We showed your empire exactly what happened to their invincible High Director."

Kael’s jaw trembled, the blood draining completely from his face.

"Your 'loyalists' were just terrified slaves, Kael," Vance told him coldly. "Without you holding the detonator, the corporate command structure collapsed in less than twelve hours. The mercenary fleets laid down their arms. The S.H.O.P. infantry regiments mutinied. Apex Defense Solutions is dead. We've already dispatched humanitarian fleets to shut down the deep-crust labor camps."

The dictator's knees buckled. He wasn't a martyr, and he wasn't a catalyst for a galactic war. He was just a defeated, powerless man who had outlived his own terrifying masquerade.

"Load them up," Vance ordered his Marines.

The Earth Remnant soldiers moved in, securing the prisoners in heavy stasis-cuffs and marching them up the ramp into the transport's reinforced holding block.

Vance turned back to Wyatt and Sarah, his expression softening into one of profound, deeply ingrained respect.

"We've been hunting him for six years," Vance said, shaking his head. "He cracked our planet to the mantle, and we couldn't even scratch his armor. And you two... you just sat on a mountain and swatted him like a bug."

"He knocked on the wrong door," Wyatt said, his arm wrapping around Sarah’s waist.

"Commander," Sarah spoke up, her dark eyes entirely focused on the legal reality of what came next. "Kael destroyed Earth to hide his drilling operation in Sector 4. Wyatt and I were in the Operations Center of *The Nomad* when his flagship dropped the kinetic rod. We possess the raw, unencrypted telemetry of the mantle fracture, and we possess the audio logs of him admitting to the strike."

Vance’s eyes widened. "You have the telemetry?"

"We have everything," Sarah confirmed fiercely. "When you convene the tribunal, you are not going to lack evidence."

"Mrs. Colton, the Coalition Tribunal is going to need you to testify," Vance said, a note of absolute urgency in his voice. "You are the only living witnesses to the origin of the anomaly. You are our star witnesses. But..." He looked around at the pristine, untouched beauty of Eden. "I understand if you refuse to leave your home."

Wyatt and Sarah exchanged a warm, knowing smile. They had built a multi-billion-credit digital infrastructure. They didn't need to get on a ship to be in a courtroom.

"We aren't leaving our kids, Commander," Wyatt rumbled, looking back at the camouflaged rock face where the Vanguard was safely playing in the living room. "But if you provide Aria with the Earth Remnant's secure subspace frequency, we will gladly establish a quantum-holographic link. Sarah and I will testify from our living room. We'll give you everything you need to bury him under the prison."

Vance let out a long, deeply relieved exhale. He reached out, offering his hand.

"The human race owes you a debt we can never repay, Administrators," Vance said sincerely.

Wyatt took the Commander's hand, his heavy, calloused grip sealing the pact. "Just keep the galaxy on your side of the shield, Commander, and we'll call it even."

Vance saluted them both, turning on his heel and marching up the ramp. The heavy doors of the transport sealed shut, and the repulsorlifts hummed to life.

Wyatt and Sarah watched as the GDC vessel lifted off the plateau, banking sharply upward and accelerating into the sky. It passed seamlessly through the hexagonal iris in the Aegis grid. The moment the ship's thrusters cleared the barrier, the glowing blue hard-light shield snapped shut behind them, sealing the planet completely.

Wyatt let out a deep, chest-rattling sigh, pulling Sarah flush against his side. He looked up at the completely unbroken, perfect blue sky.

The hunt was over. The corporation was dead. The monsters were in chains.

"Well," Wyatt murmured, looking down at his beautiful wife. "I think that officially concludes the business of the day. What do you say we go back inside? I believe Ellie has some intense questions about why I cut the audio feed to the basement."

Sarah laughed, a bright, uninhibited sound that echoed freely across the empty plateau. "You're definitely going to have to answer to the Commander for that one."

They turned their backs on the sky, walking hand-in-hand toward the camouflaged entrance of their mountain fortress. The universe was finally safe, and it was time to go back to being a family.
