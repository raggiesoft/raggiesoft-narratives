# Part 3: The Extraction (2:58 PM)

The digital clock on the terminal wall clicked to 2:58 PM when the heavy glass automatic doors slid open, letting in a rush of cold November air.

Nate saw them instantly. Evelyn and Sarah Mercer pushed through the crowd, their eyes scanning the chaotic waiting area with laser focus. The moment Sarah spotted the wheelchair, she broke into a run.

"Nate!" Sarah called out, completely ignoring the sea of strangers around them.

She reached his chair and didn't hesitate. Sarah threw her arms around him, pulling him into a fierce, grounding hug. Nate wrapped his arms around his sister, leaning into the deep-pressure embrace that instantly silenced the blaring alarms in his nervous system. For the first time all day, he could breathe.

Valerie immediately lost her mind.

"Get off of him!" she shrieked, making a massive scene that caused half the terminal to turn and stare. She stepped toward them, her face flushed with fury, the physical ache in her lower back making her voice even more jagged. "You are pathetic! Both of you! He is supposed to be focusing on me!"

Nate didn't flinch. Safely anchored in his sister's arms, he looked straight into Sarah's eyes. The terror of the last few hours was gone, replaced by absolute clarity. "I want to go home right now, Sarah."

"I know," Sarah whispered fiercely. "We've got you."

Evelyn stepped up beside the wheelchair, placing a warm, steadying hand on her son's shoulder. She looked him directly in the eye, her voice cutting through Valerie's hysterical screaming with calm, maternal authority. "You are coming back home, Nate. You are absolutely welcome back home."

"He can't go home!" Valerie yelled, waving a crumpled envelope in Evelyn's face. She was desperate, feeling the control slipping through her fingers. She decided to deploy her ultimate weapon against the two women. "I bought non-refundable tickets to Los Angeles! We are leaving in thirty minutes! He has to come with me—I'm pregnant! He is not abandoning his child!"

Evelyn and Sarah didn't gasp. They didn't look at Nate with betrayal or panic. They simply turned their attention to Valerie, their expressions hardening into unadulterated, icy disgust. They looked at the girl who had spent months systematically isolating and abusing Nate, and they completely stripped her of her remaining power.

"Azoospermia, Valerie," Evelyn said, her tone dropping to an absolute, chilling zero. "We have had the medical records since he was fourteen years old. He cannot father a child. So either you are pregnant by someone else, or you are standing in the middle of a bus terminal lying to my face to trap my son."

Valerie’s jaw dropped. The venom died in her throat, replaced by genuine shock. Her eyes darted between Evelyn, Sarah, and Nate. The trap hadn't just failed; it had been atomized.

But abusers do not surrender gracefully when their reality is shattered. Valerie’s shock instantly mutated into a full-blown, hysterical tantrum.

"You're ruining his life!" she screamed, her voice reaching a shrill, piercing pitch that echoed off the high ceilings of the terminal. She pointed a shaking finger at Sarah. "You suffocate him! You treat him like a baby! He's MINE! You can't just take him away from me!"

She kept screaming, hurling insults and venom, her volume escalating with every word. For Nate, the sheer acoustics of her rage, combined with the heavy diesel fumes, the staring crowd, and the agonizing stress of the entire day, finally breached his limits.

His neurodivergent mind simply short-circuited. The sensory overload hit him like a physical blow. Nate squeezed his eyes shut, his breathing hitching violently. He brought his hands up to cover his ears, trying to block out the agonizing frequency of her voice, and broke down. Tears spilled hot and fast over his cheeks as he sobbed, completely overwhelmed by the auditory and emotional assault.

Sarah saw her brother breaking. The protective fury in her eyes was lethal, but her first priority was the extraction. She stepped seamlessly behind Nate's chair and gripped the push-handles.

Evelyn stepped between the wheelchair and Valerie, forming an impenetrable physical barrier. "You can go to Los Angeles for all we care," Evelyn stated over the screaming. "Or you can go to Washington, D.C. But Nate is going back to Virginia Beach. We are done here."

Nate took a ragged, sobbing breath. He dropped one hand from his ear just long enough to look at Valerie. He didn't see a girlfriend anymore. He just saw a broken, cruel person who had built a cage around him.

"I am not your boyfriend anymore," Nate choked out through his tears, desperate to sever the final cord. "We're done."

Sarah didn't wait for Valerie to recover her breath. "Alright," she said softly to her brother, tilting him back just a fraction to clear a crack in the linoleum. "Let's roll out."

They turned their backs on Valerie, leaving her standing entirely alone in the middle of the crowded Pathfinder terminal, screaming into the void with two useless, non-refundable tickets to the West Coast. Sarah pushed the wheelchair through the sliding glass doors and out into the crisp, quiet autumn air, steering her crying brother directly toward the safety of the family car waiting at the curb.

The perimeter was secured. The fortress had held. He was finally going home.
