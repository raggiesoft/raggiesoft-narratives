# Part 1: Joint and Several – 10:15 AM

The wall-mounted telephone in the kitchen rang, shattering the mid-morning quiet of the house.

Abbey, who was at the counter prepping a massive caloric meal for herself and Sarah, wiped her hands on a towel and pulled the receiver from the cradle.

"Whitmore residence. Abigail speaking."

"Hi, Abigail," a bright, heavily polished voice chirped through the line. "My name is Linda Vance, I’m one of the transition coordinators for the Math and Science Academy over at Ocean Lakes High School. I’m calling regarding Tobias Mercer’s Intent to Enroll paperwork. We received it in the central office this morning, and it looks like the 'NO' box was checked by mistake. I just wanted to get that cleared up so we can get his roster spot finalized!"

Abbey’s expression did not change. She leaned her broad shoulder against the doorframe. "It was not a mistake, Ms. Vance. The paperwork is accurate. Toby will be attending Northwood High School."

There was a brief pause on the line. The chipper cadence faltered. "Oh. Well, I understand Northwood is a fine community school, but Toby’s test scores are truly exceptional. He is the first student with his... specific physical profile to qualify for the Academy. Is your grandmother available? I really should be speaking with Eleanor, as she is the primary guardian on file."

Abbey’s jaw tightened. "My grandmother is unavailable. However, my sister Sarah and I hold joint and several guardianship over Toby, alongside Eleanor. That means I possess the full, unilateral legal authority to make educational and medical decisions on his behalf. You are speaking to the correct person. The answer is no."

The coordinator let out a nervous, breathy laugh, clearly unaccustomed to being stonewalled by a twenty-one-year-old. She quickly pivoted to the standard sales script.

"I completely understand, Abigail. Sometimes the idea of a magnet program can feel overwhelming for a family. That’s exactly why I called. We would love to offer Toby a personalized, after-hours VIP tour of the Ocean Lakes campus. Just him, your family, and our department heads. We want to show him the robotics bay and the accessible laboratory stations. Once he sees the facility, I know he'll feel right at home."

"Toby requires a highly structured environment and the constant presence of his older sister, Samantha, for his physical and emotional safety," Abbey replied, her voice remaining perfectly level and entirely devoid of warmth. "Unless the district intends to admit Samantha to the Academy and provide dedicated, synchronized transportation for both of them, a tour is a waste of our time and yours."

"Well, Samantha is a rising junior, we can't exactly bend the zoning laws for a sibling," Linda Vance countered, a hint of defensive frustration creeping into her tone. "Abigail, we are talking about university-level academics. Toby is a gifted young man. It would be a tragedy to let his physical limitations keep him from reaching his full potential just because he's attached to his sister."

The air in the kitchen seemed to drop ten degrees.

"His potential," Abbey said softly, the terrifying, extrajudicial restraint she carried bleeding directly into the phone line, "is none of your concern. His safety is our absolute priority. We do not value your academic prestige over his central nervous system. You have our legally binding response. Do not call this number again."

Abbey placed the receiver back on the cradle with a soft, definitive click. She turned back to the kitchen counter, completely unbothered, and resumed prepping her meal.
