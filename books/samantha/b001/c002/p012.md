# Part 12: The Medical Perimeter – 8:30 PM

The shift in gravity and the soft mound of pillows had bought me a temporary reprieve, but the human body can only compartmentalize so much trauma. As the quiet settled over the living room, the localized, blinding agony in my rear end spiked with terrifying ferocity.

My spastic muscles locked entirely. I couldn't breathe through it. I couldn't suppress it. A jagged, involuntary scream tore from my throat, raw and utterly panicked.

The absolute stillness of the fortress shattered.

Samantha instantly tightened her arms around my chest, pressing her face against mine, her own tears starting to fall as she tried to anchor my thrashing, misfiring body. Above us, the massive silhouettes of my cousins snapped to attention.

Grandmother Eleanor was out of her rocking chair in a fraction of a second. She knelt beside the mattress camp, her sharp aristocratic features pale. "Toby, Toby, look at me," she urged, but I couldn't. The pain was deafening.

"It’s not just his CP," Samantha choked out, her voice trembling but fiercely loud in the quiet room. She looked over my head, locking eyes with our grandmother and our cousins. "It's... I have to tell you what they did to him."

The room went completely still as Samantha spoke. She didn't spare them the horrific truth of what she had witnessed in that house in Kentucky. She outlined the exact, brutal reality of why I was screaming.

The air in the living room turned to lead. I felt the immense, physical weight of Abbey and Sarah's rage. Their broad shoulders went completely rigid, the dense muscle of their arms straining against their shirts as their hands balled into massive fists. But they didn't shout. They didn't punch the walls or lose their emotional baseline. They were a highly coordinated, trauma-informed unit.

Grandmother Eleanor stood up, her face a mask of absolute, cold authority.

"We are not doctors," Eleanor stated, her voice slicing through the heavy air. She turned to the twins. "We cannot treat this here. He needs a trauma unit immediately. Sarah, get the car."

Sarah didn't say a word. She spun on her heel, her heavy boots thudding against the hardwood floor as she rushed to the garage to start the sedan.

Eleanor moved to the dining table, snatching up her heavy legal binder. As the legal architect of the family, she knew exactly what was about to happen. She was grabbing the absolute, state-sanctioned proof of her sole legal custody. No doctor or triage nurse was going to separate us; she had the paperwork to mandate that our perimeter remained intact.

Abbey knelt back down beside me. Her stoic face was hard as stone, but her hands were incredibly gentle. "I've got you, Toby. We're getting you help."

Once again, she utilized her towering, elite-caliber strength to carefully slide her arms beneath my upper back and lower thighs. She lifted my compact frame off the mattress block, bearing one hundred percent of my weight to ensure my lower half remained completely suspended in the air. Every step she took toward the front door was perfectly measured, her thick legs absorbing the impact so my shattered body wouldn't have to.

Samantha walked right beside us, her hand gripping my shoulder. We weren't just staying in the fortress anymore. The matriarchy was taking the perimeter mobile, and they were bringing the full weight of their physical and legal power to the hospital.
