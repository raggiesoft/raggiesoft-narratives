# Part 14: The Medical Perimeter – 8:55 PM

Trauma Bay Three was blindingly bright and freezing cold. The moment the ER tech locked the wheels of my gurney into the center of the room, the space was flooded with medical personnel. It was a highly coordinated, rapid response—doctors and nurses moving with practiced, urgent efficiency.

I knew I was in a hospital. I knew the rules here were absolute. And despite the terrifying volume of strangers suddenly surrounding me, the panic in my chest didn't completely override my logic.

I was safe because my family was here, and they were playing exactly by the hospital's rules.

Grandmother Eleanor didn't even enter the bay. True to her role as the legal vanguard, she remained out in the hallway. Through the glass, I could see her already intercepting a hospital social worker and two police officers, her heavy legal binder open as she controlled the narrative and kept the uniforms completely out of my line of sight.

Inside the room, the perimeter was perfectly established. Abbey and Sarah immediately retreated to the absolute furthest edge of the trauma bay. They stood shoulder-to-shoulder, pressing their broad backs flat against the sterile white wall. They crossed their massive arms over their chests and went completely still. They didn't speak. They didn't offer to help lift me. They knew that hospital liability meant the nurses had to do the physical work, and any interference from them—given their towering, tank-like size—would be seen as a threat.

Samantha stayed exactly where she was allowed: pinned to the top left corner of the gurney, right beside my head. She leaned over, pressing her forehead gently against my temple, whispering a steady, grounding stream of reassurance while a nurse quickly prepped my arm.

"His muscles are locking up from the pain," the attending doctor said quickly, observing my rigid, thrashing frame. "Let's get an IV established. Push a muscle relaxant and Dilaudid, stat."

It took two nurses to carefully stabilize my spastic arm, but within seconds, I felt the sharp pinch of a needle. Almost immediately, a wave of icy cold rushed up my vein.

The relief was staggering. The medication hit my shattered nervous system like a heavy blanket. The blinding, white-hot agony radiating from my lower half began to dull into a heavy, distant ache. As the pain receded, my autonomic spasticity finally broke. My locked joints softened, and I melted heavily into the pillows Abbey had arranged.

"Okay, buddy, the medicine is going to help," a new voice said.

A woman in specialized, dark blue scrubs stepped up to the side of the bed. Her badge identified her as a SANE—a Sexual Assault Nurse Examiner. Her voice was incredibly calm, slow, and deliberately gentle.

"We need to examine your injuries so we can help you," she explained, looking directly into my eyes, treating me with absolute dignity despite my inability to speak. "But to do that safely, we have to roll you over. My team is going to do all the lifting. You don't have to do anything."

I instinctively looked past the nurse, searching the room until my eyes found Abbey.

Abbey was watching the medical team like a hawk, her dark eyes tracking every single movement they made. But she remained perfectly flat against the wall, her massive frame completely motionless. She was trusting them, which meant I had to trust them, too. Abbey gave me a single, firm nod.

I blinked slowly, signaling my consent to Samantha, who immediately translated it to the nurses. "He understands. He's ready."

The medical staff did not ask my cousins for help. Instead, three nurses positioned themselves around the gurney. Using a specialized medical slide board and their own coordinated leverage, they smoothly and clinically rolled my relaxed frame onto my side, exposing my back to the SANE nurse.

"You're doing great, Toby," the specialist said softly. She examined the area for only a few seconds before stepping back, her face hardening slightly as she processed the severity of the trauma. She looked to Samantha, and then to Abbey and Sarah on the wall.

"The injuries are severe," the specialist stated quietly, keeping her voice even so as not to spike my heart rate. "We need to clean and treat them immediately, but he has been through too much trauma today. I don't want him awake for this."

The specialist looked back down at me. "Toby, I am going to have the doctor give you a medicine called Propofol. It's twilight sedation. It’s going to make you fall into a very deep, very safe sleep. You won't feel any pain, and you won't remember the exam. When you wake up, it will be over."

The heavy narcotic pain medication was already pulling me under, making the bright lights of the trauma bay blur. I felt Samantha’s hand squeeze mine tightly.

I looked one last time at the wall. My towering cousins were still there, immovable and absolute, guarding the door. Grandmother Eleanor was in the hall, guarding the law. And Samantha was right beside me, guarding my heart.

As the cold rush of the sedative hit my IV, I closed my eyes and finally let the darkness take me, knowing with absolute certainty that no one would ever hurt me again.
