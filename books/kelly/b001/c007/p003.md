# Part 3: A Necessary Anchor ⚓

The tour of the Infinite Loop campus was a dizzying experience for the twins. As they walked, Ryan’s limp became undeniable. A sharp, shooting pain went up his leg, and he winced, stopping for a moment. He was exhausted, and his pride was the only thing keeping him from asking to sit down.

Jessica stopped her wheelchair beside him. "Ryan," she said, her voice soft but direct. "You're in pain. Put your hand on the headrest of my chair and let it take some of the weight."

"No, I'm okay, really," he started, his pride flaring. He tried to take another step, but a sharp intake of breath betrayed the pain he was trying to hide.

Jessica's gaze was steady, cutting through his denial with a look of professional concern. "As your supervisor, your well-being on this trip is my responsibility. That's an order."

He still hesitated, his jaw tight with stubbornness. Seeing this, Kelly stepped in. She didn't say a word. She simply took her brother's hand and gently, firmly, placed it on the solid headrest of Jessica's power wheelchair.

The moment his hand rested there, Ryan felt a wave of relief as he was able to take the pressure off his aching leg. He looked from his sister's determined face to Jessica's patient one, his defenses finally crumbling. Defeated by the pain and by their combined, unspoken insistence, he relented. With a quiet sigh, he let his weight lean into the support.

They continued down the hall in a slow, careful procession: Jessica leading, Ryan using her chair as a steadying point, and Kelly walking protectively on his other side.

Kelly watched her brother, her heart aching with every pained step he took. She saw him trying to be the strong anchor he had always been for her, and she knew this wasn't a sustainable solution. She made a decision.

She quickened her pace to catch up with David. "David?" she said, her voice quiet but firm. "My brother, he's not going to admit it, but he's in a lot of pain. I can't let him keep walking like this. I know this is a strange question, but... does MagnaByte have a wheelchair we could borrow? Just for the rest of the tour today?"

David’s face broke into a look of immediate understanding and relief. "That's not a strange question at all," he said warmly. "It's the right one. And yes, of course we do. It's no trouble at all. I'll make a quick call and have one brought to the OS lab."

When they arrived at the lab, a simple, lightweight manual wheelchair was waiting just inside the door. Ryan looked at it, then at his sister, his expression a complex mixture of gratitude and the painful surrender of his own physical independence. Today, his sister was his anchor.
