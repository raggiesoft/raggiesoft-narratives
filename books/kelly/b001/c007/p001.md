# Part 1: Company Policy 📋

The dark blue **Stark Rancher** van glided to a smooth stop in front of the main building of the **MagnaByte** campus. **Ryan** and **Kelly** stared in silence at the iconic, curved glass facade of 1 Infinite Loop.

"Welcome, everyone," **David** said as he led them into the bright, minimalist lobby. The air buzzed with a focused, creative energy.

The woman at the reception desk looked up and broke into a wide, genuine smile. "Jessica! It's been too long. Welcome back."

"Hey, Maria," **Jessica** replied warmly. "Good to see you. I brought some new talent with me this time."

Even as they exchanged friendly greetings, the receptionist was professionally sliding a digital sign-in tablet toward them. "I'll just need all three of you to sign in, and I'll get your visitor badges printed."

Jessica signed without a moment's hesitation, followed by the twins. A moment later, they each had a temporary badge clipped to their shirts. Despite being treated like family, she was still an employee of a rival company, and corporate policy was absolute. David, as their host, would be their official escort for the entire visit.

As they walked through the security gates, David began the tour. "Okay, so down this hall is the main..."

He was interrupted by Jessica, who was already pointing things out to the twins with the easy familiarity of a resident. "The Caffe is down that way," she said. "Best coffee on campus. David, are we still meeting the OS team in the main lab in Building 4, or did they get moved to the new wing?"

David chuckled. "Still in Building 4. You probably know the way better than I do."

The twins walked between them, taking it all in. Their boss, the legendary engineer from their chief competitor, was navigating the halls of MagnaByte's sacred headquarters like she owned the place, all while wearing a visitor's badge. It was their first, powerful lesson in the strange and wonderful dual reality of the developer community.
