# Part 8: A Sister's Insistence 杖

As the workday wound down, **David** came to collect the team. He handled the return of the borrowed manual wheelchair, and for the walk back through the long corridors to the main lobby, **Ryan** once again placed his hand on the headrest of **Jessica’s** power wheelchair for support. He was quiet, the exhaustion from a day of masking his pain clearly visible on his face.

Once they were back in the **Stark Rancher** van and heading toward the hotel, a determined look settled on **Kelly's** face. She had watched her brother struggle all day, and she would not let it continue.

"David, is that a pharmacy up ahead?" she asked, pointing to a brightly lit drugstore. "Can we please stop for a minute?"

David pulled into the parking lot without a word of protest. Kelly turned to her brother. "Come on," she said, her tone gentle but firm.

"What for?" Ryan asked, not moving. "I'm fine, Kel. Let's just go back to the hotel and rest."

"No," she said, her voice unwavering. "We're getting you a walking cane. I am not going to watch you be in pain all week because you're too proud to accept help. You needed a wheelchair today, Ryan. You need help."

"I don't need a cane," he protested, his voice tight with frustration and embarrassment. "It's just my old job. It'll go away."

From the front of the van, Jessica spoke, her voice quiet but clear. "She's right, Ryan. There's no shame in it. It's a tool, just like my chair. It's smart to use the right tool for the job."

Defeated by their unified front, Ryan let out a long, slow breath and nodded. He followed Kelly into the store. A few minutes later, they returned, Ryan holding a simple, adjustable aluminum cane. He looked at it not as a relief, but as a symbol of a weakness he wasn't ready to accept. Kelly, however, looked at it as a victory. She had taken care of her anchor.
