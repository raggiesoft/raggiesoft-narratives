The whiteboard was a dizzying, beautiful web of logic. Jessica’s explanation was clearer and more insightful than any textbook Kelly had ever tried to read. It was everything she had wanted. It was perfect.

And it was too much.

The gratitude, the relief, the sheer joy of finally learning—it all converged into a single, overwhelming wave of emotion. Her breathing hitched, tears welling in her eyes as a choked sob escaped her lips.

Ryan didn't hesitate. He was out of his chair in a second, wrapping her in the firm, tight, grounding hug she needed. Jessica, seeing this, quietly wheeled back to her desk, giving them a silent, judgment-free space to exist. *Take all the time you need.*

Slowly, under the steady pressure of Ryan's hug, Kelly’s ragged breathing began to even out. The tears subsided. She leaned back into her brother, the overwhelming feeling receding, replaced by a profound sense of safety. And in that quiet, protected space, a final, horrifying puzzle piece clicked into place.

The confusion she had felt at Aurora, the feeling that Evan "never really saw me"... it wasn't because he was overlooking her talent. It was because he wasn't looking at her face. The memory of his gaze, which she had always tried to ignore, came back with a sickening clarity.

She pulled back slightly from Ryan's hug, her expression no longer one of emotional overload, but of cold, dawning horror.

"Ryan," she whispered, her voice trembling with a new and terrible understanding. "Evan... He *was* looking at me. He was staring at my breasts. The whole time. That's all he ever saw."

Ryan’s expression shifted from concern to a quiet, glacial fury. He pulled her back into a hug, but this one was different. It was fiercely protective. "He will never be near you again," he said, his voice a low, hard promise. "Ever."

She was in the office of one of the most respected engineers in the industry, having an autistic meltdown, and in the safety of that moment, she had finally understood the true nature of the horror she had escaped. And she wasn't being judged or dismissed. She was being protected. Here, she was welcome. All of her.
