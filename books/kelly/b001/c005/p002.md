# Part 2: Starting with 'Why' 💡

An hour later, after their official HR check-ins were complete and they knew the location of every snack jar, Kelly and Ryan returned to Jessica Taylor’s corner office. The afternoon sun streamed through the large windows, illuminating the complex diagrams on the massive whiteboard. Jessica swiveled her power wheelchair away from her monitors to face them, a small, encouraging smile on her face.

"Alright, settled in?" she asked. They both nodded. "Good. Let's get to the fun part."

She gestured toward a small conference table in the corner of her office. "Pull up a chair. We're not going to start by throwing you at a thousand lines of code you don't understand. We're going to start with the 'why'."

Jessica looked at Kelly, her gaze both knowing and kind. "I know some of the Aurora team was giving you lessons at the library and the college," she said, her voice soft. "And I'm grateful they did. We're going to build on that." She then turned to Ryan. "And for you, we're starting from the first step. There are no dumb questions here. Ever."

She picked up a marker and wheeled over to a clean section of the whiteboard. "Aurora Presenter has to feel seamless on MagnaOS. That means we have to understand how their operating system thinks, how it draws to the screen, how it handles memory. Before you write a single line of code for me, you're going to understand the philosophy behind the machine you're writing it for."

For the next hour, Jessica didn't just teach them syntax; she told them a story. She drew boxes and arrows, explaining the core concepts of object-oriented programming not as abstract rules, but as a way to build digital tools that felt intuitive and human. She explained that Kelly's previous lessons were a great start on the "how," but here, they would master the "why." She treated them not as interns, but as apprentice engineers, entrusting them with the foundational principles that made good software great.

Ryan, who had been nervous all morning, was now leaning forward, completely absorbed. Kelly felt a sense of calm settle over her. This was what she had wanted from the start: not just a job, but a chance to learn from the best, the right way. Here, in the quiet of Jessica’s office, with the afternoon light filling the room, her real education was just beginning.
