The bell over the door of **Jester's Burger Box** chimed as **Kelly** and **Ryan** stepped out into the cool Seattle evening. The frantic, joyful energy of their dinner conversation had settled into a quiet, profound sense of hope. Their house was only a few short blocks away, a familiar walk they’d made a thousand times.

As they started down the sidewalk, Ryan instinctively found her hand, his thumb stroking the back of hers in a slow, steady rhythm. It was a simple, grounding gesture she had relied on since they were children, a silent reminder that he was right there.

"It's just... a lot," Ryan said, breaking the comfortable silence. "One day I'm stocking shelves, the next I'm an intern at **Quantum**."

"You'll be amazing," Kelly said, bumping his shoulder with hers. "We'll learn it all together. They know we're a team."

"I can't believe they just... did that," Ryan mused. "Created a job for me just to make sure you were okay."

"They see you as my anchor," Kelly said softly. "They see what I've always known. I work best when you're there." She paused, looking at the warm lights of the houses they passed. "After being at Aurora, I didn't think places like that actually existed. Places that care."

They passed the bus stop where Kelly had gotten off just a couple of hours before, a stop that now felt like a relic from a different lifetime. They crossed the street and walked up the familiar path to their front door.

Ryan put an arm around his sister's shoulder. "Ready for this? For everything to change?"

Kelly looked up at him, her face illuminated by the porch light, and a real, unguarded smile spread across her face. "With you?" she said. "Always."
