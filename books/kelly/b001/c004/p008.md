# Part 8: Making it Official 🎓

The Career Services office at **Cascade View Community College** was a small, welcoming space lined with bookshelves full of catalogs and career guides. **Dr. Alisha Reed**, the Internship Coordinator, greeted **Kelly** and **Ryan** with a warm, beaming smile that immediately put them at ease.

"Come in, come in!" she said, ushering them into her office. "It's so good to see you both. I have to say, in all my years here, I have never seen a company step up the way **Quantum** has. Their proposal was truly extraordinary".

She gestured for them to sit as she retrieved a folder from her desk. "I've already been in contact with Sarah Chen at Quantum, and we have all the necessary paperwork ready to go. This will officially transfer your internship placement, Kelly, and add you to the program, Ryan. Your academic credit is completely secure".

She slid the forms across the desk. "I was so impressed by their commitment to you both," Dr. Reed continued as they signed. "Their recognition of your support needs, Kelly, and their willingness to create such a thoughtful and inclusive solution... it truly is a model for how these partnerships should work".

"We were really surprised," Kelly admitted, a shy smile on her face.

"You've earned it," Dr. Reed said firmly. "After what you went through at Aurora Systems, you deserve a supportive and challenging environment. You are both representing this college, and I have no doubt you will make us proud."

They left her office a few minutes later, the signed paperwork officially severing the last administrative tie to the Aurora disaster. Their new future wasn't just a verbal offer anymore; it was a reality, formally recognized and supported by their college.
