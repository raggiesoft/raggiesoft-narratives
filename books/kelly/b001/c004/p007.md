# Part 7: Leaving on Good Terms 📝

The next day, **Ryan** arrived for his shift at **PriceSavers** with a knot of nervous energy in his stomach. He found his manager, a perpetually busy but fair woman named Sharon, organizing schedules in the small back office of the electronics department.

"Sharon? Have you got a minute?" he asked.

She looked up from her paperwork. "Sure, Ryan. What's up?"

He took a breath, reciting the script his father had helped him practice. "I wanted to let you know that I've been offered an internship in the field I'm studying at college, and I've decided to accept it. I'd like to put in my two weeks' notice."

Sharon leaned back in her chair, a look of genuine disappointment on her face. "Wow. I'm sorry to lose you, Ryan. You're one of my best. But I can't say I'm surprised. What's the internship?"

"It's a programming internship at **Quantum**," he said.

Her eyes widened. "Quantum? Good for you. Seriously, that's incredible."

"Thank you," Ryan said, feeling a surge of pride. "And I just want you to know, I'm not going to slack off. I'll work just as hard for these last two weeks as I did on my first day, and I'm happy to help train whoever replaces me. It's important to me that I leave on good terms."

A rare, appreciative smile spread across Sharon's face. "Ryan, most kids your age just stop showing up. You've always been a class act. We'll be sad to see you go, but you're on to much bigger things. You've earned it."

They shook hands, and as Ryan walked back out to the sales floor, he felt a profound sense of relief. He had handled his first professional transition with the integrity his father had taught him. The door to his past was now closing, respectfully and correctly, leaving the path to his future wide open.
