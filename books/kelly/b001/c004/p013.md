# Part 13: Breaking the Ice 🧊

**Jessica** finished her phone call with a quiet "Thanks, talk to you then," and swiveled her chair back around. She immediately saw the look on the twins' faces. They were staring at her framed diploma, their jaws practically on the floor, a silent, shocked conversation passing between them. She knew that look. She'd seen it before.

A small, wry smile touched her lips. "It's the graduation date, isn't it?"

**Kelly** and **Ryan** both jumped, mortified at being caught. "Oh! Uh, sorry," Ryan stammered. "We didn't mean to stare."

"Don't worry about it," Jessica said, her voice calm and reassuring, completely devoid of any offense. "It happens all the time. Let me guess," she added, a knowing twinkle in her eye. "You thought I was in my forties?"

The twins just stared, their silence a clear confirmation.

"The reputation gets here about twenty years before the rest of me does," she said with a gentle, self-aware laugh. The tension in the room instantly evaporated, replaced by the twins' relieved smiles.

She leaned forward slightly, her tone shifting from amusing to mentoring. "Look, age doesn't matter in this industry. Skill does. You two have it. That's why you're here." She gestured to the monitors on their new desks. "Let's get back to work and show them what we can build together."

In that moment, the last of their intimidation melted away. Jessica was still a legend, still their boss, but she was also a person—a brilliant, kind, and disarmingly down-to-earth person. They got back to their coding exercise, their new team dynamic finally, comfortably, locked in place.
