# Part 13: Bedtime and Beach Dreams (Sunday Evening, Labor Day Weekend 1999)

The remnants of a simple Sunday supper –sandwiches and lemonade shared between the two families in the Millers' backyard – had been cleared away. The sun had dipped below the horizon, painting the Virginia sky in hues of orange and purple. Crickets had begun their evening chorus, and a comfortable, slightly weary peace settled over the two households. The big beach trip to the Virginia Beach Oceanfront was set for tomorrow, their last official day of summer vacation.

Alex and Chloé were in the Millers' living room, watching the end of a family movie with James and Emily, the shared anticipation for the beach mingling with the ever-present, low hum of nerves about high school starting on Tuesday.

As the evening wound down and yawns became more frequent, Chloé remembered her practical needs for the next day. "Oh!" she said, "If we're leaving early for the beach tomorrow, I should probably bring my swimsuit and a change of clothes over tonight. That way, I can just get ready here in the morning, and we won't have to run back and forth between houses. Streamline things a bit, eh?"

Emily Miller smiled. "That's a very thoughtful idea, Chloé dear. Of course. You know where the spare towels are if you need one after your shower in the morning too." It was a given that Chloé would use Alex's accessible bathroom at the Miller house if she stayed over; it was simply more practical.

"Thanks, Mrs. Miller!" Chloé said, then turned to Alex. "Okay if I just run next door for a minute and grab my stuff?"

Alex nodded, a happy anticipation for their beach day making his eyes sparkle. GOOD IDEA, CHLOÉ. TOMORROW WILL BE FUN.

With a quick "Be right back!" Chloé dashed out the Millers' back door, across the well-worn path in the grass, and into her own house, returning a few minutes later with a small overnight bag containing her swimsuit, a fresh set of clothes for the next day, and her pajamas.

Soon, it was time for bed. James and Emily said their goodnights, perhaps with a final reminder about sunscreen for the beach. Alex, with some assistance from his parents for his more involved evening routines in the accessible bathroom, was soon settled into his full-sized bed.

Chloé, having changed into her pajamas in the Millers' guest bathroom (or perhaps even Alex's, by now so familiar and comfortable in their home was she), came to his doorway with her overnight bag and her ever-present stuffed otter, Barnaby.

"All set for the beach invasion?" she asked softly, a tired but happy smile on her face.

Alex patted the empty space beside him on his full-sized bed, an unspoken, entirely natural invitation. There was no other place she would sleep when she stayed over; this had become their norm since that first night of his nightmare. No better place to be for them.

Chloé smiled back, understanding completely. She placed Barnaby on the pillow, then slid under the covers beside Alex. They settled in, perhaps talking quietly for a few more minutes about what they were most excited to show each other at the beach – Alex eager for Chloé to experience the feel of the specialized beach wheelchair on the sand, Chloé curious about the vastness of the Atlantic after a childhood near the much smaller lakes of Vermont and Québec.

"Think we'll see any dolphins?" Chloé whispered in the dark. MAYBE, Alex typed on his AAC, its screen a dim glow. HOPE SO. BUT EVEN IF NOT… SAND. WAVES. YOU. WILL BE PERFECT.

Chloé reached out in the darkness, and their hands found each other, a familiar, comforting clasp. The anxieties about high school were still there, lurking at the edges, but tonight, cocooned in the quiet safety of Alex's room, with the promise of a shared adventure by the ocean in the morning, those fears felt a little smaller, a little further away. They were together, and that, as always, made all the difference. Soon, their breathing evened out, two best friends drifting off to sleep, dreaming of sandcastles and salty air.
