# Part 3: Sandwiches and Shared Worlds (Sunday Afternoon, Summer 1999)

The Sunday following their inaugural ice cream adventure dawned warm and bright. Megan Carter, as was her custom on many summer weekends, arrived at the Miller house just in time for breakfast. The moment she walked in, Alex’s face lit up with a joy and relief that was palpable. He wheeled over to her, and she immediately enveloped him in their familiar, comforting hug.

“Morning, cuz,” she said, ruffling his hair. “You look like you’ve got something on your mind.”

He hugged her back fiercely, then pulled away, his fingers already flying across his AAC keypad. They sat down at the kitchen table with James and Emily, a comfortable, familiar scene.

THE NEW GIRL. CHLOÉ. SHE CAME OVER TO TALK TO ME YESTERDAY, Alex typed, his excitement clear.

“Oh yeah?” Megan said, her interest piqued. “And? How was it?”

Alex paused, a thoughtful, almost wondrous expression on his face. IT WAS… GOOD, he typed. REALLY GOOD. SHE’S NICE, MEGAN. NOT LIKE… He didn’t have to finish the sentence. Megan and his parents knew exactly who he was talking about.

Emily Miller reached over and gave his hand a reassuring squeeze. "She seems like a lovely girl, honey. Her parents are wonderful, too. We've spoken with them a few times."

"They're good people," James added, a look of quiet, paternal approval in his eyes. "The Masons seem like a very solid family." Their words were a gentle reassurance, not just for Alex, but for Megan, letting her know that they had already vetted these new people in their son's life.

SHE WASN'T WEIRD ABOUT MY CHAIR, Alex continued, his excitement growing. AND SHE GAVE ME A HUG. A REAL HUG. IT FELT… SAFE. LIKE YOUR HUGS. He looked at her, his eyes full of a new, fragile hope. SHE’S COMING OVER AGAIN. FOR LUNCH.

Megan’s face broke into a wide, genuine smile. “Well, then,” she said, her voice full of a profound, sisterly approval, “I can’t wait to meet her.”

After the breakfast dishes were cleared, the anticipation of Chloé's lunchtime visit settled over the Miller house. With a few hours to themselves, Alex had something he was incredibly excited to show Megan. He led her back to his bedroom, which was not just a place to sleep, but the command center for his entire digital world.

"Whoa," Megan said, genuinely impressed as she looked at his new setup. "Uncle James and Aunt Em really went all out for you."

His new Quantum OS computer was a beast for 1999, its large CRT monitor dominating his desk. But the real main event was the device that had given him his true voice: the "Quantum Communicator XT"

IT'S THE NEWEST MODEL, he typed, his pride evident in the clear, synthesized voice. THE WORD PREDICTION IS TWICE AS FAST AS MY OLD ONE.

"That's awesome, cuz," Megan said, her own genuine interest in the technology shining through. "Can I see?"

He spent the next half-hour giving her a full, enthusiastic demonstration, showing her the new features and the more natural cadence of the voice synthesizer. She was the one person in his life who didn't just see it as a "disability aid"; she saw it as an incredible piece of technology, and she was just as fascinated by its inner workings as he was.

Then, with the technical demonstration complete, it was time for their real shared passion.

RESONANCE CASCADE? Alex typed, a hopeful, mischievous glint in his eye.

Megan's face broke into a huge grin. "Oh, you are so on. I've been dying to see if we can beat that new level."

For the next two hours, they were a perfect, symbiotic team, deep in the alien-infested hallways of the Argos Science & Technology Division. Their unique, two-player setup, a feat of Alex's own programming genius, was a testament to their profound, unspoken connection. Megan, with her hands on the keyboard, controlled their shared character's movement. Alex, with the mouse, was the designated marksman. She was the legs; he was the arms.

"Okay, okay, Skull-jumper in the air vent above us," Megan would whisper, her own focus absolute. "On three. One... two..."

On "three," she would strafe them out into the open, and Alex, with a single, precise click of the mouse, would blast the creature out of the air before it even had a chance to drop. Their teamwork was a beautiful, efficient, and deeply nerdy ballet.

They were in the middle of an intense firefight with a group of Cascade Husks when the doorbell rang. Chloé had arrived. Alex, with a final, perfect shotgun blast, cleared the room.

SAVED BY THE BELL, he typed, a triumphant grin on his face.

Megan just laughed, her own heart full. This was their sanctuary, their shared world. And now, they were about to invite a new person in.

Chloé appeared at the Millers' front door, a cheerful "Hello, Mrs. Miller!" preceding her.

"Chloé, dear, come on in!" Emily Miller welcomed her with a warm smile. "They're in the living room. Alex has been looking forward to you coming over."

As Chloé stepped inside, she saw Alex in the living room, his face lighting up. But he wasn't alone. Sitting comfortably on the sofa was another girl, a few years older, with bright, curly blonde hair and a warm, easy smile. Alex immediately began typing on his AAC, his excitement palpable.

CHLOÉ, his device voiced, the tone bright and proud. I WANT YOU TO MEET SOMEONE. THIS IS MEGAN CARTER. SHE IS MY FAVORITE COUSIN. AND MY MOST FAVORITE WOMAN IN THE WHOLE WIDE WORLD.

Megan let out a warm, embarrassed laugh, her cheeks flushing slightly as she stood up. "He lays it on a little thick, doesn't he?" she said, her voice friendly and genuine. "It's so great to finally meet you, Chloé. He hasn't stopped talking about the new girl next door."

Chloé felt a small, happy blush rise to her own cheeks, but she felt no jealousy at Alex's grand declaration. She was just happy. Happy to see him so openly affectionate, and happy to finally meet this cousin who was clearly so important to him. "It's so nice to meet you, too," Chloé said, shaking her hand.

She immediately went to Alex, and they shared one of their now-frequent, easy "big squeeze" hugs—a gesture that had become as natural as saying hello. Megan watched the exchange, a look of pure, sisterly approval on her face.

"I was just about to make some sandwiches for lunch," Emily continued, her heart warming at the sight of the easy affection between the three of them. "Tuna salad, chicken salad or ham and cheese?"

"Ham and cheese sounds great, Mrs. Miller, thank you!" Chloé said.

The three of them settled in the living room, a comfortable and easy energy between them. Emily soon emerged with a tray laden with sandwiches, chips, and sodas. They ate their lunch, the conversation flowing easily. Chloé talked more about her old life on the border of Vermont and Québec. Alex, in turn, shared some of his anxieties about Northwood High, with Megan offering the wise, "it's-not-that-scary" perspective of a seasoned upperclassman.

After lunch, Alex, feeling a new level of comfort, had an idea. He typed a message on his device.

WOULD YOU LIKE ME TO SHOW YOU HOW THIS ACTUALLY WORKS? MY VOICE?

Chloé’s eyes lit up. "Are you kidding? I'd love that! I've been super curious."

Alex wheeled his chair closer. "He's a total pro with it," Megan said proudly, watching from the sofa.

YES, Alex typed, the familiar, clear-but-robotic voice emanating from the small speaker. IT RUNS QUANTUM OS 98. THE PROGRAM IS CALLED QUANTUM SPEECH SUITE.

On the screen, Chloé could see the program's interface. Alex expertly demonstrated how he typed, used the word prediction, and accessed his saved phrases for simple conversations.

Chloé looked from the complex device to Alex’s face, her expression full of admiration. She wasn't seeing a disability aid anymore; she was seeing an incredibly advanced piece of technology that her brilliant new friend used with impressive skill. "That is seriously one of the coolest things I have ever seen, Alex," she said with genuine conviction.

Alex’s face beamed with a mixture of pride and relief. Her praise earned her another delighted hug, which she returned with equal warmth.

Later, Chloé pulled a fantasy novel from her backpack, and she began to read a chapter aloud. Alex and Megan listened intently. When she finished a particularly exciting passage, she leaned over and rested her head on Alex's shoulder as they discussed a plot twist. Alex, surprised but pleased by her easy comfort, returned the gesture by putting his arm around her. Megan just smiled, watching the two of them, a perfect, unspoken connection forming right before her eyes.

As the afternoon light began to soften, Chloé knew she should probably head back home. "This was super fun, Alex," she said. "And it was so nice meeting you, Megan!"

"You too, Chloé!" Megan replied.

As Chloé stood to leave, the ritual was already established. Alex met her goodbye with an open invitation in his eyes, and Chloé leaned in for a final, warm "big squeeze" hug.

"Maybe tomorrow you can come over to my place?" Chloé suggested as she pulled back. "My mom said she’d teach us how to make her famous maple cookies."

ABSOLUTELY, Alex typed, his eyes already sparkling. YOU HELP ME UP THE STEPS?

With a final wave, Chloé slipped out the front door. Alex watched her go, a feeling of deep contentment settling over him. He had introduced his new friend to his favorite cousin, and it had felt... easy. Comfortable. Right. The last of his fears from the Jennifer incident, the wall he had built around his heart, was finally, truly, gone. He had two trusted women in his life now, two people who genuinely enjoyed his company, and he realized, with a profound sense of peace, that he trusted Chloé just as much as he trusted Megan. This summer, he knew, was going to be the best one yet.
