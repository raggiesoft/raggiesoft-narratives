# Part 7: The Reunion (Late Summer 1999)

The thirty minutes felt like an eternity to Alex. After Chloé disappeared into the bathroom, Megan, seeing his distress, made a suggestion.

"Hey," she said softly. "You'll be more comfortable on the sofa than just sitting in your chair. Come on, let's get you settled."

She helped him transfer from his wheelchair to the comfortable living room sofa. The moment he was settled, she sat beside him and put a firm, grounding arm around his shoulders. "It's okay, cuz," she said softly. "I'm right here. I'm not going anywhere."

He leaned heavily into his cousin's familiar, comforting hug, letting her cuddle him, a silent anchor in his moment of deep unease. Still, his eyes were fixed on the clock on the VCR, the green numbers a slow, agonizing countdown. 20 minutes... 10 minutes... 5...

He was so focused on the clock that he didn't hear the soft click of the bathroom door.

"Alex?"

He looked up, and his entire world snapped back into focus. Chloé was standing in the doorway, her expression soft and a little tired, but her eyes were full of a warm, reassuring light. She was back. Just like she promised.

A wave of profound, overwhelming relief washed over him, so intense it was almost dizzying. The buzzing anxiety that had filled his chest for the last half-hour vanished in an instant, replaced by a deep, shuddering sense of peace. She was here. Everything was okay.

Megan, sensing the seismic shift in his emotional state, gave his shoulder one last, firm squeeze and then gently released him. She understood. Her job as the temporary anchor was over. The person he truly needed was back.

Alex didn't need his AAC for this. He just opened his arms from his spot on the sofa.

Understanding everything his silent gesture meant, Chloé walked directly to him. She didn't hesitate. She knelt on the floor in front of the sofa and enveloped him in a hug that was different from all the others they had shared. It was more than just friendly or affectionate; it was a hug of profound reassurance, a fierce, protective embrace that was an apology for his anxiety and a celebration of their reunion.

Alex held on just as tightly, burying his face in the familiar, safe space of her shoulder. He breathed in her scent, the simple, uncomplicated reality of her presence chasing away the last of the shadows. This hug was a promise kept. It was the confirmation that even when she had to be separate from him, she would always, always come back. He was so incredibly happy to see her again.

When they finally, reluctantly, pulled apart, Chloé’s hands rested on his shoulders. "See?" she whispered, her voice full of a gentle, loving conviction. "I told you I'd be back."

He just nodded, his own eyes shining with a gratitude that went beyond words. He looked from Chloé's face to Megan, who was watching them from the other end of the sofa with a warm, proud smile. He was so appreciative that she had let him snuggle up with her, that she had been his rock while he waited. He didn't understand the "why" of Chloé's absence, and it still scared him, but in that moment, he understood what mattered most. He trusted them both completely. And that, he was beginning to realize, was enough.
