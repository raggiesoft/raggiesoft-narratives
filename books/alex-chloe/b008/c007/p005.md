# Part 5: A Quiet Thanksgiving (Thursday, November 23, 2003)

The first pale light of Thanksgiving morning 2003 filtered through the blinds of Chloé Mason’s small one-bedroom Lynnwood apartment. Alex Miller stirred beside her in the king-sized bed, surfacing slowly from a deep, exhausted sleep.

Chloé woke a few moments after him, her first thought, as always, Alex’s well-being. She saw him blinking, orienting himself, the haunted look in his eighteen-year-old eyes slightly softened by rest, but still present.

"*Bonjour, Alex, mon ami,*" she whispered in their now-default Québécois French. "*Bien dormi, un peu?*" (Good morning, Alex, my friend. Did you sleep well, a little?)

Alex turned his head on the pillow, offering her a small, fragile smile. He reached for his AAC device, which Chloé had carefully placed on the nightstand. OUI, CHLOÉ. MIEUX. MERCI D'ÊTRE LÀ. (Yes, Chloé. Better. Thank you for being here.)

"*Toujours, Alex,*" she replied, her hand finding his for a reassuring squeeze. (Always, Alex.)

While Alex rested, Chloé spent the morning not on her own coursework, but in a frantic, desperate research session on her MagnaByte laptop. She knew he was malnourished. She knew that reintroducing food had to be done carefully, but she didn't know why or how. She sat at her small desk, her laptop connected wirelessly to her MagnaByte AeroLink Base Station, a small, UFO-shaped device that provided her apartment with a fast broadband internet connection. She typed search terms like "refeeding after starvation" and "malnutrition recovery diet" into the search engine, her heart sinking as she read about the complex and dangerous medical realities of refeeding syndrome.

She wasn't a doctor. She was a terrified eighteen-year-old trying to save her best friend's life with a high-speed internet connection and the contents of a student pantry. Based on her reading, she formed a plan. It wasn't perfect, but it was the best she could do.

Later that afternoon, the apartment filled with a new, comforting aroma. She sat beside Alex at her small table.

"*Bon, Alex,*" she said gently. "*Il faut manger quelque chose de plus substantiel aujourd'hui.*" (Okay, Alex. You need to eat something more substantial today.)

He looked at her, his trust absolute. In front of him, she placed a small bowl of simple, boiled white rice and another bowl of clear, warm chicken broth she had made from a bouillon cube. It was bland, simple, and, based on her frantic research, one of the safest things she could possibly give him.

"It's not much," she said, her voice full of a quiet apology for the meager Thanksgiving feast. "*Mais d'après ce que j'ai lu, c'est ce qu'il y a de plus sûr pour toi en ce moment. Il faut y aller doucement.*" (But from what I've read, this is the safest thing for you right now. We have to go slow.)

Alex looked at her, at her tired, worried face, at the simple, carefully prepared meal. He understood, with a profound clarity, the immense effort and love that had gone into this simple bowl of rice.

He reached for his AAC, his fingers moving with a new strength. CHLOÉ, ÇA SEMBLE… PARFAIT. C'EST LE MEILLEUR THANKSGIVING QUE JE POUVAIS ESPÉRER. MERCI. (Chloé, that sounds… perfect. That's the best Thanksgiving I could hope for. Thank you.)

A warm, genuine smile lit Chloé’s face. "Alors, c'est un plan, mon ami! Un Thanksgiving simple, rempli de bonne nourriture de base, peut-être un marathon de films quétaines, et absolument aucun drame. Juste nous." (Then, it's a plan, my friend! A simple Thanksgiving, filled with good, basic food, maybe a cheesy movie marathon, and absolutely no drama. Just us.)

Alex nodded, a sense of peace settling over him. After the darkness with Vera, Chloé's simple, loving offer of rice and safety, spoken in their shared language of trust, felt like the most profound blessing in the world.
