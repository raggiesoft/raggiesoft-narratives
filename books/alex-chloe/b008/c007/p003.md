# Part 3: First Meal in Sanctuary – Simple Comforts (Wednesday, November 22, 2003)

Wrapped in Chloé Mason’s softest, oversized NPU t-shirt and her largest, most comfortable pajama pants, Alex Miller sat in his wheelchair in the small, combined living and dining area of Chloé’s one-bedroom off-campus apartment in Lynnwood. The lingering scent of her gentle shampoo from his recent bath was a comforting contrast to the grime he’d endured for days. The raw, angry marks of the Stage I pressure sores on his hips and lower back were a dull, throbbing reminder of Vera’s neglect, but the immediate, gnawing ache of hunger was beginning to assert itself with surprising force.

As Chloé ended the video call with his parents, a gentle but determined look settled on her face. "*Bon, Alex,*" she said, her voice soft but practical as she turned from her computer, continuing in their shared, safe Québécois French. "*Tu dois mourir de faim, mon pauvre. Qu'est-ce qui te ferait plaisir?*" (Okay, Alex. You must be starving, my poor thing. What sounds good to you?)

Alex looked at her, a wave of gratitude so profound it almost brought fresh tears to his eyes. He reached for his AAC device, its screen still a bit dim from the low battery but functional enough for now. His fingers, still a little shaky, carefully typed in Québécois French: DES ŒUFS BROUILLÉS? ET DES TOASTS? C'EST FACILE À MANGER. (Scrambled eggs? And toast? It's easy to eat.)

Chloé’s face lit up with a warm smile. "*Des œufs brouillés et des toasts? Parfait! Ça arrive tout de suite!*" (Scrambled eggs and toast? Perfect! Coming right up!)

She went into her small kitchen and rummaged through her meager, student-sized pantry. She wasn't a doctor. She had no idea about the complexities of refeeding a malnourished person. She just knew that her best friend was starving, and that eggs and toast sounded simple, gentle, and nourishing. It was a choice born from pure, loving intuition and the simple luck of what she had on hand.

The apartment soon filled with the comforting, mundane sounds and smells of breakfast being prepared. She whisked the eggs with a bit of milk and a generous pat of butter, just as her mother had always taught her, not knowing that the fat might be difficult for his stressed system to process. Soon, she was placing a plate before him on the small table: fluffy, perfectly scrambled eggs, two slices of golden-brown toast, and a glass of apple juice.

Alex looked down at the plate. It wasn't fancy. It was simple, honest food. But in that moment, it looked like the most exquisite meal he had ever seen. He ate slowly at first, then with more eagerness as his body registered the nourishment it had been craving. When he was finished, having cleaned his plate, a profound sense of warmth and contentment spread through him, deeper than just the food. It was the feeling of being cared for, of being safe.

He reached for his AAC again, typing a heartfelt message in Québécois French: CHLOÉ. MERCI. LES MEILLEURS ŒUFS BROUILLÉS DU MONDE. JE ME SENS… TELLEMENT MIEUX. (Chloé. Thank you. Best scrambled eggs in the world. I feel… so much better.)

Chloé’s smile widened. "*N'importe quand, Alex. N'importe quand. Concentre-toi seulement sur te reposer et reprendre des forces.*" (Anytime, Alex. Anytime at all. Just focus on resting and getting your strength back.) She then added with a twinkle in her eye, "*Mais d'abord, je pense qu'une sérieuse sieste s'impose pour nous deux. Sauver et être sauvé, c'est épuisant!*" (But first, I think a serious nap is in order for both of us. Rescuing and being rescued is exhausting work!)

Alex couldn't have agreed more. Food, safety, and the promise of peaceful, uninterrupted rest in Chloé's shared king-sized bed – it was more than he could have hoped for just that morning. He was finally, truly, beginning to heal.
