# Part 6: A Debrief at Lisa Court

It was almost midnight when Megan’s Holt sedan pulled into the quiet cul-de-sac of Lisa Court. The lights were on in the Miller house, a welcoming beacon in the darkness. When she pulled up, the front door opened, and all four parents—James, Emily, Thomas, and Sophie—were waiting on the porch, their faces etched with a shared, profound anxiety.

They met them on the lawn, Emily immediately pulling her son into a fierce, relieved hug. Sophie was right beside her, her hand a comforting presence on Alex’s back. Alex just nodded, the exhaustion of the night evident in his slumped shoulders.

They all settled in the living room, a quiet, worried circle around Alex. It was Megan who did the talking. She calmly and concisely recounted the events of the evening.

"It wasn't pretty," Megan concluded, her voice low and serious. "She's possessive, she's manipulative, and he is not safe with her. He needed to get out of there."

The four parents listened, their initial relief hardening into a cold, grim resolve.

"This is a dangerous situation," James said, his voice heavy.

Megan took a deep breath, her expression shifting to one of quiet, strategic purpose. "I know," she said. "Which is why I kind of... already did something about it."

The four parents looked at her, confused.

"I saw a flyer for an Open House at VDU a few days ago," she explained, a little sheepishly. "And I was already worried about him. I thought he could use a weekend away, to get some perspective. So... I secretly registered both of us to go. It's in a few weeks. I figured, best case scenario, we just cancel. Worst case..." she gestured to a tired, emotionally drained Alex, "...we'd need it."

A profound, stunned silence filled the room, followed by a wave of pure, unadulterated gratitude.

"Megan Carter," Thomas Mason said, his voice full of awe and deep respect. "You are a brilliant strategist."

"That's a wonderful idea, Megan," Emily said, a new hope in her voice. The parents immediately seized on the plan. It was the perfect, non-confrontational solution.

Seeing how late it was, Emily added, "Honey, you can't drive back to the dorm this late. Please, just stay the night here."

Before Megan could answer, Alex looked at her, his expression full of a quiet, vulnerable plea.

MEGAN? his Quantum Note voiced softly. CAN YOU... STAY? IN MY ROOM? WITH ME? LIKE WHEN WE WERE KIDS?

The request, so full of a raw need for safety and comfort, hung in the air. The four parents looked at their son, then at their niece, and their hearts broke with a profound, aching love. They understood completely.

"Of course, she can, sweetheart," Emily said without a moment's hesitation.

Megan’s own eyes were shining with tears. She knelt by his chair and took his hand. "You got it, cuz," she whispered. "I'm not going anywhere."

The parents, understanding the need for privacy and the deep trust between the two cousins, said their goodnights.

Megan helped Alex with his evening toileting routine, her movements familiar and comforting. She then brought him to his room and helped him into his bed. She went to get a pillow and a blanket for the floor, but he stopped her.

He just patted the empty space beside him in the bed.

She smiled, a small, sad, and deeply loving smile, and climbed in beside him, pulling the covers up over them both. She promised him that in the morning, she would help him with his shower, that she would be there for whatever he needed. He just curled up beside her, and for the first time that night, the terror began to recede, replaced by the profound, unconditional safety of his first and truest friend.
