# Part 7: The Morning Debrief

Alex woke up slowly, the morning light filtering into his own familiar bedroom. For a moment, the terror of the previous night rushed back. Then, he felt a steady, comforting weight beside him. He turned his head and saw Megan, still fast asleep, her arm resting protectively over his shoulders. He was safe. He was home.

An hour later, the six of them were gathered around the Millers' kitchen table. The atmosphere was quiet and serious. Emily had made a simple, comforting breakfast of cereal. She deliberately placed a large, cold pitcher of milk in the center of the table, a small, unspoken act of reclamation.

After a few minutes of quiet eating, James Miller finally broke the silence. "Alex," he began, his voice a gentle but firm anchor. "We need to talk about what happened last night." He paused, his expression full of a deep, profound pride. "First, and most importantly, we are not mad at you. At all. We are so, so incredibly proud of you for making that call. That took a tremendous amount of courage, son. We are so glad you called for help."

The other three parents immediately chimed in with their own heartfelt agreement. "He's absolutely right, Alex," Thomas said, his voice a low, supportive rumble. "You did the right thing."

Alex looked around the table at their faces, at the pure, unconditional love and support, and a huge weight he didn't even know he was carrying lifted from his shoulders. He took a deep breath and reached for his Quantum Note.

SHE... SHE WOULDN'T LET ME LEAVE, he began to type, recounting the events of the previous night. I TOLD HER I WAS UNCOMFORTABLE AND WANTED TO GO HOME. SHE SAID NO. THAT I WAS BEING RIDICULOUS. THAT I HAD TO STAY.

Sophie Mason let out a small, sharp gasp. "*Mon Dieu.* She trapped you."

YES, Alex typed. I FELT TRAPPED. I WAS SCARED. SO I CALLED MEGAN.

"And you were right to," Emily said, her own voice a mixture of fierce love and barely concealed rage at Vera. "You never, ever have to stay somewhere you don't feel safe."

"Her reaction at the door was... hostile," Megan added, her voice low and serious. "The moment I hugged you, Alex, she started screaming. And her comment about you not being able to 'say' you wanted to leave... it was just cruel."

"It's about control," Thomas stated, his expression grim. "She's trying to isolate you, son. It's a classic, dangerous pattern."

James looked at his son, his expression now one of gentle, probing concern. "Alex, what is it about her that scares you so much?"

Alex was quiet for a long moment, struggling to put the complex, confusing feeling into words. He looked at Megan, his gaze full of a lifetime of trust, and then his eyes drifted to a picture of him and Chloé that sat on the mantelpiece. He began to type.

HER HUGS... THEY ARE NOT LIKE THE HUGS I GET FROM CHLOÉ... OR FROM YOU, he typed, the message directed at Megan. The words were a raw, vulnerable confession. CHLOÉ'S HUGS ARE KIND. MEGAN'S HUGS ARE SAFE. VERA'S... THEY FEEL LIKE SHE IS HOLDING ON TOO TIGHT. LIKE SHE IS AFRAID I WILL LEAVE. IT DOESN'T FEEL... KIND. IT FEELS... NEEDY.

The four parents exchanged a look of profound, dawning horror. It was worse than they thought. He wasn't just being controlled; he was being smothered.

Megan, seeing her cue, took a deep breath. "Okay," she said, her voice a calm, strategic anchor. "So, we talked about this a little bit last night, but now that we're all awake and thinking clearly... I really think this is the best first step. The Open House at VDU. It's in a few weeks. It's the perfect, low-stakes excuse for you to spend a whole weekend away from her. To clear your head. To remember what normal feels like."

The four parents immediately voiced their support. "It's a fantastic idea, Megan," Thomas Mason said, his tone full of a quiet, firm resolve. "We're all in."

All eyes turned to Alex. He had been listening, a profound internal battle raging on his face.

I... I WANT TO GO, his Quantum Note voiced, the words quiet and full of a deep, weary longing. BUT VERA... SHE WILL BE SO ANGRY. SHE WILL NOT UNDERSTAND.

Megan's expression hardened. "I don't give a damn how Vera would react," she said, her voice a low, protective growl. "You are allowed to spend time with your family, Alex. Period."

"Your cousin is absolutely right," James Miller added, his own voice a calm, unshakeable wall of paternal authority. "This is not up for debate. You will spend time with this family. Her feelings on the matter are completely irrelevant."

The unified, unwavering support from his family was a powerful, healing force. He looked around the table and felt a flicker of his own strength return.

All too soon, it was time for Megan to leave for her own classes. The goodbyes at the front door were long and heartfelt.

"You be strong, cuz," she whispered, pulling him into a final, fierce hug. "And you call me. Anytime. For anything. I will always come."

I WILL, he typed, hugging her back.

He stood in the doorway with his parents and watched her drive away. The moment her car disappeared, the quiet, safe bubble of the morning seemed to pop. The house felt empty. The profound sense of loneliness and the cold, creeping dread of having to face Vera again began to creep back in. He was safe, for now. But he was still trapped. And he was sad all over again.
