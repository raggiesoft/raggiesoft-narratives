# Part 5: A Night of Healing

After the plates from their home-cooked dinner were cleared and loaded into the dishwasher, Megan’s mission of healing continued. She scrolled through a small collection of DVDs she kept by her television.

"Alright, cuz," she said, her eyes twinkling. "I found the absolute worst sci-fi movie in my collection. It's called *Galaxy Marauders 3*, the special effects are terrible, and the plot makes no sense. I figured it would be right up your and Chloé's alley."

A rare, genuine smile spread across Alex's face. A CINEMATIC MASTERPIECE, his device voiced with his signature dry wit.

"Exactly," she grinned. "But you can't watch a cinematic masterpiece from way over there. The sofa is much more comfortable."

Alex hesitated for a moment, the simple invitation feeling like a monumental step. He hadn't just relaxed, truly relaxed, with anyone besides Chloé in months. Seeing his hesitation, Megan's voice softened. "Come on," she said gently. "It's just me."

He nodded and transferred from his wheelchair to the comfortable sofa. Megan settled in beside him, leaving a comfortable space, and hit play. As the movie’s ridiculous, over-the-top opening scene began, she spoke again, her voice a quiet, casual invitation.

"Okay, now get over here," she said. "You look like you could use a proper hug."

He willingly, gratefully, curled up beside her. She wrapped a strong, warm arm around his shoulders, pulling him into a tight, grounding, and completely safe hug. It wasn't possessive or needy; it was a simple, powerful act of familial love, a silent lesson designed to remind him what real, safe affection was supposed to feel like.

They watched the movie, Megan providing a running commentary of sarcastic jokes, and somewhere in the middle of a scene involving a rubber-suited alien and a very obvious spaceship-on-a-string, it happened. Alex let out a sound he hadn't made in a very long time: a real, genuine, unforced laugh.

The sound was so unexpected, so wonderful, that Megan’s own joking commentary died in her throat. She looked at him, at his face alight with simple, uncomplicated joy, and felt tears prick her eyes. The sound of his laughter was the most beautiful thing she had ever heard.

She just hugged him a little tighter, her heart aching with a fierce, protective love. The weekend was already a success. She was giving her cousin back a piece of himself that had been stolen. They finished the movie in a comfortable, happy silence, the quiet sound of Alex's occasional chuckle a quiet victory against the darkness he had been living in.
