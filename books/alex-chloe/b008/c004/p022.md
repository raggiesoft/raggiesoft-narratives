# Part 22: The Heart-to-Heart

Sunday morning in Megan's apartment was quiet and serious. After their gentle, peaceful waking, Megan made them a simple breakfast of scrambled eggs and toast. They ate at her small kitchen table, the air thick with the weight of a necessary, inevitable conversation. This was not a casual chat; it was an intervention of love.

Megan put her fork down, her green eyes full of a deep, earnest concern. "Alex," she began, her voice gentle but firm. "We need to talk. About Vera."

Alex tensed, his own fork stilling in his hand.

"I've seen how you are around her," Megan continued, "and I saw you this weekend when you were away from her. You were happy. You laughed. You were *yourself*. With her... cuz, you're a ghost. She's hurting you. This relationship... it's unhealthy. And I can only see more hurt in your future if you stay with her."

Alex looked down, the truth of her words a heavy, undeniable weight. He began to type on his Quantum Note, which was on the table beside him.

SHE... SHE ACCUSED ME... he typed, the words a raw, painful confession. OF CHEATING. WITH YOU. SHE SAID THE ONLY REASON I WANTED TO COME HERE THIS WEEKEND WAS TO HAVE SEX WITH YOU.

Megan's face, which had been full of concern, hardened into a look of pure, cold fury. She was disgusted, but she kept her voice calm for him. "Alex, that is the most insane, twisted, and disgusting thing I have ever heard."

AND WHEN I TOLD HER YOU WERE MY MOST FAVORITE WOMAN IN THE WORLD, he continued, SHE GOT SO ANGRY. SHE SAID I WASN'T ALLOWED TO FEEL THAT WAY. THAT \*SHE\* HAD TO BE THE ONLY ONE.

Megan shook her head, a look of profound, aching pity in her eyes. She knew, with an absolute certainty that came from a lifetime of loving him, that Chloé would never react that way. Chloé would be thrilled that Alex had someone like Megan in his life. Chloé would never be threatened by their bond; she would celebrate it. The contrast was a chasm, a night-and-day difference between possessive control and real, supportive love.

"Alex, listen to me," Megan said, her voice unwavering. "Your mom and dad see it. Thomas and Sophie see it. Chloé sees it. And I see it. And we are all so, so worried about you."

She reached across the table and took his hand. "We have a plan," she said, her voice dropping to an intense, loving whisper. "An escape plan for you. I've already talked about it with all of them—my parents, your parents, and the Masons. Everyone is on board."

She took a deep breath. "I graduate in December. I'll be getting my own apartment, a real one, off-campus. The offer is this: you can come live with me. Starting over Christmas break. You can leave her, and you can come and be safe with me. I will make sure you get to all of your CCHR classes. I will make sure you have the space and peace to get your homework done. I will be your rock, your support, your family, until it's time for you to go and start your real life with Chloé at NPU."

She squeezed his hand, her gaze intense. "There's a song by Kat Miles & The Resistors, 'Your Constant North,' you know the one. That's what I'll be for you, Alex. I will stand by you, every step of the way, to get you out of this. You just have to say the word."

The offer was a lifeline, a tangible, fully-formed escape hatch from the prison his life had become. It was everything he secretly wanted. He looked at his cousin, at her fierce, loving, determined face, and tears welled in his eyes. He was so grateful, so profoundly loved. But he also felt a deep, crushing sense of exhaustion. The thought of the confrontation, of the fight it would take to leave Vera, felt like an impossible, insurmountable mountain.

He didn't say yes. But he didn't say no. He just sat there, his hand in hers, and cried, a quiet, heartbreaking acknowledgment of a truth he was still too scared to act on. The escape plan was on the table. But for now, he was too trapped, too worn down, to pick it up.
