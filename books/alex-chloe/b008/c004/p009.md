# Part 9: An Early Start

The insistent blare of an alarm clock cut through the quiet darkness of Megan’s dorm room at precisely 5:30 AM. With a practiced groan, Megan rolled over and slapped the snooze button, silencing it. She lay there for a moment, then turned and gently shook her cousin’s shoulder.

"Good morning, Alex," she whispered. "Time to get up. We've got a big day."

The morning routine was a quiet dance of familiar, supportive choreography. Megan helped Alex with his morning needs, her movements efficient and completely devoid of the awkwardness or resentment he had grown used to. She helped him get dressed in a comfortable pair of jeans and a VDU sweatshirt she had bought for him the previous year. Once he was settled in his chair, she quickly got herself ready. By 6:10 AM, they were both ready to go, a full twenty minutes ahead of Megan’s self-imposed deadline to get from her apartment to the Sinclair Center for the 7:00 AM check-in.

The VDU campus was quiet at this early hour, the air cool and crisp. They made their way to the Sinclair University Center, the bustling hub for the day's events. A long table draped in a VDU banner was set up for check-in, staffed by cheerful student volunteers.

Megan wheeled Alex up to the table, her own smile bright and proud. "Good morning!" she said to the young woman holding a clipboard. "We're here for the Open House. I'm Megan Carter, a senior here," she explained, "and I'm showing my cousin, Alex Miller, around. He's a prospective student, and I thought the open house would be a great way for him to see the campus."

"That's awesome! Welcome to VDU, Alex," the volunteer said, handing him a welcome packet and a name tag. "The continental breakfast is set up just inside the ballroom. The opening remarks will start at eight, and the first campus tours leave right after that."

"Perfect, thank you," Megan said.

She led Alex into the large ballroom, where long tables were laden with pastries, fruit, and carafes of coffee and juice. It was a simple, normal college event, but for Alex, after weeks of neglect and instability with Vera, the simple act of being welcomed and offered a warm muffin felt like an incredible luxury. They filled their plates and found a small table, ready for the day's adventure to officially begin.
