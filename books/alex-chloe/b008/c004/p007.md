# Part 7: The Bedtime Routine

After the lifeline of the video call with Chloé, a quiet, more serious mood settled over the small apartment. Megan looked at Alex, her expression one of gentle, loving determination. "Alright, cuz," she said softly. "It's getting late, and we have an early start tomorrow. Time to get you ready for bed."

She led him into the bedroom and towards the accessible bathroom. This was a familiar routine, one they had navigated countless times during family vacations and childhood sleepovers. But tonight, it felt different. It felt charged with a new and unspoken significance.

Megan helped him with his toileting needs with a familiar, matter-of-fact ease. There was no hesitation, no awkwardness. For Alex, the simple, respectful assistance was a stark, painful contrast to Vera's constant refusal, her sighs of annoyance, and the shame she made him feel for his own body's basic needs.

"Okay," Megan said, turning on the water for the tub-shower combination. "Your turn. I'm going to give you a proper scrub-down. You look like you haven't had a real, hot bath in ages."

Her words were a simple observation, but they were also a quiet accusation against the person who was supposed to be caring for him. As she helped him into the tub, Alex felt a wave of profound relief. This was something Vera had refused to help with since their first week together, leaving him to struggle with difficult and incomplete sponge baths.

Megan's goal was clear. This was a mission of restorative care. She worked with a gentle but firm efficiency, her hands scrubbing his back, his arms, and his legs, washing away weeks of accumulated grime and, with it, a layer of the neglect that had settled over him like a shroud. When she washed his hair, she used her own shampoo. The scent—a clean, familiar fragrance of green apple and chamomile—hit Alex with the force of a memory. It was the same shampoo Chloé used. He closed his eyes, and for a blissful moment, the small, tiled bathroom in a VDU dorm felt like the safest place in the world, a perfect fusion of the two women who were his anchors.

Megan was thorough, her movements respectful and clinical as she washed his intimate areas, a necessary act of care she had performed for him many times before. She was determined to make sure he was truly, properly clean. Her touch was not just about hygiene; it was a deliberate, loving act designed to remind his autistic brain, his entire being, what safe, appropriate, and caring affection was supposed to feel like.

After she was done, she turned the water off. A quiet, steamy silence filled the room. Alex didn't make a move to get out. He just sat there, the clean, warm water a profound comfort. MEGAN? his device, resting on a dry towel on the counter, voiced softly. CAN YOU... JUST SIT WITH ME? FOR A MINUTE?

"Of course," she said, pulling a small stool over and sitting beside the tub.

He looked at her, his eyes full of a quiet, unspoken plea. He slowly extended his wet hand from the water. CAN I HOLD YOUR HAND?

A wave of profound, aching love washed over Megan. She reached out without a second's hesitation and took his hand, her warm, dry fingers lacing with his cool, wet ones. She gave his hand a firm, steady, reassuring squeeze.

He just sat there, holding her hand, thinking. He thought about Vera's coldness, her refusal to provide the most basic care. He thought about Chloé's love, so powerful it could cross a continent through a video screen. And he thought about Megan, his cousin, his friend, who was right here, her hand a solid, grounding anchor in the darkness. He was beginning to understand, with a terrifying and liberating clarity, that the life he was living was not the life he deserved.

After a few minutes, he gave her hand a gentle squeeze back and let go. She helped him out of the tub and dried him off with a thick, soft towel, her movements gentle and sure. After a quick shower for herself, she emerged from the bathroom. They got into their pajamas, and she reminded him of her earlier invitation.

"Come on," she said softly, gesturing to the bed. "I want you in here with me."
