# Part 16: Homework Sanctuary

After the adrenaline of the elevator rescue and the comfort of a good meal, the open house officially concluded. The crowds of prospective students and their families began to disperse. Megan, however, had one more stop in mind.

"I still have a ton of homework to do for my senior seminar," she explained as they left the Sinclair Center. "How about we go find a quiet corner in the library? It'll be a good way to decompress after... well, all of that."

The VDU library was a vast, modern building, its quiet, scholarly atmosphere a stark contrast to the boisterous energy of the open house. Just inside the entrance was a small, cozy coffee shop.

"My treat," Megan said, wheeling Alex up to the counter. "What can I get you?"

HOT CHOCOLATE, PLEASE, his device voiced. LARGE. WITH WHIPPED CREAM.

Megan smiled at the barista. "Two large hot chocolates with extra whipped cream, please. And can you make sure they're both made with whole milk?" she added, a deliberate, pointed emphasis in her voice.

A few minutes later, she handed Alex a steaming cup. The simple, decadent treat was a quiet act of rebellion. It was a direct, loving counterpoint to Vera's fabricated lactose intolerance, a rule she had forcibly extended to Alex, denying him the simple comfort of a glass of milk. This warm, rich, creamy drink was a taste of freedom.

They found a perfect table in a quiet section of the library, nestled conveniently next to a wall with a series of electrical outlets. They plugged in both her MagnaByte laptop and his Quantum Communicator XT, the two devices charging side-by-side.

"Okay, let's get you connected," Megan said, pulling Alex's device closer. He displayed his network settings screen, and she quickly and expertly typed in her own VDU student credentials, connecting his communicator to the high-speed university network.

For the first time in what felt like an eternity, Alex was able to open his CCHR homework files in a peaceful, public space without fear. There was no Vera looking over his shoulder, questioning his methods, interrupting his focus, or demanding his attention. There was just the quiet, companionable tapping of two keyboards, the occasional sip of hot chocolate, and the profound, wonderful sense of academic and emotional safety.

Megan, noticing his focused calm, leaned over. "You know, you could probably get a whole week's worth of homework done right now," she whispered with a grin. "I'll make you a deal. You work ahead, and afterward, I'll treat you to dinner at that new chicken tender place in Griffin Square."

Alex looked up from his screen, a look of pure, unadulterated contentment on his face. He was safe. He was productive. He was with his favorite person. He gave her a grateful nod and turned back to his work, feeling more like himself than he had in months.
