# Part 14: The Famous Fried Chicken

Once they were safely in the lobby of Jefferson Hall, surrounded by concerned-looking university officials, a team of paramedics approached them. "We'd like to check everyone out, just as a precaution," a calm, professional paramedic said.

One by one, the other members of the tour group were checked for signs of distress. When the paramedic came to Alex and Megan, they both agreed to a quick check of their vitals.

"Blood pressure is good, heart rate is a little elevated, but that's to be expected," the paramedic said after checking Alex. "Do you feel any pain? Dizziness? We can take you over to the ER to get fully checked out if you'd like."

Alex looked at Megan, who already knew his answer. I AM FINE, his device voiced. NO HOSPITAL. THANK YOU.

"Same for me," Megan added. "We're both okay. Just a little shaken up."

The other family from their tour group, thoroughly spooked by the ordeal, decided to call it a day and head home. The RA, Kevin, was busy giving a detailed report to a housing official. For a moment, Alex and Megan were left alone in the now-quiet lobby. The adrenaline was wearing off, replaced by a deep, bone-weary exhaustion and a powerful hunger.

Megan looked at Alex, a wry, tired smile on her face. "Well," she said, "I don't know about you, but after being trapped in a metal box for an hour, I could really use some lunch."

The residential tour had been derailed, but the open house lunch, located in the very same dormitory's cafeteria, was still in full swing. As they walked in, they were greeted by the most incredible, mouth-watering aroma of perfectly seasoned fried chicken.

The cafeteria manager, a large, jovial man named Mr. Henderson whom the students all called "Hendy," was a campus legend. His fried chicken, served only on special occasions, was famous throughout the university. Today, in a gesture of hospitality for the open house, he had prepared a massive batch.

They filled their plates with crispy, golden-brown chicken, mashed potatoes, and green beans. They found a quiet table in the corner, away from the main crowd. For a few minutes, they just ate, the simple, delicious, comforting food a perfect antidote to the stress of the morning.

"Okay," Megan said after a few bites, a bit of her good humor returning. "I have to admit, almost falling ten stories is a small price to pay for Hendy's fried chicken."

Alex, who was meticulously de-boning a chicken thigh, just nodded, a look of profound, simple contentment on his face. The weekend had been a rollercoaster of high-stakes confrontations, emotional revelations, and literal high-wire rescues. But right now, in this moment, sitting in a noisy cafeteria next to his favorite cousin, eating the best fried chicken he'd ever had, he felt completely and utterly normal. And it was the best feeling in the world.
