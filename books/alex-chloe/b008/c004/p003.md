# Part 3: A Safe Harbor

The drive to Virginia Dominion University was a peaceful, liberating journey. The tension of the confrontation with Vera slowly dissipated, replaced by the easy, familiar comfort of just being with Megan. When they arrived at her apartment-style dorm, Dominion Commons, she parked in a visitor spot and led him to the main front office.

Inside, she checked him in with the resident advisor on duty, officially documenting him as her overnight guest for the weekend. Once the paperwork was done, they exited the office and walked around the outside of the brick building to a quieter, residential entrance that was closer to her unit. The building had no central lobby, just long, anonymous hallways.

Megan unlocked her door and pushed it open. "Welcome to my humble abode," she said with a grin.

The apartment was small but cozy and immaculately clean. The front door opened directly into a combined kitchen and dining area. A small but fully equipped kitchen, complete with a stove, microwave, and a full-sized dishwasher, was on the right. Straight ahead was a comfortable living room. To the left of the living room was the door to her bedroom.

"It's not much, but it's home," she said. "Bedroom's in there, and the bathroom is inside the bedroom to the left."

Alex set his overnight bag down, his gaze taking in the space. He immediately needed to use the restroom after the long drive. Megan, seeing the look on his face, understood instantly.

"Come on," she said, her tone the same matter-of-fact, comforting one she'd used his whole life. "Let's get you sorted."

She helped him into the bathroom, her assistance with his toileting needs as familiar and easy as it had been a hundred times before. There was no awkwardness, no hesitation. As she was helping him, a powerful, painful thought struck Alex with the force of a physical blow: *Vera would never do this.* She always refused, making him feel ashamed and helpless. But here was Megan—and his mom, his dad, Chloé—all the people who truly loved him, who never saw these simple, necessary acts of care as a burden, but as a normal part of his life. The contrast was a stark, bright line drawn in his mind.

When they came back out into the living room, Megan gestured to the kitchen. "Are you hungry? I can make us some dinner. A real, home-cooked meal."

YES, his Quantum Communicator XT voiced, the word full of a quiet, profound longing.

Another realization hit him. He couldn't remember the last time he'd had a real meal that wasn't from a fast-food bag or cobbled together from the meager, processed scraps in Vera's kitchen, with the single exception of the one meal her mother had cooked.

"Excellent," Megan said, already pulling a pan from a cupboard. "How does chicken parmesan with pasta sound? It's cheap, it's easy, and it's my specialty."

Alex just nodded, a wave of gratitude washing over him. He sat at her small dining table and watched as she expertly breaded the chicken, the simple, domestic sounds of a real meal being prepared a healing balm to his frayed nerves. He was safe. He was cared for. And he was about to have a home-cooked meal, a simple act of love that felt like the most profound luxury in the world.
