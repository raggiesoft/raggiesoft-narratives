# Part 2: A Dinner of Dissent

An hour later, the five of them were gathered around the Millers' dining table. In the center of the table, on a small stand, the MagnaByte laptop was open, Chloé's smiling face a warm, digital presence. She had her own small meal, a simple salad, ready to eat alongside them from her dorm room three thousand miles away.

The main event arrived with a flourish. Sophie and Emily emerged from the kitchen, carrying a massive platter. It was a beautiful, authentic poutine—a mountain of golden-brown french fries, dotted with fresh, squeaky cheese curds, all smothered in a rich, dark gravy.

POUTINE! Alex's Quantum Communicator XT blared, its synthesized voice full of pure, unadulterated joy.

Thomas Mason's face lit up. "*Ah, magnifique!*" he declared.

Vera, however, just stared at the platter. She remembered Chloé's excited question from the video call: "*Did you use the good cheese curds? The ones we brought back from Stanstead?*" This wasn't just dinner; this was *Chloé food*. It was a taste of Québec, a symbol of the deep, shared history that connected this entire family, a history she was not a part of.

Emily, a gracious hostess, served everyone a generous portion. When she got to Vera, she offered the serving spoon with a polite smile. "Vera, dear? You have to try some. It's a family favorite."

Vera's own smile was a mask of polite, theatrical regret. "Oh, it looks... wonderful," she said, her voice dripping with a false sincerity. "But I couldn't possibly. All that grease and gravy... my stomach is a little sensitive. I think I'll just have a small salad, if that's okay."

The refusal, as polite as it was, landed on the table with the force of a thrown gauntlet. Alex, who was already halfway through his first, delicious bite, stopped, a flicker of confusion on his face. The four parents and Chloé, on the screen, all exchanged a single, knowing look. They saw the move for what it was: a quiet, passive-aggressive act of protest.

Vera watched, her own internal monologue a bitter, venomous stream. *Look at him. Shoveling in that disgusting, foreign food. He loves it. He loves her food. He loves her.* She forced herself to maintain her polite facade. The memory of the late-night rescue, of Megan Carter's cold, dismissive fury, was still fresh in her mind. She would not make another tactical error. She would be the perfect, polite guest, even as she seethed on the inside.

The family, for their part, continued their meal, their conversation warm and bilingual. The parents' quiet, loving attempt to drive Vera away with a taste of their shared culture had not worked, not exactly. She was still here. But her refusal to partake in their family's comfort food had drawn a new, clearer line in the sand. It was a quiet, delicious, and deeply symbolic battle, and Vera, with her polite refusal and her bowl of plain lettuce, had just decisively lost.
