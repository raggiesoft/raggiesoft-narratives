# Part 5: A Lifeline from Lynnwood

The constant interruptions and the growing strain of Vera's presence were taking their toll on Alex. He missed the easy, uninterrupted conversations with Chloé with an ache that was a constant, physical presence. He knew he needed to end things with Vera, but the thought of the confrontation, of her anger, and of the subsequent, crushing loneliness, was paralyzing.

One afternoon, he worked up the courage for a small act of rebellion. He told Vera he needed to stay home on Lisa Court to work on a major project for his programming class, a plausible excuse that she reluctantly accepted. The moment he was alone in his own living room, the sense of peace was so profound it almost made him dizzy. He looked at the clock. It was time for his call with Chloé.

When her face appeared on the screen, bright and beloved, he felt a wave of relief so intense it was almost painful. For the first time in weeks, there was no Vera hovering, no threat of a sudden interruption.

"Hey, Alex!" she said, her smile warm but her eyes full of a quiet concern. "It's so good to see you. Really see you. How are you?"

He typed, his fingers flying, the words a torrent of pent-up frustration and fear. I HATE THIS, CHLOÉ. I HATE IT. VERA... SHE'S... TOO MUCH. I DON'T KNOW WHAT TO DO.

Chloé’s expression softened with a deep, aching empathy. She had been waiting for this, hoping he would finally feel safe enough to tell her the truth. "I know, Alex," she said softly. "I can see it. She's trying to cut you off from everyone."

She took a deep breath, then a new, determined light entered her eyes. "Alex," she began, "I have something to show you. Something I've been working on. I was waiting for the right time, but I think the right time is now."

She shared her screen. A moment later, a familiar webpage appeared: the official registration portal for the Community College of Hampton Roads. And on the screen was her official class schedule for the Spring 2004 semester.

Alex stared, his heart stopping for a beat. English Composition. Calculus I. General Biology with Lab. All at the Virginia Beach campus.

CHLOÉ... WHAT IS THIS? he typed, his fingers trembling.

"It's my new plan," she said, her voice full of a quiet, fierce conviction. "My dream school isn't a place, Alex. It's you. I can't do this for two years. I'm coming home." She pointed to the screen. "I've already been accepted. I've already registered. And I picked these classes because I know you'll need to take them too. Here," she said, bringing up a text document, "are the Class ID numbers. If you want... we can have the same schedule. Just like in high school."

Alex stared at the screen, at the tangible, undeniable proof of her plan, of her unwavering commitment to him. It was a lifeline, a rescue mission, a future he hadn't dared to hope for, delivered through a video screen. The fear that had been paralyzing him, the fear of being alone, was instantly obliterated by the fierce, powerful truth of her love. He wasn't going to be alone. Chloé was coming home.
