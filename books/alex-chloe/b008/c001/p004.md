# Part 4: The Promise (Late August 2003)

Later, lying beside Vera in the dim light of the bedroom, Alex felt a blush creep from his neck to his ears. A profound sense of warmth, vulnerability, and an almost dizzying newness suffused him. The intensity was different, so much faster and more direct than the gentle, slow unfolding of intimacy he had shared with Chloé. With Vera, this was only their second meeting.

That thought—*years with Chloé versus two meetings with Vera*—flickered at the edge of his mind, a brief, unsettling whisper. It felt… incredibly fast. Almost too fast. But he quickly, forcefully, pushed the thought aside. Chloé was gone. The crushing loneliness of the past day was a pain he couldn't bear to feel again. Vera was here, she was real, and this thrilling new connection was a potent shield against the emptiness. He focused on that, on the feeling of being wanted.

He carefully reached for his AAC device on the nightstand. "VERA," his device whispered into the quiet room. "THAT WAS… I… I’VE NEVER FELT QUITE LIKE THAT BEFORE."

She stirred, pressing a soft, sleepy kiss to his shoulder. "Shhh," she murmured. "It was amazing, wasn't it?"

YES, he typed, the word emphatic, a conscious choice to focus on the pleasure and ignore the fleeting comparison. I… I WANT MORE OF THIS. WITH YOU.

Vera chuckled, a low, satisfied sound. She propped herself up on an elbow, looking down at him, her eyes glittering. "Oh, Alex," she said, tracing a finger along his lips. "There will be plenty more where that came from. This is just the beginning for us." She kissed him again, deeply.

Then, she glanced at the clock on her nightstand. "As much as I'd love to spend the rest of the day right here, my mom will be home in a couple of hours," she said, her tone shifting to one of practical secrecy. "This was our private time, you know? Just for us."

A small knot of disappointment formed in Alex's chest, but he accepted her reasoning. This secret, intense connection felt precious.

"So," Vera continued, already moving to find her clothes, "you should probably head home soon. But don't worry," she added, leaning down to give him another lingering kiss. "We'll do this again. Very soon. This is our special thing."

The promise hung in the air as Alex got dressed, his mind still reeling. Vera saw him to the door, another quick, passionate kiss sealing her words.

Heading home, the familiar streets seemed different. He was blushing, exhilarated, and completely smitten. The gaping, terrifying void left by Chloé's departure was still there, but Vera's promises—"plenty more," "just the beginning," "very soon"—were like the first, desperately needed shovels of earth being used to fill it in. The unsettling thought about the speed of it all was deliberately kept at bay, overshadowed by the intoxicating promise of not being alone anymore.
