# Part 3: Coffee, and an Open Wound (Late August 2003)

The student union buzzed with a low hum of conversations, a sound that felt abrasive and meaningless to Alex. He sat opposite Vera at a small table, a fruity "Refresher" in front of him, its sweetness lost on him. Vera, with her steaming mug, leaned forward, her attention a focused, intense beam that managed to cut through the fog of his grief.

She had listened intently as he haltingly described his work on predictive text algorithms. He was going through the motions, talking about his passions because it was what he was supposed to do, but every explanation felt hollow without Chloé there to share in his excitement. Vera, however, was a masterful audience.

"It's just brilliant, Alex," she'd said, her eyes shining with what he desperately wanted to believe was admiration. "The way your mind works is truly fascinating."

Now, there was a pause. Alex felt a flicker of warmth, a brief respite from the cold loneliness that had been his constant companion since Chloé's plane had left the ground. Vera sighed contentedly.

"I've really enjoyed this, Alex," she said. "It's so rare to find someone you can have a real conversation with." She glanced at her watch. "Look, this is a bit spontaneous, but my afternoon is free. I'd love to continue this conversation without all the..." she gestured vaguely at the noisy union, "...distraction. Would you consider coming over to my place in Portsmouth? It's quiet. We could just relax."

Portsmouth. A drive. Being alone with someone new. A year ago, a week ago, he would have politely declined. But the thought of leaving this conversation, of returning to his empty house where every corner screamed Chloé's absence, was a physical pain. Vera was offering him an extension, a way to keep the crushing loneliness at bay for a few more hours. The phrase that had been echoing in his thoughts—*it's nice to have someone in his life again*—solidified into a desperate need.

He typed on his Quantum Communicator XT: THAT SOUNDS… REALLY NICE, VERA. YES. I’D LIKE THAT.

Vera's smile was radiant. "Wonderful! My car is just parked out back."

The drive in her older Holt Automotive sedan was filled with more easy conversation. Her apartment was small but tidy. "Make yourself comfortable, Alex," she said. "Can I get you another Refresher?"

He opted for water. As she went to the kitchen, he took in the room, his heart thrumming with a nervous excitement. She returned and, instead of sitting opposite him, she sat on the sofa quite close, turning to face him.

"So," she began, her voice a little softer now, her gaze direct and warm. "You were saying..." But her words trailed off. "You know, Alex," she said, her voice barely above a whisper, "you really are quite something."

She leaned in, and her lips were on his. It was a firm, confident kiss that quickly deepened. Alex’s mind went blank. This was new. Overwhelming. It wasn't the gentle, exploratory curiosity he had shared with Chloé; this was something else entirely, a current that was pulling him under. A cascade of unfamiliar sensations washed over him, and in his desperate need for connection, he found himself responding.

When she finally pulled back slightly, her eyes were dark and searching. "Wow," she breathed. Alex was speechless, his face burning. He fumbled for his AAC.

Vera chuckled softly, a low, intimate sound. "No need for words just yet." She took his hand. "Come on," she whispered, her gaze flicking towards a doorway he presumed led to a bedroom. "Let’s go somewhere more comfortable."

A wave of nervousness hit Alex. This was moving so fast. But Vera’s touch was confident, her eyes so inviting. And the alternative—leaving, going home, being alone with the crushing weight of his missing best friend—was an unbearable thought. *I don't want to be alone.* The thought was a desperate, silent scream. He wanted this. He needed this connection, this distraction from the pain.

He gave a small, shy nod.

Vera’s smile widened. She stood, guiding him as he maneuvered his wheelchair towards the bedroom. She closed the door, creating an intimate cocoon. She sat on the edge of the bed, then patted the space beside her. "Alex," she said softly, her voice a caress. "You're so special. I feel such a connection with you." She reached out, her fingers tracing the line of his jaw. "I want to be closer to you, Alex. Much closer. Do you... do you feel that too?" Her eyes were locked on his, her unspoken desire a tangible thing in the room.

He knew what she was asking. And in that moment, all he knew was that she was here, and Chloé wasn't.
