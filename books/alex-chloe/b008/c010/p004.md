# Part 4: A New Normal

The drive back from the doctor's office was a heavy, thoughtful silence. The seven of them gathered in the Millers' living room, the grim reality of Dr. Adams's words a tangible presence. After the profound, unified declaration that Alex's new normal required both Chloé and Megan, the practical question of logistics arose.

"Okay," Emily said, her voice a calm, steady anchor in the emotional storm. "Then this is our new reality. And it starts tonight."

The question of sleeping arrangements was a complex one. The pull-out sofa bed had been a perfect, one-night emergency solution, but setting it up and taking it down every single day was impractical.

Alex, who had been listening intently, looked around the room, at his family, at his two protectors. He knew what he wanted. He wanted his own room. His own bed. It was the one space in his life, his one true sanctuary, that had never, ever been tainted by Vera's presence.

He typed on his Quantum Communicator XT, his decision clear and firm.

I WANT MY ROOM. MY BED.

Chloé, understanding him perfectly, picked up the thought. "He's right. His room is his safe space." She then looked at Megan. "But we can't all fit..."

Megan, however, had already solved the problem in her head. "It's fine," she said, her tone leaving no room for argument. "You two take Alex's room. I'll take the guest room down the hall. It's perfect. We're all in the same house, under the same roof. That's what matters."

It was the perfect, loving, and logical solution. Chloé would have, of course, offered to stay in her old room at the Mason house next door, but they all knew that was a non-starter. Alex did not want to be separated from Megan, and there was no spare room for her at the Masons'. The Miller house was now, officially, their base of operations.

Later that night, a new, more peaceful routine began. Megan settled into the guest room, a temporary but welcome haven. And in Alex's familiar bedroom, Chloé opened the bottom drawer of his dresser, the one that had been her "satellite locker" all through high school, and began to put her own things inside.

She didn't need her old room next door. She would never want to be there without Alex. Her home, her sanctuary, was wherever he was. And with Megan just down the hall, their entire, unbreakable team was together, ready to face the long, difficult, but beautiful work of healing, together.
