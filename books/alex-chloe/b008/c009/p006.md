# Part 6: Magna Cum Laude

The day of Megan Carter’s commencement ceremony was Alex’s first venture out of the protective bubble of Lisa Court since his return from Washington. He was still profoundly weak, his body still reeling from the trauma of his ordeal, but he was absolutely, unshakeably determined to be there. This was for Megan.

The Dominion Convocation Center at VDU was a cavernous space, echoing with the excited chatter of thousands of people. The family had arrived early to secure accessible seating. All eight of her allotted guests were there: Alex and Chloé, James and Emily, Megan’s proud parents, her younger sister, Cassie, and their grandmother.

Alex, wrapped in a warm blanket despite the indoor heating, was already looking pale, the effort of just being there a visible strain. Chloé sat beside him, a calm, steadying presence, her hand a constant, reassuring weight on his arm. She saw him subtly adjust the high-fidelity earplugs in his ears, a necessary buffer against the overwhelming wall of sound.

The ceremony began. The low, powerful notes of an organ filled the arena, and the procession of graduates began to file in, to the stately, familiar tune of "Pomp and Circumstance."

The music hit Alex like a physical force. It wasn't just the sound; it was the overwhelming tidal wave of emotion. The grandeur of the song, the sight of his entire loving family gathered together, the profound, gut-wrenching relief of being home, of being safe from Vera, of having his two most favorite women in the world right here at his side—it was all too much for his frayed nerves and exhausted body.

His carefully constructed composure began to fray. A tremor started in his hands. He felt his breathing hitch, the sheer, crushing weight of his own safety and happiness becoming a sensory overload. He didn't want to ruin this for Megan. He fought to hold it together, his focus blurring.

Chloé saw it instantly. She didn't make a scene. She just reached over and placed a firm, grounding hand on his knee, her touch a silent, powerful anchor. She leaned in close, her lips brushing his ear, and whispered in the soft, safe cadence of their shared Québécois French.

"*Respirez, mon amour. Je suis là,*" she murmured, her voice a private current in the ocean of noise. "*C'est juste de la joie. Laisse-la entrer. Je te tiens.*" (Breathe, my love. I am here. It's just joy. Let it in. I've got you.)

Her voice, her touch, her language—it was the perfect combination. The roaring in his head subsided. The tremor in his hands eased. He took a long, shuddering breath and found his focus again, just as the dean began to announce the graduates from the College of Arts and Letters.

When Megan's name was called, a proud, booming announcement echoed through the hall: "Megan Elizabeth Carter, Magna Cum Laude!"

Their family erupted in a joyous, tearful cheer. Alex, now fully present, watched with a look of pure, unadulterated pride as his cousin, his first and truest friend, walked across the stage to receive her diploma. He reached for his Quantum Note, his fingers moving with a new steadiness, and showed the screen to Chloé.

SHE DID IT.

Chloé squeezed his knee, her own eyes shining. "She sure did."

The rest of the ceremony passed in a happy, peaceful blur. Alex had weathered the storm. He had been overwhelmed not by fear or by trauma, but by joy, and for the first time, he had been able to ride the wave instead of being pulled under. He had shown up for his cousin, and that was all that mattered.
