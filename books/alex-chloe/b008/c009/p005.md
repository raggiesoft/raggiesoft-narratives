# Part 5: Sanctuary

They landed in Norfolk late in the evening. The familiar, humid Virginia air, even in winter, felt like a welcoming blanket. The deplaning process was slow, but the quiet, smaller scale of ORF was a blessed relief after the day's long journey.

The drive back to Lisa Court was filled with a quiet, contented silence. They pulled into the familiar driveway of the Miller house. Home.

As they got inside, a profound, bone-deep weariness, the culmination of months of trauma and stress, finally settled over Alex. He looked at his familiar living room, at his parents, at the Masons, at Chloé, and at Megan. He was safe.

He reached for his AAC, his fingers moving with a slow, deliberate purpose.

I NEED MY BED.

It was a simple statement, but it was laden with a world of meaning. He didn't just want to rest; he wanted the ultimate sanctuary. But as he looked at his familiar room, a new, cold spike of fear lanced through him. His room was at the front of the house. The window faced the street. A dark, irrational terror gripped him: *Vera. What if she comes in the middle of the night? What if she tries to take me again?*

He looked from Chloé to Megan, his eyes wide with a silent, desperate plea. He needed them both. He needed them to be his guards, his fortress.

Emily Miller’s eyes filled with tears, and she nodded, her expression full of a deep, maternal understanding. She knew what he was asking. They all did.

A few minutes later, the familiar evening routine began. Chloé and Megan worked as a seamless team, helping him into his accessible bathroom. After he was in his pajamas, they went to his room. Chloé and Megan exchanged a look, a silent, perfect understanding passing between them.

Megan was the first to address the unspoken problem. "Cuz," she said gently, looking at the full-sized bed. "I don't think all three of us are going to fit in there very comfortably." She then offered a loving, practical solution. "But... the pull-out sofa bed in the living room is a queen. It's in the back of the house, away from the street. Why don't the three of us have a proper sleepover out there tonight? We can build a nest. It'll be safe."

Chloé, understanding Alex’s unspoken fear and Megan's brilliant solution, immediately agreed. "That's a great idea. The ultimate slumber party."

And so, it was settled. They pulled out the sofa bed, piling it high with pillows and warm blankets. They helped Alex get settled in the middle. Then, Chloé climbed in on one side, and Megan climbed in on the other.

It wasn't awkward. It wasn't strange. It was a fortress. It was the physical manifestation of the promise they had all made: to surround him with a wall of unwavering love and safety.

He turned on his side towards Chloé, and she met him, wrapping her arms around him in a warm, protective embrace. He buried his face in her shoulder, breathing in the familiar, safe scent of her. A moment later, he felt Megan's arm come to rest across his back, her hand a steady, grounding presence.

He was flanked, held, and protected on all sides by the two most important women in his life. The sheer, overwhelming feeling of being so completely loved, so utterly secure, was the most powerful medicine in the world. The terror of the night receded, replaced by a deep, profound calm. The long, terrifying journey was finally, truly, over. He was home. He was safe. And he knew, with a certainty that settled deep in his soul, that he would never be alone again.
