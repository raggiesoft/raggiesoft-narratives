# Part 4: A Turn for the Worse

The first night was a long, tense vigil. James and Emily rotated in and out of Alex's room, their faces growing more worried with each update they gave to the Masons and a heartbroken Chloé in the waiting area.

"He's so scared," Emily whispered, her own eyes red-rimmed. "He keeps asking for you, Chloé. He keeps typing your name."

By the next morning, Alex's condition had worsened. The pneumonia was aggressive, and his already compromised respiratory system was struggling. The doctor's updates became more grave. He was in respiratory distress. They were increasing his oxygen and starting more aggressive antibiotics.

The waiting room had become Chloé's prison. She felt helpless, useless, her entire being thrumming with the desperate need to be by his side.

Late that afternoon, a charge nurse came out to speak with James and Emily. "Mr. and Mrs. Miller, Alex's heart rate and blood pressure are elevated. He's very agitated, which is making it harder for him to breathe. We're doing everything we can to keep him calm, but he's fighting us. He's... he's asking for someone named Chloé."

This was the breaking point. James Miller, his face a mask of desperate resolve, turned to the nurse. "That's her," he said, gesturing to Chloé. "She's his best friend. His... his anchor. For his autism, his anxiety... her presence is a medical necessity for him. I know what the rules say, but my son is in distress, and he needs her. She knows him, his health, his cues, as well as we do."

Emily added her own tearful, desperate plea. "Please. If his agitation is making it harder for him to breathe... then keeping her out is actively harming him. She's not just a visitor. She's... she's part of his support system."

The charge nurse looked from the parents' desperate faces to the pale, determined fifteen-year-old girl sitting with them. She saw the profound, unspoken truth of their words. She sighed, a small sound of a professional bending a rigid rule in the face of a greater need.

"Okay," she said, her voice quiet but firm. "One visitor. For fifteen minutes. She must be accompanied by one of you. And she does not leave his side. Let's see if it helps."
