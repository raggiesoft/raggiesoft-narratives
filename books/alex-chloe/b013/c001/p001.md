# Part 1: The Matchup is Set (February 26, 2010)

The living room of the Leclerc house in Stanstead was a sanctuary of nervous, hopeful energy. It was Megan Carter's second trip to Canada, and she was getting a full immersion into the most sacred of Québécois traditions: hockey. The entire extended family—Alex, Chloé, her parents, the Leclercs, and Megan—were gathered to watch the men's hockey semi-final games of the 2010 Vancouver Winter Games.

The first game, USA vs. Finland, was a decisive American victory. Alex and Megan, a two-person island of patriotic pride, watched with a quiet, intense focus. When the final horn sounded, they exchanged a triumphant, fist-bumping "Go USA!" The rest of the room offered polite, reserved applause; they were impressed, but they were saving their true passion for the main event.

As the second game—Canada vs. Slovakia—began, the atmosphere in the room shifted, becoming electric. Every pass, every shot, every save was met with a collective gasp or a roar of encouragement. The final minutes were agonizing, but the Canadian squad held on for a nail-biting 3-2 win.

The moment the game ended, the living room erupted in a cheer of pure, unadulterated relief and joy. But that joy quickly morphed into something else: a dawning, electrifying realization.

"*Mon Dieu,*" Aunt Geneviève breathed, her hands clasped together. "*C'est ça. C'est la finale de rêve.*" (My God. This is it. It's the dream final.)

Canada versus the United States. For all the glory!

In the midst of the celebration, Uncle David Leclerc, a man of action, had already grabbed the phone from the kitchen wall. "I have to call Renaud, right now!" he announced.

He dialed, his expression urgent. "Renaud, c'est David! Écoute, la finale, c'est Canada-États-Unis! Il me faut la grande table dans le coin, dimanche. Pour toute la gang!" (Renaud, it's David! Listen, the final is Canada-USA! I need the big corner table on Sunday. For the whole gang!)

He listened for a moment, a grin spreading across his face. "Excellent! Maintenant, juste pour te prévenir... on amène deux Américains avec nous! Oui, le mari de ma nièce, et sa cousine. Des fans des Brawlers! On va leur montrer comment les vrais champions célèbrent! Assure-toi que tes gars sont gentils, hein? C'est de la famille." (Translation for the reader: "Excellent! Now, just to warn you... we're bringing two Americans with us! Yes, my niece's husband, and his cousin. Brawlers fans! We're going to show them how real champions celebrate! Make sure your guys are nice, eh? They're family.")

David let out a booming laugh at whatever Renaud said on the other end, then hung up the phone. He turned to the room, his face a mask of pure triumph.

"It is done!" he declared. "We have the corner booth at La Taverne des Voyageurs for Sunday afternoon. The battleground is secured!"

Alex looked at Megan, and they exchanged a look of quiet, determined solidarity. They were officially behind enemy lines. Chloé, who was already giving them both a look that was equal parts love and a merciless, competitive fire, just grinned. The friendly family visit had just been elevated. They were now heading into a diplomatic incident, and she had two of her favorite "uncivilized Americans" to vanquish.
