# Part 5: Setback Two - The Catastrophe

Weeks later, the infection was finally cleared. Chloé, now more than two months into her recovery, was finally given the green light to begin partial weight-bearing physical therapy. She went into her first session full of a desperate, fragile hope. Alex and Megan were both there with her, a silent, two-person cheering section, their own faces a mixture of nervous excitement and unwavering support.

The reality was brutal. Her ankle was incredibly stiff, the muscles in her leg had atrophied, and the first attempt to put even a little weight on it sent a sharp, terrifying pain through her foot. The physical therapist was patient, guiding her through the simple exercises at the parallel bars.

"Just a little more, Chloé," the therapist encouraged. "You're doing great. Alex and Megan are right here for you."

Gritting her teeth, Chloé tried to take another small, supported step. But her weakened leg trembled, her ankle buckled, and in a split second of instability, she stumbled. She reached for the parallel bars, but her grip slipped. She went down.

The sound of her own horrified scream was followed by a sickening, familiar crack. The pain was immediate, white-hot, and a thousand times worse than the first time.

For a half-second, the entire room was frozen in a tableau of pure horror. Then, Alex and Megan were in motion. Megan was on the floor beside Chloé in an instant, her face ashen. Alex was frantically getting the attention of the therapist, his Quantum Communicator Pro V already preparing a message, a raw, guttural sound of pure terror escaping him.

Chloé had fallen. She had re-broken the same ankle. And the three of them were now facing a new, even more devastating chapter in her long, painful recovery.
