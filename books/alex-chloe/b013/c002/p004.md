# Part 4: Setback One - The Infection

The first few weeks of recovery were a frustrating grind. Alex was a whirlwind of focused, adaptive energy, taking over all the household chores and acting as Chloé's unwavering logistical support. Megan, for her part, was the evening and weekend reinforcement, her calm, steady presence a comforting constant. But about three weeks after her surgery, a new complication arose.

"Hey, guys," Chloé said one evening, her voice tight with a pain that was different from the deep ache of a healing bone. "My ankle is killing me. It's hot, and it's throbbing."

Alex was beside her in an instant, his face a mask of concern. Megan, who was sitting nearby, came over to take a look. She gently placed the back of her hand near the incision site, her expression turning serious.

"She's right, Alex," Megan said, her voice low and worried. "It feels really warm. Warmer than it should. I think we need to call the doctor's after-hours line."

A trip back to the doctor the next morning confirmed their fears: a post-surgical infection had set in around the incision site. The news was a major blow. It meant a course of powerful antibiotics that left Chloé feeling nauseous and even more fatigued. More importantly, it delayed the start of her physical therapy, pushing her recovery timeline back by several weeks.

The light at the end of the tunnel had just gotten much, much further away, and now the three of them had to face a new, dispiriting battle together.
