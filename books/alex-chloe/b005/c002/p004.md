# Part 4: MEGAN!!!! (The Chaperone Arrives)

Wednesday afternoon brought the moment Alex had been most anticipating all week. Just as planned, Megan Carter's familiar Holt sedan pulled into the Miller driveway. She had come straight from her last class at VDU, a duffel bag and a stack of textbooks in her arms.

The moment she stepped inside, the quiet, domestic energy of the house shifted, becoming brighter and more complete. Alex met her in the entryway, his face alight with a pure, uncomplicated joy.

"Hey, cuz!" she said, dropping her bags and immediately pulling him into a big, familiar hug. "Survive your first few days as masters of the universe?"

IT HAS BEEN QUIET, his device voiced. TOO QUIET.

"I can fix that," she laughed.

They showed her the remnants of their lasagna experiment and the DVD evidence of their bad movie marathon. Then, Alex and Chloé led her to the home office, where the real mission for her visit was laid out on the desk.

"Whoa," Megan said, her eyes widening as she saw the official Quantum OS XN Professional CD-ROM. "You guys are really doing it. The big one."

"We've been doing all the prep work," Chloé explained, "backing up all his files. But we were waiting for you to be here, just in case something goes wrong."

"And because my main device will be offline for a few hours," Alex's Quantum Communicator XT added. I WILL HAVE NO VOICE.

Megan didn't hesitate. She immediately unzipped her own backpack and pulled out her sleek MagnaByte laptop. "Problem solved," she said, placing it on the desk next to his communicator. "It doesn't have your fancy software, but it's got MagnaWorks Suite. You can type in PageWrite, and we can be your voice. The three of us are a team."

For the next three hours, the office was a hub of high-stakes, collaborative focus. Alex, the lead surgeon, performed the delicate digital operation. Chloé and Megan acted as his nurses, reading steps from the manual and handing him disks. For communication, Alex would type his thoughts into a blank PageWrite document, and one of the two women would read his words aloud, their voices a seamless, collaborative extension of his own.

Part of the complex installation involved a special, secondary disc. Megan picked it up, her expression curious. "What's this one?"

Alex started to type the explanation on her laptop but stopped, a look of mild frustration on his face. He was trying to type the word "Québécois" and realized he didn't know how to create the "é" on this foreign machine. He looked at Megan, a silent question in his eyes.

Megan, seeing him stalled, leaned over. "Oh, you need an accent?" she asked. "On a MagnaByte, it's super easy. You don't have to change any settings. Just hold down the Option key."

She pointed to the key next to the Ctrl key. "Hold down Option and press e at the same time, then let go and press e again."

Alex did. A perfect é appeared on the screen. His eyes widened in genuine, technical appreciation.

THAT's... ELEGANT, he typed, this time using the new trick. ON QUANTUM OS, I HAVE TO INSTALL THE ENTIRE US-INTERNATIONAL KEYBOARD LAYOUT TO DO THAT.

Megan grinned. "Yeah, MagnaByte has always been good with that creative stuff. For a 'ç', it's just Option + c."

He tried it. A ç appeared. A slow, impressed smile spread across his face. He was a Quantum OS power user through and through, but in that moment, he felt a profound respect for the simple, intuitive design philosophy of this other technological world.

He finished his original thought for Megan to read aloud. "That is the French (Canada) MUI pack. My parents had to jump through a lot of hoops with the vendor to legally acquire it. It will let me install the Québécois voice synthesizer on the new OS."

Megan looked at him, a slow, proud smile spreading across her face. Of course. Even in a complex software upgrade, his connection to Chloé was a central part of the plan.

After a tense, final reboot, the communicator's screen flickered to life with the sleek, new interface of Quantum OS XN. It was a success. Their celebration was a triumphant, three-person group hug.

That evening, they celebrated by cooking dinner together—a simple but delicious meal of spaghetti and garlic bread. Later, as bedtime approached, Chloé, exhausted from a day of intense focus, announced she was going to take a quick shower in the main bathroom.

This left Alex and Megan with a quiet moment. "Alright, cuz," Megan said gently. "Your turn. Let's get you ready for bed."

She helped him into his own accessible bathroom, the routine familiar and comfortable. With the noisy bathroom fan turned on for privacy, creating a small, isolated bubble for the two of them, Megan’s mission of gentle therapy continued. As she was helping him with his evening routine, she asked a question, her voice soft and full of a genuine, loving curiosity.

"So," she began, "how are you, really? With her? With Chloé?"

Alex, who was sitting on the shower chair, looked at his cousin. He trusted her completely. He knew he could be open and honest with her in a way he couldn't be with almost anyone else. He reached for his Quantum Note, which was resting on the counter.

SHE IS... MY FAVORITE PERSON, he typed. I AM HAPPIEST WHEN I AM WITH HER. I LIKE SHARING MY LIFE WITH HER. IT DOESN'T FEEL LONELY ANYMORE.

Megan read the words, her heart swelling. He was describing, in the purest and most uncomplicated terms, the very definition of a high school sweetheart.

"I'm really glad, Alex," she said softly. "You deserve to be happy. She's a wonderful girl."

Alex watched her, a slow, mischievous grin spreading across his face. He typed one more, crucial sentence.

OF COURSE... YOU'RE STILL MY \*MOST\* FAVORITE.

Megan’s breath hitched for a half-second, and then she let out a soft, watery laugh, her own eyes shining. She leaned in and gave him a fierce, loving, one-armed hug. "You're a brat, you know that?" she whispered, her voice thick with emotion. "And I love you too, cuz."

After his bath, they returned to his room just as Chloé, fresh from her own shower and in her pajamas, was turning down the covers on his bed. 

"Alright, you two," Megan said, a warm, sleepy smile on her face. "I'm heading out to the sofa. Try not to stay up all night plotting world domination."

She turned to Chloé first, giving her a quick, friendly hug. "Night, Chloé."

Then, she turned to Alex. Her hug for him was different—longer, firmer, a familiar and deeply grounding embrace that had been a constant in his life since he was a small child. "Night, cuz," she whispered, giving his shoulder one last, affectionate squeeze. "I'm really proud of you."

NIGHT, MEGAN, his device voiced softly. THANKS FOR... EVERYTHING.

After Megan left, closing the bedroom door softly behind her, Alex and Chloé settled into their familiar sleepover routine, the quiet, peaceful house a perfect sanctuary.

Megan went to the living room and claimed her spot on the comfortable sofa. Before turning in for the night, she opened her MagnaByte laptop. She typed out a brief, reassuring email, addressing it to all four parents.

Subject: All's Quiet on the Lisa Court Front

Hi everyone,

Just wanted to let you know that the natives are behaving themselves. The great OS upgrade was a huge success, and we celebrated with a massive amount of spaghetti. They are both happy, healthy, and currently doing homework. No wild keggers to report. Hope you're all having a great time on your trips!

Love, Megan

She hit send, a satisfied smile on her face. Her charges were safe. Her cousin was happy. Her part of the mission was a success.
