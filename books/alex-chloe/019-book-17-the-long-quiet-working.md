# Book 17: The Long Quiet *(Working Title)*

## Chapter 1: A Future in the Townships

### Part 1: A Quiet Question (Summer 2030)

The late summer of 2030 found Alex, Chloé, and Megan, now in their mid-forties, taking a rare and much-needed vacation. They were back in the Eastern Townships of Québec, staying with Chloé's cousins, Mathieu and Isabelle, in Stanstead. The Lynnwood Discovery and Care Center and Echo Bridge, LLC were in the capable hands of their senior staff, allowing them this brief respite.

It was a quiet Tuesday afternoon. The three of them were sitting on the back porch of the Leclerc family home, the same porch where they had been so warmly welcomed as teenagers thirty years prior. The air was warm, and the scent of late-summer flowers hung in the air.

Alex had been quiet for a while, a thoughtful, almost pensive expression on his face as he looked out over the familiar, rolling green hills. The past two years, since he had received his new AI-integrated wheelchair and eye-gaze AAC device, had been a period of profound adaptation. The new technology was a miracle, but it was also a constant, undeniable reminder that his body was changing, that the physical resilience of his youth was beginning to fade.

He turned to Chloé, who was sitting beside him, her hand resting comfortably on his. Megan was on his other side, a quiet, steady presence. He used his eye-gaze system to type, his AAC device voicing the words, the synthesized English calm and thoughtful.

CHLOÉ. MEGAN. I'VE BEEN THINKING. ABOUT... THE FUTURE. OUR FUTURE.

They both turned to him, their expressions full of their usual, unwavering attention. "Okay," Chloé said softly. "What are you thinking about?"

THIS PLACE, he typed, gesturing with a slight nod towards the landscape. I'VE ALWAYS LOVED IT HERE. IT'S... QUIET. PEACEFUL. IT FEELS LIKE HOME. He paused, then continued, his next words full of a quiet vulnerability. MY BODY... IT'S GETTING HARDER. THE WORK AT THE CENTER IS GOOD, BUT IT'S DEMANDING. SOMEDAY... I'D LIKE TO RETIRE. WITH BOTH OF YOU. SOMEWHERE LIKE THIS.

Chloé’s heart ached with a familiar, loving empathy. "I'd like that too, Alex," she said, her voice full of a deep sincerity. "More than anything. Retiring here, with you... it would be like coming home for good."

Megan, however, was already in project manager mode, the practical, logistical part of her mind gently surfacing. "It's a beautiful dream," she said. "But you know, for you and me to live here permanently, there are rules. The biggest one is the language. To become a resident in Québec, you have to pass their official language exams." She gave Alex a small, loving, teasing smile. "I know you're fluent, cuz. But the official government tests... they're a whole other beast. It would mean months of studying the formal grammar."

Alex looked at her, his expression unreadable for a moment. Then, a slow, almost mischievous smile spread across his face. He looked down at his screen, and with a few quick commands with his eyes, he changed the primary language of his Echo Bridge Axon software from English to French.

The voice that spoke next was the perfect, fluent, and deeply familiar Québécois French Chloé knew so well, the language Megan had spent years mastering.

"Chloé, ma chérie. Megan, ma chère," the device said, the synthesized voice warm and full of a love that needed no translation. "Pensez-vous vraiment qu'après trente ans avec vous, avec votre famille, que je n'aurais pas appris? Je vous écoute. J'ai toujours écouté. J'aurais probablement pu passer ces examens-là quand j'étais encore au lycée." (Translation for the reader: "Chloé, my darling. Megan, my dear. Do you really think that after thirty years with you, with your family, that I wouldn't have learned? I listen to you. I have always been listening. I probably could have passed those exams when I was still in high school.")

Chloé and Megan stared at him, their jaws slightly agape, tears instantly welling in their eyes. The surprise wasn't that he was fluent; they had known that for decades. The shock was the depth of his mastery, the quiet confidence with which he claimed he could have passed those impossible exams as a teenager. It meant that all these years, his fluency wasn't just a passive skill. It was something he had actively, secretly, perfected to a level they had never suspected, all in quiet preparation for a future they hadn't even known they were planning yet.

It was the most romantic, most profound declaration of love he had ever made.
