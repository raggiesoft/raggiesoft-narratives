# Part 1: The 20-Year Reunion (Fall 2023)

The Northwood High School gymnasium, usually echoing with the squeak of sneakers, was tonight awash with slightly more formal footwear and the murmur of adults reconnecting. Banners proclaiming "Class of 2003 – 20 Year Reunion!" hung with a charming lack of professional precision. The trip itself had been a gift from their four parents, who had insisted the three of them take a much-needed break from the all-consuming work of overseeing the LDCC's construction.

Alex, Chloé, and Megan had been chatting with a small group of interested former classmates. One of them, a woman named Jessica from their old English class, looked from Megan to a group across the room.

"Hey, Megan Carter," Jessica said, a curious smile on her face. "Is that your brother over there? Ben Carter? I saw you guys talking earlier, I just assumed you were related since you have the same last name."

Megan laughed, a warm, easy sound. "Oh, no. Just a classic Virginia Beach coincidence. There are a lot of Carters around here. Ben's a great guy, but definitely no relation."

"We used to get that all the time in high school," Chloé added with a grin.

Near the refreshment table, Ben Carter, looking sharp and confident in a well-tailored suit, was laughing easily with his wife, Morgan McAllister Carter. Morgan, equally poised and successful as an upper-level executive, had an arm looped comfortably through his.

Josh Riley, a bit thicker around the middle now, his youthful sneer settled into a permanent cynical downturn of his lips, was holding court with a couple of other men. He turned, his eyes narrowing as he focused on Ben and Morgan across the room.

"Carter? Married?" Josh bellowed, loud enough to turn several heads. "To McAllister? You gotta be kidding me! The guy who used to be my goon, married to 'Morgue' McAllister?" He threw his head back and roared with laughter.

Then, his gaze flickered over to Alex's group, and he saw Megan. His sneer deepened. "And what, is that his sister over there?" he sneered, pointing with his beer bottle towards Megan. "She still babysitting Miller after all these years? Pathetic."

He then took in the whole scene. "And look over there! Miller still dragging that robot voice around, and he's got Mason *and* his cousin still following him around? What is this, a reunion for freaks and losers who somehow tricked people into staying with them?"

Before Josh could continue his tirade, a familiar, clear voice cut through the air. "Mr. Riley."

Josh Riley froze mid-sneer, a look of genuine, twenty-year-old fear momentarily flashing across his face.

Mrs. DeMarco, her hair grayer but her eyes as sharp as ever, had risen silently from a table. She approached Josh, her posture erect and unwavering.

"Mrs. DeMarco," Josh stammered. "Just… catching up."

"Indeed, Mr. Riley," she said, her voice dangerously calm. Her gaze flickered warmly towards Ben and Morgan, and then with equal warmth towards Alex, Chloé, and Megan, a hint of a proud smile touching her lips before returning, like chilled steel, to Josh. "Mr. and Mrs. Carter, and the Miller-Carter family," she used their names with clear emphasis, "are testaments to growth, resilience, and enduring partnership. Qualities that, I observe, still seem to elude you."

Josh started to bluster, "Now, hold on, I was just—"

"Were you perhaps about to volunteer for clean-up duty after this event, Mr. Riley?" Mrs. DeMarco interjected smoothly. "I believe the janitorial staff could use an experienced hand tomorrow morning."

The blood drained from Josh’s face. "N-no, Mrs. DeMarco," he mumbled. "Understood."

"Excellent," she said crisply. "Do try to enjoy the remainder of your evening with a renewed focus on civility."

Later, as the three of them were preparing to leave, Mrs. DeMarco approached them. Her earlier sternness was replaced by a genuine warmth.

"*Monsieur Miller, Madame Miller,*" she said, her Parisian French as flawless as ever. "*C'est un réel plaisir de vous voir. Et vous aussi, Mademoiselle Carter.*" (Mr. Miller, Mrs. Miller, it's a real pleasure to see you. And you as well, Miss Carter.) "*J'ai entendu parler du travail merveilleux que vous faites à Lynnwood. C'est un délice de voir comment 'mes enfants' ont prospéré.*" (I've heard about the wonderful work you're doing in Lynnwood. It's a delight to see how 'my kids' have flourished.)

Chloé smiled, replying in her own fluent French, "*Merci, Madame DeMarco. C'était merveilleux de vous revoir aussi.*" (Thank you, Mrs. DeMarco. It was wonderful to see you too.)

Megan, who had been listening with a warm, respectful smile, added in English, "It really was, Mrs. DeMarco. Thank you for… well, for still being you."

Mrs. DeMarco's smile widened. She then looked directly at Alex, her expression now full of a new, more personal kind of gratitude. "And Mr. Miller," she said, her voice dropping slightly, "I have something to thank *you* for."

Alex looked up, a flicker of genuine surprise on his face.

"My youngest grandson," Mrs. DeMarco continued, a profound, grandmotherly love in her eyes, "he's seven. And he has Angelman syndrome. He is non-verbal. For the past six months, he has been using a new piece of software on his tablet. A program called Echo Bridge Axon."

A profound, stunned silence fell over their small group. Alex, Chloé, and Megan just stared at her, their minds struggling to process the impossible, beautiful coincidence.

"It has changed his world," Mrs. DeMarco said, her voice now thick with an emotion she rarely showed. "It has given him a voice. It has given our family a way to connect with him that we never had before. I didn't know it was you until I saw the story on your website last week." She looked at him, her gaze direct and full of a deep, heartfelt respect. "Your work is more than just code, young man. It is a gift. From my family to yours... thank you."

Alex was completely overwhelmed. He looked at Chloé, at Megan, his own eyes shining with tears. He had known, in an abstract sense, that his software was helping people. But to see it here, in this person, his legendary, formidable, multilingual cafeteria guardian, was a validation so profound it was almost too much to bear.

He typed, his fingers moving with a shaky, emotional speed. MRS. DEMARCO. YOU ARE STILL A LEGEND. THANK YOU. FOR EVERYTHING. ALWAYS.

Mrs. DeMarco actually chuckled, a warm, genuine sound. "One does what one can, Mr. Miller. And some lessons, it seems, bear repeating across the decades." She gave them a knowing wink. "Safe travels back to Lynnwood. And keep building bridges."
