# Part 1: A Much-Needed Breather (Early December 2022)

The weeks after submitting the grant application were a unique form of torture. The intense, focused work had been replaced by an agonizing, nerve-shredding quiet. The burnout from the preceding months was catching up with all three of them, leaving them feeling frayed and exhausted.

One evening, they were on a video call with Chloé's parents and the Leclercs, the familiar, cheerful faces a welcome distraction.

"*Alors, mes amours,*" Aunt Geneviève said, her voice full of warmth as her gaze took in the tired faces of Alex, Chloé, and Megan. "*Comment ça va? You look exhausted, all three of you.*"

"We're okay, Tante," Chloé admitted with a weary smile. "Just... waiting. It's hard."

"You need a break," Sophie said, her mother's intuition cutting through the screen. "You have all pushed yourselves too hard. You need to come home. Come for Christmas. To finish the trip that was cut short. Just relax with family."

The idea was a lifeline. A trip. A real break, their first since quitting their jobs. Alex, Chloé, and Megan exchanged a look. It was the perfect idea.

"We'd love that," Chloé said, a genuine smile returning to her face for the first time in weeks.

As they were making plans, Uncle David boomed, "*Et bien sûr, vous resterez pour le Boxing Day!*" (And of course, you'll stay for Boxing Day!)

Megan looked up, a confused expression on her face. "Boxing Day? What's that? Like... a boxing match?"

A delighted laugh erupted from the Canadian contingent on the screen. "*Non, non, ma nièce américaine!*" Uncle David roared. "It is the biggest shopping day of the year! The sales! The crowds! It is a national sport, yes, but not that kind!" (No, no, my American niece!)

"He's right," Chloé explained, a mischievous glint in her eye as she looked at both Alex and Megan. "It's like Black Friday, but after Christmas. It's a Canadian institution. Oh, you two have to experience it. It's chaos. You'll love it."

The plan was set. They would fly to Montréal a few days before Christmas and stay through the 26th. The trip wasn't just a vacation anymore; it was a cultural mission for their two resident Americans.
