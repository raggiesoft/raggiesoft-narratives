# Part 6: Return to Lynnwood

The morning of their departure was a flurry of heartfelt, bilingual goodbyes. The entire Leclerc clan gathered at the house to see them off, a chaotic, loving convoy of hugs and last-minute gifts of maple syrup and homemade jam.

"You come back soon, you hear?" Uncle David said, giving each of them a fierce, bone-crushing hug. "*On vous aime, les enfants.*" (We love you, kids.)

The drive from Stanstead to the Montréal-Trudeau International Airport was quiet and reflective. The two-week vacation had been more than just a break; it had been a profound and necessary reset. They had arrived frayed and burned out, and they were leaving with a new, unified vision for their future.

The flight from YUL back to Seattle was a stark contrast to their previous journeys. There was no anxiety, no frantic race against a closing border. It was just a calm, comfortable, and beautifully normal trip home.

They settled into their seats, a familiar three-person unit. Alex was already on his laptop, not playing a game, but sketching out a new, more refined version of the Echo Bridge logo, the ideas from the night before still fresh and exciting. Chloé had a legal pad out, a new, determined glint in her eye as she began to outline the first official sections of the LDCC's articles of incorporation. And Megan, the project manager, had a new spreadsheet open, its title simple and powerful: "Echo Bridge: Q1 Marketing and Outreach Plan."

The vacation was over. The work had already begun.

When they finally landed in Seattle and made their way back to their Lynnwood apartment, the space felt different. It was no longer just a home where they worked on a dream; it was the official, full-time headquarters of a mission that was now fully underway. The quiet hum of the 3D printer, the stacks of non-profit law textbooks, the whiteboards covered in code—it was all a testament to their shared, unwavering purpose. They were no longer just dreaming. They were building. And they were ready for whatever came next.
