# Part 1: An Unexpected Encounter (Lynnwood, Washington – Summer 2028)

The early morning sun cast long shadows across the polished floors of the still-empty Lynnwood Discovery and Care Center. In their shared back office, the three founders were reviewing the day’s online ticket manifest. It was a comfortable, practiced routine. Chloé handled the main overview, Megan cross-referenced group bookings with their outreach calendar, and Alex monitored the system's performance.

It was Chloé who saw it first. She stopped scrolling, her body going still. "Alex," she said quietly, her voice carefully neutral as she turned her monitor. "Look at this booking for the 10:30 AM slot."

Alex and Megan both wheeled their chairs over. Alex’s gaze fell upon the entry: "Kowalski, Lily. Group of 10."

A visceral chill snaked down his spine, immediate and suffocating. *Kowalski*. The name alone was a physical blow, dredging up a deep well of trauma. His heart began to hammer, his breath catching. *Vera.* The Cease and Desist letter flashed in his mind. His first instinct was pure self-preservation: *Get them out. Now.* He thought of the "third-party intermediaries" clause. Was this Vera, using her daughter?

Megan, seeing the stark terror in his eyes, reacted instantly, her own expression hardening into a mask of fierce, protective anger. Chloé placed her hand gently over his on the desk. "Alex," she murmured, her voice incredibly soft, "*Breathe, mon cœur. Look again.*"

She pointed to "Thompson, Noah" on the booking. "It might be her—Lily, Vera's daughter—with her newfound paternal half-siblings. Remember what your cousin Cassie and Ethan told your parents about her being estranged from Vera?"

The mention of his own recently discovered family pierced through Alex’s initial terror. This Lily Kowalski was Ethan’s half-sister. Kicking them out felt… complicated. But the fear of Vera was a powerful force.

He took a shaky breath. He typed on his sleek, modern "Quantum Design Studio Pro," running his own Echo Bridge Axon software. WE NEED TO SPEAK WITH HER. LILY KOWALSKI. DISCREETLY. THE MULTIPURPOSE ROOM. BEFORE THEY ENTER.

Chloé nodded. Megan, already in project manager mode, picked up the internal radio. "We'll face this together," Chloé said, squeezing his hand. "All three of us."

At 10:25 AM, the radio crackled. "Front desk to Directors. The Kowalski party is here. Lily Kowalski seems… a bit frightened to come alone. She’s clinging quite desperately to a young man, a Noah Thompson. She says he’s her helper."

Alex looked at Chloé and Megan, his old trauma whispering: *It's a trick.* But the memory of what his family had told him about Lily won out. Chloé took the radio. "Tell her Noah may join Lily. We'll meet them in the multipurpose room."

Soon, the door opened, and Lily Kowalski, now a young woman of 24, entered, her green eyes wide and nervous, clinging tightly to the arm of Noah Thompson. The other eight siblings waited in the main lobby, their excitement now tinged with anxious whispers.

"Lily Kowalski? Noah Thompson?" Chloé asked gently. "Thank you for coming. I'm Chloé Miller, and these are my partners, Alex Miller and Megan Carter."

Lily nodded, her gaze darting to Alex, then quickly away. Alex took a breath. He forced himself to type.

LILY. MY NAME IS ALEX MILLER. YOUR MOTHER… VERA KOWALSKI… WE KNEW EACH OTHER. A LONG TIME AGO. Each word felt like dredging up poison.

Lily’s breath hitched. Her eyes met Alex's, and he saw not malice, but a reflection of his own haunted look. "Mr. Miller… Alex," she began, her voice trembling slightly. "I… I know who you are. My Nana Robyn told me about… about what my mother did to you. Years ago. I am so, so incredibly sorry for the pain she caused you."

Alex listened, his expression unreadable. He glanced at Chloé and Megan, who both offered tiny, almost imperceptible nods of encouragement. He typed, WHAT. EXACTLY. DID YOUR NANA. TELL YOU?

Lily took a deep breath. "She told me… everything, Alex. About how my mother pursued you when you were just eighteen. About your disabilities, your autism, and how Vera exploited your kindness. She told me about the... the awful bus trip to Kent, how Vera neglected your medical needs, how she nearly… nearly killed you. And how Chloé had to rescue you." Tears welled in Lily’s eyes. "And she told me that my mother then lied, saying I was your child, even though she knew you're infertile. Nana explained what that meant too, and about the Cease and Desist letter Vera got."

Alex felt a tremor run through him. *She knows.* He looked at Chloé, then at Megan. *This might not be a trick after all.*

Lily continued, her voice gaining strength. "Vera has no part in my life. She hasn't since I turned eighteen. What she did to you was monstrous. I want nothing to do with her, and she has nothing to do with me."

Noah added calmly, "That’s true, Alex. Lily made her choice. Vera is completely out of her life. Vera doesn't even know Lily is in Washington."

Alex processed this. The sincerity in Lily’s voice, the directness of her knowledge… it was hard to dismiss. His initial fear still lingered, but a new emotion was beginning to surface: a hesitant empathy. He typed, his synthesized voice softer now. LILY. VERA’S ACTIONS… THEY WERE… VERY DAMAGING. He paused, then looked directly at her. YOU ARE NOT YOUR MOTHER. YOU ARE ETHAN’S SISTER.

Lily nodded, tears streaming freely now. "I know I can't change what she did. But I needed you to hear from me… that I know. And I'm sorry."

A long silence filled the room. The wall of fear around Alex’s heart began to show a crack. She was right; she wasn't responsible for Vera. And she was family. Slowly, he typed his next words. LILY. HEARING YOUR TRUTH… AND KNOWING VERA IS TRULY GONE FROM YOUR LIFE… IT MEANS… A LOT. He took another breath, the decision forming. I… I ACCEPT YOU, LILY. AS ETHAN’S SISTER. AS PART OF MY EXTENDED FAMILY. WELCOME.

A watery, tremulous smile broke through Lily’s tears. "Thank you, Alex," she whispered. Noah put a comforting arm around her shoulders. Chloé and Megan, on either side of Alex, both reached out to briefly touch his hand, their eyes shining.

Alex, feeling a surprising lightness, a release of tension he hadn’t realized he was carrying so tightly, looked at his partners. He typed again. CHLOÉ. MEGAN. OUR VISITORS. THEY ARE WAITING. PERHAPS… WE COULD OFFER THE ENTIRE FAMILY A SPECIAL TOUR? BEHIND THE SCENES?

Chloé’s smile widened. Megan’s was just as bright. "Alex, that’s a wonderful idea," Chloé said. She turned to Lily and Noah. "What do you say? Would your family like a private, behind-the-scenes tour of the Lynnwood Discovery and Care Center, led by its founders? You'll get to see some of the amazing custom Echo Bridge prosthetics my husband has designed. And afterwards," she added, a twinkle in her eye, "perhaps a 10% discount at our gift shop and cafe, on the house?"

Lily’s eyes lit up. "They would… they would love that. Thank you, Alex. Thank you, Chloé. Thank you, Megan."

The fear in Alex’s heart had finally, truly, begun to yield to a cautious, hopeful peace. The name Kowalski would always carry a shadow, but today, a new light, in the form of Lily and her siblings, had begun to shine through.
