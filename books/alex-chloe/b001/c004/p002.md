# Part 2: The Resonance Cascade (Winter 1998)

The glow of a large CRT monitor was the only light in Alex's otherwise dark room. The sounds were a tense, atmospheric mix of humming fluorescent lights, distant, unearthly screams, and the sharp, percussive blast of a shotgun.

He and Megan were deep in the Argos Science & Technology Division, the setting for the groundbreaking new game, "Resonance Cascade."

The game was a single-player masterpiece. But that wasn't good enough for Alex. In a feat of programming genius far beyond his thirteen years, he had spent weeks painstakingly writing a mod that allowed for a crude but functional two-player cooperative mode. It was their secret project, their own private version of the game.

Their control scheme was a perfect, unspoken metaphor for their entire relationship. Megan, sitting beside him, had her hands on the keyboard, controlling their shared character's movement. Alex had the mouse, controlling all the aiming, shooting, and interaction with the world. She was the legs; he was the arms. Together, they were a single, perfectly synchronized entity.

"Okay, okay, left at the next junction," Megan whispered, her voice a mixture of tension and excitement as she navigated their character down a dark, blood-streaked corridor. "The security guard said the elevator is just past the..."

A horrifying, gurgling moan echoed from around the corner.

"Nope, nope, bad hallway," Megan said, instinctively making their character backpedal. "That sounds like a Cascade Husk."

IT'S JUST ONE, Alex typed quickly into a small chat window on the screen. I CAN TAKE IT. GO FORWARD.

"Are you sure?" she whispered.

POSITIVE.

Megan took a deep breath. "Okay, cuz. Your show." She expertly strafed their character out from behind the corner. The shambling figure of a former scientist was right there. Alex, with a calm, precise movement of the mouse, lined up the crosshairs and fired a single, perfect shotgun blast. The Husk collapsed in a heap.

"Nice shot!" Megan breathed, a grin on her face.

They spent hours like that, a perfect, symbiotic team, navigating the labyrinthine, alien-infested complex. In the background, they would occasionally catch a glimpse of the game's most mysterious figure, "The Administrator," a man in a blue suit who would watch them from a distant balcony before calmly walking away.

After they finally finished their session for the day, a new, intense look of concentration was on Alex's face. He wasn't thinking about the aliens or the story anymore. He was thinking about the *world itself*.

MEGAN, he typed, his tone now full of a new, different kind of excitement. THE GAME CAME WITH A LEVEL EDITOR. A PROGRAM TO BUILD YOUR OWN MAPS.

"Oh yeah?" she said, intrigued. "You want to try and make one?"

I WANT TO LEARN HOW IT WORKS, he corrected, his passion evident in the simple words. I WANT TO UNDERSTAND THE SYSTEM. HOW THEY BUILT IT.

Megan smiled. It was a perfect Alex moment. He wasn't just content to play in the world; he had to understand its architecture. It was the first, brilliant spark of the engineer he would one day become, a desire born in the digital, monster-filled hallways of the Argos facility.
