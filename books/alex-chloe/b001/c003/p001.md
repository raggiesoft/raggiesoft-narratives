# Part 1: The Dare and The Setup

The plan was born, as most cruel middle school plans are, from a potent combination of boredom and a desire for social power. It was a Tuesday in early October, and Ashley Parker, the undisputed queen bee of her eighth-grade friend group, was holding court in the cafeteria.

Her target was Jennifer Thompson, a quieter, more insecure member of her circle. Her weapon was Alex Miller, the silent, intelligent boy in the wheelchair who usually sat alone, absorbed in a book.

"Okay, Jenny," Ashley said, her voice a low, conspiratorial whisper that was meant to be overheard by the entire table. "Time to prove your loyalty. I dare you to ask him out." She gestured with her head towards Alex. "The quiet kid. I bet you can't get him to be your boyfriend for a month."

A wave of cruel, tittering laughter went around the table. Jennifer’s face flushed a deep, mortified red. "Ashley, no," she pleaded. "That's... that's so mean."

"It's not mean, it's a game," Ashley said, her smile sharp and predatory. "Unless you're too scared?"

Pressured and mortified, Jennifer eventually relented. After lunch, she nervously approached Alex's table, stammering through an awkward invitation to "go out sometime." To her surprise, Alex, more out of a passive curiosity than any real interest, agreed.

The "relationship" that followed was a series of stilted, public performances, always under the watchful eye of Ashley and her friends. The culmination of Ashley's plan was the group "date" to the movies a few weeks later.

"We have to see *Senior Week*," Ashley had declared, having been completely taken in by the movie's action-packed trailer. "It's supposed to be, like, the most epic party movie ever."

Her real goal, however, had nothing to do with the film. As she was coordinating the plan with her friends, away from Jennifer, her true, ugly motive became clear.

"Okay, so here's the real plan," Ashley had whispered to her inner circle. "We get them in the theater, in the back row. We make them hold hands. Then, after the movie, we're going to convince them to go into the family bathroom. Just the two of them. We'll tell them it's part of a dare, that they have to... you know." She wiggled her eyebrows suggestively. "If we can get them to do it, it'll be the funniest story of the entire year."

The other girls had giggled, a mixture of shock and cruel excitement. The plan was not just about a date; it was a deliberate, malicious attempt to orchestrate an inappropriate sexual encounter between two vulnerable teenagers for their own amusement. And Jennifer, and more importantly, Alex, were just the pawns in her sick and twisted game.
