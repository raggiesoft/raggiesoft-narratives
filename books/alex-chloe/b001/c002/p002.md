# Part 2: The Afternoon Block

The short bus ride from the elementary school to the Carter's neighborhood was a familiar, rumbling journey. At precisely 12:15 PM, the bus hissed to a stop at the corner, and the aide helped Alex down the lift. Waiting for him on the sidewalk, as she was every day, was his grandmother, Eleanor Prescott.

"There's my handsome boy," Eleanor said, her voice a warm, familiar welcome as she took the handles of his small wheelchair. "Did you learn all the important things today? How to finger-paint? The proper technique for napping on a mat?"

Alex just gave her a small, tired smile. The morning, as new and overwhelming as it had been, was over. Now, he was back in his sanctuary.

The Carter house was unusually quiet. Jessica was at Northwood High, and Cassie was at her own afternoon kindergarten session. For a few precious hours, the house belonged just to Alex and his grandmother. This was their time. The routine was always the same. Eleanor would wheel him into the kitchen and make him lunch—usually a simple grilled cheese sandwich, cut into perfect, crustless triangles, with a side of apple slices.

After lunch, they would settle in the living room for their shared ritual: watching an episode of "Bookworm Adventures." It was the perfect show for Alex. It was calm, it was quiet, and it was about the one thing in the world he loved most: stories. He would sit in his chair, completely captivated by Mr. Andre's gentle voice.

Around 2:45 PM, the front door would open, and Jessica, the oldest cousin, would arrive home from the high school bus. She'd usually just offer a quick "Hey, Alex," before disappearing into her room with the phone.

The real shift in the house's energy came at 3:30 PM. That's when the rumbling of the elementary school bus would be heard down the street. Alex would already be by the living room window, his face full of a quiet, happy anticipation.

The bus would hiss to a stop, and a moment later, Cassie and Megan would come through the front door, a chaotic tumble of backpacks and excited chatter.

And, almost like clockwork, the fighting would begin.

"It's my turn to pick the cartoon!" Cassie whined, making a dash for the television remote.

Jessica, emerging from her room, snatched it just before she could grab it. "No way, you little pest! I was here first! I'm watching my show!" The familiar, bickering energy of the sisters would fill the house.

But Alex didn't care. He wasn't focused on them. His entire world, in that moment, was focused on Megan.

She, in turn, would roll her eyes at her sisters' drama, her priority clear. She'd drop her own backpack by the door and walk directly over to Alex, her face breaking into a wide, genuine smile.

"Hey, cuz," she'd say, leaning down to give him a big, familiar hug.

He would hug her back fiercely, a profound sense of rightness and peace settling over him. He was no longer just the quiet observer in his grandmother's peaceful house. He was Alex, Megan's cuz. The chaos of the Carter sisters could rage on. He was in his sanctuary. He was home.
