# Part 6: A Cross-Border Introduction

The main event was the big, American-style Christmas dinner. Emily's magnificent roast turkey was the centerpiece of a table groaning under the weight of mashed potatoes, stuffing, cranberry sauce, and her famous green bean casserole. The house was now filled to bursting with the loud, loving, and chaotic energy of the full Miller-Mason-Carter clan.

As they were all finding their seats, the phone rang. Sophie Mason, who had been anticipating the call, answered with a wide, joyful smile.

"It's my sister, Geneviève!" she announced, her voice full of excitement. "From Stanstead! Everyone, come on in! It's time for you all to finally meet!"

The entire Virginia contingent—James, Emily, Alex, Karen, Steve, and the Carter girls—trooped into the kitchen. Sophie put the landline phone on speaker, its crackling connection a bridge across a thousand miles.

A warm, boisterous, and unmistakably Québécois voice, thick with a musical accent, filled the room. "*Allo, ma soeur! Joyeux Noël!*" (Hello, my sister! Merry Christmas!)

What followed was a beautiful, chaotic, and slightly overwhelming multilingual conversation. Sophie acted as the proud, happy translator. Geneviève and her husband, David, would speak in rapid-fire, passionate French, and Sophie would laugh and translate their warm, welcoming words.

"Geneviève says it is so wonderful to finally put a voice to the names of the people who have been so kind to her Chloé," Sophie translated, her eyes shining. "And David wants to know if you are all ready to become Montréal Voyageurs fans, because it is non-negotiable!"

James Miller laughed, a warm, genuine sound that needed no translation. "You can tell him that he'll have a fight on his hands! We're a Boston Brawlers family down here!"

Sophie translated his good-natured challenge back into French, which was met with a booming, delighted roar of laughter from Uncle David on the other end of the line.

The conversation flowed easily, a warm and wonderful fusion of two families and two cultures. For the Millers and the Carters, it was a fascinating and deeply heartwarming experience. They weren't just talking to strangers; they were being welcomed, sight unseen, into another family.

Alex, Chloé, and Megan watched the entire exchange, a quiet, supportive audience. For Alex, hearing the warm, boisterous sound of Chloé's extended family, even through a crackly speakerphone, was another piece of the beautiful puzzle of her life clicking into place.

The call ended with a heartfelt promise from Geneviève. "It was a true pleasure to meet all of you," Sophie translated, her own voice thick with emotion. "She says that she hopes, very soon, we can all be together in one place. In person."

The seed was planted. The invitation was extended. The two halves of their new, cross-border family had finally met, and the quiet, unspoken promise of a future, real-life reunion hung in the air, a perfect, hopeful end to their very first holiday season together.
