# Part 4: The Real Victory

Later that evening, after Josh's spectacular self-destruction and the crowning of the trash can, the DJ announced the final slow dance of the night. Alex, his face full of a quiet, triumphant peace, looked at Chloé. MAY I HAVE THIS DANCE? his Quantum Communicator XT voiced.

She just smiled and took his hand. She led him, in his manual wheelchair, to an open space on the edge of the dance floor. Other couples were swaying in the center of the gym, but they created their own quiet world.

She stood in front of his chair and gently placed her hands on his shoulders. He, in turn, rested his own hands at her waist. It wasn't a dance of grand, sweeping movements. It was a slow, gentle sway, a quiet rhythm that was just for them. He would use one hand to gently turn his wheels, guiding them in a slow, graceful circle, while his other hand remained securely with her.

They didn't win Prom King and Queen. They didn't even care. They had each other. And as they danced, surrounded by their friends, in a school they had somehow, against all odds, made their own, they knew, with an absolute certainty, that they had already won.
