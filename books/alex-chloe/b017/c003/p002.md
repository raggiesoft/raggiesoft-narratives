# Part 2: The First Venture Out (Summer 2020)

After another two weeks of strict, self-imposed isolation following their recovery, the day finally came when the four walls of their apartment felt less like a sanctuary and more like a cage. The sun was shining, and a restless energy buzzed between them.

"I need to see something that isn't our parking lot," Chloé announced, a note of desperation in her voice. "Even if it's just through a car window."

"I'm with you," Megan said, looking up from her laptop. "I'm starting to forget what trees look like."

Alex, who was feeling stronger each day, readily agreed. The idea of simply being in motion felt like a profound luxury.

The preparations for this simple outing were like a pre-flight checklist for a moon mission. All three of them donned cloth masks. Chloé, the designated mission commander, stocked the Holt Wayfarer with a bottle of hand sanitizer and a container of disinfectant wipes. The plan was simple, designed for maximum safety and minimal contact.

Their first stop was a fast-food drive-thru. Chloé was at the wheel. In the back, Alex was in his secure lockdown position, with Megan sitting on the bench seat beside him, her arm wrapped comfortingly around his shoulders. The interaction at the drive-thru was a strange, disembodied ballet. They ordered through a muffled speaker, paid with a credit card held out at arm's length, and received their bag of food from a gloved hand through a tiny opening in a plexiglass shield.

They didn't drive far. They simply found a quiet, empty corner of the restaurant's parking lot, shut off the engine, and finally, gratefully, pulled down their masks. They ate their burgers and fries in the car, the simple, greasy meal feeling like the most decadent feast they had ever had. It was the first food they hadn't cooked themselves in over a month.

After lunch, they just drove. Chloé stayed on the less-traveled back roads of Snohomish County, windows down to let the fresh, clean air rush in. In the back, Alex leaned his head against Megan's shoulder, her steady, grounding presence allowing him to relax and simply watch the world go by. They drove past green fields and dense forests, past farms with grazing horses. After being confined for so long, the familiar landscape looked vibrant, new, and achingly beautiful.

Even the necessary stop for gas was a carefully choreographed procedure. Masks on. Disposable glove to handle the pump. Pay at the pump. Hand sanitizer immediately after.

When they finally pulled back into their own apartment complex, a sense of profound relief washed over them. They had successfully navigated the outside world, a small but significant victory. They had been out for less than two hours, had spoken to no one face-to-face, and yet, it felt like a grand adventure. As they walked from their car back to their front door, masks still firmly in place, they knew they had just taken their first, tentative step into the new normal, together.
