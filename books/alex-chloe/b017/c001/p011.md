# Part 11: A Full House (April 2020)

The days after their arrival in Lynnwood were a strange, compressed, and slightly chaotic form of family lockdown. The seven of them were now camped out in Alex, Chloé, and Megan's small, two-bedroom apartment. The space, a perfect sanctuary for three, became a crowded but loving command center. The four parents slept on a combination of the pull-out sofa and inflatable air mattresses on the living room floor, a nightly routine that felt like a bizarre family slumber party.

Their first priority was the SUV that had so valiantly carried them across the country. As James had predicted, the vehicle was in rough shape. Chloé spent an entire morning on the phone, and the earliest any shop could even look at it was a week from now.

And so, their unexpected family time was extended. The parents, unable to fly home, took over the domestic duties. This freed up Alex, Chloé, and Megan to focus on their most pressing concern: their aquarium.

The moment they saw it, a collective sigh of relief went through the three of them. The extended feeders had worked. The plants were a little overgrown, but the fish were all alive and healthy. Megan, who had helped build their first ecosystem, was particularly overjoyed. "I can't believe it," she said, her voice full of a genuine, happy relief. "Our little world survived."

The three of them spent hours together, their familiar, quiet teamwork a comforting anchor. The extended family watched, fascinated, as they meticulously changed the water, trimmed the plants, and restored their small, living piece of art to its former glory.

A week later, the news from the auto shop was not good. Thomas, as the owner of the vehicle, took the call. The mechanic's diagnosis was grim: the transmission was shot, the brakes were worn down to the metal, and there was a worrying list of other issues.

"He said we basically drove it into the ground on that trip," Thomas reported back to the family. "He can do enough work to make it safe for the drive back to Virginia, but... this car's days are numbered."

With the repairs underway, the end of their strange, forced family vacation was in sight. A few days later, the SUV, patched up and as road-worthy as it could be, was ready. The goodbyes in the apartment parking lot were emotional.

"You call us," Emily said, pulling Alex into a tight hug. "Every day. Let us know you're alright."

Sophie hugged Chloé fiercely. "*Prenez soin les uns des autres, mes amours,*" she whispered. (Take care of each other, my loves.)

Alex, Chloé, and Megan stood on the sidewalk and watched as the four people who had driven across a continent to rescue them piled back into the weary SUV to begin the long journey home. As the car turned out of the parking lot and disappeared down the street, a profound quiet settled over them. The adventure was truly over. They were back in their own lives, in their own home, just the three of them again, left to navigate the strange new reality of a world in lockdown, together.
