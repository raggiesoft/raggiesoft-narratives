# Part 6: Home Safe (Lynnwood)

An hour later, the phone rang one last time.

"Okay," Chloé's voice said, now with a new undercurrent of profound weariness, but also a sense of finality. "I'm here. In the apartment. It's... small. And empty. But I'm here. I'm safe."

She had made it. All the way to her new home.

"Now you have to get some sleep," she whispered. "You have classes tomorrow. We'll set up the video chat as soon as I get my computer hooked up, I promise."

OKAY, CHLOÉ. GOODNIGHT. BE SAFE.

"Always," she promised. "Goodnight, Alex. I miss you already."

After hanging up, Alex finally felt the adrenaline of his grief and anxiety begin to recede, replaced by a profound exhaustion. He went back to Chloé's room, to her bed. He still clutched the single strand of her hair, but the room no longer felt entirely empty. Her voice was in his ears, her promise in his heart. The three-thousand-mile distance between them felt vast and terrifying, but the lifeline of their connection, now proven to be strong enough to span a continent, was a fragile but powerful thread of hope. For tonight, it was enough to finally let him sleep.
