# Part 5: A Voice Across the Continent (Seattle)

It was nearly 1:00 AM in Virginia when the phone rang again. This time, all four parents and Alex were gathered in the Millers' living room, waiting.

"I'm here," Chloé's voice announced, thick with the exhaustion of a cross-country journey, but also with the relief of arrival. "I landed. I'm in Seattle. I'm safe."

A profound, bone-deep wave of relief washed over Alex, so powerful it almost made him dizzy. The final, terrifying part of the separation was over. She was there. She was okay.

"It's only ten o'clock here," she continued, "but I know it's super late for you. I just... I had to get my bags and find the taxi to the apartment. But I couldn't go to sleep without talking to you."

He reached for his AAC device, his fingers finally steady. I WAS WAITING, the device voiced, full of his exhaustion and overwhelming gratitude. I WAS SO SCARED. I MISS YOU SO MUCH.

"Oh, Alex, I miss you too," she said, her voice choked with tears. "So, so much. I hate this. But I'm okay. And you're going to be okay. It's just until Christmas, remember? I promise."
