# Part 6: L'Unité Inséparable (The Unbreakable Unit)

Fifteen minutes later, the door to the small, quiet safe room opened. Alex emerged, Chloé on one side and Megan on the other, their presence a silent, unwavering fortress of support. He was calm, the last of the emotional storm having passed, leaving a profound sense of peace in its wake. He was ready to rejoin his own wedding.

As the three of them re-entered the ballroom, a quiet, respectful hush fell for a moment before the band, taking a cue from the MC, struck up a lively but not overwhelming tune. Their family, who had been watching with quiet, anxious concern, saw the calm on Alex's face and the steadfast, protective presence of Chloé and Megan, and a collective, unspoken wave of relief washed over them. The crisis had passed. The celebration could continue.

For the rest of the night, they were a single, indivisible unit. When it was time to visit the tables and thank their guests, they moved as one—Chloé gently guiding the conversation, Alex offering heartfelt, typed thanks, and Megan acting as a quiet, watchful guardian, ensuring no one overwhelmed him. They were a perfect, self-contained team, their movements a seamless, practiced dance.

Later in the evening, Chloé leaned over to her two companions. "Okay," she whispered, "I think after that last glass of Orchard Crest Sparkling Blush, I need to find the restroom."

Without a word, Megan stood up, and Alex turned his chair. The three of them began to make their way across the ballroom together. Aunt Geneviève, seeing them all moving in a group, looked a little confused. "*Tout va bien, mes chéris?*" she asked. (Is everything alright, my dears?)

"Everything's perfect, Tante," Chloé said with a warm, reassuring smile. "We're just going to the restroom."

They found the large, accessible family restroom, and the three of them went inside, closing the door behind them. It wasn't a big, dramatic statement. It was just a simple, unshakeable fact of their new life. Where one went, the others went. There was no longer any question of privacy between them; there was only the absolute, non-negotiable priority of Alex's safety and comfort.

They returned to the party a few minutes later, their bond a quiet, powerful presence that everyone in the room could feel, even if they didn't fully understand it. As the night wound down, the three of them found their way back to the dance floor for the final, slow song. They came together for a final, three-person hug, a perfect, quiet moment that celebrated their unique, unbreakable, and now publicly declared family unit.
