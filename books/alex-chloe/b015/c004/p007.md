# Part 7: Les Discours (The Toasts)

As the evening deepened and the dance floor pulsed with a happy, bilingual energy, the Master of Ceremonies once again took the stage, calling for everyone's attention. A hush fell over the ballroom as he announced, first in French and then in English, that it was time for the toasts.

The first to speak was the bride's father, Thomas Mason. He stood, a glass of Orchard Crest Sparkling Blush held high, his expression one of profound, paternal love. He spoke entirely in his warm, native Québécois French.

"Mes amis, ma famille," he began, his voice thick with emotion. "Aujourd'hui, je n'ai pas perdu une fille, j'ai officiellement gagné un fils. Alex, depuis le premier jour où tu as franchi notre pelouse, nous avons vu la joie que tu apportais à notre Chloé. Nous avons vu ta force, ton intelligence, et ton cœur immense. Te voir aujourd'hui, debout et fier à côté d'elle, c'est le plus beau cadeau qu'un père puisse demander. À Chloé et Alex! À une vie de bonheur! Santé!"

(My friends, my family. Today, I have not lost a daughter; I have officially gained a son. Alex, from the very first day you crossed our lawn, we saw the joy you brought to our Chloé. We saw your strength, your intelligence, and your immense heart. To see you today, standing tall and proud beside her, is the greatest gift a father could ask for. To Chloé and Alex! To a life of happiness! Cheers!)

Next, James Miller stood, his own eyes shining. He spoke in clear, heartfelt English. "I have to agree with my good friend Thomas, though I'm not even going to try to say it as elegantly as he did," he said, a warm laugh rippling through the American guests. "When our son Alex met Chloé, it was like a light turned on in his world. She taught him how to be brave. She showed him a world of friendship he had never known. And then, she saved his life. Chloé, you are more than a daughter-in-law to us; you are our daughter in every way that matters. To Alex and Chloé, my son and my new daughter. I love you both."

Finally, the MC announced, "And now, a few words from our Best Woman, Megan Carter."

Megan stood, her own glass raised, a mischievous but deeply loving smile on her face. "For those of you who don't know me, I'm Alex's favorite cousin," she began, which earned a happy laugh from the crowd.

She paused, and her gaze found Chloé's across the table. Chloé gave her a small, almost imperceptible nod, her eyes shining with a brilliant, loving smile that said, *Go on. Say the whole thing. You've earned it.*

Megan's grin widened. "And," she continued, her voice full of a fond, playful confidence, "I am also, for the record, his most favorite woman in the whole wide world."

This earned an even bigger, more appreciative laugh, especially from the family members who understood the profound truth behind the joke.

"I've had a front-row seat to this incredible story since the very beginning," Megan went on, her tone now shifting to one of deep affection. "I was there for the bad sci-fi movies, the quiet talks, and the day this brilliant girl with coppery red hair first crossed the lawn and changed my cousin's entire world."

She turned her gaze to the happy couple. "I have never seen two people who are a more perfect team. You are each other's quiet place, each other's strength, and each other's biggest fan." She then looked directly at Alex, her voice full of a deep, sisterly love. "Cuz, seeing you standing here today, so full of joy and confidence... it's one of the proudest moments of my life." She raised her glass high. "To the happy couple, and to the third musketeer who is so honored to be a part of their team. To Alex and Chloé!" The room erupted in a final, thunderous cheer.
