# Part 11: La Fin de la Soirée (The End of the Night)

As the final, slow song faded and the house lights of the ballroom came up, a profound sense of happy, bone-deep weariness settled over the wedding party. The reception was over. It had been a perfect, joyous, and emotionally overwhelming day.

The exit was a long, happy, and tearful affair. The entire Miller and Carter clan from Virginia enveloped Alex, Chloé, and Megan in a massive group hug, a final, loving send-off before they headed back to their hotel in North Hatley.

"We are so, so proud of all three of you," Emily Miller said, her own face wet with happy tears.

"Best wedding I've ever been to," James added, giving his son's shoulder a firm, proud squeeze.

Once the American contingent had departed, the "three musketeers" were swept up by the Leclercs for the short drive back to their family home in Stanstead.

When they arrived, Aunt Geneviève, a warm, knowing smile on her face, led them past the main guest rooms. "We have a special place for you three tonight," she said, opening the door to what was once her son Mathieu's childhood bedroom.

The room had been completely transformed. The single bed was gone. In its place, the Leclercs had set up a comfortable, temporary king-sized bed, piled high with pillows and the family's softest quilts. It was an unspoken, profound, and deeply loving gesture. They had seen the dynamic of their family, and they had prepared a sanctuary that would fit them all.

"We figured you would be more comfortable all together," Uncle David said with a warm, gentle smile. "A team should stick together."

Chloé’s eyes filled with a fresh wave of grateful tears. She just hugged her aunt and uncle fiercely.

Later, after the final, quiet goodnights, the three of them were finally alone. They went through their familiar, practiced bedtime routine. Alex settled into the middle of the big, comfortable bed, a look of profound, peaceful contentment on his face.

Chloé climbed in on one side, and Megan on the other. It wasn't a pull-out sofa or a temporary arrangement. It was a bed, a space, prepared for them with love and a complete, unquestioning understanding of their unique bond.

He was flanked, held, and protected by his two most favorite women in the world, in the heart of his new, extended family. Yes, a bet is a bet, and a toque is a toque, but in the quiet darkness of the Leclerc house, surrounded by love on all sides, Alex Miller was the luckiest man in the entire world, eh?
