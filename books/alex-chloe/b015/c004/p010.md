# Part 10: La Dernière Danse (The Last Dance)

As the evening began to wind down, the dance floor a happy, chaotic mix of classic rock and Québécois folk tunes, the Master of Ceremonies stepped up to the microphone one last time. A hush fell over the ballroom as a new, different kind of music began. It wasn't a party anthem or a slow ballad. It was the powerful, soaring opening notes of "Mon Nord Constant (My Constant North)," the iconic, emotionally charged ballad by the legendary Québécoise singer Arielle Dubois.

The MC's voice was a warm, quiet hush over the room. He spoke first in French, then in English. "Ladies and gentlemen, for the final dance of the evening, Alex has a special request. This song... it was a favorite of a person who caused him a great deal of pain. Tonight, surrounded by the people who truly love him, he is not just listening to it. He is taking it back."

He continued, his voice full of a deep, heartfelt respect. "This is not a dance of romance. It is a dance of victory. It is a dance to celebrate the two women who have shown him what it truly means 'to be loved more.' Please welcome to the floor, for one last dance, Alex, his wife Chloé, and his Best Woman, Megan."

There was no confusion in the room, only a profound, quiet understanding. The guests parted, creating a space in the center of the dance floor.

Alex, who had been sitting at their table, engaged the standing function of his chair, rising to his full height. Chloé and Megan, who were in on the plan, walked onto the floor from either side of him.

They met in the middle. He didn't choose between them. He simply opened his arms.

Chloé stepped into his embrace on one side, her head finding its familiar place on his shoulder. Megan stepped into his embrace on the other, her own arm wrapping securely around his back. Alex's arms, in turn, held them both, one around his wife, the other around his cousin.

As the band launched into a powerful, moving, and ultimately triumphant rendition of the song, it wasn't a dance in the traditional sense. It was a slow, gentle, three-person sway in the center of the room. A quiet, perfect, and unbreakable circle of love. It was a symbolic act of conquering his last demon. He was surrounded not by the needy, possessive "love" of his past, but by the true, selfless, and powerful love of his present.

From their tables, their four parents watched, their faces wet with tears, a look of profound, absolute peace on their faces. They were not just watching a dance; they were watching the beautiful, triumphant culmination of everything they had fought for. This was the sanctuary they had all built, together.

As the final, soaring notes of the song faded into a respectful silence, the three of them did not immediately break apart. They just held on for a moment longer, a single, indivisible unit. The wedding was over. The party was ending. And the last ghost of Vera Kowalski had been officially, and permanently, exorcised. For Alex, Chloé, and Megan, in the quiet, shared space of that final embrace, the rest of their lives had just truly, and beautifully, begun.
