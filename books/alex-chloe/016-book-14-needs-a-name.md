# Book 14: Needs a Name

## Chapter X: The Revenge of the Brawlers

### Part 1: The Boston Showdown (Fall 2011)

The trip to Southern New England in the fall of 2011 was a nostalgic journey. The week culminated in the ultimate pilgrimage, a high-stakes event that had the entire family buzzing: a trip to the arena in Boston for Game 7 of the Federated Hockey League Championship Finals. It was the ultimate showdown: the Boston Brawlers versus the Montréal Voyageurs.

This time, the tables were turned. Alex, Megan, and their parents were on home turf, a confident island of black and gold. Chloé and her parents were the brave ambassadors of the visiting team, proudly wearing their red, white, and blue jerseys.

Before the game, in the bustling concourse, Alex and Megan, a unified front of mischievous energy, laid down the challenge.

CHLOÉ, Alex's AAC device announced, its voice clear over the arena noise. THE ULTIMATE BET. A REMATCH.

"That's right, Mason," Megan added, a wicked grin on her face. "Loser wears the winner's colors for the rest of the trip. The *full* uniform."

Chloé, full of confidence after the legendary 2010 victory, laughed. "*Absolument!*" she declared. "Get ready to wear red again, you two!"

The game was a brutal, hard-fought battle. It was tied heading into the final minutes of the third period. A face-off in the Voyageurs' end. The Brawlers won the draw, a quick pass, a slapshot... Goal! The Brawlers took the lead with forty seconds left. The Boston crowd erupted in a single, deafening roar that shook the building to its foundations.

This time, it was Chloé, Sophie, and Thomas who slumped in their seats in stunned, horrified disbelief. Alex and Megan let out a triumphant shout, high-fiving each other with a force that echoed a year of pent-up anticipation. The final horn sounded. Boston had won the championship.

A triumphant, almost feral grin spread across Alex's face. He turned to Chloé, who looked utterly devastated. He typed a single, perfect, merciless sentence.

TIME TO PAY UP, MRS. MILLER.

James and Megan, a look of pure, unadulterated glee on their faces, both reached under their seats and pulled out large, full bags from the pro shop. "I believe this is for you, Chloé," James said, his voice dripping with satisfaction.

He pulled out a pristine Boston Brawlers jersey. Chloé groaned. But then Megan pulled out a pair of black and gold fleece pants. And then Brawlers socks. And finally, a black knit hat with the Brawlers logo on it.

Chloé's jaw dropped. "Oh, no," she breathed, realizing the full, pre-meditated horror of her fate. "You even got the toque."

James Miller, who overheard her, spun around. In his theatrical, comical outrage, his native Rhode Island accent flared up. "A what?" he demanded. "Honey, I love you like a daughtah, but that is blasphemy! Sacrilege! In this holy temple of Bahston sports, that is a BEANIE! A Brawlahs beanie!"

Chloé, even in her moment of defeat, drew herself up with a look of unshakable conviction. "In the civilized world, Mr. Miller," she said, "it's called a toque."

A group of nearby Brawlers fans roared with laughter and approval. "Listen to yah fathah-in-law, kid! It's a beanie!" one of them yelled. The supportive chant immediately began. "Put the beanie on! Put the beanie on!"

Chloé sighed theatrically. With the entire section now watching, she slowly, painfully, pulled her beloved Voyageurs jersey off, handing it to a grim-faced Thomas. She then took the proffered Brawlers jersey and, to a fresh wave of triumphant and good-natured applause, pulled it on.

Alex and Megan were already there with their phones, snapping pictures to immortalize her "surrender." They leaned in for a photo, two victorious Americans flanking their newly-Brawlerized Canadian, their faces masks of pure victory.

"It looks good on you," Alex's device deadpanned.

As they made their way out of the arena, a group of celebrating Brawlers fans saw Chloé and cheered. "Alright! New fan!" one of them yelled.

This was the moment Alex and Megan had been waiting for.

"Oh, she's not a new fan!" Megan called out, her voice full of glee. "She's a die-hard Voyageurs fan! From Québec! She lost the bet on the final!"

The Brawlers fans roared with a new level of appreciative, merciless laughter. Chloé's face turned a deep crimson.

"I'm from Vermont, technically!" she protested, trying to find a loophole.

Alex, savoring his victory, typed a quick, devastating rebuttal. YOU DIDN'T SEEM VERY 'VERMONT' IN THAT MONTRÉAL SPORTS BAR LAST YEAR WHEN YOU WERE YELLING AT US IN FRENCH.

The crowd howled. Chloé was beaten. She had no defense. She buried her face in Alex's shoulder, laughing despite her humiliation. The rivalry had come full circle. It was her turn to endure the teasing, to wear the uniform of shame, and to be, for one night, the most devoted-looking Boston Brawlers fan in the entire city.

### Part 2: The Boston Victory Parade (Fall 2011)

The morning after the championship win, the city of Boston was a triumphant sea of black and gold. In their hotel room, Chloé Miller stared at the Brawlers uniform of shame, laid out on a chair. "I can't believe I have to wear this in public," she groaned.

"A bet's a bet, my love," Alex's device chirped from across the room, its synthesized voice dripping with feigned sympathy.

Megan, already proudly wearing her own Brawlers beanie, just grinned. "It's a good look for you, Chloé. It really brings out the color of your eyes."

Just then, James Miller burst into the room, his face beaming, holding a copy of the *Boston Beacon*. "Good news!" he announced, his voice full of glee. "They've thrown together a last-minute victory parade! It starts near the arena in two hours. We can't miss it!"

Chloé stared at him in horror. "A parade? Mr. Miller, you can't be serious. I can't go to a Brawlers victory parade dressed like... like *this*." She gestured to the pile of enemy clothing.

"Of course you can!" James said, his grin widening. "It's the perfect way to show your respect for the champions. It's non-negotiable!"

Reluctantly, she pulled on the black and gold fleece pants, the socks, the slightly-too-large jersey, and finally, the offending knit hat. The family's mission for the day was now set: they were going to the parade, and Chloé was the main attraction.

They took The B to the parade route, which was already packed shoulder-to-shoulder with thousands of ecstatic fans. The moment Chloé, in her full Voyageurs-fan-in-Brawlers-clothing glory, stepped onto the street, the good-natured teasing began. A group of college kids started a "Let's Go Brawlahs!" chant in her direction. Chloé just smiled a tight, pained smile and endured it.

They found a spot with a good view of the street. As the duck boats carrying the victorious players rolled by, the crowd's roar was deafening. James, in his element, made sure everyone around them knew the story.

"See her?" he'd yell to a group of nearby fans, pointing a proud thumb at Chloé. "She's a die-hard Montréal Voyageurs fan! All the way from Québec! She lost a very important bet last night!"

A fresh wave of appreciative, merciless laughter erupted from the crowd each time. Chloé's face turned a deep crimson. "I'm from Vermont, technically!" she protested, her voice a desperate plea that was mostly lost in the noise.

Alex, savoring his victory, just grinned. Chloé saw his thumb move to the main screen of his AAC device and press a single, custom-made icon he had clearly programmed the night before just for this occasion. A look of amused annoyance crossed her face as she realized his revenge was pre-meditated. A moment later, his device delivered the devastating, pre-recorded rebuttal.

YOU DIDN'T SEEM VERY 'VERMONT' IN THAT MONTRÉAL SPORTS BAR LAST YEAR WHEN YOU WERE YELLING AT US IN FRENCH.

The fans who were close enough to hear his device howled with laughter. Megan, who had been laughing so hard she was crying, gave her cousin a triumphant high-five. Chloé was beaten. She had no defense. She buried her face in Alex's shoulder, laughing despite her humiliation. The rivalry had come full circle. She had endured the ultimate good-natured punishment: being forced to celebrate her most hated rival's greatest victory, surrounded by their most passionate fans, while dressed as one of them.

### Part 3: The Long Drive of Shame (The Next Day)

The morning after the victory parade was a study in contrasts. Alex, Megan, and James Miller were a cheerful, efficient unit, packing their bags with the crisp energy of a winning team. Chloé, however, moved with the theatrical slowness of a condemned prisoner, staring at the pile of black and gold clothing she was required to wear.

"Do I really have to wear it in the car?" she asked, her voice a low grumble.

"A bet's a bet, Mrs. Miller," Alex's device chirped from its charging station. The synthesized voice was cheerful. Too cheerful.

With a final, profound sigh, she pulled on the uniform of shame. In the hotel lobby, they met Thomas and Sophie. Thomas, a loyal Voyageurs fan to his core, just shook his head with a pained smile. "Ma pauvre fille," he murmured, patting her on the back. (My poor girl.)

The drive out of Boston was slow, but the mood in the two-car convoy was light. As they finally left the city traffic behind, merging from I-93 onto the straight shot of State Route 24, Megan, who was co-piloting for her father in the lead car, picked up the walkie-talkie.

"Now entering the final leg of the punishment tour, Chloé!" Megan's voice crackled through the speaker in the van where Chloé was driving her parents. "Next stop, the glorious Sakonnet River Bridge and Aquidneck Island!"

Chloé just grumbled into her own walkie-talkie, "Just drive, Carter."

The hour-long drive passed with a steady stream of good-natured ribbing from the lead car. They crossed the gleaming Sakonnet River Bridge, the water sparkling below, leaving the mainland behind. The landscape shifted to the quieter, more coastal feel of Portsmouth, Rhode Island. Chloé followed the familiar line of cars down West Main Road, passing through Portsmouth and into Middletown.

"According to the map, our hotel is just past One Mile Corner, before Broadway gets into Newport proper," Megan's voice announced.

True to their practical nature, they pulled into the parking lot of a clean, unassuming, and reasonably priced chain hotel in Middletown. As they unloaded their bags, Chloé finally pulled off the offending black knit hat, running a hand through her hair with a sigh of relief.

"I can't believe I wore that thing all morning," she muttered to herself, tossing it onto the passenger seat. "Stupid... toque."

Alex, who was maneuvering his power chair out of the van nearby, had his Quantum Note in hand. He'd heard her perfectly. He simply typed one word, the small device's robotic voice a final, perfect jab.

BEANIE.

Chloé just shot him a look of pure, loving, and utterly defeated annoyance. The Boston leg of the trip was officially over, but the victory tour, it seemed, was just getting started.

### Part 4: Breakfast with the Enemy

The next morning, the family convoy set out for a Rhode Island institution: BayView Creamery. They went to Store Number 1, the original location in Middletown, a place James Miller had been going to his entire life.

"Ah, out in the country," James said with a nostalgic sigh as they pulled into the parking lot. "This was all farmland when I was a kid. You came out here for an ice cream, it felt like a real trip."

Inside, the air smelled of coffee and sizzling bacon. The seven of them slid into a large booth. Alex and Megan, looking like proud, happy fans in their Boston Brawlers jerseys and jeans, sat on one side. Chloé, however, sat beside Alex in her full, glorious "Uniform of Shame"—the Brawlers jersey, the black and gold fleece pants, and the offending black knit hat pulled down over her ears. She looked less like a fan and more like a mascot who had lost a very public, very painful bet.

The waitress, a cheerful woman with a thick local accent, came over. "Well now, look at you!" she said, smiling directly at Chloé. "Biggest Brawlahs fan in the whole place, I bet! What can I get for yah?"

"Just coffee, please. Black," Chloé managed, her smile tight and pained.

James, Megan, and Alex all ordered the restaurant's signature platter, the "BayView Bovine Breakfast," and with it, a regional delicacy. "And we'll all have a Coffee Milk," James added.

Sophie Mason, seated next to her husband Thomas, looked curious. "Coffee Milk? What is that?"

"It's a Rhode Island thing," Megan explained happily. "It's like chocolate milk, but with a sweet coffee syrup instead. It's the best." She then turned a brilliant, wicked smile on Chloé. "You know, Chloé, you really should try one. You can't get the full, authentic New England experience without it."

Chloé's eyes narrowed. "Wait a minute. Is this... part of the bet?"

Alex and Megan exchanged a knowing, silent smirk but didn't answer. Chloé let out a long, theatrical sigh. She knew it wasn't *technically* in the terms, but it was absolutely part of the punishment.

"Fine," she conceded to the waitress. "One Coffee Milk. But I'm officially lodging a protest with the international governing body of hockey wagers."

As the food arrived, the happy, boisterous chaos of seven family members catching up over breakfast began to swell. The clatter of silverware, the multiple, overlapping conversations, the scraping of chairs on the linoleum floor—a familiar, buzzing static began to build in Alex's mind. He tensed slightly, his focus starting to drift as the sensory input became overwhelming.

Chloé, sensing his shift, instinctively moved a little closer, her presence a quiet buffer against the noise. She engaged him in a low, direct conversation about their plans for the day, giving his brain a single, calm thread to focus on. On his other side, Megan subtly steered the main family conversation towards a less chaotic topic, lowering the overall volume. He appreciated their seamless, unspoken teamwork more than he could ever say.

A few minutes later, as the noise subsided, a quiet moment settled between Alex and Chloé. He reached out under the table and took her hand. She looked at him, her expression a mixture of mock annoyance and deep affection. He gave her a slow, brilliant smile, and his AAC device voiced a new message, quiet and just for her.

I WOULDN'T TRADE YOU FOR THE WORLD, CHLOÉ. EVEN IF YOU CHEER FOR THE WRONG TEAM.

A genuine, warm smile finally broke through her theatrical misery. She squeezed his hand. "I love you too, you big jerk," she whispered. "But for the record, it's still a toque."

BEANIE, his device voiced, a final, perfect jab.

"He's right, you know," Megan added, her voice full of a triumphant, loving grin. "It's a beanie."

The three of them burst into a shared, happy laugh. The sensory overload from a few minutes ago was already a distant memory, chased away by the familiar, comforting rhythm of their favorite argument.
