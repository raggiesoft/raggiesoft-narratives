# Part 4: The Discovery

The morning they were supposed to tour Chesapeake Crossings was grey and somber, mirroring their mood. The apartment hunt felt like a series of closed doors and near-disasters.

After their afternoon classes, Chloé and Alex were driving home, both quiet and contemplative. As Chloé brought the Avalon to a stop at the stop sign for the Buckner Boulevard exit, Alex’s gaze drifted to the large, well-maintained apartment complex directly across the street. He saw a large "Now Leasing" banner.

He nudged Chloé’s arm and pointed. CHLOÉ. WHAT ABOUT THERE? his Quantum Note voiced.

On a shared, unspoken whim, she turned into the apartment complex. As they walked toward the leasing office, Chloé pulled out her phone and started a video call. "Megan," she said, "we're checking out a place on a whim. Be our eyes and ears from afar."

Megan's cheerful face appeared on the screen, a professional-looking office visible behind her. "On it!" she said. "It's an in-office day for me, so I'm stuck at my desk anyway. Show me everything!"

They met the landlord, a kind, no-nonsense woman named Mrs. Gable. She had a first-floor, two-bedroom model unit available to show them immediately. Chloé acted as a mobile camera, showing Megan every detail as they toured the apartment. The hallways were wide, the layout was open, and the amenities—a full-sized dishwasher, an in-unit washer and dryer—drew impressed comments from both Chloé and Megan.

Mrs. Gable explained that the unit was already equipped with grab bars and that all the other reasonable modifications they needed could be written into the lease.

From the phone, Megan’s voice was full of excitement. "Guys. This is it. This is the one."

CHLOÉ. THIS IS THE ONE, Alex's device voiced, a profound certainty in its tone.

"It really is," she breathed. She turned to Mrs. Gable. "We need to call our parents. Right now. Can they come see it?"

Less than twenty minutes later, the four parents arrived. They walked through the apartment, their caution melting away at the practical layout and the sheer, unadulterated happiness on their children's faces. Within the hour, they were all in the leasing office, with Megan still present via video call on Chloé's laptop, which was set up on the desk.

A lease was drawn up for all three tenants: Alex, Chloé, and Megan.

"Now, for the signatures," Mrs. Gable said. "Alex and Chloé are here, but what's the best way to get Megan's signature?"

James Miller, ever the planner, looked at the laptop screen. "Meg, you're at the office, right? Do you have a fax machine there?"

"Yep, there's one right down the hall in the mailroom," Megan replied.

"Perfect," James said to Mrs. Gable. "Could you please fax the signature pages of the lease and the addendum to this number?" He read Megan's office fax number from the screen. "She'll sign them and fax them right back to you."

"That's an excellent solution," Mrs. Gable agreed, taking the number.

She fed the pages into her office fax machine. A few minutes of tense, quiet waiting followed, broken only by the low hum of the office equipment. Then, the fax machine on Mrs. Gable's desk whirred to life, slowly spitting out a new set of pages. She picked them up and smiled. On each one was Megan's clear, confident signature.

A deposit was paid. A move-in date was set for the beginning of April.

As they left the office, the weight of the past month of searching lifted completely. They stood on the sidewalk, looking at the building that would be their first home, with Megan's excited, triumphant voice cheering through the laptop speakers. The plan was now a signed, sealed, and fax-delivered reality.
