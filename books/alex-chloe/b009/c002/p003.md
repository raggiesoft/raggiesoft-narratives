# Part 3: The Final, Desperate Call

The quiet of the Miller house that evening was a fragile thing. After the raw, ugly confrontation on the front lawn, Alex was a wreck. He sat on the living room sofa, flanked on both sides by Chloé and Megan, who had formed a silent, unwavering cocoon of safety around him. He was crying, quiet, shuddering sobs of a boy who just wanted the nightmare to be over.

In her own apartment, defeated and consumed by a venomous, impotent rage, Vera stared at the small plastic stick in her hand. Two pink lines. It was positive. The vague, terrifying suspicion she'd had on the lawn was now a concrete, biological fact. She was pregnant.

And in her panicked, desperate mind, there was only one possible conclusion: *It has to be Alex's. It has to be.* The thought wasn't a happy one; it was a weapon. It was her trump card. It was the one thing she was sure would bring him back under her control. He owed her this.

She picked up her phone and, with a shaking, determined finger, dialed the one number she knew to call: the Miller family home.

The phone rang, a shrill interruption in the quiet living room. James Miller, his own face a mask of cold fury from the afternoon's events, answered it, putting it on speakerphone for everyone to hear.

"Hello, Miller residence."

"Mr. Miller," the voice on the other end was syrupy sweet, but with an undercurrent of steel. "It's Vera Kowalski. I need to speak with Alex. It's urgent."

The sound of her voice made Alex flinch, a fresh wave of tears streaming down his face. He buried his face in Chloé's shoulder, just wanting it to go away. Chloé and Megan immediately tightened their protective embrace around him, shielding him from the voice on the phone.

James's voice was dangerously quiet. "Vera. You have a lot of nerve calling this house after what happened this afternoon."

"Things have changed," Vera said, her voice taking on a new, triumphant tone. "You need to put Alex on the phone. He needs to know that I'm pregnant. With *his* baby."

A stunned, disgusted silence fell over the room. James, who knew the irrefutable medical fact of his son's infertility, felt a wave of pure, white-hot rage. He knew this wasn't a mistake; it was a malicious, calculated lie. He refused to even engage with it.

"This conversation is over," James stated, his voice devoid of any warmth.

"No, it's not!" Vera shrieked, her desperation mounting. "He's the father! He has responsibilities! You can't just keep him from me!"

"Listen to me very carefully, Vera," James continued, his voice a low, dangerous growl. "My son has made his choice. He has chosen to build his life with his wife. He has also chosen to sever all ties with you. Permanently. This is your final and only warning. You will not come near our son or his wife again. You will not call them. You will not contact this family. If you violate this, if you cause any more trouble, we will not hesitate to involve the police and pursue a formal restraining order. Is that understood?"

The line was filled with the sound of Vera's ragged, furious breathing as her final, desperate gambit failed. It was followed by a torrent of screamed threats and curses before she finally slammed the phone down.

James slowly replaced the receiver. He looked at his son, who was still trembling, safely cocooned between Chloé and Megan. The final, toxic tie was severed. Alex was finally, truly, safe.
