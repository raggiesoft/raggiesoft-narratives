# Part 1: The Morning Commute (Monday, January 12, 2004)

The alarm clock in Alex Miller's room on Lisa Court beeped at 6:30 AM, a familiar sound heralding an entirely new kind of day. Chloé stirred beside him in their new queen-sized bed, a sleepy murmur escaping her. The past week since their quiet courthouse wedding had been a whirlwind of family, legal paperwork, and settling back into the comforting rhythm of their new, expanded household. Today was the first day of the rest of their plan.

Down the hall, another alarm went off in what was now firmly "Megan's Room." Their mornings had quickly fallen into a new, efficient rhythm. While Alex and Chloé focused on getting ready for their first day of classes, Megan was in her own professional mindset. Her graduation from VDU meant the job hunt was on, and her days were a structured routine of updating her resume, scouring online job boards, and preparing for interviews.

After a quick, cheerful breakfast with Emily and James, the three of them headed out into the crisp January air. Megan was the first to leave, hopping into her own reliable Holt sedan. "Good luck today, you two!" she called out, giving them a confident wave. "Don't cause too much trouble. I'll see you for dinner!" She pulled out of the driveway, off to the public library to begin her day's work of building her own future.

Alex and Chloé, meanwhile, began their own familiar routine. Chloé backed her used Horizon Avalon out of the Masons' driveway. It wasn't fancy, but it was reliable, and most importantly, the trunk was just large enough for Alex’s folded wheelchair, the tray, and the mounting arm. His primary AAC device was safely stowed in its padded laptop case on the back seat.

With James's help, Alex transferred into the passenger seat. Chloé slid behind the wheel, a confident smile on her face. "Ready for our first day of college, Mr. Miller?" she asked, a playful glint in her eye.

Alex grinned, the morning light catching the simple gold band on his finger. He reached into his jacket pocket and pulled out his "Quantum Note QN-300." It was a wedding present from all four of their parents, a tool for the moments when his main communicator was packed away. His thumbs moved deftly over the tiny, backlit keyboard. The device's speaker was small, its synthesized voice simpler and more robotic than his main one, but the message was clear.

READY, MRS. MILLER. DON'T BE LATE FOR BIOLOGY.

Chloé laughed, a bright, happy sound that filled the car. "Right. Wouldn't want to get on Dr. Sharma's bad side on day one."

The drive from Lisa Court to the CCHR campus was short and familiar. For Alex, it was a world away from the terrifying, lonely bus ride to Portsmouth. There was no anxiety, no dread. There was just the quiet hum of the Avalon's engine, the low melody of a rock station on the radio, and Chloé's steady presence beside him. This wasn't a journey into a hostile unknown; it was their commute.
