# Part 5: Building a Foundation

The excitement of finding their first apartment settled into a quiet, purposeful energy. With their new home secured, a new topic began to surface in the evenings. One night, as they were reviewing their shared budget, Chloé pointed to a line item labeled "Personal Spending/Entertainment." It was empty.

"The parents are amazing," she said. "But I was thinking... it would be nice to be able to afford a pizza on a Friday night. And more importantly," she added, her expression turning serious, "we need to start our 'Survival Fund' for Washington. We can't rely on them forever."

Alex nodded, his focus absolute. I AGREE, his Quantum Communicator XT voiced. A JOB. FOR BOTH OF US. ON CAMPUS.

The next day, between classes, they went to the student services office. The plan clicked into place with a perfect symmetry. Chloé saw a posting for a "Library Circulation Desk Assistant" and her face lit up. A few listings down, Alex pointed to one that read: "Student Aide, Academic Computer Labs."

They were both offered jobs on the spot, their interviews a testament to their skills and maturity.

They returned to their apartment that afternoon, buzzing with the news. They found Megan in their small office, a huge, triumphant grin on her face. "You are not going to believe this," she said, holding up a formal offer letter. "I got a job! A real one!"

"Meg, that's amazing!" Chloé exclaimed, rushing to give her a hug.

"It's perfect," Megan continued, her eyes shining. "It's a full-time, entry-level position as a Technical Writer for a medical device company over in Norfolk. I'll be writing the user manuals for their new equipment. And the best part? It's a hybrid role. I only have to go into the office two days a week. The rest of the time, I can work from home—from right here."

A profound sense of victory settled over the three of them. It was all falling into place.

That afternoon, they sat down together to draft an important email. After accepting the computer lab position, Alex, with Chloé's and Megan's help, composed a formal request for an accommodation.

He wrote: "Thank you for the job offer. I am excited to accept. I do have one request regarding my placement. As you know from my student file, I have several medical conditions that can occasionally require immediate support. My wife, Chloé Miller, who has just been hired at the library circulation desk, is my primary caregiver and designated emergency contact. For my safety and well-being, it would be a necessary and deeply appreciated accommodation if I could be stationed at the computer lab located within the main library, to ensure I am in close proximity to her during my work hours."

They addressed the email to the head of academic computing and CC'ed the CCHR Disability Support Services office. The reply came less than an hour later. The accommodation was approved without question.

A deep, shared sense of accomplishment filled their small apartment. The three of them had done it. They were no longer just a support system; they were a fully employed household, a team of young adults, all building a future, together.
