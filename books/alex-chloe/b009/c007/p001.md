# Part 1: The Keys (Friday, April 2, 2004)

The first week of April arrived on a wave of warm, sunny Virginia weather, a perfect parallel to the bright hope that filled their small household. The day had finally come. Their apartment was ready.

After their last class on Friday afternoon, Alex and Chloé walked across Buckner Boulevard from the CCHR campus. They met Megan, who had just finished her workday, in the parking lot of their new apartment complex. The three of them walked together to the leasing office, a quiet, purposeful team ready to take the next big step.

They met the landlord, Mrs. Gable, who greeted them with a warm, genuine smile.

"Everything is all set for you three," she said. "The modifications were completed this morning, right on schedule. I think you'll be very pleased." She handed Chloé a small envelope. Inside were three shiny new keys on a simple metal ring.

Chloé took them, her hand trembling slightly. She immediately handed one to Alex and one to Megan. They all looked at their keys, then at each other, their faces beaming with pure, unadulterated joy. This was it. The key to their future, to their first home, just in time for the weekend.

THANK YOU, MRS. GABLE, Alex's device voiced, the words full of a gratitude that went beyond just the keys. FOR EVERYTHING.

"It's my pleasure," she replied kindly. "Welcome home. Have a wonderful weekend moving in."
