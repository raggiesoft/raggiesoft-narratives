# Part 2: The Demand

That evening, the seven of them—the Millers, the Masons, and Megan—gathered again in the Millers' living room. James and Emily relayed Ms. Albright's assessment.

"She's confident we can shut this down," James said. "She's going to draft a letter stating our position and threatening a countersuit for malicious prosecution."

A quiet, determined relief settled over the room. But Megan, who had been listening with a focused, intense stillness, shook her head.

"It's not enough," she said, her voice cutting through the quiet. All eyes turned to her. "A countersuit threat is good, but it's just one lawyer's word against another's. Vera will just keep lying. She'll say the medical records are fake, that you're all conspiring against her. She'll drag this out for months, maybe years. You have to end it. Forever."

"What are you thinking, honey?" Emily asked.

Megan looked directly at Alex, her expression one of fierce, strategic loyalty. "You need to demand a DNA test," she stated, her voice devoid of any doubt. "A pre-natal one, as soon as it's legally possible. We all know you're infertile. We know that baby isn't yours. This isn't about proving anything to *us*. It's about getting cold, hard, scientific proof that she is lying. Proof that no judge, no lawyer, and not even *she* can argue with. It's the one thing that will make her go away for good."

Chloé, who had been sitting beside Alex, her hand gripping his, looked up, a proud, fierce smile on her lips. "She's right," Chloé said, her own voice ringing with conviction. "That's a checkmate. That's how we end this."

James and Emily looked at each other and nodded in unanimous, resolute agreement. It was an aggressive move, but it was the right one.

Alex, who had been listening to this debate about his future, felt a surge of empowerment. He was surrounded by a team of warriors, all ready to fight for him. He looked at Megan, at the brilliant, strategic mind of his favorite cousin. He looked at Chloé, at the unwavering, absolute belief in her eyes. He was not a victim. He was a part of this team. He reached for his AAC, his fingers moving with a new, steady resolve.

YES, his device voiced, the single word a powerful, definitive command. DO IT. I WANT THE DNA TEST.

The decision was made. The strategy was set. They were no longer just defending against Vera's attack; they were launching a counter-offensive, armed with the one weapon she could never defeat: the truth.
