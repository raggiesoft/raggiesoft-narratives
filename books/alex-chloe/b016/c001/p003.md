# Part 3: The Long Drive of Shame (The Next Day)

The morning after the victory parade was a study in contrasts. Alex, Megan, and James Miller were a cheerful, efficient unit, packing their bags with the crisp energy of a winning team. Chloé, however, moved with the theatrical slowness of a condemned prisoner, staring at the pile of black and gold clothing she was required to wear.

"Do I really have to wear it in the car?" she asked, her voice a low grumble.

"A bet's a bet, Mrs. Miller," Alex's device chirped from its charging station. The synthesized voice was cheerful. Too cheerful.

With a final, profound sigh, she pulled on the uniform of shame. In the hotel lobby, they met Thomas and Sophie. Thomas, a loyal Voyageurs fan to his core, just shook his head with a pained smile. "Ma pauvre fille," he murmured, patting her on the back. (My poor girl.)

The drive out of Boston was slow, but the mood in the two-car convoy was light. As they finally left the city traffic behind, merging from I-93 onto the straight shot of State Route 24, Megan, who was co-piloting for her father in the lead car, picked up the walkie-talkie.

"Now entering the final leg of the punishment tour, Chloé!" Megan's voice crackled through the speaker in the van where Chloé was driving her parents. "Next stop, the glorious Sakonnet River Bridge and Aquidneck Island!"

Chloé just grumbled into her own walkie-talkie, "Just drive, Carter."

The hour-long drive passed with a steady stream of good-natured ribbing from the lead car. They crossed the gleaming Sakonnet River Bridge, the water sparkling below, leaving the mainland behind. The landscape shifted to the quieter, more coastal feel of Portsmouth, Rhode Island. Chloé followed the familiar line of cars down West Main Road, passing through Portsmouth and into Middletown.

"According to the map, our hotel is just past One Mile Corner, before Broadway gets into Newport proper," Megan's voice announced.

True to their practical nature, they pulled into the parking lot of a clean, unassuming, and reasonably priced chain hotel in Middletown. As they unloaded their bags, Chloé finally pulled off the offending black knit hat, running a hand through her hair with a sigh of relief.

"I can't believe I wore that thing all morning," she muttered to herself, tossing it onto the passenger seat. "Stupid... toque."

Alex, who was maneuvering his power chair out of the van nearby, had his Quantum Note in hand. He'd heard her perfectly. He simply typed one word, the small device's robotic voice a final, perfect jab.

BEANIE.

Chloé just shot him a look of pure, loving, and utterly defeated annoyance. The Boston leg of the trip was officially over, but the victory tour, it seemed, was just getting started.
