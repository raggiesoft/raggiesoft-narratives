# Part 4: The Final Push

The final weeks of the spring semester were a blur of intense focus. The excitement of their NPU acceptance letters gave way to the demanding reality of their final capstone projects. Their apartment's second bedroom, now a fully functional three-person office, became their command center.

Chloé's project was a labor of love, a deep, scholarly dive into "The Preservation of Regional Dialects in North American Libraries." She spent hours in the CCHR library, a place where she had once just been a student aide, now a true scholar, weaving together history, linguistics, and information science with a passionate, nuanced understanding that no non-native speaker could ever replicate.

Alex's project was a different kind of masterpiece. For his Computer Science capstone, he poured every ounce of his technical genius and lived experience into designing a prototype for a more intuitive, context-aware AAC interface. His project only required a design document and a simulated presentation, but in the evenings, he would work on a secret, personal extension, writing real, functional code to bring his ideas to life.

Megan was their unwavering support system, the cheerleader for their final push to the finish line. She would come home from her own job to an apartment humming with quiet, academic energy. She kept the coffee pot full, made sure they both remembered to eat, and acted as a sounding board for their ideas. She'd listen, fascinated, as Chloé explained a complex theory about linguistic preservation or as Alex demonstrated a new feature in his prototype.

The day of his capstone presentation was a triumph. He presented his design to a panel of three computer science professors, his arguments clear, logical, and revolutionary. At the end, he took a deep breath.

AND NOW, his device voiced, A BRIEF, PARTIALLY FUNCTIONAL DEMONSTRATION.

He launched the prototype application. A new, sleeker interface appeared on his screen. The professors leaned in, their expressions a mixture of surprise and intense interest. He typed a single starting word: "The." A series of contextually relevant phrases instantly appeared. He selected one with a single click.

THE THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK OF THIS PROJECT IS BASED ON…

The professors were stunned. It was a working proof-of-concept that went far beyond the requirements of the assignment. It wasn't just a capstone project; it was a powerful statement of who he was, a brilliant fusion of his lived experience and his profound technical skill.

He and Chloé both received perfect scores on their final projects, not just for the quality of their work, but for the deep, personal passion that had so clearly fueled it. They weren't just finishing their degrees; they were announcing to the world who they were and what they were capable of. They were ready for the next stage.
