# Part 1: The Power Couple of Campus

The CCHR Virginia Beach campus in the spring of 2006 was Alex and Chloé's kingdom. Alex, now twenty, was a master of his "Quantum Glide" power chair, navigating the busy hallways of the Advanced Technology Center with an effortless confidence that turned heads. His new communicator was lightning-fast. Chloé was his equal partner, walking beside him, their rhythm perfectly in sync.

Their unofficial headquarters was the library. Chloé, now a trusted senior student aide, managed the circulation desk with a calm authority, often training new hires. In the glass-walled computer lab, Alex was a legend. Students with crashed hard drives or corrupted project files would bypass the other aides and wait specifically for him, the quiet, brilliant guru who could solve any problem with a few lines of code and a patient, synthesized explanation.

One afternoon, a young woman named Jessica, a student from his statistics class, approached his station in a state of near-panic.

"Alex, you have to help me," she said, gesturing wildly at her screen. "My spreadsheet for my final project is completely messed up. The formulas are all broken, and it's due tomorrow!"

IT'S OKAY, his Quantum Communicator Pro V voiced, its tone calm and reassuring. LET ME TAKE A LOOK.

For the next fifteen minutes, Alex was a paragon of focused competence. He methodically diagnosed the problem—a circular reference in a complex formula—and patiently walked her through the steps to fix it. He was kind, he was brilliant, and he didn't make her feel foolish for her mistake.

Jessica was completely captivated. She had seen Alex in class, but this was the first time she had interacted with him one-on-one. She watched, impressed, as his hands flew across his keyboard, his intelligence a palpable, magnetic force.

"Wow," she said, leaning a little closer, placing a hand on his arm. "You're, like, a total genius with this stuff. I would have been completely lost without you." She either didn't see, or chose to ignore, the simple gold wedding band on his left hand.

Alex, focused entirely on the screen, didn't seem to notice the gesture. IT IS A COMMON MISTAKE, he typed. YOU JUST NEED TO CHECK YOUR CELL RANGES. He finalized the fix and turned the monitor back towards her. IT SHOULD BE WORKING NOW.

"You're a lifesaver," she breathed, her smile now a clear invitation. "Hey, I owe you one. Maybe I could buy you a coffee sometime? To say thank you?"

From her post at the circulation desk, Chloé watched the entire exchange, a slow, knowing, and deeply amused smile spreading across her face. She wasn't jealous. She wasn't threatened. She was profoundly, fiercely proud. For years, she had been the only one who had truly seen him, this brilliant, kind, capable man. Now, the rest of the world was finally starting to catch on.

Alex, however, was beautifully, simply, and completely oblivious to the subtext. He took her offer at face value.

THAT IS A KIND OFFER, JESSICA, BUT I AM GOING TO HAVE COFFEE WITH MY WIFE AFTER OUR SHIFT, his device stated, the tone polite and matter-of-fact. He gestured with his head towards the front of the library. CHLOÉ IS WAITING FOR ME. I AM GLAD I COULD HELP YOU WITH YOUR SPREADSHEET.

Jessica's face flushed with a brief wave of embarrassment as she finally followed his gaze and saw Chloé, who gave her a small, friendly, and completely unthreatening wave. "Oh," Jessica said, her voice a little smaller. "Right. Of course. Well, uh... thanks again." She gathered her things and hurried away.

Alex watched her go, a little confused by her abrupt departure, before turning back to his work. A moment later, his communicator pinged with a new instant message. It was from Chloé's MagnaByte laptop at the front desk.

NICE JOB, GURU, the message read. TRY NOT TO BREAK TOO MANY HEARTS. YOU'RE A MARRIED MAN, YOU KNOW.

Alex looked up and met her gaze across the library. He grinned, and for the first time, a flicker of understanding dawned on his face. He quickly typed a reply.

ONLY EVER YOURS.

She smiled back, a private, perfect communication in their shared, quiet kingdom.
