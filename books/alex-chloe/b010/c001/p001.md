# Part 1: Delivery Day (Fall 2005)

The air in Alex and Chloé's apartment across the street from CCHR was thick with an almost unbearable anticipation. It was a crisp autumn day, nearly a year after the family had made the decision to pursue new equipment for Alex. A year of specialists, evaluations, mountains of paperwork, and a grinding, persistent battle with the insurance company had all led to this moment. Today was Delivery Day.

A large van from their accessibility vendor was parked outside. In their small living room, which had been their sanctuary for over a year now, two large cardboard boxes dominated the space. All four parents—James, Emily, Thomas, and Sophie—and Megan were gathered, perched on the edge of the sofa and kitchen chairs, their faces a mixture of excitement and nervous hope. A friendly, competent technician named Dave was carefully unboxing the first, revealing the sleek, black frame of a state-of-the-art power wheelchair.

While Dave worked on the chair, he unboxed the second package: Alex’s new AAC device. "This one's a beast," Dave said, clearly impressed. "Natively running Quantum OS XN Professional, and we had the vendor pre-install the French-Canadian MUI pack you ordered. It's also got the latest Vocal-Path Suite Pro on it," he added, "fully loaded with the newest American English, Québécois French, and Metropolitan French voices. She's ready to fly."

After mounting the new device and transferring Alex's core files, Dave spent another hour with Alex, Chloé, and Megan, meticulously fine-tuning the joystick controls and seating positions while the parents watched with rapt attention. Finally, he sat back on his heels.

"Alright, Alex," Dave said, a grin on his face. "She's all yours." He paused, his eyes twinkling. "Now... for the main event. Ready to try standing?"

A tremor of fear and excitement went through Alex. He looked at Chloé, and then at Megan, his two anchors. He looked at his parents, and at Thomas and Sophie, their expressions full of love. He took a deep breath and gave Dave a firm nod.

"Okay," Dave said, talking him through it. "It's the toggle on the right side of your joystick. Just a gentle push forward."

Alex reached out, his hand steady, and engaged the control. A series of soft, powerful whirs emanated from the chair's base. It was a smooth, fluid, and silent ascent, lifting him until he was standing at his full height, his head nearly touching the ceiling of their small apartment. He looked down at his own feet, then across the room, over the back of their sofa.

Then he looked at Chloé.

She was standing a few feet away, her face wet with tears, her smile the most beautiful thing he had ever seen. For the first time, he wasn't looking up at her. They were looking directly at each other, eye to eye.

The technician, sensing the profound intimacy of the moment, cleared his throat. "I'm just... going to go organize some of this paperwork out in the hall," he said quietly, and with a knowing look at the parents, discreetly slipped out of the room.

Alex, his heart hammering in his chest, used the joystick to slowly, carefully, turn to face Chloé fully. She stepped forward, closing the distance, and wrapped her arms around his neck. Alex’s own arms, strong and sure, went around her waist, holding her close. It was a real, solid, two-feet-on-the-ground hug, a hug of perfect, balanced reciprocity.

After a long, perfect moment, they slowly pulled apart, their hands still linked. Chloé was laughing through her tears. Alex, his own eyes shining, reached for his new AAC device. The screen was bright, the new processor impossibly fast. He typed a short, simple sentence.

The new, more natural-sounding synthesized voice spoke the words, clear and full of a wonder that was all Alex's.

CHLOÉ... I CAN SEE YOUR EYES.

A fresh wave of happy tears streamed down Chloé's face. As she squeezed his hand, Alex, still standing, turned his gaze to his cousin. Megan was standing nearby, her own face a mask of pure, unadulterated pride and joy for him. He opened his other arm, a silent, clear invitation.

Megan closed the distance in two steps and wrapped him in a fierce, powerful hug of her own. This hug was different. It was a hug of profound, sisterly gratitude, a thank you for being his rescuer, his guardian, and his unwavering friend. He held her just as tightly, his own silent thank you for everything she had done.

When they pulled back, Alex didn't let go. He kept one arm around Megan's shoulders and reached again for Chloé's hand, pulling her back in. He then wrapped his other arm around his wife, bringing both of them into a three-person embrace.

He stood there, strong and tall, a fortress of love on either side of him, holding the two most important women in the entire world. From the sofa, their four parents watched, openly weeping with joy. This was it. This was the first moment of the rest of his new, unburdened life.
