# Part 6: A Sweet Discovery (Summer 2006)

After a wonderful breakfast, the three of them said a temporary goodbye to Aunt Geneviève. Back in their Holt Wayfarer, Chloé checked the time on her phone.

"Well, it's just after ten," she said. "Our check-in at the hotel isn't until three. We've got about five hours to kill. What do you guys want to do?"

Alex thought for a moment, a slow grin spreading across his face as he typed. I THINK, his device voiced, THAT WE SHOULD BEGIN OUR TRIP THE SAME WAY WE ENDED OUR LAST ONE. WITH A QUEST FOR FORBIDDEN KNOWLEDGE.

Megan, who was in the back with Alex, looked confused. "Forbidden knowledge? What are you talking about?"

Chloé laughed, catching his meaning immediately. "To the *dépanneur* it is! A pilgrimage to the site of the great Koko-Kapsel."

The Stanstead corner store was just as they remembered it. They browsed the aisles for a few minutes before inevitably making their way to the candy section. There, in a familiar, brightly colored display box, were the classic Koko-Kapsels.

"Okay," Megan said, picking one up. "I give up. What's the big deal with these things?"

"They're illegal in the States," Chloé explained, a mischievous glint in her eye. "It's a hollow chocolate egg with a little toy inside a plastic capsule. The FDA says it's a choking hazard, so you can get a massive fine for trying to bring one across the border."

"You're kidding," Megan said, her eyes wide. "A chocolate egg is a federal crime?"

"It's true," Chloé confirmed. "It’s a shame, too. The chocolate is the best."

I’VE ALWAYS WONDERED WHAT IT TASTES LIKE, Alex’s device voiced wistfully.

"Wait a second," Chloé said, her eyes lighting up. She pointed to a bright blue bag hanging on a hook right next to the display. The logo was the same, but the name was different: Koko-Bons. The bag showed a picture of small, bite-sized chocolate eggs. Chloé grabbed the bag and read the description.

"'Bite-sized milk chocolate eggs, filled with a milk and hazelnut cream.' Guys... there's no toy. It's just the chocolate!"

A look of genuine delight dawned on Alex's face. SO… WE CAN ACTUALLY BUY THESE ONES?

"Yes! They're totally legal!" Chloé said triumphantly. She reached for a bag, then paused, her practical nature taking over. "But... you know what? Let's not risk it. Imagine trying to explain the subtle difference to a U.S. border officer who's having a bad day. It's not worth the hassle. These will have to be a 'Canada-only' treat for us."

Alex and Megan both nodded, understanding immediately. The potential stress was not worth the candy.

"But," Chloé added, her grin returning, "that doesn't mean we can't enjoy them now." She turned to Megan. "And it's time for French lesson number two."

She held up the bag. "*On va manger des chocolats,*" she said slowly and clearly. (We are going to eat some chocolates.)

"*On... va... manger... des... chocolats,*" Megan repeated, her pronunciation clumsy but enthusiastic.

"Perfect!" Chloé cheered.

They bought a single bag of the Koko-Bons and sat on a nearby park bench. Chloé tore it open and handed one of the small, solid chocolate eggs to Alex, and another to Megan. They tried them. The creamy, rich milk chocolate with the smooth hazelnut filling was unlike any American candy they’d ever had. It was delicious.

"See?" Chloé said, watching their happy reactions. "Isn't it good?"

It was a small victory, but a deeply satisfying one. Alex finally got to taste a piece of Chloé's Canadian childhood, and Megan got her first real taste of Québec—both the language and the famously illegal chocolate.
