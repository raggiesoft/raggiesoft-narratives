# Part 5: An Aunt's Advice and a New Itinerary (Summer 2006)

After the warm, welcoming hugs in the driveway, Aunt Geneviève ushered the three of them into her cozy kitchen, a space that smelled faintly of yeast and cinnamon. As she poured them tall glasses of cold lemonade, she settled at the table with them, her expression full of genuine happiness.

"*Alors, mes amours, tell me everything,*" she said in her warm Québécois French, initially directing her question to Chloé and Alex. "*You are here for a whole week! What are your plans?*"

Chloé, feeling a rush of nostalgia, leaned forward. "Oh, we have so many ideas! We were thinking tomorrow morning, we could drive over to Newport for breakfast, then maybe come back here for the afternoon to see the museum..."

Geneviève listened patiently, a fond but sad smile touching her lips. When Chloé paused, she reached out and gently patted her hand, switching seamlessly to English for Megan's benefit.

"My dear," she began softly, "that sounds like a wonderful trip... from the old days. But you cannot do that anymore."

Chloé and Megan both blinked, confused. "What do you mean?" Chloé asked.

"The border, it is not a revolving door now," Geneviève explained gently to the whole group. "When you told the officer you are here for a week, they logged it. Your visit has started. If you cross back to America tomorrow, even for one hour, your 'visit to Canada' is over. To come back, you would have to go through the whole process again. They are very, very strict now. You must stay on the Canadian side."

The reality of the new rules hit them with a sudden, frustrating thud. "Oh," Chloé said, her enthusiasm deflating. "I... I didn't realize it was that strict."

A disappointed silence fell over the table. All of their fluid, easy plans of weaving back and forth between their two hometowns were impossible.

Alex, who had been listening intently, watched the disappointment on Chloé's face. He reached for his AAC. CHLOÉ. IT’S OKAY. WE CAN MAKE A NEW PLAN. WHAT IF WE DO ALL THE CANADA THINGS FIRST? THEN, ON OUR LAST DAY, WE OFFICIALLY LEAVE, AND SPEND THE WHOLE DAY ON THE AMERICAN SIDE BEFORE WE DRIVE TO THE AIRPORT.

Chloé looked at him, a new idea sparking. "You're right," she said, her practical nature taking over. She turned to her aunt. "Okay, what about this? We spend from now until Thursday morning here in Quebec. We'll do Montréal, we'll drive around the Townships, and have the big family dinner. Then, on Thursday, we say our goodbyes and officially cross back into the U.S. That gives us all day Thursday to be tourists in Derby Line and Newport before our flight."

Aunt Geneviève beamed. "*Voilà!*" she exclaimed. "Now that is a good plan! A smart compromise. You see? You can still visit both your worlds. You just have to do it one country at a time."

Megan, who had been listening intently, spoke up, a look of quiet resolve on her face. "You know," she said, looking from Chloé to her aunt, "listening to you guys talk... I want in. I don't want to just be the tourist who needs a translator. I want to learn."

Chloé and Geneviève looked at her, surprised and delighted.

"I want to learn French," Megan declared. "For real. The way you guys speak it. The *real* stuff."

Uncle David, who had just walked in, overheard her. A huge, booming laugh erupted from his chest. "*Fantastique!*" he roared. "An American who wants to learn properly! Your first lesson starts now!"

He leaned in, a conspiratorial twinkle in his eye. "Forget 'bonjour'. The most important word you need to know is *'dépanneur'*. It is where you get the chips, the soda, the lottery tickets. It is the heart of Québec civilization."

The room erupted in laughter. Megan repeated the word, her pronunciation a little clumsy but full of genuine enthusiasm. Lesson One in Québécois French had officially begun.
