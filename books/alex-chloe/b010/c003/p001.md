# Part 1: The Launch (Summer 2006)

The late July morning on Lisa Court was a study in organized chaos. The brand-new, accessible Holt Wayfarer minivan stood in the Miller driveway, its sliding door open like a welcoming mouth, ready to be filled. This wasn't the final move to Washington—that was still a month away—but the beginning of a final, grand adventure: a two-week "farewell tour" up the coast, culminating in Megan Carter's first-ever trip to Canada.

The entire "family of seven" was in motion. James and Thomas were consulting a large paper road atlas, debating the merits of one route versus another, a familiar and comforting ritual. Emily and Sophie were packing a massive cooler with enough snacks and drinks to sustain a small army.

And at the center of it all were the three travelers. Chloé, the seasoned co-pilot, was expertly arranging their suitcases in the back of the van. Megan, buzzing with the excitement of her first trip to Canada, was in charge of the "fun bag"—a collection of CDs, travel games, and ridiculously oversized bags of candy. Alex, the official navigator, was doing a final diagnostic on his Quantum Communicator Pro V, ensuring the "Quantum World-Trekker" software and his GPS puck were in perfect sync.

The goodbyes were a long, loving, and tearful affair.

"Now you call us every night, you hear?" Emily said, pulling all three of them into a massive group hug.

"And if you get lost," James added, giving Alex's shoulder a proud squeeze, "remember what I taught you. The Tappan Zee Bridge is your friend."

"*Amusez-vous bien, mes amours,*" Sophie said, kissing each of them on both cheeks. "*Et Megan, bienvenue au Canada, en avance!*" (Have fun, my loves. And Megan, welcome to Canada, in advance!)

With a final round of hugs and promises to call, the three of them piled into the van. Chloé was behind the wheel, Megan was in the passenger seat, and Alex was in his secure, locked-down position in the middle, a perfect command center.
