# Part 6: The Last Swim

One sun-drenched afternoon in the middle of August, with the date of their departure looming, the three of them made one last pilgrimage. They drove the Holt Wayfarer to the Fairlawn Recreation Center, the beloved, imperfect staple of their shared childhood. Megan wouldn't miss it for the world.

They pulled into the parking lot, expertly navigating around the familiar, crater-sized potholes. The old brick building looked exactly the same, a monument to a thousand memories.

"First things first," Megan declared as they walked into the lobby. "Let's see if a miracle has occurred." She led them to the bowling alley, only to be greeted by a familiar, hand-written "Out of Order" sign taped to the door.

AGAIN? Alex's communicator voiced, a note of deep, comical resignation in its tone.

Chloé just laughed. "Of course it is. Some things never change."

As they passed the door to the Youth Lounge, a wave of nostalgia washed over them. A young staffer, seeing them pause, smiled. "Looking for the lounge?" she asked. "The Adult Lounge is just down this hall to the left."

The three of them looked at each other, a shared, silent acknowledgment passing between them. They weren't kids anymore.

They headed up to the second floor to check in for the pool. Alex, having prepared for this, was already wearing his swimsuit, water shoes, and a swim shirt under his regular clothes. "Alright," he said to Chloé and Megan, a look of quiet confidence on his face. "I've got this. I'll meet you two on the pool deck."

He confidently wheeled himself into the men's locker room, able to change and secure his belongings independently. He met the two women out on the pool deck a few minutes later, a look of profound, quiet victory on his face.

The moment the warm, humid, chlorinated air of the natatorium hit them, they were home.

The next hour was pure, uncomplicated joy. The three of them—Alex, his wife, and his favorite cousin—swam, and splashed, and floated in the warm water. They weren't just visiting a rec center; they were revisiting their own history.

They all met in the shallow end. Alex, standing steady on the pool floor, looked at Chloé, his anchor, and then at Megan, his guardian. He opened his arms wide, a silent, joyful invitation. They both swam into his embrace at the same time, the result a happy, chaotic, and laughing tangle of limbs. It was a perfect, three-person standing hug, a final, beautiful memory in the place where so many of their foundational moments had happened.

But as the afternoon wore on, the bright sunlight streaming through the high natatorium windows began to dim. Dark, heavy-looking clouds rolled in, casting the pool in a sudden, grey gloom. A moment later, a brilliant flash of lightning lit up the sky, followed almost instantly by a deafening crack of thunder.

Immediately, a lifeguard’s whistle cut through the air. "Everybody out of the pool!" the young guard shouted. "Lightning strike in the area. Per city policy, the pool is closed for a minimum of thirty minutes!"

They made their way to the edge of the pool. Just as they were getting out, another flash of lightning lit up the now-darkened windows.

"And that resets the clock!" the lifeguard called out.

They went through the routine of changing and emerged back into the main hallway, the dream of a long, lazy afternoon of swimming now over.

"Well," Chloé said as they emerged into the main hallway, "we're stuck here for a while. What do you want to do?"

Alex gestured with his head down the hall. LET'S CHECK OUT THE ADULT LOUNGE, his Quantum Communicator Pro V voiced. WE'RE FINALLY OLD ENOUGH.

A small smile touched Chloé's lips. He was right. This was a new frontier for them. They walked past the familiar, boisterous sounds of the Youth Lounge and into the quiet, more subdued atmosphere of the Adult Lounge. They found a comfortable spot, ready to wait out the storm, no longer children seeking refuge in the noisy arcade, but three adults, quietly and patiently weathering a storm, together.
