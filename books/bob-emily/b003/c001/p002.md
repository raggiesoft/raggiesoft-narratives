Later that evening, the weight of the day settled heavily in the small living room Bob now shared with Emily. Diane and David were seated on the sofa that Emily would sleep on, and Bob and Emily were huddled together on the sofa Bob would sleep on, a tight-knit island of solidarity. The room was their fortress against the world. It was time to make the call.

Diane took a deep breath and tapped the screen of her phone, putting it on speaker. After two rings, a bright, happy voice answered.

"Hey, Mom! What's up?"

"Madi, honey," Diane began, her own voice strained. "Are you sitting down? I... we need to tell you something. It's about Bob. He was in the hospital after being assaulted, and honey... it does not look good."

"The hospital?" Madi's voice shifted immediately from cheerful to concerned. "What happened?"

Before anyone could answer, a flurry of angry, frustrated grunts erupted from Bob.

"Bob? Is that you?" Madi asked instantly. "What is it? What are you saying... about some boy at school harassing Emily?"

A series of insistent, distressed chirps came from Emily, who was curled tightly against Bob's side.

"Oh my god, Emily," Madi said, her voice sharp with alarm. "He kept asking you out? And you didn't want him to because you just want to hang out with Bob? And he got meaner?"

David's voice cut in, grim and clear. "That's when he assaulted Bob, Madi. He attacked him and stuffed him into their locker."

"He wanted to intimidate the family," Diane added, her voice hardening. "But instead, he got arrested for his troubles. The police arrested him that same day".

A sharp intake of breath on the other end of the line. "Arrested? Good. But... Bob? What did the hospital say?"

A low, mournful moan came from Bob, a sound that conveyed a world of pain and fear.

Madi's voice cracked. "Bob... no... what is that sound? Your leg... Mom, Dad, what is it? Tell me."

Diane finally broke, the words tumbling out in a choked whisper. "Honey... his leg was damaged very badly. He was trapped for a few hours. The doctors... they say it's permanent. He's going to need a wheelchair".

A strangled sob came through the speakerphone. "Oh, Bob, I'm so, so sorry."

Just then, a new sound filled the room. It was Emily. Her sounds were no longer distressed, but a series of fierce, determined, and resolute chirps and grunts.

"Emily? What is that?" Madi asked, her own sorrow momentarily forgotten.

"She's... she's making a promise," Diane translated, her voice now full of awe. "She says she's going to take care of Bob’s every need. She's starting right now, as a rehearsal, to make sure she’s ready once Bob actually needs it".

Madi fell silent for a moment, absorbing the incredible weight of her cousin's vow. "Oh, Em," she finally whispered. Her voice then filled with a fierce resolve of her own. "I'm coming home. My spring break is in March. I'm coming home then. I need to see you all".

"We'll be here, Madi," Diane promised.

After they said their goodbyes and ended the call, the four of them sat in the quiet of the room. The difficult task was done, but now, a new, powerful promise hung in the air, a testament to the unbreakable bond between the two cousins.
