It was a late spring afternoon, several weeks after the planetarium trip, during a comprehensive follow-up appointment with Bob’s orthopedic surgeon and his physical therapist. Bob, now weighing about 90 pounds, was in his wheelchair, and Emily and Diane were with him.

As the appointment was winding down, Emily, who had been quiet the whole time, saw her opening. She had been diligently training, now able to comfortably bench press over 100 pounds with iron plates, and she had a direct, specific question. She pulled out her mPad and typed. She held the screen up for the doctors to read.

I can bench press over 100 pounds now. Bob weighs 90 pounds. When will his leg be healed enough for me to bench press him again?

The two doctors stared at the screen, then at Emily, then back at the screen. The surgeon was the first to speak. "I'm sorry," he said, completely baffled. "Did you say... bench press him?"

"It's something they did once, Doctor," Diane explained quietly. "In the school weight room to send a message to a bully. She's incredibly strong, and her form was perfect. It's... a thing for them."

The physical therapist looked from Emily's powerful, athletic build to Bob's atrophied legs. Her professional expression was a mixture of clinical concern and disbelief. She understood the question wasn't a joke.

"Emily," the surgeon began, his voice gentle but firm. "I understand the question. But I need you to listen very carefully. The answer is never. You will never be able to bench press Bob again."

A low, protesting moan escaped Emily’s throat.

"It's not about your strength," the surgeon continued, seeing her reaction. "It's about his leg. When you lifted him before, he was able-bodied. He could hold his entire body rigid, like a solid bar. He was an active participant. Because of the permanent nerve damage from the assault, the muscles in his legs will never function that way again. He can't hold himself stable enough for that kind of lift. Attempting it would be incredibly dangerous for you both. One slip, one loss of tension on his part, and he could fall and be reinjured catastrophically."

Emily stared at the doctor, her face a mask of disbelief turning into heartbreak. She had thought this was just another temporary restriction, another thing to overcome with time and strength. She never considered that it was a door that had been permanently slammed shut.

She looked at Bob, who reached out and put his hand on her arm, giving it a soft, reassuring squeeze. He had already accepted this loss; now he was comforting her.

Emily's shoulders slumped. She was strong enough to lift the world, but her strength couldn't fix this. It couldn't bring back the one thing they had lost. She looked at the doctor and gave a single, slow, heartbroken nod. She was extremely upset, but she was not a child. She would not argue with a doctor's orders. The rule was absolute, and she would follow it, for his safety.
