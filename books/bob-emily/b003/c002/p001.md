# Part 1: A Trip to the Stars

The first Friday in April arrived, buzzing with an energy that felt like a holiday. It was the day of the planetarium field trip. For Bob, who had clung to the promise of this day through the darkest weeks of his recovery, it felt like Christmas, his birthday, and the Fourth of July all rolled into one.

Mr. Shaneyfelt's Earth Science class gathered outside Bluewater High in the designated bus loop, a chaotic cluster of excited freshmen and their chaperones: David Scott, Ms. Albright, Mr. Henderson, and Principal Ragsdale, who was accompanied by her daughter Caroline and Caroline's friend, Opal Raymond, Jr. The students looked up and down the bus loop, expecting to see a white activity bus, but none was there.

"Alright, everyone," Mr. Shaneyfelt called out after a quick headcount. "Follow me! Time to head across the street!"

A confused murmur went through the students. *Why are we going to the Transit Center?* Bob and Emily exchanged a questioning look, just as puzzled as their classmates. None of them, not even Caroline, knew that the school's only accessible activity bus had a broken wheelchair lift and that no other school district had one to spare. They only knew that the adults were leading them away from where the school buses normally picked them up.

The group walked over to the Bluewater Transit Center, arriving just as the 8:45 AM lineup was in full swing. It was a well-choreographed dance of a small city's transit system. The downtown Red Line circulator and the Green Line from the Park & Ride out by I-77 were already letting off passengers. The Purple Line from the Army base hissed to a stop just as the Orange Line from Galax and the familiar Blue Line bus—the one that would normally take the cousins, Dr. Ragsdale, and Caroline home—pulled into their bays. Commuters and students bustled across the platform, making their connections. The Maroon Express was in its bus bay, prepared to take passengers to the New River Valley, for getting to Christiansburg, Blacksburg, and Commonwealth Polytechnic Institute.

It was into this ordinary, busy scene that their special charter arrived. A 35-foot bus, not displaying any of the regular route colors, pulled into a spare bay. Its destination sign was unique, cycling through three messages in bright orange letters: BHS FIELD TRIP, then GALAX COM COLLEGE, then GCC PLANETARIUM. As the doors opened, a clear, automated voice called out, "Bluewater High School Field Trip to Galax Community College Planetarium."

The regular passengers waiting for their transfers stopped and watched. They saw the large, excited group of high school students led by Principal Ragsdale herself. They saw David Scott wheel his son, Bob, toward the front of the line. A quiet, knowing murmur went through the crowd. This wasn't just a field trip; this was for *him*.

As the driver cheerfully deployed the ramp for Bob, a woman waiting for the Green Line nudged her friend, a soft, proud smile on her face. "Look at that," she whispered. "Good for that boy."

The town was, indeed, rooting for him. Bob was wheeled on first and secured, followed by Caroline. Instead of taking a regular seat, Emily immediately went to the fold-down jumpseat directly in front of Bob's wheelchair space. David smiled, understanding completely.

With everyone on board, their special, fare-free charter pulled out of the bustling transit center. The 45-minute drive was filled with happy chatter, but Bob and Emily were in their own world, their private Heart-Hum conversation a constant, buzzing undercurrent of excited chirps and anticipatory hums.

Soon, they arrived at the Galax Community College campus. As they neared the transit stop, they saw a regular Bluewater Transit Orange Line bus sitting there, a reminder that their bus was on a special mission. Their driver continued past the stop, pulling right up to the curb in front of The Sagan Sciences Building. Waiting to greet them was a Bluewater Transit supervisor, who helped the driver efficiently offload both Bob and Caroline.

The chatter from the students grew more excited. The building loomed before them. School staff did a second headcount to make sure everyone was present and accounted for.

"Okay, folks," Mr. Henderson announced. "We have about fifteen minutes before our show starts. Restrooms are right inside to the left."

As students headed for the restrooms, Emily instinctively moved to wheel Bob toward the men's room. David put a gentle hand on her shoulder. "I've got this one, sweetie," he said kindly but firmly. Emily hesitated, her protective instincts warring with the reality of the situation. She hated not being the one, but she understood. She gave a small nod and stepped back, allowing her uncle to take over.

Finally, with everyone regrouped in the lobby, it was time. The doors to the planetarium dome swung open, inviting them into a universe of waiting stars.
