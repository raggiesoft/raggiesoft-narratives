# Part 2: A Double Honor

With everyone regrouped in the spacious lobby of the planetarium, the excitement was a palpable, buzzing energy. Massive, backlit photos of nebulae and distant galaxies adorned the walls, and Bob and Emily stared at them, completely captivated. They were finally here.

Bob looked from a swirling image of the Orion Nebula to Mr. Shaneyfelt, who was laughing with a group of students. He then looked at Dr. Ragsdale, who was watching over the group with a quiet, proud smile. In his mind, the connection was absolute: these two adults were the reason they were standing under these stars.

He caught Emily's eye. A low, questioning hum passed between them, a silent proposal. Emily gave a single, firm, decisive nod. It was a rapid-fire conference, and a momentous decision had been made. David, standing nearby, saw the look pass between them and a slow, proud smile spread across his face. He recognized what was happening.

Bob wheeled himself over to his teacher, with Emily a step behind him. He made a soft sound to get Mr. Shaneyfelt's attention.

"Yes, Bob?" Mr. Shaneyfelt said, turning with a kind smile. "Everything okay?"

Bob and Emily looked up at their teacher, their faces full of a profound gratitude. Then, in perfect unison, they produced a new sound—one he had never heard before. It was a soft, steady hum that slowly rose in pitch, like something ascending into the sky, and ended with a soft, definitive "pop" sound.

Mr. Shaneyfelt looked at them, touched but confused. Before he could ask, Bob and Emily immediately turned their gaze to Dr. Ragsdale, who had walked over to see what was happening. They looked directly at her and produced a second, entirely different new sound. It was a sharp, percussive "thump" made by tapping their own chests once, followed by a soft, sweeping "shhh" sound.

The two teachers looked at each other, then at David, who was wiping a tear from his eye.

"Mr. Shaneyfelt," David began, his voice full of emotion. "Bob and Emily just gave you a name. In the Heart-Hum. It's a rare honor they have to decide on together. That name... is their sound for a promise-keeper. For someone who shows them the stars."

He then turned to the principal, his voice full of even more reverence. "And Dr. Ragsdale... they just gave you a name as well. That sound... the thump over the heart, followed by that 'shhh'... that's their name for a shield. For a protector."

Mr. Shaneyfelt was speechless. But Dr. Ragsdale, the mother of her own special needs child, understood the depth of the gesture completely. Tears welled in her eyes. "Thank you," she managed to say to the cousins, her voice thick with emotion. "Both of you. That is the greatest honor I have ever received."

Just then, the doors to the dome theater swung open, inviting them into a universe of waiting stars.
