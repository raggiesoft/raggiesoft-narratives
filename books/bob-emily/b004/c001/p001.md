# Part 1: The Christiansburg Connection

The humid, heavy heat of a southwestern Virginia summer settled over Bluewater, bringing with it a quiet, lazy rhythm. With the grueling pressure of their freshman year and the stress of the SOLs officially behind them, Bob and Emily finally had time to breathe.

One lazy Tuesday afternoon in late June, the two cousins were lounging in the air-conditioned comfort of the living room. Bob was resting on his designated sofa, his legs propped up on his usual mountain of pillows, while Emily sat on the floor, leaning comfortably against his side.

Emily's mPad chimed with an incoming video call request on InfoLink Messenger. The caller ID read: Madi.

Emily tapped to accept, and the screen filled with the bright, slightly chaotic energy of the Brooks family basement in Christiansburg. Madison was front and center, her face glowing with a happy, relaxed summer tan. Behind her, Bob and Emily could see the rest of Madison's new "pact"—Zoe typing meticulously at a desk, Lauren and Sarah laughing together on a portable bed, and Aubrie Kelly sitting close to Jordan. Jordan's face looked healthier and far more at peace than it had when Madison had described his tense arrival the previous fall.

"Hey, squirt! Hey, Em!" Madison cheered, waving enthusiastically at the camera.

Bob let out a happy, trilling chirp in the Heart-Hum, waving back eagerly. Emily smiled, her fingers flying across her keyboard case to type a quick response. We miss you, Madi. How is the summer job?

"Exhausting, but totally worth it," Madison grinned. She picked up her laptop and walked over to a coffee table, pointing the camera down at a massive roll of architectural blueprints. "Look what Daniel brought home. We've been planning the layout for the basement suite of the new compound all week."

Jordan wheeled into the frame next to Madison, giving the camera a warm, genuine smile. "Hey guys. We're making sure there's a guest room right down the hall for when you two come visit."

Bob and Emily looked at each other, a shared, resonant hum of approval passing between them. They were so incredibly happy for Madison. They had heard firsthand from Madison's calls about the trauma Jordan had survived, and knowing that her older sister had found such a beautiful, protective family to build a life with lifted a huge weight off Emily's shoulders.

"It's going to be huge," Madison continued, tracing a section of the blueprint with her finger. "We're even mapping out where the Alaskan King bed is going to go."

After a few more minutes of sharing summer updates and promising to visit once the compound was built, they ended the call. The living room in Bluewater was quiet again, but it was a comfortable, secure silence. Madison was actively building her permanent sanctuary in Christiansburg. It was time for Bob and Emily to perfect theirs.
