# Part 7: The Last Drop of Summer

The late afternoon sun of August 23rd, 2015, slanted through the upstairs window of the Scott household, casting long, golden geometric shapes across the interlocking foam mats of Bob and Emily’s bedroom. The air conditioning hummed a steady, quiet rhythm, holding the thick, late-summer Virginia humidity at bay.

Tomorrow, the alarm would ring, the Blue Line city bus would arrive at the corner, and their sophomore year at Bluewater High School would officially begin. But today was sacred. It was the final, uninterrupted drop of their summer freedom.

The centerpiece of their upgraded sanctuary was impossible to miss. Taking up a significant portion of the room was a brand-new, plush queen-sized bed. Emily’s massive, dramatic growth spurt over the last year had pushed her to a towering 5'11", and Bob had hit his own growth milestone, reaching 4'11". The full-sized bed they had shared during freshman year had simply become too cramped. But because they utterly and absolutely refused to be separated, Diane and David had replaced it with the sprawling queen mattress, ensuring the two teenagers had plenty of space to sleep side-by-side every single night.

Bob was sitting on the soft foam floor, leaning his back comfortably against the sturdy wooden frame of the new bed. His upper-body strength had grown formidable over the summer, allowing him to navigate their matted floor with an easy, sliding grace. Next to him sat Emily, her long legs crossed, the wiry, athletic muscle she had built from her daily carrying routines unmistakable.

Between them sat two empty canvas backpacks and a neat pile of school supplies.

Emily picked up a package of spiral notebooks and let out a soft, questioning coo. She gestured to her blue backpack, then to his black one, dividing the pile. Bob responded with a low, agreeable grunt, sliding his share of the notebooks and a handful of pens into the front pocket of his bag.

They worked in a comfortable, synchronized silence. Emily picked up a freshly printed copy of their convoluted bell schedule and held it up. Bob took one look at the confusing, staggered grid of their classes and let out a sharp, exasperated click of his tongue, shaking his head.

Emily let out a melodic, rumbling hum of laughter, bumping her shoulder playfully against his. We'll figure it out, her hum said. Just like last year.

Once the backpacks were packed and zipped, the finality of the summer settled over them. There were no looming court dates. There were no impending hospital visits. For the first time in a very long time, their horizon was completely clear.

Bob reached for his mPad, tapping the screen to wake it up, but Emily gently placed her hand over his. She shook her head, offering a soft, upward-lilting whimper while slightly lifting her arms. Carry you? Let's go outside.

Bob’s eyes lit up. He nodded eagerly, returning a warm, trusting coo, and stretched his arms upward.

Emily stood up, moving with a fluid, confident grace. She bent down and scooped her cousin into a secure piggy front carry. Bob wrapped his arms tightly around her neck and his legs around her waist. Because she was now exactly a foot taller than him, lifting him brought his head to rest securely and perfectly against her collarbone. The familiar weight of him against her chest was an instant, grounding anchor, settling any lingering nervous energy she had about the upcoming school year.

She carried him out of the room, down the staircase, and through the house to the back deck. Diane and David were already out there, enjoying the evening breeze. David was relaxing in a canvas chair with a paperback book, while Diane was watering a row of potted marigolds.

"Backpacks all set?" Diane asked, looking over her shoulder with a warm smile.

Emily nodded, giving Bob a gentle bounce to adjust her grip. She walked over to the patio table and carefully lowered him into his wheelchair, which David had left parked near the sliding glass door for exactly this purpose.

David set his book down on the table. "You two realize this is the last night you get to stay up late reading about orbital mechanics, right? Tomorrow, it's back to a reasonable bedtime."

Bob let out a series of bright, melodic chirps, a sound of pure joy and anticipation. He wasn't dreading the routine; he was looking forward to it. He was looking forward to Mr. Shaneyfelt's science class, to eating lunch with Emily in the cafeteria, and to riding the city bus together like everyone else.

Emily pulled up a chair right next to his wheel, leaning her elbows on the patio table. She looked at her cousin, noting the healthy color in his cheeks and the relaxed slope of his shoulders. She thought back to the shattered, terrified boy who had been pulled from a dark metal locker just months ago. That boy was gone. In his place sat a resilient, brilliant young man, ready to face the world.

She felt a profound wave of protective love. She reached out and placed her hand over his heart.

Bob immediately mirrored the gesture, placing his hand firmly over hers. They locked eyes as the evening sky above the tree line shifted from bright blue to a deep, bruised violet. Together, they released a deep, resonant, simultaneous hum.

I love you. I love you, too.

The vibration passed through their connected hands, a perfect, symmetrical exchange of strength and devotion. Tomorrow, they would re-enter the crowded hallways of Bluewater High. But tonight, they were just Bob and Emily, perfectly safe and entirely free, soaking in the very last drops of a victorious summer.
