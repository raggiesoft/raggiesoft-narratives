In the quiet corner of the hospital waiting area, Diane knelt before the two children, her own heart aching. She was grieving the loss of her brother-in-law, but her own sorrow would have to wait. Emily and Bob needed her to be the strong one. *I will honor your request, Richard,* she thought, making a silent vow. *I will take care of your little girl.*

She gently rubbed Emily's back, speaking in a low, soothing voice. "It's okay now. She's gone. You're safe."

Emily was still holding Bob tightly on her lap, her face buried in his hair like a scared child clinging to their favorite teddy bear. After a long while, her panicked crying began to subside into shuddering, exhausted sobs. She let out a small, shaky grunt. Bob, still in her arms, cooed softly in response. It was their own language, and Diane understood it to mean they were ready.

"Emily, sweetie," Diane began gently. "I need to know what you want. What do you want to do now?"

Emily shook her head, still unable to form words. A kind nurse from the nearby station, who had witnessed the earlier confrontation, approached with a small notepad and a pencil. "Maybe this will help," she offered.

Diane took it gratefully and handed it to Emily. With a trembling hand, Emily wrote two short, powerful sentences on the page and handed the pad back to her aunt.

Diane read the note. The first line was a gut punch: I never want to see my mother again. The second line was a declaration: I want Bob with me. No matter what.

Diane looked at the two of them, huddled together, and her resolve hardened. She understood completely. "Okay, sweetie," she said, her voice full of a promise. "Okay. You can come home with us. With me, and your uncle, and Bob." She brushed a stray tear from Emily's cheek. "You can stay in Madison's room while she's off at college. We'll figure it all out. Together."

A single, profound nod from Emily was all the answer she needed. Their new life was about to begin.
