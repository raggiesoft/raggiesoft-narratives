When Emily and Bob came back downstairs, Emily still carrying him, she chirped urgently at her aunt and gestured toward her own arm, mimicking a shot.

"You want to get your vaccinations?" Diane asked, understanding immediately. Emily nodded emphatically. "Of course, sweetie," Diane promised. "Absolutely. We will call the doctor first thing tomorrow." Emily chirped her appreciation, her relief palpable.

The three of them—Diane, Emily, and Bob—were emotionally numb, the day's events having finally drained them of all energy. Seeing this, David stood up. "You all need to eat something," he said gently. He returned a few minutes later with simple peanut butter and jelly sandwiches, chips, and apple juice.

After they ate, Emily stood up and once again gathered Bob into her arms. David watched them, his expression one of gentle curiosity. "Emily, honey," he began softly, "can I ask you something? I'm only asking so I can understand better. Why do you need to carry Bob?"

"It helps keep her calm, David," Diane explained, her voice quiet. "It's grounding for her. For Emily, carrying Bob right now is like a frightened child carrying their favorite stuffed animal for protection."

David nodded, his face full of complete understanding. He was fully on board with whatever helped these two special needs teenagers feel safe. The two cousins chirped their quiet thanks for his understanding, then cooed their goodnights to the two adults. It was still early, but they were mentally exhausted.

"Get some sleep, you two," Diane said. "We'll go to Bluewater High School in the morning and try to untangle the mess Karen made of your classes and the fact that you don't have an IEP or a 504 plan."

Emily groaned, the frustration of her situation bubbling up. Diane understood completely. "I know you're upset about being a freshman, sweetie."

Bob chirped from Emily's arms. *At least we can be together.* Emily's groan softened into a sound of agreement. At least they would be together.

She carried Bob into the upstairs bathroom. This was the one time she had to separate from him, and the brief moment of privacy as they each took their turns using the toilet and changing into pajamas brought a fresh wave of anxiety. She was immensely happy to be reunited with Bob once they were both done.

They went to Bob's bedroom and gently closed the door. Bob climbed into his twin bed while Emily turned off the light. She then joined him, immediately becoming the big spoon. As she held him, his suspicions from earlier were confirmed—Emily definitely felt taller than she had at the hospital. They chirped their goodnights to each other, finally finding a sliver of peace at the end of the longest day of their lives.
