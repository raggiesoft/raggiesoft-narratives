# Part 13: A Message in Muscle

It was a Friday in late November, during the school's "One Lunch" period. After finishing their meal, Bob and Emily brought their trays to the return window. For the past month, they had developed a new routine: lunch, followed by a trip to the weight room where Emily would lift weights and Bob would do cardio on a recumbent bike.

Once their trays were gone, Bob looked up at Emily and made a soft, questioning coo while slightly lifting his arms. *Will you carry me?*

Emily’s face broke into a warm smile. She responded with a happy, rumbling hum. *Of course, my little man. I love carrying you, why wouldn't I?*

She lifted her 85-pound cousin with an easy strength into their familiar carry. Bob wrapped his arms around her upper back and his legs around her waist, resting his head contentedly on her shoulder. They exited the cafeteria and started down the hall toward D-Wing, where the gymnasium and weight room were located.

They didn't get far. Caleb and one of his so-called friends—more of a lackey—intercepted them, physically blocking the hallway.

"Okay, that's enough," Caleb DEMANDED, his voice arrogant and loud. "Put him down. You and me, we're going out. Now."

Emily stopped. Slowly, she drew herself up to her full, formidable 5'8" height. The muscles in her arms, honed by weeks of lifting weights and her cousin, flexed visibly under the short sleeves of her V-neck t-shirt. She loomed over Caleb.

As she straightened up, a primal, unexpected fear shot through Caleb. It was a sudden, cold dread so jarring that a warm wetness spread instantly through his jeans. He had just wet himself.

Before he could even process his own humiliation, both Emily AND Bob let him have it.

From his perch nestled comfortably in Emily's arms, Bob joined his cousin in unleashing a torrent of harsh, guttural, staccato grunts and growls in perfect, terrifying unison. This was the invention of Heart-Hum foul language, a sound designed to put the Fear of God into the hearts of mere mortals. It was raw, primal, and utterly unnerving.

The sea of students in the hallway instinctively went silent and pulled back, creating a wide path. The onlookers were amazed and terrified, and every one of them made a silent promise to themselves to never, ever cross Emily Jenkins.

Caleb and his lackey flinched as if they had been physically struck, their smirks vanishing, replaced by pure, wide-eyed terror. The lackey, glancing down and noticing Caleb's predicament, grabbed his arm. "Dude," he whispered urgently, trying to discreetly pull him toward the nearby men's room. "C'mon, man, let's go. Just... just leave her alone. For good."

But Caleb shoved his hand away, ignoring him completely. His terror was quickly curdling into a dark, obsessive rage. *Nonsense,* he thought, watching Emily's retreating form as she walked right past him. *She will be mine. No one is going to stop me.*

Emily, still carrying Bob, continued down the D-Wing hallway to the weight room as if nothing had happened. A new series of calmer, more practical sounds passed between them as they discussed their workout plans. Bob made the sound for "bike" and then held up all ten fingers, followed by the sign for "two more," and a sound like a slowing puff of breath. *Ten minutes on the bike, then a two-minute cooldown,* he confirmed, knowing there wasn't much time left in the lunch period.

Emily responded with the sound for "weights" and a questioning hum. *I want to see if I can bench press 95 pounds today.*

Emily entered the weight room carrying Bob, as was their custom. The weight room attendant, a gruff but fair gym teacher named Mr. Gable, looked up from his desk and simply nodded. He was used to their routine. He slid off her back and headed for his usual recumbent bike while Emily approached a free bench press station.

Across the room, Caleb was posturing for his lackey. He lay on a bench, his setup improper, his feet not planted. He had two small 10-pound dumbbells. As he lifted them from the rack, they wobbled unsteadily. His face turned red as he strained to lower them to his chest and then, with a grunt, tried to push them up. They tilted precariously, and after one shaky rep, they came down hard on his chest. It took both him and his equally struggling friend to awkwardly shove the light weights back onto the rack.

Meanwhile, Emily calmly approached a free bench. She slid a **10-pound plate onto each end of the 45-pound barbell, for a total of 65 pounds.** Caleb watched out of the corner of his eye as she lay down, her form perfect, and breezed through two clean sets of twenty reps.

Bob, seeing Caleb's pathetic attempt, stopped his cardio workout mid-way. The halfhearted effort was a complete joke to him. A crazy, brilliant idea sparked. He wiped down the bike and walked over to Emily. A quiet, conspiratorial exchange in the Heart-Hum passed between them. The idea was not just a flex; it was a direct, mocking response to Caleb's laughable attempt. Emily's eyes lit up, and she let out a delighted giggle, nodding enthusiastically.

Then, in unison, they turned and stared daggers directly at Caleb. Their message was silent, but it was crystal clear: *Watch this, weak man.*

The whole weight room, knowing Caleb had been harassing Emily for months, sensed something was about to happen and went silent. Exercises stopped. Mr. Gable looked up from his paperwork, sensing the shift in the atmosphere.

Emily lay back down on the bench. Trusting her completely, Bob carefully positioned himself face down, his rigid 85-pound body resting perfectly balanced on her hands and forearms. Mr. Gable stood up, his mouth slightly agape. He instinctively reached for the clipboard with the official weight room rules, his finger scanning the page. He found rules about re-racking weights and wiping down benches. He found no rule, anywhere, about using another human being as a barbell. Other students—jocks and casuals alike—are frozen mid-workout. They stare in a mixture of stunned disbelief and pure awe. The only thing that was heard was the low hum of the fluorescent lights and Emily's steady, even breathing. It is a moment of impossible strength, absolute trust, and a legend being born in real-time.

With a look of intense focus, Emily lowered her cousin to her chest and then, with a powerful, smooth push, pressed him back up. She powered through a full set of twenty repetitions as if it were nothing.

She took a brief rest and then began her second set. As she powered through the tenth... fifteenth... sixteenth rep, the relentless, humiliating display of power was too much for Caleb. A strangled sound escaped his throat. He scrambled to his feet, his face a mask of terror and shame after wetting himself again, and ran out of the weight room with his tail tucked between his legs.

The moment he fled, the tension in the room broke, and the other students erupted in spontaneous applause and cheers.

Through the applause, Emily calmly finished her second set of twenty clean reps like it was nothing. She carefully sat up, helped Bob to his feet, and then wiped down the bench. All in a day's work. She would have easily continued, but a glance at the clock on the wall told her the One Lunch period was almost over.

She picked Bob back up, and the two of them exited the weight room, leaving a crowd of stunned and deeply impressed students in their wake. The story of that day became a legend at Bluewater High, and from then on, every bully in the school—everyone except Caleb, who didn't get the hint—gave Emily and Bob a very, very wide berth.

Mr. Gable simply shook his head in utter disbelief, knowing he would be telling this story for years to come.
