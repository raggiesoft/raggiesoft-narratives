# Part 9: The Principal's Wrath

Dr. Rachel Ragsdale was in her office reviewing budget proposals when the email from Alan Harrison arrived. The subject line alone—"Following up on your note from my Period 2 Language Arts class"—was enough to get her attention. She had been copied on the substitute's initial, vaguely worded note and had been waiting for the other shoe to drop.

As she read Mr. Harrison's polite but firm correction of the record, her expression hardened. When she got to the part about Bob being accused of defiance for using his school-approved communication device, a muscle in her jaw tightened. She minimized the email and, with a few swift clicks, navigated to her "Sent" folder from that morning.

There it was. An email sent at 7:15 AM to Mr. Evans, the substitute, with the subject line: "Important: Student Accommodations for Today's Classes." Attached was the standard PDF outlining the needs for several students, with Bob Scott's specific 504 plan requirements—including the use of his mPad for all verbal communication—clearly highlighted on the first page. She, Dr. Ragsdale, was a CC recipient. The substitute had received it. He simply hadn't read it.

A cold, quiet fury settled over her. This wasn't a communication breakdown. This was willful negligence. This was an adult in a position of authority making a vulnerable, grieving child feel disrespected and unsafe in his own classroom.

She immediately picked up her office phone and dialed the extension for the county's substitute teacher placement office.

"Hi, Janice, it's Rachel Ragsdale at Bluewater High," she said, her voice dangerously calm. "I'm calling about the substitute you placed with us today, Mark Evans... Yes, that's the one. I need you to add a permanent note to his file. He is not to be placed at Bluewater High School again, under any circumstances... The reason is a failure to follow a student's 504 plan, resulting in a public reprimand of a special needs student. It was a serious professional lapse."

She listened for a moment. "Thank you, Janice. I appreciate your quick attention to this."

She hung up the phone. It was done. That substitute would not be returning to her school. She then forwarded the entire email chain—the substitute's note, Mr. Harrison's response, and her own confirmation of the original email—to the district superintendent with a single line of her own: *"For your records. This has been handled."*

Dr. Ragsdale leaned back in her chair. She moved heaven and earth for her students. Any adult who got in the way of that, through malice or sheer carelessness, would find that the earth would move right back at them.
