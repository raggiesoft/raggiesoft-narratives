# Part 3: Planning with the Counselor

Mr. Henderson led the family down the hall to his office, a space much cozier and more cluttered than the principal's. The walls were covered in college pennants, photos of graduating classes, and student artwork.

"Please, have a seat," he said with a kind, grandfatherly smile. "I know this is all happening very fast, with classes starting this very morning, but I promise you, we will get you both settled in." He acknowledged the immense weight of the situation. "What happened to your father," he said, looking at Emily, "was a tragedy. I am so sorry for your loss."

He turned to his computer, his tone becoming gentle but efficient. "Dr. Ragsdale has briefed me. Our top priority is building a supportive schedule for you both right now."

"They have very similar interests," Diane offered. "They both love anything to do with space, so we were hoping for an Earth Science class."

"Perfect," Mr. Henderson said, his fingers clicking on the keyboard. "Mr. Shaneyfelt's Earth Science class it is. It's the only section we offer for freshmen, so you two will be together for that one. Normally we try to give family members separate schedules where possible, but in this case, it can't be helped." He looked over his glasses at them. "And frankly, I think it will be a very good thing for you both."

He pulled up the course syllabus on his screen. "And it looks like you're in for a treat. I see Mr. Shaneyfelt has his annual field trip to the Planetarium at Galax Community College scheduled for the spring. That's always the highlight of the year for his students."

At the mention of the planetarium, Bob and Emily looked at each other, and a simultaneous, excited chirp escaped them—a shared sound of pure, nerdy joy. It was the one thing they were both looking forward to most.

Working together, they quickly built out the rest of their schedules, finding that their duplicate interests in subjects like coding and history made creating similar timetables easy.

Once the schedule was set, Mr. Henderson produced a thick packet of papers for Emily's IEP. "This just gets the process in motion," he explained to Diane and David. "We'll have a full team meeting with her teachers in a couple of weeks."

He then made a quick call on his desk phone. "Ms. Albright? Could you join us for a moment?"

A moment later, the kind-faced woman from Dr. Ragsdale's office stepped in.

"Bob, Emily," Mr. Henderson said with a smile. "This is Ms. Albright. She will be your dedicated aide for the year. She'll have a copy of your schedules and will be your go-to person for anything you need."

Ms. Albright knelt slightly to be closer to Bob's eye-level and then looked up kindly at Emily. "I'm very happy to be working with you both," she said softly.

They had a schedule. They had a plan. And now, they had an ally right there in the school with them. The day felt a little less daunting.
