# Part 14: The Gable Report

Jim Gable had been the weight room attendant and a gym teacher at Bluewater High for twenty-five years. He thought he had seen it all. He was wrong. After Emily and Bob left the weight room, he just stood there for a long moment, staring at the empty bench press station while the rest of the students buzzed with the story they would be telling for the rest of high school. He knew, with a certainty born from decades of dealing with school bureaucracy and potential liability, that he had to report this.

Later that afternoon, Jim Gable sat in a chair opposite Dr. Rachel Ragsdale's desk.

"I... uh... I need to report a highly unusual incident from the weight room during One Lunch today," he began, still not quite sure how to phrase it.

"What happened, Jim?" Dr. Ragsdale asked, looking up from her work.

"Well," Gable said, deciding to just be direct. "Emily Jenkins bench pressed Bob Scott."

Dr. Ragsdale stared at him, her pen hovering over a document. "She what?"

"For two sets of twenty reps," Gable added, as if that would make it make more sense.

Without another word, she turned to her computer and pulled up the security camera feed from the weight room. She found the timestamp and clicked play.

Together, they watched the entire event unfold in silent, grainy color. They saw Caleb's pathetic failure, the cousins' silent conspiracy, and then the lift itself.

Coach Gable, a man with 25 years of experience, just looked on in awe and disbelief, muttering, "Perfect form... I've never seen anything like it."

Dr. Ragsdale, no physical education teacher herself, could still recognize the display for what it was: **pure, raw power.** She was stunned into silence as she watched the footage of Emily, a fifteen-year-old girl, flawlessly completing two sets of twenty reps bench pressing her 85-pound fourteen-year-old cousin.

She watched the clip again, then leaned back in her chair, shaking her head. She looked at her gym teacher, her expression now one of firm, administrative resolve.

"Jim," she said, her voice calm but absolute. "Emily will not be punished for this. Given the circumstances with Mr. Anderson, I think her message was... creative. However, she is never to bench press Bob, or any other student for that matter, ever again. We need a new rule, effective immediately."

"I'll have it posted by Monday morning," Gable replied, immensely relieved.

He left her office knowing he had just witnessed a story he would be telling until the day he retired. The legend of Emily Jenkins was just beginning.
