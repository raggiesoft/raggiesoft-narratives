# Part 8: The Teacher's Apology

The atmosphere in Language Arts on Thursday was tense. The students who had witnessed the incident with the substitute two days prior were quiet, watching Bob with supportive but worried eyes. Their regular teacher, Mr. Harrison, a calm and respected presence, was back at his desk.

As soon as the bell rang to signal the start of class, Mr. Harrison didn't begin the lesson. Instead, he walked over to Bob's desk and knelt beside it, speaking so only Bob could hear.

"Hey, Bob," he said gently. "I got a... confusing note from the sub on Tuesday. Can you tell me what happened?"

The memory came rushing back, and Bob's face hardened with the same anger and humiliation he had felt then. He let out a low growl, a sound his classmates had come to recognize as the Heart-Hum for feeling deeply disrespected.

He pulled his mPad forward, his fingers typing with sharp, angry jabs at first, which then slowed as he carefully composed his statement. Mr. Harrison watched the words appear on the screen, his expression growing more concerned with each sentence.

When he was finished, Bob took a steadying breath. He looked up, his gaze sweeping across the classroom to the other students who had tried to help him. He decided they all had a right to hear his side of the story. With a look of fierce resolve, he turned up the volume and pressed the "Speak" button.

A clear, synthesized voice filled the quiet room.

"On Tuesday, the substitute teacher asked me a question. I knew the answer. I began typing it on my mPad, with the intention of pressing the 'Speak' button when I was satisfied with my answer, which is how I participate in class. The teacher rudely demanded to know why I was fiddling with my tablet instead of answering. My classmates tried to explain to him that this is how I answer questions because I cannot speak. He did not listen to them."

The robotic voice finished, leaving a heavy silence in its wake.

Mr. Harrison stood up, his face a mask of disappointment and fury—not at Bob, but at the substitute.

"Bob," he said, his voice ringing with sincerity for the whole class to hear. "I am so, so sorry. That should never have happened. You are a valued member of this class, and you were doing exactly what you are supposed to do. Thank you for telling me."

He then turned to the rest of the students. "And for those of you who spoke up on Bob's behalf, thank you. You did the right thing. Let me be perfectly clear to everyone: this is how Bob participates. We will always show him the patience and respect he deserves. Always."

A wave of relief washed over Bob. He felt seen and validated. He exchanged a grateful glance with Kevin and the other boys who had defended him. The tension in the room broke, replaced by the quiet, supportive hum of their normal classroom.
