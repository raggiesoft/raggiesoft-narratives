As they left the principal's office, the relief was palpable, but the emotional residue from the incident remained. Bob and Emily stood in the hallway with Ms. Albright, the final bell ringing to signal the start of their lunch period. The corridor began to fill with the familiar roar of students.

Emily looked down at Bob. After a day and a half of feeling helpless, of watching him be disrespected and having to rely on adults to fix it, she needed to re-establish her role. She needed to feel the solid, reassuring weight of her cousin, her "little man," secure in her arms. It was the only way to quiet the storm inside her.

She let out a soft, questioning chirp, a specific sound in the Heart-Hum that meant, *May I carry you?*

Bob looked up at her and understood instantly. He gave a single, tired but trusting coo in response. *Yes, please.*

Ms. Albright started to speak, a flicker of concern on her face about the practicality of it, but she stopped. She saw the look that passed between them—the sheer, undeniable need. She recognized this wasn't about convenience; it was a necessary ritual. She simply nodded and took a step back to give them space.

With a practiced ease that belied the emotional weight of the moment, Emily scooped Bob into a secure piggy front carry. The familiar act settled her immediately, the buzzing anxiety in her chest quieting with each step she took.

As they moved through the crowded hallway, a low, steady stream of the Heart-Hum passed between them. It was a private conversation of soft hums and reassuring coos, a bubble of calm in the surrounding chaos. Emily was reaffirming her promise: *I've got you. I'll always protect you.* And Bob was responding: *I know. I'm safe with you.*

The sea of students parted for them without comment. By now, the sight of the tall girl carrying her smaller cousin was a familiar, if unique, part of the landscape of Bluewater High.

When they reached the entrance to the bustling cafeteria, Emily gently set Bob down on his feet. The journey was over, its purpose served. They stood side-by-side for a moment, both visibly calmer, their shared equilibrium restored. Grounded once more, they walked together into the lunch line, a single, solid unit ready to face the rest of their day.
