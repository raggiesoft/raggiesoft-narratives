# Part 6: Emily’s Rejects a Suitor

Like most students who lived in town, Emily and Bob’s day started at the Bluewater Transit Center, located directly across the street from the high school. At 6:45 AM, the Blue Line bus hissed to a stop, and they disembarked with the first wave of students heading to class.

As had become their habit over the past few weeks, Emily carried Bob from the bus stop, across the street, and into the school lobby. The daily routine had begun to sculpt her arms and shoulders with a wiry strength that was becoming noticeable. At 5'4", she carried her cousin with an easy, practiced grace. The sight was already a familiar one in the hallways of Bluewater High; they were simply Bob and Emily, a single unit.

As she walked, a quiet series of excited, melodic chirps passed between them—the part of the Heart-Hum that was their shared language for astronomy and the wonders of Mr. Shaneyfelt's class. They were excitedly anticipating what they might learn today.

It was in the middle of this private conversation that Caleb Anderson, a 5'4" senior, chose to make his move. He seemed to not even register that Emily was carrying another person; his focus was entirely on her, the "prize." He stepped in front of them, blocking their path.

"Hey, uh, Emily," Caleb said, trying to look cool. "I was wondering if you wanted to, you know, go to the movies sometime?"

The Heart-Hum conversation stopped. Emily looked at Caleb with a flat, neutral expression. She gave a slight shake of her head and let out a short, breathy puff of air—a simple, clear "no, thanks." At the same time, Bob, from his secure place in her arms, gave Caleb a dismissive glance before pointedly turning his head back to resume his silent conversation with Emily. They both brushed him off as a single entity.

Caleb, annoyed at being so easily and utterly dismissed, flushed with embarrassment and walked away without another word.

A couple of junior girls standing nearby saw the whole thing. "A senior asking out a freshman?" one whispered to the other. "And one who's literally carrying someone and in the middle of a conversation? Talk about absurd."

Her friend just shook her head. Emily and Bob, having already forgotten the interruption, continued their happy, silent chatter about Earth Science as they headed for the B-Wing.
