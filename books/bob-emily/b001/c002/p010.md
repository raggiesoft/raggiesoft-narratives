Later that Thursday afternoon, Ms. Albright found Bob and Emily at their shared locker. Seeing them, she approached with a gentle smile, making sure her posture was relaxed and non-threatening.

"Hey you two," she began softly. "First thing, Bob, I need you to know you are not in trouble at all. Dr. Ragsdale was hoping she could see you in her office for a few minutes, just to make sure you're okay after what happened."

Despite Ms. Albright's reassuring words, a call to the principal's office was still a call to the principal's office. A flicker of anxiety passed between the cousins. Bob was still visibly nervous as they followed their aide, his shoulders tense.

When they entered Dr. Ragsdale's office, they found not just the principal, but Mr. Henderson as well. The atmosphere was calm and supportive, but Bob remained on edge, waiting for the other shoe to drop.

Dr. Ragsdale gestured to the comfortable chairs in front of her desk. "Bob, Emily, thank you for coming in," she began, her voice soft. She looked directly at Bob and said, "First things first, I want you to know you are not in trouble. At all."

Only then, hearing the words directly from the principal herself, did Bob's tense shoulders finally relax. The anxiety began to drain from his face, replaced by relief.

"I saw the email exchange with Mr. Harrison," Dr. Ragsdale continued, "and I am so sorry that happened to you. I called you here because I wanted to check on you myself and make sure you feel safe and comfortable at this school."

Mr. Henderson leaned forward. "That substitute's contract with the county has been flagged, Bob. He will not be returning to Bluewater High. We failed to protect you in that classroom on Tuesday, and we are taking every step to ensure nothing like it ever happens again."

Ms. Albright, who had stood quietly by the door, added, "And I want you both to know, if you ever feel uncomfortable or disrespected by anyone—a teacher, a sub, another student—you come find me immediately. That is what I'm here for. I'm on your team."

Throughout the meeting, Emily sat silently beside Bob, her hand resting reassuringly on his shoulder. After the adults had finished speaking, Bob looked at Emily, a soft, questioning chirp escaping him—a sound in the Heart-Hum that clearly meant, *Are we okay?*

Emily responded with a low, reassuring hum, squeezing his hand. *Yes. We're safe here.*

Dr. Ragsdale watched their private, quiet exchange, recognizing the calm tone of it even if she didn't know the words. Bob then turned his attention to his mPhone. His thumbs moved quickly across the screen. When he was done, he held it up for the three adults to see. The screen read:

Thank you. All of you.

Dr. Ragsdale smiled warmly. "You are very welcome, Bob," she said. "You belong here. Both of you. My door is always open."
