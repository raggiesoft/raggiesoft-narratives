# Part 4: The Daily Vigil

The days in the pediatric recovery room fell into a quiet, monotonous rhythm. David, having moved his entire work-from-home setup to the small hospital visitor's table, filled the daytime hours with the soft clicking of his keyboard. His steady, calm presence was a comfort, but Bob's days were largely spent watching the clock, his mind counting down the minutes until school let out.

He had Emily's schedule memorized. Dismissal at 2:30 PM. A short walk across the street to the Transit Center. The 2:45 PM Green Line bus. By 3:15 PM, his anticipation would be a palpable, buzzing energy.

Right on schedule, David's phone buzzed with a text from Emily: Here. At the front desk.

"Alright, buddy, be right back," David said, closing his laptop. "The eagle has landed." He gave his son a reassuring pat on the shoulder before leaving the room to escort his niece up from the lobby.

A few minutes later, the door pushed open, and Emily was there, dropping her backpack with a familiar thud. The entire energy of the room shifted. She went straight to Bob's bedside, and they immediately performed their now-practiced hospital ritual—each placing a hand over the other's heart, sharing a deep, resonant hum that spoke volumes.

As Emily settled into the recliner, David turned on the local news. They sat together, a temporary, strange kind of family normal. As the news turned to the weather report, the meteorologist pointed to a swirling mass on the map.

"...and folks, we're keeping a very close eye on a system moving in from the west," she said. "Still a few days out, so it's too early to say for sure, but there is the potential for some significant winter weather toward the end of the week. We'll have more on that on the 5 o'clock news."

David and Emily exchanged a brief, worried glance. A bad snowstorm was no longer just an inconvenience; it was now a serious complication in their already complicated lives. But for now, they pushed the thought aside. Emily was here, and for the next few hours, their small family was whole again in the quiet of the hospital room.
