# Part 5: The Storm Arrives

The day started like any other in their new hospital routine. Emily caught the 2:45 PM Green Line bus, arrived at the hospital around 3:15 PM, and was escorted up to Bob's room by David. The weather reports had been consistent all day: a major snowstorm was coming, but it wasn't expected to arrive until well after midnight.

Inside, the atmosphere was one of watchful waiting. The sky outside was a heavy, slate-grey, but no snow had yet begun to fall. They settled into their familiar rhythm: David on his laptop, Emily in a chair she pulled up to the hospital bed, her hand holding Bob's. They had the local news on the television, and soon, the weather report came on.

"Okay, folks, this is an updated 'Storm Watch' alert," the meteorologist said, standing in front of a swirling map. "The models are all in agreement now. The leading edge of this storm system is projected to arrive well after midnight tonight, but it is going to be a big one."

He pointed to a graphic showing potential accumulation totals. "We are looking at sustained, heavy snowfall through the overnight hours and all day tomorrow. Accumulations for Bluewater and the surrounding plateau could easily top **two feet** by this time tomorrow evening. I can't stress this enough: if you don't have to be on the roads tomorrow, please stay home."

David, Emily, and Bob all looked at each other.

"Well," David said, breaking the silence. "Looks like we'll be making it home just in time tonight. But I don't think we'll be able to get back here tomorrow."

A low, worried hum passed from Bob to Emily. The thought of her not being able to visit, of being separated by a wall of snow for a whole day or more, was a new and unwelcome anxiety. She squeezed his hand reassuringly, but she felt the same dread coiling in her own stomach.

For now, they were together. But they all knew that the storm, and a long separation, was coming.

As evening approached, David and Emily made their usual trip down to the cafeteria, getting their food to-go so they could eat with Bob when his dinner tray arrived. As they settled in to eat, the first few flakes of snow began to drift lazily past the window.

Within moments, the gentle flurry became a fierce, swirling blizzard. The world outside vanished into a wall of white. On the television, a breaking news banner flashed across the screen with reports of sudden, treacherous road conditions and multiple accidents. Everyone knew the storm was coming, but no one expected it to arrive so early and with such ferocity.

David looked at the raging blizzard outside, then at the news reports of wrecks already happening. He made a decision. "That's it," he said firmly. "We're not going anywhere tonight. It's not safe."

Just then, a young nurse in her twenties came in to do a routine check on Bob's vitals. "The roads look pretty bad out there," she commented.

"That's what I was just saying," David replied. "It doesn't look like my niece and I will be able to get home tonight."

The nurse's expression became concerned. "Well, sir, you're welcome to stay, but I have to tell you, hospital policy prohibits overnight visitors who are minors."

"I understand," David said calmly. "I am both Emily's uncle and her legal guardian, and given the emergency weather conditions, I'm more than willing to sign a waiver or any other document required for her to stay here with me."

The nurse checked Bob's vitals—he was recovering as well as could be expected—and nodded. "Okay. Let me go get my supervisor."

She returned a few minutes later with the head nurse for the floor. After the situation was explained—that Emily was a minor who couldn't get home safely and that her legal guardian was present and willing to take full responsibility—the arrangements were made. The waiver was signed.

David called Diane, who was safe at home. "Hey, honey. The storm hit early. We're snowed in... Yes, we're fine. The hospital is letting Emily stay with me... Yep, signed the waiver. You stay safe and warm. We'll see you when the roads are clear."

As the staff brought in extra blankets, Bob's excitement was impossible to hide. He let out a happy chirp in the Heart-Hum; Emily was staying the night! But his excitement was immediately followed by a low, sad moan as he looked from his hospital bed to the recliner a few feet away.

Emily heard it and responded with her own soft, sad hum. Bob desperately wanted her to be in the bed with him, to snuggle up like they always did. She wanted it too. But they both understood it wasn't safe for his legs while he recovered. He was happy she was here, but sad she couldn't be right *here*. David took the sofa by the window, and Emily settled into the recliner for the long night ahead.
