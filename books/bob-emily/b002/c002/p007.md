# Part 7: Discharge Day

The morning after the third snowed-in night was bright and clear. The main roads were finally passable, and the doctor officially signed Bob's discharge papers. After twelve long days, they were finally going home.

David went to get the car while Diane and Emily helped Bob get packed. A short while later, David was pushing Bob in the wheelchair through the hospital's main doors for the last time, with Emily walking silently beside them.

When they got to the car, David opened the front passenger door.

Emily stopped, a confused and questioning chirp escaping her. She looked at the open front door, then at the empty back seat where she had expected Bob to be. That was their space.

David saw the look on her face and his expression softened. "Honey," he explained gently. "It's just for now. It's much easier to get him in and out of the front seat than to try and maneuver him into the back. There's more room for his legs here."

A wave of frustration and sadness washed over Emily. It was another small, simple thing that Caleb's actions had stolen from them. She hated it, but she looked at Bob's carefully supported legs and understood. With a reluctant, unhappy sigh, she nodded and opened the rear door.

After David carefully helped Bob transfer from the wheelchair into the front passenger seat, he folded up the chair and put it in the trunk. Diane got into the driver's seat, and David, instead of getting in the front with her, slid into the back seat next to Emily. He put a comforting arm around her as she stared at the empty space beside her, then at the back of Bob's headrest. The distance between them felt immense.

As they pulled away from the hospital, a new chapter was beginning. They were going home, but they were leaving another piece of their old normal behind.
