# Part 1: The Long Wait

The controlled chaos of the children's hospital emergency room swirled around them, but the Scott family was an island of tense, focused silence. Bob lay on a gurney in a curtained-off bay, his face pale with pain and shock, his mangled legs covered by a thin white sheet.

An emergency physician, Dr. Evans, stood speaking in low, urgent tones to Diane and David. "I need to be very direct with you," he said, his expression grave. "The X-rays show severe fractures, but the primary issue is the time delay. The pressure from being trapped for hours caused extensive and, I'm afraid, irreversible nerve and tissue damage." He took a deep breath. "Had Bob been brought here immediately, we would be looking at a full recovery. Because of the delay... the damage is catastrophic. He will not be able to put weight on either of his legs again and will require a wheelchair for the rest of his life."

Diane let out a choked sob, and David wrapped an arm around her, his own face a mask of disbelief and rage.

"Our surgical team is prepping a room for him now," Dr. Evans continued gently. "The priority is an emergency operation to save as much of his legs as they can."

Beside the gurney, Emily sat on a stool, her hand gripping Bob's tightly. Their own quiet, frantic conversation was happening in the Heart-Hum. He squeezed her hand and managed a series of faint but furious grunts. *It was Caleb. Caleb did this.*

Emily's expression hardened. She pulled out her mPhone, her thumb shaking as she brought up a text message from a classmate. She held the screen so Bob could see it. It read:

OMG Emily they just took Caleb Anderson out of the office in handcuffs

A sliver of dark, terrible satisfaction passed between them just as two orderlies came through the curtain. "It's time," they said gently. After a final, tearful moment, Bob was wheeled away, leaving his family alone to begin the long, agonizing wait.
