# Part 1: The Assault

The legend of the "Emily Jenkins Rule" gave the cousins a wide berth of respect and safety in the crowded hallways of Bluewater High. Bullies and troublemakers, having heard the story of the girl who was stronger than half the football team, left them completely alone.

Everyone got the hint. Everyone except Caleb Anderson.

For Caleb, the weeks after the weight room incident were a torment of festering rage and humiliation. Every time he saw Emily, he didn't see a girl to be feared; he saw a prize that was being unfairly kept from him. In his twisted, obsessive mind, his failure to impress her wasn't his own fault. It was Bob's. Bob was the obstacle. Bob was the reason she didn't see him.

It all came to a head on a Tuesday in January.

Bob was at their shared locker between classes, swapping out his books. The hallway was momentarily quiet. He didn't notice Caleb approach until the senior slammed a hand against the locker next to his head, trapping him.

"You think you're so clever, don't you?" Caleb snarled, his 175-pound frame looming over the much smaller boy. He smelled of stale sweat and anger. "You and her, laughing at me. Making a fool out of me."

Bob shook his head frantically, letting out a small, frightened whimper as he pressed himself back against the cool metal of the lockers.

Caleb laughed, a cruel, dismissive sound devoid of any humor. *Still won’t even speak to me, huh?* he thought, his rage boiling over. *I'll teach you to get in my way.* He saw Emily's strength as a challenge he couldn't meet, but this? This he could do.

"You need to stay away from her," he growled, his voice low and dangerous. "She's mine."

He grabbed Bob, whose feet scrambled for purchase on the linoleum floor. In a shocking act of violence fueled by weeks of impotent fury, Caleb began stuffing this smaller boy into the locker. Bob cried out, a sharp, piercing sound of pain and terror. His leg was twisted at a sickening, unnatural angle as Caleb used his full weight to force him into the cramped metal box.

There was a final, agonizing cry from Bob, and then the locker door was slammed shut, plunging him into a world of darkness and white-hot pain.

Caleb stood there for a moment, his breath ragged, his heart pounding with a sick sense of victory. He hadn't just punished Bob; he had removed the obstacle. He turned and walked away laughing, ready to go find his "prize," leaving Bob broken and trapped in the dark.
