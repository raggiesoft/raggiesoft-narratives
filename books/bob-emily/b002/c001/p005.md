# Part 6: The Perp Walk

The journey from Dr. Ragsdale’s office to the main entrance of the school was the longest walk of Caleb Anderson’s life. With his hands cuffed securely behind his back, he was led by Officer Miller and Officer Davis through the crowded lobby during the busiest part of a class change.

The usual roar of high school chatter died down as he passed. Students stopped talking and simply stared. These weren't looks of idle curiosity; they were looks of cold, hard judgment. The story of what had happened to Bob Scott had spread through the school like wildfire. Everyone knew. They saw Caleb not as a misunderstood troublemaker, but as the person who had put a well-liked, disabled freshman in the hospital. The whispers that followed him down the hall were not sympathetic.

"That's him," a sophomore girl muttered to her friend. "The one who put Bob in the hospital."

Caleb kept his head down, his face burning with a shame so intense it felt like a physical fever. His earlier bravado had evaporated, leaving only the humiliating reality of his situation.

When they reached the main lobby, a marked Bluewater Police car was waiting at the curb just outside the glass doors. Officer Davis opened the rear door, and Officer Miller firmly guided the now-compliant Caleb into the back seat. The door slammed shut with a heavy, final thud.

From her office window, Dr. Ragsdale watched the police car pull away, her expression grim. There was no triumph in this, only a profound sadness for all the lives that had been shattered. Her duty, however, was not yet over. She turned from the window, sat down at her desk, and picked up the phone. She dialed the number for the county school superintendent's office.

"Yes, this is Rachel Ragsdale at Bluewater High School," she said, her voice steady and professional. "I need to report a serious student incident resulting in a felony arrest on school grounds."
