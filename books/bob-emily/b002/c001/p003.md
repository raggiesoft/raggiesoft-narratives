The quiet hum of the main office was a stark contrast to the violence that had unfolded in the hallway. Dr. Rachel Ragsdale sat at her desk, her expression a mask of cold, controlled fury. The school's guidance counselor, Mr. Henderson, sat opposite her, looking pale and shaken.

"I've reviewed the security camera footage from the C-wing hallway," Dr. Ragsdale said, her voice clipped and precise. She gestured to her monitor, which showed a grainy but clear image of a tall, lanky senior cornering Bob at his locker. "It's exactly who I suspected."

Mr. Henderson leaned forward, his shoulders slumping in grim recognition. "Caleb Anderson."

Dr. Ragsdale clicked open the student's digital file on her screen. "His disciplinary record is a mile long," she stated flatly, scrolling through the list of infractions. "Page after page of petty violations. Detention for insubordination, cutting class, disrespect to staff, vandalism... He’s a repeat offender who clearly thinks the rules are for everyone else."

"He's spent more time in detention this year than some students spend in study hall," Mr. Henderson confirmed, his voice heavy with frustration. "We've tried every internal tool we have. Warnings, parent conferences, more detention. Nothing gets through to him. And all year, he's been harassing the Jenkins girl."

"I know," Dr. Ragsdale said, her voice dropping to a low, dangerous tone. "So let me be perfectly clear on the facts. We have a student with a long-established pattern of defiance. On top of that, he spends the first semester as a 17-year-old harassing a 15-year-old girl. Then he turns 18, and as a legal adult, he violently assaults her 14-year-old cousin."

She looked up from the monitor, her eyes meeting Mr. Henderson's. "The school's ability to discipline him has reached its end. He has made that clear himself. This is a criminal matter now."

She picked up her desk phone, her movements sharp and deliberate. She pressed the button for her administrative assistant. "Patricia," she said, her voice firm. "Please get me Chief Brody of the Bluewater Police Department on my private line. Tell him it's an urgent matter regarding a violent felony committed by an adult student against a minor on school grounds."

She hung up the phone and looked at Mr. Henderson, her eyes like chips of ice. "I'm having him arrested. Today."
