The decision hung heavily in the heated air of the McKinney hotel suite.

Victoria Vance stood by the floor-to-ceiling windows, staring down at the paralyzed, iced-over arteries of the Dallas North Tollway. Behind her, the extraction team sat around the massive dining table in silence. The heavy SUVs were in the garage, equipped with chains. The Bombardier Global 7500 was secured in the hangar. But none of it mattered against the sheer, lethal unpredictability of the Texas roads.

"The extraction is officially delayed," Victoria announced, her voice leaving absolutely no room for debate. "Nobody leaves this hotel tonight. Not even at midnight."

Ashley leaned forward, resting her head in her hands, a jagged sigh escaping her lips. She didn't argue. She knew the logic was flawless, but it still felt like a physical blow.

"I know I can make the drive," Emma said quietly from the corner of the room, her arms crossed over her chest. As the Keep's heavy machinery specialist, she was the only person in the room who drove eighty-thousand-pound rotary snowplows through high-alpine whiteouts for a living. "If it were just the ice, I could get us there."

"I know you could, Emma," Victoria replied, turning away from the glass to face the room. "But it's not the ice I'm worried about. It's the variables we can't control. Tomorrow morning, people in this city are going to wake up freezing in the dark, panic, and try to drive rear-wheel-drive sedans on a frictionless, untreated surface. We are dealing with millions of unguided kinetic missiles. If one of them loses traction on a cross street and T-bones our transport, Donald doesn't get rescued. He ends up in a crushed vehicle with emergency services that are entirely paralyzed and unable to respond."

Victoria walked to the table, resting her hands firmly on the polished wood. She looked directly at Ashley, and then down at Johnny.

"We are making a promise right now," Victoria stated, her tone fierce and absolute. "The absolute second that ice begins to melt and the roads are physically secure, we launch. Emma, you will take the wheel of the lead vehicle. We will breach that house, and we will pull him out. But I will not risk killing that boy in a traffic accident on the way to the airport. Morgan, you have the floor. Tell us exactly when the ice breaks."

"Because of my AMS certification, I bypassed their public switchboard and went straight to the forecast desk. Ashley looked up, her brow furrowing. "Did he have better news?"

Morgan let out a short, humorless laugh. "He was mostly just in shock. He saw my routing ping on their internal network and asked what the hell a Cascade micro-climate specialist was doing sitting in Collin County during a polar vortex. I had to explain exactly why we were down here."

"And when you told him?" Victoria asked.

"He bypassed the standard public forecast and gave me direct access to their raw, unfiltered radar telemetry," Morgan said, tapping the screen. A brutal timeline of temperature drops appeared. "And the raw data is ugly. Tonight is going to be the thermal bottom of this event. The ambient temperature at Donald's house is going to plunge to around negative two degrees Fahrenheit. With the power grid offline, the interior temperature of that McMansion will likely drop into the low forties, maybe even the thirties, by morning."

Ashley closed her eyes, her hands balling into fists on the tabletop.

"Wednesday afternoon at the earliest, Thursday at the latest," Morgan confirmed gently, closing the laptop screen. "The mountain doesn't negotiate, Ashley. And neither does the ice. We have to wait it out."
