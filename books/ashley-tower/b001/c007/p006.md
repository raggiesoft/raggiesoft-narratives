# Part 3: The Long Night – 5:15 PM

The gray light in the bedroom finally died, plunging the room back into complete, freezing darkness.

Underneath the suffocating pile of blankets, Mary shifted carefully, her numb fingers blindly searching the edge of the mattress. She had prepared for a power outage, but not for a siege. When she had raided the pantry on Wednesday night, she had grabbed things that didn't require a microwave or a refrigerator.

Her hand brushed against cold metal. She pulled a pop-top can of ravioli and a plastic bottle of water into the center of their makeshift nest.

"We need to eat," Mary whispered into the dark. Her breath plumed instantly, chilling her own face. "Just a little bit, Donald. We have to make it last. I don't know when the ice is going to melt."

She popped the metal tab, the sharp *hiss* and *crack* echoing too loudly in the dead silence of the house. She guided the edge of the cold aluminum to Donald's lips, helping him take a few slow, metallic-tasting bites of the freezing food. They washed it down with sips of water that were already painfully close to freezing solid in the bottle.

Every swallow felt like swallowing a stone, but it was calories. It was fuel.

Mary carefully set the half-empty can aside, re-sealing their blanket cocoon as tightly as possible. She lay back down, pulling Donald flush against her chest.

Tomorrow was Tuesday.

It was supposed to be the finish line. Tomorrow was his eighteenth birthday. For months, they had clung to the fantasy of what that Tuesday would look like. They were supposed to walk out the front door, get into a friend's car under the guise of a birthday dinner, and drive away from this house forever. Donald was supposed to finally meet Ashley face-to-face. He was supposed to be free.

Instead, the universe had locked them in a freezer. There would be no friends pulling into the driveway. There would be no birthday dinner. There was only the suffocating dark, the groaning timber of the freezing house, and the terrifying reality that his father was still pacing downstairs.

Suddenly, Donald’s chest hitched.

He let out a sharp, ragged sneeze that shook his entire frame.

Mary’s heart dropped into her stomach. She felt his muscles lock up, his arms pulling rigidly against his chest as a deep, uncontrollable shiver wracked his body. The spasticity was misfiring, his nervous system overwhelmed by the aggressive drop in core temperature. The blankets were no longer enough; the ambient air in the bedroom was just too violently cold, leeching the heat right out of their shared pocket.

"I've got you," Mary breathed, panic threading through her voice as she wrapped both arms around him, burying her face into his shoulder to share every ounce of body heat she had left. "I'm here, Donald. Keep breathing. Just keep breathing."

He let out a low, frightened whine, his teeth chattering uncontrollably against his bottom lip.

Mary squeezed her eyes shut as the reality of the sunset fully set in. The temperature was still dropping, and there was no heater, no cell service, and no rescue coming today. The night had only just begun, and it was going to be an agonizingly long, terrifying fight to simply stay alive until dawn.
