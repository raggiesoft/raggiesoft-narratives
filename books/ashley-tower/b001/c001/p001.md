# Part 1: Best Laid Plans – 7:30 PM

The deep, heavy rumble of his father’s oversized pickup truck pulling into the driveway vibrated right through the floorboards, but up here, behind a closed door, the noise was at least tolerable.

Donald let out a long, silent breath as Mary gently lowered him onto the edge of their shared mattress. His stomach always tied itself in knots during the agonizing carry up the sweeping, curved staircase. The sprawling suburban McMansion was built entirely to project wealth—vaulted ceilings, grand entrances, and absolutely zero considerations for a wheelchair. Down in the kitchen, the booming, arrogant laughter of Mary’s two older brothers began to echo off the walls, blending with the sharp clatter of their parents.

Donald hated the noise down there. He liked it right here, where it was safe and relatively quiet.

On the small, cracked screen of the television resting on their dresser, a local meteorologist gestured broadly to a digitally rendered map of the Texas panhandle.

"Looking ahead to next week, it seems we have a standard winter cold front moving into the region," the weatherman noted with a cheerful wave. "Expect a dip into the low forties by Monday and Tuesday, so keep those light jackets handy, but the skies should remain clear."

Donald only half-listened, his brain processing the information as a simple logistical fact: *I will need to make sure Mary grabs my heavy jacket for the party.*

He wasn't looking at the weather map anyway. His eyes were fixed on Mary as she reached under her pillow and pulled out a worn, spiral-bound notebook. A bright, humming vocalization slipped past Donald's lips, his hands moving in a rapid, excited rhythm against his thighs. He couldn't help it; his entire body practically vibrated with anticipation.

Mary grinned, the exhaustion of the stairs instantly melting away. She sat beside him on the bed, shifting closer until their shoulders pressed firmly together. Donald leaned into her, the deep, grounding pressure instantly helping to settle the excited tremors in his hands.

"I know," Mary whispered, her eyes shining in the dim light of the television. "Exactly one week. Next Tuesday."

She flipped the notebook open to a page completely covered in careful scribbles, highlighted dates, and hidden logistics.

"The girls have it all locked down," she said, pointing to a list of her friends' names. "They are covering the whole bill. Dinner, dessert, everything. They know neither of us has a dime, and they don't care. We're going to celebrate your eighteenth and my nineteenth exactly how we want to."

Donald let out a soft, breathy laugh. He reached for his sluggish, outdated tablet, his fingers tapping across the cracked glass until he pulled up a single, heavily encrypted text thread. He pushed the screen toward Mary, displaying a short message from Ashley.

*Flight confirmed. See you next week.*

Mary’s smile widened, though she instinctively lowered her voice, glancing toward the closed bedroom door. "And the absolute best part," she murmured, "is that the second the clock strikes midnight next Tuesday, your dad can't do a single thing to stop you from walking out that door. You'll be a legal adult. He can yell, he can threaten, but he won't have a shred of authority left."

Donald nodded emphatically. The thought of finally seeing his sister again after a decade in the dark—of never having to be carried up those terrifying stairs again, of leaving the suffocating shadow of the arrogant people downstairs—made his chest ache with a fierce, brilliant hope.

Everything was falling perfectly into place. He just had to make it through one more week.
