# Interlude: The Texas Head Start – 8:15 AM

The brutal, historic freeze of Winter Storm Uri was finally receding, leaving behind a trail of ruptured pipes, water-damaged drywall, and furious residents across North Texas. For the Prosper Independent School District, Monday morning was a chaotic attempt to return to normalcy. The power grid had stabilized, the roads were mostly cleared of slush, and the first bell at Prosper High School had officially rung.

Inside the attendance office, the phones were ringing non-stop as parents called in regarding displaced students and lingering power outages.

Standing in the kitchen of his heavily damaged McMansion, Richard Caldwell gripped his cell phone tight enough to make his knuckles white. He had spent the entire weekend stewing in a venomous, impotent rage. His daughter—the estranged rebel he had written off years ago—had brazenly kicked his door in on Saturday morning with a corporate extraction team and stolen his son.  

But Richard wasn't a man who surrendered control easily. He knew the Vanguard team had flown back to the West Coast. He also knew that because Texas sat in the Central Time Zone, he had a massive, two-hour head start on the Pacific Northwest. While Ashley and her lawyers were likely still fast asleep at 6:15 AM on the coast, the Texas business day had already begun.

He was going to use the school district to trigger a truancy and welfare check, forcing local authorities to track Donald down and compel his return.

"Prosper High School Attendance, this is Sarah. How can I help you?"

"This is Richard Caldwell," he snapped, his voice vibrating with authoritative anger. "I am calling to report my son, Donald Caldwell, missing. He is not in his first-period class. He was unlawfully taken from my home this weekend, and I need the district to initiate an immediate truancy investigation and contact the police."

There was a brief pause on the line, accompanied by the rapid clatter of a keyboard.

"One moment, Mr. Caldwell. Let me pull up Donald's file," the clerk said, her voice remaining perfectly calm despite his escalating tone.

Richard paced across the hardwood of his foyer. He felt a dark sense of triumph. Donald couldn't speak for himself, and Mary was just a nineteen-year-old girl. Ashley might have money, but she couldn't stop a Texas state educational mandate. He held all the legal cards.

"Mr. Caldwell?" the clerk's voice returned, laced with a polite but firm confusion. "Sir, I'm looking at the Texas Records Exchange system right now. Donald isn't marked truant."

"What do you mean he isn't truant? He isn't in the building!"

"He isn't in the building because he is no longer enrolled in the Prosper Independent School District," the clerk explained smoothly. "An emergency, out-of-state transfer request was processed through our digital portal at precisely 8:00 AM this morning. His entire academic profile has been legally withdrawn and transferred to the Meridian Unified School District in Washington State."

Richard stopped dead in the center of the foyer. The blood drained from his face. "Excuse me? I didn't authorize any transfer. I am his father. You reverse that withdrawal immediately, or I will have your job."

The clerk’s tone instantly dropped its customer-service warmth, shifting into the rigid, uncompromising posture of a school official backed by federal law.

"Sir, you do not have the authority to reverse this," she stated flatly. "According to our records, Donald turned eighteen years old last Tuesday. The transfer request we received this morning was accompanied by a digitally notarized, federally binding release of information, signed by Donald himself."

"He doesn't know what he's signing!" Richard roared, losing his temper completely. "He has disabilities! Someone manipulated him into doing this!"

"The document was countersigned and submitted by a licensed attorney—an Amanda Kensington, representing a corporate trust," the clerk countered, completely unmoved by his screaming. "As of his eighteenth birthday, the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act—FERPA—legally transferred all privacy and operational rights regarding his educational records directly to him. You are no longer authorized to access his files, dictate his enrollment, or request his location data."

"Listen to me—"

"I am legally obligated to inform you, Mr. Caldwell, that any further attempts to access Donald's records without his explicit, written consent will be documented and reported as a federal privacy violation," the clerk finished with clinical precision. "Is there anything else I can assist you with today?"

Richard stood frozen, the phone pressed to his ear. The two-hour time zone advantage he thought he held had meant absolutely nothing. Amanda Kensington hadn't waited until she woke up on Monday morning; she had likely queued the automated filings over the weekend, ensuring the digital paperwork slammed into the Texas servers the exact millisecond the school district's computers came online.

Before he could even marshal a response, the line clicked, followed by the dull, droning hum of the dial tone.

The digital firewall had slammed shut. He had lost.
