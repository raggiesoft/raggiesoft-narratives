# Part 9: The Legacy Boardroom – 6:30 PM

The heavy, armored doors of the Owner Express sealed shut, locking the family inside the massive steel cabin. Ashley tapped her master security fob against the panel, bypassing the lockouts before pressing the button for Floor 16.

As the massive traction motors engaged and the car began a smooth descent, Susan leaned against the heavy steel wall, looking down at Donald.

"You're riding in a tank right now," Susan said, her voice echoing slightly in the oversized car. "This isn't a standard elevator. Arthur over-engineered this specific shaft to be the tower's primary Fire Service Access Elevator, or FSAE. It's heavily armored and completely sealed against smoke intrusion. In an emergency, we can override the system to bypass compromised zones and stop at every single one of the 135 floors to get people out".

Donald looked at the thick steel doors, his brow furrowing. His fingers moved quickly across his AAC device. *"Like in that old movie? The Crown Point Picture?"*

Susan’s expression sobered immediately, sharing a brief, heavy look with Amanda.

"Exactly like that," Susan nodded. "But Crown Point wasn't just a movie, Donald. It was a real skyscraper in San Francisco that suffered a catastrophic electrical fire in December 1974. Exactly two hundred people lost their lives because the developers cut corners on the electrical grid and the fire suppression systems."

"Arthur watched that disaster on the news," Ashley added quietly. "He had two children in wheelchairs. When he saw that standard protocol was to shut down the elevators and force people into the stairwells, he realized a fire would be a death sentence for his family. He indefinitely delayed the opening of this building to tear out the core and install the shielding protocol you are riding in right now".

"He actually formed a production company and completely bankrolled *The Crown Point Picture* himself," Amanda noted. "He wanted to make sure the world never forgot the cost of corporate negligence".

With a soft chime, the elevator slowed. The doors slid open, revealing a drab, utilitarian maintenance corridor deep within the interior concrete core.

Mary pushed Donald’s wheelchair out of the armored car. They followed Ashley down the short hallway until she pushed open a heavy access door.

The transition was jarring. They stepped out of the concrete corridor and directly into the polished, intimidatingly sleek environment of Floor 16—the official, legally registered headquarters of the family's LLC.

Donald’s wheelchair rolled silently over the plush, ultra-expensive carpeting. He looked at the frosted glass partitions, the immaculate reception area, and the staggering skyline views pouring in through the floor-to-ceiling windows.

He stopped typing for a moment, his hands hovering over his keyboard as he looked from the polished corporate armor of the floor to his oldest cousin.

*"This is mine?"* Donald typed, the synthesized voice sounding incredibly small in the massive space. *"I own one-seventh of this?"*

Amanda offered a warm, proud smile. She stepped up to a set of massive, heavy double doors at the end of the hall and pushed them open, revealing the Legacy Boardroom.

It was an intimidating, cavernous space, featuring a massive mahogany table surrounded by heavy leather chairs, framed by spectacular views of Meridian City.

"Yes, Donald. You are a one-seventh owner," Amanda confirmed, walking into the room. "Back in our grandfather's era, this company was publicly traded on Wall Street. This room is where the original board met. This is where the multi-million dollar deals were made to build the empire".

Donald rolled forward slightly, staring at the endless expanse of the mahogany table. The sheer, crushing weight of the history, the wealth, and the unimaginable responsibility suddenly crashed down on his exhausted nervous system. For his entire life, his father had made him feel small, stupid, and incapable. Now, he was being handed the keys to a billion-dollar corporate kingdom.

His breathing hitched. His hands locked in a death grip on his armrests, his chest tightening as the room seemed to spin.

Mary recognized the physical cues of an impending sensory crash instantly. She didn't hesitate. She stepped directly in front of his wheelchair, completely blocking his view of the massive boardroom. She leaned down and wrapped her arms tightly around his shoulders, burying his face against her chest in a firm, deep-pressure grounding hug.

"Close your eyes, Donnie," Mary whispered fiercely, ignoring the billionaires standing in the room with them. "Just breathe. It's just a room. I've got you."

The rest of the family froze, instinctively giving them space. As they watched Mary seamlessly anchor him, any lingering, protective doubts they might have harbored about the teenager from Texas evaporated. They saw with absolute clarity that Mary didn't care about the mahogany table, the LLC, or the money. Her entire universe was the boy in her arms.

Slowly, the rigid tension in Donald's shoulders began to melt. He took a deep, shuddering breath, nodding against Mary's shoulder to signal he was regulating.

When Mary finally stepped back, keeping a protective hand resting on his shoulder, Amanda picked up the conversation as if the pause hadn't even happened, her voice remaining perfectly steady and entirely devoid of pity.

"You don't have to run this room today, Donald," Amanda explained gently. "The executive power of this Trust operates on a rotating basis. The Chairwoman seat rotates every calendar year, strictly alphabetically by first name. I legally deferred my inaugural term so that Ashley could hold the absolute executive authority when she went to Texas to extract you. Right now, it is Ashley's turn to sit at the head of that table."

Ashley stepped forward, offering him an easy, pressure-free smile. "I handle the stress of the lawsuits and the leases right now. You just get to explore."

"So," Amanda asked, folding her hands in front of her. "What would you like to see next?"

Donald looked down at his keyboard, the panic fully replaced by a grounded, burning curiosity. He thought about what his cousins had told him in the kitchen.

*"Susan said she runs the lungs of the building,"* Donald typed. *"I want to see how they work."*

Susan’s face immediately lit up with a massive grin. "Oh, you are going to love this. Follow me."

She led them straight out of the Legacy Boardroom, guiding them back through the polished executive suite and into the hidden concrete corridor. The armored doors of the Owner Express opened, and Susan stepped up to the massive button panel.

She bypassed the polished upper floors entirely and pressed the button marked *B4*.
