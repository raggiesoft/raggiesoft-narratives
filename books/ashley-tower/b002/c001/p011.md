For the first twenty minutes, the rhythmic, low-frequency thrum of the B4 mechanical level was a grounding, predictable sensation. But as Susan led them deeper into the labyrinth of structural utilities, the sheer volume of the massive HVAC lungs and the heavy-duty water pumps began to compound.

The steady heartbeat of the machinery slowly morphed into an overwhelming wall of sound that pressed heavily against Donald's exhausted nervous system. He stopped rolling, his shoulders tensing as he closed his eyes, his hands coming up to press against the sides of his head.

Mary saw the physical shift instantly. She stepped in front of his wheelchair, gently resting her hands over his. "Too much?" she asked quietly.

Donald nodded once, keeping his eyes shut.

Susan didn't ask questions or push him to tough it out. She immediately pivoted, gesturing back the way they came. "Tour's over," Susan announced easily, flashing an apologetic smile. "I'm sorry, Donald. I get so used to the noise down here I forget how heavy it is for everyone else. Let's get back to the box."

They retraced their path along the painted yellow safety lines, moving quickly back to the heavy armored doors of the Owner Express.

The second the doors sealed shut behind them, the oppressive roar of the mechanical floor vanished, replaced by the absolute, insulated silence of the ghost shaft. Donald let out a long, shuddering breath, dropping his hands to his lap as his sensory system immediately began to regulate.

Mary unclipped the strap of his hard hat and lifted it off, placing it back into the metal storage cage along with the others. She brushed his hair back affectionately. "Better?"

*"Much better,"* Donald typed on his AAC, offering Susan a small, appreciative look. *"Thank you for showing me. It was just loud."*

"Anytime," Susan smiled, leaning against the steel wall.

Ashley stepped up to the massive button panel. "We need to let you get back to the 131st floor so you can fully decompress," she noted, glancing at Amanda. "But we completely drained the 130th-floor pantry during the Texas extraction. Since we're already in the elevators, do you guys mind if we make a quick stop to grab some groceries for the week?"

Donald looked up at Mary. He was tired, but the insulated safety of the elevator had brought his baseline back down. He tapped his keyboard. *"I can do a grocery store. It is quiet."*

"Alright," Ashley smiled. She pressed the button for Floor 75.

The heavy traction motors engaged, pulling the armored car up from the subterranean depths and launching it rapidly into the upper residential tiers.

"We keep the commercial and residential floors strictly separated," Amanda explained as the floor numbers on the digital display rapidly climbed. "Floors 75, 76, and 77 make up the Residential Sky Lobby. It functions as a private town square in the sky for the permanent residents".

Ashley pointed to the glowing panel. "Because of the shielding protocol, I could take this elevator directly to 76, but it dumps out into a heavily soundproofed freight vestibule right behind the grocery store's stockroom. It's full of high-speed freight elevators coming up from the B4 receiving matrix. It's ugly, and it's loud. Let's do the proper public entrance."

The elevator slowed, and the heavy doors slid open.

They stepped out onto Floor 75. Because it was past seven o'clock in the evening in the dead of winter, the sun had long since set. Instead of daylight, the sprawling, multi-level public square was illuminated by the breathtaking, glittering grid of Meridian City glowing against the dark night sky, visible through the massive floor-to-ceiling windows.

The 75th floor was the primary transfer deck, spacious and impeccably clean, bustling with residents returning from work or heading out for the evening.

In the center of the transfer deck, swift internal architectural escalators glided upward, ferrying residents to the amenities on the floors above. Because the escalators were structurally inaccessible for Donald's manual wheelchair, Susan guided them seamlessly to a sleek, glass-enclosed ADA-compliant local elevator nestled directly beside the escalator bank.

They rode it up exactly one floor. The glass doors opened onto Floor 76, depositing them directly at the sprawling, polished entrance of the full-sized luxury grocery store.

"Grab a cart, Mary," Susan grinned, grabbing one for herself. "Put whatever you want in it. The LLC covers the tab."
