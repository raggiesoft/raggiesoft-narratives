# Part 4: The Ascent – 12:25 PM

The heavy glass doors slid open, and the roaring noise of the city abruptly vanished, replaced by the hushed, echoing acoustics of the massive Grand Atrium.

Mary pushed Donald’s wheelchair inside, both of them staring up in awe. The lobby was a triple-height cavern, currently locked in a heavy 1970s aesthetic with massive concrete pillars, brass fixtures, and vintage chandeliers suspended high above.

Ashley led them straight to the heavily guarded concierge and security desk that separated the public space from the restricted upper-level elevator banks. The uniformed guards immediately straightened as she approached. Ashley smoothly signed a ledger, her signature bypassing the need for temporary visitor badges for her brother and his protector.

"This way," Ashley said, gesturing toward a gleaming bank of elevators.

As they approached, Mary’s eyes tracked the massive, gold-tinted glass shafts. She realized with a jolt that these were the Scenic Elevators, and that after clearing the first few floors, the tri-car exterior shaft broke through the building's shell to run completely exposed on the outside of the tower.

Mary stopped the wheelchair abruptly. "Ashley, wait."

Ashley turned, her brow furrowing in concern. "What's wrong?"

"Donald is terrified of heights," Mary explained quickly, her protective instincts instantly overriding her awe. "If that elevator goes outside the building, he's going to panic."

Ashley looked at the glass cars, then immediately down at Donald. Her desire to give him a grand, cinematic view vanished instantly, replaced by the sudden, heavy reminder of the gap between them. She was twenty years old, and she hadn't been allowed in the same room as her brother since she was four. She was still learning who he was.

"You're completely right," Ashley said, her expression softening into something quietly vulnerable. She offered Mary a deeply appreciative nod. "I have sixteen years of catching up to do. Thank you for telling me. We'll take the interior core."

She pivoted without hesitation, leading them away from the polished glass shafts and deep into the towering concrete center of the building. She stopped in front of a pair of heavy, unmarked doors that looked exactly like a restricted mechanical closet.

Mary frowned, looking at the drab entrance. "Wait, where are we going?"

In his lap, Donald quickly typed on his AAC device. The digitized, synthesized voice echoed quietly in the corridor. *"Where exactly are we going?"*

"You'll see," Ashley said, a warm smile returning to her face as she pulled her master security badge and tapped it against the hidden RFID reader.

The heavy doors slid open in absolute silence, revealing the massive, heavily armored interior of the Owner Express Elevator. It was an oversized car, easily large enough to accommodate Donald's wheelchair with plenty of room to spare.

Mary rolled him inside, and Ashley stepped in after them. The doors sealed shut, cutting them off from the atrium entirely.

Ashley tapped her badge against the glowing control panel to bypass the lockouts, then reached toward the heavy 1970s push-button system. She pressed a button near the absolute top of the massive steel panel.

*130*.

The digitized voice of Donald's AAC immediately fired off. *"Wait. We are going WHERE!?"*

Mary’s eyes went completely wide, her jaw dropping as she stared at the illuminated button. "The 130th floor? Ashley, wait, but that's... that's over 1,500 feet in the air! We're going to be in the clouds, aren't we?"

Ashley laughed softly, the sound bright and echoing in the armored car as the massive traction motors engaged, launching them smoothly and silently upward.

"Not today," Ashley smiled, leaning back against the steel wall. "There isn't a single cloud in the sky. We're just going to see the whole world."
