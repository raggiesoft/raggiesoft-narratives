The apartment was operating at peak holiday efficiency. "Morning, Bear!" Rachel called out brightly, her hands covered in butter and herbs.

"Why is the bird so huge?" I asked, rubbing the sleep from my eyes as I looked at the massive pan. "There are only four of us."

"Logistical overflow," Jessica explained, wheeling past the oven to grab a roll of aluminum foil. "I bought this turkey over a week ago. She paused, looking over at me with a fierce, brilliant smile. "But the universe course-corrected and delivered the exact brother I needed to fill the void. Now go sit down. You are officially off-duty today."

I walked over to the sofa. Kristin sat down first, immediately lifting her arm to create a space for me. I sank into the cushions, letting her wrap her arm around my shoulders and pull me flush against her side.

"Kyle from the testing team gave me a digitally remastered bootleg of the 1978 *Winter Solstice Broadcast*," Jessica grinned, holding up the blank disc. "Complete with the original commercials. We are watching the whole saga."

For the next three hours, we were completely absorbed in the galaxy. We watched the original theatrical trilogy, cheering as the Guardians ignited their light-blades and the Scrap-Bears toppled the Sovereign’s walkers in the forest. But when we transitioned to the bootlegged *Winter Solstice Broadcast*, the tone shifted into pure, unhinged absurdity.
