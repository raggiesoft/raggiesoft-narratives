The digital alarm clock on the nightstand emitted a low, rhythmic beep, cutting through the heavy silence of the bedroom.

Rachel’s hand shot out from under the weighted blanket, instantly silencing the alarm before the noise could disrupt the ambient peace of the apartment. She rolled onto her side, the deep, regulating pressure of her weight shifting against my ribs.

"Bear," Rachel whispered, gently nudging my shoulder. "It's five o'clock."

I opened my eyes, blinking against the pitch-black darkness of the room. It took my processor a fraction of a second to log the geography—I wasn't in Kent, and I wasn't in Renton. I was safe in my little sister's Queen-sized bed in Lakewood.

"I'm awake, Bug," I murmured, rubbing my eyes.

Rachel propped herself up on one elbow, her dark hair falling over her shoulders. Even in the dim, pre-dawn shadows, I could see the fierce, excited spark in her eyes. The trauma of the East Coast was finally bleeding out of our system, leaving room for the things she actually loved to rush back in.

"Tomorrow is going to be a good day," Rachel promised, her voice a low, raspy hum of pure anticipation. "We are going to the Emerald Coliseum for AWE *No Quarter*. We're sitting in the front row."

I smiled, pulling the blanket up slightly. "I know we are. You’ve been waiting for this for months."

"What match are you looking forward to most?" Rachel asked, genuinely wanting to share her hyper-fixation with me.

I wasn't a walking encyclopedia of professional wrestling lore like she was. I didn't know the win-loss records or the intricate backstage politics. But I knew exactly what had happened on Monday in Portland.

"The Texas Bull Rope Match," I answered without hesitation.

Rachel’s eyes widened in delight. "Really?"

"Yeah," I nodded. "I mean, Diego Chavez literally bought our merchandise using the money he 'stole' from The Tycoon Preston Sinclair. I feel like we are financially invested in seeing Diego win that match now."

Rachel let out a quiet, vibrating laugh of absolute joy. The fact that I had absorbed the storyline and connected it to our real-world interaction in the arena meant the world to her. She reached over, throwing her arms around my neck for a tight, high-compression pack hug, and pressed a firm kiss to my cheek.

"I am coming straight to Renton the exact second I get off work tonight," Rachel promised fiercely against my collarbone. "I'm not wasting a single minute."

Before I could answer, a soft, polite knock echoed from the heavy wooden bedroom door.

"Michael? Rachel?" Kristin's voice filtered through the wood, quiet and respectful of the boundary. "Are you guys awake?"

Rachel released her grip on my neck and sat up. "It's unlocked!"

The door clicked open. The warm, yellow light from the hallway spilled into the bedroom. Kristin stepped inside, fully dressed in her dark *Franklin's Market* uniform polo and khaki pants, ready for her opening shift.

She walked over to my side of the mattress. Seeing me lying in bed next to my younger sister didn't trigger a single ounce of neurotypical jealousy or confusion in Kristin. She implicitly understood the survival mechanics of our pack.

Rachel immediately scooted back against the headboard, giving Kristin complete access to my perimeter.

I sat up, pushing the weighted blanket down to my waist. Kristin leaned over, wrapping her arms tightly around my neck and pulling me into a warm, lingering hug.

"I wanted to say goodbye before I walked across the street," Kristin whispered, her cheek resting against mine.

"I'm glad you did," I breathed back, wrapping my arms securely around her waist.

Kristin pulled back just enough to look me in the eyes. The protective Sentinel armor was entirely gone, replaced by a profound, breathtaking vulnerability.

"I love you, Michael," Kristin said, her voice soft but carrying the absolute, immovable weight of a physical fact.

My heart hammered against my ribs.

"But I also know exactly what you are recovering from," Kristin continued, her blue eyes locked onto mine, explicitly laying out the parameters to ensure I maintained total control. "I am leaving this entirely up to you. You set the pace. We can just be the pretend boyfriend and girlfriend to keep Delores away. We can just be best friends. Or, if you want, we can be actual boyfriend and girlfriend. There is no pressure. Whatever you choose, I am right here."

She handed me the keys to the entire relationship. There was no coercion. No trap.

I looked at the beautiful, fierce woman standing by the bed. She had thrown her body between me and a monster. She had patiently dismantled my trauma triggers in the dark of a movie theater. And she loved my sister enough to protect her life.

"I don't want to pretend," I whispered, the absolute certainty settling deep into my chest. I pulled her into an even tighter hug, burying my face in her shoulder. "I love you too, Kristin. Actual girlfriend."

Kristin let out a shaky, beautiful breath of relief, her arms tightening around me.

"Just so we are completely clear on the perimeter," Kristin murmured, pulling back slightly with a warm, knowing smile. She looked over at Rachel sitting on the bed, and then back to me. "I have absolutely no problem with the other women in your life. I know your family is your survival."

She paused, her expression softening into deep, profound respect. "And I know about Ashley Raybourn. I know what she meant to you when you were kids. It is a tragedy that you lost contact with her. If you ever find her again, I will respect that bond. She was the original Sentinel, and I have nothing but respect for the girl who kept you safe before I got here."

The sheer, overwhelming emotional maturity of her statement brought tears to my eyes. Delores had spent two years violently isolating me, actively trying to destroy my memories of Ashley and sever my connection to Rachel. Kristin was doing the exact opposite. She was actively protecting my history and embracing my pack.

I reached up, gently cupping the side of her face.

"You're amazing," I breathed.

I leaned in, and we shared a deep, romantic kiss right there in the dim light of the bedroom. It was a seal on the absolute reality of our new dynamic. The pretenses were gone.

"I'll see you later," Kristin smiled, her cheeks flushed a brilliant pink as she finally pulled away and walked backward toward the door. "Have a safe trip back up to Renton."

"Have a good shift," I replied, watching her slip out into the hallway.

The heavy wooden door clicked shut. The apartment felt slightly emptier without the Sentinel, but the warmth she left behind was undeniable.

Rachel let out a long, happy sigh and stretched her arms over her head. She looked at the digital clock, which now read 5:15 AM.

"Well," Rachel rasped, kicking the weighted blanket off her legs and swinging her feet over the side of the mattress. "Since we're already up, there is absolutely zero use in going back to sleep. I have to be at *The Spot* soon anyway. Let's get up and start the coffee. We're going back to King County today."
