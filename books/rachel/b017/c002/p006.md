We left Café 34 feeling completely different than when we had walked in. My chest felt lighter, the crushing weight of the East Coast officially severed by fifty-six thousand dollars and the welcoming cheers of the Quantum testing team.

Jessica escorted us back out to the Overlake transit shelter, giving both of us a tight hug.

"I have a few hours left on my shift," Jessica told us, pulling her coat tight against the damp chill. "My apartment is locked, and I don't have spare keys cut for you yet. Go find somewhere warm and low-impact to decompress. Keep your receipts. I will reimburse you for whatever you spend."

Kristin checked her watch. "We'll head down to the theater, and then we'll catch a bus right back up here to Overlake to meet you when you clock out. We can all ride the 565 home to Renton together."

Jessica smiled, clearly loving that plan, and headed back into Building 35.

Kristin and I boarded the southbound TAPS 230. The teal and yellow bus hissed away from the corporate campus, carrying us down the commercial corridor. The adrenaline from the morning's massive emotional whiplash was finally leaving my system, leaving behind a heavy, bone-deep exhaustion. My lower back throbbed, and my eyelids felt like they were made of lead.

Kristin noticed me slumping against the window.

"You're crashing," she noted softly. "Would you like to find somewhere to just snuggle up and rest for a few hours?"

My processor hitched. For two years, physical proximity was never free. It was always a precursor to a demand, a punishment, or a violation of my boundaries. But looking at Kristin, with her warm, steady eyes, I knew I was safe.

"Simple snuggling only," I whispered, a single tear escaping and rolling down my cheek.

"I understand exactly what you’re trying to get at," Kristin replied, her voice soft and entirely devoid of judgment. "No pressure. Just rest."

She reached up and pulled the yellow stop-request cord as the massive, sprawling footprint of Crossway Plaza came into view. We stepped off the bus and walked across the wet asphalt toward the towering neon facade of Monarch Cinemas.

Because it was early Wednesday afternoon, the massive theater lobby was essentially a ghost town.

"Two for *Casino Imperiale*, please," Kristin told the teenager at the box office.

I felt the heavy weight of the two rolls of quarters pressing against my thigh—the transit fare Jessica had given me. It was the only physical money I had on my person. I started to reach for my pocket. "Kristin, I have the quarters, I can—"

"Absolutely not," Kristin interrupted smoothly, placing a firm hand over mine to stop me while sliding her own debit card into the machine. "That heavy metal in your pocket is your emergency bus fare. Your cash stays exactly where it is. Jessica gave me strict orders to save the receipts for reimbursement, and I am not disobeying a Quantum Program Manager."

She led me straight to the concession stand and completely took charge. She ordered the largest tub of buttered popcorn they had to share, two massive sodas, and then turned to me. "Pick a candy. Pick two. Get whatever snacks you want, Michael. Jessica is paying for all of it."

I grabbed a box of theater candy, deeply appreciative of her handling the logistics so I didn't have to think.

We made our way down the long, carpeted hallway to Theater 8. When we pushed through the heavy acoustic doors, the room was beautifully, perfectly empty. It was the ultimate controlled perimeter.

We walked all the way up to the back row, dead center. As soon as we sat down, Kristin reached over and smoothly folded the padded armrest between us up and out of the way. She didn't pull me toward her. She simply removed the barrier, offering me the unbroken space as an option.

I sat there for a moment in the dimming lights, staring at the empty space beside her. I thought about my little sister. Rachel was viciously protective. She didn't trust anyone. But my Bug explicitly vouched for Kristin. If Rachel trusted this woman with her life, that was all the proof my trauma-wired brain needed.

I took a deep breath, letting the tension bleed out of my muscles, and slowly leaned my weight to the right. Kristin immediately wrapped her arm around my shoulders. I rested my head against her chest, listening to the steady, calm rhythm of her heartbeat beneath her sweater.

It was warm. It was dark. And for the first time in years, I wasn't waiting for a trap to spring.

For the first hour, I was mesmerized by the booming, gritty, high-contrast action sequences splashing across the massive screen. But eventually, the gunfire faded, and the movie inevitably transitioned into the classic Julian Drake romance scene.

I watched the massive screen as Operative 009 and the beautiful "Drake Girl" fell into bed together. I knew perfectly well it was movie magic and that the actors weren't actually having sex, but the heavy, implied intimacy on screen triggered a sudden, painful spiral in my processor.

My breathing hitched. I squeezed my eyes shut, my chest suddenly tightening with a terrifying wave of grief. Delores had spent two years weaponizing my body. She had turned sex into a transaction, a punishment, and a tool for absolute control. She had convinced me I was a disgusting, broken toy. What if my wiring was permanently destroyed? What if I could never trust a woman to provide me with safe, sexual intimacy ever again?

Kristin felt the sudden shift in my breathing. She looked down, instantly reading the heavy panic radiating from my posture.

"Kristin," I whispered into the dark, my voice shaking violently. "My processor... it's stalling."

"I've got you," Kristin whispered back, her hand gently rubbing my arm. "Talk to me. What is it?"

"That," I whispered, nodding blindly toward the screen. "Delores... she turned my body into a transaction. A punishment. I feel like my wiring is completely destroyed. I don't know if I can ever do that again. I don't know how to trust a woman to touch me without a trap."

Kristin didn't offer empty platitudes. She understood exactly how my black-and-white brain operated. I didn't need vague comforts; I needed explicit, logical parameters to stop the spiral.

She shifted, leaning her head down so her mouth was right next to my ear.

"Michael, listen to me," Kristin whispered, her voice soft but completely firm. "Your wiring is not destroyed. It is just running in safe mode because you were burned. You do not have to do anything today, or tomorrow, or next year."

I let out a shaky breath, the absolute boundary instantly grounding me.

"But I need your processor to log a new data point right now," Kristin continued. "Intimacy is not a weapon. It is not a transaction. It does not come with a bill. Sometimes, it is just a safe place to land."

She shifted slightly, lifting her hand to gently cup the side of my face in the flickering light of the projector.

"I am going to give you a friendly kiss," Kristin whispered, explicitly laying out the logistics so there was zero ambiguity. "Just a soft kiss on the lips. It is not romantic. It is not a prelude to anything else. It is simply a physical reminder that you are safe, and that your body belongs to you. Is that okay?"

My black-and-white brain instantly grasped the clear, unambiguous rules. There was no hidden agenda. There was no guessing game. She had handed me the exact blueprint of the interaction before it happened.

"It's okay," I whispered.

Kristin gave me a warm, reassuring smile. She leaned down, closing the remaining distance.

It wasn't a deep, passionate kiss. It was exactly what she had promised—a soft, chaste, incredibly gentle press of her lips directly against mine. It lasted only a second, but the psychological impact was seismic. It was a seal of absolute trust. It was her silent, physical promise that the female body was a sanctuary.

When she pulled back, she rested her chin comfortably on the top of my head and turned her attention back to the movie.

I closed my eyes, my face pressed safely against her shoulder. The ice inside my chest finally began to crack. The friendly kiss hadn't magically fixed my trauma, but the explicit logic she provided had done something much more important. It proved that the door to sexual intimacy wasn't bricked over. It was just locked. And Kristin had just gently placed the key in my hand, letting me know that whenever I was finally ready to open it, she would be there.

I didn't need to open it today. Today, I just needed to sleep.

I wrapped my arm around Kristin’s waist, let out a long, shuddering exhale, and drifted off into the deepest, safest sleep of my life.
