We migrated into the living room, leaving the kitchen behind.

The furniture here was completely different from the old Delaney Street house. There was no pull-out mattress to unfold, and the layout didn't support a sprawling, floor-level base camp. Instead, dominating the center of the room was a large, cool, blue leather sofa.

Mounted on the wall directly to the right of the sofa was a brand-new, highly expensive widescreen plasma television. Mom's boyfriend, Jamie, had bought it recently, proudly upgrading the living room's technology. In almost any other scenario, a massive, state-of-the-art screen like that would be the absolute focal point of a lazy Sunday afternoon.

I looked at the remote control resting on the glass coffee table, then looked down at Rachel.

She gave her head a tiny, decisive shake.

"No TV," Rachel rasped softly.

"No TV," I agreed instantly.

The thought of the bright, flashing screen and the unpredictable, chaotic noise of daytime television sounded completely exhausting. My brain was already running on empty. We didn't want to watch anything. We didn't want to play video games. We just wanted absolute quiet.

We walked over to the edge of the sofa. I was completely drained. I kicked my slippers off and practically collapsed backward, stretching my stiff, aching legs out and resting my head firmly against the soft, overstuffed pillow. The cool blue leather felt incredible against my back. Out of sheer, bone-deep exhaustion, I was completely sprawled out, taking up the entire length of the cushions.

Rachel stood at the edge of the sofa, looking down at me. She wanted to lie down, and she clearly wanted to cuddle close, but there wasn't an inch of leather left to sit on. She glanced down at the carpet. She definitely wasn't going to sleep on the floor.

So, she just climbed directly on top of me.

It was the very first time we had ever tried lying like this. She settled down perfectly flat, stomach to stomach, wrapping her arms securely around my back and tucking her head comfortably right beneath my chin.

It made perfect, absolute sense. Her entire body weight pressed down heavily on my chest, and that heavy, physical pressure was exactly what I needed. It manually forced my racing heart to slow down into a calm, steady rhythm. In return, the steady rise and fall of my chest gave her a quiet, predictable motion to fall asleep to.

I reached down with one hand, grabbed the heavy, knitted afghan resting on the back of the sofa, and pulled it entirely over us, insulating us from the cool air conditioning of the house. I wrapped both of my arms around her back, locking her securely against me.

On the carpet nearby, Teddy let out a soft, contented sigh, his dull golden fur blending into the rug as he circled twice and went to sleep. Jimbo was already snoring softly near the entryway.

The outside world could have their pranks, their schedules, and their high-definition screens. Wrapped in the heavy afghan, holding each other tightly and anchored to the blue leather sofa, we closed our eyes and let the three-day biological reset wash over us in total, perfectly regulated silence.
