Dad walked through the front door right at seven o'clock, still wearing his *Baja Taco* manager's uniform, completely exhausted but smiling the second he saw the energy in the house. Mom had gotten home from the mall at five and had immediately taken over the kitchen.

Dinner was a delightfully simple, completely kid-approved affair: a massive, steaming pot of homemade macaroni and cheese, served with a stack of buttered bread.

We crowded around the dining room table, our seating arrangement reflecting the absolute comfort of the day. Instead of taking her own chair, Rachel was sitting directly in my lap sideways. I had both arms wrapped tightly around her waist, holding her securely against my chest while I ate over her shoulder. Right next to us, Ashley had pulled her chair as close as physically possible. She had one arm draped over the back of my chair and the other resting over Rachel's knees, effectively hugging us both while she ate her dinner.

Dad took his seat at the head of the table, looking over the chaotic, happy huddle of kids.

"Alright, tent commanders," Dad said, pointing his fork at Melissa and Jessica. "We are losing the daylight. As soon as dinner is done, we pound the stakes into the grass."

Rachel vibrated with pure excitement against my chest, her hands gripping my forearms tightly.

"And let's review the safety protocol one last time," Mom added, passing a bowl of macaroni to Melissa. "Your father and I are leaving the master bedroom window wide open tonight. The screen stays locked so the bugs stay out, but the glass stays up."

"If you hear a weird noise, if you get scared, or if you just decide you want to sleep in your real beds, you just yell," Dad instructed firmly. "I don't care what time it is. You scream, and I will be out that back door in three seconds flat. Understood?"

"Understood," Melissa and Jessica echoed together.

"We... will... yell," Rachel rasped happily, completely unafraid.

Once the plates were cleared, the entire pack migrated to the backyard. The humid Virginia heat was finally starting to break, replaced by a comfortable evening breeze. Dad, Melissa, and Jessica unrolled the massive nylon dome tent on the flattest patch of grass, snapping the fiberglass poles together and driving the metal stakes deep into the dirt with a rubber mallet.

While they built the structure, I handled the technology. I carried my heavy, silver portable stereo out the back door. It had everything we needed for a campout: an AM/FM radio, a dual cassette deck, and a top-loading CD player. I set it carefully just inside the tent door, then grabbed the heavy-duty, bright orange outdoor extension cord Dad kept in the garage. I ran the thick cord across the grass, plugging one end into the stereo and the other directly into the exterior outlet on the back wall of the house, securing our shore power for the night.

Inside the tent, it was pure, unadulterated chaos. Instead of claiming individual corners, the girls decided to pool our resources. We unzipped all five sleeping bags completely flat, layering them over the nylon floor to create one massive, wall-to-wall communal bed. We dragged out an absurd number of pillows and heavy quilts from the house, throwing them onto the sleeping bags until the inside of the tent looked like a giant, soft fortress.

"Alright, the perimeter is secure!" Melissa announced, clapping her hands as the last pillow was tossed inside. "Everyone inside to change and hit the bathroom. When we come back out, we are officially camping."

The house became a flurry of synchronized activity. I headed upstairs to my bedroom to change into my softest pajama pants and a loose t-shirt. Jessica and Rachel went to their shared bedroom across the hall to dig out their pajamas, while Melissa and Ashley claimed the downstairs bathroom to change.

We spent the next twenty minutes rotating through the bathrooms, brushing our teeth, and taking one final, highly mandated toilet break so nobody would have to navigate the dark yard in the middle of the night.

Finally, the entire pack met by the back door, armed with flashlights and extra blankets. Dad stood on the patio, holding the back door open for us.

"Have fun, base camp," Dad smiled, giving Rachel a gentle tap on the shoulder as she walked past him.

We spilled out into the dark backyard, the orange extension cord leading the way like a lifeline. Melissa unzipped the heavy nylon door, and one by one, we crawled into the massive, padded fortress, ready to spend the entire night under the stars.
