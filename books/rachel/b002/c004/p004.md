As the last of the pizza bagels disappeared from the coffee table, Melissa clapped her hands together, brushing a few stray crumbs off her jeans.

"Alright, troops. Lunch is done, the movie is over, and we have serious logistics to discuss," Melissa announced, shifting into full commander mode. At seventeen, she naturally took the lead on major operations. "Tonight is the backyard campout."

Rachel practically vibrated against my chest. She had been staring at the heavy nylon bag containing Dad’s brand-new, multi-room dome tent ever since he brought it home last week. She squeezed my arms, a huge, toothy smile spreading across her face.

"I... am... ready," Rachel rasped, her dark eyes shining with pure excitement.

"I know you are," Jessica smiled, reaching over to playfully ruffle Rachel’s hair.

"Okay, here is the rundown," Melissa said, pulling a small spiral notebook out of her canvas bag. "The tent is massive, so we have plenty of room. It’s going to be me, Jessica, Michael, Rachel, and Ashley. Five sleeping bags. I am officially in charge of the flashlight and battery inventory. Jessica, you're on snack duty. We need chips, soda, and those little powdered donuts."

"Already handled," Jessica confirmed with a mock salute. "I hid the donuts on the top shelf of the pantry so Dad wouldn't eat them when he gets home."

"Perfect," Melissa nodded, clicking her pen. "Now, safety protocol. Mom and Dad already gave us the official briefing this morning before they left for work. The tent is going to be pitched directly under their second-story bedroom window."

"And the window stays open all night," Ashley chimed in, knowing the neighborhood drill.

"Exactly," Melissa pointed her pen at Ashley. "The screen stays locked, but the glass stays open. If we need anything, if we get spooked, or if Archie starts barking at a raccoon, we don't even have to unzip the tent. We just scream for help, and an adult *will* hear us instantly. Understood?"

I nodded, resting my chin on Rachel's head. It was the classic Delaney Street security measure. The adults gave us the absolute freedom of the backyard, but they never actually took themselves off the clock.

"We... will... be... safe," Rachel whispered, perfectly content with the rules.

"Totally safe," I agreed, tightening my arms slightly around both Rachel and Ashley in the cuddle knot.

"So, we wait until Dad gets home at seven," Melissa concluded, snapping her notebook shut and tossing it back into her bag. "He said he’d help me and Jessica drive the tent stakes into the grass before it gets dark. Then, the backyard is officially ours for the night."

Rachel wiggled happily, tapping a rapid, joyful beat against my forearm. Between her newfound voice, the arcade invitations, and the prospect of sleeping outside under the stars in a brand-new canvas fortress, it was shaping up to be the greatest Wednesday of the entire summer.
