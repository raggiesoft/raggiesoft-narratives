The doorbell rang right at ten o'clock, sending Archie scrambling to his feet. His tail immediately started thumping against the wall.

"I'll get it," Jessica announced, carrying her empty plate to the sink and walking to the entryway.

She pulled the front door open, and a tornado of kinetic energy instantly swept into the house. It was Ashley, flanked closely by her older sister, Melissa.

"Hey, guys!" Ashley practically vibrated with excitement. She bypassed the kitchen completely, sprinting directly toward the living room where I was already settling into Dad's oversized recliner.

She didn't stop to ask. She didn't hesitate. Ashley threw herself onto the armrest of the recliner, throwing her arms around both Rachel and me in a fierce, breathless, crushing hug. It was the absolute baseline of our friendship—loud, physical, and completely safe. I pulled one of my arms out from around Rachel to wrap it around Ashley, pulling her tight against my side, returning the platonic affection without a second thought.

Rachel laughed, a happy, genuine sound, returning the hug with her free arm.

"Okay, look what I have," Ashley announced, pulling back just enough to reach into the pocket of her jeans.

She produced three brightly colored, glossy envelopes and slapped them triumphantly onto the armrest right next to Rachel's knee. Printed in bold, neon-pink letters across the front of the envelopes were the words: *Captain Charlie’s Pizza Arcade.* "The official invitations," Ashley beamed. "The party is next Saturday, the 26th. The arcade was totally booked on my actual birthday on Sunday, so we get to party on Saturday instead."

"Awesome," I grinned, looking down at the envelope. *Captain Charlie's* meant pizza, ball pits, and endless tokens for the arcade cabinets. It was the absolute pinnacle of ten-year-old birthday locations.

"Jessica, yours is in there too," Ashley added, pointing at the stack as Jessica and Melissa walked into the room.

"Thanks, Ash," Jessica smiled, dropping onto the sofa next to Melissa. "We wouldn't miss it."

Ashley looked back down at Rachel. She climbed fully onto the wide armrest of the recliner and wedged herself in tightly next to us. It was a specific, honorary-pack variation of the lap cuddle. Rachel was sitting sideways across my lap, anchored against my chest, and Ashley sat right beside her, leaning her shoulder against Rachel’s so that the three of us formed an impenetrable knot of comfortable, physical contact.

"I heard about the Dojang," Ashley said, her voice dropping into a tone of absolute awe as she bumped her shoulder gently against Rachel's. "Jessica told Melissa on the phone. You used your voice in front of everyone."

Rachel ducked her head slightly, feeling suddenly shy, but she leaned harder into Ashley's side.

"She called Master Curtis mean in front of the whole class yesterday," I bragged for her, tightening my arm around Rachel’s waist.

Ashley’s blue eyes went wide. "You talked back to the karate teacher? That is so brave."

Rachel looked up, her dark eyes shining with pride. She took a deep breath, the rhythm of her lungs expanding easily against my chest and Ashley's shoulder.

"I... am... coming... to... your... party," Rachel whispered, forcing her vocal cords to navigate the sentence, the rasp heavy but determined.

Ashley gasped, completely overwhelmed with excitement at hearing Rachel's voice directed right at her. She threw her arm around Rachel's shoulders, squeezing her tightly.

"And we're going to play roller-bowling until our arms fall off!" Ashley declared.

I laughed, resting my chin on Rachel's head again while keeping Ashley tucked safely against my side.

"Alright, the invitations are delivered," Melissa announced from the sofa, unzipping her canvas bag. "Now it's movie time."

She pulled out a black VHS tape in a white clamshell case: the 1990 live-action *Galactic Samurai Geckos* film.

A collective cheer went up in the living room. It was our absolute favorite movie in the world. We had seen it a hundred times. We knew every single martial arts sequence, every camera angle, and every punchline by heart.

Melissa slid the tape into the VCR, grabbed the TV remote, and hit play, turning the volume up so it filled the room. She and Jessica sprawled out on the sofa, while Baby the black lab wandered over and rested his heavy chin directly on Ashley's knee. Archie curled up at the foot of the recliner, happily gnawing on a knotted rope.

For the next hour and a half, the living room was an interactive theater. We quoted the jokes right along with the characters on the screen, completely relaxed in our shared fortress. The physical affection was easy and constant—whenever the action on screen got intense, Rachel would grip my arms, and Ashley would lean closer, her shoulder pressed firmly against mine.

Finally, the movie reached its climax. The four hero geckos were on the rain-slicked rooftop, fighting the main villain, the heavily armored warlord known as The Steel Claw.

The choreography was deeply familiar. On the screen, the villain overpowered the leader in the blue mask, knocking his weapons away and sending him crashing backward onto the concrete. The music swelled with ominous, heavy brass horns. And then, the warlord raised his heavily booted foot and drove it down, stepping with his full body weight directly onto the hero's chest to pin him to the roof.

The reaction in the living room was a synchronized, highly practiced routine.

"Oh no!" Ashley gasped dramatically, putting her hands over her cheeks in mock horror.

Rachel let out a loud, theatrical gasp of her own, turning her head to hide her face playfully against my t-shirt, peeking out with one eye to watch the screen.

"Get up, get up!" I urged the blue leader, completely invested in the movie magic, wrapping my arms tighter around Rachel in a protective, fun squeeze.

We loved the tension. We loved the high stakes. We knew exactly what was coming next, but it didn't make the scene any less thrilling. The hero struggled helplessly under the warlord's heavy boot, the villain raising his bladed weapon for the final strike.

And then, stepping out of the shadows, the wise, elderly master appeared.

The master challenged The Steel Claw, distracting him just in time. The warlord lifted his foot off the hero's chest to face the new threat. The master executed a flawless, flipping counter-attack, sending the villain tumbling off the edge of the roof into the back of a garbage truck far below.

"Yes!" Ashley cheered, throwing her arms up in the air.

Rachel clapped her hands together, a bright, triumphant smile lighting up her face as the heroes celebrated their victory on the screen. I let out a loud whoop, high-fiving Ashley over Rachel's head.

The credits rolled, a catchy, upbeat pop-rap song playing over the speakers. The tension evaporated, replaced by pure, adrenaline-fueled excitement.

"That part never gets old," Jessica laughed from the sofa, tossing a throw pillow at Melissa.

"Never," I agreed, leaning my head back against the recliner. Rachel shifted comfortably in my lap, her breathing steady, her body completely relaxed.

It was just a movie. The violence on the screen was just choreography and costumes, safely contained inside the heavy glass tube of the television. As we sat there in the late morning sunlight, wrapped up in the lap cuddle with the dogs at our feet, the world felt entirely safe, predictable, and fun.
