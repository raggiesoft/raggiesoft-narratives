With the movie finished and the hero geckos victorious, Melissa hit the stop button on the VCR. The screen dropped to static for a brief second before she clicked the heavy CRT television off entirely.

"Alright," Jessica announced, stretching her arms over her head and swinging her legs off the sofa. "Saving the city works up an appetite. Melissa, help me make pizza bagels?"

"On it," Melissa agreed, grabbing her canvas bag off the floor and following Jessica into the kitchen. Soon, the sounds of the refrigerator opening and the toaster oven clanking echoed down the hallway.

Back in the living room, we stayed perfectly locked in our honorary-pack cuddle. Rachel was resting comfortably against my chest, and Ashley was leaning heavily against Rachel's shoulder.

"Oh, wait, I almost forgot!" Ashley suddenly announced, shifting her weight just enough to reach into her back pocket. "I brought the photo from last weekend!"

She pulled out a glossy 8x10 photograph, perfectly preserved in a thick cardboard sleeve with the *Brewster Gardens: The Old World* logo stamped in gold foil across the bottom.

It was the official on-ride photo from *The Loch Serpent*—the massive, interlocking-loop rollercoaster in the park's Scotland section. Park regulars affectionately called the ride "Serpy." It had been Ashley's first time ever riding a major coaster.

She proudly held the photo out so Rachel and I could see it.

In the picture, the coaster train was plunging down the steepest, most terrifying drop. I was sitting in the outside seat, laughing wildly with my hands thrown straight up in the air. Right next to me, taking up the inside seat, was Ashley. Her eyes were squeezed entirely shut, her mouth was open in a terrified scream, and both of her hands were clamped onto my left arm in a desperate, white-knuckled death grip.

"Look at my face," Ashley giggled, pointing at her terrified expression.

"You almost broke my arm," I teased her, nudging her shoulder. "I thought you were going to rip my sleeve off."

"I was so scared!" Ashley admitted. She threw her arms around my neck and hugged me fiercely, burying her face playfully into my shoulder. "But I did it! I rode Serpy!"

I didn't freeze. I didn't pull away, and I didn't feel awkward. I just laughed, wrapping my free arm securely around her back and hugging her right back. "You did do it. You were incredibly brave."

Rachel tapped the photo, a wide smile on her face. She gave Ashley a supportive thumbs-up.

"You were so brave, Ash!" Jessica called out from the kitchen, having heard the conversation.

"Even if you did cut off the blood circulation to Michael's arm!" Melissa added, laughing over the hum of the toaster oven.

"It was totally worth it," Ashley declared, pulling back from the hug and settling happily back into the cuddle knot against Rachel.

A few minutes later, Jessica and Melissa walked out of the kitchen balancing two plates loaded with perfectly toasted pizza bagels. They set them down on the coffee table, and the five of us dug in, sharing the food and laughing as we recounted the rest of the rides from the theme park.

As we sat there eating lunch, wrapped up in the lap cuddle with the dogs resting heavily at our feet, there was no need to brace for anything. The movie was a hit, the house was ours, and it was just a perfect, golden Wednesday afternoon.
