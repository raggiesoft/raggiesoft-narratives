The morning light bleeding through the bedroom blinds felt heavy and exhausting.

I woke up on the bottom bunk, completely locked in place. Rachel was still curled tightly against my side, her breathing shallow. Overnight, the acute, sharp sting in our stomachs had transformed into a deep, stiff, agonizing muscular throb. Every microscopic shift of my weight caused my abdominal wall to seize violently.

Up on the top bunk, I heard the soft rustle of blankets. Jessica was already awake. She carefully climbed down the metal rungs, landing silently on the carpet.

Before anyone could speak, a soft, hesitant knock sounded at the bedroom door.

It wasn't Dad’s heavy, demanding pounding from the night before. It was quiet.

"Jessica?" Mom’s voice murmured through the painted wood. "It's Mom. Is it unlocked?"

Jessica looked at me. I gave a tiny, rigid nod. She stepped over to the door, twisted the lock, and pulled it open just a few inches, keeping her body positioned as a physical barricade in the frame.

Mom looked terrible. She was still wearing her bathrobe, her face pale, and the dark circles under her eyes proved she hadn't slept a single wink. The fierce, unified front she had tried to maintain in the car yesterday had completely dissolved.

"Dad left for work early," Mom whispered, her voice trembling slightly. She looked past Jessica, her eyes finding Rachel and me huddled on the bottom mattress. "I couldn't shake it. My gut was telling me I was wrong last night. I need to see."

Jessica’s posture softened just a fraction. She opened the door wider, allowing Mom to step into our sanctuary.

Mom approached the edge of the bunk bed. She dropped to her knees on the carpet so she was exactly at eye level with us.

"Rachel, baby," Mom asked gently. "Can I please see your stomach?"

Rachel looked at me. I gave her shoulder a reassuring squeeze. Slowly, with trembling hands, Rachel lifted the hem of her tank top.

Mom gasped, her hands instantly flying up to cover her mouth.

Overnight, the red inflammation had blossomed into a dark, angry mosaic of deep purple and sickly blue. But the most horrifying part wasn't the color; it was the geometry. Bruised into the soft tissue of my eight-year-old sister's stomach was the undeniable, localized imprint of a grown man's foot.

"Oh my god," Mom choked out, tears instantly welling in her eyes. She turned to me, her face completely stripped of any remaining martial arts illusions. "Michael. Let me see."

My chest seized. My heart hammered wildly against my ribs.

As an overweight ten-year-old boy, my body was already a source of immense vulnerability and shame. Master Curtis had actively weaponized my weight against me just yesterday, staring down at my stomach in disgust. The idea of pulling my shirt up and exposing my midsection—even to my mother—felt incredibly humiliating.

I hesitated, my hands frozen near my waist.

Jessica immediately stepped up to my side. She didn't push me; she just rested a warm, grounding hand on my shoulder. "It's okay, Michael," she promised softly. "Show her."

I gritted my teeth, closed my eyes, and pulled my undershirt up.

Because of my extra weight, the physical mechanics of the crush were even worse. The localized pressure had violently pinched the tissue against my abdominal wall. The bruise on my stomach was massive, a swollen, dark purple mark that radiated pain with every breath.

Mom let out a jagged, devastated sob.

"He stepped on you," Mom wept, reaching out as if to touch the bruises, but stopping her hands inches away, terrified of hurting us more. "He actually stepped on you."

"He stepped on us twice," I corrected her, my voice shaking as the anger finally overrode my shame.

Mom froze. "Twice?"

"The first time was a drill," I explained, the words rushing out of me. "He stepped on everyone in the class. But when he was done, he turned around and came back just for Rachel and me. He was punishing us for Tuesday."

"He didn't just step over you?" Mom asked, the horrific reality of the premeditation dawning on her.

"No," Rachel rasped, finding her voice in the safety of the bedroom. "He stayed."

"He put all his weight on us," I continued, tears finally spilling hot down my cheeks. "His foot lingered. He pressed down. And while he did it, he whispered at us. He mocked Rachel for not having a spirit shout, and he called me fat. He told us we were pathetic."

The air completely left the room.

The culture of 'discipline' and 'building character' evaporated in a microsecond, burned away by the undeniable evidence of targeted, malicious abuse.

Mom broke. She slumped forward, resting her forehead against the edge of the mattress, and cried harder than I had ever heard her cry.

"I am so sorry," Mom sobbed, reaching out to blindly grasp my hand and Rachel's hand, holding them in a desperate, trembling grip. "I am so incredibly sorry. I didn't listen to you. I let your father convince me you were just being soft. I am the adult, and I failed to protect you."

She lifted her head, looking up at Jessica, tears streaming down her face.

"You were right, Jessica," Mom confessed, her voice cracking with profound remorse. "You and Janette were entirely right. I promise you—all three of you—I will never dismiss you like that again. From this second forward, I am listening to you."

Mom wiped her face roughly with the sleeve of her bathrobe, a new, fierce determination suddenly replacing her sorrow.

"Get dressed," Mom commanded, her tone shifting into absolute, protective logistics. "Don't put jeans on. Wear sweatpants. Whatever doesn't press against your stomachs."

"Where are we going?" Rachel whimpered.

"We are going to Dr. Young," Mom stated firmly. "Right now."
