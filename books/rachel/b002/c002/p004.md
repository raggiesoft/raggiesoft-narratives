When the Dan candidates were called, the energy in the room shifted drastically. Master Ray called Master Curtis forward, hoping for a smooth, impressive display to secure the Dojang's reputation.

Curtis walked out like he owned the floor. He bowed to the flags. He turned and bowed deeply to Master Scott. And then, in a petty, calculated display of ego, he offered only the most rigid, minimalistic head-jerk to the twenty-two-year-old woman sitting beside him, pointedly ignoring the intricate silver embroidery down her lapel that officially marked her as a *Sunhoe Sabomnim*.

Scott caught the disrespect instantly. Instead of issuing the command to begin, the 7th Dan Regional Director took a deliberate step backward. He turned toward April, brought his heels together, and executed a formal, respectful bow.

"As tradition dictates," Master Scott announced, his voice carrying clearly across the silent Dojang, "the highest authority on a testing floor is the Voice of Korea. *Sunhoe Sabomnim*, you hold the *Gwonhan*. The floor is yours to administer this examination."

The room went dead silent.

Master Scott knew exactly what he was doing. He knew Curtis harbored a deep, toxic resentment toward the young woman who used to instruct him. By formally yielding the floor to her, Scott wasn't just testing Curtis's physical forms; he was testing his ego and his humility.

Curtis froze. The muscles in his jaw pulsed. The traditional rules of the governing body were an absolute iron cage. Because this was a testing environment, the young woman with the silver embroidery outranked every single person in the room—including Scott.

Curtis turned to face April.

April didn't blink. She just looked at him with the cold, clinical apathy of absolute authority.

*"Sijak,"* April commanded. Begin.

Curtis launched into his form. He wanted to prove what *real* martial arts looked like. He wanted to erase the "weakness" of the children's test. His form was fast. It was sharp. It was violently loud, his uniform snapping like a bullwhip, his kihaps echoing off the high ceiling.

To the parents, it looked lethal. But April wasn't looking for violence. She was looking for mastery.

Curtis’s transitions were aggressive and sloppy. He was relying on momentum instead of balance. His center of gravity was high. It was a performance driven entirely by ego, devoid of internal *Tongje*—control.

When Curtis finished, he stood tall, his chest heaving, staring defiantly at her and waiting for the nod of approval.

April let the silence stretch. *"Dasi,"* the *Sunhoe Sabomnim* ordered, her voice completely devoid of emotion. Again. "Slower."

Curtis blinked. The humiliation was instantaneous. He had to take a correction from her in front of the entire franchise. "Ma'am—"

"Slower," April repeated, her tone dropping, leaving absolutely no room for debate.

Curtis’s face flushed dark red. He ran the form again. He slowed it down, but the resentment radiated out of him. His shoulders were rigid. His eyes burned with defiance.

When he finished, his wounded ego couldn't take the silence. He needed to justify himself, and he couldn't stand submitting to her. He looked directly at the Green Belt line, locking eyes with Michael and Rachel to deflect his failure onto them.

"With respect..." Curtis said, addressing the panel but projecting his voice to the room. "We’re handing out passes for half-techniques and no kihap now?"

The room went ice cold. Master Ray’s stomach dropped.

Curtis hadn't just questioned a grade. He had argued with a *Sunhoe Sabomnim*.

In the rigid, ancient hierarchy of Korean martial arts, it was the ultimate, unforgivable sin. You do not argue with the Voice of Korea unless you have an absolute, undeniable demonstration of mechanics as to why—and petty, wounded pride did not qualify.

On the edge of the mat, Master Scott’s entire body went rigid. The instinct of a 7th Dan Regional Director was to immediately step forward and crush the insubordination.

But Scott did not take a single step. He did not speak.

Even in his blinding fury at the blasphemy, the 7th Dan knew the absolute law of the *Seonduwon* better than anyone. He would never step on the toes of the Voice of Korea. He turned his head slightly, his eyes locking onto April, silently requesting the floor.

April didn't blink. She held Curtis’s defiant gaze for a fraction of a second, then gave Master Scott a single, discreet nod.

Permission granted.

With the Emissary's blessing, Scott unleashed hell. He took one heavy, devastating step forward, the suffocating gravity of a 7th Dan crashing down on the mat.

"I evaluated *Jeongsin*! I evaluated *Tongje*!" Master Scott bellowed, his voice booming across the Dojang like a physical shockwave, deliberately projecting his volume so every single parent in the second-floor observation deck could hear the execution. "When a green belt attempts a requirement, I evaluate spirit, mechanics, and control. They attempted! They demonstrated discipline under limitation!"

Scott stepped closer, locking onto Curtis like a targeting laser. "You are not a green belt. You are testing for leadership. You do not question a pass for a child who cannot physically complete a technique. You recognize the effort, and you adapt. You do not challenge a student who cannot produce sound. You recognize the spirit, and you protect it!"

Scott stopped three feet from Curtis. "And you do not stand in front of this room and attempt to elevate yourself by diminishing them!"

Curtis’s face was dark purple. "Sir, I’m only asking for fair—"

"No!" Scott cut him off. The word was a guillotine. "You are no longer asking. You are arguing. In front of students. In front of parents. And you are displaying absolute *Murye*—insolence—with a *Sunhoe Sabomnim*."

Scott let the absolute weight of that title crush whatever was left of Curtis's ego, ensuring the audience upstairs fully understood the geopolitical magnitude of the disrespect.

"To argue with the Emissary is to argue with the Grandmasters themselves!" Scott continued, his voice ringing with lethal, undeniable authority. "Third Dan is leadership. It is humility. It is safety. You have demonstrated none of those today. Your technique is not the problem. Your ego is!"

Having delivered the public lecture, custom and absolute law dictated that Master Scott could not issue the final grade. He turned his head, formally deferring back to the ultimate authority in the room.

*"Sunhoe Sabomnim,"* Scott said quietly, his volume dropping back to a respectful murmur. *"Pangyeol-eun-yo?"* Your verdict?

April looked at the thirty-four-year-old man standing on the mat. He was a bully, a coward, and a disgrace to the art she had dedicated her life to. She didn't raise her voice, but her words carried the absolute, unyielding weight of the Grandmasters in Korea.

*"Bulhapgyeok,"* Master April decreed. *"Mulleogara."* Fail. Step off the mat.
