The loud, grating buzzer echoed across the cavernous gymnastics floor, signaling the official end of the 7:00 PM classes.

Normally, that sound was our starter pistol. It was the cue for Rachel and me to scramble down the metal bleachers, our bare feet slapping against the steps, to rush the lobby doors so we could hug Jessica and Janette the second they walked off the spring floor. It was the moment our perimeter expanded, taking us from a frightened unit of two back to a secure pack of four.

But tonight, we didn't move an inch.

I stayed firmly planted against the cold cinderblock wall in the top corner of the observation deck. Rachel was completely curled up in my lap, her knees pulled tight against my chest. She was weeping into the collar of my *dobok*, her small frame shuddering with every breath. I kept my arms locked tightly around her, resting my chin on top of her head. My leg was entirely numb from the circulation being cut off, and the localized, bruised ache in my stomach throbbed with every heartbeat, but I wasn't letting her go.

Down below, the gymnastics floor rapidly emptied out.

Through the wooden railings, I saw Jessica and Janette grabbing their gym bags and water bottles. They looked up toward our corner, expecting to see us already halfway down the stairs.

When Jessica saw that the bleachers were empty and we were still huddled in the shadows of the top row, her posture instantly changed. The relaxed, post-workout ease vanished. Her pragmatic, protective radar spiked. She dropped her water bottle and bolted for the stairs.

I heard her light, rapid footsteps bounding up the metal grating, taking the steps two at a time. Janette was right on her heels, matching her frantic pace.

"Michael? Rachel?" Jessica called out, her voice echoing in the empty observation deck.

She rounded the corner and stopped dead.

She took in the scene—me sitting completely rigid against the wall, my face pale, holding Rachel, who was shaking and crying so hard her tears were actively soaking through both of our uniforms.

Janette’s heavy canvas gym bag hit the bleachers with a loud thud. The shock on her face instantly melted into a deep, alarmed panic. She dropped to her knees right beside Jessica, leaning in close.

"Michael? What's wrong?" Janette pleaded, her voice trembling as she reached out, gently resting her hand on my shoulder. She looked down at Rachel's shaking frame. "Why is she crying like this? Are you guys hurt?"

Jessica’s eyes were wide, scanning us frantically for visible blood or broken bones. Her protective instincts immediately jumped to the most logical conclusion based on the Dojang's hostile environment.

"Who was it?" Jessica demanded, her voice dropping into a fierce, terrifying whisper. She rested her hand on Rachel's back, right next to Janette's. "Did Curtis pair her with one of the older boys again? Did someone kick her too hard?"

"It wasn't sparring," I rasped, my throat tight. I swallowed hard, fighting back my own tears.

"Then what happened?" Jessica pressed, her jaw clenching. "Michael, tell me what happened."

"It was Curtis," I whispered, the admission tasting like ash in my mouth.

Janette gasped, her grip on my shoulder tightening protectively. "What did he do to you?"

"He made us do leg lifts," I explained, my voice shaking as the sheer humiliation and terror of the drill washed over me again. "And while we were holding them... he stepped on us. He walked down the line and put his full weight on our stomachs. All of us."

"He stepped on you?!" Janette repeated, her voice cracking with absolute outrage. She looked at Rachel, then back at me, her protective older-cousin instincts fully igniting. "But you're just kids! He's a grown man!"

"I know," I whimpered, the tears finally spilling over my eyelashes. "But it wasn't just a step. He came back to us at the end. To punish us for Tuesday. He put his foot on Rachel, and he shifted his weight, and he just... he stayed there. He kept his foot on her stomach for an extra second, and he did it to me, too. It hurts so bad."

The color completely drained from Jessica's face.

The fierce, tactical protector was suddenly replaced by a fourteen-year-old girl staring at the undeniable reality of an adult abusing her siblings behind a closed door. Pure, unadulterated horror flooded her eyes.

"He crushed you," Jessica breathed, her hands balling into fists on her knees.

Rachel let out a loud, jagged sob, burying her face deeper into my chest. The situational mutism had locked her voice away, but the pain radiating from her was deafening.

Janette didn't hesitate. While Jessica was paralyzed with fury, Janette slid closer, wrapping her arm securely around my back and leaning her head against my shoulder, pulling me into a tight side-hug. She was physically supporting me the exact same way I was supporting Rachel.

"It's going to be okay, Michael," Janette promised fiercely, her own eyes shining with angry tears. "He is not getting away with this."

"We need to get out of here," Jessica stated, snapping out of her shock. Her voice trembled with a mixture of absolute fury and helpless panic. "We are telling Mom. Right now. He is never touching either of you again."

Janette immediately stood up, reaching down to grab Rachel. "Come here, sweetie," Janette cooed softly.

Rachel unlatched her arms from my neck, and Janette carefully scooped her up. But the second her body shifted, Rachel let out a sharp, breathless shriek, clutching her left side in absolute agony. She buried her face into Janette's neck, trembling violently.

Jessica looked at me. "Can you walk?"

"I don't know," I admitted.

I tried to unfold my legs and hinge my torso upright. The second my core muscles engaged, a blinding fire ripped through the center of my gut. I gasped, my knees buckling instantly. I would have hit the wooden bleachers if Jessica hadn't shot her hands out and caught me.

Jessica’s jaw set into a hard, determined line. She realized we couldn't walk. Not without searing pain.

I was ten, and I was heavy. But Jessica was fourteen, a competitive gymnast with incredible core and upper body strength, and currently fueled by pure, unadulterated adrenaline.

"Put your arms around my neck," Jessica commanded, stepping down one row so her back was perfectly level with me.

I reached forward, and Jessica reached back, hooking her arms securely under my knees. With a sharp grunt of effort, she hoisted my entire weight onto her back. I kept my stomach pushed out just enough so it wasn't pressed flat against her, terrified of the pressure, and she adjusted her grip so my weight rested on her hips.

We moved as a desperate, limping caravan. Janette carried Rachel tightly in her arms, and Jessica carried my dead weight on her back, hauling me down the steep metal staircase.

When we reached the lobby, we didn't look left or right. We kept our heads down, rushing straight for the heavy glass exit doors, terrified that Curtis or Ray would step out of the office and see us.

We burst out into the muggy August night, practically running across the asphalt to where Mom’s sedan was parked under the streetlights.

Mom was standing by the trunk. When she saw her fourteen-year-old and twelve-year-old staggering across the parking lot, physically carrying us, she let out an exasperated sigh. She didn't drop her purse in horror. She didn't look terrified. She just looked tired.

"What on earth are you girls doing?" Mom asked, shaking her head as she unlocked the back doors.

"Mom, they're hurt," Jessica panted, carefully turning sideways so I could slide off her back and onto the soft fabric of the car seat. "Master Curtis hurt them."

"Oh, stop being so dramatic, Jessica," Mom dismissed, waving her hand as Janette gently lowered a weeping Rachel into the seat beside me. "Dad already talked to Master Ray. He warned us they were going to be sore tonight. It was a tough conditioning class."

"Mom, no, you don't understand—" Janette pleaded, her voice cracking.

"Get in the car, girls," Mom ordered, her voice firm and completely closed off to debate. "They just need to toughen up. They're fine."

She reached past me to pull my seatbelt across my chest. As the heavy strap dragged across my stomach, I flinched, biting back a groan. Mom didn't notice. The "tough love" narrative Dad had planted earlier that evening had completely blinded her to the reality right in front of her face. We were sitting in the dark backseat, broken and in agonizing pain, and the adult world was driving us straight home without a second glance.
