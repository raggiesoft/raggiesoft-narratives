title: "Breaking Camp"
date: 1995-08-17
start_time: "07:30"

Waking up inside a nylon tent always felt fundamentally different than waking up in a house.

The morning sun didn't filter through curtains; it hit the dome of the tent directly, turning the entire interior into a glowing, warm pocket of bright orange light. The air smelled like damp grass and morning dew, and the sound of the neighborhood birds was incredibly loud through the thin walls.

I blinked my eyes open, my back slightly stiff despite the massive layers of sleeping bags and quilts we had piled onto the ground.

On my left, Rachel was still fast asleep, her face buried into my shoulder, her fingers loosely gripping the fabric of my t-shirt. On my right, Ashley was already starting to stir. She let out a long, quiet yawn, stretching her arms over her head and kicking her legs out inside her sleeping bag.

"Morning," Ashley whispered, her voice thick with sleep. She rolled onto her side, resting her cheek on her hand to look at me.

"Morning," I whispered back, carefully shifting my weight so I wouldn't wake Rachel just yet.

At the back of the tent, Melissa was already sitting up. She was running a brush through her hair, looking incredibly awake for seven-thirty in the morning. Jessica was parallel to the door, stubbornly pulling her quilt over her head to block out the bright orange sunlight.

"Alright, troops. The sun is up," Melissa announced, using her hushed commander voice. She leaned over and gave Jessica’s blanket a firm shake. "Up and at 'em. We have to break camp before the yard gets too hot."

Jessica let out a muffled groan of protest from under the quilt, but she slowly pulled the blanket down.

The commotion finally woke Rachel. She blinked her dark eyes open, squinting against the bright light of the tent. She looked around at the patchwork of blankets, her brain taking a few seconds to process that we had actually spent the entire night in the backyard. When the realization hit, a wide, triumphant smile spread across her face.

She pushed herself up on her elbows and looked at me.

"We... did... it," Rachel rasped, her morning voice heavy but filled with absolute pride.

"We survived the wilderness," I grinned, reaching over to ruffle her messy hair.

Melissa crawled over to the front of the tent and pulled the heavy zippers of the door apart. The cool morning air rushed inside, carrying the unmistakable scent of the Virginia humidity starting to build for the day. Sitting directly on the concrete patio, patiently waiting for us, were Archie and Baby. The second the door unzipped, Archie’s tail started thumping a rhythmic beat against the pavement.

"Hey, boys," Ashley smiled, crawling toward the door to give both black Labs a thorough morning scratch behind the ears.

The teardown was a synchronized pack effort. Nobody complained. We all knew the rule: if we wanted the privilege of using the tent again, we had to leave the equipment exactly as we found it.

I unplugged the portable stereo and began wrapping the thick, bright orange extension cord around my elbow, disconnecting our shore power from the back of the house. Jessica and Ashley worked together to fold the heavy quilts, while Melissa meticulously rolled the sleeping bags into incredibly tight cylinders, tying them off with nylon straps. Rachel was put in charge of carrying the pillows inside, making three separate trips through the back door with her arms completely overflowing.

By eight-fifteen, the gear was stacked neatly in the living room, and the five of us were sitting around the kitchen table in yesterday's clothes, eating large bowls of sugary cereal. Dad was already upstairs sleeping off his closing shift, and Mom was sipping her morning coffee at the counter, already wearing her *Wellington's* uniform for her opening shift.

"You guys did a great job cleaning up the yard," Mom praised, rinsing her coffee mug in the sink. "Did anyone get cold?"

"Not at all," Melissa answered through a mouthful of cereal. "It was perfect. But Ash and I need to head home and shower. Choir practice is at noon."

"And I have gymnastics at two," Jessica added, resting her chin on her hand, looking a little exhausted now that the adrenaline of the campout had worn off.

"And we have the Dojang at five," I said, looking over at Rachel.

Rachel didn't flinch. The heavy, suffocating dread that normally accompanied the word 'Dojang' was completely absent from her posture. She just nodded confidently around her spoon. She had her voice, and she was going to use it.

Melissa and Ashley carried their bowls to the sink. Ashley walked back over to the table, stepping right up to Rachel's chair. She wrapped her arms around Rachel from behind, resting her chin on top of Rachel’s head for a quick, affectionate squeeze.

"See you guys later," Ashley smiled. She reached over and gave my shoulder a quick, solid squeeze as well. "Ten days until the arcade!"

"Ten days," I echoed, smiling back.

We watched the Raybourn sisters walk out the front door, the heavy wood clicking shut behind them. The house instantly felt quieter without them. Jessica let out a loud yawn, stretching her arms over her head, and announced she was going upstairs to catch a few more hours of sleep before gymnastics. Rachel carried her empty bowl to the sink, her steps light and energetic. The tent was packed away, the morning chores were done, and it was time to start our Thursday.
