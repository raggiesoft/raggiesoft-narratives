The heavy thud of a car door shutting in the driveway signaled the end of our living room training session.

Aunt Janet, Mom's sister, walked through the front door a moment later, followed closely by her daughter, our cousin Janette. Janette was already wearing her shimmering team leotard under a pair of athletic shorts, carrying her heavy canvas gym bag over her shoulder.

"Alright, Delaney Street, let's get moving!" Aunt Janet announced warmly, her keys jingling in her hand. "Gymnastics starts at two o'clock sharp, and the parking lot is always a nightmare!"

"Coming!" Jessica called out. She un-pretzeled her legs from her center split, grabbed her paperback novel off the carpet, and shoved it into her own gym bag.

Rachel and I grabbed our kick shield and quickly tossed it into the hall closet. We ran upstairs to our bedrooms to change out of our day clothes. I pulled on the heavy, stiff white canvas of my *dobok*, meticulously lining up the lapels before wrapping my Brown *tti* around my waist and tying it into a perfect, symmetrical knot.

When I walked back downstairs, Rachel was already standing in the entryway in her matching uniform, her own Brown belt securely tied, practically vibrating with energy.

Jessica stood by the front door, slipping her slides onto her feet. She looked at the two of us standing there in our crisp uniforms. She reached out and gave Rachel’s shoulder a gentle, affirming squeeze.

"You're going to blow the roof off the place today, Rachel," Jessica said, her voice filled with absolute pride. "I want to hear that shout all the way from the balance beam."

"I... will... be... loud," Rachel promised, her raspy voice steady and sure.

Jessica smiled, then turned to me. "And you got great air on that jump today, Michael. You didn't quit when your leg locked up. I'm really proud of you."

"Thanks, Jess," I smiled back, adjusting my belt.

We piled into Aunt Janet's station wagon and made the short drive to Tidewater Tumblers.

The facility was massive. The front half of the warehouse-sized building was dedicated entirely to the gymnastics and dance programs, filled with the constant, echoing sounds of spring floors, chalk-dusted uneven bars, and the heavy thuds of dismounts. Tucked all the way in the back of the building, separated by a set of heavy double doors, was the martial arts dojang.

Because our class didn't start until five o'clock, Rachel and I had a heavily established, three-hour waiting routine.

We walked into the main lobby with the girls, the blast of air conditioning hitting us immediately. Jessica and Janette bypassed the cubbies and walked straight out onto the massive blue floor mats to join their teammate, Sarah, who was already sitting in a straddle stretch near the vault runway.

Rachel and I bypassed the floor entirely and headed for the metal stairs.

We climbed up to the second-floor observation deck, a long stretch of aluminum bleachers that overlooked the entire gymnastics training area. We walked past the clusters of waiting parents and headed straight for the top row, making our way to the absolute furthest right corner of the deck.

It was our designated spot.

I sat down first, sliding all the way over until my left shoulder and back were pressed firmly into the solid, ninety-degree inside corner of the drywall. It provided the perfect physical anchor. Rachel immediately climbed up onto the bleacher next to me and sat on my right.

She didn't hesitate. She tucked her knees up slightly, leaned her weight heavily against my right side, and wrapped her arm around my stomach. I rested my arm over her shoulders, pulling her close into a secure, comfortable cuddle.

Down below, the two o'clock buzzer blared through the facility.

Jessica, Janette, and Sarah lined up with the rest of the competitive team. Jessica looked up toward the observation deck, easily finding us in our top right corner. She waved up at us, a bright, confident smile on her face.

I lifted my free hand and waved back. Rachel wiggled her fingers in a happy greeting against my side.

The floor below erupted into a synchronized flurry of warm-ups and tumbling passes. Rachel rested her head against my shoulder, her breathing steady and relaxed as she watched the gymnasts flip across the spring floor. We had three full hours to pass before the heavy double doors in the back would open for the five o'clock class. But tucked securely into the corner of the wall, wrapped up in the safety of our physical grounding, the wait didn't feel daunting at all. It was just another perfect, routine Thursday afternoon.

We sat in comfortable silence for a long time, the rhythmic thumping of the springboards below acting as a steady, predictable metronome.

Rachel shifted slightly against my ribs. She reached down, her small fingers tracing the heavy, solid brown fabric of the belt tied around her waist.

"Michael?" she whispered, her raspy voice barely carrying over the ambient noise of the gym.

"Yeah?" I murmured, resting my chin lightly on the top of her head.

"Do you think we'll still be doing this when we're grown up?"

I didn't even have to think about it. The Dojang, despite Master Curtis's coldness, was a permanent fixture in our lives. The forms, the structure, the absolute predictability of the movements—it was a system that made perfect sense to us.

"Absolutely," I promised. "We're going to go all the way."

Rachel let out a soft, contented hum. "What do you think we'll look like?"

I closed my eyes, letting the image materialize in my mind. It was a crisp, perfect picture, drawn like a bright panel from a comic book.

"We're going to be tall," I whispered, building the future for her. "We'll still be standing right next to each other, side-by-side in the center of the floor. We'll have brand new *doboks*. Pure, crisp white V-necks, completely plain, without any of the black trim the beginner uniforms have. Just solid, clean white canvas."

"And the patch," I continued. "The Pinnacle Mastery School patch on our chests is going to be perfectly stitched. Navy blue, with the gold tiger and the crossed swords shining under the lights."

"And the belts," Rachel breathed, her voice filled with quiet reverence.

"The belts won't be brown anymore," I agreed. "They'll be midnight blue."

"But everyone calls them black belts," Rachel whispered, her brow furrowing slightly at the technicality.

"They do, just so the parents and regular people understand it," I explained, remembering a lesson Master Ray had taught the advanced class once. "But they're actually midnight blue. True black means an ending. It means you're totally finished and there's nothing left to learn. But midnight blue is the color of the sky right before it gets completely dark. It means the journey never actually ends. We just keep growing."

Rachel squeezed my arm, her imagination syncing perfectly with mine. For a mind that thrived on predictability, a routine that never ended was the ultimate safety net. It meant we would always be together.

"And when we drop into our *Narani Junbi Sogi* ready stance," I continued, "nobody is going to look at us and see a clumsy kid with stiff legs, or a little girl who doesn't talk. They're just going to see two masters."

I opened my eyes and looked down at her. She was staring straight ahead, completely lost in the vision of our adult selves. In her mind, she saw a fierce, unshakeable woman. In my mind, I saw a strong, grounded man whose muscles finally did exactly what his brain commanded. We were a permanent, indestructible team.

"We're going to be black belts," Rachel stated, not as a dream, but as an absolute, geographical destination.

"Yes, we are," I said, tightening my arm around her shoulder.

We settled back into the corner, our eyes tracking Jessica down on the balance beam. As the clock slowly ticked toward our five o'clock class, we sat wrapped in the warm, invincible certainty of a future that belonged entirely to us.
