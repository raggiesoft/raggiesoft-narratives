The large analog clock mounted high above the vault runway clicked to exactly four-thirty.

I nudged Rachel's shoulder gently. She opened her eyes, having spent the last half hour comfortably dozing against my side, anchored securely in our top-right corner of the bleachers. She blinked a few times, looking up at the clock, and a massive, instant smile spread across her face.

It was time.

She immediately untangled her arm from my waist and sat up straight. We both stood up, stretching our stiff legs after three hours of sitting on the hard aluminum benches. Down below, the competitive team was just rotating to the uneven bars, chalk dust hanging heavily in the air under the bright fluorescent lights. We didn't bother waving goodbye to Jessica and Janette—they were deep into their training block and wouldn't be finished until seven o'clock.

We climbed down the metal stairs, our bare feet slapping lightly against the aluminum treads, and headed straight for the hallway restrooms.

It was our standard pre-deployment routine. I went into the boys' room to use the restroom and splash some cold water on my face, fully waking myself up. I checked my reflection in the mirror, pulling the crisp white canvas lapels of my *dobok* straight and giving the knot of my Brown *tti* one final, firm tug to make sure it was perfectly tight.

When I stepped back out into the hallway, Rachel was already waiting for me by the water fountain.

Her uniform was impeccable. Her belt was perfectly tied. She wasn't shrinking into her shoulders, and she wasn't chewing nervously on the inside of her cheek. She was bouncing slightly on the balls of her feet, entirely energized.

We walked together to the very back of the lobby, leaving the loud, echoing sounds of the gymnastics spring floors behind us.

The heavy double doors leading to the martial arts *dojang* were still closed, the standard protocol before a class began. A few other students were already milling around the staging area—a mix of lower-ranked Green and Purple belts, shifting nervously and quietly talking among themselves.

Rachel and I walked right up to the front of the group, claiming our spot near the doors. As Brown Belts, we were some of the highest-ranking students in the five o'clock class, and today, Rachel completely carried the confidence of that rank.

She turned to face me, dropping her feet shoulder-width apart into a casual *Narani Junbi Sogi* stance. She took a deep breath, her chest expanding against the heavy white canvas of her uniform.

"I... am... ready," Rachel rasped quietly, looking up at me, her dark eyes shining with anticipation. She tapped her fingers lightly against her throat. She couldn't wait for Master Curtis to line us up. She couldn't wait for the forms to begin. She was finally going to unleash her *kihap* with the rest of the class.

"You're going to do great," I smiled, stepping up right beside her and squaring my own shoulders toward the closed doors. "We both are."

We stood there in the quiet lobby, perfectly calm and completely focused, waiting for the heavy wooden doors to swing open and our Thursday lesson to begin.
