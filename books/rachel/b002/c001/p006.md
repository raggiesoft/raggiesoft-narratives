The massive dinner of roasted chicken and mashed potatoes was officially cleared from the table, signaling the start of the night's main event. It wasn't just a Friday; we were having a full pack sleepover.

Ashley grabbed her duffel bag from the hallway and ducked into the downstairs bathroom to change into her pajamas.

"Alright, upstairs, let's go," Jessica directed, effortlessly shifting into her role as the pack's logistical commander. "Everyone get changed and grab your blankets."

I followed Jessica, Janette, and Rachel up the wooden stairs. At the landing, we split off. I went straight into my room with the twin bed, stripping off the sweaty clothes from April's garage and pulling on my comfortable sleepwear. Across the hall, the girls filed into Jessica and Rachel's room, where Rachel's designated safe space was the bottom bunk.

When I emerged carrying a precarious stack of pillows and a heavy quilt, the girls were already waiting in the hallway, armed with an absolute avalanche of blankets.

We made our way back downstairs, and the living room was already undergoing a massive, chaotic transformation. The loud, high-pitched whine of electric air pumps completely drowned out the television. Ashley's parents, Jim and Joan—who always seamlessly ran logistics when my parents were on shift —were expertly navigating the floorplan. Jim was inflating one massive queen-sized airbed near the coffee table, while Joan was wrestling a second queen airbed into place near the armchair.

Right on cue, the heavy front door unlocked and swung open.

Mom and Dad walked in, looking exhausted in their work clothes, but their faces instantly broke into huge smiles at the sight of the living room takeover. They were immediately met by a frantic, tail-wagging assault from Baby and Archie, who were practically vibrating with excitement over the sheer number of people in the house.

"Well, it looks like we missed dinner," Dad laughed, dropping his keys into the bowl and giving Archie a heavy scratch behind his good ear.

"There are plates warming in the oven for both of you," Joan promised over the noise of the air pump, waving from the floor.

"You guys are lifesavers," Mom smiled, dropping her purse and pulling Melissa into a quick hug. "Look at this camp you've got set up!"

With the airbeds taking up the center of the carpet for the older girls and Ashley, Rachel and I immediately went to work on our designated station. We grabbed the heavy fabric cushions off the living room sofa and tossed them onto the floor. I grabbed one side of the hidden metal handle under the frame, and Rachel grabbed the other.

With a synchronized, practiced heave, Rachel and I pulled the heavy folding mechanism out, unfolding the mattress into the room.

We threw our quilts over the thin mattress and tossed our pillows against the headboard. It was the absolute perfect setup. Tomorrow, Rachel and I were going to face the terrifying pressure of the 7th Dan Regional Director on that massive spring floor. But tonight, surrounded by the hum of the air pumps, the smell of dinner in the kitchen, and the entire, undivided pack settling into the living room, the anxiety couldn't touch us. The perimeter was perfectly secure.
