Even though the August sun was still casting long, golden shadows across the Raybourns' backyard, our physical and emotional batteries were operating at zero percent. The combination of the brutal Virginia heat and the absolute psychological devastation of the morning had completely drained us.

We headed upstairs to start our wind-down routine, taking turns in the hallway bathroom to get changed, brush our teeth, and use the toilet.

Personally, I didn't care about the concept of privacy anymore. Having been Jessica's primary caretaker after her accident, the human body had long ago become just a logistical reality to me. I had helped lift, bathe, and dress my older sister; I already knew how things worked. But my processor also understood that the neurotypical world *did* care about closed doors and boundaries, so we respectfully maintained the perimeter, taking our individual turns in the bathroom.

Once we were all in our spare pajamas, teeth brushed, and bladders empty, we migrated back into Ashley's bedroom.

Ashley’s bed was a massive, sprawling island of comfort. We climbed in, immediately falling into our standard, non-negotiable geometry. I took the dead center. Rachel curled up flush against my right side, and Ashley settled in tightly on my left. I wrapped my arms around both of them, locking us into our usual three-way cuddle. The heavy, grounding weight of my two favorite women pressing against my ribs was the perfect counter-pressure to the anxiety that had been threatening to crush my chest all day.

As I lay there, staring up at the ceiling of the Raybourn house, a heavy, sinking ache settled in my stomach.

I wished, with every fiber of my being, that we could just move in here. I wished we could permanently trade the volatile, screaming minefield of 1852 Delaney Street for the quiet, predictable safety of 1802 Rich Court. But logic dictated the harsh reality of our situation. We were teenagers, not adults. We couldn't just adopt ourselves out to the neighbors. I knew that after band camp tomorrow, the sanctuary would expire, and Rachel and I would have to pedal our bikes back home and face the music.

It was a terrifying thought, and one we were absolutely dreading. But for tonight, the war couldn't reach us.

I tightened my grip on both of them, taking a deep, shuddering breath of the cool, air-conditioned air.

We gave each other one last, fierce hug in the center of the mattress. I leaned to my right, pressing a quick, exhausted peck onto Rachel's cheek, and then turned to my left, doing the exact same for Ashley.

"Goodnight, Bug," I whispered.

"Night, Bear," she mumbled, her breathing already slowing into the rhythm of sleep.

"Goodnight, Ash. Thank you."

"Always," Ashley whispered back, her arm tightening securely across my chest. "Goodnight, Michael."

I closed my eyes, letting the safety of the perimeter finally pull my exhausted processor into the dark.
