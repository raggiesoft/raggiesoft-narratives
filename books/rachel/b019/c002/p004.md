I pulled the van's gearshift back into drive, my processor completely electrified by the concept of the custom fortress.

"Executing the reroute," I announced, pulling away from the rotting house and continuing down Moosewood Drive.

I took a left onto Mossycup Drive, the heavy van gliding smoothly through the quiet neighborhood. I made another left onto Southern Pines Drive, taking it for exactly one block before arriving at the intersection for Green Meadows Drive.

I paused at the stop sign, my internal map actively calculating the traffic flow. I knew that taking a left onto Green Meadows would plunge us right back into the snarled, chaotic bottleneck of the Independence detour.

"Right turn," I muttered, avoiding the gridlock entirely.

I took Green Meadows Drive in the opposite direction, following it until we hit the traffic light. I executed a clean left turn onto State Route 165—Princess Anne Road. The wide, multi-lane arterial road was completely clear.

We followed the sweeping curve of Princess Anne until we hit the massive intersection, executing a left turn onto SR-414, Lynnhaven Parkway. We cruised up Lynnhaven, taking a right onto SR-411, Rosemont Road. From Rosemont, it was a quick right onto Buckner Boulevard, bypassing the commercial chaos entirely, and finally, a smooth right turn onto Canadian Arch.

I slowed the van, turning left into our driveway at 3701.

The driveway was perfectly sized for exactly two vehicles. I parked the massive, wheelchair-accessible transit van on the right side. Parked securely on the left side was the sleek, reliable sedan Jessica had purchased for Kristin to use. Because the narrow streets of Canadian Arch explicitly forbade curb parking, our two-vehicle fleet maxed out our current footprint.

I killed the engine. The pack didn't wait. We initiated our standard, frictionless extraction. Kristin and Ashley hopped out, keeping Rachel safely flanked between them, while I deployed the hydraulic lift for Jessica.

As we walked up the ADA-graded concrete path to our front door, Rachel laced her fingers tightly through mine, leaning her head against my bicep.

"A custom house, Bear," Rachel rasped, her eyes shining with pure excitement in the glow of the porch light. "Our own fortress."

"Our own fortress, Bug," I agreed, unlocking the deadbolt.
