The steel bucket of the excavator slammed into the front porch of the rotting house.

The sound of the wood splintering and the drywall collapsing was violently loud. The machine chewed through the facade, ripping the roof down and tearing the living room completely open. A massive cloud of gray dust and debris billowed up into the humid air, drifting across the dead lawn.

In the neurotypical world, watching a house get violently shredded by heavy machinery is a chaotic, unsettling spectacle. But standing shoulder-to-shoulder on the pavement, the pack watched the destruction with profound, overwhelming euphoria.

"Look at it go," Ashley whispered, her blue eyes shining as the excavator ripped through a load-bearing wall.

For Ashley, the rotting house wasn't just a building; it was a physical manifestation of the squalor and the trapped, suffocating misery she had endured in the RV with David Lawson. Watching it get effortlessly crushed by the steel bucket was a massive, psychological release. The old world was being permanently deleted.

Kristin stood on my right, her fingers laced firmly through mine. She wasn't watching the house; she was watching us. She saw the heavy, defensive tension bleeding completely out of my shoulders with every crunch of the timber. She gave my hand a deep, grounding squeeze, manually anchoring my nervous system to the present.

"It's loud, Bear," Rachel rasped happily, completely leaning her weight into my side.

"It's the best noise I've ever heard, Bug," I replied, the corners of my mouth turning up into a fierce smile.

We had spent the last two years running from monsters. We had cowered behind locked doors, fled across the country on buses and trains, and relied on legal injunctions just to stay alive. We had spent our entire lives adapting to a world that violently refused to accommodate us.

But as the heavy diesel engine roared and the roof of the foreclosed house completely caved in on itself, the physics of our universe permanently shifted. We weren't running anymore.

Jessica sat in her wheelchair, her hands resting calmly in her lap, watching the dust settle over the wreckage. She had weaponized her intellect, extorted a global tech empire, and funded the absolute annihilation of this property just so her siblings would never have to be afraid of a staircase or a closed door again.

"The debris will be cleared by Monday," Jessica announced to the pack, her voice cutting cleanly through the mechanical roar. She didn't take her eyes off the flattened lot. "The foundation gets poured on Wednesday. The steel tracks for the platform lift and the soundproofing insulation arrive the following week."

She turned her wheelchair slightly, looking up at me, Rachel, Ashley, and Kristin. The brilliant, unmistakable light of a Director who had successfully locked her perimeter shone behind her glasses.

"We break ground on the fortress next week," Jessica promised. "We're going home."
