By 7:30 PM, the heavy, post-dinner exhaustion had officially settled over Grandma Loretta's house. The goodbyes were loud and chaotic, echoing out into the freezing Virginia night as we all spilled out onto the driveway.

Jim, Joan, Melissa, and Ashley piled into their dark blue minivan. Ashley rolled her window down just long enough to shoot me a final, knowing grin and wave before Jim threw the van into gear and pulled out onto the street.

Over by Mom's sedan, the logistical extraction was underway. Jessica executed her pivot-transfer into the front passenger seat, pulling her heavy winter boots inside the cabin. Since Dad was finally here, Rachel and I didn't have to fight the freezing metal alone. Dad effortlessly collapsed the heavy manual wheelchair and hoisted it into the trunk of Mom's car.

"I'll see you guys at home," Dad said, his breath fogging in the cold as he slammed the trunk shut. He zipped up his jacket and jogged over to his truck, ready to follow us back to Delaney Street.

Rachel and I scrambled into the back seat of Mom's sedan. The instant the doors slammed shut, Rachel slid across the upholstery and pressed her side flush against my arm, re-establishing our baseline. Mom turned the heater on full blast and backed out of the driveway.

"Alright, you three," Mom announced from the driver's seat, her eyes catching mine in the rearview mirror. "As soon as we get home, teeth get brushed, and you all need to get straight to bed. Santa is coming tonight."

The car was quiet for exactly two seconds.

Then, sitting in the passenger seat, Jessica let out a sudden, sharp gasp. She whipped her head around to look at me and Rachel in the back seat.

"Wait a minute," Jess said, a massive, thrilled grin breaking across her face. "Mom. We sleep in the living room."

My processor stalled for a microsecond before the architectural math hit me. Since the Black Friday drop, the upstairs bedrooms had been completely abandoned. Our entire universe had migrated to the pull-out sofa bed. That sofa bed was positioned directly in the center of the dark blue polyester carpet, mere feet from the front-wall brick fireplace and the Christmas tree.

We were the literal, physical perimeter of the drop zone.

"We're going to see him!" I gasped, the sheer magnitude of the realization completely overriding my usual need for predictability. "He has to walk right past us! If he comes down the chimney, we're right there!"

Beside me, Rachel's dark eyes went wide with absolute, vibrating excitement. She gripped my arm tightly, bouncing slightly against the seat. We had spent years sneaking out of our upstairs bedrooms to try and catch a glimpse of the jolly old elf, but tonight, he was going to be dropping presents directly into our Base Camp. We had the ultimate tactical advantage.

Mom let out a soft, melodic laugh from the driver's seat, steering the car down the dark, quiet streets.

"I wouldn't hold your breath, guys," Mom smiled, her tone perfectly preserving the holiday magic. "Santa has been doing this for a very long time. He is incredibly stealthy. I promise you, he will be extremely quiet, and he'll be gone before you even notice he was there."

"Not if we stay awake," I countered, the excitement humming under my skin.

We pulled into the driveway of 1852 Delaney Street. Dad's truck hadn't arrived yet, which meant the heavy lifting fell back on the youth guard.

Mom opened the passenger door to help Jessica, leaving the trunk to us. Rachel and I didn't hesitate. We hopped out into the cold, popped the trunk, and grabbed the freezing metal frame of the manual wheelchair. Working in flawless, silent synchronicity, we heaved the bulky chair over the bumper and set it up on the concrete, ready to wheel our sister back into the ultimate Santa stakeout.
