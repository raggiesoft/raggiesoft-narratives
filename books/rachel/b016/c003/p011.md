After the sheer, unadulterated chaos of Craig Bronson’s massive upset over Avalanche, the Rose City Center finally took a collective breath.

The ringside crew finished clearing the remnants of the broken instruments from the stage, and the house lights dipped to a low, moody blue to signal the witching hour. We had reached the top of the broadcast. The main event was imminent.

Without the commentary desk to guide us through the television transition, the live audience relied entirely on the massive TitanTron above the entrance stage. The screen cut to black, and a low, dramatic, synthesized heartbeat began pulsing through the PA system.

Even through our heavy isolation headphones, the deep frequency of the heartbeat vibrated steadily against my collarbone.

LAST WEEK.

The text flashed across the fifty-foot screen. The video package showed Marcus Sterling standing in the ring from the previous Monday. The production truck had deliberately desaturated the color grading, washing out the bright arena lights to make the General Manager look even colder and more clinical.

"Effective immediately, all arena pyrotechnics are banned from AWE programming," Sterling's voice echoed through the arena, perfectly synced to the footage. "Welcome to the bottom line."

The Portland crowd immediately showered the screen in heavy, sustained boos as a rapid-fire montage showed furious fans, intercut with the heavy, echoing slam of a massive steel vault door.

TONIGHT.

The audio track instantly shifted gears, dropping the heartbeat for a heavy, driving, high-BPM rock instrumental. The footage cut to the opening segment we had witnessed just under two hours ago. Sterling was in the ring right here in Portland, soaking in the nuclear heat.

"You wanted the explosions..." Sterling sneered on the tape. "Well, my first official act was to ban the pyrotechnics because you don't deserve them!"

The sound of shattering glass tore through the speakers. Jaxson Vance’s heavy grunge theme hit the video package. The screen showed Vance marching down the ramp, staring daggers right through the corporate executive.

Sitting next to me, Rachel was completely mesmerized by the masterfully edited hype video. She had her foam finger resting in her lap, her eyes glued to the TitanTron, watching the narrative pieces snap together.

"You hide behind Silas Thorne's World Championship to protect your precious bottom line..." Vance growled on the video. "Put Silas Thorne in this ring with me. Tonight."

Sterling was shown laughing nervously, shaking his head.

"If Silas beats me, my team will forfeit at No Quarter, and you can keep your cheap, pathetic pyro ban forever," Vance countered, his taped finger pointing like a loaded gun. "But... when I beat Silas Thorne tonight... the stakes for No Quarter change. If your team loses this Sunday, the pyrotechnic ban is lifted forever."

The production team used stutter-cuts to emphasize the crowd going absolutely ballistic. The camera pushed in agonizingly tight on Sterling’s face, broadcasting the exact moment his corporate composure completely fractured under the pressure of his own massive ego.

"FINE!" Sterling screamed on the tape, looking completely unhinged. "Tonight's main event! Silas Thorne versus Jaxson Vance for the AWE World Championship!"

The massive screen flashed blindingly white.

A gorgeous, high-definition 3D render of the gleaming gold AWE World Heavyweight Championship spun slowly on the screen. A deep, cinematic voiceover boomed over the heavy rock instrumental, vibrating the barricade just inches from our knees.

"TONIGHT. TWO MEN. ONE CHAMPIONSHIP. AND THE FUTURE OF THE RESISTANCE."

The screen split. On the left was Jaxson "The Outlaw" Vance, looking gritty, taped-up, and fiercely determined. On the right was "The Blueprint" Silas Thorne, looking arrogant, immaculate, and furious. In the center, the AWE World Championship spun gracefully above the *No Quarter* logo.

The TitanTron faded to the live camera feed, showing the roaring crowd.

"VANCE! VANCE! VANCE! VANCE!"

The chant started immediately, rolling down from the upper deck and slamming into the ring like a physical wave. Portland was absolutely desperate to see their hometown boy win the gold and bring back the fireworks.

But sitting dead center in the front row, I realized the incredibly strange, paradoxical reality of our situation.

We loved Jaxson Vance. He was the gritty, blue-collar soul of the promotion. But if he won tonight, he would lift the pyrotechnic ban this Sunday in Seattle. He would bring the concussive, terrifying explosions back to the arenas, instantly turning the AWE back into a sensory minefield for Rachel.

To keep Rachel safe, to keep this environment accessible for her... the hometown hero had to lose.

I looked over at Kristin and Jessica. They had come to the exact same realization. Kristin grimaced, while Jessica just offered a wry, helpless smile from her chair. We were sitting in the epicenter of the Resistance, but for the sake of our sister's neurological safety, we had no choice.

We were officially rooting for the corporate villains.
