Marcus Sterling stood in the center of the ring, basking in the absolute hatred of the Portland crowd. He had all the power, and he wanted every single person in the arena to know it.

Suddenly, a heavy, distorted guitar riff shredded through the arena PA system, drowning out Sterling’s arrogant sneer.

The Rose City Center didn't just cheer. The building completely came unglued. The pop was so incredibly massive, so viscerally loud, that I actually felt the steel barricade rattle against my knees.

Stepping out onto the entrance stage was Jaxson "The Outlaw" Vance.

He didn't wear a shiny corporate suit or a flashy, sequined robe. He was wearing taped wrists, worn denim jeans, and a faded leather vest. He was the gritty, blue-collar soul of the AWE, and more importantly for tonight, he was born and raised just ten minutes down Highway 26 in Beaverton. This was his house.

Next to me, Rachel was practically levitating out of her seat, aggressively waving her foam finger as her hometown hero marched down the ramp.

Vance didn't play to the crowd or hype them up. He looked dead-eyed and serious. He climbed the steel steps, stepped through the ropes, and walked right up to the General Manager. Sterling cautiously took a half-step back as Vance raised a microphone to his lips, his chest heaving, his voice raw with authentic emotion.

"You know, Marcus..." Vance growled, his voice cutting through the cheers. "I was sitting in the back, listening to you call my friends and my family out here in Portland 'spoiled children.' You sit up in your corporate suite and you fire good men just so you can save a few bucks to buy another ugly suit."

The crowd roared in agreement. Vance paced a tight circle around the executive.

"You banned the pyro because you're cheap," Vance continued, stopping dead in his tracks and staring Sterling down. "And you hide behind Silas Thorne's World Championship to protect your precious bottom line."

Sterling’s eye twitched again. He quickly motioned for a ringside technician to hand him another microphone. He snatched it through the ropes, his face flushing with anger.

"My team is anchored by 'The Blueprint' Silas Thorne!" Sterling sneered, leaning in close but keeping a safe distance from Vance's fists. "He is genetically superior to you in every conceivable way, Vance. You're just a glorified bar brawler from a suburb that doesn't matter."

Vance smiled—a dangerous, knowing smile that instantly signaled to the 14,000 fans that he had Sterling exactly where he wanted him.

"A glorified bar brawler?" Vance asked quietly, tilting his head. "If you really think your golden boy is unstoppable... prove it. Put Silas Thorne in this ring with me. Tonight. For the AWE World Heavyweight Championship."

The pop was deafening. The idea of getting a World Title match on free television instead of a Sunday Pay-Per-View was the holy grail for a live crowd. Jessica leaned forward in her chair, her jaw dropping, while Kristin grabbed my arm and shook it in sheer excitement.

Sterling let out a high-pitched, nervous laugh, shaking his head. "You're delusional, Vance. Risk my World Champion in a title match six days before a Pay-Per-View? I make the decisions, not you!"

"You're terrified, Marcus," Vance shot back, cutting off his escape route. "You know if I beat your boy, your whole corporate facade crumbles. So I'll make you a deal. Put the World Title on the line tonight. If Silas beats me, my team will forfeit at No Quarter, and you can keep your cheap, pathetic pyro ban forever."

Vance took a heavy step forward, pointing a taped finger firmly into the center of Sterling's chest.

"But... when I beat Silas Thorne tonight... I walk out of Portland as the new AWE World Champion, AND the stakes for No Quarter change. If your team loses this Sunday, the pyrotechnic ban is lifted forever."

It was a strange, paradoxical moment for our section of the front row. We absolutely needed that pyro ban to stay in effect for Rachel’s neurological safety. But trapped in the infectious, chaotic energy of the arena, surrounded by the deafening roar of our hometown crowd, we were completely swept up in the narrative.

The chant began immediately, rolling down from the upper deck and hitting the ring like a battering ram.

"COWARD! COWARD! COWARD! COWARD!"

Kristin, Jessica, and I joined right in, screaming at the top of our lungs. Rachel pumped her giant foam finger to the exact rhythm of the chant, completely lost in the magic of the show.

Sterling's corporate composure completely shattered under the weight of his own massive ego and the thunderous, undeniable taunting of 14,000 fans. His face turned a deep, furious crimson. He raised the microphone, completely unhinged.

"I AM NOT A COWARD!" Sterling screamed, his voice cracking violently through the PA system. "You think you can blackmail me?! You think you can beat The Blueprint?! You want the title?! You want your little sparklers on the line for Sunday?! FINE! You got it!"

The arena floor literally shook as the crowd realized what had just happened.

"Tonight's main event!" Sterling roared over the pandemonium. "Silas Thorne versus Jaxson Vance for the AWE World Championship! And when Silas breaks you in half, the pyro ban is permanent!"

The Rose City Center absolutely exploded. The volume was astronomical, a pure, unadulterated wall of sound.

Jaxson Vance didn't celebrate. He just offered Sterling one last, gritty smirk, dropped his microphone onto the canvas with a heavy thud, and simply walked away.

We had a World Heavyweight Championship main event.
