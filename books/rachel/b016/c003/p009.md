As the ringside crew scrambled down the ramp to sweep up the splintered remains of the bass guitar and drag the broken synthesizer away, the massive TitanTron above the stage flickered.

The live feed cut backstage, but the pristine, high-gloss lighting of Marcus Sterling’s luxury office was entirely gone.

Instead, we were looking at the Resistance locker room. It was dimly lit, concrete-walled, and purely functional. The Portland crowd roared at the sight of their hometown hero, Jaxson "The Outlaw" Vance. He was sitting on a metal folding chair, methodically wrapping his wrists in white athletic tape. He looked intensely focused, staring a hole right through the concrete floor.

Pacing back and forth in front of him like a caged tiger was the grizzled veteran, "Iron" Tommy Brooks. Even on the fifty-foot screen, you could clearly see the heavily scarred tissue around his forehead and eyes—the unmistakable hallmark of a man who had spent twenty years bleeding for the industry.

Deep in the background, sitting cross-legged in the shadows on top of a row of metal lockers, was Fracture. He was completely silent, wearing his tattered mask, slowly and rhythmically whittling a piece of scrap wood with a pocketknife.

"Sterling thinks he's playing chess," Brooks growled, his gravelly, intense voice echoing cleanly through the arena PA system. "He thinks he can just wave his hand and send Avalanche and Carter Finnley down to the ring to steal your World Championship tonight. He thinks he's got the numbers."

Brooks stopped pacing. He knelt down, looking Vance dead in the eyes.

"You listen to me, kid," Brooks said firmly. "You focus on Silas Thorne. You focus on taking that gold and ending this pyro ban for good. If Avalanche or Finnley even *think* about taking a step down that ramp tonight... the Resistance will intercept them. We will tear them apart before they ever reach the barricade. You have my word."

Vance slowly looked up. He ripped the end of the athletic tape with his teeth, spat it out, and gave Brooks a single, determined nod.

Suddenly, a loud crash and off-camera screaming blared through the speakers.

The locker room door on the screen flew open, and a panicked production assistant stuck his head in. "We need help! Out on the loading dock! Avalanche is out of control!"

The TitanTron feed instantly switched to a frantic, handheld tracking shot. The camera operator sprinted down the concrete hallway, following the assistant out to the arena's massive, cavernous loading dock.

It was absolute chaos.

Danny Gibson, a highly popular mid-card babyface, was writhing on the cold concrete floor in pure, unadulterated agony, clutching his right shoulder. Standing directly over him was the 6-foot-9, 320-pound monster known as Avalanche, breathing heavily and looking completely devoid of emotion.

Standing safely behind the giant was the cowardly opportunist, Carter "The Fox" Finnley, laughing and obnoxiously adjusting his designer sunglasses.

Referees and EMTs swarmed the scene, desperately pushing Avalanche back to create space. Gibson was screaming at the top of his lungs that his collarbone was snapped.

Knowing the behind-the-scenes mechanics of the industry, I knew exactly what I was watching. Danny Gibson was suffering from a legitimate, severely torn rotator cuff. This brutal, unannounced backstage assault was the perfect narrative sleight-of-hand to write him off AWE television so he could go undergo real-world surgery. But to the thousands of fans in the Rose City Center, it was a terrifying, unprovoked mauling.

Suddenly, "The Anvil" Craig Bronson kicked open the heavy fire doors. He took one look at his tag-team partner and best friend writhing on the concrete, and he saw red.

Bronson charged straight at Avalanche. Four referees immediately intercepted him, throwing their bodies in front of him and violently shoving him backward to prevent a full-blown brawl right there on the loading dock.

"You want to take cheap shots in the back?!" Bronson screamed over the chaos, pointing furiously at the giant. "You want to break my partner's arm, you cowardly son of a bitch?!"

Carter Finnley stepped out from behind Avalanche, a sickening smirk plastered on his face.

"Take it easy, Anvil," Finnley said smoothly, his voice dripping with condescension. "It's just business. Unfortunately, it looks like your boy Danny can't compete tonight. Which means Avalanche wins via forfeit. See you on Sunday!"

Bronson violently shoved one of the referees aside, stepping right up into Avalanche's massive chest. Avalanche didn't even flinch. He just stared coldly downward at the smaller man.

"There's no forfeit tonight," Bronson snarled, his teeth bared. "If Danny can't go... I'm taking his spot. You and me. Right now. Get out to the ring!"

Bronson turned and stormed down the hallway, heading straight for the entrance curtain. Finnley's smirk instantly vanished. He looked up at Avalanche, clearly nervous about the audible, but the giant simply cracked his massive knuckles, completely unbothered by the challenge.

The TitanTron faded to black.

Without the commentary desk explaining the transition, the raw energy of the arena took over. The crowd was buzzing, furious at the ambush but fully hyped for the impromptu grudge match. We were moments away from watching a 320-pound giant step into the ring, and the World Heavyweight Championship main event was looming right behind it.
