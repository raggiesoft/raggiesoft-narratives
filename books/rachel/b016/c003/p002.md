The TitanTron faded to black. For a split second, the massive arena held its breath.

Then, "In Control" hit the arena PA system. It was a slick, aggressively sterile corporate-rock anthem that belonged in a boardroom, not a wrestling ring. The sheer excitement in the Rose City Center instantly curdled into a physical wall of venomous, localized heat.

The crowd absolutely despised Marcus Sterling. We all did. He was the corporate parasite ruining our favorite show.

Sterling stepped through the entrance curtain, pausing at the top of the ramp. He slowly adjusted his French cuffs with a sickening, calculated arrogance, looking out at the 14,000 screaming Oregonians as if they were a stain on his expensive leather shoes.

As he strolled down the long ramp, the hatred only intensified. Next to me, Kristin and Jessica were mercilessly booing him. Rachel, though entirely non-verbal in this chaotic environment, thrust her hands forward, giving the General Manager two emphatic, highly aggressive thumbs down, her giant foam finger waving in protest.

Sterling stepped into the ring and walked dead center. He raised a microphone to his mouth, but the Portland crowd refused to let him speak. The second the metal touched his lips, the tipping point was reached.

It started somewhere in the upper deck—a low, rhythmic rumble that quickly cascaded down through the lower bowl until all 14,000 fans were united in a single, deafening, hostile chant that shook the television cameras. It was the absolute centerpiece of our collective outrage.

"ASS-HOLE! ASS-HOLE! ASS-HOLE! ASS-HOLE!"

Through my heavy isolation headphones, the chant felt like a physical shockwave vibrating against my chest. Next to me, Kristin and Jessica were enthusiastically joining in, screaming the word right along with the rest of the arena. Rachel, fully understanding the assignment, pumped her foam finger to the exact rhythm of the profanity.

Sterling didn't flinch. He just stood dead center in the ring, holding the microphone at his side, soaking in the absolute venom of 14,000 people calling him exactly what he was. He loved every single second of it.

He slowly brought the microphone back to his lips, a cold, condescending smirk cutting across his face.

"Are you quite finished?" Sterling asked, his voice echoing cleanly through the massive PA system, dripping with disdain.

The crowd responded with another tidal wave of boos.

"Portland, Oregon... Listen to yourselves," Sterling continued, pacing the canvas like a king surveying peasants. "You sound like a bunch of spoiled, ungrateful children."

"WHAT?!" the crowd fired back in absolute unison, executing the legendary, weaponized interruption perfectly.

Sterling paused, his left eye twitching just a fraction. He hated that chant. "You're angry..."

"WHAT?!"

"You bought your tickets tonight expecting a flashy, expensive light show..."

"WHAT?!"

"You wanted the explosions..."

"WHAT?!"

"I said you wanted the explosions!" Sterling barked, his calm facade finally cracking as he pointed an angry finger at the front row. "But let me explain the real world to you mouth-breathers! AWE was bleeding money!"

"WHAT?!"

"Stop saying what!" Sterling roared, his face flushing red as the Rose City Center erupted in mocking laughter. He took a deep breath, smoothing the lapels of his suit to regain his composure. He glared directly at the hard camera, completely unfazed by the vitriol raining down on him.

"I was brought in to get out-of-control costs under control!" Sterling yelled, projecting iron-clad authority. "And my first official act was to ban the pyrotechnics because you don't deserve them! I am trimming the fat! I am protecting the bottom line!"

The boos grew louder, shaking the steel barricade just inches from our knees.

"I don't care if you boo me out of this building!" Sterling sneered over the jeers. "I am building a corporate machine that doesn't need fireworks to succeed! Because I hold the contracts. I hold the power. And quite frankly, I am the only reason any of you people have a show to watch tonight!"
