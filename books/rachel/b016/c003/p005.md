After a quick commercial break for the television audience, the arena lights dimmed once again. The massive TitanTron above the entrance stage flared to life, and the broadcast seamlessly transitioned to a backstage camera feed.

The Portland crowd immediately erupted into a chorus of heavy boos.

The camera opened on a tight, high-definition shot of a massive, professionally lit oil painting of General Manager Marcus Sterling. In the ridiculous portrait, Sterling was standing on a mountain peak, wearing a pristine tuxedo, looking thoughtfully into the distance like a conquering emperor.

The camera slowly panned out to reveal the rest of his luxury backstage office. It was a masterclass in excessive, obnoxious corporate wealth. There was a polished mahogany desk, imported white leather couches, and no fewer than three more framed, high-gloss portraits of Marcus Sterling adorning the walls.

Sterling was standing behind his desk, pouring himself a glass of sparkling water. He looked slightly flushed from his confrontation in the ring, but he was trying desperately to project an aura of total, unbothered calm.

BANG.

The audio of the heavy oak door being violently kicked open echoed through the arena PA system.

"The Blueprint" Silas Thorne stormed into the frame, and the crowd noise shifted from boos to an excited, mocking roar. The AWE World Heavyweight Champion was usually the picture of arrogant perfection, but right now, he looked absolutely unhinged. He was wearing his immaculate, tailored three-piece suit, but his tie was pulled loose, his hair was uncharacteristically messy, and he was clutching the massive gold World Championship belt to his chest like a life preserver in a hurricane.

"Are you out of your mind?!" Silas screamed, the veins bulging visibly in his neck on the fifty-foot screen. "Are you completely, medically insane?!"

Sterling winced, holding up a manicured hand. "Silas. Lower your voice. You're going to wrinkle the Italian wool."

"I don't care about the suit! I care about this!" Silas roared, slamming his free hand onto the mahogany desk. He shoved the World Championship belt directly into Sterling's face. "I am the AWE World Heavyweight Champion! I am the face of your franchise! And you just put my title on the line against a deranged, taped-up brawler just because he hurt your feelings in front of a bunch of lumberjacks!"

Next to me, Kristin let out a loud, genuine laugh. Silas was completely losing his grip, and it was incredible television.

On the screen, Sterling smoothly pushed the heavy gold belt away and took a calm sip of his water. He leaned back against his desk, projecting total corporate arrogance.

"Silas, please. Sit down," Sterling said, his voice dripping with condescension. "You are overreacting. I didn't gamble anything. I made a strategic corporate maneuver. Vance thinks he trapped me, but all he did was walk right into a buzzsaw."

"A buzzsaw?!" Silas yelled, frantically pacing the length of the lavish office. "Have you listened to that crowd out there?! Vance is a madman, Marcus! And we're twenty minutes from his hometown! If he hits me with that lariat, he takes my title, he lifts your pyrotechnic ban, and our entire team looks weak going into No Quarter!"

Sterling just chuckled. It was a cold, infuriating sound.

"Do you really think I'd leave our most valuable asset unprotected?" Sterling asked, walking over and placing a reassuring hand on the champion's expensive shoulder. "Do you really think I'm going to let some blue-collar hoodlum walk out of Portland with my World Championship?"

The entire arena leaned in.

"You aren't going out there alone tonight, Silas," Sterling promised, a wicked smile spreading across his face. "I've already spoken to Avalanche and Carter Finnley. They are going to be stationed at ringside for the entire main event. If Vance even breathes in a way I don't like, Avalanche is going to tear his head off while the referee is distracted."

The Rose City Center instantly erupted into a deafening wall of heat. It was blatant, cowardly cheating, perfectly designed to make us hate the corporate faction even more.

Sterling patted Silas on the shoulder and walked back to his desk, carefully adjusting one of the framed photos of himself.

"So go get your gear on. Stretch. Relax," Sterling ordered smoothly. "Tonight is going to be a piece of cake. We embarrass Vance in his own backyard, we keep the title, we secure the pyro ban, and we ride that momentum straight through Sunday."

Sterling sat down in his plush leather chair, completely satisfied with his own brilliance.

But the backstage camera didn't stay on the General Manager. It slowly zoomed in, lingering tightly on Silas Thorne's face.

Despite Sterling's iron-clad promises of outside interference, the champion did not look reassured. The fifty-foot high-definition screen broadcasted his absolute dread to the entire arena. Silas stared down at the gold championship belt in his hands. He swallowed hard, his eyes darting back and forth as his mind calculated the risk.

The sheer panic was painted vividly across his features. The Blueprint knew exactly what 14,000 fans in Portland already knew: Marcus Sterling had completely lost control of the situation, and Jaxson Vance was coming to collect.
