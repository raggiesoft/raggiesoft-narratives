The Rose City Center was completely unglued. The visual of Jaxson Vance holding the AWE World Heavyweight Championship high above his head sent the Portland crowd into an absolute frenzy.

Down in the front row, the sheer volume was physically staggering. Rachel and I immediately reached up, clamping our hands tightly over the hard plastic shells of our isolation headphones, pressing them firmly against our ears to seal out -the concussive, deafening wave of noise. Despite the intense sensory spike, we were both beaming, completely caught up in the pure, localized ecstasy of the title change.

Inside the ring, Vance knew his window to celebrate was incredibly short. He pointed to the Portland crowd, kissed the gold center plate of the championship, and rolled quickly under the bottom rope.

But he didn't go up the entrance ramp. He knew exactly what was waiting for him back there. Instead, Vance hopped the heavy steel barricade on the far side of the arena, holding the belt high as he disappeared into a sea of grabbing, cheering fans, safely escaping through the concourse.

Inside the ring, Silas Thorne slowly rolled onto his stomach. He was groggy, clutching his forehead exactly where the heavy gold plates of the championship belt had struck him.

He pushed himself up to his knees and looked around the ring. His waist was empty. He looked toward the entrance ramp. Nothing. Finally, his eyes tracked to the far side of the arena, where the tail end of Vance's chaotic exit through the crowd was still visible.

The realization hit him like a freight train. He lost the title. He lost the pyro ban.

Silas Thorne completely snapped.

His face contorted into a mask of pure, unhinged rage. He spun around and saw Senior Official Jack Miller slowly trying to stand up in the corner. Thorne charged. He grabbed the twenty-five-year veteran official by the collar and violently hurled him through the middle ropes, sending the referee crashing to the floor.

Thorne slid out of the ring, his eyes wild. He marched directly toward the broadcast desk.

Even without hearing the commentary, we could see the panic. Cal Stone scrambled backward out of his chair, retreating into the crowd for his life. But Dallas Ward wasn't fast enough. Thorne reached across the desk, grabbed the legendary play-by-play announcer by the lapels of his suit jacket, and effortlessly hurled him completely over the broadcast table. Dallas crashed into a heap of cables and heavy monitors with a sickening thud.

But Thorne wasn't finished.

He grabbed Dallas Ward's heavy, steel-framed broadcast chair and hurled it violently against the steel ring post.

CLANG!

He marched down the ringside perimeter, breathing heavily, the veins visibly bulging in his neck. He grabbed a folded steel chair from the timekeeper's area and threw it with blinding speed against the steel barricade, directly in front of our section.

CRASH!

For us, the sensory experience was terrifyingly real. The unamplified, metallic screech of the steel chairs hitting the barricade and the concrete floor cut right through the ambient crowd noise. Instinctively, I squared my shoulders, planting my boots into the floor and standing firm as a physical buffer between the flying hardware and Jessica’s wheelchair. I didn't move an inch in the face of Thorne’s violent tantrum, and Jessica just watched calmly, completely trusting the anchor.

Thorne kicked the heavy steel ring steps apart, sending the massive top half crashing to the floor. He stood amidst the absolute wreckage of the ringside area, his chest heaving, his silver-and-black gear stained with sweat.

Slowly, he turned his head toward the entrance stage.

Standing halfway up the long ramp was General Manager Marcus Sterling.

Sterling’s expensive suit was completely disheveled. He was backing away slowly, his hands raised in a defensive posture, looking absolutely terrified of the monster he had just unleashed by booking this match.

Thorne locked eyes with his boss. It was a look of pure, unadulterated murder. The former champion pointed a single, trembling finger squarely at Sterling, silently and intensely blaming the executive for the catastrophic decision to put the belt on the line.

Sterling stopped retreating. He realized his corporate empire was completely crumbling on live television. Desperate to assert whatever shred of authority he had left, Sterling snatched a microphone from a terrified production assistant near the stage.

He raised the mic. His hands were visibly shaking.

"You are out of control, Thorne!" Sterling yelled, his voice cracking violently as it echoed through the arena. "You have one chance to fix this! If you cost Team Sterling the match this Sunday... YOU ARE SUSPENDED INDEFINITELY!"

Thorne's eyes widened at the threat. He took a slow, menacing step toward the ramp as the crowd roared at the massive ultimatum.

Sterling dropped the microphone, turned, and sprinted through the curtain, fleeing his own champion.

The digital clock above the stage hit 7:59 PM.

Silas Thorne stood completely alone at ringside. He was breathing like a caged animal, surrounded by the physical destruction he had just caused, staring a hole through the empty entrance stage.

Above him, the massive TitanTron flashed a final, ominous graphic: *AWE NO QUARTER – LIVE THIS SUNDAY ON PAY-PER-VIEW.*

The arena plunged into total darkness. The broadcast was over.
