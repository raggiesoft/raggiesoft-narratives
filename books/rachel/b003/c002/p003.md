The grandfather clock in the hallway chimed noon, cutting through the sweeping orchestral finale of *The Coral Kingdom*.

Right on cue, the heavy wooden door to the kitchen opened. Uncle Jim stepped into the living room, followed closely by Grandma Loretta.

The relaxed, movie-watching atmosphere instantly vanished. The pack tensed. It was time for the midday bathroom protocol, and for the very first time since the assault, Dad wasn't the one executing it.

"Alright, base camp," Jim said, his voice incredibly calm and steady. He walked over to the edge of the sofa bed, rolling up the sleeves of his flannel shirt. "Twelve o'clock. Let's get this done so you guys can have lunch."

Rachel’s hand instantly clamped down on mine over the pillow wall. Her breathing hitched, her chest rising and falling in quick, shallow increments.

Jim saw it immediately. He knew exactly what was causing her panic. He was a grown, adult male stepping into her physical space, preparing to put his hands on a body that had just been shattered by another grown, adult male.

Jim didn't rush. He didn't try to dismiss her fear. He stopped moving and looked directly at me.

"Michael, I'm going to take you first," Jim stated, making a highly calculated, deliberate choice. He wanted to demonstrate to Rachel that he knew exactly what he was doing before he ever laid a finger on her.

"Okay," I swallowed hard, bracing myself.

Jim stepped up to the right side of the mattress. "I'm coming under your shoulders and your knees," he announced, telegraphing his exact movements. "Keep your stomach completely limp. Let me take the weight."

I closed my eyes and let my body go slack against the medical wedge. Jim slid one strong arm beneath my knees and the other behind my shoulder blades. With a smooth, controlled motion, he lifted me off the bed, pulling my back flush against his chest to lock my spine in place.

It wasn't the exact same massive, rumbling embrace as Dad, but the mechanics were completely flawless. My core didn't engage. The torn fascia didn't pull. Jim carried me in a rigid plank down the short hallway to the half-bath, gently setting me on my feet and stepping out to give me privacy. Five minutes later, he reversed the process, carrying me back and lowering me gently onto my wedge.

"Good job, buddy," Jim murmured, stepping back.

He took a slow breath, then walked around the foot of the bed to the left side.

Rachel was shaking. She had her eyes squeezed tightly shut, her free hand gripping the edge of her quilt. She trusted Uncle Jim—she had known him her entire life—but the trauma response hardwired into her nervous system was completely overriding her logic. A man was looming over her.

Jim didn't stand over her. He immediately dropped down to one knee, putting his face perfectly level with hers so he wasn't looking down at her.

"Rachel," Jim said, his voice a low, incredibly gentle rumble.

She opened her eyes, tears already pooling in the corners.

"I know you're scared," Jim told her with absolute honesty. He didn't tell her it was fine. He acknowledged the terror. "I know this is the last thing you want right now. But I promise you, I am not going to let your core bend. I am going to keep you perfectly flat, and I am going to be as fast as I can."

Jim stayed on one knee, keeping his hands resting on his own thighs. He was waiting for consent.

Rachel stared at him for a long, agonizing moment. She looked at me over the pillow wall. I squeezed her hand twice. *He's safe, Bug. He's pack.*

Rachel took a shaky breath. She didn't speak, but she reached out and tapped the back of my hand with her index finger.

"She's ready," I whispered for her.

"Okay. Here we go," Jim said gently. He stood up, but he kept his movements agonizingly slow. "Arm under the knees. Arm under the shoulders."

When his hands slid under her, Rachel flinched violently, but Jim held perfectly steady. He didn't jerk or overcorrect. He lifted her with extreme care, locking her small, fragile frame against his chest.

Rachel kept her eyes squeezed shut the entire time, her jaw clamped tight to keep from crying out, but Jim moved with military precision. He carried her to the half-bath, waited outside the door, and carried her back.

When he finally lowered her back onto the mattress and slid his arms out from under her, Rachel let out a massive, shuddering exhale. The tension drained out of her body, leaving her completely exhausted.

Jim stepped back, letting out a quiet breath of his own. He looked at Jessica and Ashley. "You girls are up. Five minutes, then Grandma is bringing out lunch."

As the girls took their turns in the bathroom, Jim gave my shoulder a quick, reassuring squeeze before heading back into the kitchen. The first hurdle of the day was cleared. The base camp had successfully adapted to the new logistics.
