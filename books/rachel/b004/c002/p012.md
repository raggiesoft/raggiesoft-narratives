We walked down the exit ramp of *The Shadow Wolf*, our legs slightly wobbly from the intense, swinging drops, but our adrenaline was absolutely peaking. The post-storm energy of the park was incredible. The oppressive humidity was completely gone, leaving behind a cool, vibrant spring afternoon.

"Okay," I said, looking at the park map in my head. "We are officially out of Germany. Next stop is the Italian sector."

"Which means it's time for the screaming contest," Ashley grinned, cracking her knuckles in preparation.

Bonnie looked intrigued, her hand still comfortably locked in mine. "The screaming contest?"

"You'll see," I promised.

As we wove through the crowded cobblestone pathways toward the Italian sector, a massive, synchronized roar echoed over the trees. The deafening sound of fifty people shrieking at the absolute top of their lungs rolled through the plaza, followed by a brief, heavy pause, and then an answering roar from a different group of people.

Bonnie’s head snapped up, her dark eyes widening with immediate, genuine interest as she tracked the noise. The sheer volume of the ongoing contests bleeding through the park was the perfect advertisement.

We followed the sound until we reached our destination: *The Siege Engine*.

It was a massive, imposing pendulum ride designed to look like a giant, heavily reinforced wooden ship. Two massive metal arms suspended the ship in the air, swinging it back and forth in a massive, terrifying arc. It didn't complete a full 360-degree loop, but it swung just high enough to make you feel like the entire ship was about to go completely vertical, leaving you entirely weightless at the apex.

Because the storm had passed and the park was fully operational again, the line for *The Siege Engine* was packed. This was exactly what we wanted. The ride wasn't just about the physical drop; it was a highly competitive, massive group activity.

We waited in the queue until the metal air gates finally opened.

The seating arrangement on *The Siege Engine* was bench-style, with each row holding exactly five people. As we stepped into our row, Ashley immediately orchestrated the geometry.

She let a random teenager from another middle school walk into the row first, taking the far-left seat. Then, Ashley stepped in. I followed, taking the absolute dead center of the bench, and Bonnie slid in right next to me. A random tourist took the final seat on the far right, perfectly boxing our trio into the middle.

We sat down on the hard fiberglass bench. A few seconds later, a single, massive, hydraulic lap bar lowered from above, clicking firmly into place across all five of our waists at the exact same time.

"Alright, Bonnie, here are the rules," Ashley said, leaning forward to look past me. "This ride is a battle. Half the ship faces forward, half the ship faces backward. When the pendulum swings and *our* side goes up to the sky, you throw your hands up and scream as loud as humanly possible."

"We have to drown out the other half of the ship," I added, feeling the familiar, competitive thrill of the ride. "It is a point of absolute local pride. We do not lose the screaming contest."

Bonnie’s eyes lit up with a fiercely competitive spark. "Oh, you are so on."

A loud siren blared, and the massive mechanical drive tires beneath the ship engaged. The pendulum gave a heavy lurch, swinging backward to gain momentum, and then swept forward.

The first few swings were gentle, just building up the kinetic energy. But with every pass, the arc got wider and higher. The wind whipped through my hair, the G-forces pressing us heavily into the bench at the bottom of the arc.

Then, we hit the maximum height.

Our side of the ship swung violently upward, pointing almost straight at the Virginia sky. For two glorious, terrifying seconds, the heavy gravity completely vanished. We were entirely weightless, hovering in the air before the pendulum inevitably dragged us back down.

*"NOW!"* Ashley shrieked.

We threw our hands high into the air, and our entire side of the ship erupted. It was a massive, deafening, chaotic roar of fifty people screaming at the absolute top of their lungs. I yelled until my throat vibrated, completely lost in the sheer, unadulterated joy of the moment.

The ship plummeted backward, the G-forces slamming us back down into our seats, and the other half of the ship took their turn, their screams echoing across the Italian plaza as they hit their own apex.

But as we swung back up for our second massive arc, my processor registered a subtle shift in the physical data.

At the absolute peak of the swing, completely suspended in weightlessness, Bonnie didn't just throw both of her hands in the air. She kept her left hand raised to the sky, but she reached her right hand down and grabbed mine over the lap bar.

She squeezed my fingers tightly as we plummeted back down to the earth, screaming and laughing at the exact same time.

Sitting in the exact center of the bench, the math of my existence felt absolutely flawless. On my left, my honorary sister was yelling insults at the other half of the ship, fiercely defending our pack's honor in the screaming contest. On my right, my new girlfriend was holding my hand, sharing the exact same hyper-fixated thrill of the rollercoasters that I loved.

The Base Camp perimeter hadn't been breached by romance. It had successfully expanded to protect it. For the first time in my life, the ghost of the Dojang was completely silent, drowned out entirely by the triumphant, deafening roar of *The Siege Engine*.
