We stepped out of the exit queue for *The Timber Mill Plunge*, our clothes dripping wet from the final drop, and immediately looked up.

The sweltering, oppressive Virginia morning sun was gone. Over the last thirty minutes, the sky had bruised into a deep, heavy charcoal. The thick humidity had suddenly dropped by ten degrees, replaced by a sudden, cool breeze that carried the distinct, metallic scent of ozone.

"We were going to head next door to *The Frost Wraith*," Ashley noted, looking at the towering, inverted white tracks of the massive coaster looming over the trees. She narrowed her eyes at the sky, her tactical radar pinging. "But I think we need to call an audible. Those clouds are moving fast. We need to get to the *Bavarian Grand Hall*."

"Lunch," Bonnie agreed, shivering slightly as the cool breeze hit her wet t-shirt. "Good idea."

We didn't run immediately, but our pace definitely quickened. We marched out of the 'New France' section, crossing the wooden bridge over the water and weaving quickly through the cobblestone streets of 'Rhinefield'. By the time the massive, festive archways of the 'Bavarian Village' came into view, the first heavy, fat drops of water began to smack against the hot pavement.

The crowd around us began to scatter. As we passed the outdoor arcade games, the sky completely gave way.

"Run!" Ashley shouted.

We broke into a full sprint, laughing as the torrential downpour instantly soaked whatever the log flume had missed. We raced across the courtyard and threw our weight against the massive, heavy wooden doors of the *Bavarian Grand Hall*.

The second the doors slammed shut behind us, a blinding flash of lightning illuminated the stained glass windows, followed instantly by a massive, rattling boom of thunder.

We had made it inside just in time.

Stepping into the Grand Hall was a massive, instantaneous sensory relief. The blast of the industrial air conditioning cut through the muggy dampness of the rain. The cavernous, echoing dining room was alive with the sound of a live brass band playing on the center stage, and the air was thick with the incredible, mouth-watering smell of roasted chicken, smoked sausage, and hot pretzels.

We grabbed our plastic food trays and shuffled through the cafeteria-style line, loading up on heavy, warm food to combat the chill of the storm.

We found an empty spot at one of the long, communal wooden picnic tables near the front of the stage. Ashley immediately slid onto the wooden bench, and I sat down squarely next to her. It was our standard, non-negotiable geometry: Ashley acting as my honorary sibling anchor on my left side to shield me from the chaotic noise of the massive dining hall.

Bonnie set her tray down on the bench directly across the table from us.

She looked down at her plate of fries, then frowned. "Oh, we forgot condiments. I'm going to grab extra napkins and ketchup. Be right back."

The absolute second Bonnie disappeared into the crowd, Ashley struck.

She dropped her fork, leaned across the table, and kicked my sneaker hard under the wood.

"She totally likes you," Ashley grinned, her blue eyes sparkling with absolute, unfiltered excitement.

My chest seized. I flushed a dark, panicked red, my processor immediately locking up. The sudden influx of direct social data was blinding. "Shut up, Ash. No she doesn't."

"Michael, I am literally a girl. I know exactly how we look at people," Ashley countered, her tone dripping with absolute, undeniable confidence.

She leaned back in her seat, her expression softening from an excited middle-schooler into the fierce, protective loyalty of a best friend. She looked directly at me, officially dropping the massive secret she had been carrying since yesterday afternoon, stripping away all the confusing social static to give my autistic brain the clear, factual translation it desperately needed.

"And you like her back," Ashley stated. "That hug in the band room this morning? The way she held your hand on Heart Attack Hill? The way you pulled her in on the log flume? That wasn't a pack cuddle, Michael. That was a romantic cuddle. I saw it."

I swallowed hard. Hearing the data spoken out loud hit me like a physical shockwave. *That* was why my heart had raced. *That* was why the touch felt so entirely different. It wasn't just a survival anchor; it was a spark. I wasn't just experiencing sensory overload—I was experiencing my first real crush.

"But..." I stammered, my brain desperately trying to protect the foundational safety net of our Base Camp. "But our trio..."

"The trio is untouchable," Ashley promised softly, understanding my exact fear. "I already talked to her about it. That's why I was blushing yesterday at your house. I told her you're my brother, and I gave her my absolute blessing. She respects our boundaries, Michael. She isn't trying to replace me or Rachel. She just wants to be with you."

Armed with the exact vocabulary I needed to understand my own feelings, the panic completely dissolved into a profound, overwhelming clarity. The math finally balanced. The perimeter wasn't breaking; it was expanding.

Ashley spotted Bonnie walking back through the tables holding a handful of ketchup packets.

Moving with flawless, tactical wingman precision, Ashley picked up her food tray. She stepped completely over the wooden picnic bench and took the seat on the opposite side of the table.

When Bonnie arrived, she paused, looking at the rearranged seating. The only available space left was the empty spot directly next to me.

Bonnie caught Ashley's eye. Ashley just gave her a massive, encouraging smile and nodded toward the bench.

Bonnie’s cheeks flushed a beautiful, brilliant pink. She sat down directly on my right side, sliding her tray close to mine. Underneath the table, entirely hidden from the rest of the Grand Hall, she reached her hand out. She didn't grab my wrist. She just slid her palm softly against mine, her fingers interlacing perfectly with my own.

This time, I didn't need to ask Ashley for a translation, and I didn't panic at the new physical data.

I just smiled, lightly tracing my thumb over her knuckles, and held her hand right back. The storm was raging outside, but inside the Grand Hall, my reality had completely shifted. We were officially a couple.
