The violent, torrential downpour outside the stained-glass windows of the *Bavarian Grand Hall* eventually exhausted itself, slowing to a light, misty drizzle. The live brass band finished their set, and the massive crowd of teenagers sheltering inside the dining hall began to slowly filter back out into the park.

We threw our trash away and pushed through the heavy wooden doors.

The physical atmosphere of the park had completely transformed. The suffocating, sweltering humidity of the morning was entirely gone, replaced by a cool, crisp breeze and the distinct, nostalgic smell of rain evaporating off hot asphalt.

My internal atmosphere had completely transformed, too.

I was walking out of the dining hall holding Bonnie's hand. It wasn't the rigid, utilitarian grip I used to pull Rachel through a crowd; our fingers were loosely, comfortably interlaced. It felt entirely new, but my processor wasn't throwing any error codes. Thanks to Ashley’s translation at the picnic table, the unmapped data had been successfully categorized.

As we stepped onto the wet cobblestones of the Bavarian Village, Ashley suddenly stopped and turned around, physically blocking our path.

She planted her hands on her hips, looking incredibly pleased with herself.

"Alright," Ashley announced, her blue eyes shining with absolute triumph. "Cards on the table. No more secrets. Michael, I wasn't just guessing in there. I actually pulled Bonnie aside on Thursday after band class."

I blinked, momentarily surprised. "You did?"

"I did," Ashley confirmed, nodding. "Because you are completely blind to social cues, and Bonnie was too terrified of stepping on my toes to actually make a move. I needed to explicitly clear the perimeter for her."

Ashley looked at Bonnie, sharing a quick, conspiratorial smile with her, before her expression softened into that fierce, deeply loyal warmth she always carried for our pack. She looked directly at me.

"Michael," Ashley said gently. "I know I yelled at Julia on Heart Attack Hill that I was your sister, but I realized I've never actually said it directly to *you*. I don't just see you as my best friend. You are my brother. Period."

I stared at her, the sheer weight of the declaration hitting my processor.

"We shared the Base Camp," Ashley continued, laying the facts out with perfect, logical clarity so my brain could lock them in permanently. "I've seen you at your absolute worst, and you've seen me at mine. You're my family. That means the physical way we interact—the holding hands, the deep-pressure hugs—is a survival baseline. It's a tether. It will never be a romance. I told Bonnie exactly how our math works. I told her the perimeter was safe to expand, and I gave her my complete, absolute blessing to pursue you."

Hearing the tactical breakdown of how Ashley had actively protected our pack, while explicitly defining our bond as permanent, chosen family, was the ultimate proof of her loyalty. It completely cleared away any lingering, subconscious anxiety I had about the geometry of our trio changing.

"Now," Ashley grinned, taking a step back and gesturing between the two of us. "Hug your girlfriend. Properly this time."

*Girlfriend.* Hearing the word spoken out loud sent a massive, electric jolt straight through my nervous system. It was an entirely new operating parameter. I let go of Bonnie’s hand and turned to face her.

Bonnie’s cheeks were flushed a beautiful, brilliant pink, but she wasn't hiding it anymore. She looked up at me, a soft, radiant smile breaking across her face.

I stepped into her space and wrapped my arms around her.

This time, I didn't apply the heavy, rib-crushing compression designed to manually reset a system crash. I didn't brace my weight against her like a physical shield. It was a proper, romantic embrace. I wrapped my arms gently but securely around her waist, and Bonnie immediately slid her arms up around my neck, pressing her body softly against mine.

It was warm, it was close, and it was completely devoid of the heavy utility of the Dojang trauma. It was a hug just for the absolute joy of holding her. I buried my face lightly in her dark hair, my heart racing a mile a minute, entirely amazed that this was actually my reality.

"Finally," Ashley muttered playfully from the sidelines. "You guys were making me crazy."

Bonnie laughed, her breath warm against my neck. She gave me one last, tight squeeze before slowly stepping back, her dark eyes shining.

"Alright, enough being cute," Ashley declared, clapping her hands together to re-initiate our momentum. "The storm is officially far enough away. The rides are going to start cycling again. It is time for *The Shadow Wolf*."

I instantly grinned. *The Shadow Wolf* was the absolute crown jewel of the Bavarian Village. It was a massive, suspended swinging coaster that tore through a mock-German village before taking a terrifying, plunging drop over the river.

We practically jogged the rest of the way to the ride's entrance. Because the storm had just passed, the queue lines were entirely deserted. We walked straight through the winding wooden queue house, right up to the loading platform.

The ride operators were currently sending empty red trains out onto the track to clear the rainwater off the rails.

"Ten minutes, guys!" one of the operators called out to us, wiping down the control panel. "Just have to finish cycling the test trains!"

We happily leaned against the wooden railings of the queue, the cool breeze blowing through the station. Bonnie stood right next to me, our arms brushing. We didn't need to hold hands the entire time; the baseline had been established. The silent, undeniable proximity between us was all the data I needed.

Ten minutes later, the air gates hissed open.

Because the suspended cars swung freely from the track above, they were designed to hold four people each—two to a row. Ashley, operating with flawless wingman efficiency, immediately climbed into the back row of the very front car, claiming the space all to herself. Bonnie and I climbed into the front row together, pulling down the heavy, black over-the-shoulder restraints.

"Clear!" the ride operator shouted, checking our harnesses and giving the control booth a thumbs-up.

As the floor of the station mechanically dropped away, leaving our feet dangling in the open air, the operator leaned into the microphone to deliver the legendary, in-universe dispatch line.

"Enjoy your ride," his voice echoed over the station speakers as the train lurched forward out of the station. "*At the speed of fright!*"
