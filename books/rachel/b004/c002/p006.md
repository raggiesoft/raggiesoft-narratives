The morning sun was rapidly baking the asphalt, transforming the Virginia humidity into a thick, unseasonably sweltering sauna. We left the yellow tracks of *The Loch Serpent* behind and started the long trek down the steep, paved hill from the 'Heatherdowns' hamlet toward 'Hastings.'

Halfway down the hill, we spotted Sloane. He was standing near a lemonade cart, puffing out his chest and desperately trying to impress a group of eighth-grade girls wearing band jackets from a different middle school. He was talking loudly, trying to project the aura of a rebellious tough guy, but the physics simply weren't on his side. Being an eighth grader trapped in a fifth-grader's physical stature made the entire display look ridiculous. The girls clearly saw through the act, completely ignoring him as they walked away to get their drinks.

We paid him no mind either, taking the junction midway down the hill and turning the corner to head deep into the medieval-themed village of Hastings.

Our target was a massive, fortress-like building housing *The Iron Aeronaut*. As we predicted, the line spilling out of the entrance was agonizingly long.

"An hour wait, at least," Ashley estimated, looking at the queue winding through the outdoor switchbacks. "Everyone has the exact same idea."

Because both the queue line and the actual ride were completely enclosed indoors, it was the ultimate refuge from the brutal heat. We happily joined the back of the line, slowly inching our way inside until the heavy wooden doors closed behind us, instantly hitting us with a massive, glorious blast of industrial air conditioning.

As we stood in the dimly lit, steampunk-themed hallway, slowly shuffling forward, Ashley turned to me.

She pulled me into a standard, deep-pressure pack hug, wrapping her arms securely around my ribs. But as she leaned her head against my shoulder, I could feel her grinning.

"I cannot wait for school to be over," Ashley whispered, keeping her voice just loud enough for Bonnie to hear. "We are going to use our summer passes for Brewster Gardens, and then we're going to spend every single weekend at *Tidal Springs*."

I instantly perked up. *Tidal Springs* was the massive water park just a few miles down the highway in Williamsburg. The thought of floating in the wave pool and riding the tube slides with the pack was the ultimate summer motivation.

Ashley released her grip and stepped back, her blue eyes shining with that encrypted, buzzing secret again.

Before I could say anything about the water park, Bonnie stepped into my space.

She didn't ask; she just reached out and pulled me into a hug of her own. Once again, my processor completely stalled as the data shifted. It wasn't Ashley's heavy, rib-crushing compression. Bonnie's arms wrapped gently around my waist. She pressed her cheek lightly against my chest, her proximity radiating a soft, electric heat that completely overrode the chill of the air conditioning. Over her shoulder, I saw Ashley and Bonnie exchange a rapid, knowing smirk.

The two women were absolutely sharing a secret, and they were actively coordinating their movements around it. I still didn't have the vocabulary to translate it, but as I hugged Bonnie back, lightly resting my chin near her dark hair, I realized something undeniable: this new, terrifying, unmapped physical touch felt entirely *right*.

"When we get off this ride, we need to find ice water," Bonnie murmured, stepping back from the hug with a brilliant blush coloring her cheeks. "It is dangerously hot out there today."

"Agreed," I nodded, my heart still hammering against my ribs. "Hydration is priority one."

We spent the next forty-five minutes shuffling through the cool, winding corridors until we finally reached the pre-show room. A ride operator was assigning groups to stand on specific numbers painted on the carpet in front of the heavy automated doors.

Because *The Iron Aeronaut* was a massive motion simulator, the seating wasn't divided into individual cars. It was a theater filled with long, bench-style rows that strapped dozens of people in together.

Ashley immediately took charge of our geometry.

"Bonnie, stand on the far side of the number," Ashley directed, physically steering her into place. Then, Ashley pointed to the floor right next to her. "Michael, you're in the middle. I'll take the end."

She locked me into the absolute center of the perimeter again.

The pre-show video played, explaining the lore of the steampunk flying ship, and then the heavy doors hissed open. We filed into the massive, hydraulic theater, sliding into the third row. I sat down, the comfortable bench pressing against my back, with Ashley anchoring my left and Bonnie securing my right. We pulled the heavy lap bar down until it clicked snugly across our waists.

"I've never actually been on a motion simulator before," Bonnie admitted, gripping the lap bar as the lights in the cabin began to dim. "They didn't have these back home."

"You're going to love it," I promised, the familiar thrill of the ride kicking in.

The massive screen in front of us lit up, and the entire theater violently lurched upward as the hydraulic pistons engaged. The floor dropped out from under us, perfectly syncing with the footage of the flying ship diving off a cliff. We plummeted, soared, and banked hard to the left and right. Bonnie laughed out loud, her shoulder pressing heavily against mine every time the theater tilted, completely immersed in the chaotic, synchronized illusion of flight.
