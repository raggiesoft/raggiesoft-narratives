By the time I finished meticulously crafting the digital avatar of myself in *The Denizens 2* and embedding the screenshots into the *Vertex Creative Studio* lore document, the adrenaline that had been keeping my processor online finally flatlined.

The heavy, residual heat from the hydrotherapy pool, combined with the massive influx of calories from the room service feast, hit the pack like a localized gravity well.

"Command center offline," Jessica announced from the sitting area, her voice thick with exhaustion as she closed the lid of her corporate laptop. "We have a 6:00 AM wake-up call tomorrow. Transit math requires us to be fully rested before we march to the Rose City Center for the broadcast."

I saved the HTML architecture, backed up the neighborhood files, and powered down the massive 17-inch rig. The fans spun down, leaving the hotel room in a heavy, insulated silence.

The physical geometry of the room needed to be configured for sleep.

We had two queen beds. Because Jessica required a specific perimeter and physical clearance to safely transfer from her wheelchair in the middle of the night, she claimed the bed closest to the bathroom. That left the second queen bed, situated near the heavy blackout curtains of the window, for the rest of the pack.

The zero-privacy baseline we had established earlier that afternoon was about to hit its absolute, logical conclusion. We were simply too exhausted to dig through our duffel bags to change out of our comfortable loungewear and into designated sleepwear.

I stood beside the bed, Rachel directly in front of me.

"No armor," Rachel rasped softly, her dark eyes completely clear of the frantic separation anxiety that had plagued her since Thursday.

"No armor," I agreed.

We stripped off our heavy flannel and sleep shirts, dropping them onto the carpet. Kristin stood up from the edge of the mattress and did the exact same thing. We didn't stop at our shirts. We stripped down to our bare skin.

For two years, my biological defense mechanism had been violently conditioned to equate female proximity and physical vulnerability with absolute, predatory danger. Delores and Brandi had turned the female body into a weapon of degradation. Stripping away all of my physical armor in a room with three women should have sent my trauma-wired processor into a catastrophic, blinding Redline.

But as I stood there in the soft, dim light of the hotel lamps, my threat-detection system remained entirely dormant. There was no panic. There was only my older sister, my fiercely loyal Bug, and the sentinel who had sworn to protect us. The weaponization of the female body had been completely and permanently dismantled.

Rachel climbed into the bed first, taking the side nearest the window. I climbed in next, taking the direct center of the mattress. Rachel immediately plastered herself against my right side, throwing her bare leg over mine and wrapping her arm securely across my chest. Skin-to-skin contact provided the ultimate, undeniable sensory data that her Bear was real and present.

Then, the mattress shifted on my left.

Kristin climbed under the heavy hotel comforter. She didn't stay on the far edge. She slid right up against my left side, her bare shoulder pressing flush against mine, her arm resting casually across my stomach to complete the physical perimeter.

I was literally sandwiched in the center of the bed, entirely boxed in by absolute safety.

Jessica clicked off the main room lights, leaving only the soft glow of the digital clock on the nightstand.

For a few minutes, the only sound in the room was the quiet hum of the air conditioner. But then, in the darkness, I felt Rachel's chest begin to shake against my ribs.

A quiet, highly amused giggle escaped her lips.

"What's funny?" I whispered, my voice heavy with sleep.

"I was just thinking," Rachel whispered back, her voice dancing with vindictive joy. "If Delores could somehow magically see through that door right now... her head would literally detach from her neck and explode."

A low, rumbling chuckle started deep in my chest.

Delores’s paranoia regarding my family wasn't just toxic; it was clinically insane. She couldn't comprehend a pure, fiercely protective, platonic bond. She had spent two years projecting the most vile, deeply twisted accusations onto us. She genuinely believed—and routinely screamed at me—that I was engaged in wildly inappropriate relationships with Jessica, Rachel, and even our cousins Janette, Maria, and Katherine.

"She seriously thought you were sleeping with your cousins?" Kristin asked from my left, her voice a mix of disgust and sheer disbelief.

"Oh, absolutely," Jessica chimed in from the other bed in the dark. "She thought our entire family was just a giant, incestuous threat to her territory. She couldn't handle the fact that Michael's primary anchors were women who actually loved him."

"So," Rachel giggled, tightening her arm across my chest. "If she saw her hostage sleeping completely naked, wrapped up in the arms of his little sister..."

"She would burn the entire hotel down," I laughed, the absurdity of the monster's delusions finally stripped of all their power.

"Wait," Kristin said, shifting slightly against my left side. I could hear the sharp, intelligent smirk in her voice. "It's even worse than that. Because Delores knows I'm not related to you."

Rachel gasped in the dark, a fresh wave of laughter hitting her. "Oh my god. You're right."

"Exactly," Kristin continued, lightly tapping her fingers against my ribs. "I'm the roommate. I'm not blood. Technically speaking, as two unrelated consenting adults currently sharing a bed with zero clothes on, we *could* actually be doing exactly what Delores was terrified of."

The absolute absurdity of the situation hit all of us at once. I was lying naked between my little sister and her gorgeous, fiercely loyal brunette best friend. The logistics were perfectly aligned for Delores's ultimate nightmare.

"We *could*," I noted, my processor recognizing the hilarious biological variable. "But I think my muscles are too exhausted to even lift my arm right now."

"Same," Kristin laughed softly, resting her head heavy against the pillow right next to mine. "The sentinel is officially off-duty. No adult activities tonight. Just spiteful, glorious sleep."

"Good," Rachel murmured happily, her breathing already starting to slow as she buried her face into the crook of my neck. "Delores is a joke. We won."

We laid there in the dark, the laughter slowly fading into the heavy, comfortable silence of the pack. I closed my eyes, surrounded by the physical warmth of the women who had saved my life, and for the first time in two years, I drifted to sleep without a single trace of fear.
