The absolute silence of the Crown Regency hotel room was a luxurious contrast to the chaotic roar of the ARTS train. Rachel was curled against my side on the queen bed, her breathing slow and steady as her overloaded processor finally found equilibrium.

Jessica sat at the small table near the window, flipping open the heavy, leather-bound hotel directory.

"This is exactly why we booked this specific grid," Jessica announced, tapping the glossy map in the binder. "The Rose City Center is less than a five-minute walk from the lobby. When the wrestling broadcast ends tomorrow night, the CTS platforms are going to be an absolute nightmare. We are just going to walk right past the mob, cross the plaza, and lock ourselves back in this room."

It was a flawless logistical maneuver. But for this afternoon, there was a different priority.

"We need to handle your physical maintenance," Jessica continued, looking over at me. "Carrying me down those train aisles and dealing with the transit transfers has your muscles locked into granite. The directory says there is a premium indoor pool and an oversized hydrotherapy hot tub on the second floor."

A sudden, defiant spark ignited in my chest.

For two years, Delores had absolutely despised swimming. She hated the water, and by extension, she forbade me from going anywhere near it. It was just another arbitrary, suffocating rule designed to police my joy and restrict my physical autonomy.

"Let's go," I said, my voice firm. "All of us."

Rachel sat up immediately, nodding in agreement.

I stood up from the bed, walking over to my duffel bag. The physical geometry of the room instantly shifted into the zero-privacy baseline.

I didn't take my clothes into the bathroom, and neither did anyone else. We stood in the open space between the beds. Rachel pulled her flannel shirt over her head, her dark eyes locked onto mine. I stripped off my heavy button-down at the exact same time, maintaining the absolute, unbroken visual tether.

Kristin stood near the desk. She had shared the cramped bed with us the night before, but this was the first time she was actively participating in the pack's daylight exposure. She saw Rachel's lingering need for constant visual verification that her brother wasn't disappearing. And she fully understood the violent, irredeemable trauma Delores had inflicted to weaponize the female body against me.

Kristin didn't turn her back. She stood tall, looked me in the eye with quiet, unyielding solidarity, and pulled her shirt over her head, completely normalizing the environment.

We suited up. Rachel pulled on a sleek, athletic one-piece swimsuit. Jessica opted for a practical tankini—a tank top swim shirt paired with bikini bottoms—while Kristin wore a true two-piece bikini. I pulled on the brand-new swimming trunks and a protective swim shirt Kristin had helped me pick out at Franklin's Market.

To maintain a presentable, modest perimeter while navigating the civilian hallways, the three women pulled lightweight athletic shorts and loose undershirts over their swimwear. We all strapped on neoprene water shoes and grabbed thick, white towels from the hotel bathroom rack.

Ten minutes later, we stepped off the elevator and pushed through the heavy glass doors onto the hotel's Lido Deck.

The humid, chlorine-scented air hit us instantly. The massive, blue-tiled pool stretched across the room. A large sign bolted to the wall outlined the parameters: *NO LIFEGUARD ON DUTY. SWIM AT YOUR OWN RISK. NO SPLASHING. NO DIVING.* The water was a shallow three feet deep for most of its reasonable length, sloping gently down to five feet at the far end.

We dropped our towels and over-shirts on a cluster of lounge chairs in a defensible corner. Keeping our water shoes securely on our feet, we approached the edge.

Mounted to the pool deck was a mechanical transfer chair. Jessica maneuvered her wheelchair alongside it, shifting her weight onto the plastic seat. With a slow, mechanical hum, the chair lowered her into the three-foot shallow end. Rachel, Kristin, and I slipped into the warm water right beside her.

As the water reached Jessica's chest, the physics of her reality fundamentally changed.

The buoyancy of the water completely neutralized the gravity that normally bound her to her chair. She grabbed my shoulders, her arms trembling slightly, and for the first time since the Atlanta high school band trip in the spring of 2000, my older sister pushed herself upright and stood entirely on her own two feet.

Tears immediately pricked my eyes. Jessica wrapped her arms around my neck, and I hugged her back fiercely, both of us standing shoulder-to-shoulder in the water. It was a profound, overwhelmingly beautiful moment of reclaimed freedom. She let go of me and turned, pulling Rachel into a tight, standing embrace, and then reached out to hug Kristin, holding her best friend while completely upright.

We stayed in the pool for twenty minutes, letting Jessica experience the weightless autonomy, before the heavy ache in my lower spine reminded me of the primary objective.

"Hot tub time," I murmured, wincing slightly as I shifted.

Getting Jessica out of the pool required a bit of a struggle. The mechanical chair didn't drop quite deep enough into the water for her to easily swim onto the seat, forcing me to brace my legs and muscle her upward until she was secure.

We walked over to the oversized, in-ground hydrotherapy hot tub. We slipped off our water shoes, leaving them at the edge. But as we approached the tub's transfer chair, a laminated out-of-order sign hung over the controls, explaining that a replacement part was on order and staff were aware.

"I've got you," I told Jessica. I crouched down, hooking my arms securely under hers, and physically lifted her over the tiled lip, carefully lowering her onto the submerged bench.

The hot tub was three-and-a-half feet deep, with wide benches built directly into the sides. I climbed in, sinking into the boiling water with a long, ragged groan as the high-pressure jets instantly began to melt the granite-like tension out of my spastic muscles.

The seating geometry settled naturally into pairs. I sat on one bench with Rachel plastered firmly against my side. Jessica sat directly across from us next to Kristin.

Rachel and I shared a badly needed, heavy cuddle. She rested her head on my shoulder, sighing as the deep heat regulated her processor. But as the minutes ticked by, the absolute security of the hotel grid began to work its magic. Her separation anxiety, which had been burning since Thursday, slowly ticked down from *Managed* to *Maybe*.

She sat up, looking across the bubbling water at Jessica. Rachel wanted to test her own baseline.

"Swap," Rachel rasped softly, nudging my arm.

I waded across the hot tub, trading places with Kristin. I sat down next to Jessica. My older sister beamed, immediately pulling me into a tight, affectionate cuddle, resting her head against mine. She was so happy to have her brother by her side, the hot water doing absolute wonders for the physical toll we had both endured over the last forty-eight hours.

A few minutes later, the rotation shifted again. I slid over to the next bench, sitting directly next to Kristin.

She didn't hesitate. Kristin reached out and pulled me into a fierce, tight cuddle. I rested my head against her shoulder and upper chest, completely trusting her physical perimeter. Despite the fact that she was wearing a bikini, my threat-detection system remained entirely dormant. There was no fear, no flashback to the assault. There was only the absolute, protective warmth of a sentinel who had proven her loyalty.

Across the hot tub, Rachel watched us and suddenly let out a bright, echoing giggle.

I looked up, confused. "What?"

"I was just thinking about Delores," Rachel smiled, her eyes dancing with vindictive amusement.

I instantly understood the joke. Delores had hated the fact that my family was entirely female. She despised that my favorite thing to do was cuddle with my two sisters, constantly trying to pathologize our closeness to isolate me. Yet, with massive, blinding hypocrisy, Delores never had a single problem demanding unlimited time and physical affection with her own little brother, Tony.

"Imagine if she could see you right now," Rachel continued, grinning. "Sitting in a boiling hot tub, fully relaxed, cuddling with a gorgeous brunette in a bikini. She would absolutely explode."

Kristin let out a sharp laugh, her arm tightening comfortably around my back. She looked across the water at Rachel, and then tilted her head to look up at me.

"You know what?" Kristin said, her voice dropping into a tone of absolute, iron-clad conviction. "If we ever cross paths with Delores in the civilian world... I am officially volunteering for the role."

I blinked, my processor stalling for a second. "What role?"

"The girlfriend," Kristin stated smoothly, a fierce, protective light burning behind her intelligent eyes. "If she ever tries to come near you, I will happily pretend to be your girlfriend, Michael. I will throw myself right between you and that monster, I will hang off your arm, and we will watch her absolutely burn to the ground with jealousy."

The words hit me like a physical weight. I didn't have to guess what she was thinking or try to read her eyes. She was laying her loyalty out in plain English. She was offering herself as a human shield—not just to protect me physically, but to actively dismantle my abuser's ego.

*A spark.* It wasn't just trust anymore. It was a profound, cementing bond.

"I'd actually pay to see that," Jessica chuckled from the opposite bench, leaning back into the jets.

"It's a deal," I told Kristin softly, resting my head back against her shoulder.

By 4:00 PM, the intense heat of the water had fully exhausted our recovering bodies.

We climbed out of the tub. I physically hauled Jessica over the lip and carefully transferred her back into her wheelchair. We dried off, pulled our athletic shorts and undershirts back on to maintain our modest hallway perimeter, and took the elevator back up to the third floor.

The hydrotherapy had worked perfectly, but it had entirely drained our fuel reserves.

"We need calories," Jessica announced the second the heavy wooden door of our room clicked locked. She tossed the leather-bound room service menu onto the desk. "Michael. Order whatever the pack wants. Do not look at the prices."

I smiled, picking up the hotel phone. The muscles in my back felt brand new, the ghost of Kent was washing away, and it was time for a feast.
