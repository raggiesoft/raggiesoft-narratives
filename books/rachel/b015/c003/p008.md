(Back to Michael’s POV)

I was profoundly, overwhelmingly grateful that both of my sisters didn’t mind one bit that I just needed to completely fall apart in the entryway. After the heavy, suffocating trauma of the last forty-eight hours, letting the dam break while Rachel held me together was the only way my processor could reboot.

Eventually, the tears slowed. We peeled ourselves off the floor, but Rachel flat-out refused to detach. She shifted her grip, linking her arm fiercely around my waist and pressing her shoulder flush against my ribs. She was acting as a physical tether, absolutely terrified that if she let me out of her grasp for even a single second, I would disappear again.

We shuffled together, side-by-side, back into Jessica’s bedroom, where my brand-new command center was quietly humming on the desk.

Rachel stopped in her tracks, her dark eyes going wide as she took in the massive, 17-inch piano-black monolith. The sleek, translucent glass window borders of Quantum OS Clarity Ultimate glowed brilliantly on the high-definition widescreen.

"Whoa," Kristin breathed, dropping the canvas overnight bags on the floor. "Michael, what is that?"

"I bought him a real gaming rig," Jessica stated proudly, wheeling into the room behind us. She looked at Rachel and Kristin, her expression softening with a fierce, protective edge. "It was originally meant to be his Christmas present. I already had the hardware budgeted, and I pulled strings at Quantum weeks ago to secure the RTM enterprise disc for Clarity Ultimate. But after Delores threw him out and left him for dead in the freezing rain at four o'clock this morning... I made an executive decision. I decided we weren't waiting for December. He needed a win today."

"It's an absolute beast," I rasped, clicking the mouse to show Rachel the fluid, lightning-fast interface of the new operating system. "It runs everything effortlessly."

"I love it," Rachel smiled. She didn't let go of my waist, simply reaching out with her free hand to gently trace the edge of the screen.

"Let me go make a quick dinner," Jess announced, shifting back into her pragmatic logistical mode. She spun her wheelchair toward the door. "I'll start some sandwiches."

Rachel turned to me, her eyes filled with absolute empathy. Overwhelmed by the relief of finally having me back, she shifted her grip from my waist, reaching up to wrap both of her arms fully around my upper back, pulling me into a solid, two-armed hug to officially welcome me back to the pack.

The moment her arm wrapped around my upper back and applied pressure, a sudden, blinding spike of white-hot agony tore through my muscles.

I screamed out in pain, my knees buckling violently as I ripped myself backward out of her grip.

"What’s wrong?!" Rachel gasped, her hands flying up to her mouth in pure panic.

"Michael!" Kristin shouted, lunging forward to catch my arm before I hit the floor.

From the hallway, the rubber tires of Jessica's wheelchair squeaked loudly against the linoleum. She hadn't even started dinner yet. She wheeled back into the bedroom at maximum speed, her eyes darting across the room to assess the threat. "What happened?!"

I was gasping for air, clutching my chest, the phantom ghost of Kent rushing back into the room. My hands were shaking uncontrollably. I reached behind me, grabbing the hem of the clean Quantum t-shirt Jessica had given me, and slowly pulled it up over my shoulders.

I heard Rachel suck in a sharp, horrified breath.

Right across my upper back, stark and violent against my pale skin, was a massive, dark red welt. It was the undeniable physical scar of the agonizing, coercive torture I had endured the night before. When Delores and Brandi had cornered me, sleep-deprived and trapped in that room, they hadn't just manipulated my mind; Delores had struck me hard, physically enforcing her absolute dominance to keep me compliant while they used me.

"Oh my god," Kristin whispered, covering her mouth.

Jessica stared at the welt, her face hardening into a mask of pure, glacial fury. The pragmatic Quantum engineer was entirely gone.

"I think this settles it," Jessica declared, her voice dropping into a cold, absolute decree. She looked at Rachel and Kristin, establishing the perimeter. "Tonight, I want all of us to climb into my bed. Tonight, I do not want you alone for a single second."

Rachel immediately stepped back into my space. She didn't hesitate, but her movements were entirely recalibrated. She carefully slid her arms under mine, wrapping them securely around my lower ribs, completely avoiding my upper back while still delivering the deep, grounding pressure I desperately needed. She locked her hands together behind my back and buried her face into my chest, promising silently that she wasn't letting go again.

"She hurt you," Rachel said very gently, her raspy voice vibrating against my sternum, thick with unshed tears. "I wish you had told someone, but it’s over now. You’re here. You are safe in Jessica’s apartment."

I closed my eyes, leaning my weight into my younger sister, profoundly grateful that she understood exactly how to adjust her grip so I could still be held.

"I’m glad I’m not having to sleep alone tonight," I whimpered, the lingering terror of the locked bedroom in Kent still making my skin crawl.

"I understand," Rachel promised fiercely. "Tonight, we will all be with you. And if you ever come down to my apartment in University Place, I will gladly share my bed with you, exactly like we’ve done before in Vancouver. You don’t ever need to ask, Michael. Just know that no matter where you are—here with Jess, or in U.P. with me—we will share our beds. Always."

Ten minutes later, Jessica wheeled back into the bedroom balancing a tray on her lap. She had made grilled cheese—two hot, perfectly toasted sandwiches for each of us—alongside steaming mugs of hot chocolate.

"I figured you would want something warm," Jess said softly, handing me a mug.

"Thank you," I whispered, gripping the warm ceramic with trembling hands. I was still looking over my shoulder, half-expecting Aunt Lynne's front door to materialize in the shadows.

"It’s all right. You’re safe here," Jessica reassured me, reading the persistent terror in my posture.

"We understand you are still scared," Rachel added. She finally let go of my waist just long enough to take her own plate, but she immediately sat shoulder-to-shoulder with me on the edge of the mattress, keeping her leg pressed firmly against mine. "We are here for you."

As we ate our dinner in the quiet sanctuary of the bedroom, Jessica began outlining the tactical parameters for the weekend.

"Tomorrow is a strict decompression day," Jessica instructed. "No long transit rides, and absolutely no stress. I want you to explore the new computer, rest, and get your bearings right here in Renton."

"But we do need to make one trip," Jessica added, looking at the oversized sweatpants I was wearing. "I am going to buy you some decent clothes. You need a completely fresh start. Kristin, do you mind if I use your employee discount at Franklin's Market?"

"That won’t be a problem at all," Kristin confirmed instantly, "as long as I’m the one physically paying at the register."

"Perfect," Jessica nodded, her mind snapping the local transit schedule into place. "There's a massive Franklin's Market right near the Renton Transit Center. We can hop on the Route 105, get you completely outfitted, and come right back to the apartment to decompress."

"Thanks, Jess," I said, the structured, low-stress plan providing a massive anchor for my frayed nerves.

Jessica collected our empty plates and mugs onto her tray. She looked back at the three of us, her eyes heavy with a shared exhaustion. "Now, we all need our sleep. It’s been a very, very rough day for all of us."
