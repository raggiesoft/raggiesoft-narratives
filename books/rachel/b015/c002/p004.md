The heavy TAPS Route 564 bus hissed as the air brakes engaged, pulling into the sprawling concrete expanse of the Overlake Transit Center. Through the tinted window, I immediately noticed a coordinated, intimidating wall of professionally dressed people waiting exactly where our bus was pulling up.

"I don’t leave for twenty minutes," the driver announced over his shoulder as he opened the pneumatic doors.

"We know. We’re intercepting," a woman in a sharp blazer said, stepping right up to the threshold. She scanned the aisle with absolute, laser-focused authority. "My program manager's brother and his girlfriend should be on board."

My stomach dropped. I hopped off the bus, with Delores close behind me. I looked around the crowd of unfamiliar, intense faces. Across the busy street of 156th Avenue NE, the massive, glass-and-steel structures of the Quantum campus loomed like a fortress.

"Where’s my sister?" I asked, my anxiety instantly spiking. "Where’s Jessica?"

"She’s making her way across the street from the campus," the woman in the blazer replied smoothly. "Could I have a private word?"

She didn't wait for permission. She took me by the arm and guided me away from the bus bays, steering me toward a quiet concrete bench on the far edge of the transit plaza.

Delores, her eyes narrowing with sudden, vicious suspicion, immediately moved to follow us.

The woman held up a flat, commanding hand, stopping Delores dead in her tracks. "I need a private word with just Michael." She turned her attention to the rest of the group. "Ellen, could you come here, please? Lucy, Kyle... please ensure Delores stays right here at the shelter."

Delores opened her mouth to shriek a protest, but Lucy and Kyle flanked her instantly. Their polite but unyielding corporate smiles acted as a physical wall, completely boxing her in on the concrete transit platform.

"I am Andromeda, Jessica’s director," the woman introduced herself, turning back to me as we walked out of Delores's earshot. "And this is Ellen. Ellen, would you please describe how you found Jessica, and in what state, this morning?"

Ellen stepped forward, her expression incredibly distressed. "I found her on the TAPS Commuter Rail from Kent. She was sobbing, Michael. Just completely breaking down. She is terrified for you." Ellen reached out, gently touching my arm. She looked me point-blank in the eyes. "Your sister... she’s crying right now. She’s hurting, badly. She’s scared for you."

I completely froze. I didn’t know how to react. The logical part of my brain knew that Jessica never cried. Ever. The fact that she was breaking down in front of her coworkers meant her processor was in absolute crisis. But the heavily conditioned, abused part of my brain immediately flared with defensive anger. I didn’t like that she was broadcasting my life, and I didn't like that she wanted me to dump my girlfriend.

"Michael!" a desperate voice called out.

I looked toward the intersection. The walk signal was flashing, and Jessica was propelling her manual wheelchair across 156th Avenue NE at maximum speed, her dark hair flying back.

She hit the curb cut, rolled onto the transit plaza, and slammed the brakes just a few feet away. It broke my heart into a thousand pieces. She was sobbing badly, her face pale and her eyes red-rimmed.

She didn't say a word. She rolled the final few feet, and grabbed me, pulling me down into a desperate, bone-crushing hug right there on the pavement.

"I’m going to be blunt, and I am getting straight to the damn point," Jessica choked out, her voice trembling but laced with absolute, razor-sharp steel. "Why the hell do you stay with her? I need a straight answer, Michael, especially after what I saw last night."

I couldn’t believe this was happening. We were standing out in the open at a major transit hub, surrounded by her senior team. "Jess, what—" I stammered, unable to keep a straight face, my gaze darting nervously toward the ground.

Jessica noticed my evasion immediately.

My sister reached up, grabbing my chin with a firm, uncompromising grip, and physically forced my head up so I had to look her directly in the tear-filled eyes.

"Look at me!" Jessica barked, the tears streaming down her face. "Why do you stay with her? She’s hurting you! I want you to come to Renton with me tonight!"

She continued sobbing, her shoulders shaking violently. I just couldn’t bear seeing my big sister this upset. The cognitive dissonance was tearing me apart.

"Jess, why are you acting like this?!" I asked, my voice rising in defensive, terrified frustration.

*SMACK.*

The sound of her hand striking my cheek echoed sharply over the cold concrete.

I stumbled back a half-step, my hand flying to my face in pure shock. My jaw dropped. Never, in my entire life, had either of my sisters slapped me. It completely short-circuited my brain.

"Don’t you get it?!" Jessica cried out, her voice cracking with pure, desperate agony. "She’s treating you like gutter trash! Leave her and come to Renton!"

I got angry. The sting on my cheek flared, but before I could even formulate a shout, Jessica lunged forward, grabbing my jacket and pulling me right back into a fierce, suffocating hug.

"I love you," Jessica wept into my chest, her pragmatism entirely shattered. "I hate seeing your life being destroyed in front of my eyes. I’m sorry I slapped you. I am so sorry. But I hope it knocked some sense into you. Please understand, Michael... I have been crying like this ever since I got on the train in Kent this morning."

I stood there, the anger bleeding out of me, replaced by a hollow, terrifying realization of how deep I was buried in Delores's control.

"I’m out here now, and I can hear you better, Mom!" a harsh, screeching voice echoed across the transit plaza.

I looked back. Delores had finally broken through Kyle and Lucy's perimeter at the bus shelter and was marching toward us. She was holding her cell phone to her ear, glaring daggers at the Quantum team. "You want to fly me home?!" Delores demanded loudly into the receiver, clearly putting on a show.

As she closed the distance, Andromeda stepped directly in front of my sister’s wheelchair, acting as a physical shield.

"That's close enough," Andromeda stated, her voice dropping to a glacial, executive freeze. She pointed back toward the bus bays. "You are not permitted to harass my team. Back off."

Delores stopped, sneering at Andromeda, but she didn't step any closer.

Across the street, Trevor was jogging through the crosswalk from the main Quantum building. He was carrying a small plastic deli basket from one of the campus cafeterias.

"Here," Jessica said, her breathing finally starting to level out as she wiped her face. She took the basket from Trevor and handed it directly to me. "I had them make lunch. A BLT with no tomato, a big deli pickle, a bag of chips, and a cold soda. You look like you haven’t eaten in ages. Please, eat."

I took the basket, my hands shaking violently. I was so exhausted I was practically hollowing out. I took a massive bite of the sandwich right there on the concrete path, my scrambled brain just desperately focusing on the calories.

Delores crossed her arms, staring viciously at my food. She scoffed, her posture radiating a dark, demanding energy. "What, I get nothing? I know how these rich tech companies work. You people get all your meals for free. Go back inside and get me a plate."

Trevor stopped dead in his tracks.

"Free?" Trevor repeated, his polite, professional demeanor instantly snapping into a hardened, icy glare. "I just paid twelve dollars out of my own wallet to make sure he had a hot meal."

Every single one of Jessica’s coworkers turned to look at her. The collective, unyielding perimeter of the Quantum team locked into place.

"You get nothing," Andromeda stated with absolute finality.

My stomach violently dropped, the BLT suddenly turning to ash in my mouth.

To Jessica’s team, they were bravely standing up to a bully. But my threat-detection system instantly redlined into sheer, blinding terror. They didn't understand the physics of my reality. Every single time someone stood up to Delores in public, she absorbed the humiliation, bottled it up, and unleashed it on me the absolute second the front door closed. They were actively arming a bomb that I was going to be locked in a room with.

Delores’s face flushed a violent, toxic red. She didn't yell at them. She marched over to me, grabbed my arm with a bruising, punitive grip, and practically dragged me away from my sister.

"We're leaving," she hissed.

A few minutes later, as we stood shivering near the kiss-and-ride loop, a battered sedan pulled aggressively up to the curb. It was Delores’s aunt.

"It’s time to go," Aunt Lynne insisted through the rolled-down window. "Get in the car, now!"

Jessica and her team were still standing a few yards away by the transit benches. I could see the horror on their faces, but my processor was completely consumed by the impending punishment. I didn't see an extraction team; I just saw the people who had accidentally signed my death warrant for the evening.

"That does it…" was the last thing I heard Trevor say before the sedan doors slammed shut, and we sped off toward Interstate 405.

The ride back to Kent was a suffocating, terrifying silence. When we finally arrived back at the squalid house, the true nature of the trap sprang shut.

"We’re going to have Mac & Cheese and Cheese Dogs for dinner," Aunt Lynne explained casually, dropping her keys on the counter. She turned to me, a sickening, conspiratorial smile on her face. "Delores, you, and Brandi will have the whole upstairs to yourselves tonight. I’m going to sleep on the couch."

I froze, the blood draining from my face.

Aunt Lynne looked at her niece. "Delores, I know you’ve been aching to have sex these past two days... so why not all three of you have some naughty fun upstairs. Just try not to be too loud. Brandi is going to join in, and she planned on staying up all night so you three can have all the fun in the world."

My stomach violently revolted. It wasn't an invitation; it was a coordinated, coercive ambush.

As we headed up the steep, narrow stairs after dinner, my mind was racing. I started thinking about Jessica’s offer. I thought about the safety of her apartment in Renton, and the desperate, protective tears in her eyes.

But that fleeting hope all went away the second I walked into the cramped bedroom.

I saw both girls, completely naked, waiting for me. The door clicked shut behind me, sealing me inside.

My heart hammered against my ribs like a trapped bird. I backed up against the wall, staring at the floor. I then started thinking to myself in a panic, *I’ll go along with this to survive the night, but I'm terrified. I'm not going to enjoy it. I'm just trapped.*

As the oppressive, horrific reality of the room closed in on me, a single, devastating thought echoed in my mind: *Now I wish I had listened to my sister.*
