With that, Jessica had me help her drag the inflated air mattress all the way to the opposite end of the room, putting as much physical distance between us and Delores as the walls would allow. We pushed it right up against the closet doors, firmly establishing our localized perimeter.

Jessica reached into her canvas bag, pulling out a stack of clean blankets and a thick comforter, spreading them meticulously over the plastic.

"I think it’s supposed to snow overnight and dip into the teens," I said, my voice trembling slightly as I looked down at the thin air mattress resting on the filthy carpet. "Do you think this will be enough?"

"Yes," Jess replied smoothly, patting the comforter to smooth out a wrinkle. "Worked just fine during the snowstorm up in Vancouver."

I crawled under the blankets, remaining fully clothed in my heavy sweatpants and t-shirt, and lay down beside my sister.

I felt violently torn. My processor was redlining, caught in a massive, agonizing tug-of-war. A part of my brain—the part that had been ruthlessly conditioned by months of psychological manipulation—was screaming that I was in terrible danger, and that Delores was going to severely punish me for this tomorrow. But the other part of my brain... the part that I had buried deep down under layers of fear... felt so much happier lying here, safely hugging my older sister instead of the woman across the room.

I rested there at Jessica’s side, though my skin was crawling with unease. I knew Delores was lying in her birthday suit just ten feet away, radiating a dark, venomous anger in the dark.

"You’re welcome to be with me for Christmas," Jess whispered softly, her arm wrapping protectively around my shoulders, offering an immediate escape hatch. "You don't have to stay here."

As she spoke the words, the digital clock radio on the nightstand—which had been set with a low-volume sleep timer—transitioned to the next track. The soft, hauntingly isolated piano notes of *Stille Nacht* drifted into the freezing, tense room.

It was an arrangement built to be a winter lullaby, a melody promising that all was calm, and begging for sleep in heavenly peace. But lying there, the tension vibrating in my jaw, peace was a completely fictional concept.

And then, the arrangement on the radio shifted.

The piano notes suddenly climbed into a higher, more delicate octave, carrying a fragile, mournful weight. I looked up, staring into the shadows of the ceiling. *This feels different,* my brain registered, the sudden shift in the audio frequency bypassing my exhausted defenses.

A few seconds later, a massive, melancholic swell of synthesized strings rose up behind the piano, filling the quiet bedroom with a sweeping, cinematic ache.

That was it. I mentally lost it.

The dam I had been desperately holding back for months completely shattered. A hot, silent tear slipped down my cheek, followed immediately by a ragged, suppressed sob that shook my entire frame. I clamped my hand hard over my mouth, utterly terrified that Delores would hear me break.

Jess instantly sensed the crash.

She shifted her weight, rolling slightly so her back acted as a physical barricade, completely blocking my face from Delores's line of sight. Without missing a beat, my sister reached up and pulled our thick comforter completely over our heads, creating a dark, sound-dampening micro-perimeter under the heavy blankets.

Across the room, Delores still had her own covers pulled violently over her head, stewing in her toxic anger, while the swelling strings of the radio acted as an ambient mask between us.

Jessica pressed her lips directly against my ear, her voice barely a breath. "Hey. Don’t cry," she whispered, holding me tighter, her tone breaking with a fierce, desperate love. "It’s entirely up to you... but please, Michael. Please come with me to Renton. I’m so worried about you."

"Can we sleep for now," I sobbed quietly, burying my face deep into her shoulder so the fabric of her shirt would muffle my breakdown. "Though to be honest, I’m not sure if I’m able to, since I’m so uneasy."

Realizing the absolute depth of my terror, and knowing that words weren't enough to override my panic, my sister pulled me completely flush against her beneath the blankets.

"I understand how you’re feeling," Jessica breathed, her voice a steady, unbreakable anchor trapped perfectly inside our localized perimeter. "I’m uneasy too. For now, let’s just hug and try to sleep."

She held me tightly, her steady heartbeat providing the deep-pressure rhythm my autistic nervous system desperately needed to calm down.

As the orchestral track neared its end, the music shifted one last time. Faintly, mixing into the final fading chords, the rhythmic, cheerful jingle of sleigh bells began to play.

Wait.

My tear-soaked brain processed the festive sound, the auditory data instantly linking to childhood memories of Santa Claus. In my mind's eye, I pictured the sleigh flying high above the freezing Washington sky. But the image wasn't joyful; it was devastating. I pictured Santa flying right over this squalid house, passing by completely without stopping, because I was trapped inside this toxic filth with Delores.

*I'm going to spend Christmas with this absolute SLOB?*

The thought flared hot and sudden in my chest, snapping the grief into a sharp, desperate panic. *I want Christmas with my sisters!*

As the final notes of the song faded into the winter night, a profound, terrifying new calculation began to run in my processor. I started trying to figure out exactly how to get out of Delores's clutches.

I clung to Jessica in the dark. She was holding me, completely unaware of the massive, structural shift taking place inside my head. Just like I couldn't read her mind, she couldn't read mine. She didn't know that my internal monologue was silently begging her to get me out of this mess and take me to her home. To her, I was just a terrified hostage needing comfort.

The monster was sleeping on the other side of the room, but the gears of my escape were finally turning, and for tonight, my perimeter was secure.
