"Yeah, we both do at home," I muttered nervously. "But I figured she was going to remain dressed out of respect, since you’re my sister. I know I planned on remaining dressed. I remember that Thanksgiving..."

"Michael," she whispered, her pragmatic brain catching a massive logistical error. "When you brought the gear up earlier, we missed a piece of the payload. I left my fourth bag—the heavy canvas one—downstairs by my folded chair."

I nodded, dreading the descent. "I'll go get it."

I slipped out the bedroom door, bracing myself as I faced the steep, narrow staircase. I meticulously navigated my way down, keeping my eyes peeled for the cat feces I had actively dodged while carrying her up earlier. The living room was quiet; Aunt Lynne and Brandi were completely absorbed in the glowing television screen, ignoring my presence. I grabbed Jessica's fourth bag from the foyer, keeping my head down to avoid engaging with the hostile perimeter, and hauled the dead weight back up the miserable flight of stairs.

"I don’t mind sharing with you, Michael," Jessica stated firmly, her voice raising just enough to ensure Delores heard her, officially establishing her protective shield over the dismal twin bed against the wall. "I don’t know how you two are able to share that tiny mattress."

Jessica pulled the pump out, inspecting the small toggle switch on the side. It was a dual-power unit, specifically engineered to run off internal batteries or shore power. My stomach violently dropped. It was a suffocating, coercive trap.

Delores’s face flushed a dark, furious red. The sweet facade was completely gone, replaced by a venomous glare. But she didn't argue. She turned and crawled into the squalid twin bed, pulling the mismatched covers violently over her head.

Jessica completely ignored her. She turned her attention entirely to me. "Let’s move this mattress to where there’s room."
