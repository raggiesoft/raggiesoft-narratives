# Part 11: The Getaway Driver

## Section 1: 02:10 PM – The Final Bell

The bell rang, signaling the end of Fourth Period and the conclusion of the first day of Sophomore year.

The silence of the library shattered as chairs scraped against the floor. Mrs. Periwinkle stood up behind the circulation desk, her gaze sweeping the room one last time to ensure no one left a chair out of place.

"Have a pleasant afternoon," she whispered, which in Periwinkle-speak was a dismissal.

Luna and Leo packed up their binders. They had survived. They had navigated the architectural hostility of the clinic, the parallel separation of second period, the linguistic sanctuary of Latin, and the chaos of lunch.

"Let's roll," Luna said, grabbing the handles of his wheelchair.

They exited the library back into the Commons, which was rapidly filling with students streaming toward the bus loop and the student parking lot.

As they passed the cafeteria entrance, the kitchen doors swung open.

Josh Riley emerged.

He looked exhausted. His hair was slightly damp with sweat, and his clothes... well, the smell hit them before he even spoke. It was a heavy, cloying scent of old fryer oil and stale tater tots.

Josh was a Sophomore, just like them. But while Luna and Leo were just trying to get through the day, Josh had spent the last year cultivating an unearned sense of superiority over the incoming Freshmen. He clearly thought that surviving ninth grade gave him the right to be the king of the cafeteria. Mrs. DeMarco had swiftly corrected that assumption.

He saw the twins and scowled, trying to muster some of his usual bravado despite the fact that he smelled like a deep fryer.

"What are you looking at, Porter?" Josh snapped, wiping his hands on his jeans.

Leo wrinkled his nose involuntarily. He typed a single word on his **Quantum Communicator**, turning the screen so Josh could see it, though he didn't play the audio.

GREASE.

Josh flushed red. "Whatever. At least I don't need my sister to fight my battles."

"At least I don't smell like a French fry," Luna shot back cheerfully, not breaking her stride. "Enjoy the scent of justice, Josh."

They left him standing there, fuming and fragrant, as they headed for the exit.

## Section 2: 02:20 PM – The Squeeze

They emerged into the bright afternoon sun of the pickup loop. The line of cars was even longer than it had been in the morning, a snake of parents waiting to collect their children.

Luna wheeled Leo to their designated spot near the curb, scanning the line of massive SUVs and minivans.

"There she is," Luna said, pointing.

The **1990 Horizon Avalon** turned the corner. It was a fading light blue compact sedan, and in the sea of larger vehicles, it looked almost toy-like. It puttered down the lane, the engine making a slight wheezing sound as the AC compressor kicked on.

As Stella pulled up to the curb, she waved. Beside her in the passenger seat sat **Arthur Bennett**, looking sharp in his driving cap, waving enthusiastically at his grandchildren.

"Okay, load up!" Stella called out, popping the trunk.

The extraction was a struggle. The Avalon sat low to the ground, and the trunk opening was narrow. Stella had to wiggle the wheelchair, turning it at a precise angle to get it past the spare tire bump.

"Ugh, I hate this trunk," Stella grunted, shoving the wheels in. "It's like playing **Quadra-Blox** with fifty pounds of steel." She slammed the lid, wiping her hands on her pants. "I am going to buy that Sentinel next week, I swear."

"Hold your horses, Stella," Arthur said calmly from the front seat, leaning out the window. "We talked about this. You wait until October. That's when the 2001 models hit the lot and the dealers are desperate to move inventory. You want the best price on the LS trim? You wait four weeks."

"Four weeks of this trunk might kill me, Grandpa," Stella sighed, opening the back door.

Luna helped Leo transfer into the back seat. It was a tight squeeze. The Avalon didn't have the spacious bench of a full-size sedan; Leo's knees were practically touching the back of Arthur's seat. Luna climbed in beside him, tossing their backpacks onto the floorboards because there was no room left in the trunk.

"Everybody in?" Stella asked, getting back into the driver's seat. The car sank noticeably lower with the weight of four people and a wheelchair.

"We're in," Luna said, buckling up. She put her arm around Leo, who leaned into her, grateful for the contact after the long day.

Stella merged carefully into the traffic, the little engine working hard.

Arthur turned in his seat to look at the twins. "Well? Don't keep us in suspense. How was the first day of Sophomore year? Anybody give you trouble?"

Leo tapped his screen, a mischievous grin spreading across his face.

"LATIN WAS GOOD. LUNCH WAS LOUD. JOSH RILEY IS CLEANING GREASE TRAPS."

Arthur’s eyebrows shot up. "Grease traps? That sounds... aromatic."

"Mrs. DeMarco got him," Luna explained with relish. "First day. He tried to tax a Freshman for tater tots. She went nuclear. He has to scrub the kitchen for two weeks."

Arthur threw his head back and laughed, a deep, booming sound that filled the small car. "That woman is a national treasure! Remind me to send her a Christmas card."

"He smells like old oil," Leo added via his device.

"Justice is served," Stella said, tapping the steering wheel as she navigated the traffic. "And it smells like fries."

As the little blue Avalon puttered down the road, carrying them away from Northwood High, Leo relaxed into the cramped seat. His hip ached from the day, and his knees were knocking against the seat in front of him, but he didn't mind. He was safe. He was with Luna. And with Grandpa Arthur navigating the deal, he knew that in a few weeks, they’d be riding in a tank.

Sophomore year had officially begun.
