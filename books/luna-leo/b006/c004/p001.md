# Part 1: Difficult News

It was a Friday afternoon, the air buzzing slightly with the anticipation of the evening's home football game and halftime performance. Stella was making an early dinner, while Luna and Leo were likely running through music mentally or relaxing before the pre-game rush. Her maternal grandparents had stopped by for a visit, wanting to see the twins and wish them luck before heading to the stadium later themselves. Stella poured them coffee at the kitchen table.

There was a comfortable lull in the conversation about band preparations before Grandmother sighed softly, reaching across the table to place her hand gently over Stella's. "Stella, honey," she began, her voice quiet and laced with a familiar sadness, "there's something your grandfather and I need to tell you."

Stella looked up, meeting her grandmother's gaze, sensing the weight behind the words. "What is it, Grandma?"

"It's about your mother," Grandmother said gently. "We saw her doctor last week... well, we were listed as contacts since... since your father left. It's not good news, honey. The years of heavy drinking... and the smoking she took up after the divorce... it's done significant damage. Her liver, her lungs... the doctor says it's... very advanced. She doesn't have much longer."

Stella absorbed the news quietly, her expression somber but not shocked. It felt like the inevitable conclusion to a long, self-destructive path. There was a pang of sadness, not for the relationship they had, but perhaps for the one they never could have had.

Grandfather cleared his throat. "We visited her briefly," he added, his voice gruff with emotion. "Tried to talk to her. But..." He trailed off, shaking his head slightly.

"She's still the same, Stella," Grandmother finished sadly. "Still blaming everyone else – us, your father, you... for everything. No acceptance of her own part in... in any of it. Still insists she was a good mother."

Stella nodded slowly, a deep weariness settling over her. "I'm sorry you both have to deal with that, Grandma, Grandpa," she said sincerely. "Truly."

There was a moment of silence, then Grandmother asked gently, "We know there's the restraining order... but given the circumstances... have you thought about...?"

Stella knew what she was asking. She met her grandmother's gaze directly, her own expression clear and resolved, though not unkind. "No, Grandma," she said softly but firmly. "Thank you for telling me, I appreciate knowing. But no. I won't be going to see her. Nothing has changed. She hasn't changed. Seeing her now... it wouldn't bring closure, it wouldn't heal anything. It would just reopen wounds for me, and potentially for Luna and Leo down the line. She made her choices, over and over again. We made ours – to build a safe life away from that."

Her grandfather reached over and squeezed her hand. "We understand, Stella. Completely. We don't blame you one bit."

"We just had to let you know," Grandmother added quietly. "Whatever peace you can find with this, honey, you hold onto it. You've earned it."

Stella gave them a small, grateful smile. It was sad news, the end of a difficult chapter finally approaching, but her path forward, alongside Luna and Leo, remained clear. There would be no deathbed reconciliations built on denial. Their life was here now, built on truth and mutual support.
