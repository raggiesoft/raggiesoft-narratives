# Part 5: The Clean Break

## August 23, 2006 8 Ocean View Lane - The Twin Wing 07:30 PM

The schedule for tomorrow was set. The bags were packed. Now, it was time for the final prep.

Leo rolled into his bedroom. He looked at Olivia. He tapped his chest.

*"BATH,"* he typed. *"DEEP CLEAN. FOR THE FIRST DAY."*

"Full scrub?" Olivia asked.

Leo nodded. He looked at the door, where Stella and Luna were standing. He waved them in. He didn't want to be alone. He wanted the full team.

They moved into the massive Jack-and-Jill bathroom. It was designed for exactly this—plenty of turning radius, heated floors, and a deep soaking tub.

## The Transition

The routine was efficient. Olivia helped Leo out of his shirt. Stella started the water, checking the temperature with her wrist until it was perfect.

Then, the women began to undress. Not with fanfare, and not with shame. They simply removed their clothes because they were about to get wet helping him, and because they were all queuing up for showers immediately after.

Leo sat in his chair. He looked at them. He saw Stella’s strong shoulders. He saw Luna’s curved spine (the scoliosis from her CP). He saw Olivia’s height.

He looked them in the eye. They looked back, smiling, chatting about tomorrow’s schedule.

Suddenly, a memory spiked in his brain.

*Kent, Washington. The guest room. The heat.*

He remembered Delores taking her clothes off. But she hadn't looked like this—efficient, caring, neutral. She had looked *expectant*. She had stared at him with a hungry, angry intensity, waiting for a reaction he didn't know how to give. He remembered the confusion—*Naked means bath? Where is the water?*—and then the flash of her rage when he didn't respond the way she wanted.

Leo flinched. His breath hitched in his throat.

The room went silent instantly.

"Leo?" Stella asked, her hand hovering over the faucet. "You okay? Water too loud?"

Leo shook his head. He looked at Stella. He looked at Luna. He looked at Olivia.

They weren't looking at him with hunger. They were looking at him with love.

He let out a long, shaky breath. The memory dissolved, washed away by the reality of the steam and the lavender soap.

*"BAD MEMORY,"* he typed on his Scribe. *"DELORES. SHE WAS WRONG."*

"She was wrong about everything," Olivia said fiercely. "We are right."

*"YES,"* Leo typed. *"SAFE."* Stella immediately put the Scribe on the counter, away from the bath water.

## The Bath

Stella turned off the tap. "Ready?"

"Ready," Luna said.

Luna and Olivia stepped to either side of his chair. They performed the two-person lift—smooth, practiced, safe. They lowered him into the warm water.

Leo sighed. The heat loosened the spasticity in his hips. He floated.

For twenty minutes, they washed him. It was a ritual of care. Stella washed his hair. Luna scrubbed his back. Olivia managed the handheld sprayer. They talked about the train schedule, about the ocean, about everything and nothing.

When he was clean, the reverse lift happened.

Olivia had already draped a thick, warm towel over his **Titan X-Treme**. They lifted him out, dripping and warm, and settled him into the chair. They swaddled him in towels instantly to keep the chill off.

## The Rotation

"You dry," Stella said. "I'm jumping in."

Stella stepped into the roll-in shower.

Olivia wheeled Leo back into the bedroom. She dried his hair with a towel, rubbing vigorously until it spiked up. She helped him into his softest flannel pajamas.

A few minutes later, Stella emerged from the bathroom, wrapped in a robe, her hair wet. She walked over to Leo. She smelled of the same soap he did.

"You clean?" she asked.

Leo nodded.

"Good," she said, hugging him tight. "Tomorrow is going to be a good day. You're ready."

She kissed his forehead and headed down the hall to the Command Wing to dress.

"My turn," Luna called out from the bathroom.

Luna took her shower. When she was done, she wrapped herself in a towel and padded to her room to change into pajamas. Then she came back to Leo’s room.

She saw Leo sitting by the window, looking at the dark glass. She knew the "Delores Memory" had rattled him.

She didn't say anything. She just walked over and pulled him into a hug, holding him tightly in his chair, her wet hair dampening his pajama shirt. She grounded him.

"Go," Luna told Olivia. "I've got him."

Olivia went to shower.

Ten minutes later, she returned, dressed in her own pajamas. The "Shift Change" was complete. Everyone was clean. Everyone was safe.

"Living room?" Olivia asked.

Leo nodded.

They headed down the hall. In the living room, the lights were dim.

Stella was pacing near the window, her phone to her ear. "Yes, Jim, I know the invoice is due. The check is in the mail. Just make sure the landscaping crew doesn't block the bus stop."

Eleanor sat in her rocker by the fire. The blue wool was growing longer.

"Look at you all," Eleanor smiled, her needles clicking. "Scrubbed and shiny. Ready for school."

Leo rolled up to her. He smelled the wool and the fire.

*"CLEAN SLATE,"* he typed.

"That's right," Eleanor said. "Clean slate. Now come here and tell me if this sleeve looks long enough for your arm."
