# Part 2: The Gilded Age Tour

## August 23, 2006 OSTA Route 67 - "The Mansion Loop" 09:45 AM

They caught the **Route 67** bus right outside the Gateway Center. It was a "trolley-replica" bus—wood benches, brass rails, but crucially, a modern wheelchair lift in the back.

Leo rolled into the securement bay. The bus was filled with tourists holding guidebooks.

"Next stop: Bellevue Avenue," the driver announced.

The bus wound its way up the hill, past the wall in front of the former Almacs and onto the legendary avenue. The houses changed from colonial cottages to massive stone palaces set back behind iron gates.

"The Gilded Age," Stella narrated. "Where the robber barons spent their summers."

They saw the massive mansions come into view.

Then, the bus slowed in front of the largest, most imposing structure of them all—a massive Italian Renaissance palazzo overlooking the sea.

## STANTON’S REACH 

"Look at that pile of rocks," Eleanor said, shaking her head.

"It's huge," Luna marveled. "Look at those stairs."

"It actually has an elevator," Eleanor corrected, pointing to the massive limestone facade. "Arthur checked. It was an original, installed when they built it in 1895. He was fascinated by the hydraulics. He said it was the only thing in the house that made sense."

"So you could have gotten upstairs?" Olivia asked.

"Technically," Eleanor smiled. "But Arthur looked at the maintenance schedule for a gold-plated, turn-of-the-century lift and said, 'El, I sell cars. I know a money pit when I see one.' We bought the ranch in Middletown instead. No elevator needed."

Leo looked at the mansion. He appreciated the data point—an 1895 Otis was cool. But he saw the gravel paths. He saw the sheer, cold scale of the place.

He tapped his **Quantum Communicator**.

*"GOOD ENGINEERING,"* he typed. *"BAD HOME."*

"Exactly," Stella said. "Our Fortress fits us. That place fits an ego."

## The Turnaround

The bus reached the end of the line near **Rough Point**. Usually, tourists would get off here to walk the **Cliff Walk**.

Stella looked at the path entrance. It was narrow, unpaved, and riddled with stairs.

"Cliff Walk is a no-go," Stella said. "And Forty Steps is... well, forty steps."

"We've seen the ocean," Luna said. "I'm hungry."

"Me too," Leo typed.

They stayed on the bus as it looped around. They rode it back down Bellevue, past the mansions again, and got off at the stop across from a familiar landmark on West Main Road.

**The Golden Cow.**

## 11:30 AM Bayview Creamery

They rolled across the street. The sign was exactly as they remembered it from their trip in 2001: **BAYVIEW CREAMERY**.

They entered. It smelled of burgers and sugar.

They found a large booth. Leo rolled up to the end.

"Okay," Stella said. "It's lunch, but we have a tradition to uphold."

The waitress came over. "What can I get you folks?"

"Five Patty Melts," Stella ordered. "And five **Colossal Coolers**."

"Flavors?"

"Chocolate," Leo typed instantly.

"Coffee," Stella and Eleanor said in unison.

"Strawberry," Luna said.

"Vanilla," Olivia chose.

When the drinks arrived, they were massive—thick, frosty milkshakes that required a spoon.

Eleanor raised her glass.

"To Arthur," she said. "He introduced us to these. He said, 'Colossal Big, Colossal Good.'"

"To Arthur," they echoed.

Leo took a spoonful of the thick chocolate shake. It was cold, sweet, and perfect. He looked around the diner. It was noisy, unpretentious, and full of locals.

He preferred this to the mansion. The mansion was cold stone. This was warm food.

*"COLOSSAL GOOD,"* he typed.

"It sure is," Stella smiled, wiping a bit of foam from her lip. "Now drink up. We have to catch the ferry back to the Fortress."
