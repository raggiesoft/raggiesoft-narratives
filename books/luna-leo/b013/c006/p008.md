# Part 8: The Return of the Fleet

## July 28, 2006 8 Ocean View Lane - Living Room 05:30 PM

The "Twin Frequency" had done its work. The static in Leo's brain had cleared, replaced by a heavy, comfortable drowsiness.

He was still lying on top of Luna on the sectional, his weight settling into her. The desperate, medical need for deep pressure had faded, but neither of them made a move to separate. Now, it was simply affection—a silent, gravity-based hug between two halves of the same soul.

Olivia sat on the floor beside them, her back against the sofa. Her hand was still moving in slow, rhythmic circles on Leo’s back, working out the last tension knot near his shoulder blade.

"You good?" Olivia whispered.

Leo didn't type. He just hummed—a low, contented sound that vibrated through Luna’s chest.

*I am good.*

The rumble of the garage door opener broke the silence. The heavy, insulated door whirred upward, then clattered shut.

"Reinforcements," Olivia said softly.

The door from the mudroom opened. Stella and Eleanor walked in, carrying brown paper bags from **Heritage Market**.

The smell hit the room instantly—a warm, yeasty, sweet scent that triggered a memory for everyone.

"We got it," Eleanor announced, pulling a loaf of dark, dense bread from the bag. "Cranberry Walnut. Just like Arthur used to buy on Sunday mornings."

"And the fuel," Stella added, holding up two fresh jugs of **Autocrat** syrup.

Stella walked to the edge of the living room. She saw the pile on the sofa. She smiled.

"Okay, cuddle puddle," Stella said gently. "Time to deploy. We have a dinner to cook, and I need my sous-chefs."

Leo let out a sigh, but he nodded. He looked at Olivia.

"Up," he signaled with a small lift of his chin.

Olivia stood up. She leaned over the sofa.

"Alright, *mon chum*, let's get you vertical."

She slid her arms under his shoulders. Leo braced his core. With practiced ease, she lifted his upper body while Luna gently pushed from beneath. It was a seamless transfer. Olivia pivoted him, and he settled back into the plush seat of his new **Titan X-Treme** power chair, which was parked nearby.

Luna sat up, stretching her arms over her head. "You're getting heavy, Leo."

*"GRAVITY IS STRONG TODAY,"* Leo typed on his Scribe, his eyes bright again.

*KNOCK-KNOCK-KNOCK.*

A firm, professional rap sounded at the front door.

Stella checked the time. "That should be the delivery."

She walked to the foyer. The **APS Wall** stood its ground, blocking the view of the living room. Stella stepped around it and opened the door.

A driver in a **Metro Mobility** polo shirt stood there. Next to him, unloaded from a van, was Leo’s **TiLite Aero Z** manual wheelchair.

"Delivery for Porter?" the driver said. "One manual frame, rigid titanium."

"That's us," Stella said. "You can roll it right in."

The driver wheeled the chair into the foyer. He looked at the stone wall, impressed. "Nice entryway. Keeps the drafts out."

"Keeps everything out," Stella agreed. She signed the clipboard. "Thank you. You saved us a headache trying to get that on the bus."

"No problem. Enjoy the new power chair."

The driver left. Stella rolled the manual chair down the hall to the mudroom. She parked it next to the Titan’s charging bay.

"The fleet is assembled," Stella called out. "Manual chair is docked."

Leo drove his Titan into the kitchen. He looked at the manual chair. It was his "Indoor Slipper." The Titan was his "Hiking Boot." He was glad to have both.

## The Rhode Island Feast

"Okay," Eleanor said, tying on an apron. "First night in the new house. That means we eat like locals."

She unpacked the Heritage Market bags.

"Menu," Eleanor declared. "**Stuffies** for the appetizer. **Johnnycakes** on the griddle. And that Cranberry Walnut bread with real butter."

"Stuffies?" Luna asked.

"Stuffed Quahogs," Stella explained, pulling out a package of the massive, breadcrumb-and-clam-stuffed shells from the deli counter. "It’s a Rhode Island religion. Spicy sausage, clams, peppers, breading, baked in the shell."

They set to work.

Stella managed the oven. Eleanor mixed the cornmeal batter for the johnnycakes (the thin, crispy Rhode Island version, not the fluffy pancake kind). Olivia poured the milk and stirred in the Autocrat until it was the color of toffee.

Leo parked his Titan at the head of the table. He hit the **ELEVATE** button, raising himself up to proper dining height.

He looked at the spread. It wasn't fancy. It was brown, fried, and hearty. It was the food of the coast.

He looked at the **Cranberry Walnut Bread** in the center of the table. He thought of Arthur.

He tapped his Scribe.

*"GRANDPA WOULD LIKE THIS MENU."*

"He's having a slice right now," Eleanor said, placing the warm bread basket next to the urn on the mantle before bringing a second basket to the table. "Now eat. You need your strength for school."

They ate. The stuffies were spicy and briny. The johnnycakes were crisp and earthy. The coffee milk was sweet.

It was their first meal in the Fortress. They weren't visitors anymore. They were Rhode Islanders. And they were home.
