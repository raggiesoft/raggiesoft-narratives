# Part 3: The Showroom

## 10:15 AM Metro Mobility - Main Street

The shop was large, smelling of rubber tires and machine oil. The floor was lined with scooters, hospital beds, and manual chairs.

But in the center of the room, on a raised dais, sat the **Titan X-Treme**.

It was jet black with neon green suspension springs. It looked less like a medical device and more like an ATV.

A salesman walked over. "Can I help you folks?"

"He wants that one," Stella said, pointing. "The Group 3. Mid-wheel drive. Seat elevator."

"That's a serious machine," the salesman said. "Do you have a prescription? Insurance usually takes about six months to process a claim like this."

"No insurance," Stella cut him off. "Private pay. Today."

The salesman blinked. "Oh. Well, then. Let's get him in it."

They helped Leo transfer from his manual **TiLite Aero Z** into the Titan. The seat was plush, contoured to his body.

The salesman adjusted the joystick mount. "Okay, power on."

Leo pressed the button. The control panel lit up. **BATTERY: 100%.**

He pushed the stick forward.

The chair didn't lurch; it glided. The motors were silent but powerful. He spun a tight 360-degree turn in place. He drove it toward the door, then stopped on a dime.

He hit the **Elevate** button. The seat rose 12 inches, bringing him to eye level with the salesman.

Leo grinned. He tapped his Scribe, which he had transferred to the new mount.

*"I AM TALL,"* the robotic voice said.

"It fits," Luna said. "It looks like you."

"We'll take it," Stella said. "And we need the heavy-duty charger, the rain cover, and the backpack hooks."

"Excellent," the salesman said, rushing to the register. "I can schedule delivery for Tuesday..."

Leo spun the chair around. He drove up to the counter, still elevated.

*"NO DELIVERY,"* he typed.

"Sir?" the salesman asked.

*"I AM DRIVING IT HOME."*

"You... you can't drive it to Iron Point," the salesman stammered. "It's thirty miles."

"We're taking the train," Stella corrected. "He's driving it to the bus stop."

She reached into her wallet. She didn't pull out a debit card. She pulled out the **black titanium credit card** linked to the Bennett Trust.

"The total is **\$28,450**," the salesman said, his voice shaking slightly.

Stella handed him the card. "Run it."

The machine whirred. **APPROVED.**

"Now," Stella said, pointing to Leo’s empty manual wheelchair. "I don't want to carry that frame on the bus. Can you ship it to us?"

"For a twenty-eight thousand dollar sale?" the salesman beamed. "I'll have my driver drop it off at your house this afternoon. Free of charge."

"Perfect," Stella said.

## The Exit

Ten minutes later, the automatic doors of Metro Mobility slid open.

Leo Porter rolled out onto the sidewalk of Main Street. He wasn't pushing rims. He wasn't sweating. He was piloting a 350-pound electric tank.

He pushed the joystick forward. The chair surged to 6 MPH.

Olivia had to jog slightly to keep up.

"Fast enough for you?" she laughed.

Leo looked at her. He looked at the bus stop ahead. He looked at the city.

*"MAXIMUM POWER,"* he typed. *"LET'S GO CATCH THE 4."*

They headed for the bus stop, Leo leading the way in his new chariot, leaving the manual chair behind to be delivered by the staff. He didn't need it anymore. He had the power.
