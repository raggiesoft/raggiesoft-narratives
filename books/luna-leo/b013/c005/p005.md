# Part 5: The Capital Request

## July 27, 2006 The Grey Gull Inn - Dining Room 07:00 PM

The dining room of the Grey Gull was cozy, lit by candles and the fireplace. The smell of slow-cooked pot roast filled the air. The Fortress sat at a round table, exhausted but satisfied.

They had mapped the house. They had met the Judge. They had survived the Cross Bronx Expressway (vicariously).

As Mrs. O'Malley cleared the plates, serving warm apple crisp for dessert, Leo looked at Luna. He tapped the table with his spoon in a rhythmic pattern.

*Tak-tak-tak.*

Luna grinned. "Go for it."

Leo turned to Stella. He mounted his **MagnaByte Scribe**.

*"PROPOSAL,"* he typed.

"I'm listening," Stella said, putting down her fork.

*"THE HOUSE IS BIG. THE STUDIO IS SOUNDPROOF. MY STUDENT BELL KIT IS TOO SMALL FOR THE ROOM."*

He pulled up a browser window he had saved earlier. It showed the **Vanguard Soloist** (the oak-cased glockenspiel) and a massive, gleaming **Vanguard** drum kit in "Midnight Sparkle" finish.

*"I WANT TO UPGRADE THE RHYTHM SECTION,"* Leo typed. *"REAL DRUMS. REAL STEEL."*

Stella looked at the screen. "Drums? In the house?"

"The room is isolated," Luna interjected. "We checked the specs. Double drywall, Green Glue. He could play a marching snare in there and you wouldn't hear it in the kitchen."

"And it's good for his range of motion," Olivia added. "Reaching for cymbals is basically physical therapy."

Stella smiled. She remembered Arthur’s checkbook philosophy: *Buy the tool that does the job.*

"Approved," Stella said. "If we're building a studio, let's fill it. Add it to the shopping list."

Leo grinned. But he wasn't done.

*"ITEM 2,"* he typed.

He pulled up the map of the **Braxton State University** campus. It was a sea of buildings spread over 300 acres.

*"THE CAMPUS IS HUGE. THE TUNNELS ARE LONG. TODAY, I WAS TIRED AFTER ONE MILE."*

He looked at his manual chair.

*"I CANNOT PUSH TO ENGINEERING EVERY DAY. I NEED A MOTOR."*

He switched tabs to the **Titan Mobility** website. He showed the **Titan X-Treme**—the mid-wheel drive beast with the seat elevator and the transit tie-downs.

*"I NEED THE TANK,"* Leo typed. *"IT HAS A 20 MILE RANGE. I CAN GO FROM THE DORM TO THE LAB TO THE TRAIN WITHOUT STOPPING."*

Stella looked at the chair specs. She looked at the price tag (which was substantial). Then she looked at Leo’s shoulders, which were still slumped from the fatigue of the tunnel walk.

"You're right," Stella said instantly. "The manual chair is for the house. The Titan is for the world."

She looked at Olivia. "Can we find a dealer in Braxton?"

"Already found one," Olivia said, checking her phone. "**Metro Mobility** on Main Street. They have a demo unit in stock."

"We go tomorrow," Stella decided. "We buy the drums. We buy the chair. We equip the soldier for the war."

Leo sat back, satisfied. He had his house, his train, his drums, and his tank.

*"FORTRESS UPGRADE COMPLETE,"* he typed.

"Almost," Eleanor said from across the table, finishing her tea. "You still need to teach me how to use that fancy coffee machine you bought for the kitchen. I'm not drinking instant."

"Deal," Leo typed. *"I WILL ENGINEER THE COFFEE MILK."*
