# Part 8: The Wall Test

## August 15, 2006 8 Ocean View Lane 11:00 AM

The moving truck had finally arrived. The crew from **Hercules Van Lines** was hauling boxes into the house.

Inside the living room, the chaos of the move—the banging of dollies, the shouting of movers—had triggered a minor sensory overload for Leo. He wasn't in full meltdown, but he was vibrating.

He lay on the new sectional sofa. Luna lay on top of him, her weight pressing him down into the cushions, her arms locked around his back. They were whispering to each other, their foreheads touching, creating a bubble of calm in the middle of the storm.

Suddenly, the front doorbell rang.

The front door was wide open for the movers, but the **APS Wall**—the 6-foot slate partition—blocked the view of the living room completely.

Stella walked to the foyer. Standing there was a **Police Officer** from the Iron Point PD.

"Morning," the officer said, tipping his hat. "Just wanted to welcome you to the neighborhood. I'm Officer Miller—Judge Miller's nephew. He told me to keep an eye out for the new residents."

Stella smiled, stepping into the foyer to block the gap. "Thank you, Officer. We appreciate it."

"Moving day is stressful," the officer noted, peering past her shoulder at the wall of boxes. "Is everything okay? I heard... some noise?"

From behind the slate wall, Leo let out a low, resonant hum—part of his calibration with Luna. To an uneducated ear, it might have sounded like a moan of distress.

The officer frowned, tilting his head. "Is someone hurt?"

Stella didn't flinch. She pointed to the wall.

"My brother is autistic," Stella said calmly. "He's overwhelmed by the movers. He's doing deep pressure therapy on the sofa. It helps him regulate."

The officer looked at the wall. He couldn't see Leo. He couldn't see Luna lying on top of him. He couldn't see the intimacy that a stranger might misinterpret as abuse.

He just saw a wall. And he heard a sister who was in control.

"Understood," the officer said, relaxing. "Well, if you need anything—traffic control for the truck, noise complaints—you call dispatch. Welcome to Iron Point."

"Thanks, Officer," Stella said.

She closed the door. She walked back around the wall into the living room.

Luna looked up from Leo’s chest. "Who was that?"

"The Police," Stella said.

Leo’s eyes widened. He tapped his **Quantum Communicator**.

*"DID HE SEE?"*

"Nope," Stella grinned, patting the slate tiles of the partition. "He saw the wall. He saw me. He didn't see the Fortress."

Leo let out a long breath. He squeezed Luna tighter.

*"GOOD WALL,"* he typed.

"Best money we ever spent," Stella agreed. "You guys stay there. I'll tell the movers to put the boxes in the garage until you're calibrated."

The wall had passed its first test. It had turned a potential APS investigation into a friendly neighborly visit. The Fortress was secure.
