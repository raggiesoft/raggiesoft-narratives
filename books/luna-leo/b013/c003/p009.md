# Part 9: The Engine Stops

## December 30, 2005 The University Inn - Room 103 03:00 AM

The phone on the nightstand in **Room 101** rang. A soft, electronic warble in the quiet darkness of the suite.

Stella woke instantly. She didn't need to look at the caller ID. She knew. The connecting door to her grandparents' room was unlocked, but Eleanor had called instead of walking through. It was a formality. A signal that the world had changed.

Stella picked up the receiver. "Grandma?"

"He's gone, honey," Eleanor’s voice was calm, steady, and filled with a profound, quiet grace. "He went in his sleep. He didn't wake up. He just... stopped."

Stella felt a wave of sadness wash over her, but beneath it was an immense, stabilizing peace. He hadn't died waiting. He hadn't died worrying. He had finished his meal, toasted his victory, and closed his eyes.

"I'm coming over," Stella said. "Right now."

"No rush," Eleanor said, her voice sounding stronger than Stella expected. "He's not going anywhere. And neither am I. I still have work to do."

## The Gathering

Stella walked into Room 103. Luna, Leo, and Olivia followed a moment later, wrapped in their robes.

The room was peaceful. Arthur lay in the bed, the covers pulled up to his chest. He looked like he was deep in a pleasant dream. The lines of pain and fatigue that had etched his face in Virginia were smoothed away.

Eleanor sat in the armchair beside the bed, her hand resting on his. She wasn't crying. She looked like a woman who had just said a long, satisfactory goodbye at a train station.

"He waited," Eleanor told them, looking at the twins. "He woke up briefly around two. He told me he liked the quiet here. He said, 'El, the deal is done. The kids are safe. The house is solid. I think I'm going to rest now.'"

Leo rolled his manual chair to the side of the bed. He looked at his grandfather. He remembered the wink on the train. *Mission accomplished.*

He reached out and touched Arthur’s cold hand. It was the hand that had signed the checks, steered the cars, and pointed the way North.

Leo tapped his Quantum Communicator.

"ENGINE OFF. RACE WON."

"That it is, Leo," Eleanor agreed. "He crossed the finish line."

## The Decision

The sun began to rise over the Atlantic, filling the room with a brilliant, cold winter light.

"We have to make arrangements," Stella said gently. "We can transport him back to Virginia for the burial, if you want."

Eleanor shook her head firmly. "No. He didn't want to go back. He told me yesterday. He said he didn't want to leave the Fortress."

She looked out the window at the **Narraganset Esplanade Station** across the plaza.

"We'll handle it here," Eleanor decided. "Cremation. He always liked the ocean. And then..." She turned to Stella, her eyes clear and determined. "Then we go back to Virginia to pack."

"You're coming with us?" Stella asked. "To pack the house?"

"I'm coming back to pack *my* house," Eleanor corrected. "I'm not staying in that big empty place in Virginia with the ghosts. I'm selling it. I'm moving to Iron Point."

She looked at Luna and Leo.

"I want to see that kitchen I designed," she said, a small, fierce smile touching her lips. "I want to sit on the porch and watch the trains. And someone needs to teach Olivia how to make a proper clam chowder before you embarrass yourselves in front of the Yankees."

Leo rolled up to her. He placed his hand on her knee.

"GRANDMA IS COMING," he typed.

"Grandma is coming," Eleanor agreed, covering his hand with hers. "I promised Arthur I'd keep an eye on you. And I never break a promise."
