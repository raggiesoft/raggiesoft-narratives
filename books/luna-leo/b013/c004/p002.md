# Part 2: The War Room

## January 16, 2006 1091 Daniel Maloney Drive, Virginia Beach 10:00 AM

The house was quiet. The morning sun slanted through the blinds, illuminating dust motes dancing in the air.

On top of the entertainment center, in the place of honor usually reserved for family photos, sat the polished blue granite urn. Arthur James Bennett was home. The chrome band around the rim caught the light, a silent, sturdy presence watching over the room.

Stella sat at the dining room table. She wasn't wearing her usual work-from-home attire; she was dressed in "Business Casual," ready for appointments. Spread out before her was a legal pad, a stack of folders, and a calendar.

Eleanor sat opposite her, a cup of tea warming her hands. She looked tired, but her eyes were clear. She was no longer just a grandmother; she was the co-architect of the migration.

Luna, Leo, and Olivia were at the table, too. Leo had his MagnaByte Scribe out, ready to take notes.

"Okay," Stella said, uncapping her pen. "Grandpa did his part. He secured the future. Now we have to handle the present. We have six months to dismantle two lifetimes in Virginia and move them six hundred miles north."

She wrote a large \#1 on the pad.

"Academics. We are scheduled to start the Spring semester at CCHR on Monday. But if we do that, we are tied here until May. We’ll be writing term papers while trying to pack boxes."

Leo looked at Luna. They had worked so hard to get their degrees. But the goal wasn't CCHR anymore. The goal was Braxton.

He typed on his Scribe.

"DROP THE CLASSES. FULL REFUND."

"Agreed," Luna said immediately. "I don't need to take 'Music Appreciation' when I've already been accepted to a Conservatory. Let's take the refund and put it toward the moving costs."

"Done," Stella said, drawing a line through the semester. "I'll go to the Registrar today. We are officially on a 'Gap Semester.' Our full-time job is The Move."

She wrote \#2.

"The Legal Transfer. This is the big one. We have to move the Guardianship and Conservatorship."

She opened the file Sarah Jenkins had prepared.

"We have to petition the Second Judicial Circuit here in Virginia for permission to transfer. Then, we have to file for acceptance in Rhode Island. The lawyer says RI handles this in Probate Court. We'll be filing in Iron Point."

"Local control," Eleanor noted approvingly. "Arthur would like that."

"Exactly," Stella said. "Mrs. Higgins at the Town Hall will handle the paperwork. We’ll need to go up there to appear before the judge eventually, but for now, Sarah handles the Virginia exit."

She wrote \#3.

"Real Estate. Two houses to sell."

"Mine is ready," Eleanor said calmly. "I've already called the estate liquidators. Take what you want for the new house—Arthur’s chair, the good china, the tools. Sell the rest. Put the house on the market next week. I don't want to linger."

"And this place," Stella said, looking around the living room. "My name is on the deed. I can sell it whenever we're ready. I say we list it in April. That gives us time to pack without living in a showroom."

Finally, she wrote \#4.

"The Fleet."

She looked out the window at the 2001 Holt Sentinel parked in the driveway. It was a tank. It was paid for. But driving it 600 miles fully loaded was a risk she didn't need to take.

"I've made a decision on the car," Stella announced. "We aren't selling it. It’s a good car, and with snow tires, it’ll handle Iron Point just fine. But we aren't driving it up."

"Shipping?" Olivia asked.

"Shipping," Stella confirmed. "I'm hiring a professional auto transport carrier. They'll put it on a truck here and drop it in the driveway in Iron Point. It saves us the wear and tear, and it means we don't have to split up for the trip."

Leo looked at the map on his screen. He traced the rail line.

"WE TAKE THE TRAIN," he typed.

"We take the train," Stella agreed. "We book Business Class on the ARTS Coastal Service. We ride together, relaxed. We get off at Iron Point Station, and we walk the two blocks to our front door."

"And the stuff?" Luna asked. "The furniture? The MagnaBytes?"

"Professional movers," Stella said. "A full-service van line. They pack it, they drive it, they unload it. We just supervise."

She looked at the list. It was a massive logistical operation. But they had the funding, they had the plan, and they had the team.

"Alright," Stella said, standing up. "I'm going to the college to drop the classes. Luna, you start sorting the sheet music. Leo, you start backing up your hard drives. Eleanor... you just tell us what to keep."

"I'll make the coffee," Eleanor said, standing up with a resilience that belied her age. "We have a lot of boxes to fill."

Leo watched Stella grab her keys. He looked at the urn on the shelf.

"THE GENERAL HAS A PLAN," he typed.

"The General always has a plan," Olivia smiled. "Let's get to work."
