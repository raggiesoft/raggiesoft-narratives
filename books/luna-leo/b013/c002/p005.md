# Part 5: The Subterranean City

## December 28, 2005 - The University Inn - The Chancellor Dining Room 08:45 AM

The blizzard had arrived in earnest. Beyond the floor-to-ceiling windows of the hotel dining room, the world was erased. The Plaza, the Station, and the Ocean were gone, replaced by a swirling vortex of white. The wind howled against the glass, vibrating the silverware on the tables.

Inside, it was warm, smelling of maple syrup and bacon.

The Fortress (plus Arthur and Eleanor) occupied a large round table near the fireplace. They were finishing what Arthur declared to be "a proper breakfast"—eggs, sausage, home fries, and fresh fruit.

"We have a problem," Stella said, watching a snow plow struggle against a drift outside. "The Fine Arts Center is three blocks away. In this wind, with Leo’s manual chair... it’s going to be a fight. We’ll need to wrap the flute case in plastic."

"I can push," Olivia offered. "But the sidewalks might be impassable even if they plow them."

Leo looked out the window. He tapped his **Quantum Communicator**.

"FRICTION COEFFICIENT IS ZERO. TRACTION IMPOSSIBLE."

Their waitress arrived with a fresh pot of coffee. Her nametag read **ASHLEE - Junior / Hospitality**. She saw the coats piled on the extra chair—heavy wool, scarves, hats.

"Heading out?" Ashlee asked, pouring Arthur a refill.

"We have a 10:00 AM meeting with Dean Sterling," Stella explained. "We're trying to figure out the best route to the Fine Arts Center without turning into popsicles."

Ashlee blinked. She looked at the window, then back at them. She laughed gently.

"You're not going *outside*, are you?"

The table fell silent.

"Is there... a teleporter?" Luna asked.

"Better," Ashlee smiled. "You're in the **University Inn**. We're connected to the **Bull Run**."

"The bus?" Leo tapped.

"No," Ashlee corrected. "The Tunnels. The entire campus is connected underground. Every building. The Library, the Union, the Dorms, the Station. It's all linked."

She pointed to the elevators.

"Go down to **Level 1**. Turn left at the station entrance. You'll see a set of double doors marked **BULL RUN - NORTH CAMPUS**. That's the main artery. It takes you straight to the basement of the Fine Arts Center. You never have to touch snow."

"Is it open?" Stella asked. "It's Winter Break."

"Usually it's ID access only at night," Ashlee explained. "But when a 'Code White' weather emergency is declared—like right now—Administration unlocks the mag-locks. It's open 24/7 for everyone until the storm clears. It's a safety protocol."

Leo looked at the window, where the wind was screaming. Then he looked at the warm, carpeted hallway leading to the elevators.

"UNDERGROUND," he typed. "WE ARE MOLES."

"Warm moles," Arthur chuckled, wiping his mouth. "Let's go see this tunnel."

## 09:15 AM The Bull Run - The Nexus

They took the elevator down to the Concourse Level. They bypassed the station ticket hall and found the doors Ashlee had described.

THE BULL RUN

Pedestrian & Accessibility Network

Sector: North Campus

They pushed through.

It wasn't a utility tunnel. It was a wide, brightly lit corridor with polished terrazzo floors (perfect for Leo’s wheels). The walls were painted in wayfinding colors. HVAC vents hummed, keeping the air a steady 70 degrees.

"Look at the signs," Luna pointed.

The corridors were named like streets.

- **↑ ACADEMIC AVENUE** (To Library & Engineering)

- **→ ARTS ALLEY** (To Conservatory & Theater)

- **← THE STAMPEDE** (To Student Union & Dorms)

They turned right onto **Arts Alley**. The walls here were lined with acoustic tiles and framed posters of past concerts.

They passed a massive set of glass doors on their left. Inside, they saw rows of ergonomic chairs, where students could be seen studying probably, during the semester.

THE CENTENNIAL LIBRARY

24/7 Study Commons - Level B1

"It's a city," Eleanor marveled, her cane tapping rhythmically on the floor. "They built a whole city under the snow."

"It's **Universal Design**," Stella said, checking the map on the wall. "If you can't make the weather accessible, you move the path."

They passed another junction. **THE FOUNDRY DINING HALL**. The smell of fresh bread wafted out.

"And that must be the Student Union," Olivia said, pointing to a junction marked **THE STAMPEDE UNION**. "Bowling alley, cinema, bookstore. All down here."

Leo rolled ahead. His manual chair glided effortlessly on the smooth floor. He didn't have to fight sludge. He didn't have to worry about slipping. He was completely independent.

He tapped his screen.

"LOGIC WINS."

09:45 AM

The Fine Arts Center - Lower Level

They reached the end of **Arts Alley**. A set of elevator doors awaited them.

FINE ARTS CENTER

Lobby / Recital Hall / Dean's Office

They rode up to the **4th Floor**.

When the doors opened, they were in a sun-drenched (or, today, snow-blinded) atrium. Through the glass walls, they could see the blizzard raging outside. The trees were bent double. The sidewalks were buried under two feet of drift.

But they were dry. They were warm. And they were ten minutes early.

Dean Sterling’s assistant looked up from her desk as the Fortress rolled in. She saw their dry coats and lack of snow boots.

"Took the Bull Run?" she guessed.

"We did," Stella said. "Best commute of my life."

The door to the inner office opened. Dean Elise Sterling stepped out. She saw Luna clutching her flute case. She saw Leo in his chair. She saw the grandparents standing tall.

"You made it," Sterling smiled. "And you brought the Orpheus."

"I brought the whole team," Luna said.

"Good," Sterling said, holding the door wide. "Come in. Let's hear what silver sounds like."
