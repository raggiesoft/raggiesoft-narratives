# Part 9: The Last Train Out

## December 28, 2005 Iron Point Station - Platform 12:35 PM

The walk back from **Lot 4B** had been brutal. The wind had picked up, turning the snowfall into horizontal needles of ice. Leo’s shoulders burned from pushing through the accumulating drifts on the sidewalk, even with Stella and Olivia taking turns helping him gain traction.

But they had made it.

They rolled onto the sheltered platform of **Iron Point Station**, stamping snow off their boots. Leo’s manual chair was caked in white, but the wheels were still turning.

Suddenly, the station PA system crackled to life. The calm "Conductor" voice was replaced by an urgent, live human dispatcher.

"Attention all BTA Regional Rail passengers. Due to extreme blizzard conditions and rapid ice accumulation on the catenary lines, the BTA is suspending all rail service."

The Fortress froze.

"The approaching train, arriving in ten minutes, will be the **final service** to Braxton Central and Narraganset Esplanade. Repeat: This is the last train. Service will be suspended until further notice."

Leo looked at the digital display board.

SOUTH SHORE LINE - INBOUND - 12:45 PM STATUS: LAST TRAIN SUBSEQUENT TRAINS: CANCELLED

"We cut it close," Luna breathed, her face red from the wind.

"We timed it perfectly," Stella corrected, checking her watch. "Ten minutes. Just enough time to thaw out."

Leo looked back toward the dark line of trees where **Lot 4B** lay hidden under the snow. He thought about the struggle to get there—the slush, the wind, the effort.

He tapped his Quantum Communicator.

"THE TEST PASSED."

"It did," Olivia agreed, brushing snow off his jacket. "If we could do it today, in this nightmare, we can do it on a sunny Tuesday in May."

"IT IS ROLLABLE," Leo typed. "IT IS SURVIVABLE."

In the distance, the twin headlights of the **BiLevel Train** appeared, cutting through the whiteout. The train was moving slowly, pushing a wave of snow ahead of it. It looked heavy, unstoppable, and incredibly safe.

The train hissed to a halt. The doors slid open.

"Last call for the city!" the conductor shouted, leaning out into the storm. "Get on board or get stuck!"

They rolled into the warm, bright car. They collapsed into their seats.

As the doors sealed shut and the train began to move, accelerating away from the freezing suburbs, Leo watched the station disappear into the white void.

He knew two things for certain:

They had found their home.

They were on the last lifeboat back to the warmth of the hotel.

The train picked up speed, racing the storm back to the city.

## 01:30 PM Narraganset Esplanade Station - Sky Platform

The train arrived at the University station. The platform was deserted. The storm had swallowed the city.

"This is the final stop," the automated voice announced. "Service is now suspended. Please exit the station immediately."

They took the glass elevator down to **Level 1**. They entered the **Concourse Tunnel**.

The contrast was jarring. Outside, the world was ending. Inside the tunnel, it was quiet, warm, and lined with student art. The moving walkways hummed peacefully.

They walked (and rolled) back to **The University Inn**.

As they entered the lobby, the Front Desk clerk, Jessica, looked up.

"You made it!" she said. "We just heard the BTA shut down. We thought you might be stranded in the suburbs."

"We caught the last one," Stella said, unzipping her coat. "Just in time for lunch."

Leo rolled to the window. He looked out at the Plaza. The buses were gone. The trains were stopped. The snow was piling up against the glass.

But he was inside. He was warm. And he had a plan.

He tapped his screen.

"MISSION ACCOMPLISHED. WE BUY THE DIRT."

Stella smiled, pulling out her phone to call Barbara the real estate agent. "Yes, we do. Room service first, then real estate."

They headed to the elevators, leaving the storm outside to rage against the walls of their new city.
