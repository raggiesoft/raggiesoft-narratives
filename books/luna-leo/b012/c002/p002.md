# Part 2: The Separation

After their celebratory breakfast, a new, more focused energy took hold of the house. It was time to get ready. The plan was for the day to unfold like a traditional wedding, which meant adhering to one specific, challenging tradition: the bride and groom were not to see each other until the ceremony.

This was only the second time Leo would be voluntarily separated from Olivia since she had rescued him from Seattle, and the knowledge of the impending separation sat like a cold, heavy stone in his stomach.

Stella, ever the logistical general, laid out the plan. "Okay, team," she announced, her voice calm and organized. "Luna and I will help Leo get ready here. Arthur and Eleanor will be back in about an hour to help us get to the venue. Olivia," she said, turning to her with a soft, understanding smile, "your parents will be here any minute to pick you up so you can get ready with them at their place."

Olivia’s parents, **Daniel and Sarah Walker**, lived just around the corner, a convenient and necessary arrangement for the day's preparations.

The final moments before Olivia’s parents arrived were filled with a tense, quiet intimacy. Leo clung to Olivia’s hand, his knuckles white. He didn't need his communicator to convey the anxiety that was radiating from him in palpable waves.

"Hey," Olivia whispered, leaning in so her voice was for him alone. "It's just for a little while. Like yesterday. I'll be back before you know it, and I'll be waiting for you at the end of the aisle. I promise." She sealed the promise with a soft, reassuring kiss.

When the doorbell rang, signaling the arrival of her parents, Leo's grip tightened for a fraction of a second before he forced himself to let go. It was a monumental act of trust. He watched her walk to the door, give her parents a hug, and then turn back to give him one last, brilliant smile before she was gone.

The moment the door clicked shut, the fragile dam of his composure began to crumble. A low, keening sound, the precursor to a meltdown, started deep in his chest.

Stella and Luna were on him in an instant. They didn't coddle or dismiss his fear; they met it head-on.

"I know, buddy. I know it's hard," Stella said, her voice a firm, steady anchor as she knelt in front of his chair. "But this is the last hard part. You can do this. We're right here with you."

Luna took his hand, her touch familiar and grounding. "It's just like the old days, Leo," she said softly, invoking their shared history of mutual support. "Just you and me and Stel. We've got you. We're going to get you looking so handsome for your *ma blonde*. Think how surprised she'll be."

Leo took a deep, shuddering breath, focusing on his sisters' faces, on their unwavering presence. The anxiety was still there, a frantic, buzzing thing just beneath his skin, but their combined strength was a powerful counterweight. He gave a small, jerky nod. He would do this. For Olivia. For them. The fortress would get him to the finish line.
