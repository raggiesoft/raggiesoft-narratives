# Part 11: The Send-Off

As the last song of the final dance faded away, a happy, contented quiet settled over the remaining guests. The reception was drawing to its natural, peaceful close. It was time for the final event of the evening: the send-off.

Stella, in her role as the evening's gentle coordinator, began to herd the small group of family and friends toward the grand main entrance of the country club. The staff handed each person a small, elegant bottle of wedding bubbles, a quiet and beautiful choice for the farewell.

The guests formed two lines, creating a pathway from the clubhouse doors out into the crisp, starry September night. A few moments later, the doors opened, and Mr. Leo Porter and Ms. Olivia Walker Porter appeared, their faces absolutely radiant with the joy of the day.

As they began their walk down the path of their loved ones, the air filled with a shimmering, iridescent cloud of bubbles. They floated and danced in the warm light from the clubhouse, catching the moonlight and creating a magical, dreamlike canopy over the happy couple. Laughter and cheers of "Congratulations!" and "*Félicitations!*" echoed in the night air.

Leo and Olivia walked slowly, savoring the final moments of their perfect day. They looked at the faces of their family—Stella, Luna, Arthur, Eleanor, Olivia's parents, and her Canadian relatives—all of them smiling, their faces full of love and happiness for them.

At the end of the path, the Holt Sentinel was waiting, its interior light glowing invitingly. Stella, having run ahead, was already in the driver's seat. She would be their chauffeur for their "getaway," driving them back to the quiet, peaceful sanctuary of their home on Daniel Maloney Drive.

As they got into the car, Leo and Olivia turned for one last wave. They saw their entire world, their entire fortress, standing there, united and beaming, sending them off with a final, shimmering cloud of good wishes. It was the perfect end to a perfect beginning.
