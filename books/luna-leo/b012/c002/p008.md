# Part 8: The Toasts

As the final dance ended and guests returned to their seats, a new kind of joyful anticipation filled the ballroom. It was time for the toasts. Stella, in her role as Best Woman and head of the family, was the first to step up to the microphone, a glass of sparkling cider in her hand.

"Hi, everyone," she began, her voice clear and steady, though her eyes were shining with unshed tears. "For most of my life, my world has revolved around protecting my brother and sister. My primary goal was just to keep them safe. I never dared to dream about what it would look like for them to be truly, deeply happy." She turned her gaze to Leo and Olivia, a brilliant, heartfelt smile breaking across her face. "Olivia, you weren't part of my original plan," she said, and a soft laugh went through the room. "You were the wonderful, brilliant, fiercely loyal variable I never saw coming. You didn't just join our fortress; you made it stronger. You brought a joy to my brother's life that I could only have dreamed of."

She raised her glass a little higher. "So, to my new sister, Olivia Walker Porter, and to my amazing brother, Leo: thank you for showing me what true happiness looks like. And to the official birthday girl, Happy 19th Birthday, Liv. We love you both so much. *Santé!*" (Cheers!)

Next, Luna stepped up, her expression a mirror of her sister's love. "I don't have a big speech," she said softly. "I just know that for my entire life, my first thought has always been, 'Where's Leo?' Seeing him with Olivia, I know the answer is 'right where he belongs.' Olivia, you're the sister I always wanted. Leo, you're my other half, and seeing you this happy is the best gift in the world. I love you both."

Then, Olivia's father, Daniel Walker, rose, his expression full of paternal pride. "When your daughter grows up, you hope she finds a partner who is kind, who is loyal, and who truly sees her. Leo, you are all of those things and more. You have a strength and a heart that are truly remarkable. Welcome to our family, son. We are so proud to call you one of our own."

Finally, Olivia’s aunt, Tante Isabelle, stood, representing the entire Québécois contingent. She began in heavily accented but heartfelt English, "To see our Olivia find such a love... it fills our hearts." She then seamlessly switched to her native French, her words flowing like music, directed at the happy couple. "*Votre amour est une belle chanson. On vous souhaite une vie de bonheur, de rires, et de poutine! À votre santé, Léo et Olivia!*" (Your love is a beautiful song. We wish you a life of happiness, of laughter, and of poutine! To your health, Leo and Olivia!)

The room was filled with love and happy tears. Then, all eyes turned to Leo. He typed a message on his communicator, and his clear, confident voice provided the final, perfect toast of the evening.

"TO MY MA BLONDE. YOU ARE MY EVERYTHING. I LOVE YOU."
