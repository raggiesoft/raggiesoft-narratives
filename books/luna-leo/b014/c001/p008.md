# Part 9: The Library Rendezvous

## August 28, 2006 Fine Arts Center - The Music Library (Reading Room) 06:10 PM

The Music Library was a quiet, wood-paneled sanctuary on the second floor of the Fine Arts Center. While the practice rooms were for noise, this room was for silence. It was filled with students studying scores, writing papers, and hiding from the chaos of the first day.

Stella and Olivia had claimed a large, rectangular table near the window.

Stella: Had her laptop open, coding a C# backend for a new e-commerce client. Her fingers flew across the keys with the same precision Luna used on her flute.

Olivia: Was reading her Urban Sociology textbook, highlighting passages about zoning laws.

The double doors opened.

Leo rolled in, flanked by Sarah and Amanda. They looked like a flight squadron returning from a mission. They were still wearing their "shop energy"—alert, focused, and slightly smelling of ozone and metal.

Leo spotted the table. He rolled straight to Olivia.

He didn't type. He just reached out.

Olivia put down her highlighter. She leaned forward. Leo wrapped his arms around her waist, burying his face in her sweater for a second. It was a grounding hug—a quick reset after a long day of sensory input.

Sarah and Amanda stopped a respectful distance away. They watched the interaction. They saw the way Olivia held him—protective but equal. They saw the silver rings flash.

They exchanged a look. Understood. He is safe here.

Leo pulled back. He looked at Stella. He offered her a fist bump.

"MISSION SUCCESS," he typed on his Scribe. "THE M.U.L.E. IS DESIGNED."

"Good," Stella said, not looking up from her code but smiling. "Did you measure twice?"

"MEASURED THREE TIMES," Leo typed. "CUTTING THURSDAY."

He gestured to the empty chairs. Sarah and Amanda sat down.

"Hi," Sarah said, a little shyly in front of the 'General.' "We didn't want to interrupt, but Leo said this was the rendezvous point."

"It is," Olivia said warmly. "Pull up a chair. We're just waiting for the Principal Flute to finish holding court."

Amanda unpacked her laptop. "We need to run some stress simulations on the bracket design. Leo thinks the cantilever might flex under dynamic load."

Stella stopped typing. She looked at Amanda.

"Dynamic load?" Stella asked. "Like when he hits a curb at 6 MPH?"

"Exactly," Amanda said seriously. "We're factoring in a 2G impact force. We don't want his laptop to become a projectile."

Stella nodded approval. "Good. Over-engineer it."

Sarah turned to Olivia. She hesitated, then pulled out her FR-Q 101 syllabus.

"So," Sarah said. "About French class tomorrow. Leo said you're... fluent?"

"Native," Olivia corrected. "Born in Sherbrooke, raised in Stanstead."

"Right," Sarah said. "My grandmother was Acadian, but she spoke... differently. Fast. And she used words I never saw in high school French. Like... 'Aweille'?"

Olivia laughed. "That means 'Hurry up' or 'Let's go.' It comes from envoye."

"Oh," Sarah breathed. "That makes sense. She used to yell it at me when I was slow getting my shoes on."

"If you want," Olivia offered, closing her sociology book, "we can do a quick primer before tomorrow. Gagnon is going to test us on greetings."

"Please," Sarah said, opening her notebook. "I don't want to look like an idiot."

"Okay," Olivia said, switching to her teacher mode. "First rule: Forget 'Bonjour, comment allez-vous?' That's for tourists. In the Townships, you say: 'Salut, ça va bien?'"

As Olivia taught Sarah the nuances of the dipthong, and Amanda and Leo debated aluminum grades on their screens, Stella watched them.

The table was full. The work was happening. The connections were being made.

She looked at the door. Luna would be here any minute, flushed with victory from the rehearsal.

The Fortress was working. It wasn't just a wall anymore; it was a hub.

"DATA SYNC," Leo typed, showing Amanda his screen.

"Solid," Amanda noted. "Let's run the render."

Stella went back to her C# code. The family was safe. The semester was launched.
