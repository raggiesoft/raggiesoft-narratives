# Part 3: The Vivaldi

## 08:15 PM Program Item 3: Concerto No. 4 in F Minor, "Winter" Movement II: Largo Soloist: Luna Porter

Dr. Sterling lowered her baton. The band fell silent.

The piece began with the gentle, pizzicato-like raindrops of the clarinets and marimbas (played by Leo’s percussion friends).

Then, the melody entered.

Luna raised the Orpheus. She didn't force the sound. She let it float.

Daaa... da-da-da... daaa...

Vivaldi’s Largo is one of the most beautiful melodies ever written. It evokes the feeling of sitting by a warm fire while watching the rain fall outside. It requires a tone that is warm, comforting, and endless.

Luna played. Her vibrato was shimmering—not fast and nervous, but slow and wide. The silver flute resonated with a dark, woody timbre that filled the massive hall.

She reached the long phrases. The audience held its breath, waiting for her to gasp for air.

She didn't.

She played through the phrase, turning the corner into the next measure without a break in the sound. Her "Tenor Sax Lungs" were powering the flute with hydraulic pressure.

In the box, Leo closed his eyes.

He knew this song. It was the classical version of "The Stardust Engine." It was the sound of safety.

He tapped his hand on his armrest. Tap. Tap. Tap.

I hear you.

On stage, Luna felt the connection. She wasn't playing for the Dean. She wasn't playing for the grade. She was playing for the boy in the box who needed the signal to know he was safe.

She hit the final note—a long, tapering F natural that faded into nothingness.

She lowered the flute.

For three seconds, there was absolute silence in the hall. The "Holy Silence."

Then, the applause broke. It wasn't polite golf claps. It was a roar.

"Bravo!" someone shouted from the balcony.

Dr. Sterling stepped down from the podium. She didn't take a bow. She gestured to Luna.

Luna stood up. She bowed. She looked up at Box 4. She saw Leo waving his hand. She saw Stella beaming. She saw Eleanor dabbing her eyes.

She sat back down, her face flushed.

Jenna leaned over. "Show off."

"I learned from the best," Luna whispered back.
