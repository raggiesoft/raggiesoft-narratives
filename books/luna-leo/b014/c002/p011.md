# Part 11: The Plastic Anchor

## December 18, 2006 Rhode Island DMV - Oakhaven Branch 03:45 PM

"Transaction?" Brenda asked.

"New License," Stella said. "And a legal name change."

Stella slid the paperwork across the counter:

1.  **The Court Order** (Signed by Judge Miller). *Sarah Halloway to Maya Steele Porter.*

2.  **The Old ID** (Ohio Driver’s License).

3.  **The Birth Certificate** (US Citizen).

4.  **The Residency Proof** (Affidavit from Stella + Deed).

Brenda reviewed the Ohio license. "You drive?"

"Since I was sixteen," Maya said softly. "He insisted on it. Part of the cover."

"Well, Ohio reciprocates with Rhode Island," Brenda said, punching the hole in the old card. "No road test required. Just vision."

Maya looked into the machine. She read the letters.

"Okay, Maya," Brenda said. "Stand on the line. Look at the blue dot. Don't smile."

Maya stood. She touched her new, sharp black hair. She looked at the camera. She didn't smile, but her eyes were fierce. She looked like someone who had taken the wheel of her own life.

*FLASH.*

## The Production

The machine behind the counter whirred. A moment later, a warm piece of plastic dropped into the tray.

Brenda handed it over.

RHODE ISLAND DRIVER'S LICENSE

NAME: PORTER, MAYA STEELE

ADDRESS: 8 Ocean View LN, IRON POINT, RI 02840

DOB: 04/12/1988

HAIR: BLK EYES: HAZ

CLASS: D (Operator)

Maya held it. It wasn't just an ID; it was a license to move.

"Thank you," Maya whispered.

"Welcome to Rhode Island," Brenda said. "Watch out for the rotaries."

**The Exit**

They walked out to the parking lot.

Leo rolled up to her. He looked at the card.

*"LICENSE VERIFIED,"* he typed. *"YOU ARE A PILOT."*

"I can drive," Maya realized, a slow smile spreading across her face. "I can drive us to the beach. I can drive us to the store."

"You can drive the Sentinel," Stella promised, handing her the keys just to hold for a moment. "Once you get used to the snow tires."

Maya clutched the keys and the license. She had a name. She had a home. And she had the keys to the tank.

"Let's go home," Maya said. "I'm starving."

"Clam chowder," Stella said. "Clear."

"Clear," Maya agreed.
