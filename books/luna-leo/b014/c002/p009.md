# Part 9: The Identity Crisis

##### December 18, 2006 Iron County Superior Court - Witness Room 10:30 AM

The hearing was over. Viktor Krov was in federal custody.

Sarah sat at a small table in the witness room. Stella and Leo were with her.

**Special Agent Ross** entered. He wasn't wearing his raid jacket anymore; he was in a suit. He carried a thick file.

"Ms... Halloway," Agent Ross said, hesitating on the name. "We need to talk."

"Is my name even Halloway?" Sarah asked, her voice dull.

"Legally? Yes," Agent Ross said, sitting down. "Your birth certificate says **Sarah Emily Halloway**, born in Cincinnati, Ohio. Your mother was **Katherine Miller** (no relation to the Judge), a US Citizen. That makes you a US Citizen by birth and by blood. You are American. You are safe."

Sarah let out a breath. "Okay. So I'm not a spy."

"No," Ross said. "You're a cover story. Your father—Colonel Viktor Krov—entered the US in 1984 using a stolen identity. He married your mother to cement his cover. He had a child—you—to look like a family man."

Sarah flinched. "He... he had me to be a prop?"

"He had you to be a shield," Ross corrected gently. "But that doesn't mean your mother didn't love you. She was a victim, too. We believe she discovered his identity three years ago. That’s likely why she died."

"He said it was cancer," Sarah whispered.

"We are reopening the autopsy," Ross said grimly. "If we find evidence of poisoning... the charges will escalate from Espionage to Capital Murder."

Sarah stared at the table. The monster wasn't just mean; he was a killer.

"What happens to his money?" Stella asked. "The tuition? The house?"

"The assets are frozen," Ross said. "The government is seizing everything connected to the Halloway identity under the **RICO Act** and **Espionage Act**. The bank accounts, the house in Ohio, the cars. It’s all evidence."

Sarah looked up. "So I have nothing? I can't pay for school?"

"You have something better," Stella interjected. "You have a claim."

She looked at Agent Ross.

"She is a victim of a federal crime," Stella said. "Under the **Victims of Crime Act**, she is entitled to restitution. And until then..."

Stella put her hand on Sarah’s shoulder.

"We cover the gap. You are living in the Fortress. I am paying your tuition. We will file for financial aid as an 'Independent Student' now that your father is incarcerated. You aren't dropping out."

Agent Ross nodded. "Your friend is right. The government will eventually release funds for you, but it will take time. In the meantime, Sarah... I suggest you change your name. 'Halloway' is about to be on every news channel in the world."

Sarah looked at Leo. Leo was watching her, his face calm and steady.

He tapped his **Quantum Communicator**.

*"NAME IS JUST DATA,"* he typed. *"YOU ARE THE HARDWARE. THE HARDWARE IS GOOD."*

Sarah managed a weak smile. "Hardware is good."

"Take your time," Stella said. "Pick a new name. Pick a new life. You get to choose this time."

Sarah looked at the file Agent Ross was holding. It contained the lies of her father.

Then she looked at the Fortress crew. The people who had saved her.

"I don't know who I am yet," Sarah whispered. "But I know where I'm staying."

*"YOU ARE STAYING HERE,"* Leo typed. *"IN IRON POINT."*

"Exactly," Stella said. "Let's go home. We have a guest room with your name on it. Whatever name that is."
