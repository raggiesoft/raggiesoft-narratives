# Part 6: An Impromptu Recital

After the magnificent American Christmas dinner, the entire, sprawling family retired to the living room at Arthur and Eleanor's house. The atmosphere was warm and deeply contented, a happy mix of full stomachs and the quiet joy of being together.

As the adults chatted, Olivia's cousin Chloé, who had been sitting with Luna, turned to her with a curious smile. "So, Olivia tells us you have been practicing your French," she said, her English still carrying the soft, musical lilt of Québec. "How are the lessons coming along?"

"They're so much fun!" Luna said immediately. She looked at Stella and Leo, a mischievous sparkle in her eye. "We've been practicing a lot. Want to see?"

This drew the attention of the whole room. Tante Isabelle and Oncle Mark smiled encouragingly. "But of course!" Isabelle said. "We would love to see your progress!"

What followed was less of a surprise and more of an impromptu, informal skills check. Olivia, acting as the proud prompter, would give them a cue, and they would respond with the phrases she had taught them.

"Stella," Olivia prompted, "how would you say it's a pleasure to have them here?"

Stella, ever the diligent student, smiled and replied with carefully practiced pronunciation, "*C'est un plaisir de vous avoir ici.*"

The Canadian family applauded warmly. "Très bien, Stella!" Oncle Mark praised.

Next, it was Luna's turn. "Luna, what did you think of the turkey?"

"*Le dindon était délicieux!*" Luna declared with a flourish.

Then, all eyes turned to Leo. He looked at his new extended family, his expression open and happy. He lifted his MagnaByte Scribe and typed, the robotic voice clear and steady.

"NOUS SOMMES TRÈS CONTENTS QUE VOUS SOYEZ ICI. MERCI BEAUCOUP." (We are very happy that you are here. Thank you very much.)

The sincerity and warmth of his message, delivered with the help of his device, was deeply touching. The Canadian family was not just impressed; they were visibly moved and incredibly proud of how quickly and enthusiastically the Porter siblings had embraced their language and, by extension, them.

"You are all learning so fast!" Tante Isabelle said, beaming. "Olivia is a very good teacher."

Olivia simply smiled, her heart full. Watching her two families connect so beautifully, through the language of her home, was the greatest Christmas gift she could have ever asked for.
