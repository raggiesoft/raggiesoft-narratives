# Part 10: Mon Chum, Ma Blonde

The moment they got home from Cardinal Foods, the forced composure Leo had maintained shattered. This rude woman had been a deeply rattling experience. He was trembling, his breathing shallow.

While Stella and Luna quickly and quietly put the groceries away, Olivia guided Leo to the sofa. "Come on," she whispered, settling beside him and pulling him into a secure cuddle. "I've got you. You're safe. She's not here."

She began to murmur soft, calming words in her heart language, the gentle cadence of Québécois French a soothing balm. In that moment, holding him, protecting him from the ghosts of his past, a profound clarity settled over her. The words she chose were no longer just those of a friend.

"*C'est correct, mon chum... Je suis là,*" she whispered into his hair. (It's okay, my boyfriend... I'm here.)

She continued to murmur the term, a word of deep, possessive, and protective affection. She saw Leo relax under her touch, but she also saw the slight furrow in his brow. He knew her tone was comforting, but he didn't understand the specific, powerful word she was using for him.

She pulled back just enough to look at him. "Do you know what *'mon chum'* means?" she asked softly in English.

He shook his head, then typed on his Scribe: "NO. WHAT?"

"In Québec," she explained, her voice gentle, "it's what a girl calls her boyfriend. Her guy. The most important one."

Leo stared at her, the meaning of her words sinking in. She wasn't just his best friend. She saw him as her *chum*. Her boyfriend. A warmth spread through his chest, pushing back the last of the fear. He liked that. He liked that very much. He typed again, a hopeful question on the screen.

"HOW DO I SAY GIRLFRIEND? FOR YOU?"

Olivia's heart melted. "*Ma blonde*," she said, her voice thick with emotion. "*Tu es mon chum, et je suis ta blonde.*" (You are my boyfriend, and I am your girlfriend.)

Just then, Stella and Luna finished with the groceries and came into the living room, sensing the quiet, intense mood. "What was today's French lesson?" Luna asked with a soft smile.

Olivia looked at Stella, then back at Leo, who was still holding her hand tightly. "Today's lesson was *mon chum* and *ma blonde*," she said simply.

Stella looked at them, curled together on the sofa, and a deep, knowing smile touched her lips. There was no alarm, no concern. It was just... right. "Okay," she said, her voice full of warmth and complete acceptance. "I'm okay with that. We're all okay with that."

As if on cue, the mail carrier's footsteps were heard on the porch, followed by the familiar clink of the mail slot. Stella went to retrieve it, shuffling through the envelopes. She stopped, holding up a thick manila envelope from Sarah Jenkins's law office.

"Well," she said, her voice filled with a strange sense of perfect timing. "Speak of the devil."

She brought the envelope over to the sofa. "Liv," she said, her expression now serious and full of profound trust. "This is the paperwork. The petition to add you as a co-guardian and co-conservator for Luna and Leo, with jointly and severally authorizations." She held out the papers. "Are you ready for this? For all of it?"

Olivia, still holding Leo in a protective cuddle that felt more right than anything ever had, looked up at Stella, her gaze fierce and unwavering.

"Yes," she said, her voice clear and strong. "I'm ready."

With Leo nestled securely at her side, she took the pen from Stella, and the two women began to fill out their respective parts of the paperwork, officially and legally cementing the fortress that had already been built in their hearts.
