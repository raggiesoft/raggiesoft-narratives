# Part 14: The Fortress Suite

After the introductions were made and the happy chaos began to settle, Arthur clapped his hands together, getting everyone's attention. "Alright, one last thing," he said, a mischievous twinkle in his eye. He walked over to a grand, unmarked set of double doors at the end of the main living area of the suite.

"We told you we booked the Presidential Suite," he said, "but we may have left out one small detail."

With a theatrical flourish, he pushed both doors open, revealing another, even more luxurious, private wing of the suite that had previously been locked. This new space was a massive, beautifully appointed bedroom, but its centerpiece was what drew everyone's breath away: an enormous, impossibly large bed.

"Is that...?" Stella began, though she already knew the answer, a grin spreading across her face.

"That," Arthur announced proudly, "is an Alaska King bed. Nine feet by nine feet. Your grandmother and I found it when we secretly toured the hotel a few months ago to plan this little operation. We knew it was perfect."

Olivia and Leo stared, completely speechless. While Stella and Luna were in on the secret, it was a complete surprise to them.

Arthur's gaze softened as he looked at Leo. "After that nightmare bus trip, son," he said, his voice full of a grandfather's love, "we decided you deserved a bit of pampering. A proper vacation. Your grandmother and I will be staying at Stella's house, just to keep an eye on things and make it look occupied. This," he said, gesturing to the grand room, "is for you four. Your own private fortress for the holiday."

The plan was a masterstroke of love and foresight. It was a way for Leo to have a true vacation, a change of scenery, without ever having to be separated from his support system.

Leo looked at the massive bed, then at Stella, Luna, and Olivia. The idea of all four of them, his entire fortress, sharing this luxurious, safe space together was a gift of such profound understanding and love that it left him breathless. He wouldn't have to worry about nightmares. He wouldn't have to be afraid. He would be surrounded.

Olivia, equally moved by the immense generosity, simply whispered, "Arthur, it's... perfect."

The fortress now had its own holiday headquarters, a grand and beautiful sanctuary where they could celebrate and heal, together.
