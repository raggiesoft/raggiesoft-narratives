# Part 4: Water and Trust

The quiet of the living room on the evening of December 30th was a gentle, healing balm. Held securely between Luna and Olivia on the sofa, Leo felt the exhaustion of the past week finally begin to recede. The constant social energy of the family gathering, while wonderful, had left his muscles tight and achy, a common side effect of his cerebral palsy when he was tired or stressed. The warmth of his sisters' embrace helped, but he knew what would truly ease the painful spasticity.

He shifted slightly and reached for his **Quantum Communicator**, which was on the cushion beside him. He typed a short, simple request.

"CAN I HAVE A BATH? MY MUSCLES HURT."

Instantly, the atmosphere in the room shifted from quiet relaxation to gentle, purposeful action. For the fortress, this was a familiar and important part of his care routine.

"Of course, buddy," Stella said immediately, getting up from her armchair. "Let's get the water running."

Preparing Leo's bath was a coordinated, practiced effort that the three of them had perfected. It was a testament to their teamwork and the deep, unspoken trust between them. Stella went into the accessible bathroom first, starting the warm water and ensuring the temperature was just right—warm enough to soothe his muscles but not too hot. She laid out his large, soft towels and clean pajamas.

Back in the living room, Olivia and Luna helped Leo. "Okay, *mon chum*," Olivia said softly, "Let's get you ready."

The process of getting him to the bathroom and into the tub required all three of them. Olivia, with her height and strength, was the primary support, her movements sure and steady. "Ready, Leo? On three," she'd say, as she and Stella worked together to safely transfer him from his wheelchair. Luna's role was one of constant, direct comfort. She held his hand, her presence a steady anchor, talking to him in a low, soothing voice, her descriptions of their quiet day a gentle distraction.

Once he was settled in the tub, the warm water began to work its magic. A long, relieved sigh escaped Leo as he felt the tight, painful knots in his shoulders and legs begin to soften. This wasn't about being "dirty"; this was about therapy. It was about finding relief from the constant, exhausting battle his body waged against itself.

The three women worked with a quiet, clinical efficiency born of love. Luna gently washed his hair, her touch careful and familiar. Stella and Olivia were there for support, ensuring he was stable and comfortable, their conversation light and easy. For them, this was not an awkward or sexualized moment in any way. It was an act of care, as normal and necessary as helping him eat or get dressed. It was family. It was trust made manifest.

After the bath, wrapped in a warm towel, the tension had visibly drained from his body. They helped him into his pajamas and back into his wheelchair. He felt relaxed, his muscles finally at ease, a deep, bone-deep comfort settling over him.

He typed one last message on his communicator before they wheeled him to his bedroom.

"THANK YOU. I FEEL BETTER."

Stella smiled, stroking his damp hair. "Anytime, buddy," she said softly. "That's what we're here for."
