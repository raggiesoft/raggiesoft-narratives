# Part 7: Kent?

Sleep offers little respite. Sometime in the middle of the night, the gentle undulation of the waterbed beneath him begins to feel wrong, subtly shifting, becoming too stiff, unfamiliar. The comforting darkness around him seems to condense, the air growing thick and heavy. He feels a flicker of disorientation – is he still in Stella's room? No, the walls feel closer now, the air tasting stale, reminiscent of the oppressive Portsmouth apartment he shared with Delores. Panic begins its insidious crawl up his spine. Then the scene shifts again, the stale air replaced by a cloying floral perfume, unnaturally warm and stuffy. The walls press closer still – the narrow, confining space of the guest room in Kent. Carol Mahone's house. He’s back. Trapped. But it warps even further, reality twisting under some unseen, evil force. The simple bedroom walls ripple and solidify into damp, cold stone. The carpet vanishes beneath him, replaced by uneven, chilling flagstones that glisten wetly as water drips almost constantly from unseen sources. The ceiling presses down, visibly lowering, transforming into oppressive, dark rock studded with menacing spikes, like some kind of horrific trap room. It’s the same layout as the guest room, but rendered as a terrifyingly evil stone dungeon. Leo shrinks back, afraid of the dampness seeping from the walls, the cold radiating from the floor, the threat implicit in the spiked ceiling above. Alone. Exposed. Something evil is definitely at work here.

A shadow detaches itself from the corner of this stone cell, coalescing into a figure terrible to behold. He recognizes the face as Delores, but it's wrong, distorted by a chilling, palpable evil, like some witch of darkness. The air crackles with malevolent energy radiating from her. The instant his mind registers *her* in this terrifying space, cold, heavy chains erupt from the very shadows she emerged from, twisting around his wrists, pinning his arms tight against the damp stone wall. More chains snake around his ankles, anchoring his legs immovably to the floor. A heavy shackle clicks shut around his neck – not choking, but confining, pressing uncomfortably. The phantom ache of the pressure sores on his hips flares, adding physical torment to the paralyzing fear. He is utterly, terrifyingly trapped, rendered completely powerless by her mere presence.

The twisted visage of Delores drifts closer, her voice a venomous hiss that seems to echo from the cold stone walls. "You thought you could leave? How dare you disobey me! How DARE you choose them!"

He tries to move, tries to scream, tries to find his voice – but the chains hold fast, and only a choked silence emerges. She advances slowly, the rage melting into a chillingly calm, possessive smile that promises something far worse than shouting. "You belong to me," she whispers, leaning closer, her eyes empty voids reflecting pure darkness. "And now..."

As she speaks, the chains binding his wrists and ankles seem to tighten infinitesimally at her unspoken command, the metal biting harder, the neck shackle pressing firmly, emphasizing his complete entrapment. What is she going to do? The unknown horror tightens around his chest, stealing his breath. He squeezes his eyes shut, bracing, a silent scream building—

Suddenly, amidst the terror, an ethereal voice seems to echo from the stone walls all around him, calling his name: *"...Leo...?"* At the same time, something starts poking insistently at his shoulder – a strange, physical sensation intruding into the horror. He tries to ignore it, shrinking deeper into the fear, but the voice comes again, louder this time, sharper, strangely familiar: *"Leo...!"* The prodding continues, gentle but persistent. He hears his name a third time, cutting through the oppressive darkness with undeniable clarity – it's *Luna!*

The sudden, violent movement woke Luna instantly. She didn't startle or panic; she simply transitioned from deep sleep to a state of calm, focused alertness. She recognized the signs immediately: the specific, frantic kicking, the choked, terrified sounds. This was one of Leo’s deep terrors, the ones rooted in the helplessness he’d felt with Delores.

Olivia was woken a second later by the commotion, her heart lurching with alarm. She propped herself up on an elbow, her eyes struggling to adjust to the dim light. "Leo? What's wrong?" she whispered urgently.

But what she saw next made her freeze in confusion. In a fluid, practiced motion, Luna was already moving. She gently but firmly rolled the thrashing Leo onto his stomach. For a brief, terrifying moment, Olivia's mind couldn't process what was happening. She saw Luna climb on top of her struggling brother, and an initial spike of alarm shot through her. *What is she doing? Is she hurting him?*

Then she saw it. Luna wasn't fighting him; she was enveloping him. She wrapped her arms securely around his torso and her legs around his, a human weighted blanket designed to provide deep, calming pressure. Leo struggled against the restraint for a moment, his nightmare-addled mind not registering the comfort, but Luna held firm, her breathing slow and steady, a silent, rhythmic reassurance. She would do whatever was needed—restrain, massage, even a startling pressure to his chest if necessary—to pull him back.

It was in that moment that Olivia understood. This wasn't a struggle. It was a practiced, deeply intimate act of care. This was a non-verbal language of safety she had never seen before, a technique perfected over countless dark nights. She watched, mesmerized, as the frantic tension slowly drained from Leo's muscles. His thrashing ceased, replaced by a deep, shuddering sigh as his conscious mind, anchored by the familiar, safe weight of his twin, was pulled back from the nightmare.

Luna, however, did not let go immediately. She remained, a steady, anchoring presence, first to verify that he was truly back, and then for a few moments longer, simply to enjoy the hug for what it now was: a grounding, safe, and deeply loving embrace. Only when she was satisfied did she release her hold and shift back to her side of the bed.

Olivia looked on, a profound sense of awe washing over her. She had just witnessed the deepest, most sacred part of the twins' bond. She had always seen herself as Leo's protector, but in that moment, she understood that Luna and Leo had their own ancient language of safety, a way of grounding each other that had been perfected long before she had ever entered their lives.

Leo's eyes flew open, a choked gasp tearing from his throat, the menacing stone dungeon and Delores's face vanishing instantly. He was disoriented for a second by the heavy, weighted feeling on his back—it was Luna. But the terror clung to him, sickeningly real, the memories of the past week—Kent, the bus ride, the flight home—flashing vividly through his mind. His body was drenched in a heavy sweat, his heart hammered against his ribs, and the phantom feel of cold chains still seemed to bite at his wrists. He was trembling uncontrollably, hot tears streaming down his face, blurring the safe reality around him. Was this real? Or was that—the dungeon, the abandonment—the horrifying truth, and this warmth, this safety, just a fleeting dream? He was crying, utterly terrified and confused.

"Leo? Hey!" It was Olivia, her voice sharp with alertness now that he was awake, leaning over him, her face etched with concern. "You're okay! You're awake!"

He flinched violently at her voice, still caught between realities. As he began to register her, solid and real beside him, Luna, feeling the shift as he returned to the present, slowly and gently released her hold, shifting off of him and back to her side of the bed. "Leo? We saw you shaking," she whispered, her voice full of worried warmth.

The sudden release of pressure and the two voices on either side of him finally anchored him. He reached out desperately, fumbling for Luna’s AAC device on the nightstand. Olivia understood immediately and placed it in his trembling hands. A broken sob tore through him as he typed, needing confirmation, needing anchoring: WAS THAT REAL? KENT? BUS? PLANE? IS THIS THE DREAM???

"Shhh, shhh!" Olivia reacted immediately, reading the terrified question as she wrapped her arms around him, hugging him as tightly as she could, pulling him close against her. Her own fear for him was palpable in the strength of her embrace. "No, Leo, no! *This* is real! You're here! You're safe!" she murmured fiercely into his hair, her voice thick with sleep but fiercely protective. "That was the nightmare! Just a bad dream! She's not here!". Stella reached across Leo from the other side, placing a steady, grounding hand on his back. "Olivia's right, Leo. This is real. You're home. You're safe. We're right here," Stella added softly, her voice a calm anchor. "You're safe, Leo, you're safe!".

Olivia held him tight, rocking him gently on the undulating mattress, trying to absorb his trembling into her own body. Hearing Olivia and Stella insist this was real, feeling their solid presence, he clung to his best friend, utterly spent, the terror slowly receding, replaced by the profound, grounding reality of his sisters and his best friend surrounding him in the safe darkness of Stella's room.

After a few more minutes, when his breathing had steadied, Leo gently shifted, reaching for the AAC device again. Cradled securely between Olivia and Stella, he began to type, needing to share the horror now that he knew it wasn't real. His fingers moved slowly as he recounted the nightmare's terrifying progression: ROOM CHANGED. PORTSMOUTH. THEN KENT. THEN... DUNGEON. STONE. WET. COLD. SPIKES ON CEILING. SHE CAME FROM SHADOW. FACE WRONG. EVIL. CHAINS GRABBED ME. ARMS, LEGS, NECK. SHE SAID... 'BELONG TO ME'... THEN 'AND NOW'... CHAINS GOT TIGHTER... SO SCARED... .

Stella, Luna, and Olivia read the messages, their faces paling with shared horror. They held him tighter, murmuring fresh reassurances. "Oh, Leo, that sounds absolutely terrifying," Stella whispered, stroking his hair. "And yes, some of that fear comes from what really happened—she *did* leave you stranded out there in Washington, that part was horribly real. But look around now". Stella gently tilted his chin up. "THIS is real. You are SAFELY HOME in VIRGINIA, with us, where you BELONG. That nightmare was just twisting the terrible memory. She's miles away, and she is never, ever getting near you again". Luna leaned over Stella to gently touch his arm, needing to offer comfort after hearing the details.

"And hey," Stella added softly, "after that crazy early start yesterday, there's absolutely no rush to get up in the morning, okay? We sleep in tomorrow. No alarms". Sharing the nightmare, voicing the specifics in the safety of their presence, seemed to lance the boil of fear, allowing the last vestiges of the terror to finally dissipate, leaving only exhaustion and the deep, unwavering security of being held by those who loved him most. They settled back down on the waterbed, the three women holding Leo close, hoping the remainder of the night would bring only peaceful, dreamless sleep.
