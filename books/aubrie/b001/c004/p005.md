# Part 5: Logistics of a Co-Ed Suite – 3:30 PM

The practicalities of their arrangement soon came to the forefront. It was Lauren who brought it up. "Okay, so... logistics," she said, gesturing between the two rooms and the single connecting toilet. "Six people, one toilet, and no privacy. Jordan, you were expecting guy roommates, and we were expecting another girl. How do we make this not... super awkward?"

There was a beat of silence before Madison shrugged, a pragmatic look on her face. "Look, we're all adults. We have one toilet. The doors connect. There's no room to be shy. I say we just agree that what happens in the suite, stays in the suite. No need to hide, no need to turn away. There's literally no room for privacy, so let's not pretend there is."

A surprising murmur of agreement went through the group. It was a radical solution born of necessity, but it felt right.

As the clock neared 4:00 PM, the five women decided to head to the dining hall for their first official college meal. They were gathering their student IDs when Zoë noticed Jordan sitting quietly by his desk. He looked small and isolated. She also noticed the subtle, practiced way he had his hand cupped slightly behind his ear, straining to catch their words over the ambient noise drifting in from the hallway.

"Are you hearing impaired, by chance?" Zoë asked aloud, her crisp British accent cutting through the room's chatter. "The data suggests you're relying heavily on acoustic redirection."

Before he could process the blunt but accurate observation, a wave of collective understanding passed between the girls. They recognized the look of someone who was used to being left out.

"Hey, Jordan," Lauren said kindly, naturally raising her volume just a fraction to accommodate him. "We're all going to dinner. You should come with us."

Jordan looked up, nervous and surprised. He quickly hauled his heavy laptop onto his lap and popped the lid open. He hunched uncomfortably close to the screen, squinting intensely as his fingers found the keys to type into his *SimpleSpeak* software. He hit the execute key.

*Oh. You do not have to...* the flat, digitized voice announced to the room.

"We want to," Aubrie insisted, completely unfazed by the software as she already moved to position herself behind his wheelchair. "You're our suitemate. Our family, for the next two weeks at least. We stick together."

A genuine, wide smile spread across Jordan's face.

As the six of them filed out of Room 214 and into the corridor together, they were a strange and motley crew. The freshmen and parents who had witnessed the name card change earlier were still milling about, breaking down cardboard boxes. Whispers immediately flared up again.

"Wait, so he is really living with them?" a guy leaning against a doorway muttered. "A guy living in a suite full of girls?"

His roommate paused, looking at the heavy wooden door to 214 as it swung shut. He pointed at the center of the door. "Look at the peepholes. There are two of them. One up high, and one down low."

The realization rippled through the small group of onlookers. It was the handicap-accessible suite. The guy in the wheelchair belonged there because he needed the modified room, even if Housing had clearly messed up the gender assignments.

The bystanders watched as Aubrie confidently took the handles of Jordan's wheelchair, pushing him toward the elevators while Madison and Zoë flanked them, chatting effortlessly. The girls seemed completely unbothered by the presence of a guy in their suite. Seeing the easy camaraderie among the six of them, the whispers in the hallway began to die down. The scandal evaporated into simple fact: it was an unusual arrangement, but the six of them were clearly okay with it.

In the bustling cafeteria, they found a large table and sat together, sharing stories about their hometowns and their anxieties about starting college. For the first time, Jordan didn't feel like an outsider; he felt like a friend.
