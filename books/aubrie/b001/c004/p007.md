By the time dinner finished, the emotional and physical toll of the long, chaotic day had caught up with Jordan. His shoulders slumped, and he quietly admitted through his text-to-speech software that he was exhausted and just wanted to head back to the quiet of the dorm.

The group moved as a unit back to Bennett Hall. As they reached the second floor, Mark paused outside the suite doors. With polite respect for their shared space, he looked at Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison.

"Mind if we all hang out in 214 for a bit?"

Jordan gave a welcoming nod, using his Poly Pass to trigger the automatic door.

The seven of them filed into the cramped room. Mark, Hannah, Lauren, and Zoë found spots on the floor and the desk chairs, while Jordan wheeled himself toward his bed.

A sudden, urgent pressure hit Jordan. A sudden flush of embarrassment creeping up his neck, he looked toward the connecting bathroom door. He hurriedly pulled his heavy laptop onto his lap, popping the lid open to type out a request for assistance.

He got as far as typing *I need to use—* before the screen flickered, froze, and went completely black. The ruined battery had died yet again.

Jordan froze, panic and mortification flooding his face. He gestured helplessly toward the dead machine and then pointed toward the bathroom door, completely unable to voice his urgent need.

Aubrie, whose eyes had barely left him since they sat down at dinner, read his distressed body language instantly. A sudden, protective flutter sparked in her chest as she realized he was trapped without his voice.

"I'll help!" Aubrie blurted out.

The words left her mouth a fraction of a second too quickly. She scrambled to her feet, her cheeks instantly blooming into a deep, vivid shade of crimson.

A beat of total silence fell over the room. Madison's eyebrows shot up. Lauren suppressed a smile, and Mark let out a quiet, amused chuckle.

Aubrie, refusing to make eye contact with anyone in the room, kept her gaze fixed firmly on the floor as she hurriedly grasped the handles of Jordan's wheelchair. "Let's go," she muttered, quickly steering him into the connecting ADA-compliant bathroom and shutting the door behind them.

Inside the small room, Aubrie took a deep breath, forcing her blush to recede as she shifted into a mode of practical, judgment-free care.

But as she turned to face him, Jordan was completely frozen, his knuckles white as he gripped his armrests. He had never, in his entire life, allowed anyone other than his mother to assist him with this. He was bracing himself for what he had always known: a sudden, dictatorial loss of agency where his body was handled without warning, dignity, or permission. Because he had never been taught the actual mechanics of his own care, he had absolutely no idea how to guide Aubrie, and without his laptop, he couldn't even attempt to explain his needs.

Aubrie, despite her racing pulse and the undeniable butterflies swirling in her stomach, saw the sheer, paralyzing terror in his eyes. She realized instantly that neither of them had a script for this.

"Okay, Jordan," Aubrie whispered softly. She knelt down so she was perfectly at eye-level, refusing to loom over him. "I've never done this before, and I know you can't speak right now. So we're going to figure this out together. Just nod or shake your head for me, okay?"

He swallowed hard, his breathing shallow, and gave a small, shaky nod.

Instead of just reaching for his waistband, she pointed to it. "Do you want me to unbutton your jeans first?" He nodded. She did it slowly, deliberately, ensuring he was comfortable with every single movement. "Okay. Do you need me to help you pivot to the grab bars, or just support your waist?" She pantomimed the motions, giving him the power to choose.

It was a painstakingly careful process of trial and error, but she assisted him with a gentle, clinical respect that completely bypassed the awkwardness of the situation. For Jordan, it was a revelation. She was asking for his consent. She was treating his body with a dignity his mother had never shown him. Aubrie found that she cared far more about protecting his dignity than she did about her own embarrassment.

When the door clicked open a few minutes later and Aubrie wheeled Jordan back into the main room, she was met with a wall of knowing, playful looks.

Madison was leaning against the top bunk, a wide, unmistakable smirk on her face. "Wow, Aubrie. You were certainly eager to volunteer for bathroom duty".

"It's called being a helpful suitemate, Madison," Aubrie said defensively, her face immediately turning bright red all over again. She busied herself with straightening the blanket on Jordan's bed, acting as if it were the most engrossing task in the world.

Zoë adjusted her glasses, her tone perfectly deadpan. "The swiftness of your response time was statistically notable," she observed, her crisp London accent underscoring the dry remark.

Hannah just smiled warmly, watching Aubrie fret over the blanket. The entire room had put two and two together. Aubrie Kelly was falling head over heels in love with the quiet boy in the wheelchair, and even if she wasn't quite ready to admit it out loud yet, her blush had already given her away.
