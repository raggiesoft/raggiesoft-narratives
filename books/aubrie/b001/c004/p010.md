# Part 10: The Antidote – 6:30 PM

With the new laptop safely resting on Jordan's lap, Daniel checked his watch and gave a warm smile. "Right, I should be heading back," he said.

"I'll escort you down to the lobby, Dad," Zoë offered, stepping toward the heavy wooden door. She looked back at Jordan. "I will return shortly to assist you with the rest of the operating system navigation, as you requested."

Mark also took that as his cue to leave. He gave Hannah a quick, affectionate kiss and offered a friendly wave to the room. "See you all tomorrow. Try to get some rest, J."

As Mark headed out into the hallway, Hannah and Lauren gathered their things. With a few cheerful goodnights, they slipped through the connecting ADA-compliant bathroom to retreat to Room 216 for the evening.

A few minutes later, Zoë returned to Room 214. She pulled up the wooden desk chair and sat directly across from Jordan's wheelchair. Aubrie and Madison remained in the room, settling onto the lower bunk and the other desk chair, respectively, offering a quiet, supportive audience.

"Let us review the file hierarchy," Zoë began, her crisp British consonants matching her analytical pace. She reached over to guide the trackpad. "Aura Glade organizes directories slightly differently than Quantum OS. Your root directory is here, and your *OmniDoc Suite* applications are located in this executable path..."

She moved quickly, opening windows and explaining system processes. For Jordan, whose brain was already completely exhausted by the day's massive emotional swings, the sudden influx of technical data was too much. The screen began to blur, and a familiar, suffocating tightness gripped his chest. He froze, his breathing turning shallow as he squeezed his eyes shut, his hands dropping rigidly to his armrests.

Aubrie, who had been watching him closely, recognized the sudden tension instantly. She didn't hesitate. She stepped forward, sliding right between Zoë's chair and his wheelchair, and gently wrapped her arms around his shoulders. She pulled him into a warm, firm, and secure hug.

Jordan instinctively leaned into the embrace, burying his face against her shoulder. He remembered the unconditional hug from this morning, the one that had grounded him during his massive meltdown, and he desperately wanted more of it. As he held onto her, a profound realization washed over him. Her name was Aubrie. His mother’s name was Aubrie. But this Aubrie—Aubrie Kelly—was the absolute antithesis of Aubrie Sinclair. His mother's touch was a cage of control and anxiety; Aubrie Kelly's touch was a shield. She was the perfect, healing antidote to the poison of his past.

He took a long, shaky breath, letting the steady rhythm of her heartbeat calm the static in his mind.

"Apologies," Zoë said softly, recognizing the data overload. "I am delivering the information too rapidly. We will pace it."

As Jordan calmed down, Aubrie slowly pulled back, giving him a warm, reassuring smile. Zoë resumed the lesson, this time moving at a much slower, deliberate speed. She showed him how to open the web browser and navigated directly to the CPI Student Portal.

Jordan squinted at the screen, but the layout of the Aura Glade environment felt remarkably similar to what he was used to. He typed a few keystrokes, navigating the portal with ease. He was catching on quickly, the logical structure of the system appealing to his mind.

After another fifteen minutes of exploration, Zoë stood up and stretched. "The data indicates we have achieved a functional baseline," she announced, stifling a small yawn. "I believe I am ready to retire to my room for the evening. Goodnight, everyone."

With a nod, Zoë disappeared through the connecting bathroom door, leaving Aubrie and Madison alone in Room 214 with Jordan.

It was still early in the evening, but the steady drum of rain against the single window and the sheer exhaustion of the day made the small cinderblock room feel like a quiet, isolated cocoon. There wasn't really anything else to do.

Aubrie and Madison exchanged a quiet, meaningful look over Jordan's head. They both understood the assignment perfectly. They could see the lingering, fragile terror in Jordan's eyes—the deep-seated fear that this entire day, their kindness, and their acceptance was just a cruel, elaborate joke waiting for the punchline.

As they sat with him, keeping the atmosphere light and comfortable, the two women quietly admitted something to themselves. They actually liked having Jordan in their room. It wasn't just because Aubrie was already developing a massive, undeniable crush on him. It was because, stripped of the panic and the fear, he was genuinely easy to get along with. He was a calming, gentle presence, and they were more than happy to share their space with him.
