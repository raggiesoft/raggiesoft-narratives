# Part 6: The First Choice – 4:15 PM

The university dining hall was a cavernous, bustling space filled with the chaotic energy of thousands of new freshmen. The noise and the smell of the food stations could easily be overwhelming, but for Jordan, anchored by the five women walking with him, it felt like an adventure.

As they approached the long, winding serving lines, Jordan paused, trying to figure out the logistics of holding a tray while pushing his wheelchair. He popped open the lid of his heavy laptop to type out a question, hunching low and squinting fiercely at the dim screen just to find the keys.

Aubrie, walking right beside him, watched him struggle. She noted the extreme proximity of his face to the monitor, then watched as he looked up toward the massive, illuminated menus above the different stations. He squinted again, his eyes narrowing to slits as he stared at the glowing, blurry shapes that he assumed were words.

Aubrie instantly stepped to his side. "It's a pain to try and balance a tray in your lap," she said over the din of the cafeteria. "Let me just grab yours for you."

Jordan instinctively cupped his hand behind his ear, straining against the chaotic wall of background noise just to decipher her words.

Aubrie caught the movement. Acting on pure instinct, she leaned in close, cupping her hands around his ear to block out the ambient cafeteria noise. "I can read them to you," she said, her voice suddenly cutting through the static with startling, crisp clarity. "They have pizza, pasta, a deli, and a grill."

Jordan froze, his eyes widening. He had never heard a voice so clearly in a crowded room before. It was a revelation. He looked back toward the blurry menus, completely overwhelmed by the choices. His entire life, food had been a simple, non-negotiable command. His mother made something, and he ate it. He was never asked what he wanted.

He turned back to his laptop, squinting hard as he typed into *SimpleSpeak*. He clicked the execute key.

*Can I... can I have the grill? A cheeseburger? And... fries?* the robotic voice asked, sounding small but hopeful. His mother never allowed such things.

"A burger? Heck yeah. Good choice," Aubrie beamed, not blinking an eye at the synthesized voice.

As they waited in the grill line, Aubrie gently kept her hands cupped near his ear. "Jordan, do you have trouble seeing and hearing? I noticed you squinting a lot, and you always cup your ear."

Jordan hunched over his laptop and typed. He clicked play. *The world is just fuzzy and muffled. Isn't it like that for everyone?*

Aubrie frowned slightly, her heart aching for him as she realized he genuinely didn't know he had profound sensory deficits. "I don't think so," she said gently.

Jordan typed again. *I have a meeting with Educational Accessibility on Monday at 1:00 PM to finalize my academic accommodations.*

"You should definitely bring your vision and hearing up with them," Aubrie suggested. "Though, I'm a little confused. Usually, if you had academic accommodations in public school, they just automatically transfer over and are already set up."

Jordan just gave a small, noncommittal shrug.

When they reached the register, Jordan pulled his new Poly Pass from his wallet. His hands trembled slightly with a mix of nerves and exhilaration as he swiped the card himself, watching the screen deduct the meal from his own dining plan. The simple, mundane act of paying for his own food—food he had chosen for himself—sent a profound wave of empowerment washing over him.

Aubrie effortlessly balanced his plate alongside her own salad on a single tray, insisting on carrying it for him. As she walked beside his wheelchair, a soft, unconscious smile played on her lips.

They found a large round table near a window and settled in. The conversation flowed easily as the women shared pieces of their backgrounds. Madison talked brightly about her hometown of Bluewater, Virginia, while Zoë pragmatically explained the layout of nearby Christiansburg, where she was from. They discussed their majors—Hannah's passion for animal sciences, Lauren's excitement for graphic design, and Zoë's logical approach to industrial and systems engineering.

When the conversation naturally pivoted to Jordan, asking about his own hometown and high school experience, he visibly tensed. The light in his eyes dimmed, and his hands gripped the armrests of his chair. Hannah, immediately recognizing his discomfort, smoothly and gracefully changed the subject to the terrible campus parking situation, expertly steering the spotlight away. The others followed her lead without missing a beat, establishing a silent, protective boundary around his past.

"Hey, is this seat taken?"

A young man with a solid, broad-shouldered build, short brown hair, and a friendly smile stood by the table.

"Mark!" Hannah’s face lit up. She stood up and gave him a quick, happy kiss.

She turned to the group. "Everyone, this is my boyfriend, Mark. He's a mechanical engineering major here".

Mark gave a cheerful wave to the girls, and then his eyes landed on Jordan. Before any awkwardness could settle, Hannah quickly explained the massive clerical error that had placed a guy in their female suite.

Mark didn't even blink. He reached out, offering Jordan a firm, friendly handshake. "Hey man, Mark. Good to meet you," he said. "That's a wild mix-up, but I'm glad you guys are making it work. Sounds like you scored the best roommates on campus."

His immediate, unquestioning acceptance was a breath of fresh air. The seven of them sat together, chatting easily about classes and sports, a newly formed, chaotic, and wonderful little family.
