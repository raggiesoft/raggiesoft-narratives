It was Madison who brought up making the name change official right away. A few minutes later, Jordan, Aubrie, and Madison were sitting in the Resident Advisor's office down the hall. As Jordan wheeled up next to Aubrie, he nervously reached out and took her hand under the table. She responded instantly, holding his hand firmly in her lap—their first quiet, public declaration of their budding connection.

Feeling braver with Aubrie's hand in his, Jordan used his free hand to flip open the lid of his heavy, battered laptop resting securely on his lap. The old machine, running Quantum OS 7 Home Basic, whirred loudly. He hunched close to the dim display, squinting hard as his fingers clumsily navigated the keyboard to type into his *SimpleSpeak* software. He hit the execute key.

*Henry, sorry to bother you again,* the robotic, digitized voice announced to the small office. *But since the name 'Aubrey' caused this whole mess, and I hate it anyway, is there any way we can get a new door card? I go by my middle name, Jordan.*

"Yeah, absolutely, man," Henry said, eager to make up for the morning's chaos. "I can print you a new door card right now."

As the printer whirred, Henry turned his monitor toward them. "We should do one better," he explained. He guided Jordan through the CPI student portal to the 'Personal Information' section. "If you update your 'Preferred Name' here, that's the name that will show up on all the official class rosters for your professors. Everyone will know you as Jordan."

Jordan leaned forward in his wheelchair, squinting intensely at Henry's monitor just to make out the small, blurry text on the screen. Once the letters finally resolved into focus, he typed a quick response.

*Wow. Thank you, Henry,* the mechanical voice stated, conveying a surge of genuine relief.

With the newly printed card in hand, Henry walked with the trio back down the bustling corridor. To Jordan, the chaotic noise of the move-in crowds was a loud, muffled drone. As they navigated around the rolling bins of moving students, the conversation turned to their rumbling stomachs.

"Okay, I'm starving," Madison declared. "Are we doing a suite lunch? Jordan, you're coming with us, right?"

Jordan instinctively cupped his hand slightly behind his ear to catch Madison's words over the hallway din. He hurriedly balanced his laptop on his knees to type.

*Oh, you don't have to—* the text-to-speech engine began, but he was interrupted before he could type the rest.

"We insist," Aubrie cut in smoothly, giving his wheelchair a gentle nudge forward. "We're a suite now. We eat together."

Madison pulled out her mPhone to check the move-in weekend dining hours. She groaned. "Bad news. The main cafeteria doesn't open until 4:00 PM today for the early dinner rush."

"That's fine," Madison continued brightly. "I brought a ton of snacks from home. I've got granola bars, trail mix, chips. I'll share with you two to hold us over until four."

They arrived back at Room 214. Henry used a small tool to pop open the clear plastic holder on the door frame. He slid out the old card with the typed name Aubrey Jordan Fuller, and slid in the new one.

The effect was immediate and powerful. There, on the door to his new home, were the words: Jordan Fuller.

"Come on, Jordan, let's go raid my snack stash," Madison said cheerfully, holding the heavy wooden door open for him and Aubrie.

In the hallway, a few freshmen and their parents, who had paused their unpacking to watch the RA, took in the entire scene. They saw the RA deliberately change the door card to a distinctly male name. They heard Madison happily inviting Jordan inside to share snacks. The pieces quickly clicked together. Whispers began to ripple down the corridor over the sounds of rolling carts.

"Wait, is he actually living in that suite with them?" a girl muttered to her neighbor.

"I think so," the neighbor whispered back, eyes wide. "Did Housing seriously put a guy in there?"
