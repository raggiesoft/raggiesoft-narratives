# Part 9: The New Voice – 6:15 PM

Zoë set the sleek Pinnacle Vanguard Pro down on Jordan's desk, right next to his old, battered machine.

"Right, let's get you properly set up," Zoë said, her fingers flying across the keyboard as she opened a dark terminal window. "I am creating a dedicated user account for you. And I'm adding you to the sudoers file, granting you full sudo rights. You have root access. It is completely your machine now."

Jordan watched in awe. In his mother's house, he had never been given administrator privileges to anything, let alone his own life.

Zoë produced a small USB stick from her pocket. "Let's transfer your data over. Documents, photos, music, whatever you need." She popped the lid of the old laptop, waking the sluggish Quantum OS 7 from its slumber. After a moment of navigating the file system, her analytical brow furrowed. She looked at Jordan, puzzled. "Jordan, the directories are completely empty. There is absolutely no data on this drive."

Jordan looked down, a hot flush of shame warming his cheeks. He instinctively gripped the armrests of his wheelchair. His mother had never allowed him to save anything personal. He had no photos, no music, no life of his own to transfer. The empty hard drive was a stark, embarrassing reflection of his isolated existence.

Daniel Brooks noticed the immediate return of Jordan's terror. He saw the boy shrink into himself, clearly bracing for an imposing "girl dad" to reprimand him or view him with suspicion. Understanding exactly what the boy needed, Daniel stepped forward slowly, keeping his movements deliberate and unthreatening.

He gently closed the lid of the old, heavy laptop with a soft click.

"Well," Daniel said, his voice a warm, reassuring rumble that carried no judgment whatsoever, "that just means you get to start with a completely clean slate. Let's put this old relic up here on the dresser, shall we? That way, you'll have plenty of room in your backpack for the new one."

Daniel carefully lifted the heavy machine and placed it out of the way on top of the wooden dresser, treating the boy not as a threat, but with a careful, fatherly respect. It was a physical act of packing away his old life, deliberately demonstrating to Jordan that he did not need to be terrified of him.

With the old machine gone, Jordan reached out and carefully pulled the new Vanguard Pro onto his lap. It was significantly lighter and cooler than his old machine. Since it was going to be his primary communication device, he needed to know how it felt to type on it while sitting in his chair. It rested perfectly across his legs.

He logged into his new account. The Aura Glade interface was clean, lightning-fast, and reassuringly familiar. Zoë leaned over to guide him through his new text-to-speech software.

"This is *EchoType*," Zoë explained. "You have several vocal output profiles to choose from."

Jordan scrolled through the list. On his old laptop, his mother had strictly enforced the use of "Quantum David," a flat, grating, and deeply robotic male voice, insisting that anything else was "inappropriate" for him to use. Now, sitting in a room entirely free from her gaze, he felt a quiet spark of rebellion.

He bypassed the male options entirely. He scrolled down and paused on a female voice profile named "Sydney." It possessed a bright, clear Australian accent. He could not explain exactly why he liked it—he had never been to Australia, nor did he know much about it—but he liked the way it sounded in his head. And for the first time in his life, that simple preference was all that mattered. Choosing a female voice felt like a profound act of defiance.

He selected the profile, typed a quick sentence into the text box while the machine rested securely on his lap, and hit the execute key.

*Testing. This is my new voice,* the crisp, female, Australian-accented voice announced to the crowded room.

Jordan instantly tensed, bracing his shoulders. He waited for someone to tell him it was wrong, or weird, or unacceptable for a guy to use a female voice.

Instead, Aubrie’s face lit up with a brilliant smile. Madison let out a cheerful, "I love it!" Lauren and Hannah beamed at him. Mark offered an approving thumbs-up, and Daniel gave a warm, encouraging nod. Even Zoë gave a small smile of logical approval.

The entire room—Aubrie, Zoë, Lauren, Hannah, Mark, Madison, and Daniel—happily and seamlessly accepted his choice without a single question or judgment. A wave of profound empowerment washed over Jordan as he rested his hands on the keyboard. He finally had a voice, and it was entirely his own.
