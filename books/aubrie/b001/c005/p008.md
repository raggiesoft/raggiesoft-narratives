"Come on," Lauren said, grabbing Tom's hand. "You have to come meet my suitemates".

"Hey, Lauren," a voice interrupted.

Mark stepped up to the beverage station, holding two empty cups. "Hannah wanted to know if you could grab some extra napkins on your way back".

Lauren paused, turning to Mark with a bright smile. "Oh, perfect timing! Mark, this is my boyfriend, Tom. Tom, this is Mark, Hannah's boyfriend".

Tom extended his hand, instantly radiating an easy, effortless charm. "Good to meet you, man," Tom said.

But the physical reality of the introduction immediately betrayed him. Mark stood at a solid 5'8" with a broad-shouldered, muscular build. Tom, still stinging from his total failure to physically dominate the towering Australian woman moments earlier, launched into an exercise of desperate overcompensation. His handshake was forced and overly firm, his chest puffed out to a brittle extreme as he tried to claw back the masculinity he felt he had just lost.

"Likewise," Mark smiled, returning the firm handshake, though his sharp eyes easily registered the aggressive posturing. "You an engineering major too?"

"Nah, business," Tom replied. "But I'm dreading the humanities requirement. I ended up stuck in an Intro to Political Science lecture on Monday afternoons".

"Wait, really? Dr. Harrison’s class at 2:00 PM?" Mark asked.

"That's the one," Tom laughed.

"No way, I'm in that same section," Mark said, pulling his mPhone from his pocket. "We should swap numbers and be study partners".

"Absolutely, man," Tom agreed, pulling out his own phone to exchange contact info.

Lauren beamed, watching the two most important guys in her and Hannah's lives hitting it off. "Okay, now that you two are best friends," she teased, "Tom, let's go. They're waiting for us".

Just as Tom put his phone away, it buzzed loudly in his hand. He glanced at the screen and let out a small, apologetic sigh.

"Ah, babe, I'm so sorry. I hate to eat and run, but I've got to bounce," Tom announced, slipping the phone back into his pocket. "My roommate is waiting on me outside".

He offered a general, charming wave to Mark. "It was great meeting you, man. I'll see you on Monday".

Tom turned to Lauren to say goodbye. She stood up on her tiptoes, smiling as she wrapped her arms around his neck. Tom returned the embrace, pulling her in.

But as Lauren pressed her cheek against his chest, something felt off. His arms were around her, but his shoulders were rigid. Instead of melting into the embrace, his posture was strangely defensive, almost performative. She could feel his chest expanding with shallow, tense breaths, and she distinctly felt the movement of his head as he looked over her shoulder, his eyes actively scanning the crowded cafeteria behind her.

He patted her back twice—a rapid, staccato rhythm—and quickly stepped away, breaking the physical contact almost before it had truly begun.

"I'll call you tonight," Tom promised, his smile bright and flawless once again. He gave her a quick kiss on the forehead and quickly navigated his way through the crowds, heading for the exit without ever laying eyes on the table where Jordan was sitting.

Beside her, Mark took a slow sip of his drink, his sharp, observant eyes lingering on the doors where Tom had just disappeared. Mark had seen the hug, too. He had noticed the way Tom’s eyes darted toward the exits, scanning the room like a man making sure the coast was clear. He had seen the rigid, perfectly managed way Tom had stepped away from Lauren. It was the body language of someone carefully managing a situation, not someone saying a relaxed goodbye to the girl they loved.

Mark didn't say a word to Lauren, not wanting to ruin her evening. He just set his cup down, making a quiet, permanent mental note of the tension, and resolved to keep a very close eye on his new Political Science study partner.
