# Part 7: Newman Hall – 5:15 PM

The walk to Newman Hall was infinitely easier with Mark leading the way. True to his word, he navigated the group along a winding, paved path that bypassed the steepest ridges of the campus, making the trip smooth and entirely manageable for Hannah and Lauren as they took turns pushing Jordan’s wheelchair.

Newman Hall was the massive, sprawling student center at the heart of campus, serving as a hub for dining, student organizations, and a constant, chaotic flow of freshmen. The cafeteria inside was brightly lit and echoing with the sound of a thousand overlapping conversations.

Mark quickly spotted a large, circular table near the floor-to-ceiling windows and led the suite family over to claim it. While the rest of the group settled in and Jordan pulled his Vanguard Pro onto his lap to look over the menus, Lauren realized she hadn't grabbed a drink.

"I'll be right back, guys," Lauren smiled, setting her purse on a chair. "Just going to hit the fountain."

Lauren navigated her way back toward the crowded beverage station, expertly dodging students carrying heavy trays. As she approached the soda dispensers, a familiar profile caught her eye near the deli line.

It was Tom.

Lauren’s heart did a familiar, happy flutter, but she paused. He was standing face-to-face with a young woman with dark hair pulled into a messy ponytail. Over the din of the cafeteria, Lauren caught a brief snippet of the woman’s voice—a bright, unmistakable Australian accent.

Before Lauren could even call his name, Tom shifted his weight and caught sight of Lauren approaching. His relaxed posture snapped into something rigid and panicked. He said a few rapid, hurried words to the girl—so fast and sharp it looked entirely out of character for him—and abruptly turned his back on her, stepping directly into Lauren's path.

The girl with the Australian accent blinked, visibly taken aback by the sudden, dismissive shift in her boyfriend of two years. She stared at the back of his head, her brow furrowing in confusion, before shaking her head and walking away into the crowd.

"Tom!" Lauren beamed, throwing her arms around his neck, entirely focused on the boy she loved.

"Hey, beautiful," Tom replied smoothly, catching her by the waist. His smile was bright and perfect, masking the terrified adrenaline spiking in his chest. "I didn't know you were eating at Newman tonight".

"We just got here," Lauren said, leaning back to look up at him. She glanced over her shoulder, trying to spot the dark-haired girl in the crowd, but she was already gone. Lauren looked back at Tom, her curiosity piqued. "Who was that you were talking to? She sounded like she had a really strong accent".

Tom didn't miss a single beat. He let out a soft, dismissive chuckle, his eyes locking onto Lauren's with absolute, unwavering sincerity.

"Her? Nobody, really," Tom said, his tone perfectly casual. He brushed a stray strand of hair behind Lauren's ear. "Just a former classmate from my hometown".
