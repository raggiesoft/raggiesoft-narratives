# Part 6: The Safe Harbor's Perimeter – 4:30 PM

By late afternoon, the golden hour light was beginning to filter through the single window of Room 214. Jordan had just finished neatly aligning his textbooks on his desk when a rhythmic knock sounded on the heavy wooden door.

Before anyone could move to open it, the door swung inward. Mark stepped into the suite, bringing with him a wave of easy, confident energy. He immediately spotted Hannah emerging from the connecting bathroom. His face lit up, and he crossed the room, wrapping his arms around her in a warm, familiar hug before giving her a polite, affectionate kiss.

"Hey," Mark said softly, leaning his forehead against hers for a brief second. "Ready for dinner?"

"Almost," Hannah smiled, keeping one hand resting comfortably on his chest. She glanced back toward Room 216, where Lauren, Zoë, and Aubrie were gathering their jackets, then looked over at Jordan, who was sitting quietly in his wheelchair.

Hannah stepped back from Mark, her expression turning serious but warm. "Hey, before we head out... I need to fill you in on a suite consensus we reached this afternoon. About Jordan."

Mark’s posture shifted, his full attention locking onto his girlfriend. "Is everything okay?"

"Everything is perfect," Hannah assured him, keeping her voice low so as not to overwhelm Jordan, but clear enough that Mark could hear every word. She quickly and efficiently explained the "Different Kind of Constellation" the women had agreed upon. She laid out the unique, poly-affectionate dynamic forming between Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, and Zoë, and clarified her and Lauren's roles as the fierce, platonic protectors of the group.

Mark listened intently, his broad shoulders relaxed. He didn't interrupt, and he didn't laugh. As an engineering student, he appreciated logic, but as a genuinely good person, he possessed a deep well of empathy. He looked past Hannah to the young man in the wheelchair—a boy who had been emotionally starved and physically isolated for his entire life.

When Hannah finished, Mark gave a slow, respectful nod.

"I think that's incredible," Mark said, his voice entirely sincere. "He needs a family, Han. And he couldn't ask for a better, safer group of people to provide it. I am one hundred percent on board."

Hannah beamed, her love for Mark deepening in that single moment of pure, unselfish understanding.

Mark then looked down at the heavy, specialized tires on Jordan's wheelchair, his practical mind shifting to the physical realities of moving as a group. "I'm glad you all have this locked in, because getting around outside is a completely different logistical challenge. I've been taking Route 40 over here all week," Mark noted, looking around the room. "I caught the Radford Connector from the McConnell Transit Center over to the CPI Transit Center a few times before move-in just to get the lay of the land again. Plus, I spent half my childhood at Forger Summer Camp right here on campus. I know these hills intimately."

Zoë adjusted her glasses, her analytical mind immediately recognizing the value of his data. "Excellent. You can serve as our primary navigator. We require the most efficient, least severely inclined routes between the residential sector and the dining halls."

"You got it. I'll lead the way," Mark smiled. "This place is essentially a mountain range. Who's on hill duty?"

"Lauren and I," Hannah answered proudly. "We figured it out on the way back from breakfast. Aubrie has the heart for it, but she burns out on the inclines. Lauren and I have the stamina."

Mark chuckled, a sound of genuine respect. "Makes perfect sense. You two are definitely the strongest of the pack. If you ever need a sub, you just let me know. I've got your backs."

"Alright, suite family," Madison announced, clapping her hands together as she stepped into the main room. "Let's go get some food."

As the group began to move toward the door, Hannah walked over to Jordan. He had his Pinnacle Vanguard Pro resting on his lap, ready to travel.

"Ready for dinner, J?" Hannah asked cheerfully.

Before he could type a response, Hannah leaned down and wrapped her arms securely around his shoulders, pulling him into a tight, lingering hug.

Instantly, Jordan’s entire body went rigid. His breath hitched in his throat, and his eyes darted frantically over Hannah's shoulder to where Mark was standing by the door.

In Jordan's limited, terrifying worldview, he knew exactly what happened when a man saw someone else touching their property. His mother had taught him that the world was territorial, aggressive, and deeply transactional. He was an eighteen-year-old male receiving physical affection from another man’s girlfriend, right in front of him.

Panic flared in Jordan's chest. He braced himself for the shout. He waited for Mark's face to twist into a mask of territorial rage, for the heavy footsteps marching across the room, for the inevitable physical removal from the suite.

But the shout never came.

Mark was leaning casually against the doorframe. He wasn't puffing out his chest, and his jaw wasn't clenched. Instead, Mark was watching them with a soft, genuine smile. He saw the sheer terror in Jordan's wide green eyes, and he understood it immediately.

Mark didn't rush forward to establish dominance. He took a slow, relaxed step into the room, keeping his hands visibly out of his pockets and his body language entirely unthreatening.

Hannah pulled back from the hug, giving Jordan's shoulder a final, affectionate squeeze.

Mark stepped up beside her. He looked down at Jordan, his friendly demeanor radiating a quiet, unshakeable security.

"Hannah's a world-class hugger, man," Mark said easily, his tone light and completely devoid of jealousy. "You're in good hands with her."

Mark held out his hand, offering a gentle, low-pressure fist bump.

Jordan stared at the extended fist, his mind struggling to process the absolute absence of anger. Slowly, with a trembling hand, Jordan reached out and tapped his knuckles against Mark's.

"Let's go get some burgers. I know a shortcut around the library that bypasses the worst of the steps," Mark smiled, stepping back to hold the heavy wooden door open for the entire group.

As Aubrie took the handles of the wheelchair and pushed Jordan out into the hallway, the tight, suffocating knot in Jordan's chest finally began to loosen. Mark wasn't angry. He wasn't jealous. He was just... happy that Jordan was being cared for. It was a profound, quiet revelation that etched itself permanently into Jordan's memory, establishing a baseline of what a real, healthy relationship looked like.
