After breakfast, the group decided to explore the campus together to locate their classroom buildings before Monday morning. As they left the dining hall and ventured further into the university grounds, the physical reality of the Commonwealth Polytechnic Institute began to assert itself. Blacksburg was situated in the mountains, and the campus pathways were carved into the rolling landscape, making it notoriously hilly.

Aubrie naturally fell into place behind Jordan's wheelchair, her hands gripping the handles. The path toward the main academic quad started with a gentle slope, but it quickly steepened into a long, grueling incline.

Within a couple of hundred yards, Aubrie's breathing grew heavy. Her pace slowed significantly as she threw her weight against the chair, her stubborn loyalty pushing her forward even as her stamina rapidly drained.

Jordan felt the chair's momentum drag. He twisted around, squinting through his poor vision. Though Aubrie's face was blurry, he could hear her labored, panting breath. A familiar, suffocating knot of guilt instantly formed in his stomach. He was a burden. He instinctively grabbed the metal push-rings of his wheels, trying to use his own limited upper-body strength to help propel the chair up the hill. However, his cerebral palsy made the coordinated effort exhausting and clumsy, his arms trembling under the strain.

His heavy laptop was zipped away in his backpack, leaving him completely non-verbal. He couldn't tell her to stop, he couldn't apologize, and he had absolutely no idea that he was allowed to ask anyone else in the group for help. He just pushed harder, his head pounding.

Hannah, walking a few paces behind with Lauren, watched the silent struggle unfold. Her sharp, "mom friend" instincts kicked in immediately. She saw Aubrie's fierce but fading determination and Jordan's frantic, guilty attempts to push his own weight. She realized instantly that neither of them was going to ask for a break.

"Aubrie, hold up a second," Hannah called out, her voice warm but carrying a gentle, unyielding authority. She stepped smoothly behind the wheelchair. "You're going to burn yourself out before we even find the science buildings. Let me take a turn."

Aubrie, chest heaving, reluctantly stepped aside, her face flushed bright red from the exertion.

Hannah gripped the handles. With her sturdy, capable build, she leaned into the incline. The wheelchair surged forward with a smooth, steady momentum that shocked Jordan. He let go of his push-rings, his arms dropping to his lap as he looked up at Hannah in awe.

"We've got to pace ourselves on these hills," Lauren chimed in, moving to walk casually beside Jordan's chair. She wasn't even out of breath. "Hannah and I can handle the steep parts. We'll just rotate. You and Aubrie take the flat stretches."

Jordan looked from Hannah's easy, effortless smile to Lauren's reassuring, gentle gaze. He didn't need his text-to-speech software to communicate his profound relief. The crushing weight of feeling like a physical burden lifted. He was learning the difficult topography of his new home, but more importantly, he was discovering that this new family had the strength to carry him over the steepest mountains without him ever having to ask.
