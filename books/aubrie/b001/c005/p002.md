Jordan’s first sensation upon waking on this Sunday morning was warmth. It was a deep, soothing heat that seemed to emanate from behind him, blanketing his entire body. For a moment, he was disoriented. He wasn’t stiff, he wasn’t alone, and the recurring nightmare of falling was absent, replaced by a profound sense of being anchored. He slowly became aware of an arm wrapped firmly but gently around his waist, and the soft, steady rhythm of breathing against his neck. The memories of the night flooded back—the nightmare, the whimpering, and then… Aubrie.

He didn’t dare move. The hug she had given him yesterday during his meltdown… that was the first time anyone had ever hugged him. Ever. His mother’s touch was always devoid of affection. That hug had been a revelation, a powerful surge of security he’d never known. This, waking up wrapped in her arms, was an extension of that miracle.

A few moments later, Aubrie stirred behind him. She blinked, her mind registering the cramped space. A blush crept up her neck, but her immediate, instinctive reaction was concern, amplified by the crush that had been blooming since she first saw him. "Are you okay?" she whispered, her voice still thick with sleep. "You were having a nightmare."

Being entirely non-verbal, Jordan couldn't speak the words of gratitude swelling in his chest. His new laptop was out of reach on the desk. Instead, he managed to turn his head slightly, squinting through his poor vision to meet her eyes. He gave a firm, earnest nod, his expression radiating a gratitude so immense it couldn't be contained in words.

A cheerful voice from above broke the quiet intimacy. "Well, look at you two."

They both looked up. Madison was leaning over the edge of her top bunk, a wide, knowing smirk on her face. *Called it,* Madison thought, silently teasing her new roommate. She’d seen the way Aubrie’s eyes lit up when Jordan talked about astrophysics. She recognized the look. And now, finding her cozied up in his bed? It wasn’t just about comfort; it was a full-blown crush in action. Guess the 'no privacy' rule is in full effect already.

Aubrie, catching Madison’s look, felt her blush deepen but held her ground. "He was cold," she said defensively, as if it were the most obvious explanation in the world.

Madison didn't buy it for a second. She rolled her eyes playfully and let out a small giggle. "Oh, I'm sure he was," she teased.

Aubrie’s face bloomed into a deep crimson blush. She was caught, but she wasn't about to admit it. Refusing to make eye contact with Madison, she gave Jordan’s shoulder a final, gentle squeeze that lingered just a second too long before she carefully untangled herself from the narrow bed.

The morning proceeded with the now-established controlled chaos of six people navigating a small space. The shower situation was raised by Zoë, and the logical solution was the accessible shower room down the hall. Madison, who also found herself fancying the quiet, kind boy in the wheelchair, readily volunteered alongside Aubrie.

In the spacious, tiled shower room, the reality of their situation was laid bare. As Aubrie and Madison helped Jordan, their motivations added a palpable layer of tenderness to their actions. For Aubrie, every touch was infused with a burgeoning affection. For Madison, her practical nature was softened by a genuine fondness.

For Jordan, the experience was earth-shattering. As they helped him out of his clothes, he was confronted with the clinical fact of their nudity. It was the first time he had ever seen a naked female, and the sight was so wrapped up in an act of profound kindness that it bypassed awkwardness and went straight to a feeling of overwhelming trust. They were helping him, and in her own way, Madison was also fancying him, but he didn't know that. All he knew was that they were there for him, and their care was a balm on the lifetime of loneliness he had endured.

Back in the suite, clean and dressed, the sense of camaraderie was stronger than ever. Madison knelt to tie his shoes, her movements efficient but gentle. When she finished, she looked up and smiled. "All set."

Jordan looked at her, then at Aubrie, then towards the connecting door of Room 216 where the others were. He thought of the hug, of waking up safe in Aubrie’s arms. He wanted—no, needed—to know that feeling again from the rest of his new family. He took a deep breath, his heart pounding with the audacity of his request, and hauled his new Vanguard Pro laptop onto his lap. He hunched over the keyboard, squinting closely at the screen as he typed into *EchoType*. He hit the execute key.

*Madison?* the crisp, Australian-accented female voice asked. *Can... can I have a hug, too?*

Her cheerful expression softened instantly. "Of course, sweetie." She leaned forward, wrapping her arms around his shoulders and pulling him into a warm, strong embrace. It was different from Aubrie's, more solid and straightforward, but just as wonderful.

Lauren and Hannah emerged from their room, drawn by the synthesized voice and the quiet moment. "What's going on?" Lauren asked with a smile.

"Jordan's decided he's a hugger," Madison said, pulling back but keeping a hand on his shoulder.

Jordan felt his face heat up, but he didn't back down. He looked at the other girls. Lauren, who had a longtime boyfriend, beamed and stepped forward immediately. "I give great hugs!" she declared, leaning down to give him a cheerful, sisterly squeeze. "Almost as good as my boyfriend, Tom."

Hannah, who was also in a relationship, followed suit, her hug quieter but no less warm. "We've got you, Jordan," she murmured.

Finally, Zoë, who had been observing with a small smile, stepped up. "My turn, I suppose," she said, her British accent lending a pragmatic but deeply fond tone to her words. Her arms, when they wrapped around him, were firm and deeply reassuring.

Five hugs. Five different girls. Each one a gift. As Madison took the handles of his chair to head for breakfast, Jordan felt an incredible lightness. He was surrounded by a fortress of friendship. In Room 214, a gentle, unspoken romantic tension was beginning to simmer between three roommates. And from Room 216, a wave of solid, unwavering platonic support. He was learning, for the first time, what it felt like to be wanted.
