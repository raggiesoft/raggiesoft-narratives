# Part 8: Afternoon Anchors – 2:00 PM

The afternoon sun was beating down on the brick walkways of CPI, but the steep inclines didn't seem nearly as daunting to Jordan anymore. Equipped with his new MagnaByte mPad, his FM receiver, and the stack of note-taker forms safely tucked into his backpack, he felt armed for the first time in his academic life.

His 2:00 PM class was Introduction to Psychology, located in one of the newer, sprawling social science buildings near the center of campus. The building was fully accessible, meaning Jordan easily navigated the automatic doors and took the wide elevator up to the third floor.

He rolled into the lecture hall ten minutes early. Unlike the massive astronomy amphitheater, this room was a wide, shallow horseshoe shape with designated accessible desks integrated right into the second row. He parked his chair at one of the wide wooden tables and began unpacking his gear.

He had just pulled his NeoEdit terminal up on his Vanguard Pro when a familiar, bright voice rang out.

"Well, look who it is!"

Jordan spun around. Madison was walking down the tiered steps, a massive iced coffee in one hand and her backpack slung over her shoulder. Her face lit up with a brilliant smile as she spotted him.

Jordan’s face mirrored her excitement. He furiously typed into his terminal: *You're in this class?*

"Psych 101? Absolutely," Madison said, dropping her bag onto the chair right next to him. She slid into the seat, perfectly comfortable invading his space. "It fulfills one of my general education requirements for engineering. Plus, it's fascinating."

She glanced at his desk, noticing the sleek silver tablet and the black FM receiver box he had just set down. "Whoa. New toys? How did the meeting with Ms. Higgins go?"

Jordan grinned. He reached into his backpack, pulled out one of the NCR-carbon-copy packets Ms. Higgins had given him, and slid it across the desk toward Madison.

*Ms. Higgins gave me the tech to mirror the projector and hear the professor directly,* he typed, turning the screen so she could read it. *And she gave me these. If you sign this form to be my official note-taker, the university will give you Priority Course Registration for next semester.*

Madison’s blue eyes went completely wide. She stared at the packet, then looked up at him. "Are you serious? Priority Registration? Jordan, do you have any idea how hard it is to get into upper-level engineering labs as a sophomore? People literally cry over scheduling."

Jordan nodded, typing quickly: *Do you want it?*

"Do I want it? Jordan, I was going to sit next to you and copy my notes for you anyway!" Madison laughed, her bright energy practically vibrating in the quiet room. She immediately dug a pen out of her bag and started filling out the top sheet. "You just saved my entire spring semester. You are officially my favorite person on this campus."

As Madison enthusiastically filled out the paperwork, the quiet, safe intimacy of their shared suite bubble settled over the desk. Jordan watched her write, feeling a sudden, overwhelming urge to share the impossible thing that had happened just before his meeting.

He took a deep breath, his fingers hovering over his keyboard.

*Aubrie dropped me off for the meeting,* he typed, hesitating for a fraction of a second before committing the rest of the sentence to the screen. *She kissed my cheek. And she told me she loved me.*

Madison stopped writing. She put her pen down and looked at the bright green text on his screen.

For a terrifying, split second, Jordan worried he had made a mistake. In any normal social dynamic, telling a girl who explicitly fancied you that her roommate had just confessed her love would result in immediate jealousy, drama, or a hasty retreat.

Instead, Madison’s face broke into a massive, radiant beam. Her eyes softened with genuine, unadulterated joy.

"She did?" Madison whispered, leaning in close so only he could hear. "Oh, Jordan. That's incredible."

She reached out and placed her hand gently over his. There was no trace of bitterness or competition in her touch. It was pure, supportive warmth.

"I told you guys yesterday afternoon," Madison said softly, her thumb lightly tracing the back of his hand. "Aubrie and I talked about this. We don't want to compete for you. We just want to love you. The Constellation is working, J. It's really working."

Jordan stared at her, his heart swelling so much it felt like it might crack his ribs.

*You're really okay with it?* he typed, his hands shaking slightly.

"I am completely, one hundred percent okay with it," Madison promised, her gaze steady and true. She squeezed his hand before pulling back to finish signing the note-taker form. "Because I love you, too, Jordan. And since Aubrie is okay with that... everything is going to be just fine."

The psychology professor walked into the room, signaling the start of the lecture. Madison quickly passed the signed carbon copy back to him and opened her own notebook, ready to work.

Jordan carefully folded the form and tucked it into his bag. He plugged his headphones into the FM receiver and looked at the MagnaByte mPad, which had just lit up with the professor's first slide.

He was sitting in a college classroom. He could see the notes. He could hear the lecture. And he was sitting next to a brilliant, beautiful girl who had just promised him that she loved him, too, without any of the messy, territorial conflict that usually destroyed relationships.

His mother's weapon had been completely dismantled. The world wasn't a terrifying, isolating prison anymore. It was a wide-open universe, and his Constellation was shining brighter than he had ever thought possible.
