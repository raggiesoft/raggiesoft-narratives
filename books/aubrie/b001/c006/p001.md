# Part 1: Morning Divergence – 7:30 AM

The sharp, mechanical buzz of a digital alarm clock shattered the quiet of Room 214.

Aubrie’s hand shot out from under the blankets, blindly slapping the snooze button on the nightstand to kill the noise. As the room settled back into a heavy silence, Jordan opened his eyes. He was greeted by the steady, reassuring rhythm of Aubrie’s breathing. She was still curled up beside him in the narrow bed, her arm draped protectively across his waist. The nightmare-fueled dread that usually accompanied his mornings was entirely absent, replaced by a profound, grounding safety.

Above them, the springs of the top bunk creaked. Madison swung her legs over the side and climbed down the wooden ladder, her hair sleep-tousled but her energy already shifting into practical motion.

"Alright, suite family," Madison whispered brightly, kneeling beside the dresser to pull out a stack of clean clothes. "First day of classes. Let's get moving."

Aubrie stirred, rubbing the sleep from her eyes. She offered Jordan a soft, lingering smile before untangling herself from the blankets. Without a word of complaint, the two women fell into a seamless, coordinated routine. They gathered Jordan’s clean clothes, a stack of towels, and their own shower caddies, then helped him transfer into his wheelchair.

They bypassed the cramped connecting bathroom and headed down the quiet hallway to the spacious accessible shower room. Once the heavy wooden door clicked shut and locked behind them, the radical, judgment-free logic of their shared living arrangement took over.

There was no hesitation and no performance of modesty. They helped Jordan navigate the difficult process of toileting with clinical, unwavering respect. Then, guided by pure pragmatism, the two women utilized the facilities themselves. They were already there, the morning clock was ticking, and they had already crossed the ultimate threshold of vulnerability with one another. It was a shared, undeniable reality: they all had physical needs, and there was absolutely no reason to hide them.

When it was time to shower, the dynamic shifted from simple caretaking to something deeper. The accessible room featured only a single large, roll-in shower stall. With the clock ticking toward their morning lectures, Aubrie turned on the warm water, letting the stall fill with steam. She looked at Jordan, her cheeks flushing slightly, but her eyes held a steady, undeniable affection.

Instead of reaching in from the outside to assist him, Aubrie stepped fully into the water with him.

It was a quiet, monumental shift. As the warm water washed over them, she carefully washed his hair, her touches lingering, infused with a tender, budding romance that went far beyond mere assistance. Jordan closed his eyes, his heart hammering against his ribs. He had never been touched with such gentleness, never been looked at as someone worthy of romantic intimacy. Having Aubrie pressed close to him in the warm steam was a revelation of absolute trust.

A few minutes later, Aubrie finished washing her own hair. She glanced through the steam at Madison, who was patiently waiting her turn near the sinks. Aubrie realized the math of the morning commute was getting tight, but more importantly, she remembered the profound pact they had made the afternoon prior. Madison fancied him, too.

Aubrie reached for a towel and stepped out of the water, offering Madison a warm, encouraging smile as she handed over the bottle of shampoo.

"I'm all rinsed off, and we're on a massive time crunch," Aubrie said smoothly, her voice completely devoid of jealousy. "Do you want to step in and finish helping him while you wash up?"

Madison’s blue eyes widened in mild surprise for a fraction of a second before softening into a brilliant, deeply grateful smile. "Yeah," Madison said softly, appreciating the beautiful lack of territorial boundaries. "I'd love to."

Madison stepped into the warm stall, taking over the shower. The energy shifted from Aubrie's quiet, romantic intensity to Madison's bright, affectionate warmth. As Madison washed her own hair and expertly helped Jordan finish his shower, she chatted easily with him, her hands gentle and caring. For Jordan, sitting in the warm steam, the experience was earth-shattering. He was being handed back and forth not as a burdensome chore, but as someone cherished. He was experiencing the absolute, undeniable proof of their Constellation.

Once they were all clean, dried, and dressed, they returned to Room 214. Madison immediately opened the connecting bathroom door and knocked lightly on the door to Room 216.

"Are you three decent?" Madison called out.

"We are," Zoë’s crisp British voice replied.

Hannah and Lauren stepped through the bathroom, fully dressed and buzzing with first-day energy. Lauren practically bounced on her heels, eager to go meet Tom before his early lecture, while Hannah gave a warm, contented smile, already anticipating her breakfast with Mark.

"Have a great first day, J," Hannah said, leaning down to wrap her arms securely around Jordan’s shoulders in a tight, sisterly hug.

Lauren followed suit, giving him a cheerful squeeze. "See you guys this afternoon!"

As Hannah and Lauren disappeared out the suite door to meet their boyfriends, Jordan watched them go. He realized, with a wave of calm clarity, that he didn't feel abandoned. He wasn't being left behind. He was simply watching his family expand their perimeter.

"Right then," Zoë said, adjusting her glasses as she walked into Room 214, her backpack slung over one shoulder. "Shall we proceed to the dining hall? I require a proper cup of tea before I can process any academic syllabi, though I am fully bracing myself for the American atrocity of lukewarm water and paper-flavored tea."

The four of them made the trek across the rolling campus to Newman Hall. The cafeteria was already a chaotic sea of freshmen scrambling to eat before their morning lectures.

While Aubrie and Madison guided Jordan toward the food stations, Zoë broke off toward the beverage line with low expectations. She anticipated finding a standard metal urn dispensing tepid water. Instead, she stopped dead in her tracks.

Mounted directly into the stainless-steel counter was a dedicated, high-pressure boiling water tap. A small digital readout on the dispenser proudly displayed **100°C**. Beside it sat a neatly organized rack of premium, robust black tea bags.

"Can I help you find anything, hon?" a cafeteria worker asked, noticing Zoë staring at the tap.

"The water," Zoë said, pointing to the readout. "It is actually boiling. Not simmering. Boiling."

The worker laughed, wiping down the counter. "Oh, yeah. CPI has a massive British exchange program. Five years ago, they nearly started a riot over the coffee urns. The university finally ripped out the old machines and installed proper boiling taps in every dining hall on campus just to keep the peace."

A rare, thoroughly impressed smile spread across Zoë's face. "Outstanding."

She prepared her tea exactly to her specifications—steeping the bag in the boiling water for precisely four minutes before adding a splash of milk—and rejoined the group with a newfound respect for the university's logistics.

She found them in the serving area, joining the mass of students staring up at the brightly illuminated breakfast menus. Jordan wheeled forward, immediately hunching his shoulders. He squinted intensely at the glowing boards, his eyes narrowing into slits as he tried to force the blurry, floating shapes into legible words.

Before Aubrie could even take a step forward, Zoë moved seamlessly to his side, cradling her perfectly brewed tea in one hand.

Zoë gently wrapped her free arm around Jordan's shoulders, pulling him into a secure, grounding side-hug. Leaning down, she cupped her hand around his right ear, expertly blocking out the overwhelming wall of cafeteria noise.

"The font scaling on these displays is highly illogical," Zoë murmured directly into his ear, her crisp London accent cutting through the static with perfect clarity. "Let us review your options. Station one offers scrambled eggs, bacon, and toast. Station two is a waffle bar. Which data set appeals to you this morning?"

Jordan relaxed instantly, leaning into Zoë's supportive embrace. He quickly pulled his Vanguard Pro onto his lap, his fingers flying across the keys.

Waffles, please, the Sydney voice of EchoType responded.

Standing a few feet away, Aubrie watched the interaction unfold. Her heart swelled. In any other dorm, in any other social circle, a girl watching another woman wrap her arm around the boy she was falling in love with would spark instant, bitter jealousy.

Aubrie felt nothing but pure, radiant joy. The pact was working. Jordan was being anchored, protected, and loved from multiple sides, exactly as they had designed.

They gathered their food and settled at a quiet table near the back of the hall. As Madison and Zoë debated the most efficient walking routes to the engineering buildings, Aubrie turned her full attention to Jordan.

"Okay, Astronomy 101," Aubrie began, her eyes sparkling with excitement. She knew his mother had forced him into the class to watch him fail, so she immediately set to work dismantling the trap.

"Don't worry about the math right now," Aubrie explained, leaning in close so he could hear her clearly. "The math is just the language we use to describe the system. Focus on the rules. A star is basically a giant balancing act. Gravity wants to crush it inward, and nuclear fusion wants to blow it apart. As long as those two forces are perfectly equal, the star survives. It’s a closed loop, Jordan. It’s entirely predictable."

Jordan watched her, captivated. His mother’s world was a chaotic, unpredictable minefield of shifting rules and sudden punishments. But here, Aubrie was offering him a universe governed by absolute, unbreakable logic.

He typed on his keyboard, hitting the execute key.

So... if you know the mass, you know exactly how it will die? the Australian voice asked.

"Exactly!" Aubrie beamed, practically vibrating with enthusiasm. "The rules never lie. If it's small, it cools into a white dwarf. If it's massive, it goes supernova. The universe doesn't play tricks, Jordan. We just have to learn how to read the patterns."

Jordan felt a slow, genuine smile spread across his face. For the first time in his life, he wasn't dreading the classroom. He was looking forward to it.
