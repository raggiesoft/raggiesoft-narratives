# Part 5: The Broken Elevator – 12:00 PM

As Dr. Gable formally dismissed the class, Sarah slung her backpack over her shoulder and looked down at Jordan.

"Where are you heading next?" she asked easily.

Jordan quickly typed into his terminal. *Newman Commons. Lunch.*

"Perfect, that’s exactly on the way to my next lecture," Sarah smiled. "I’m heading to the building right across from the lower delivery bays. You want a lift? These hills are absolute murder before noon."

Jordan hesitated for a fraction of a second before nodding. Sarah seamlessly stepped behind his wheelchair, grabbed the push handles, and steered him out of Carter Hall.

As they navigated the winding concrete paths downward, Jordan realized that his new friend was surprisingly strong. She handled the steep, downward grades with ease, holding the chair steady so gravity didn't rip it out of her control, chatting happily about the history syllabus the entire time. It was a massive relief not to have to fight the terrain, but more importantly, he was struck by how normal it felt. Sarah didn't act like she was doing a noble, charitable deed; she just saw a friend who needed to go in the same direction.

When they reached the bottom of the hill, they arrived at the rear, ground-level entrance of Newman Commons. The massive brick building was built directly into the incline, meaning the main entrance was a full story above them on the upper quad.

"Right, this is my stop," Sarah said, bringing the chair to a halt on the concrete patio just outside the delivery bays. She walked around to the front, offering a warm, polite smile and a quick wave. "Brilliant working with you today, Jordan. See you on Wednesday."

"Thank you," Jordan mouthed silently, offering a grateful wave back.

As Sarah jogged across the street toward her next class, a wave of anticipation washed over him. It was noon. He was finally going to see Aubrie.

He wheeled himself toward the glass doors of the lower level, his stomach tight with a mix of hunger and nervous excitement. But as he rolled into the dimly lit lower lobby, he found nothing but a series of loading docks and staff offices. There was no clear path to the main dining area above. He was completely lost.

His mPhone buzzed in his pocket. He pulled it out to see a text from Aubrie.

**Aubrie:** *Hey! I’m here. Just inside the main entrance, by the stairs. Waiting for you! :)*

Jordan’s heart sank. He was at the delivery entrance on the exact opposite side—and elevation—of the building. He quickly texted her back, his thumbs fumbling slightly on the screen.

**Jordan:** *I think I'm at a different door. The lower level? I'm looking for the elevator but I don't see one.*

He wheeled back and forth on the concrete patio, feeling a sudden, sharp jolt of panic. A cafeteria worker, pushing a large metal cart of supplies, noticed him spinning his wheels in the empty corridor.

"You looking for the way in, honey?" she asked kindly.

Jordan nodded quickly. He held up his mPhone, pointing to the word *elevator* on his screen.

The worker sighed, her face immediately falling into an expression of deep sympathy. "Ah, yeah, that's because the main public one is broken. The motor burned out this morning." She keyed her radio. "Hang on, let me get my manager. He can take you up the freight elevator."

A moment later, a man in a polo shirt with a manager's name tag pushed through a set of swinging metal doors. "Hi there," he said, assessing the situation. "Sorry about our elevator. Come with me, let's get you the scenic route."

He badged them through a heavy steel door marked *Staff Only.*

Jordan followed him, instantly crossing the threshold into a maze of loud, steamy industrial kitchens. The sensory assault was immediate and overwhelming. The clanging of massive metal pots, the aggressive hiss of industrial dishwashers, and the muffled, chaotic shouting of the line cooks hit him all at once. Because of his untreated hearing loss, the noise wasn't crisp; it was a terrifying, distorted wall of vibration and pressure. The heat of the ovens felt suffocating.

He hunched his shoulders, keeping his eyes glued to the manager's polo shirt, praying for it to end. The manager led him to a large, battered metal freight elevator and swiped a key against the panel. The heavy grated doors slammed shut, and they rode up one floor.

When the doors opened again, Jordan found himself emerging from an unmarked employee door directly into the bright, chaotic main dining area.

He saw her immediately.

Aubrie was standing right where she said she'd be, looking anxiously toward the front entrance. Her hair was catching the light from the massive glass windows, and she was shifting her weight from foot to foot, clearly worried about him.

Her face lit up the second she spotted him rolling out of the kitchen. But as she took a step forward, her brilliant smile faltered. She saw his expression.

Jordan stopped the wheelchair. The anxiety from the broken elevator, the claustrophobic sensory overload of the steamy kitchen, and the sheer frustration of being lost had all collided inside his chest. But underneath it all was a deeper, more profound terror.

Looking at Aubrie—radiant, safe, and waiting for him—a dark, invasive thought seized his mind. *What if this isn't real?*

The brief, terrifying moment of being lost, of being unable to find her, had felt entirely too much like his old life. He was suddenly terrified that this was all a beautifully constructed dream, a cruel trick of his own mind, and that he was going to wake up at any second, trapped back in his mother's apartment, forever at her mercy.

Aubrie saw the raw, unmistakable fright in his eyes. She didn't know the full logistical cause of his panic, only that the boy she was falling for was terrified.

She didn't hesitate. She rushed to him, closing the distance in seconds. Right there in the middle of the crowded dining hall, entirely uncaring of who was watching, she threw her arms around him and pulled him into a desperate, grounding hug. She buried her face in his neck, her arms serving as a firm, undeniable anchor against the overwhelming tide of his panic.

"Hey," she whispered into his ear, her voice fierce and absolute. "You're okay. You found me. You're safe."

She felt him trembling against her, his hands gripping the back of her shirt like it was a lifeline. Her heart ached for him. She realized something was deeply scaring him, something far heavier than just a broken elevator. She wished, with a sudden, powerful longing, that she could just skip her afternoon classes entirely and stay with him, acting as his shield for the rest of the day.

But she couldn't. They both had to keep moving forward.

The hug in the middle of the chaotic dining hall had grounded him. Jordan let out a shaky breath as Aubrie finally pulled back just enough to look into his eyes, though she kept a firm, reassuring hand on his shoulder.

She offered him a soft, affectionate smile, leaned in, and pressed a quick, gentle kiss to his cheek.

Jordan blinked, his breath hitching in his chest. It was only the second kiss of his entire life—the second one *today*—and the sudden, undeniable warmth of it chased away the last lingering chill of his panic.

"Come on," Aubrie said, her voice bright, deliberately ignoring the stares of a few passing upperclassmen. "Let's go get lunch. Together."
