# Part 3: The Ascent – 10:15 AM

"It's a date," Aubrie had said brightly in the crowded hallway of the Science Complex. Then her eyes had gone wide as she realized the weight of her own words. "I mean... not a... you know. A literal date. Just... a lunch... with a time. I'll just see you then!"

Flustered and blushing furiously, she had spun around to hurry off toward the math building. But after taking only two steps, she stopped. She turned back, marched right up to his wheelchair, leaned down, and quickly pressed her lips against his cheek.

It was a fleeting, lightning-fast peck, gone before Jordan could even register what was happening. She pulled away, her face burning an even deeper shade of crimson, and bolted down the corridor without looking back.

Jordan sat perfectly still in the middle of the rushing stream of freshmen. He slowly raised his hand, his fingertips resting against the skin of his cheek. The spot felt impossibly warm, practically glowing against the cool, air-conditioned air of the building.

It was his first kiss. From anyone. Ever.

His heart hammered a chaotic, frantic rhythm against his ribs. He felt entirely confused, completely overwhelmed, and wildly, desperately hopeful for noon to arrive.

But as the hallway began to empty out, reality slowly settled back in. For the very first time since arriving at CPI, the safety net of his suite family was entirely gone. Aubrie, Madison, Zoë, Lauren, and Hannah were all scattered across the massive campus. He was alone.

He pulled up his class schedule on his Vanguard Pro. *10:30 AM – History 101: Western Civilization.* It was located in Carter Hall, an older brick building situated near the upper quad.

Jordan took a deep breath, gripped the hand rims of his wheelchair, and pushed through the heavy glass doors back out into the humid Virginia morning.

The topography of the campus was notoriously unforgiving. CPI had been built into a series of rolling, aggressive hills, and the path to Carter Hall was a steep, winding ribbon of concrete. Jordan started up the first major incline, his arms straining with the effort. Without Aubrie or Lauren walking behind him to help push, the true, brutal weight of his own momentum settled squarely onto his shoulders.

Halfway up the first ridge, his triceps began to burn with a dull, throbbing ache. The wheelchair slowed to a crawl. He grit his teeth, forcing the wheels forward inch by inch, but gravity was pulling him backward just as relentlessly.

"Hey, man," a voice called out.

Jordan flinched, instinctively bracing himself for an insult or a sharp reprimand for blocking the walkway.

Instead, a tall guy wearing a faded CPI lacrosse hoodie stepped up behind him. "This hill is a beast. You want a hand?"

Jordan blinked, surprised by the easy, casual tone. He gave a short, grateful nod.

Without making a spectacle of it, the lacrosse player grabbed the push handles and easily rolled Jordan the rest of the way up the ridge, bringing them to the flat plateau near the library.

"Have a good one," the guy smiled, giving Jordan a quick pat on the shoulder before jogging off to his own class.

Ten minutes later, as Jordan approached the final, sweeping incline leading up to Carter Hall, his arms were practically shaking with exhaustion. The burn was intense, and his progress ground to a halt.

Once again, the universe intervened. A girl carrying a massive stack of art portfolios noticed him struggling. She didn't ask; she simply shifted her heavy folders to one arm, stepped behind his chair with the other, and leaned her weight against the handles to help him crest the final slope.

"They really should put an elevator on the outside of this hill," she joked, breathing heavily as they reached the top. She smiled warmly at him. "See you around."

As Jordan rolled toward the entrance of Carter Hall, a dark, familiar shadow crept into the back of his mind. *His mother's voice.*

He had needed help twice in the span of fifteen minutes just to get to a basic freshman lecture. His mother’s harsh words echoed in his head, sharp and undeniable: *You are a burden. You are fundamentally incapable. You don't belong here.*

For a terrifying moment, the anxiety threatened to swallow him whole. Maybe she was right. Maybe he should have just stayed home in Hampton Roads, hidden away in his bedroom where the ground was flat and the expectations were zero. If he couldn't even push himself to class, how was he supposed to survive four years here?

But then, he thought about the morning.

He thought about Madison stepping into the shower stall, taking the shampoo bottle from Aubrie with a bright, unquestioning smile. He thought about Zoë shielding him from the overwhelming noise of the cafeteria, gently reading the menu directly into his ear. He thought about Mark’s firm, respectful handshake.

And he thought about his cheek, which still felt impossibly warm.

They had seen every limitation he had. They had seen his absolute most vulnerable moments. And none of them had looked at him like he was a burden. They had looked at him like he was family. The random students on the pathway hadn't looked at him with pity, either—they had just seen a fellow student fighting a steep hill, and they had stepped in to help.

Jordan pushed open the door to Carter Hall and wheeled himself into the air-conditioned lobby. His arms ached, his chest was heaving, and he still had to figure out how he was going to hear the history professor.

But as he navigated toward the classroom door, a quiet, defiant thought blossomed in his mind.

*Maybe,* he thought to himself, *I'll just get used to the hills.*
