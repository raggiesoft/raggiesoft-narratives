Aubrie stepped behind his wheelchair and pushed him toward the long, winding serving lines. The noise and smell of the food were overwhelming, a chaotic mix of sizzling grease, baking bread, and a hundred overlapping conversations.

"Hey," Aubrie said, noticing him looking nervously at the stack of plastic trays. "It's a massive pain to try and balance a tray on your lap while rolling. Let me just grab yours for you. What do you want?"

Jordan looked up at the massive, illuminated menus above the stations. Pizza. Pasta. A deli. A grill. He froze. He was completely, utterly overwhelmed by the choices. His entire life, food had been a simple, non-negotiable command. His mother made something, and he ate it. If he didn't like what Aubrie Sinclair was serving, he didn't eat. He was never asked what he wanted.

"Jordan?" Aubrie asked gently, seeing him staring blankly at the glowing boards. "You okay?"

Then, he spotted it. At the grill station, a student in a maroon apron was flipping burgers on a flat top. A basket of fresh, golden french fries was resting right next to the fryer. His mother never made burgers, viewing them as greasy garbage, and fries were a rare, frozen-bag-in-the-oven affair that always came with a lecture about health.

He pulled out his mPhone, his thumbs moving quickly. He turned the screen toward her.

*Can I... can I have that? A cheeseburger? And... fries?*

"A burger? Heck yeah. Good choice," Aubrie said, not blinking an eye. She had absolutely no idea she had just enthusiastically approved two items his mother would have outright denied him. "What do you want on it?"

He was stunned. He typed a single word: *Everything.*

"You got it. One cheeseburger with everything, and a side of fries," she called to the worker over the glass partition. "What about a drink?"

He pointed to the soda fountains, another heavily restricted battleground he'd always lost at home. He typed: *A soda?*

"Perfect." Aubrie grabbed a plastic cup, filled it with cola, and placed it on the tray next to the paper boat of fries and the foil-wrapped burger. She then quickly grabbed her own lunch—a large salad and a bottle of water.

They arrived at the register. Jordan, suddenly realizing he had to actually purchase the food, began to panic. He fumbled awkwardly at his pocket, his fine motor skills failing him under the pressure as he tried to dig out his Poly Pass. He expected Aubrie to sigh in frustration or simply shove him aside and pay for it herself.

Instead, Aubrie set her tray down on the counter. She stepped to his side, gently placing her hand over his trembling fingers to steady them.

"Hey. Take your time. You've got this," she murmured softly. She helped him guide his wallet out of his pocket and slide the plastic Poly Pass free.

Jordan took a deep breath, reached up, and swiped his own card through the reader. The digital register chirped merrily, displaying a deduction from his tax-free **Forge Funds** balance. He had done it. He had chosen his own meal, and he had paid for it himself. It was a tiny, mundane transaction for anyone else, but for Jordan, it was a massive, intoxicating hit of pure independence.

Aubrie swiped her own card, balanced the two trays, and steered them toward a quiet table near the back windows overlooking the quad.

Once they were seated, Jordan unwrapped his burger. He took a bite, and the taste of salt, melted cheese, and absolute freedom flooded his senses. It was the best thing he had ever eaten.

Aubrie watched him with a soft smile, stabbing a cherry tomato with her plastic fork. "So? How was your mid-morning class? You survived the solo trek across the hills?"

Jordan’s face lit up. He swallowed, quickly wiping his hands on a napkin, and pulled his Vanguard Pro onto the table. He launched his sleek NeoEdit terminal, eager to tell her everything. He scaled the bright green text up so she could read it easily across the table.

*The hills were brutal. But a couple of people actually helped push me. And the class was amazing. History 101.*

"Yeah?" Aubrie leaned forward, genuinely invested. "Did the professor use the microphone? Could you hear anything?"

Jordan’s fingers flew across the keys.

*I couldn't hear him at first, and he was writing on a dusty chalkboard. But he noticed me squinting. He stopped, switched to a whiteboard with heavy black markers, and moved closer so I could hear him. He didn't even make a big deal out of it.*

"Jordan, that’s incredible!" Aubrie beamed, practically vibrating with second-hand excitement. "See? Some of the professors here actually get it."

*But it gets better,* Jordan typed, a massive grin on his face. *He asked for a volunteer to partner with me. A girl sat next to me. She’s from Australia. Her name is Sarah.*

Aubrie raised an eyebrow, smiling. "An Australian study buddy? That’s awesome."

*She was great,* Jordan continued. *She lives on the fifth floor of Bennett Hall. I told her I was in the ADA suite, and she already knew about it. She didn't think it was weird at all. She took all my notes, and when I typed answers into my terminal, she read them out loud to the class for me. I actually participated.*

Aubrie’s heart swelled. She reached across the table and lightly tapped the edge of his laptop. "Look at you," she said, her voice thick with pride. "Day one, and you're already leading classroom discussions and making friends on the fifth floor."

Jordan looked down at his keyboard, his cheeks flushing pink, but he couldn't stop smiling. The morning that had started with a terrifying astronomy lecture and a panic attack in the kitchen had completely turned around. He was capable. He belonged here.

Aubrie checked the clock on the dining hall wall. It was 12:45 PM.

"Eat up, genius," she said gently, packing up her empty salad container. "You've got a one o'clock appointment with Ms. Higgins at Educational Accessibility. It's time to make sure every single professor treats you like Dr. Gable did."
