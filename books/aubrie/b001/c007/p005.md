# Part 5: The War Room – 8:45 PM

The cramped space of Suite 214/216 was not designed to hold twelve highly emotional college students, but nobody cared about the tight squeeze.

Aubrie and Madison immediately ushered everyone inside, locking the hallway door securely behind them. They propped both of the connecting bathroom doors wide open, allowing the massive group to spill over into Room 216.

Lauren and Sarah sat side-by-side on the edge of Jordan’s Twin XL bed, a box of tissues resting between them. The atmosphere in the room was electric with protective outrage as the two girls explained exactly what had just happened under the bright lights of the Newman Hall pool tables.

When Lauren finally dropped the bombshell—*Tom was playing us both*—the reaction from the core suite was immediate. Madison let out a furious gasp, Zoë crossed her arms with a deadly glare, and Aubrie looked like she was ready to march to the student center and dismantle Tom herself.

"I can't believe he lied to my face," Sarah said, her voice tight with anger as she wiped a stray tear from her cheek. "All day, he was texting me like a paranoid lunatic, making me feel like I was doing something wrong."

Lauren suddenly sat up straighter, the final puzzle piece snapping violently into place.

"That's why he exploded on the phone last night," Lauren realized, her eyes wide as she looked at Aubrie and Madison. "It had absolutely nothing to do with Jordan. It was about the dorm! He realized that I lived on the second floor, and Sarah lived on the fifth floor. He was terrified of the Bennett Hall network. He was trying to isolate me so I wouldn't talk to any of the other girls in the building and figure out his secret!"

"He projected his own guilty garbage onto Jordan," Sarah agreed fiercely, grabbing Lauren's hand. "He is absolute trash, Lauren. You did nothing wrong. Neither of us did."

There was zero friendly fire. The shared trauma of the betrayal had instantly bonded them into a fierce sisterhood. All of their anger was directed entirely, one-hundred-percent at Tom.

While the room buzzed with loud, protective outrage, Jordan quietly unlocked his Vanguard Pro. He typed a paragraph, the keyboard clicking softly, and then gently pushed the laptop forward so the screen rested on the mattress between Lauren and Sarah.

*I know exactly what it feels like to be controlled by someone who claims to care about you,* the glowing green text read. *I know what it's like when they try to isolate you so they can keep their power. Tom's toxicity is a reflection of his own brokenness, Lauren. It has nothing to do with you. And he has no power here. Room 214 is a safe haven. For both of you. Always.*

Lauren read the words, a fresh wave of tears hitting her, but this time, they were tears of profound relief. She reached out and hugged Jordan tightly around his shoulders. Sarah read the screen next, her expression softening into deep, respectful awe at the quiet boy in the wheelchair.

"Jordan is right," Mark said from the doorway of the bathroom, his voice steady and serious. "Tom just got humiliated by nine people in the middle of Newman Hall, and Jessica has a photo of it. He's going to spiral. But he's not getting anywhere near you guys. I'll text my suitemates on the third floor. We'll be your early warning system if he or any of his lacrosse buddies show up at this building."

"And the fifth floor has your back," Jessica added firmly from where she was leaning against the dresser. "We'll keep an eye out everywhere else on campus."

As the adrenaline of the confrontation began to wear off, the exhaustion of the emotional whiplash finally settled over the room. It was getting late. Ashley, Emily, Cassie, Jessica, and Chloe began gathering their things to head back upstairs to their own dorms.

"Before you guys go," Aubrie announced, stepping into the center aisle. She looked at Lauren, then at Sarah, and finally down at Jordan. She shared a brief, unspoken look of absolute, radical trust with him. He nodded immediately, a warm, supportive smile on his face.

"We built something really special in this suite," Aubrie said softly, looking around the room. "We built a family. And families protect each other. So, Jordan and I want to make something perfectly clear. His bed is a safe haven. From now on, it is open to *any* girl in this pact who needs comfort, who feels unsafe, or who just doesn't want to be alone. No questions asked."

It was the ultimate expression of the Constellation they had built—pure, platonic, emotional security, completely devoid of jealousy or condition.

Mark, standing in the doorway, nodded slowly. He didn't look threatened; he looked deeply moved. He fully understood the profound, healing weight of what Aubrie was offering.

Hannah leaned against Mark's side, wrapping her arm around his waist. "That is the most beautiful thing I've ever heard," Hannah smiled warmly. "I'm going to lovingly opt out, of course, because I've got Mark. But it's an incredible offer."

Chloe, grabbing her backpack, smiled sympathetically from the hallway. "Same here. I've got my own guy to worry about, but you guys are seriously the best friends anyone could ask for. Goodnight, everyone."

With a final round of hugs, the five girls headed up the stairwell to the fifth floor.

Sarah, however, stayed sitting on the edge of the bed. She looked completely drained, the confident Australian spark temporarily dimmed by the sheer exhaustion of the breakup.

"You're not going upstairs tonight, Sarah," Lauren said firmly, wiping her eyes. "You're staying here with us."

"I don't want to take anyone's bed," Sarah hesitated.

"You're taking mine," Lauren insisted. "You can have Bed C in 216. It's the top bunk. It's quiet, and it's safe."

Sarah looked at Lauren, incredibly touched. "Are you sure? Where are you going to sleep?"

Lauren looked over her shoulder at Jordan. He was already maneuvering his wheelchair closer to the head of the bed, patting the mattress next to him with a welcoming smile.

"I'm going to take Aubrie up on her offer," Lauren said softly.

The sleeping arrangements for the night naturally fell into place, an organic deployment of their fortress. In Room 216, Mark and Hannah claimed Hannah's single Twin XL (Bed A). Zoë climbed into her practical lower bunk (Bed B), and Sarah gratefully climbed up into Lauren's top bunk (Bed C).

In Room 214, the dynamic was just as seamless. Madison climbed up into her top bunk (Bed C), and Aubrie settled into her lower bunk (Bed B), reaching out to click off the overhead light.

Lauren changed into a spare set of pajamas Aubrie lent her and climbed into the accessible Twin XL (Bed A) next to Jordan. He had already scooted flush against the cinderblock wall to give her as much room as possible.

Lauren lay on her side, facing him in the dim amber light of the streetlamp filtering through the blinds. Jordan reached out, gently wrapping his arm around her shoulders in a fiercely protective, brotherly embrace.

Lauren closed her eyes, letting out a long, shaky exhale. Her phone was completely powered down and shoved at the bottom of her backpack. She was surrounded by women who supported her, a boyfriend who respected her, and a boy who had offered his own bed to make sure she felt secure. As she listened to the quiet, synchronized breathing of Suite 214, the toxic memory of Tom Lax Toss completely faded away.

She was safe.
