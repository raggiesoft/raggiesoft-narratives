The massive, stadium-style lecture hall in the College of Health Sciences was already buzzing with the low, chaotic energy of three hundred freshmen. The room was steeply tiered, built to accommodate massive general education requirements, with a wide, flat accessible row running straight across the middle tier.

Aubrie pushed Jordan’s Vanguard Pro through the double doors, followed closely by Madison, Zoë, Lauren, Hannah, and Mark. They claimed a massive block of seating right in the center of the accessible row. Jordan parked his wheelchair at the end of the long desk, plugging his headphones into the FM receiver and setting up his new MagnaByte mPad next to his laptop.

The fact that all seven of them were in this exact section was a massive, statistically absurd coincidence. But the fact that Jordan was in the class at all was a masterstroke of administrative negligence by his mother.

Like all undergraduates at CPI, Jordan was required to complete two credits of Health or Physical Education to graduate. Over the summer, Aubrie Sinclair had logged into his student portal to build his schedule. She had seen "HSC 201" fulfilled the requirement, checked the box, and moved on, completely failing to read the actual course title or description.

If she had bothered to look, she would have realized she had just enrolled her heavily sheltered, medically isolated son in *Dimensions of Human Sexuality*.

For eighteen years, Aubrie Sinclair had religiously signed the opt-out forms for every single public school Family Life and sexual education module. Now, thanks to her own careless rushing, the university was about to give him a comprehensive, college-level education on the exact subject she had spent his entire life trying to hide from him.

"Jordan!"

A bright, distinctly Australian voice cut through the chatter. Jordan looked up from his terminal. Sarah Braithwaite was bounding down the tiered stairs, a massive smile on her face. Two other girls were trailing closely behind her.

Jordan’s face lit up. He quickly typed into his NeoEdit terminal, the neon-green text glowing against the sleek, dark glass of his new Dark Aero window manager.

*Sarah! You're in this class too?*

"Gen-ed requirement," Sarah grinned, dropping her backpack onto the desk next to Lauren. "I saw you guys roll in. These are my roommates from the fifth floor. Jessica, and Chloe."

Jessica, who shared Sarah's accent but carried herself with a much more grounded, no-nonsense energy, offered a friendly wave. "Nice to finally meet the famous Room 214."

Chloe smiled warmly, claiming a seat next to Madison. "Sarah hasn't stopped talking about how much you saved her in Western Civ yesterday."

Sarah laughed, sliding into the seat between Jessica and Lauren. She turned to Lauren, offering her hand. "I'm Sarah, by the way. I don't think we formally met in the lobby on Saturday."

"Lauren," she replied, shaking Sarah's hand with a bright, genuine smile. "I'm in 216. It is so nice to meet you. We were just saying how incredibly lucky Jordan was to get you as a note-taker on the very first day."

"Oh, please, he's the one who got me Priority Registration," Sarah said easily as she pulled out her notebook. "He's an absolute lifesaver. How's the whole suite dynamic going? Is it as chaotic as everyone thinks?"

"Honestly? It's amazing," Lauren admitted, her smile softening as she looked down the row at Jordan, who was currently showing Jessica how his FM receiver worked. "It just... works. We all really look out for each other."

They fell into an easy, comfortable conversation. Sitting side-by-side in the crowded lecture hall, the girls bonded instantly over the shared anomaly of Jordan’s living situation and the chaotic reality of freshman year. Neither of them brought up their boyfriends. In the bright, bustling energy of the morning, surrounded by friends, the dark, uneasy shadow of Lauren's phone call with Tom the night before felt temporarily pushed away.

At exactly 10:00 AM, the lights in the auditorium dimmed slightly. A tall, fiercely confident professor with a wireless microphone clipped to her lapel strode to the center of the stage. She didn't look like a stuffy academic; she exuded a vibrant, no-nonsense energy that instantly commanded the room.

"Welcome to Health Sciences 201: Dimensions of Human Sexuality," she announced, her voice echoing clearly through the massive room. Instantly, the same audio pumped directly into Jordan's headphones, crisp and perfectly isolated from the background noise. "I am Dr. Evans."

The projector flared to life, casting the syllabus onto the massive screen at the front of the room. A split second later, the exact same document appeared in high resolution on Jordan's silver mPad.

"Over the next fifteen weeks, we are going to have frank, mature, and clinical discussions about human biology," Dr. Evans continued, clicking to the first slide. "We will cover male and female anatomical structures, the physiological mechanics of the reproductive system, physical response cycles, and sexual health. And let me be clear right now: I expect you to know the proper medical terminology for the exam. But I also know what you all call these parts on the weekend."

A ripple of nervous laughter moved through the auditorium.

"I am not afraid to use the street slang in this room," she stated flatly, pacing the stage. "If we are talking about a penis or a vagina, we will use the clinical terms, but I will also make sure you understand the slang you're going to hear out in the real world. There is zero shame in this classroom. Anatomy is anatomy."

Lauren and Sarah exchanged a highly amused glance. Mark let out a quiet chuckle. Aubrie and Madison pulled out their notebooks, immediately respecting the professor's unapologetic vibe.

"A few ground rules," Dr. Evans said, leaning against the front podium. "First: my office is in Room 412. There is a massive glass bowl of condoms sitting right on the edge of my desk. They are free. You do not need to ask me for them. You do not need to make eye contact with me. Just walk in, take what you need, and walk out. Second: this class covers heavy, deeply personal material. If any of you need to have a private conversation with me—whether you have questions about birth control, if you need resources for an abortion, or if you simply grew up incredibly sheltered and need extra help understanding the basics without the whole class staring at you—email me. We will meet one-on-one outside of my regular office hours. Total privacy. No judgment, ever."

The room grew quiet, a genuine wave of respect washing over the three hundred students.

"Finally," Dr. Evans said, clapping her hands together once. "Before we dive into chapter one, I have a standard request from Educational Accessibility. We have a student in this section who requires a peer note-taker. The university will compensate you with Priority Course Registration. If you have decent handwriting and want the perk, come see me at the podium after class."

Down in the accessible row, all nine of Jordan's friends—Aubrie, Madison, Zoë, Lauren, Hannah, Mark, Sarah, Jessica, and Chloe—simultaneously turned their heads to look at him, grinning. Jordan couldn't help but smile, his cheeks flushing slightly at his own private army of note-takers.

But as Dr. Evans clicked to the next slide, launching into the introductory material, Jordan's smile quickly faded.

He was staring down at his mPad. The screen displayed a list of the upcoming textbook chapters. He read the clinical terminology, his eyes scanning the incredibly detailed anatomical diagrams that served as the header for the document. Dr. Evans fired off a few of the slang terms alongside the medical ones, but it didn't help.

A cold, heavy knot of pure panic began to form in his stomach.

He didn't know what any of these words meant. He didn't understand the diagrams. He didn't even know the slang. His mother hadn't just opted him out of the classes; she had actively shamed the concept of the human body to the point where he had learned to completely ignore his own physical existence. While the rest of the three hundred students in the room were comfortably absorbing the lecture, Jordan was staring at a completely alien language.

He looked down at his own hands, resting on the armrests of his wheelchair, a sudden, overwhelming wave of inadequacy washing over him. He wasn't just behind. He was entirely, fundamentally lost.
