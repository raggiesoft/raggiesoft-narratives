# Part 4: An Unconventional Study Session – 8:15 PM

That Tuesday evening, after their first full day of classes, the group was relaxing in the suite. The health class, however, was still heavily on Jordan's mind. He wheeled himself over to where the girls were gathered in Room 214, holding his *Dimensions of Human Sexuality* textbook and syllabus like a mysterious, terrifying map.

He opened his Vanguard Pro laptop, the neon-green text glowing against the sleek interface as he typed a confession that mirrored what he had told Dr. Evans earlier that day.

*That health class today,* he typed, his words glowing with hesitant but earnest vulnerability. *All those chapter titles... about the human body... I've never learned about any of that. My mother forcibly opted me out of those classes every year. She made me feel like it was dirty to even ask.*

A deep, collective empathy filled the room. They understood this wasn't just about academics; it was about a fundamental part of his education and his own humanity that had been cruelly denied to him. It was Aubrie who, looking at the confusing, sterile diagrams in the open textbook on her desk, formulated a plan rooted in the unique trust their family had built.

"The diagrams in the book are just flat drawings, Jordan. It's hard to learn from that," she said matter-of-factly. "We could... help you. It would be easier to remember if you could connect the clinical terms to physical reality."

The offer was radical, generous, and born of pure, practical kindness. Zoë and Madison nodded in immediate agreement, their faces completely devoid of judgment.

Hannah and Lauren, out of deep respect for their own boyfriends, lovingly opted out. "That is an incredibly sweet and amazing thing to do for him," Hannah said, offering a supportive smile. "Just knock on the connecting door when you guys are done." With that, the two of them retreated through the bathroom to Room 216, pulling the door shut behind them until it clicked, giving the others total privacy.

Jordan looked at the three remaining women, a mix of nervousness and profound gratitude on his face. Aubrie gave him a reassuring smile.

"Ready for your first real health class?" Aubrie asked.

Zoë walked over and clicked the heavy deadbolt on the hallway door. Madison reached up and flicked off the harsh, fluorescent overhead lights, leaving the room bathed only in the warm, gentle amber glow of their desk lamps. The atmosphere transformed instantly. It wasn't a party; it was a sanctuary.

Aubrie sat on the edge of her bed, patting the mattress. Madison and Zoë sat on either side of her, holding the open textbook. Before anything else happened, Aubrie looked Jordan directly in the eyes, setting the absolute boundaries of the room.

"Jordan, listen to me," Aubrie said softly, her voice steady and anchoring. "Here is what to expect. This isn't scary. There is absolutely no pressure. We are in complete control of this environment, and if you get overwhelmed, or if you just get cold feet, we stop immediately. No questions asked. Do you understand?"

Jordan let out a shaky breath, the last remnants of his anxiety melting away. He nodded. He trusted them with his life.

What followed was the quiet, methodical dismantling of a lifetime of trauma. The study session was clinical, gentle, and profoundly intimate, completely devoid of the shame his mother had weaponized against him. Using the textbook as a guide, Aubrie, Madison, and Zoë patiently helped Jordan map the sterile, confusing medical terminology to real, physical bodies.

They replaced the taboo with simple, practical truth.

As the quiet reassurances filled the room, accompanied by the soft turning of textbook pages, a massive psychological weight lifted off Jordan’s shoulders. He realized, with overwhelming clarity, that the human body wasn't filthy. It wasn't something to be hidden or despised. The contrast between the cold medical diagrams and the warmth, trust, and fierce protectiveness of his suitemates broke the final, lingering hold of his mother's Puritanical control.

Because they offered him such radical vulnerability, Jordan felt an unprecedented surge of empowerment. For the first time in his life, he didn't feel broken or ashamed of his own form. He willingly returned the trust, allowing them to map his anatomy clinically alongside the textbook chapters, realizing that his body was just as natural and deserving of care as theirs.

By the time the session ended, the lingering ghosts of his mother's psychological prison had been entirely evicted from Room 214.

They all changed into their soft cotton pajamas. Jordan sat in his wheelchair near his desk, feeling a deep, resounding peace settle into his bones. He wasn't lost anymore. He was whole, educated, and completely safe. A soft, confident smile rested on his face as he watched Aubrie, Madison, and Zoë chat quietly on the beds.

Then, a soft knock echoed against the heavy wooden door leading to the hallway.

"Madison?" Hannah's voice called from the other side, sounding slightly muffled through the thick wood. "It's Hannah. And Mark. And... well, everyone."

Jordan frowned slightly, exchanging a confused look with Aubrie.

Madison stood up, walking over to the wall. She pressed the large silver handicap-accessible button. The motorized mechanism whirred to life, unlatching the deadbolt, and the heavy door swung open slowly into the room.

The calm, peaceful sanctuary of Room 214 instantly collided with the chaotic aftermath of the Newman Hall confrontation. Nine people were crowding the hallway. Mark looked furious. Jessica and Chloe looked shell-shocked. And right in the center of the group, Lauren was sobbing openly into Sarah Braithwaite’s shoulder.

Aubrie was on her feet in an instant, the protective peace of the study session vanishing as she rushed toward the door.

"What happened?"
