By the time the massive ten-person entourage made it to Newman Commons, the lunchtime rush was in full swing. It took some serious maneuvering, but Mark and Sarah managed to push three rectangular tables together near the back windows, creating enough space for everyone, including a spot at the head of the table for Jordan’s wheelchair.

The energy was loud, chaotic, and incredibly warm. They traded stories about their bizarre first-day professors, the dreadful dining hall pizza, and the absolute absurdity of their shared health class.

Sitting across from each other, Lauren and Sarah naturally gravitated toward a side conversation. Despite growing up worlds apart—Lauren from Canada and Sarah from the bustling coast of Sydney—they hit it off instantly. Sarah was drawn to Lauren's gentle, steady kindness, while Lauren found Sarah's bold, unapologetic confidence incredibly refreshing.

"I still can't believe you managed to get a job at the campus coffee shop as a freshman," Lauren said, taking a sip of her water. "I tried to apply, and they practically laughed me out of the student union."

"You just have to be incredibly persistent," Sarah grinned, stealing a fry from Jessica's plate. "Or just have an accent. I swear the manager only hired me because he thinks Americans like ordering lattes from Australians."

As they laughed, Lauren’s mPhone, resting face-up on the table, buzzed loudly against the laminate wood.

The screen lit up with a new text message. The contact name simply read: **My MVP 🥍**

Lauren’s smile instantly faltered. Her stomach gave that same, uneasy flutter she had felt on the top bunk the night before. She didn't open the message; she just quickly reached out and flipped the phone face-down.

Directly across the table, Sarah’s phone also vibrated. Sarah glanced down at her screen. The contact name read: **Lax Toss 🙄**

Sarah let out a quiet, frustrated sigh, rolling her eyes as she hit the lock button to silence it and shoved the phone into her backpack. She looked up and caught Lauren's eye.

"Boy drama?" Sarah asked sympathetically, nodding toward Lauren’s face-down phone.

"Something like that," Lauren deflected with a tight, forced smile. "Just... a lot of texts today."

"I hear you. Mine's being incredibly aggro this week," Sarah commiserated, waving a hand dismissively. "It's exhausting. But anyway, what's your major? You seem like an English girl to me."

Lauren visibly relaxed, grateful for the pivot. "Nailed it. English literature."

They smoothly transitioned back into the safety of academic small talk, neither of them mentioning a name, entirely unaware that the same aggressively insecure lacrosse player was currently sitting in his room on the other side of campus, glaring at his phone and wondering why neither of his girlfriends was answering him.

By a quarter to one, the dynamic of the table began to shift as the reality of afternoon classes set in.

"Alright, team," Mark announced, standing up and grabbing his tray. "I have a two-hour chemistry lab on the other side of the quad, so I need to run."

"Same," Hannah said, standing up to join him. "And Zoë and I have a seminar at one."

Sarah checked her watch and groaned. "Jess and Chloe and I have a mandatory freshman orientation module in ten minutes. We have to bolt." Sarah slung her backpack over her shoulder, turning to Lauren. "It was so great meeting you. We all need to get together for dinner tonight. Same tables?"

"Absolutely," Lauren smiled. "See you tonight."

Within a minute, the chaotic energy of the mega-group dispersed, leaving only Jordan, Aubrie, Madison, and Lauren sitting at the long table.

With the loud buffer of the group gone, the silence felt a little heavier. Lauren looked down at her dark phone. She knew there were at least five unread messages waiting for her, and she felt physically sick at the thought of reading them.

Aubrie leaned forward, her elbows on the table, studying Lauren’s face. The protective instincts she usually reserved for Jordan immediately extended across the table.

"Lauren," Aubrie said softly, cutting right to the chase. "Are you okay? You've been looking at your phone like it's a bomb all through lunch."

Lauren let out a shaky breath, tracing the edge of her phone case. The absolute trust that defined Suite 214/216 made it impossible to lie to them.

"It's my boyfriend," Lauren admitted quietly, her voice barely carrying over the dining hall chatter. "We were on the phone last night while you guys were showing Jordan the Dark Aero desktop. I... I told him about Jordan."

Jordan, who had his silver mPad resting on his lap, looked up, his eyes wide with quiet concern.

"He didn't take it well," Lauren continued, her voice thick with unshed frustration. "He didn't just think it was weird. He immediately jumped to the worst, most disgusting conclusion possible. He accused Jordan of playing a pity card to try and sleep with all of us. He called our suite a 'messed-up thing'."

Madison's jaw dropped. "Are you kidding me?"

"I wish I was," Lauren whispered, blinking back a sudden wave of tears. "And now he's blowing up my phone because I haven't answered him since nine-thirty this morning. He's demanding to know where I am and if I'm with 'the wheelchair guy'. He's just so... angry. And controlling. And I'm just sitting here realizing that I'm not happy. I haven't been happy for a while."

She looked up at the three of them, terrified that she was bringing too much drama into their peaceful dynamic.

Instead of judgment, Jordan reached across the table. He didn't type anything. He just placed his hand gently over hers, a quiet, physical anchor of solidarity.

"Lauren," Madison said fiercely, reaching out to squeeze her other arm. "He does not get to talk to you like that, and he certainly doesn't get to talk about our family like that. Mark told you last night, and we are telling you right now: we have your back."

"You don't have to put up with him just because you're used to him," Aubrie added, her voice steady and fiercely protective. "Whatever you decide to do, we are right here."

Lauren looked at the three of them. She had known them for barely four days, but the safety she felt in this exact moment was more profound than anything she had felt in months of dating Tom.

"Thank you," Lauren breathed, a genuine, tearful smile breaking through the anxiety.

Aubrie stood up and walked around the table, pulling Lauren out of her chair. Madison immediately joined them, wrapping her arms around Lauren's shoulders. Jordan locked the wheels of his chair and leaned forward, wrapping his arms securely around Lauren's waist to complete the circle.

It was a tight, solid, profoundly grounding group hug. For Jordan, physical touch was no longer something to flinch away from; it was how his family communicated safety.

"Okay," Lauren whispered, wiping her eyes as they finally pulled back. "I have a two o'clock literature lecture. I'll be okay. I promise."

"We'll see you back at the suite before dinner," Aubrie smiled, unlocking Jordan's brakes. "You're safe with us, Lauren. Always."
