# Part 2: The Australian Voice – 7:15 AM

The morning routine kicked into gear. With a gentle, clinical respect that continued to amaze him, Aubrie and Lauren helped Jordan navigate the difficult process of getting out of his pajamas and into his day clothes.

Just as they were finishing, a soft knock echoed from the connecting bathroom door.

"Come in!" Aubrie called out.

The door opened, and Sarah Braithwaite stepped into Room 214. She was fully dressed, her dark brown hair pulled back into her signature messy ponytail.

"Morning, everyone," Sarah smiled, her fierce intelligence bright even at this early hour. She looked directly at Lauren. "I just wanted to say a massive thank you for letting me crash in your bed last night. I love my suitemates upstairs, but being down here with you guys was exactly what I needed after that absolute nightmare with Tom."

"Anytime, Sarah," Lauren beamed. "You're always welcome."

Sarah turned her dark, almost black eyes toward Jordan. Without missing a beat, she walked over to his wheelchair and wrapped her arms around his shoulders in a tight, fierce hug.

Jordan hugged her back, his eyes darting immediately to Aubrie. The girl who had held him through his meltdown was just smiling as she gathered her textbooks. It defied all of his legacy programming. How could she not be mad? How could any of this be real?

He pulled his Vanguard Pro back onto his lap, his fingers trembling slightly as he opened his EchoType software. He needed to express how overwhelming and beautiful this lack of jealousy was.

As his fingers hovered over the keyboard, Madison peeked her head over the edge of the upper bunk, Bed C, her bright blue eyes full of cheerful energy. "Morning, suite family!" she chirped, hopping down the wooden ladder.

She walked straight over to Jordan and planted a happy, affectionate kiss right on his cheek.

Sarah, watching this easy display of affection, felt a surge of belonging. She smiled, stepped forward, and placed a firm, equally affectionate kiss on Jordan's other cheek.

Jordan’s heart hammered against his ribs. He quickly typed his sentence and moved his cursor over the execute button. But he hesitated.

He looked up at Sarah. He was using the "Sydney" voice profile—a crisp, synthesized Australian accent that he had chosen specifically as a quiet act of rebellion against his mother's forced use of a grating, robotic male voice. He was suddenly terrified. What if Sarah felt mocked? What if the Australian girl was deeply offended that he was appropriating her accent through a computer program?

Taking a deep breath, he clicked the execute key.

*I am so incredibly happy to be going to class with all of you today,* the clear, bright Australian-accented voice announced to the room.

Sarah blinked, her head tilting slightly as she processed the synthesized cadence. Jordan tensed, waiting for the fierce temper he knew she possessed to flare up.

Instead, a massive, delighted grin broke across Sarah's face.

"Mate!" Sarah laughed, stepping forward to throw her arms around his neck in another fierce hug. "Is that your voice? That is absolutely brilliant! It sounds just like home!"

Jordan let out a massive, shaky breath of pure relief, a genuine smile finally matching hers.

"Alright, team," Zoë announced, stepping through the bathroom door from Room 216, her analytical gray eyes sweeping the room. "The emotional bonding sequence is complete. If we wish to secure a table at Newman Commons before the 8:00 AM rush, we must depart immediately."

"She's right," Madison said, grabbing her backpack. "Let's go get some breakfast!"
