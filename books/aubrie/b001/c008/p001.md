Jordan woke slowly, the dim morning light filtering through the single window of Room 214. The recurring, terrifying nightmare of falling was completely absent. Instead, he felt a deep, soothing warmth pressed against his side. He blinked, his poor vision struggling to focus, until the soft features and long, reddish-brown hair of the girl sleeping next to him resolved into shapes he recognized.

It was Lauren. She was sleeping peacefully in his accessible single Twin XL bed, a physical manifestation of the promise they had made the night before.

As Jordan shifted slightly, Lauren stirred. She opened her warm brown eyes and offered him a soft, sleepy smile. "Good morning, J," she whispered.

She reached down to the floor beside the bed and pulled up a thick, knitted winter hat from her overnight bag. "I was freezing last night before I got under the covers," she said, holding the hat up for him to see. "I was almost tempted to put my favorite toque on."

Jordan stared at the knitted fabric, his brow furrowing in confusion. He slowly reached over to his Vanguard Pro laptop resting on the nightstand, pulling it securely onto his lap. Bypassing the EchoType speech software so as not to wake the others, he launched his sleek, dark-themed Aura Glade terminal. He opened NeoEdit, scaling the bright, neon-green text up massively against the stark black background.

*What is a toque?* he typed, angling the screen so she could read it.

Lauren let out a quiet, melodic laugh. "Oh, right! It's what we call a winter beanie. I'm a dual citizen, Jordan. I grew up in Canada."

Jordan's eyes widened. He had spent his entire life trapped in a small, isolated bubble in Hampton Roads. The realization that he was currently sharing a bed with someone from an entirely different country was dizzying.

Before he could process this international revelation, Lauren leaned forward. With a natural, unhesitating warmth, she pressed a gentle, lingering kiss to his cheek.

At that exact moment, the springs on the lower bunk across the room creaked. Aubrie sat up from Bed B, rubbing the sleep from her eyes.

Jordan froze. The heavy, restrictive programming of his mother's world instantly flared to life in his chest. *Jealousy. Anger. Punishment.* He had just been kissed by another girl in his own bed, and Aubrie had seen the entire thing. He squeezed his eyes shut, his shoulders bracing for the inevitable screaming.

But the room remained completely quiet.

He opened his eyes to see Aubrie walking toward them. Her face wasn't twisted in anger; it was glowing with a serene, profound happiness. She knelt beside his wheelchair and leaned over the bed. Without a single word, she pressed her own warm, loving kiss to his other cheek.

Aubrie caught Lauren's eye, and the two women shared a brilliant, silent smile. The constellation was working perfectly.
