# Book: Jordan's World Trek

## Chapter 1: The Northern Ascent – Saturday, May 23rd, 2015

### Part 1: The Vanguard Awakes – 5:00 AM

The sharp, digital chime of an alarm clock pierced the cool, quiet air of the Brooks family basement.

Jordan opened his eyes, the absolute absence of his childhood nightmares still feeling like a daily miracle. He was sandwiched comfortably in the center of a mobile queen-sized bed, surrounded by the deep, rhythmic breathing of his pact. To his left, Aubrie was curled up against his side. To his right, Lauren’s auburn hair was splayed across his shoulder, her arm thrown protectively across his chest.

Around them in the dim light of the expansive finished basement, the rest of the vanguard was slowly stirring. The space had been converted into a massive, sleepover-style barracks for the night. Mark and Hannah shared a bed near the far wall, while Zoë and Madison were untangling themselves from their blankets on the adjacent mattress. Near the stairwell, the Australian duo of Sarah and Jessica were already sitting up, whispering quietly to each other.

"Morning, J," Aubrie mumbled, her voice thick with sleep as she rubbed her eyes and sat up.

There was no hesitation and no awkward morning shuffling. The clinical, judgment-free trust that had been forged over the past year in Room 214 immediately took over. Aubrie threw the covers back, grasped the handles of the basic manual push wheelchair kept downstairs specifically for this purpose, and wheeled it flush against the mattress. With Lauren's gentle assistance from the other side, they executed a flawless pivot-transfer, settling Jordan into the seat.

Aubrie wheeled him into the large downstairs bathroom. The radical, practical intimacy of their family was fully on display; there was zero shame and absolutely no demand for privacy. Aubrie assisted Jordan with his morning toileting needs with clinical respect, seamlessly taking care of her own needs in the shared space without missing a beat.

When Aubrie wheeled him back out into the main basement area, the space was a flurry of motion. The group was casually changing out of their pajamas, treating the shared space with the same absolute, unbothered trust. Aubrie and Lauren worked together, helping Jordan navigate the difficult fine-motor task of getting dressed, easing his arms into a soft, comfortable travel hoodie.

"Alright, big guy, ready for your flight?" Lauren beamed, her brown eyes sparkling with the excitement of returning to her childhood home.

Without waiting for an answer, Lauren stepped in front of his manual chair. She squared her stance, wrapping her arms securely around his waist. With a grunt of effort, she engaged her core and deadlifted him entirely out of the chair. Jordan held on tight as she carefully and securely carried him up the carpeted basement stairs.

She emerged onto the main floor of the Brooks estate and gently deposited him into his matte-black, 350-pound power chair, which had been charging by the entryway all night.

The main floor was a chaotic, beautiful symphony of logistics. An absolute army of people was moving through the kitchen. Daniel and Laura Brooks were orchestrating breakfast, sliding plates of eggs and toast across the massive island. The Brooks siblings—Megan, Caleb, Chloe and her husband Ethan, Lucas, Owen, and Jacob—were double-checking passports, Nexus cards, and luggage tags.

"Eat up, everyone!" Daniel announced, his booming London accent cutting through the chatter. "We have a tight schedule! Two vans are running, and we want to beat the morning traffic."

Jordan took a bite of breakfast, watching the chaotic, eighteen-person family operate with flawless precision. Less than an hour later, the entire group funneled out the front doors and into the cool Virginia morning. Two massive, rented passenger vans were idling in the driveway.

Megan lowered the hydraulic lift on the trailing van, allowing Jordan to smoothly roll his massive power chair into the secure rear locking mechanism. As the rest of the pact piled into the seats around him, the doors slammed shut.

The engines revved, and the two vans pulled out of the driveway, merging onto the northbound lanes of Interstate 81. They left the familiar rolling hills of Christiansburg behind, speeding toward Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport. The world tour had officially begun.

### Part 2: Operation Yellow Ribbon – 6:30 AM

Traffic on Interstate 81 was surprisingly sparse for a Saturday morning, allowing the two heavy passenger vans to make excellent time as they cruised north through the New River Valley. Inside the lead van, the atmosphere was a mix of early-morning grogginess and electric anticipation.

Jordan sat securely locked into the rear transport space, his sleek Vanguard Pro resting on his lap. Beside him, Lauren had twisted around in her seat to face him. She knew Jordan was terrified of the unknown, and she needed him to understand exactly what kind of place they were flying into.

"I was only five years old when the airspace closed," Lauren explained softly, her warm brown eyes fixed on him. "Thirty-eight airplanes full of terrified, stranded people were forced to land at our tiny airport."

Jordan watched her, his hands resting quietly on his keyboard. He knew about 9/11, but the concept of what happened on the ground in Canada was entirely foreign to him.

"My parents sat me down and explained the whole story once I was old enough to really understand it," Lauren continued, her voice thick with a fierce, quiet pride. "The population of Gander practically doubled in a few hours. We didn't have the hotels or the infrastructure for that many people, so the town just... opened its doors. I grew up watching my parents give up their beds, cook meals around the clock, and care for people they had never even met. I was raised in a culture of radical hospitality."

She reached out and gave his knee a gentle squeeze. "In my hometown, Jordan, when someone is stranded far from home, you don't look at them as an inconvenience or a burden. You take them in, and you make them safe. That's where we're going."

Jordan let out a slow, shaky breath. For eighteen years, his mother had drilled into him that the world was hostile, transactional, and eager to cast him aside as a burden. Lauren was taking him to a geographic location that fundamentally proved his mother wrong.

By the time the vans pulled into the drop-off lane at Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport, the sun was fully above the horizon. Megan expertly lowered the hydraulic lift, and Jordan rolled his massive, 350-pound power chair onto the pavement.

ROA was a small, easily navigable regional hub, completely devoid of the chaotic, aggressive crowds found in massive international airports. Still, the reality of the impending transit process made Jordan's chest tight. As the group gathered their rolling luggage and moved through the automatic sliding doors into the terminal, Jordan seamlessly adjusted his joystick, sliding his heavy-duty chair directly alongside Lauren.

Without needing to type a single word, he reached out. Lauren immediately uncurled her fingers and took his hand, lacing her fingers through his. She gave his hand a firm, grounding squeeze. For this leg of the journey into her home country, she was his Canadian guide and his absolute anchor.

They approached the check-in counters. A bright blue sign hung overhead, bearing the globe logo of Meridian Airlines.

Daniel Brooks stepped up to the counter, casually dropping a massive, heavy stack of eighteen blue, green, and burgundy passports onto the desk.

"Good morning," Daniel said cheerfully to the ticket agent. "We are checking in for the Meridian Express flight to Washington Dulles."

As the agent began the monumental task of processing the group, Daniel turned back to address his family and the pact.

"Just a quick reminder regarding the itinerary," Daniel noted, his crisp British accent carrying over the hum of the terminal. "As we discussed during the planning phase, this initial hop to Dulles is operated on a rather small regional jet. Meridian Express does not offer a First Class cabin on this specific aircraft, so we are flying standard commercial economy for the next forty-five minutes. Our premium seating arrangements will officially begin once we transfer at IAD."

The group nodded, completely unfazed. They had studied the massive global itinerary and knew exactly what to expect.

Jordan held Lauren's hand a little tighter. Armed with his Nexus card, his custom-built terminal, and an eighteen-person fortress of chosen family, he was finally ready to face the skies.

### Part 3: The Security Checkpoint – 7:00 AM

As the eighteen members of the global roster moved toward the TSA security checkpoint, Daniel Brooks gently placed a hand on the back of Jordan’s heavy power chair, bringing him to a slow stop just before the queue. Lauren stayed firmly anchored by his side, her fingers still laced with his.

"Alright, Jordan, listen to me," Daniel said quietly, kneeling down to ensure he was perfectly at eye level. "We are about to hand our passports and boarding passes to the federal agent. Because you have a Nexus card and a passport, they are going to use the name printed on those documents. They are going to call you Aubrey Fuller."

Jordan’s breath hitched, his shoulders instantly hiking up toward his ears. Hearing his legal birth name was a massive, visceral trigger, inextricably linked to the eighteen years of suffocating abuse he had endured under Aubrie Sinclair.

"I know it hurts," Daniel murmured, his deep London accent a steady, grounding force. "But it is strictly a federal security protocol. It means absolutely nothing. To me, to Lauren, to Aubrie, and to everyone else in this family, you are Jordan. You just have to let the agent say the name on the paper, and then we are through. Can you do that for me?"

Jordan looked at Daniel, then up at Lauren. She gave his hand an encouraging squeeze. He let out a long, shaky exhale, unclenched his jaw, and gave a sharp nod.

They approached the podium. The TSA officer checked the documents, read the name "Aubrey Fuller," and waved them through. Jordan flinched at the sound, but anchored by Lauren's grip and Daniel's promise, the panic didn't completely consume him.

Once past the document check, the sheer logistical weight of moving eighteen people through the X-ray belts began. While the rest of the Brooks siblings and the core pact managed the group's carry-on luggage, Daniel and Lauren stayed exclusively focused on Jordan.

A senior TSA agent in a blue uniform approached them. His name tag read *Officer Miller*. He took one look at the massive, 350-pound power chair and the non-verbal young man sitting in it, and his entire demeanor shifted into a mode of calm, profound empathy.

"Good morning, gentlemen," Officer Miller said, speaking directly to Jordan with quiet respect. He looked at the heavy machinery. "Sir, are you able to stand or walk at all for just a moment to help us screen the wheelchair?"

Jordan swallowed hard and simply shook his head no.

"That's completely fine," Officer Miller assured him without missing a beat.

Lauren immediately stepped in front of the chair. "I can carry him through the metal detector, and then we can do a private screening for his chair?"

"That would be perfect, ma'am," Miller nodded, holding the line of passengers back to give them space. "Take your time."

Lauren squared her stance, wrapped her arms securely around Jordan’s waist, and deadlifted him cleanly out of his seat. To stabilize himself, Jordan instinctively laced his arms around Lauren's neck as she carried him through the metal detector arches.

As the machine beeped green, a sudden, profound realization washed over Jordan. Resting his head against Lauren's shoulder, he realized he actually *liked* the feeling. The firm, secure hold didn't feel like a clinical transfer; it felt exactly like being wrapped in a warm, protective hug. Surrounded by her strength, the heavy, suffocating anxiety of the airport simply melted away.

Just for a moment, he didn't feel like a scared, disabled boy. He felt like a man who simply needed a small accommodation to get through security, and that accommodation was being effortlessly and lovingly met.

Lauren gently set him down on a sterile waiting bench on the other side. Officer Miller then carefully rolled the empty power chair through the designated gate and brought it right back to Jordan. Once Lauren helped him pivot back into his seat, Miller guided the three of them into a quiet, partitioned screening room away from the chaotic noise of the main checkpoint.

What followed was a revelation. In the medical and administrative settings of his past, Jordan had been handled like a piece of burdensome luggage. But Officer Miller communicated every single micro-step of the process.

"Alright, Jordan, I'm going to swab your armrests now for explosives residue," Miller explained warmly, holding up a small cloth patch. "I'm not going to touch you, just the metal on the chair."

When it came time to inspect the heavy Vanguard Pro laptop, Miller handled it with absolute, reverent care, gently swabbing the casing and setting it back on Jordan's lap. The agent's fluid efficiency and deep, unspoken understanding of Jordan's physical boundaries heavily implied that he had a special needs family member of his own waiting for him at home.

"You're all clear, sir," Officer Miller smiled, opening the partition door. "Have a great flight."

Jordan rolled out of the private screening room and into the air-conditioned concourse. Standing just a few yards away, waiting anxiously by the conveyor belts with her backpack slung over her shoulder, was Aubrie Kelly.

The moment Jordan spotted her, he pushed his joystick forward. He bypassed the rest of the group gathering their shoes and rolled straight toward her. He didn't even bother to pull up his text-to-speech terminal. He just threw his arms around her waist, burying his face into her stomach in a tight, desperate hug of pure relief.

"I've got you," Aubrie beamed, immediately wrapping her arms around his shoulders and resting her cheek against the top of his head. "You did so good, J. The hard part is over."

When he finally pulled back, a massive, genuine smile was plastered across his face. Aubrie didn't let him go far. She reached down and took his left hand, intertwining her fingers with his.

With his right hand resting securely on the joystick, Jordan steered his heavy-duty chair down the concourse, holding Aubrie's hand the entire way. Surrounded by his chosen family, they headed toward the departure gate to board the Meridian Express flight to Washington Dulles.

### Part 4: The Ten-Thousand Foot Rule – 7:30 AM

The boarding area at the Meridian Express gate was quiet, populated entirely by the eighteen members of the chosen family. Through the floor-to-ceiling glass windows, the small regional jet sat parked on the tarmac, its engines whining with a low, mechanical hum.

Before the gate agent called for pre-boarding, Daniel Brooks knelt beside Jordan’s heavy-duty power chair. He carried the sleek Vanguard Pro laptop case over his shoulder.

"Alright, Jordan, let's talk about the logistics of this aircraft," Daniel said, his deep London accent exuding a calm, steady authority. "The aviation administration updated their electronic device policies a couple of years ago. We are allowed to use small devices like an mPhone from gate to gate, as long as they are in airplane mode. However, there is a strict rule regarding larger hardware."

Daniel tapped the Vanguard Pro case. "Because your laptop is heavy, it is classified as a physical safety hazard during takeoff and landing. It must remain zipped in a bag and stowed completely under the seat in front of you until the aircraft reaches ten thousand feet. And honestly, Jordan, the time we actually spend cruising above that altitude today is only going to be about ten minutes. It is hardly worth the effort to unearth the machine just to immediately pack it away again. I recommend just using your mPhone for this leg."

Jordan’s breath hitched slightly. His Vanguard Pro was his primary voice. Transitioning to the smaller screen of his mPhone meant typing with his thumbs, which his cerebral palsy made clumsy and exhausting.

"I know it is daunting to lose your primary communication tool," Daniel reassured him, reading the anxiety perfectly. "But it will only be for a very short time. And you will not be alone."

Aubrie immediately stepped up beside the wheelchair. She rested her hand on his shoulder, her dark eyes fierce and protective. "I'm right next to you, J," she promised. "You don't need a heavy laptop to talk to me. If you're scared, you squeeze my hand. If you need me to stop talking, you tap my knee. We have our own system."

Jordan let out a shaky breath, the tension in his shoulders dropping. He pulled his mPhone from his pocket, making sure it was secure, and gave Daniel and Aubrie a firm nod. He was ready.

The boarding process for a regional jet required a heavily coordinated effort. Because the aircraft door was too narrow for the massive 350-pound power chair, Jordan had to leave his mobility fortress at the end of the jet bridge to be loaded into the cargo hold. With seamless, practiced grace, Mark and Lauren executed a supportive transfer, helping Jordan walk the few halting steps onto the aircraft and into the second row of the cabin.

The regional jet featured an asymmetrical seating arrangement: a single row of seats on the left, a narrow aisle, and two seats on the right.

Daniel took his assigned place in the single seat on the left side of the aisle. Jordan was carefully guided into the window seat on the right side, giving him an unobstructed view of the wing and the Roanoke landscape. Aubrie slid into the aisle seat directly next to him, completing the protective perimeter.

As the rest of the eighteen-person family filled the rows behind them, the flight attendant secured the cabin doors.

Aubrie reached down and took Jordan’s left hand, lacing her fingers securely through his. Jordan leaned his head back against the headrest, his right hand gripping the plastic armrest tightly as the regional jet pushed back from the gate.

Across the narrow aisle, Daniel caught Jordan's eye. He didn't offer a patronizing thumbs-up; he simply gave a slow, reassuring nod, projecting absolute safety.

The aircraft turned onto the active runway. The twin engines roared to life, a deep, vibrating mechanical thunder that shook the cabin. Jordan squeezed Aubrie's hand with white-knuckled intensity. The acceleration pressed him heavily into the back of his seat, a sensation unlike anything he had ever felt.

For eighteen years, his world had been incredibly small, confined to a single apartment and the crushing gravity of his mother's control. But as the nose of the Meridian Express jet pitched upward, the wheels leaving the Virginia tarmac, that gravity finally broke.

Jordan looked out the small oval window. The ground fell away, the buildings and roads shrinking into miniature models. A sudden, overwhelming wave of exhilaration flooded his chest. He was flying. He was actually flying.

He looked away from the window and turned to Aubrie. He didn't have his laptop, and he couldn't speak a word, but the massive, brilliant smile lighting up his face communicated absolutely everything she needed to know.

Ten minutes later, a soft, melodic chime echoed through the cabin.

The PA system above them crackled to life with a brief burst of static.

"Ladies and gentlemen," the flight attendant's voice announced over the cabin speakers. "We have reached ten thousand feet, and you are now free to use your approved electronic devices. However, due to our extremely short time in the air today, we will not be offering a beverage or snack service. Please sit back, relax, and enjoy the quick flight to Washington."

Jordan’s brow furrowed. He stared at the back of the seat in front of him, his highly analytical, literal-processing mind immediately catching the geographic discrepancy.

He pulled out his mPhone, his thumbs moving deliberately over the glass keyboard. He angled the screen toward the aisle so both Aubrie and Daniel could read it.

*Why did she say Washington?* the text read on his screen. *IAD is Dulles International Airport. Dulles is in Loudoun and Fairfax counties. We are flying within Virginia, from Roanoke to Dulles. We are not flying to Washington D.C.*

Aubrie leaned over to read the screen, and a massive, delighted smile broke across her face. Even while hurtling through the sky at four hundred miles an hour for the very first time in his life, Jordan’s brilliant, logical brain was firing on all cylinders.

Across the narrow aisle, Daniel Brooks let out a warm, booming chuckle.

"You are geographically and absolutely correct, Jordan," Daniel smiled, his crisp British accent carrying easily over the hum of the twin engines. "The physical tarmac is squarely in Virginia. But the aviation industry bases its destination names on the primary metropolitan zone the airport serves. Because Dulles is the primary international gateway for the United States capital, Meridian Airlines simply calls it 'Washington' to avoid confusing international tourists who don't understand state boundaries."

Jordan tapped his fingers thoughtfully against the plastic armrest, processing the data. He typed another quick message on his mPhone.

*That is highly illogical. They should just use accurate coordinates.*

"I couldn't agree more, mate," Daniel grinned, leaning back in his seat.

Jordan swallowed, his throat suddenly feeling incredibly dry. Between the nervous adrenaline of the TSA checkpoint, the sheer thrill of the takeoff, and the dry, recirculated cabin air, he was parched. But the flight attendant had just explicitly announced there would be no service.

Aubrie, hyper-observant as always, noticed him swallow hard and lick his dry lips.

"Well, since Meridian Express is depriving you of a beverage service on your inaugural flight, I came prepared," Aubrie announced.

She reached down into her backpack, which was stowed beneath the seat in front of her. Knowing the strict, predictable timelines of short regional hops and understanding that Jordan might need a quick comfort item, she had stopped at a kiosk in the Roanoke terminal right after they cleared Officer Miller's security check.

She proudly produced a cold bottle of apple juice, twisting the plastic cap open for him to bypass his fine-motor struggles, and handed it directly to him.

Jordan looked at the apple juice, and then up at Aubrie. His mother had never anticipated his physical needs without turning it into a heavy, transactional burden. But here, suspended thousands of feet in the air, Aubrie was simply taking care of him because she wanted to.

He took a long, refreshing sip of the cold juice, the sweet taste cutting right through his dry throat, and looked back out the oval window. The rolling green patchwork of the Virginia landscape slipped past below them. He wasn't just surviving his first flight; surrounded by his chosen family and sipping his favorite drink, he was completely safe.

### Part 5: The International Threshold – 8:30 AM

The descent into Washington Dulles International Airport was a jarring, physical reality check.

As the Meridian Express regional jet pitched downward, the change in cabin air pressure pressed heavily against Jordan's eardrums. A few minutes later, the landing gear deployed with a heavy, mechanical *thud* that vibrated straight through the floorboards. When the wheels finally touched the tarmac, the twin engines roared to life with aggressive reverse thrust, shaking the entire cabin.

For Jordan and Aubrie, both of whom processed sensory input at a raw, unfiltered volume due to their shared neurodivergence, the sheer mechanical violence of the landing was completely overwhelming. Jordan squeezed his eyes shut, his hands clamping down on his armrests. Beside him, Aubrie tensed, her breathing growing shallow as the chaotic noise and vibration assaulted her nervous system.

Instinctively, Jordan uncurled his right hand from the armrest and reached across the narrow space, tapping Aubrie's knee. She immediately grabbed his hand, interlacing her fingers with his. They held on with white-knuckled intensity, silently grounding each other through the sensory spike until the jet finally slowed to a smooth taxi.

As the plane navigated toward the sprawling, chaotic expanse of the IAD terminals, Jordan looked out his oval window. The sheer size of the airport was staggering. Massive international jets bearing the flags of foreign nations were parked at the gates.

A sudden, dizzying realization hit him, completely overriding the lingering sensory overload.

He pulled his mPhone from his pocket, his thumbs flying over the glass screen. He angled it across the aisle toward Daniel.

*The next plane goes to Canada,* Jordan typed, the cursor blinking rapidly. *I have never left Virginia before today. I need my Passport. And my Nexus card. Do I need to hold them now?*

Daniel smiled warmly, understanding the sudden spike of travel anxiety. He reached into his coat pocket, where he was safeguarding the stack of eighteen documents, ready to hand Jordan's over to ease his mind.

But Lauren, who was sitting directly behind Daniel in the single seat of row three, leaned forward over the aisle.

"You don't need to hold them just yet, J," Lauren said softly, her warm brown eyes meeting his. "Since we are flying out of the US, nobody is going to check our passports at the gate here. When we land at YYZ in Toronto, that's where we clear Canadian Customs. You and I, and everyone else with a Nexus card, get to bypass the regular lines and use the automated kiosks. It’s incredibly fast and quiet. You'll only need to scan it then."

Jordan took a deep breath, processing her calm, practical explanation. He nodded, letting Daniel keep the documents securely stowed.

"Lauren is absolutely right," Daniel affirmed, his crisp British accent carrying a reassuring authority. "Which brings us to our logistics for the next few hours. Welcome to Washington Dulles, everyone."

### Part 6: The IAD Layover Strategy – 9:00 AM

To answer the massive logistical challenge of managing an eighteen-person family on an international layover, Daniel Brooks had mapped out the morning with military precision.

"Our flight to Toronto Pearson does not board for another three hours," Daniel explained to the group as they waited for the cabin doors to open. "We are not going to sit at a noisy, crowded gate. Because we are all flying on First Class international tickets, we have complimentary access to the Meridian Premier Lounge in Concourse C."

This was the ultimate travel hack for Jordan's autistic neurology. Instead of enduring the echoing, fluorescent-lit chaos of the main terminal, the group would spend their layover in a highly controlled, sensory-friendly environment.

Once they disembarked and Jordan was safely transferred back into his massive 350-pound power chair, Daniel led the vanguard through the terminal. They bypassed the bustling food courts and swiped their boarding passes at the frosted glass doors of the Premier Lounge.

Inside, the atmosphere completely shifted. The floors were thickly carpeted, absorbing the ambient sound. Soft, warm lighting replaced the harsh overhead fluorescents. There were rows of plush, leather armchairs, private accessible restrooms, and a massive complimentary buffet.

Jordan parked his chair near the floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the tarmac, plugging his Vanguard Pro into a nearby wall outlet to top off the battery. Aubrie and Madison immediately flanked him, bringing plates of fresh fruit and pastries from the buffet, while Zoë pragmatically reviewed the terminal maps on her mPad.

"I have verified the aircraft for our next leg," Zoë announced, pushing her glasses up the bridge of her nose. "We are upgrading to a larger mainline commercial jet. The First Class cabin features a standard two-by-two seating configuration."

"Which means Zoë and I get the two seats right across the aisle from you two this time!" Madison grinned, bumping her shoulder playfully against Aubrie's.

"Exactly," Daniel confirmed, returning from the espresso bar with a cup of tea. "Jordan, you and Aubrie will maintain your window and aisle pair on the right. Madison and Zoë will have the matching pair on the left. Lauren, you will be right behind them with Sarah, and Hannah will take the pair across the aisle with Mark."

Jordan looked at the massive international jets rolling past the lounge windows, and then back at the women sitting around him. He was about to leave the United States for the very first time in his life, flying toward a foreign country in a wide, comfortable First Class seat, sitting shoulder-to-shoulder with the girl he was falling in love with.

The terrified, isolated boy from Virginia Beach was officially gone. The world traveler had arrived.

### Part 7: The Border Briefing – 10:15 AM

The hum of the Meridian Premier Lounge was a soothing backdrop as the morning stretched on. Jordan sat comfortably in his power chair by the massive windows, watching the baggage carts zip across the tarmac. The anxiety that had gripped him earlier that morning had completely dissolved, replaced by a profound, grounding warmth.

Lauren had pulled one of the plush leather armchairs right up to his left side. She was holding his left hand in both of hers, her thumbs gently tracing the back of his knuckles. On his right side, Aubrie was perched on the armrest of his chair, her hand securely laced through his right hand.

For a boy who had spent eighteen years flinching at the slightest physical contact, being anchored by the gentle, deliberate touch of two women who cared about him was nothing short of a miracle. He didn't feel overwhelmed; he felt entirely, perfectly safe.

"So, let's talk about Toronto," Lauren said softly, leaning in close so her warm brown eyes locked onto his. "When we touch down at YYZ, the airport is going to look incredibly massive, and there will be a lot of signs pointing toward Canada Border Services. But you don't need to stress about the crowds at all."

Jordan gave her hand a gentle squeeze, listening intently.

"Because you have a Nexus card, just like my family and the Brooks family, we get to bypass the regular international lines completely," Lauren explained, her voice projecting the calm, radical hospitality she had learned growing up in Gander. "We get funneled into a dedicated, expedited lane. It’s mostly just automated kiosks. You’ll scan your card, the machine takes a quick photo to verify it's you, and the gates open. It is incredibly fast, quiet, and painless."

Jordan let out a breath he didn't realize he had been holding. The thought of standing in a massive, echoing customs hall while a border agent interrogated him had been a quiet fear building in the back of his mind.

He gently untangled his right hand from Aubrie's grip just long enough to pull his mPhone from his pocket. He typed a quick question using his thumb, then angled the screen toward Lauren and Aubrie.

*What about Sarah and Jessica?* the text read.

Aubrie smiled, wrapping her arm comfortably around his shoulders now that his hand was occupied.

"Sarah and Jessica have a slightly different logistical path," Aubrie explained. "Because they are Australian nationals traveling on Australian passports, they aren't eligible for the North American Nexus program. When we hit the customs hall, our group will have to split up just for a little bit."

"They have to go through the standard international ePassport gates," Lauren added, nodding in agreement. "It’s a separate line, and because Pearson is a major global hub, it will definitely take them a bit longer to clear the border agents than it will take us."

Jordan frowned slightly, his protective instincts flaring up for his friends. *We shouldn't just leave them behind,* he typed quickly.

"We absolutely won't," Lauren promised, her smile widening at his unselfish concern. "Daniel already has it mapped out. Once our Nexus group clears the automated gates, we are going to head straight to the Maple Leaf Lounge in the domestic terminal. We will secure a quiet area, grab some Canadian snacks, and wait right there until Sarah and Jessica make it through the international line and join us."

"Nobody gets left behind, J," Aubrie murmured, resting her cheek against his shoulder. "We all cross the finish line together before we take that final flight into Newfoundland."

Jordan slipped his mPhone back into his pocket and immediately reached out, sliding his right hand back into Aubrie's waiting grip. He looked from Aubrie’s fiercely protective smile to Lauren’s warm, steady gaze. They had every single detail managed, effectively shielding him from the overwhelming friction of international travel.

As the departure screens in the lounge flashed, indicating that their mainline jet to Toronto was finally beginning its boarding process, Jordan felt a quiet, powerful surge of confidence. He was ready for Canada.

### Part 8: The Outback Lift – 11:30 AM

After a final round of snacks and a highly coordinated trip to the Premier Lounge's accessible restrooms, Daniel Brooks gathered the group. It was time.

As they walked out of the lounge and merged into the bustling concourse heading toward their departure gate, the sheer gravity of the moment finally settled squarely on Jordan’s shoulders. This was it. When the wheels of this mainline jet touched down again, he wasn't just going to be in another state. He was going to be in a completely different country. He was going to Canada.

They reached the gate just as First Class boarding was called. Jordan steered his massive 350-pound power chair down the sloped jet bridge, Aubrie walking faithfully by his side.

At the end of the bridge, parked right next to the aircraft door, a flight attendant was waiting with a standard-issue airport aisle chair. It was a narrow, rigid, heavily strapped contraption designed to wheel non-ambulatory passengers down the tight aircraft aisles.

Jordan took one look at the aisle chair and immediately stopped his power chair. His chest tightened. He remembered the cramped, restrictive, and deeply uncomfortable experience of being strapped into a similar chair on the previous flight from ROA to IAD. He absolutely hated it. He looked at the flight attendant and vehemently shook his head no.

Before Aubrie could even reach for her phone to try and negotiate a solution, a shadow fell over Jordan's chair.

Jessica Gallagher stepped forward from the line of boarding passengers. Jordan had known Jessica for a while now, and she had been smoothly integrating herself into their protective pact, but as she stepped directly in front of him, he truly *looked* at her for the first time.

Standing at an imposing 6'3", Jessica possessed a heavy-muscled, natural bodybuilder physique honed by a lifetime of physical labor. She was a towering, immovable force.

"Don't stress it," Jessica said gently. She leaned down, wrapped her strong arms securely around Jordan's back and beneath his knees, and effortlessly scooped him out of his heavy power chair.

As Jessica stood up straight, holding him securely against her chest, Jordan let out a small gasp. Cradled in her arms, he suddenly realized just how incredibly high off the ground he was. He was practically looking down at the flight attendants.

"You don't like the strapping chair, yeah?" Jessica asked, looking down at him.

Jordan gave a firm, grateful nod.

"No problem, mate!" Jessica smiled, her voice carrying a thick, unmistakable drawl from the deep Australian Outback. "I'll just carry you to your seat myself."

Jessica turned to the stunned flight attendants standing by the aircraft door. "He doesn't like the aisle chair," she stated, her tone radiating the grounded, no-nonsense authority she had developed growing up in the Northern Territory. "I'm carrying him to his seat. And if he needs to be repositioned during the flight, or if he needs to use the lavatory, I'll be carrying him then, too. I'll carry him off the plane when we land in Toronto."

### Part 2: The Cognitive Redirect – 8:45 AM

The thick slice of homemade bread on Jordan's tray table felt impossibly heavy.
