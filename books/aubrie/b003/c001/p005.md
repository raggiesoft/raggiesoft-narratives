# Part 5: The International Threshold – 8:30 AM

The descent into Washington Dulles International Airport was a jarring, physical reality check.

As the Meridian Express regional jet pitched downward, the change in cabin air pressure pressed heavily against Jordan's eardrums. A few minutes later, the landing gear deployed with a heavy, mechanical *thud* that vibrated straight through the floorboards. When the wheels finally touched the tarmac, the twin engines roared to life with aggressive reverse thrust, shaking the entire cabin.

For Jordan and Aubrie, both of whom processed sensory input at a raw, unfiltered volume due to their shared neurodivergence, the sheer mechanical violence of the landing was completely overwhelming. Jordan squeezed his eyes shut, his hands clamping down on his armrests. Beside him, Aubrie tensed, her breathing growing shallow as the chaotic noise and vibration assaulted her nervous system.

Instinctively, Jordan uncurled his right hand from the armrest and reached across the narrow space, tapping Aubrie's knee. She immediately grabbed his hand, interlacing her fingers with his. They held on with white-knuckled intensity, silently grounding each other through the sensory spike until the jet finally slowed to a smooth taxi.

As the plane navigated toward the sprawling, chaotic expanse of the IAD terminals, Jordan looked out his oval window. The sheer size of the airport was staggering. Massive international jets bearing the flags of foreign nations were parked at the gates.

A sudden, dizzying realization hit him, completely overriding the lingering sensory overload.

He pulled his mPhone from his pocket, his thumbs flying over the glass screen. He angled it across the aisle toward Daniel.

*The next plane goes to Canada,* Jordan typed, the cursor blinking rapidly. *I have never left Virginia before today. I need my Passport. And my Nexus card. Do I need to hold them now?*

Daniel smiled warmly, understanding the sudden spike of travel anxiety. He reached into his coat pocket, where he was safeguarding the stack of eighteen documents, ready to hand Jordan's over to ease his mind.

But Lauren, who was sitting directly behind Daniel in the single seat of row three, leaned forward over the aisle.

"You don't need to hold them just yet, J," Lauren said softly, her warm brown eyes meeting his. "Since we are flying out of the US, nobody is going to check our passports at the gate here. When we land at YYZ in Toronto, that's where we clear Canadian Customs. You and I, and everyone else with a Nexus card, get to bypass the regular lines and use the automated kiosks. It’s incredibly fast and quiet. You'll only need to scan it then."

Jordan took a deep breath, processing her calm, practical explanation. He nodded, letting Daniel keep the documents securely stowed.

"Lauren is absolutely right," Daniel affirmed, his crisp British accent carrying a reassuring authority. "Which brings us to our logistics for the next few hours. Welcome to Washington Dulles, everyone."
