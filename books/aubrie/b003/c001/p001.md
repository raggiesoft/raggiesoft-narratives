# Part 1: The Vanguard Awakes – 5:00 AM

The sharp, digital chime of an alarm clock pierced the cool, quiet air of the Brooks family basement.

Jordan opened his eyes, the absolute absence of his childhood nightmares still feeling like a daily miracle. He was sandwiched comfortably in the center of a mobile queen-sized bed, surrounded by the deep, rhythmic breathing of his pact. To his left, Aubrie was curled up against his side. To his right, Lauren’s auburn hair was splayed across his shoulder, her arm thrown protectively across his chest.

Around them in the dim light of the expansive finished basement, the rest of the vanguard was slowly stirring. The space had been converted into a massive, sleepover-style barracks for the night. Mark and Hannah shared a bed near the far wall, while Zoë and Madison were untangling themselves from their blankets on the adjacent mattress. Near the stairwell, the Australian duo of Sarah and Jessica were already sitting up, whispering quietly to each other.

"Morning, J," Aubrie mumbled, her voice thick with sleep as she rubbed her eyes and sat up.

There was no hesitation and no awkward morning shuffling. The clinical, judgment-free trust that had been forged over the past year in Room 214 immediately took over. Aubrie threw the covers back, grasped the handles of the basic manual push wheelchair kept downstairs specifically for this purpose, and wheeled it flush against the mattress. With Lauren's gentle assistance from the other side, they executed a flawless pivot-transfer, settling Jordan into the seat.

Aubrie wheeled him into the large downstairs bathroom. The radical, practical intimacy of their family was fully on display; there was zero shame and absolutely no demand for privacy. Aubrie assisted Jordan with his morning toileting needs with clinical respect, seamlessly taking care of her own needs in the shared space without missing a beat.

When Aubrie wheeled him back out into the main basement area, the space was a flurry of motion. The group was casually changing out of their pajamas, treating the shared space with the same absolute, unbothered trust. Aubrie and Lauren worked together, helping Jordan navigate the difficult fine-motor task of getting dressed, easing his arms into a soft, comfortable travel hoodie.

"Alright, big guy, ready for your flight?" Lauren beamed, her brown eyes sparkling with the excitement of returning to her childhood home.

Without waiting for an answer, Lauren stepped in front of his manual chair. She squared her stance, wrapping her arms securely around his waist. With a grunt of effort, she engaged her core and deadlifted him entirely out of the chair. Jordan held on tight as she carefully and securely carried him up the carpeted basement stairs.

She emerged onto the main floor of the Brooks estate and gently deposited him into his matte-black, 350-pound power chair, which had been charging by the entryway all night.

The main floor was a chaotic, beautiful symphony of logistics. An absolute army of people was moving through the kitchen. Daniel and Laura Brooks were orchestrating breakfast, sliding plates of eggs and toast across the massive island. The Brooks siblings—Megan, Caleb, Chloe and her husband Ethan, Lucas, Owen, and Jacob—were double-checking passports, Nexus cards, and luggage tags.

"Eat up, everyone!" Daniel announced, his booming London accent cutting through the chatter. "We have a tight schedule! Two vans are running, and we want to beat the morning traffic."

Jordan took a bite of breakfast, watching the chaotic, eighteen-person family operate with flawless precision. Less than an hour later, the entire group funneled out the front doors and into the cool Virginia morning. Two massive, rented passenger vans were idling in the driveway.

Megan lowered the hydraulic lift on the trailing van, allowing Jordan to smoothly roll his massive power chair into the secure rear locking mechanism. As the rest of the pact piled into the seats around him, the doors slammed shut.

The engines revved, and the two vans pulled out of the driveway, merging onto the northbound lanes of Interstate 81. They left the familiar rolling hills of Christiansburg behind, speeding toward Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport. The world tour had officially begun.
