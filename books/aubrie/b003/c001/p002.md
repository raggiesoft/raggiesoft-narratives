# Part 2: Operation Yellow Ribbon – 6:30 AM

Traffic on Interstate 81 was surprisingly sparse for a Saturday morning, allowing the two heavy passenger vans to make excellent time as they cruised north through the New River Valley. Inside the lead van, the atmosphere was a mix of early-morning grogginess and electric anticipation.

Jordan sat securely locked into the rear transport space, his sleek Vanguard Pro resting on his lap. Beside him, Lauren had twisted around in her seat to face him. She knew Jordan was terrified of the unknown, and she needed him to understand exactly what kind of place they were flying into.

"I was only five years old when the airspace closed," Lauren explained softly, her warm brown eyes fixed on him. "Thirty-eight airplanes full of terrified, stranded people were forced to land at our tiny airport."

Jordan watched her, his hands resting quietly on his keyboard. He knew about 9/11, but the concept of what happened on the ground in Canada was entirely foreign to him.

"My parents sat me down and explained the whole story once I was old enough to really understand it," Lauren continued, her voice thick with a fierce, quiet pride. "The population of Gander practically doubled in a few hours. We didn't have the hotels or the infrastructure for that many people, so the town just... opened its doors. I grew up watching my parents give up their beds, cook meals around the clock, and care for people they had never even met. I was raised in a culture of radical hospitality."

She reached out and gave his knee a gentle squeeze. "In my hometown, Jordan, when someone is stranded far from home, you don't look at them as an inconvenience or a burden. You take them in, and you make them safe. That's where we're going."

Jordan let out a slow, shaky breath. For eighteen years, his mother had drilled into him that the world was hostile, transactional, and eager to cast him aside as a burden. Lauren was taking him to a geographic location that fundamentally proved his mother wrong.

By the time the vans pulled into the drop-off lane at Roanoke-Blacksburg Regional Airport, the sun was fully above the horizon. Megan expertly lowered the hydraulic lift, and Jordan rolled his massive, 350-pound power chair onto the pavement.

ROA was a small, easily navigable regional hub, completely devoid of the chaotic, aggressive crowds found in massive international airports. Still, the reality of the impending transit process made Jordan's chest tight. As the group gathered their rolling luggage and moved through the automatic sliding doors into the terminal, Jordan seamlessly adjusted his joystick, sliding his heavy-duty chair directly alongside Lauren.

Without needing to type a single word, he reached out. Lauren immediately uncurled her fingers and took his hand, lacing her fingers through his. She gave his hand a firm, grounding squeeze. For this leg of the journey into her home country, she was his Canadian guide and his absolute anchor.

They approached the check-in counters. A bright blue sign hung overhead, bearing the globe logo of Meridian Airlines.

Daniel Brooks stepped up to the counter, casually dropping a massive, heavy stack of eighteen blue, green, and burgundy passports onto the desk.

"Good morning," Daniel said cheerfully to the ticket agent. "We are checking in for the Meridian Express flight to Washington Dulles."

As the agent began the monumental task of processing the group, Daniel turned back to address his family and the pact.

"Just a quick reminder regarding the itinerary," Daniel noted, his crisp British accent carrying over the hum of the terminal. "As we discussed during the planning phase, this initial hop to Dulles is operated on a rather small regional jet. Meridian Express does not offer a First Class cabin on this specific aircraft, so we are flying standard commercial economy for the next forty-five minutes. Our premium seating arrangements will officially begin once we transfer at IAD."

The group nodded, completely unfazed. They had studied the massive global itinerary and knew exactly what to expect.

Jordan held Lauren's hand a little tighter. Armed with his Nexus card, his custom-built terminal, and an eighteen-person fortress of chosen family, he was finally ready to face the skies.
