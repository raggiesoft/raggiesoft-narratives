As the eighteen members of the global roster moved toward the TSA security checkpoint, Daniel Brooks gently placed a hand on the back of Jordan’s heavy power chair, bringing him to a slow stop just before the queue. Lauren stayed firmly anchored by his side, her fingers still laced with his.

"Alright, Jordan, listen to me," Daniel said quietly, kneeling down to ensure he was perfectly at eye level. "We are about to hand our passports and boarding passes to the federal agent. Because you have a Nexus card and a passport, they are going to use the name printed on those documents. They are going to call you Aubrey Fuller."

Jordan’s breath hitched, his shoulders instantly hiking up toward his ears. Hearing his legal birth name was a massive, visceral trigger, inextricably linked to the eighteen years of suffocating abuse he had endured under Aubrie Sinclair.

"I know it hurts," Daniel murmured, his deep London accent a steady, grounding force. "But it is strictly a federal security protocol. It means absolutely nothing. To me, to Lauren, to Aubrie, and to everyone else in this family, you are Jordan. You just have to let the agent say the name on the paper, and then we are through. Can you do that for me?"

Jordan looked at Daniel, then up at Lauren. She gave his hand an encouraging squeeze. He let out a long, shaky exhale, unclenched his jaw, and gave a sharp nod.

They approached the podium. The TSA officer checked the documents, read the name "Aubrey Fuller," and waved them through. Jordan flinched at the sound, but anchored by Lauren's grip and Daniel's promise, the panic didn't completely consume him.

Once past the document check, the sheer logistical weight of moving eighteen people through the X-ray belts began. While the rest of the Brooks siblings and the core pact managed the group's carry-on luggage, Daniel and Lauren stayed exclusively focused on Jordan.

A senior TSA agent in a blue uniform approached them. His name tag read *Officer Miller*. He took one look at the massive, 350-pound power chair and the non-verbal young man sitting in it, and his entire demeanor shifted into a mode of calm, profound empathy.

"Good morning, gentlemen," Officer Miller said, speaking directly to Jordan with quiet respect. He looked at the heavy machinery. "Sir, are you able to stand or walk at all for just a moment to help us screen the wheelchair?"

Jordan swallowed hard and simply shook his head no.

"That's completely fine," Officer Miller assured him without missing a beat.

Lauren immediately stepped in front of the chair. "I can carry him through the metal detector, and then we can do a private screening for his chair?"

"That would be perfect, ma'am," Miller nodded, holding the line of passengers back to give them space. "Take your time."

Lauren squared her stance, wrapped her arms securely around Jordan’s waist, and deadlifted him cleanly out of his seat. To stabilize himself, Jordan instinctively laced his arms around Lauren's neck as she carried him through the metal detector arches.

As the machine beeped green, a sudden, profound realization washed over Jordan. Resting his head against Lauren's shoulder, he realized he actually *liked* the feeling. The firm, secure hold didn't feel like a clinical transfer; it felt exactly like being wrapped in a warm, protective hug. Surrounded by her strength, the heavy, suffocating anxiety of the airport simply melted away.

Just for a moment, he didn't feel like a scared, disabled boy. He felt like a man who simply needed a small accommodation to get through security, and that accommodation was being effortlessly and lovingly met.

Lauren gently set him down on a sterile waiting bench on the other side. Officer Miller then carefully rolled the empty power chair through the designated gate and brought it right back to Jordan. Once Lauren helped him pivot back into his seat, Miller guided the three of them into a quiet, partitioned screening room away from the chaotic noise of the main checkpoint.

What followed was a revelation. In the medical and administrative settings of his past, Jordan had been handled like a piece of burdensome luggage. But Officer Miller communicated every single micro-step of the process.

"Alright, Jordan, I'm going to swab your armrests now for explosives residue," Miller explained warmly, holding up a small cloth patch. "I'm not going to touch you, just the metal on the chair."

When it came time to inspect the heavy Vanguard Pro laptop, Miller handled it with absolute, reverent care, gently swabbing the casing and setting it back on Jordan's lap. The agent's fluid efficiency and deep, unspoken understanding of Jordan's physical boundaries heavily implied that he had a special needs family member of his own waiting for him at home.

"You're all clear, sir," Officer Miller smiled, opening the partition door. "Have a great flight."

Jordan rolled out of the private screening room and into the air-conditioned concourse. Standing just a few yards away, waiting anxiously by the conveyor belts with her backpack slung over her shoulder, was Aubrie Kelly.

The moment Jordan spotted her, he pushed his joystick forward. He bypassed the rest of the group gathering their shoes and rolled straight toward her. He didn't even bother to pull up his text-to-speech terminal. He just threw his arms around her waist, burying his face into her stomach in a tight, desperate hug of pure relief.

"I've got you," Aubrie beamed, immediately wrapping her arms around his shoulders and resting her cheek against the top of his head. "You did so good, J. The hard part is over."

When he finally pulled back, a massive, genuine smile was plastered across his face. Aubrie didn't let him go far. She reached down and took his left hand, intertwining her fingers with his.

With his right hand resting securely on the joystick, Jordan steered his heavy-duty chair down the concourse, holding Aubrie's hand the entire way. Surrounded by his chosen family, they headed toward the departure gate to board the Meridian Express flight to Washington Dulles.
