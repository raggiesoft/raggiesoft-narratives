To answer the massive logistical challenge of managing an eighteen-person family on an international layover, Daniel Brooks had mapped out the morning with military precision.

"Our flight to Toronto Pearson does not board for another three hours," Daniel explained to the group as they waited for the cabin doors to open. "We are not going to sit at a noisy, crowded gate. Because we are all flying on First Class international tickets, we have complimentary access to the Meridian Premier Lounge in Concourse C."

This was the ultimate travel hack for Jordan's autistic neurology. Instead of enduring the echoing, fluorescent-lit chaos of the main terminal, the group would spend their layover in a highly controlled, sensory-friendly environment.

Once they disembarked and Jordan was safely transferred back into his massive 350-pound power chair, Daniel led the vanguard through the terminal. They bypassed the bustling food courts and swiped their boarding passes at the frosted glass doors of the Premier Lounge.

Inside, the atmosphere completely shifted. The floors were thickly carpeted, absorbing the ambient sound. Soft, warm lighting replaced the harsh overhead fluorescents. There were rows of plush, leather armchairs, private accessible restrooms, and a massive complimentary buffet.

Jordan parked his chair near the floor-to-ceiling windows overlooking the tarmac, plugging his Vanguard Pro into a nearby wall outlet to top off the battery. Aubrie and Madison immediately flanked him, bringing plates of fresh fruit and pastries from the buffet, while Zoë pragmatically reviewed the terminal maps on her mPad.

"I have verified the aircraft for our next leg," Zoë announced, pushing her glasses up the bridge of her nose. "We are upgrading to a larger mainline commercial jet. The First Class cabin features a standard two-by-two seating configuration."

"Which means Zoë and I get the two seats right across the aisle from you two this time!" Madison grinned, bumping her shoulder playfully against Aubrie's.

"Exactly," Daniel confirmed, returning from the espresso bar with a cup of tea. "Jordan, you and Aubrie will maintain your window and aisle pair on the right. Madison and Zoë will have the matching pair on the left. Lauren, you will be right behind them with Sarah, and Hannah will take the pair across the aisle with Mark."

Jordan looked at the massive international jets rolling past the lounge windows, and then back at the women sitting around him. He was about to leave the United States for the very first time in his life, flying toward a foreign country in a wide, comfortable First Class seat, sitting shoulder-to-shoulder with the girl he was falling in love with.

The terrified, isolated boy from Virginia Beach was officially gone. The world traveler had arrived.
