# Part 9: The Price of Freedom – 12:30 PM

As the mainline jet reached cruising altitude, the First Class cabin settled into a comfortable, luxurious hum. The flight attendants began their meal service, rolling the carts down the wide aisle of the two-by-two cabin configuration.

When the cart reached their rows, the lead flight attendant offered a warm smile, but she didn't bother asking for drink orders. Daniel Brooks, managing the complex logistics of their eighteen-person roster, had already handled it. He had documented directly with the airline well in advance that the entire family—even the adults over twenty-one—would be declining alcohol. The flight attendants simply handed out premium hot meals, bottles of sparkling water, and fresh juices, allowing the group to eat in total peace.

Jordan sat in his window seat, his tray table lowered to hold his meal. Aubrie was sitting right beside him, happily digging into her lunch. Directly behind him, he could hear the quiet, easy conversation between Sarah and Jessica, the two Australians seamlessly blending into the protective perimeter of the pact.

Jordan took a bite of his food, but his attention was entirely captured by the view out the window. The vast, sprawling blue expanse of Lake Ontario was coming into view below them.

They were crossing the border. He was leaving the United States.

A sudden, profound realization settled over him. For eighteen years, his mother, Aubrie Sinclair, had been the absolute dictator of his small, silent universe. She had weaponized his medical vulnerabilities, isolating him and convincing him that he was nothing but a helpless burden.

As the jet hurtled toward a foreign country, Jordan searched his chest for any lingering sense of guilt or sadness about leaving her behind. He found absolutely none. The toxic, suffocating trauma bond had been entirely broken by the radical, unselfish love of the women sitting around him.

He thought back to a quiet, pivotal conversation he had experienced just a few days prior in Christiansburg.

Daniel and Laura Brooks had pulled him aside in their massive home. Daniel had sat down across from him, his deep London accent completely devoid of pity, and laid out a new reality. Daniel had calmly explained that he had already paid off the student loan for Jordan's current semester at CPI. But more importantly, Daniel and Laura had promised to pay for the remainder of his college education entirely in cash.

The gravity of that gift was staggering. It wasn't just about the money; it was about the legal leverage. Because his tuition was fully covered, Jordan would never again need to file for FAFSA. He would never again need Aubrie Sinclair’s signature on a financial aid document.

The final, bureaucratic chain she held over his life had been permanently severed.

Jordan looked away from the window and glanced around the cabin. He was flying in a First Class seat, surrounded by a massive, eighteen-person chosen family who protected him without hesitation. He had his custom-built Vanguard Pro safely stowed beneath the seat in front of him, giving him a voice that was entirely his own.

He was completely, undeniably free. And the best part of it all was that back in Virginia, Aubrie Sinclair was none the wiser. She had absolutely no idea that her prisoner was gone forever.

"We're starting our descent, J," Aubrie whispered next to him, her dark eyes shining with excitement as she squeezed his hand.

Jordan smiled, his heart soaring lighter than the aircraft itself. Next stop: YYZ.
