After a final round of snacks and a highly coordinated trip to the Premier Lounge's accessible restrooms, Daniel Brooks gathered the group. It was time.

As they walked out of the lounge and merged into the bustling concourse heading toward their departure gate, the sheer gravity of the moment finally settled squarely on Jordan’s shoulders. This was it. When the wheels of this mainline jet touched down again, he wasn't just going to be in another state. He was going to be in a completely different country. He was going to Canada.

They reached the gate just as First Class boarding was called. Jordan steered his massive 350-pound power chair down the sloped jet bridge, Aubrie walking faithfully by his side.

At the end of the bridge, parked right next to the aircraft door, a flight attendant was waiting with a standard-issue airport aisle chair. It was a narrow, rigid, heavily strapped contraption designed to wheel non-ambulatory passengers down the tight aircraft aisles.

Jordan took one look at the aisle chair and immediately stopped his power chair. His chest tightened. He remembered the cramped, restrictive, and deeply uncomfortable experience of being strapped into a similar chair on the previous flight from ROA to IAD. He absolutely hated it. He looked at the flight attendant and vehemently shook his head no.

Before Aubrie could even reach for her phone to try and negotiate a solution, a shadow fell over Jordan's chair.

Jessica Gallagher stepped forward from the line of boarding passengers. Jordan had known Jessica for a while now, and she had been smoothly integrating herself into their protective pact, but as she stepped directly in front of him, he truly *looked* at her for the first time.

Standing at an imposing 6'3", Jessica possessed a heavy-muscled, natural bodybuilder physique honed by a lifetime of physical labor. She was a towering, immovable force.

"Don't stress it," Jessica said gently. She leaned down, wrapped her strong arms securely around Jordan's back and beneath his knees, and effortlessly scooped him out of his heavy power chair.

As Jessica stood up straight, holding him securely against her chest, Jordan let out a small gasp. Cradled in her arms, he suddenly realized just how incredibly high off the ground he was. He was practically looking down at the flight attendants.

"You don't like the strapping chair, yeah?" Jessica asked, looking down at him.

Jordan gave a firm, grateful nod.

"No problem, mate!" Jessica smiled, her voice carrying a thick, unmistakable drawl from the deep Australian Outback. "I'll just carry you to your seat myself."

Jessica turned to the stunned flight attendants standing by the aircraft door. "He doesn't like the aisle chair," she stated, her tone radiating the grounded, no-nonsense authority she had developed growing up in the Northern Territory. "I'm carrying him to his seat. And if he needs to be repositioned during the flight, or if he needs to use the lavatory, I'll be carrying him then, too. I'll carry him off the plane when we land in Toronto."

The flight attendants, slightly intimidated by her towering height and sheer physical strength, quickly nodded and stepped aside.

As Jessica carried Jordan smoothly through the First Class cabin, Mark caught up to them in the aisle. He quickly assessed the logistics of the situation.

"Hey, Jess," Mark called out, tapping her on the shoulder. "Hannah and I are assigned to the seats directly behind Jordan and Aubrie. But if you're on lifting duty for the flight, it makes way more sense for you to be right behind him."

"We can totally swap," Hannah agreed, smiling warmly from the aisle. "You and Sarah can take our seats behind them, and Mark and I will take your row."

"Brilliant," Sarah Braithwaite beamed, sliding into the row behind Aubrie’s assigned aisle seat. "Works perfectly for us."

Jessica carefully lowered Jordan into his wide First Class window seat, ensuring he was comfortable before taking the seat directly behind him, right next to Sarah. The impromptu seat swap meant that the two Australians were now sitting side-by-side—Sarah, with her bold energy from Liverpool, New South Wales, and Jessica, with her unshakeable pragmatism from a remote roadhouse in Petermann, Northern Territory.

Aubrie slid into the seat next to Jordan, immediately reaching out to take his hand. She looked over the back of her seat at Jessica.

"Thank you, Jess," Aubrie whispered sincerely.

"Anytime," Jessica smiled, settling into her seat. "We take care of our own."

Jordan looked out the window as the massive jet engines began to whine. He didn't feel like a burden. He felt completely supported by a dynamic, fiercely protective family that adapted to his needs in real-time. He squeezed Aubrie's hand, feeling the rumble of the aircraft, absolutely ready to cross the border.
