When the mainline jet finally arrived at the gate at Toronto Pearson International Airport, the First Class cabin began to empty out. True to her word, Jessica Gallagher stood up from her seat in the third row, her imposing 6'3" frame easily navigating the cabin aisle.

"Alright, mate, let's get you off this plane," Jessica smiled, her thick Outback accent a warm, familiar comfort. She leaned down, wrapped her strong, heavily muscled arms around Jordan, and effortlessly deadlifted him out of his window seat.

She carried him out the aircraft door and onto the jetbridge to wait for the baggage handlers to bring up his massive 350-pound power chair. As they stood there in the cool air of the enclosed bridge, Jordan rested his head against Jessica's shoulder. Just like at the TSA checkpoint in Roanoke, a profound realization washed over him. He wasn't just being carried; he was being held. The secure, unyielding strength of Jessica's grip didn't feel clinical or mechanical. It felt exactly like a massive, protective hug.

He instinctively laced his arms around her neck, leaning into the contact and letting out a soft, contented exhale.

Jessica, whose survival instincts were honed by a lifetime in the deep Petermann bush, was incredibly observant. She felt his posture shift, recognizing the exact moment the transfer turned into an embrace. A bright, genuine smile broke across her face. Completely unbothered by the weight, she gladly leaned into it, wrapping her arms just a fraction tighter around him and rocking him gently side-to-side until the heavy-duty power chair was finally rolled up the ramp.

She carefully lowered him into the seat, making sure he was perfectly comfortable before grabbing the handles of her backpack.

"Welcome to Canada, everyone," Lauren beamed, her brown eyes shining as the group of eighteen funneled out of the jetbridge and into the sprawling, glass-lined international arrivals hall of YYZ.

As they approached the massive Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) checkpoint, the logistical reality of their passports came into play.

"Okay, this is where we split for a bit," Daniel Brooks announced. He pointed toward a bank of automated machines. "Everyone with a Nexus card, follow Lauren and me. Sarah, Jessica, you two head for the international ePassport gates over there."

"Got it. We'll see you on the other side," Sarah Braithwaite said, flashing a quick smile before she and Jessica peeled off toward the foreign national queues.

As Jordan rolled his chair toward the Nexus lane alongside Aubrie, he pulled out his mPhone. His logical, systems-based brain was already processing the geopolitical data. He typed a quick question and angled the screen toward Lauren, who was walking just ahead of him holding her dark blue Canadian passport.

*Canada and Australia are both Commonwealth nations with the same monarch,* Jordan typed. *Do Australians not get an expedited customs track here? Why do they have to wait in the long line?*

Lauren smiled, looking down at his screen. "That is an incredibly smart question, J. They are both in the Commonwealth, and that actually does give them a perk—they get to use the electronic ePassport gates instead of talking to an agent manually, which saves them a ton of time. But Nexus is a very specific bilateral security treaty strictly between the United States and Canada. Commonwealth status doesn't grant you Nexus clearance."

Jordan nodded, filing the logistical data away as they approached the front of the Nexus line.

They stepped up to the CBSA podium after scanning their cards at the kiosks. Daniel expertly placed the stack of blue United States passports on the desk for the Brooks family, ensuring they legally matched their Nexus profiles instead of causing a system error with their British passports. Lauren stepped up to the front, placing her Canadian passport and Nexus card on top of the pile.

The uniformed border agent looked at Lauren's passport and offered the standard, federally mandated bilingual greeting required at all Canadian ports of entry.

*"Bonjour. Hello."*

Lauren didn't miss a single beat. Having spent significant time in Québec, she possessed a fierce fluency that went far beyond high school language classes. She immediately answered the agent in rapid, flawless, colloquial Québécois French.

*"Bonjour! Nous rentrons au pays pour une visite, et voici mes amis des États-Unis,"* Lauren replied smoothly, her voice bright and polite as she gestured to the massive American contingent standing behind her.

The CBSA agent paused, his eyebrows shooting up in genuine surprise. Hearing an authentic Québécois accent in the middle of an Anglophone Toronto terminal was rare enough, but hearing it from a girl whose passport listed her birthplace as Gander, Newfoundland—a province famous for its radically distinct English dialect—was a massive, delightful curveball.

The agent's formal, bureaucratic posture completely melted into a warm, disarmed smile. He quickly stamped the necessary paperwork. *"Bienvenue chez vous. Welcome home."*

As they rolled past the podium and officially cleared customs, Lauren looked down at Jordan's stunned expression and laughed.

"I told him we are coming home for a visit, and that you guys are my friends from the States," Lauren translated for the group, her face glowing with excitement. "Come on. Let's head up to the Maple Leaf Lounge in the domestic departure terminal. We've got a lounge to raid while we wait for the Aussies to clear the international gates!"

Surrounded by his chosen family and officially on foreign soil for the very first time in his life, Jordan grabbed his joystick and eagerly followed her lead.
