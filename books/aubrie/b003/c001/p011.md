The Air Canada Maple Leaf Lounge in the domestic terminal was a quiet, sprawling sanctuary of polished wood and comfortable armchairs. The sixteen members of the Nexus group had easily secured a large corner section, indulging in the complimentary buffet and waiting for the final two members of their party.

Jordan sat in his heavy-duty power chair by the window, his Vanguard Pro resting securely on his tray table. He tapped his fingers rhythmically against the edge of the plastic, a subtle self-soothing stim. He was watching the clock on his dark-themed NeoEdit terminal. It had been nearly forty-five minutes since they had separated at the customs hall.

His highly structured, logical brain did not like unexpected delays. The longer they waited, the more a low, buzzing annoyance began to build in the back of his mind.

Finally, the frosted glass doors of the lounge slid open. Sarah Braithwaite and Jessica Gallagher walked in, both looking distinctly exasperated.

"There they are!" Madison called out, waving them over to the corner.

Jessica dropped her heavy backpack onto an empty chair with a weary sigh, her towering 6'3" frame sinking into the seat next to it. Sarah, radiating her usual bold, unapologetic energy, grabbed a bottle of water from the cooler and collapsed into the chair next to Lauren.

"Sorry we took so long, mates," Sarah groaned, twisting the cap off her water. "The automated ePassport gates were an absolute disaster."

Jordan frowned, his fingers moving quickly over his keyboard. *Did the system reject your Australian passports?* the bright green text read on his angled screen.

"No, our passports were fine," Jessica grumbled, her thick Outback drawl thick with annoyance. "It was the bloke standing directly in front of us in the queue."

"It was infuriating," Sarah elaborated, leaning forward. "This guy had absolutely no business being in the ePassport lane. He didn't have a chipped passport, he couldn't get the machine to scan his documents, and instead of just stepping aside, he tried to force it. He ended up completely locking down the kiosk and triggering a system error for our entire row."

"We were stuck standing there for twenty minutes just waiting for a CBSA agent to come override the machine," Jessica added, crossing her muscular arms. "They ended up taking the guy out of the line and marching him through a set of security doors, probably for additional questioning. But by the time they reset our gate, the queue was a mile long."

Jordan stared at the screen of his terminal. His initial annoyance at the delay flared up again, completely directed at the sheer inefficiency and entitlement of the stranger who had ignored the rules and disrupted the system. But as he looked at Sarah and Jessica—who had sprinted through the terminal just to get back to the family—the irritation vanished.

*I am annoyed that people refuse to read instructional signage,* Jordan typed, offering the two Australian women a warm, reassuring smile. *But I am glad you are both okay, and I am glad you are back.*

"Thanks, J," Sarah smiled, her frustration melting away instantly. "We wouldn't miss this flight for the world."

Daniel Brooks checked his watch and stood up, clapping his hands together once.

"Well, the timing is actually perfect," Daniel announced, looking around the lounge. "Our departure gate for Gander is just down the concourse, and First Class boarding begins in ten minutes. Let's gather our things."

The group sprang into action, grabbing their carry-on bags and finishing their drinks. Aubrie took her familiar place at Jordan's right side, while Lauren stepped up to his left.

As Jordan steered his power chair out of the lounge and into the bustling domestic terminal, Lauren gently rested her hand on his shoulder.

"This is it, J," Lauren said softly, her brown eyes shining with an intense, fierce pride. "Our next stop is YQX. My hometown."

Jordan looked up at her, feeling a profound wave of anticipation. He had spent his entire life trapped in a world where strangers were a threat, and the unknown was something to be feared. But he remembered the stories she had told him in the van that morning—about the thirty-eight grounded airplanes, the stranded passengers, and the town that had opened its doors without a second thought.

He wasn't flying into a hostile void anymore. He was flying directly into the heart of Operation Yellow Ribbon.

Lauren smiled, interlacing her fingers with his as they approached the gate. It was time to finally show Jordan what true hospitality in the face of the unknown was all about.
