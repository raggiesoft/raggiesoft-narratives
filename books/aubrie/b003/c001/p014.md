As the excitement of the arrival settled into a warm, comfortable evening, the practical realities of the night took over. With the house naturally lacking the wide, accessible architecture of their specialized apartment back at CPI, the pact simply adapted their routines without missing a single beat.

Lauren easily scooped Jordan into her arms, carrying him down the hallway to the family bathroom. The level of trust between them was absolute. With the same pragmatic, unbothered grace she applied to deadlifting his heavy wheelchair, Lauren helped Jordan with his toileting needs, completely normalizing the physical assistance. Once he was taken care of, she used the restroom herself, then gently helped Jordan change out of his travel clothes and into soft, comfortable pajamas before quickly changing into her own sleepwear.

Over the next half hour, Aubrie, Madison, and Zoë took their turns rotating through the bathroom, washing their faces and changing into their pajamas.

When Lauren finally carried Jordan back out to the living room, Liam and Connor had completely transformed the space. They had pushed the coffee table aside and laid down a massive, high-quality portable mattress right in front of the hearth. Lauren's mother had piled it high with thick, weighted Newfoundland quilts, flannel sheets, and a mountain of soft pillows. It wasn't just a bed; it was a sprawling, ultra-comfortable nest designed perfectly for the five of them to stay together.

Lauren gently lowered Jordan onto the center of the mattress.

As the girls gathered around, Jordan firmly patted the empty spaces directly to his left and right. He didn't need his Vanguard Pro to communicate what he wanted. He looked at Aubrie, then looked at Madison. They were his original lifelines—the very first people who had shown him what unconditional love looked like back in Room 214.

Understanding perfectly, Aubrie smiled and crawled under the heavy quilts on his right side. Madison immediately took the spot on his left, sandwiching him securely between them. Lauren and Zoë happily took the outer edges of the massive bed, completing the impenetrable perimeter.

Lauren’s parents turned off the overhead lights, leaving only the warm, flickering glow of the living room fireplace to illuminate the room.

"Goodnight, my darlings," Lauren's mother whispered softly from the doorway. "Sleep as long as you need."

As the house grew quiet, Jordan lay flat on his back, staring up at the shadows dancing on the ceiling. The heavy, weighted quilt pressed down on him, a deeply soothing physical pressure that quieted his autistic nervous system.

He was lying in a house in Gander, Newfoundland.

The geographical reality washed over him in a slow, powerful wave. He had actually done it. He had crossed an international border. He was going to fall asleep in Canada. For eighteen years, his world had been defined entirely by the suffocating, abusive walls built by his mother, Aubrie Sinclair. She had controlled every breath, every movement, and every shred of his dignity.

But right now, thousands of miles away in Virginia, Aubrie Sinclair was completely oblivious. She had absolutely no idea where he was, who he was with, or that the terrified, helpless boy she had kept imprisoned was gone forever. She had lost her power, and she didn't even know it yet.

A profound, overwhelming sense of peace settled deep into Jordan's chest. He wasn't just surviving anymore; he was victorious.

He uncurled his arms from beneath the heavy blankets and reached out. He wrapped his right arm tightly around Aubrie Kelly, and his left arm firmly around Madison, pulling both of his original roommates into a close, desperate embrace.

Sandwiched between the two women who had started it all, surrounded by his fiercely protective chosen family on foreign soil, Jordan closed his eyes and let out a long, shuddering sigh of absolute safety. For the first time in his entire life, he was exactly where he belonged.
