# Part 2: The Night Watch

## Date: Friday, July 15, 1994 Time: 9:30 PM Location: The Cockpit (Uncle Mark’s Spare Room)

The bedtime routine at Uncle Mark’s was an operation. The bathroom in the 1890s-era apartment was not designed for a wheelchair. It was a narrow, tiled chute with a clawfoot tub and a pedestal sink that seemed determined to block the door. Ellen had to leave the wheelchair in the hallway. She carried Nichole in—a "dead lift" that made Ellen grunt with effort—and balanced her on the edge of the toilet, then the edge of the tub.

It was hot. The bathroom window was painted shut, trapping the humidity. By the time Nichole was scrubbed, dried, and wrestled into her cotton pajamas, both she and her mother were sweating. "Okay," Ellen exhaled, brushing a damp strand of hair from her face. "Let's get you to the cool room."

She carried Nichole down the hall to the spare room. The transition was instant. The hallway was stifling, but the "Cockpit" had airflow. Patrick was already there, wearing his oversized t-shirt and boxers. He was sitting cross-legged on the low futon, staring at the SignalMaster radio, watching the signal needle bounce.

Ellen lowered Nichole onto the futon. "Slide," Patrick said, moving his legs to give them space. It worked exactly as Mark had promised. The firm mattress didn't sink. Nichole slid easily from her mother's arms to the center of the bed.

"You two okay?" Ellen asked, hovering by the door. "Do you need the door open for light?" Patrick looked at the glowing green face of the Quantix oscilloscope and the amber backlight of the radio. "We have lights," Patrick said. Ellen smiled, kissed them both on the forehead, and closed the door.

The Atmosphere The room was dark, but it wasn't silent. Outside, the summer storm had broken. Rain was hammering against the shingles of the house—a heavy, rhythmic drumming. In the window, the box fan was roaring on "High." It pulled the wet, salty air into the room. It was a wall of sound. *Whirrrrrr-Hisssssss.*

Usually, Nichole hated the dark. At home in Virginia, her room was quiet. In the quiet, the dark felt empty. If she dropped her pillow, it was gone forever. If a shadow moved, she couldn't run. The dark was where the spasms waited. It was where she felt the most paralyzed. She usually slept with a nightlight and the door cracked, straining to hear her parents’ TV down the hall just to know she wasn't alone.

But here, the noise wrapped around her like a blanket. And then there was the heat. It was sticky, making her pajamas cling to her skin, but the fan breeze felt good against her face.

She felt the mattress dip. Patrick lay down next to her. He didn't stay on "his side." He shimmied close until his shoulder was pressing against hers. He pulled the navy blue quilt up over them. "Loud rain," Patrick whispered.

Nichole turned her head. She could see his profile in the faint amber glow of the radio. He was right there. She didn't need to yell for Mom. She didn't need to bang on the wall. She closed her eyes. For the first time, she didn't try to fight the sleep.

## The Spasm

Sometime deep in the night—maybe 2:00 AM—the enemy attacked. It started in her left calf. A sudden, electric tightening. The muscle seized, pulling her toes down and locking her ankle in a rigid, painful point. *The Cramp.*

Nichole gasped, her eyes snapping open. Pain shot up her leg. She tried to cry out, but the shock seized her vocal cords. She let out a sharp, high-pitched whimper. *Nnnngh.* In Virginia, this would be the start of a five-minute ordeal. She would have to scream until Mom woke up, ran down the hall, and rubbed it out.

But she wasn't in Virginia. Before the second wave of pain could hit, a hand was on her leg. Patrick.

He hadn't woken up groggy. He had woken up *active*. He threw the quilt back. He found her calf in the dark. He knew the knot. He knew exactly where the muscle bunched up under the knee. He dug his thumb in. *Push. Rub. Circle.*

"I got it," Patrick whispered. His voice wasn't scared. It was calm. "Breathe, Nikki. I got it."

He used his other hand to gently flex her foot upward, fighting the spasm, stretching the tendon. "One... two... three..." he counted softly, matching the rhythm of the rain outside.

Nichole bit her lip, tears leaking from the corners of her eyes. The pain was sharp, but Patrick’s hands were warm. He wasn't just rubbing the skin; he was anchoring her. *I am here.*

Slowly, the iron knot in her leg began to loosen. The muscle fluttered, then relaxed. Her foot dropped back to a natural position. The pain faded into a dull, throbbing ache.

Patrick didn't stop. He kept rubbing, lighter now. Smoothing the nerves down. "Gone?" he whispered.

Nichole exhaled, her breath shaking. *Gone.*

She reached out in the dark. She found his arm. She pulled him. It was a clumsy movement, her motor control still fried from the pain, but Patrick understood. He scooted closer. He let her wrap her arms around his chest. He wrapped his arms around her small, trembling shoulders. *The Hug.* It meant: *Thank you. You saved me.*

Patrick squeezed her back. He rested his chin on the top of her head. "You're welcome," he whispered into her hair. He paused. The name felt natural, like it had been waiting for this moment to exist. "Any time, Nikki."

Nichole froze for a second. *Nikki.* Mom called her Nichole. Dad called her Nichole. The teachers called her Nichole. *Nikki* was short. It was fast. It sounded like a secret code. She squeezed him tighter. *Yes.*

They fell asleep like that—tangled together on the hard futon, listening to the rain and the fan, safe in the cockpit of the Bennett Machine.
