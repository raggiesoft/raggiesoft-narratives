# Part 2: The Admission

## **Date:** Tuesday, December 27, 2005 **Time:** 05:30 AM **Location:** University Medical Center, Surgical Admissions Desk

Outside the sliding glass doors, a light snow was falling. It was a gentle, post-Christmas snow—dusting the dormant grass of the hospital lawn but melting instantly on the salted asphalt of the drop-off lane.

Inside, the hospital was warm, quiet, and vibrating with the low hum of air handlers and the squeak of rubber shoes on linoleum.

Patrick pushed Nichole toward the main Admissions desk. She was wearing her "surgery pajamas"—soft, loose cotton that wouldn't irritate her skin—and clutching her new hoodie like a security blanket. Her face was pale, her eyes darting around the massive atrium which was still decorated with silver and blue holiday wreaths.

Patrick wasn't looking at the decorations. He was focused on the mission. He wore his standard gray hoodie, but clipped to the front pocket was his university ID and, right next to it, his laminated **LPN Badge**.

They rolled up to the counter. A registrar named Karen looked up from her computer, sipping a coffee from a "Happy Holidays" paper cup.

"Name?" she asked, fingers hovering over the keyboard.

"Bennett, Nichole," Patrick said clearly. "Scheduled for Bilateral Hamstring Lengthening with Dr. Evans at 0730."

Karen typed. "Okay, I see her. And you are?"

"Patrick Bennett. Brother." He paused, then shifted his tone. "And Private Duty Nurse."

He placed the heavy three-ring binder on the counter. He opened it to the first tab, displaying his LPN license and the signed orders from Dr. Thorne.

"I'm listed as the primary care provider for discharge," Patrick said. "We have a standing order for home-based skilled nursing."

Karen blinked. She looked at the license. She looked at the screen.

"Oh," she said, her eyebrows shooting up. "I see the note here from Dr. Thorne. 'Discharge to Skilled Nursing: P. Bennett, LPN.'"

She looked at Patrick with a sudden shift in demeanor. Usually, family members were confused, anxious, and needed everything explained three times. This twenty-year-old had brought his own charting system.

"We don't see this often," Karen admitted, typing in his license number. "Usually, for a bilateral procedure like this, Dr. Evans requires a three-day stay in a rehab facility for pain management. But... well, you're the facility."

"I am," Patrick confirmed. "I have the MARs ready for the post-op analgesics. I have the sterile field for the dressing changes. We're bypassing the rehab floor."

A Charge Nurse, wearing navy blue scrubs, walked behind the desk. She heard the exchange and looked at the paperwork.

"You're the LPN brother?" the nurse asked, looking at Patrick. "The one Dr. Evans mentioned?"

"Yes, ma'am," Patrick said.

The nurse smiled, a genuine, tired smile.

"Thank God," she said. "We get so many families who think they can handle a spica cast or an immobilizer, and they end up calling 911 because they can't get the patient to the bathroom. Knowing she has a licensed nurse who knows her baseline... that makes our job a thousand times easier."

She reached over the counter and handed Patrick a plastic wristband—not a "Visitor" band, but a **"Care Team"** band.

"You have full access," the nurse said. "Pre-op, PACU, and Recovery. You don't have to wait in the lobby. You're on the clock, Nurse Bennett."

Patrick took the band. He snapped it onto his wrist. It felt heavier than a watch. It felt like armor.

He looked down at Nichole. She was watching the exchange, her anxiety dial turning down from a 9 to a 6. She saw the respect they gave him.

Patrick tapped the binder.

"We're in, Nikki," he whispered. "Let's go get you prepped."

Nichole nodded. She typed on her device with a shaky hand.

"MY. NURSE. IS. BETTER. THAN. THEIR. NURSES."

Patrick grinned, grabbing the handles of her chair.

"Damn straight. Let's roll."
