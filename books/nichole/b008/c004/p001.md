# Part 1: The Handover (The Night Before) Time: 8:00 PM

The living room of Unit 404 was a mix of post-Christmas chaos and pre-surgical precision.

On the coffee table lay the spoils of the day. They had spent the morning in Portsmouth with the family, opening gifts and eating leftovers, before packing up the van for the short drive back to Braxton. There were stacks of new DVDs, a few video games purchased with electronics store gift cards, and a new hooded sweatshirt for Nichole.

But the Kitchen Island was a different world. It looked like a sterile field.

Patrick stood at the counter, a pen light in his mouth, reviewing a document he had drafted: **OP-PLAN: LEG DAY (DEC 2005).**

He wasn't just a brother packing an overnight bag. He was **Patrick Bennett, LPN**, packing a "Mobile Medical Unit."

- **Hydration:** Two sterilized 1-liter hydration bladders with bite valves. (Standard hospital cups were useless when a patient was flat on their back).

- **Infection Control:** A stack of Chlorhexidine Gluconate (CHG) wipes. He wasn't trusting the standard hospital soap; he was doing a secondary scrub of the surgical sites himself.

- **The Chart:** His three-ring binder, now thick with a year’s worth of data. It was tabbed with sections for *Current Meds*, *Vitals*, and *Neuro Checks*.

- **The "Vocal" Proxy:** A laminated sheet tucked into the front pocket titled **NON-VERBAL PAIN SCALE**. It listed Nichole’s specific distress cues: *Jaw clenching, rapid blinking, withdrawal from touch.*

"Check," Patrick muttered, ticking off the 'Emesis Basin' line item. "Anesthesia makes you pukey. We are prepared for puke."

The landline rang.

Patrick hit the speaker button. "Unit 404, Nurse Bennett speaking."

"Report, Lieutenant," Tom’s voice boomed from Portsmouth.

"We're secure," Patrick said, his voice crisp. "Traffic on the bridge was light. We got back to the Lofts about twenty minutes ago. Pre-op protocol is ninety percent complete. She's showered with the antiseptic soap. Skin is intact. We are initiating NPO—nothing by mouth—at 2400 hours."

"How is the patient?" Ellen asked. "Is she terrified?"

Nichole rolled up to the island. She was wearing her new hoodie, looking clean, scrubbed, and jittery. She typed furiously on her device.

"HUNGRY. I. WANT. A. BURGER. AND. FRIES. BEFORE. THE. FAST."

"She's displaying 'food-seeking behavior' as a stress response," Patrick translated dryly, checking his watch. "She wants a double cheeseburger."

"Get her the burger," Ellen said gently. "She needs the calories for healing. But Patrick? Listen to me. The first twenty-four hours post-op are the 'Spasm Cycle.' The muscles panic because they've been cut. Do not chase the pain."

"I know, Mom," Patrick interrupted gently. "I've reviewed the pharmacokinetics of the morphine Dr. Evans is using. Peak effect is twenty minutes. Half-life is two to four hours. If I wait until she cries, the serum level drops too low. I have to stack the doses to maintain a therapeutic baseline."

There was a silence on the other end. Ellen wasn't used to hearing her son use words like *pharmacokinetics*.

"Right," Ellen said, her voice softening with pride. "Exactly. You stack the doses."

"And Pat?" Tom added. "We can be there in forty minutes. If you need muscle to get her into the car tomorrow..."

"Dad, stop," Patrick said. "I appreciate the backup. But I have the transfer board. I have the gait belt. And I know the body mechanics now. I'm not lifting with my back; I'm pivoting with my hips."

He looked at Nichole. She was watching him. She wasn't looking at her big brother who needed help; she was looking at her nurse who had a plan.

"We have the elevator," Patrick continued. "We have the van. And I have the license. We are the Machine, remember?"

"Okay," Tom exhaled. "Okay. Call us when she's out of PACU."

"Will do. 404 out."

Patrick hung up.

He turned to Nichole. He closed the binder. The clinical mask slipped just a fraction.

"Okay, Nikki," Patrick smiled. "Medical advice says we have three hours until the midnight cutoff. Let's go get that burger."

Nichole grinned. She tapped her screen.

"LAST. MEAL. LET'S. RIDE."
