# Part 3: The Integration

Date: September 22, 2005

Time: 2:30 PM

Location: Narragansett Integrated Health Systems, Dr. Thorne’s Office

The appointment began like every other check-up for the last two years, but the energy in the room had shifted.

Patrick rolled Nichole into Exam Room 2. He didn't sit in the guest chair. He stood by the computer terminal, arms crossed, wearing his BSU nursing scrubs (he had come straight from a lab).

Dr. Alistair Thorne walked in. He was an old-school internist—silver hair, wire-rimmed glasses, and a belief in listening to the patient before looking at the chart.

"Good afternoon, Nichole. Patrick," Dr. Thorne smiled. He looked at Patrick’s scrubs. "I see the uniform isn't just for show anymore."

"It's official," Patrick said. He reached into his backpack. He didn't pull out a crumpled piece of paper. He pulled out the heavy three-ring binder he had assembled—The Chart.

He opened it to the very front page.

"License," Patrick said, pointing to the copy of his Rhode Island nursing credential. "And Insurance. Professional Liability, fully paid up."

Dr. Thorne leaned in. He adjusted his glasses. He read the license number. He read the coverage limits on the Certificate of Insurance.

He looked up at Patrick. He didn't look surprised; he looked relieved.

"You actually did it," Thorne said softly. "You built the firewall."

"I told you I would," Patrick said. "Now, I need you to make it official in the system. I don't want to be listed as 'Brother' or 'Emergency Contact' anymore. I want to be designated as her Private Duty Nurse."

Dr. Thorne nodded. He sat down at the room's computer terminal and logged in.

"This changes the liability structure completely," Thorne said, typing rapidly. "If you were just family, I could only give you advice. As a licensed provider, I can give you Orders."

He spun the monitor so they could see.

PATIENT: BENNETT, NICHOLE

CARE TEAM:

1\. Primary Care: Alistair Thorne, MD

2\. Neurology: Marcus Vance, MD

3\. Home Health / Skilled Nursing: Patrick Bennett, LPN (License Verified)

"I'm entering a standing order," Thorne narrated as he typed. " 'Patrick Bennett, LPN, is authorized to administer all prescribed medications, perform wound care, and manage catheterization protocols per standard nursing practice.'"

He hit ENTER.

"There," Thorne said. "You're not a visitor anymore, Patrick. You're staff. Which brings us to October."

He turned back to face them.

"The hamstring lengthening. Dr. Evans is a fantastic surgeon, but he runs a tight ship. He's used to discharging patients to rehab facilities, not home."

"We aren't going to a rehab facility," Nichole tapped on her screen. "HOME."

"I know," Thorne agreed. "And now, I can authorize that. Because I'm not discharging you to 'home.' I'm discharging you to 'Skilled Nursing Care.'"

He printed out a new document—the Post-Op Home Care Plan.

"Patrick, since you are the provider, I'm tasking you with the pre-op prep," Thorne said, handing the paper to Patrick, not Nichole. It was a professional handoff. "I want you to manage the bowel prep starting three days out. And I want you to run a full vitals log for 48 hours prior to admission so anesthesiology has a baseline."

"Understood," Patrick said, taking the paper. He scanned it. "I'll start the bowel protocol on the 11th. We'll have the data ready."

Dr. Thorne stood up and shook Patrick’s hand. It wasn't the patronizing handshake of a doctor to a kid. It was the firm grip of a captain handing the wheel to his first officer.

"You've leveled the playing field, son," Thorne said. "Now go get her ready for surgery."

Patrick looked at Nichole. He tapped the binder.

"We're official, Nikki."

Nichole smiled. She clicked her mouse.

"TEAM. ASSEMBLED."
