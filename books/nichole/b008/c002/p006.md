# Part 6: The Green Light

## Date: October 12, 2004 Time: 5:48 AM Location: The Master Suite, Unit 404

The phone on the nightstand rang. It wasn't the soft chirping of a social call; it was the harsh, double-ring of a professional line.

Patrick snatched it up before the second ring could finish.

"Bennett," he answered, his voice tight and alert.

"This is Dr. Kovacs, on-call for Women’s Health," a calm female voice said on the other end. "I have a message here from the service regarding Nichole Bennett. You reported a suspected candidiasis with... satellite lesions?"

"Affirmative," Patrick said, switching instantly into his 'briefing' mode. "Left inguinal fold. Main erythema is about three inches wide, bright red, shiny. Distinct pustules scattered about an inch from the margin. She's guarding the hip flexor. Pain response is high. It’s burning."

There was a short pause on the line. Usually, family members called saying "it hurts" or "it looks red." They didn't describe lesion margins.

"You're the brother? The primary caregiver?" Dr. Kovacs asked.

"I am," Patrick said. "I handle all hygiene and skin checks. This wasn't there last night at 2200. It bloomed overnight. I suspect moisture trap from yesterday's jeans combined with the lecture hall heat."

"If it bloomed that fast and she's guarding, we need to rule out a secondary bacterial infection," Dr. Kovacs said decisively. "I don't want to call in a script without eyes on it. If that skin breaks, it's a portal for Staph."

"Understood," Patrick said. "We want to come in."

"Dr. Lin opens the clinic at 8:00 AM. I’ll flag her schedule. Can you get her to Newport by then?"

Patrick looked at the digital clock. 5:50 AM.

He ran the transit math in his head.

- 06:00 AM: Leave apartment.

- 06:15 AM: Arrive Narragansett Esplanade Ferry Terminal via The Braxton Link.

- 06:30 AM: Board the first BTA Newport Flyer of the day.

- 07:00 AM: Arrive Perrotti Park, Newport.

- 07:15 AM: OSTA Bus to the hospital.

"We'll be there," Patrick promised. "We're catching the 0630 ferry. We'll be at your door at 0755."

"Good. Keep the area dry. Do not apply any creams yet—I need to see the raw presentation. See you at eight."

Click.

Patrick hung up the phone. He turned to Nichole.

She was watching him from the bed, her eyes wide and wet with pain tears. She was holding her breath, waiting for the verdict.

"Green light," Patrick announced. "They want us in at 0800. No English class today."

He walked over to the dresser and grabbed a stack of clothes. Loose sweatpants. A soft cotton t-shirt. Nothing with zippers, buttons, or rough seams.

"We have ten minutes to deploy," Patrick said, tossing the clothes onto the bed. "I'm going to get you dressed, but I'm leaving the left side loose so nothing touches that skin. We are running 'Low Friction' protocol until we get that script."

Nichole let out a shaky breath and nodded. She reached for her mouse.

"THANK. YOU."

"Don't thank me yet," Patrick said, gently lifting her leg to slide the sweatpants on, being agonizingly careful not to graze the angry red skin. "Thank me when the burning stops. Now let's move. The boat doesn't wait."

He worked fast. Within eight minutes, she was dressed, transferred to her Titan AeroMotion, and strapped in.

He grabbed the go-bag. He grabbed his keys.

"Mobilizing," Patrick said, unlocking the heavy steel door of the loft.

They rolled out into the hallway, moving with the silent, grim determination of a rescue squad. The Fortress was active. The Machine was moving. And they were going to get that prescription if Patrick had to row the boat himself.
