# Part 12: The Double Ambush

## Date: November 14, 2004 Time: 1:30 PM Location: The Student Union, Second Floor Lounge (The "Quiet Zone")

The Second Floor Lounge was supposed to be a sanctuary. It was tucked behind the administrative offices, usually empty at this hour.

Patrick sat in the corner of the oversized loveseat. His legs were spread slightly to create a cradle. Nichole was curled up between them, her head resting heavily on his chest, her legs draped over his left thigh. His arm was wrapped securely around her back, his chin resting on the top of her head.

It was the "Post-Lunch Reboot." Nichole’s sensory battery was at 5%. She needed the silence and the deep pressure of his hold to avoid a meltdown before her 2:00 PM English seminar.

Then, the double doors at the far end of the room burst open.

"GO, GO, GO! BEFORE THE S.A.C. NARCS SHOW UP!"

A guy in a backwards baseball cap—the ringleader—pushed a heavy cart into the room. He slammed the doors shut and wedged a chair under the handle.

"Alright people!" Megaphone Guy shouted. "The Student Activities Council kicked us out of the lobby because we didn't file the 'paperwork.' But true love doesn't wait for permits! Welcome to GUERILLA SPEED DATING!"

He hit a button on a massive boombox strapped to the cart.

The Music: For ten seconds, a majestic, Vangelis-style synthesizer swell filled the room. It sounded like slow-motion victory. Patrick almost relaxed. Then—CRASH. A screeching guitar slide tore through the air, followed by a pounding synth-drum beat at 150 BPM. It was *"Thunder in the Rain"* by Steel Horizon.

Thirty students flooded the room, laughing and breathless. They began pairing off randomly, shouting over the power ballad.

Nichole winced. She squeezed her eyes shut, burying her face into Patrick’s hoodie. She tapped his chest three times, fast. *Threat. Threat. Threat.*

Patrick’s jaw tightened. He covered her ear with his hand to dampen the sound.

He looked up. Two people were beelining for the loveseat. They were moving in a pincer formation.

The Predators: From the left came Jessica. She didn't look random. She looked like she had been waiting for this exact moment of chaos to make her move. From the right came Kyle. A stranger in a varsity jacket who had scanned the room, saw a girl not talking to anyone, and decided she was the easiest target.

The Approach: Jessica reached them first. She moved with the confidence of someone who had already decided how this interaction would end. She stepped between the coffee table and Patrick’s knees, closing the gap to zero.

Her intention was clear. In any other scenario, she would have simply dropped onto his lap, wrapped an arm around his neck, and planted a kiss on him before he could object. It was a shock-and-awe move.

But she couldn't land.

Patrick’s lap was fully occupied. Nichole was the physical firewall.

Jessica stopped, her hip bumping against the armrest. She looked at Nichole not with pity, but with the irritation of a driver finding a parked car in their reserved spot.

"You look trapped," Jessica shouted over the guitar solo.

Patrick looked up, his expression blank. "I'm not."

"You're a Saint, you know that?" she purred. She leaned closer, her face inches from his, trying to bypass Nichole entirely. "But a guy like you... you have needs, right? You can't be a caretaker 24/7. You need someone to take care of *you* for a change."

At the exact same moment, Kyle slid into the non-existent space on Nichole’s right side. He didn't know who she was. He didn't know she couldn't speak. He just saw a girl with her head down.

"Hey there," Kyle said, flashing a lazy, confident smile. "Your boyfriend here looks busy with the blonde. You look like a listener. I like that."

Nichole froze. She didn't look up. She couldn't.

"Aw, don't be shy," Kyle said, moving closer.

His cologne—a cheap, musk-heavy scent—washed over her like a chemical spill. It was thick, oily, and reeked of alcohol and synthetic pheromones. To Nichole’s sensory-sensitive nose, it smelled like burning plastic.

"I like the quiet ones. Keeps things mysterious, right?"

He reached out.

He didn't tap her knee. He didn't touch her shin.

His hand landed heavily on her upper thigh, well above the knee. His fingers splayed out, claiming the real estate.

The Internal Short-Circuit: Nichole stopped breathing. That specific patch of skin was a Patrick Zone. It was a zone of absolute vulnerability, associated with the safety of the bath and the toilet transfer. Kyle’s hand felt like a brand. It was hot, sweaty, and possessive. It wasn't cleaning her; it was staining her.

"You don't have to say a word," Kyle smirked, leaning in, giving her leg a squeeze—a familiar, intimate gesture he had absolutely no right to make. "Let's just see where the three minutes takes us."

The Detonation: Patrick didn't just see a hand. He saw a foreign object breaching the perimeter.

The rage that hit him wasn't emotional; it was biological. It was the reaction of a wolf seeing a stranger’s teeth near its cub’s throat.

CRACK.

The sound was like a gunshot in the small lounge.

Patrick didn't just push the hand away. He struck Kyle’s wrist with the back of his hand—a vicious, calculated strike aimed at the ulnar nerve.

Kyle yelped, recoiling instantly, clutching his wrist against his chest.

"OFF," Patrick snarled.

The word wasn't spoken; it was barked.

Patrick unfolded from the sofa. He didn't stand up like a student; he stood up like a riot shield. He placed his body physically between Kyle and Nichole.

"Whoa!" Kyle stammered, his eyes wide, nursing his stinging hand. "Chill, man! I was just being friendly!"

"You touched her," Patrick said. His voice was terrifyingly quiet. "You put your hand on her thigh."

"I didn't know!" Kyle backed up, unnerved by the sheer size of the guy looming over him. "She didn't say anything!"

"She *can't*," Patrick hissed. "And that?" He pointed a shaking finger at Nichole’s leg. "That is not for you. That is never for you."

Patrick turned back to Nichole. She was trembling so hard the sofa was vibrating. She was scrubbing at her leg, trying to wipe away the phantom sensation of the stranger’s hand.

Patrick saw the scrubbing. It broke his heart more than the anger had.

He turned to Jessica, who was still standing there, stunned by the violence of the reaction.

"And you," Patrick said to her. "You think I'm 'trapped'? You think I'm 'furniture'?"

"I'm just saying you deserve a break!" Jessica yelled, trying to salvage the interaction. "Don't you want to stand up? Talk to a girl who can actually talk back?"

"I am exactly where I want to be," Patrick said.

He leaned down toward Nichole. He didn't offer her a hand. He didn't do the gentle bridal scoop. He needed to clear the room, and he needed to show these people exactly what they were dealing with.

He bent his knees, sliding his arms under her knees and behind her back, locking his hands together. He braced his core.

The Lift: Patrick executed a perfect, heavy-duty Deadlift Carry.

He powered up, lifting her entire body weight vertically in one smooth, explosive motion. He held her high against his chest, her legs dangling, her face buried in his neck. It wasn't romantic. It was industrial. It was a display of raw, utilitarian strength that said, *I can move this mountain, and you cannot.*

The room went silent, save for the screaming guitar solo. Jessica stepped back, intimidated by the sheer physicality of the maneuver.

Patrick pivoted. He marched two steps to where the *Titan AeroMotion* was parked.

He lowered Nichole into the seat with a gentleness that contradicted the violence of the lift. He clicked the seatbelt. *Click.*

"We're leaving," Patrick announced.

He grabbed the push handles. He rammed the chair forward, forcing Jessica to jump out of the way or get run over.

"If you ever touch her again," Patrick said to Kyle as he passed, "you won't be using that hand for a long time."

He reached the double doors. The chair wedged under the handle was still there.

Patrick didn't remove it politely. He kicked the wedge. The chair flew across the hallway, clattering against the lockers.

The doors swung wide open.

Patrick pushed Nichole out into the hallway.

But he didn't let the doors close. He pulled the chair back, jamming the door wide open.

The blaring sound of *"Thunder in the Rain"* spilled out into the administrative hallway, echoing off the tile, instantly alerting every secretary, dean, and security guard on the second floor.

"Hey!" Megaphone Guy yelled from inside. "Shut the door! The narcs will hear us!"

Patrick looked back at them—at the chaos, the predators, and the noise.

"Good," Patrick said.

He turned the wheelchair and walked away, leaving the unauthorized mixer exposed to the wrath of the administration.

## Date: November 14, 2004 Time: 1:35 PM Location: The Hallway (Heading to the Server Room)

The music faded as they put distance between themselves and the lounge.

Patrick didn't slow down. He felt Nichole trembling against his chest.

He smoothed her hair as he walked. He knew what Jessica was thinking. She saw the broad shoulders, the height, the jawline. She saw raw material. She saw a potential father for her potential kids. She saw "Good Genes."

Patrick almost laughed.

*If you only knew, lady,* he thought.

He remembered the doctor’s office when he was fourteen. The white paper on the table. The awkward look on his dad’s face. The specialist explaining the word Azoospermia.

*“It means, Patrick, that you will likely never father biological children. The factory just... doesn't make that part.”*

Tom Bennett had looked devastated. He saw the family name hitting a wall.

But Patrick? Patrick had looked at the poster on the wall—a diagram of the human heart—and felt a strange, cold sense of peace.

He realized then that he didn't have to split his time. He didn't have to worry about the "One day, when you have your own kids..." speech people gave him.

He was never going to have his own kids. He was a Unipod. A singular unit.

*I shoot blanks,* Patrick thought, replaying Jessica’s hungry look. *Every time.*

It made him immune to her pitch. She was selling a future he couldn't buy. She wanted a stud; she was barking up the wrong tree. He was a mule—strong, stubborn, and sterile. Built for work, not for breeding.

He looked down at Nichole. She was looking up at him, her eyes wide, waiting for him to be angry about the interruption.

He wasn't angry. He was amused.

Nichole tapped on his chest. She made a "zero" sign with her hand. *She missed out.*

Patrick smirked. He leaned down, whispering the secret joke that only he knew.

"Trust me, Nikki," Patrick said. "She's missing absolutely nothing."

He kissed her forehead.

"Now, let's get to the server room. I'm tired of people."
