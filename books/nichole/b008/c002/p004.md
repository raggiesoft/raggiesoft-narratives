# Part 4: The First Lecture

## Date: Monday, August 30, 2004 Time: 9:00 AM Location: College of Arts & Sciences, Room 104 (English 101)

The lecture hall hummed with the low-level anxiety of forty freshmen trying to look like they belonged.

Patrick sat in the removable desk chair he had pulled into the front row. To his right, Nichole was parked in her purple titanium chair, her *Apex ConvertiBook* open and ready.

Professor Harrison stood at the podium, arranging his notes. He looked out at the sea of faces—kids from the suburbs, commuters from the city, athletes in BSU tracksuits.

Then his eyes landed on the pair in the front row.

He saw the technology—the high-end convertible tablet, the optical mouse, the wheelchair that looked more like a vehicle than a medical device.

But mostly, he saw the boy.

Patrick wasn't looking at the professor. He wasn't checking his syllabus. He was looking at his sister.

Nichole’s arm had slipped slightly off her armrest. Without breaking his conversation with her, Patrick reached out. He didn't ask. He didn't make a scene. He simply lifted her forearm and repositioned it, tucking her elbow in to ensure she had the leverage to use her mouse.

It was a tiny motion, performed with the unconscious competence of someone who had done it ten thousand times.

Nichole didn't flinch. She didn't say thank you. She just leaned slightly into his space, her shoulder brushing his arm.

Professor Harrison had seen aides with disabled students before. Usually, there was a stiffness—a professional distance, or a hovering, nervous energy.

This was different. This was a single organism with two heads.

"Good morning," Professor Harrison said, starting the class. "Welcome to English 101. I assume everyone has read the syllabus?"

Patrick finally looked up. He clicked his pen.

He felt the weight of the "Sacred Trust" he had acknowledged in the dark of their bedroom. It wasn't a burden here in the daylight. It was armor.

He wasn't just a student taking notes. He was the Secret Agent, and he had successfully infiltrated the university.

Nichole tapped her screen. A single word appeared in her document, large and bold.

BEGIN.

Patrick smiled. He wrote the date on his graph paper. The Machine was running.
