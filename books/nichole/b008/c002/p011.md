# Part 11: The Treatment Protocol

## Date: October 12, 2004 Time: 10:15 AM Location: The Master Suite, Unit 404

The steel door of Unit 404 clicked shut and the heavy deadbolt slid home. The world—the angry man, the hospital lights, the noise of the bus—was finally locked out.

They were back in the Fortress.

"Phase One," Patrick announced, dropping the pharmacy bag on the kitchen island. "Oral bombardment."

He moved to the sink and filled a tall glass with filtered water. He didn't rush. The panic of the morning had been replaced by the steady, methodical rhythm of the Machine.

He walked over to where Nichole was parked by the counter. He placed the glass in front of her. Then, he popped the blister pack on the oral antifungal pill—a single, pink tablet.

He placed the pill on a napkin next to the glass.

"All you, Nikki," Patrick said.

He stepped back. He didn't hover. He knew she hated being fed like a baby when she was perfectly capable of doing it herself.

Nichole reached out. Her hand was steady now that the adrenaline had faded. She picked up the pill, popped it into her mouth, and took a long drink of cool water. She swallowed.

She tapped the counter. *Done.*

"Good," Patrick said. "That hits the system from the inside. Now for the outside."

He picked up the tube of Nystatin cream.

"Bedroom. Transfer to bed. We need to get this on before the pain spikes again."

## Time: 10:25 AM Location: The Master Bed

Patrick helped her transfer out of the chair and onto the King mattress. He positioned her carefully, making sure she was lying flat on her back, her legs relaxed.

"Okay, dignity protocol," Patrick murmured.

He didn't just strip her off and leave her exposed. That wasn't how they operated.

He grabbed a clean, flat sheet from the linen shelf. He draped it over her lower body, covering her completely from waist to toes.

"Right leg up," Patrick instructed softly.

He gently lifted her right leg—the "good" side—and bent the knee, creating a tent with the sheet.

"Okay, exposing the target area."

He carefully pulled the sheet back *only* on the left side, exposing just her hip and the angry red crease of her groin. The rest of her—her other leg, her center—remained covered by the white fabric. It was a surgical field, precise and clinical.

Patrick walked to the ensuite bathroom and washed his hands. He scrubbed them for a full thirty seconds with hot water and antibacterial soap. He dried them thoroughly.

He returned to the bed and sat on the edge. He uncapped the tube.

"Warning," Patrick said, catching her eye. "It's going to be cold. And because the skin is raw, it might sting for a second before it numbs out."

Nichole nodded. She gripped the sheets with her hands. *Do it.*

Patrick squeezed a ribbon of yellow cream onto his finger.

"Contact in three, two, one."

He touched the cream to the red skin.

Nichole hissed through her teeth, her body tensing.

"I know," Patrick whispered. "I know."

He didn't rub. Rubbing caused friction. Friction caused fire.

Instead, he *patted* the cream onto the lesions. His touch was feather-light, dabbing the medication over the satellite dots and the deep red crease. He covered the entire area in a thin, protective layer.

"Coverage is 100%," Patrick announced softly.

He waited a moment, letting the air dry the cream slightly so it wouldn't just wipe off on the sheets.

"Okay, we're leaving this loose," Patrick said. "No pants right now. We need airflow."

He adjusted the sheet so it tented over her hips, keeping her modest but preventing the fabric from touching the treated area.

He recapped the tube and set it on the nightstand next to her *ConvertiBook*.

"The doctor said twice a day," Patrick explained, checking the label. "Morning and night. We do this for seven days minimum to make sure it doesn't come back."

He looked at her.

"The burning should stop in about two days. Until then... we just hold on."

Nichole let out a long exhale. The cold cream was already starting to soothe the worst of the heat. The fire was banking down to a smolder.

She looked at her brother. He looked exhausted. The sprint to the ferry, the confrontation in the waiting room, the hyper-vigilance—it had drained him.

She moved her hand to the mouse.

"YOU. DID. GOOD."

Patrick offered a tired, lopsided smile. He reached out and squeezed her hand—the one part of her he could touch without causing pain.

"We did good," Patrick corrected. "Now rest. I'm going to email Professor Harrison and tell him we were fighting monsters."

He stood up to dim the lights. The Fortress was secure, the medicine was deployed, and the healing could finally begin.
