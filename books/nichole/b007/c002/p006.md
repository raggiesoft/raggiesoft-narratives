# Part 9: The Foundry

## Date: April 16, 2004 Time: 10:00 AM Location: The Bennett Machine Transit Grid (Portsmouth to Braxton)

The next morning, the Rhode Island sky broke with an uncharacteristic April heatwave. The temperature was seventy-two degrees, the sun was blazing, and the winter coats were officially packed away.

It was the perfect day to show the family the grid.

Patrick and Nichole didn't drive the Liberty van. They wanted to let the family into their world.

The entire clan—Tom, Ellen, Uncle Mark, Aunt Carol, and the twins—walked to the end of the driveway on West Main Road. At 10:15 AM, the massive white-and-blue OSTA Route 60 bus pulled up.

As the heavy ramp deployed, Patrick pushed Nichole on board. The rest of the family followed, dropping their fare into the box.

"This is it, huh?" Ellen smiled, holding onto the handrail as the bus rolled south. "The lifeline."

"Step one," Patrick confirmed.

They rode the bus into Newport, disembarking at the Gateway Center. They walked the single block to Perrotti Park, the warm harbor breeze washing over them. At the ferry dock, Patrick and Nichole tapped their BTA RegionLink cards at the turnstiles. Tom bought paper tickets for the adults.

They boarded the *Newport Flyer*. As the catamaran roared across Narragansett Bay, Uncle Mark and Tom stood on the open-air upper deck, marveling at the speed, while Nichole sat inside near the massive windows with the women.

"Twenty-eight minutes," Aunt Carol said, checking her watch as the Braxton skyline loomed ahead. "No traffic. No bridge tolls. Unbelievable."

They disembarked at the Narragansett Esplanade Station. Instead of entering *The Bull Run* toward the campus, Patrick led the family to the central glass elevators and took them all the way down to Level M: The Deep.

A Blue Line Metro train was waiting. They rolled onto the heavy-rail car, the air-conditioning providing a cool relief from the spring heat.

"Three stops," Patrick announced.

*Whoosh.* The train sped through the dark tunnels under the city.

"Now arriving at... Foundry Square," the automated voice chimed.

## Time: 11:30 AM Location: The Braxton Link (Foundry Square Station)

They exited the train onto the island platform and took the elevator up to Basement Level 1.

The doors opened into *The Braxton Link*. It was a wide, brightly lit subterranean avenue lined with commercial storefronts.

"Okay, look to your left," Patrick said, pointing down the corridor.

There was a glass-walled commercial bank branch: New England Regional Credit Union.

"That's our bank," Tom noted, surprised. "That's convenient."

"It's not just convenient, Dad," Patrick grinned. "Look what's right next to it."

Directly adjacent to the credit union was a heavy, secure steel door with a polished brass plaque: THE FOUNDRY LOFTS - RESIDENT ENTRANCE.

"The bank and the apartment building share the same basement," Patrick explained. "It's all one footprint."

They took the adjacent elevator up to the street-level leasing office.

The building was a converted 19th-century textile mill—soaring ceilings, exposed brick walls, and massive industrial windows. The property manager, a sharp-dressed man named Mr. Caldwell, greeted the large party warmly.

"We got your call," Mr. Caldwell smiled, looking at Patrick and Nichole. "Dual admission to BSU. Congratulations. Let's go look at the model unit."

Mr. Caldwell led them down a wide, carpeted hallway. "All our hallways are minimum six feet wide to meet modern code," he explained.

He unlocked the door to a two-bedroom corner unit.

Nichole rolled inside. The space was flooded with natural light from twelve-foot windows overlooking the city. The floors were polished concrete—zero-resistance for her wheels. The kitchen counters were open underneath the sink, and the master bathroom featured a massive, zero-threshold roll-in shower.

It was stunning. It was an absolute Fortress.

Nichole looked at Patrick. She made a soft, high-pitched *keening* sound of pure joy. She tapped her screen.

"THIS. IS. THE. ONE."

"We have this exact floor plan available on the 4th floor for immediate move-in," Mr. Caldwell said. "Of course, there is a standard application fee of fifty dollars per person, and we do run credit."

Tom stepped forward. "We can fill out the application right now. But regarding the financials... we prefer to simplify things."

## Time: 1:00 PM Location: The Leasing Office

While the twins filled out the paperwork, Tom and Mark took the elevator back down to the basement level. They walked into the New England Regional Credit Union branch.

Ten minutes later, they returned. Tom placed a certified cashier's check on Mr. Caldwell's desk.

The check was made out for exactly \$23,397.61 (Grandma Helen's savings, minus the \$100 application fees).

Mr. Caldwell looked at the check. He looked at Tom.

"This is... substantial," Mr. Caldwell said.

"The rent is \$2,250 a month," Tom said in his best accountant voice. "That check covers the required security deposit of \$2,250, the first month's rent of \$2,250, and prepays their rent for the next eight and a half months. They are paid in full until next May."

Mr. Caldwell didn't even run the credit check. Cash of that magnitude erased all doubts.

"Well," Mr. Caldwell smiled, pulling the lease agreement from his desk. "I believe your application is approved. Sign here, please."
