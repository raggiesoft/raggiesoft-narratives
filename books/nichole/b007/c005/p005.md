# Part 5: The Blueprint

## Date: July 16, 2004 Time: 12:10 AM Location: The Master Bedroom

The storm was gone, but the adrenaline was still fading. They lay in the dark, the city lights reflecting off the wet streets outside and casting ripples on the ceiling.

Patrick was staring up at those ripples.

"Six weeks," he murmured.

Nichole knew exactly what he meant. Six weeks until Move-In Day for everyone else. Six weeks until Braxton State University opened its doors for the Fall Semester.

That was the real storm. The academic pressure. The crowds. The deadlines.

But lying there, safe in the post-storm quiet, Patrick didn't feel afraid. He felt prepared.

"We have the schedule," Patrick recited, almost like a prayer. "English 101. Psych 101. Western Civ. Pre-Algebra."

He turned his head on the pillow to look at her silhouette.

"We're in the same room every time. I'm sitting right next to you. If you need a drink, I'm there. If you drop your stylus, I'm there. If you just need to squeeze someone's hand because the fluorescent lights are buzzing too loud, I'm right there."

Nichole reached out and found his hand under the duvet. She squeezed it.

"AND. BREAKS," she thought, projecting the thought to him.

Patrick squeezed back, as if he heard her.

"And the gaps," he continued. "We built the schedule right. We have ninety minutes between the morning block and the afternoon block. That's not just lunch time. That's 'Recharge Time.' We can come back here. We can go to a quiet corner of the library. We can just... shut down."

That was the key. They weren't going to burn out because they weren't going to run at 100% all day. They had engineered the syllabus to fit their biology, not the other way around.

The storm outside had tested their physical fortress. The semester ahead would test their mental fortress.

But as Nichole drifted off to sleep, holding her brother's hand, she knew they would pass that test too. They were the Bennett Machine. And the Machine was waterproof.
