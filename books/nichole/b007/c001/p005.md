# Part 5: The Grid Returns

## Date: December 25, 2003 Time: 7:00 AM Location: The Bennett Wing, 95 West Main Road, Portsmouth, Rhode Island

Nichole woke up to the sound of absolute silence outside. The howling wind that had battered the windows all night was gone.

Inside the bedroom, the air was brisk. The baseboard radiators, running on the generator's limited emergency cycle, were just warm enough to keep the pipes from freezing, but they weren't fighting the December chill.

Under the heavy winter duvet, however, the temperature was a perfect seventy-five degrees.

Nichole shifted slightly. Patrick was still fast asleep, his breathing slow and rhythmic against her back. His arm was still draped heavily across her waist, his hand resting on her hip.

It was Christmas morning. And for the first time in their lives, they were waking up in a house built entirely to their specifications.

Nichole made a soft, happy *clicking* sound with her tongue.

Patrick stirred behind her. He let out a sleepy groan, tightened his grip around her waist for a second, and then opened his eyes.

"Morning, Nikki," Patrick whispered, his voice thick with sleep. He looked over her shoulder at the window. The blizzard had passed, leaving behind a wall of white that reached halfway up the glass. "Looks like a white Christmas."

He pulled the duvet back, and the cold air hit them instantly.

"Okay, rapid deployment," Patrick shivered, jumping out of bed.

He moved efficiently, pulling on heavy wool socks and a thick BSU sweatshirt. He helped Nichole dress quickly under the covers, layering her in a thermal long-sleeve shirt and a heavy fleece.

He lifted her from the bed, transferring her into the Titan AeroMotion. Her body was loose and rested; the deep, anchored sleep had worked wonders on her tone.

## Time: 8:30 AM Location: The Living Room

The rest of the family was gathered around the large stone fireplace in the main living room. Uncle Mark had a roaring fire going.

With the electric stove dead, Mark had dragged a heavy-duty camping burner out to the enclosed back porch to boil water. The living room smelled richly of French press coffee and woodsmoke.

"Merry Christmas, sleepyheads!" Aunt Carol beamed, wrapped in a thick quilt on the sofa.

"Merry Christmas," Patrick said, pushing Nichole up to the hearth.

Grandma Helen was parked next to the fire, a steaming mug of tea in her hand. She looked completely content.

"No elevator this morning, Mom," Tom said to Helen, handing her a fresh blanket. "Generator doesn't have the juice for the hydraulics."

"Who needs to go anywhere?" Helen smiled, looking around the room filled with her children and grandchildren. "Everything I need is on the first floor."

Nichole sat by the fire, her hands tucked under her custom thermal blanket. She looked at her family. Eighteen years ago, they had been a fractured unit, separated by distance and medical anxiety. Now, they were snowed in, running on backup power, and nobody was stressed.

Uncle Mark sat in his armchair, reading a paperback book by the light of the fire. Ellen was laughing at a joke Tom made. Patrick was sitting on the floor next to Nichole's wheel, tossing a piece of kindling into the flames.

It was perfect.

Then, the clock on the mantel ticked over.

## Time: 9:12 AM

*Ka-CLACK.* The sound of the main breaker engaging echoed from the basement. The low thrum of the generator outside cut off.

Suddenly, the living room lamps surged to full, brilliant brightness. The dormant television in the corner clicked on, blasting the morning news. The refrigerator in the kitchen kicked into its familiar hum.

And from the hallway, a soft electronic *chime* announced that the residential elevator system was back online.

"The grid is back!" Mark cheered, tossing his book onto the side table. "Alright, Carol, fire up the stove! We have a Christmas breakfast to make!"

The room erupted into movement. The spell of the quiet morning was broken, replaced by the chaotic joy of a fully operational house.

Patrick stood up and stretched. He looked down at Nichole.

"Battery is at 100%," Patrick grinned, referencing both the house and his sister. "You ready to open some presents?"

Nichole reached for her mouse on the lap tray. The Apex ConvertiBook had stayed fully charged on its internal battery all night.

She opened the text-to-speech module.

"MERRY. CHRISTMAS. TO. THE. MACHINE."

"Merry Christmas, Nikki," Patrick laughed, bending down to hug her.

Outside, the snow plows were finally clearing West Main Road. The grid was alive, the Fortress was warm, and 2004 was officially on the horizon.
