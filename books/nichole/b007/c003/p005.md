# Part 5: The Gen-Ed Pact

## Date: April 18, 2004 Time: 5:15 PM Location: The Command Center, Unit 404

The sun had finally dipped below the skyline, painting the loft in cool twilight blues.

Patrick and Nichole had migrated from the sofa to the "Command Center" in the second bedroom. It was time to test the university's digital infrastructure.

Patrick sat at his desk. Nichole rolled her chair under her custom cutout next to him.

"Alright, let's see if we can lock in the schedule," Patrick said, logging into the BSU Student Portal (Banner Web) for the first time.

He clicked on REGISTRATION - FALL 2004.

A red error message popped up: REGISTRATION CLOSED. FRESHMAN ENROLLMENT OPENS MAY 15.

"Denied," Patrick muttered. "Looks like we have to wait for the official window."

Nichole felt a spike of anxiety. May 15th felt lightyears away. She looked at the course catalog PDF they had downloaded.

She knew the curriculum. Patrick was Engineering; she was Technical Communication. Their majors would eventually split them into different buildings—him in the labs, her in the writing seminars.

But for Freshman year, everyone had to take the "Core."

She typed quickly.

"GEN. ED. MUST. MATCH."

"Way ahead of you," Patrick said, pulling up a spreadsheet he had already started. "English 101. Intro to Psychology. History of Western Civ. University Math."

He pointed to the screen.

"We don't just pick the classes. We pick the Section Numbers."

He highlighted ENG 101 - Section 04 (MWF 9:00 AM).

"If I register for Section 04, and you register for Section 04, we are in the same room, at the same time, with the same professor."

Nichole looked at the screen. She needed to hear it. The fear of walking into a classroom alone—unable to speak, unable to ask for help, unable to physically navigate a crowded row of desks—was her biggest nightmare.

She looked at Patrick.

"PROMISE?"

Patrick swiveled his chair to face her. He looked her dead in the eye.

"I promise," Patrick said, his voice dropping to that serious, 'Mission Critical' tone. "We are a package deal. I will override the system if I have to. We sit together. Every class. Every day. No exceptions."

Nichole let out a breath she didn't know she was holding. The pact was sealed.

## Time: 6:00 PM, Location: The Kitchen

"Okay, strategic planning is over," Patrick announced, standing up. "Who wants dinner?"

Nichole’s stomach gave a small rumble. The wieners from lunch were long gone.

"I'm thinking... classic New England," Patrick said, opening the pantry.

He pulled out a jar of Marshmallow Fluff.

He pulled out a jar of Creamy Peanut Butter.

He pulled out a loaf of white bread.

"The Fluffernutter," Patrick declared.

Technically, the purists in Rhode Island insisted on crunchy peanut butter to add texture to the marshmallow creme. But Patrick knew the reality of kitchen logistics: spreading crunchy peanut butter on soft white bread often tore the bread.

Creamy peanut butter was the engineer's choice. Low friction. High structural integrity.

He lined up four slices of bread.

- Step 1: Thick layer of peanut butter on one side (the structural base).

- Step 2: A massive scoop of Marshmallow Fluff on the other side (the sweet, sticky glue).

- Step 3: Merge.

He sliced them diagonally—because sandwiches taste better that way—and placed them on plates.

"Side dish," Patrick muttered, opening the crisper drawer. He pulled out two Granny Smith apples. He sliced them quickly. The tartness of the apple would cut through the intense sugar of the Fluff.

Finally, he filled two tall glasses with filtered tap water from the fridge dispenser and added ice.

He brought the plates to the island.

Nichole looked at the sandwich. It was simple. It was sticky. It was childhood on a plate.

She took a bite. The peanut butter stuck to the roof of her mouth, the Fluff was impossibly sweet, and the bread was soft.

She took a sip of the ice water to wash it down.

She looked at Patrick, who was already demolishing his sandwich.

They were sitting in their own kitchen, eating Fluffernutters, planning their joint takeover of the BSU curriculum.

She tapped the counter with her hand to get his attention.

"GOOD. SANDWICH."

"Creamy PB is the superior spreading medium," Patrick stated matter-of-factly. "Don't tell Mom."

Nichole smiled.

"YOUR. SECRET. IS. SAFE."

## Time: 10:30 PM Location: The Master Suite, Unit 404

The city outside the industrial windows was a grid of amber sodium lights and red taillights, but inside the loft, the world was quiet.

"Shutdown sequence," Patrick announced softly.

He rolled Nichole into the master bathroom. The harsh fluorescent lights of their old life—the hospital bathrooms, the dorm evaluations—were gone. Here, the lighting was warm and dimmable.

The routine began with the biological necessities. Patrick lifted Nichole to the toilet, managing the mechanics of her body with practiced, respectful efficiency. He waited, leaning against the vanity, present and available. When she was finished, he attended to her hygiene, then reset the room.

Then, he used the facilities himself. There was no shuffling in and out of the room. They were a single functional unit, operating in the same space without shame.

## Time: 10:45 PM Location: The Roll-In Shower

"Water temp," Patrick murmured, reaching for the single-handle lever. He found the sweet spot—hot enough to relax the muscles, cool enough not to scald.

He stripped Nichole of her day clothes, placing them in the hamper. Then, he stripped himself.

He wheeled the shower chair into the slate-tiled stall. The water rained down from the wide rainfall head.

Patrick stepped into the spray with her. He picked up the sponge and the body wash. He started with her shoulders, working down her back, washing away the sweat of the city and the stress of the day.

"Arms up," he whispered. She complied, lifting her arms so he could wash her sides.

When she was clean, he didn't stop to dry her yet. He turned the sponge on himself, scrubbing his chest and arms, standing right there in the steam beside her.

It was the most efficient, honest moment of the day. No barriers. No hiding. Just two humans cleaning the machine.

He turned off the water. The silence rushed back in, heavy and warm.

He grabbed the fluffy towels. He dried her gently—patting her skin dry to avoid irritation. He dried her hair. Then, he dried himself quickly.

## Time: 11:00 PM Location: The King Bed

Patrick dropped the towel. He didn't reach for the pajamas.

He leaned down and scooped Nichole up from the shower chair. Her skin was warm and clean. Her muscles, relaxed by the hot water, were heavy and loose.

He carried her out of the bathroom and over to the massive King bed. He laid her down on the cool, smooth sheets.

He didn't have to wrestle with flannel pants. He didn't have to tug at twisted sleeves.

He climbed in beside her. He pulled the heavy duvet up over both of them.

Nichole shifted. Usually, this was the moment she braced herself—waiting for the fabric of her pajamas to catch on the sheets, triggering a spasm that would torque her hips.

But there was no catch.

Her legs slid smoothly against the high-thread-count sheets. Her skin moved freely against his. There was zero friction.

Patrick settled in behind her, wrapping his left arm around her waist, pulling her back against his chest. He hooked his leg over hers, anchoring her lower body with his weight.

"Better?" Patrick whispered into the dark.

Nichole let out a long breath. She didn't need the computer to answer. She took his hand—the one resting on her stomach—and gave it two slow squeezes. Yes.

She felt the heat radiating from him. She felt the steady rise and fall of his breathing.

For the first time in years, she wasn't sleeping in "medical mode." She wasn't a patient being managed. She was just a girl, safe in the arms of the only person who truly knew her.

She closed her eyes. The fear of the spasm was gone. The fear of the institution was gone.

She was safe. She was secure. And she was home.
