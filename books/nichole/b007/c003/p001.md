# Part 1: The Zero-Friction Protocol

## Date: April 18, 2004 Time: 8:30 AM Location: The Master Suite, Unit 404

The sun hit the industrial windows of The Foundry Lofts, filling the room with a warm, brilliant light.

Patrick opened his eyes. For a second, he panicked. He reached out, expecting to feel the familiar tangle of flannel pajama pants twisted around Nichole’s legs—the result of a night battling her extensor spasms.

But there was no flannel. There was no twisting.

He looked over. Nichole was still asleep, her breathing deep and rhythmic. The heavy duvet was pulled up to her shoulders, but beneath it, she was completely unencumbered.

Without the friction of cotton pajamas catching on the sheets or bunching up around her knees, her legs had remained straighter, her hips more relaxed. The "catch" points that usually woke her up three times a night were gone.

Patrick sat up, rubbing his face. He realized he had simply forgotten. In the exhaustion of the move and the novelty of the shower, he had skipped the "Dress for Bed" step of the algorithm.

Nichole stirred. She blinked against the sunlight, then turned her head to look at him. She didn't look distressed. She looked... loose.

She reached for the Apex ConvertiBook on the bedside swing-arm.

"BEST. SLEEP. EVER."

Patrick laughed, running a hand through his messy hair. "Yeah? I forgot the PJs. Total failure on my part."

Nichole shook her head. She tapped the screen again.

"NO. FAILURE. NO. CATCHING. NO. TWISTING. SPASMS. SLIDE."

Patrick looked at the logic. It made perfect sense. Pajamas were just another layer of mechanical resistance. When a spasm hit, the fabric grabbed the sheets, creating torque on her joints. Skin against smooth high-thread-count sheets? Zero friction. The spasm would fire, slide harmlessly, and release.

He pulled the duvet back to help her sit up. As he did, he automatically scanned her hips and heels—the danger zones for any wheelchair user.

Usually, he had to wrestle elastic waistbands down or pull up pant legs to check for the tell-tale red spots that indicated a developing pressure sore.

But now? The data was right there.

"Clear," Patrick noted, inspecting her sacrum and heels in two seconds flat. "Skin integrity is perfect. And I can check you without waking you up fully."

He looked at Nichole.

"New protocol?" Patrick asked. "Birthday suits only?"

Nichole grinned. She tapped her screen.

"BURN. THE. PAJAMAS."

## Time: 9:15 AM Location: The Kitchen

Fully dressed in their day clothes—jeans and hoodies—they sat at the kitchen island.

Patrick poured two bowls of generic toasted oat rings. The milk carton sat on the counter between them.

"Okay, Logistics check," Patrick said around a mouthful of cereal. "We have the apartment. We have the shower routine. We have the sleep protocol."

He pulled a folded BTA subway map out of his back pocket and smoothed it onto the counter.

"Today is reconnaissance," Patrick announced. "We need to map the perimeter. We know the subway route to campus. But we need to find the essentials. Pharmacy. Hardware store."

He looked at Nichole. "And lunch. We've had enough pizza to last a lifetime after the move yesterday."

Nichole nodded in strong agreement. She wanted real food. Local food.

She pointed to the section of the map labeled "Downtown Waterfront."

"WE. NEED. SYSTEM. WIENERS."

Patrick grinned. It was the quintessential Rhode Island staple—"New York System" hot wieners, loaded with meat sauce, onions, mustard, and celery salt, usually washed down with a coffee milk. It was greasy, salty, and exactly what they needed.

"I heard about a place on 4th Street," Patrick said. "Buddy’s Famous System. Supposed to be the best 'all the way' order in the city."

Nichole’s eyes lit up. Hot wieners and coffee milk. It was the official meal of their new independence.

"TARGET. ACQUIRED."

Patrick rinsed the bowls and put them in the dishwasher.

"Ready to roll out, Nikki?"

Nichole spun her chair toward the door. The batteries on the Titan AeroMotion were fully charged. The tires were pumped. The grid was waiting.

"LAUNCH."
