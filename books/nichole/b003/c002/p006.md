# Part 6: The All Clear

Time: 11:10 AM

A white SUV marked VIRGINIA BEACH FIRE DEPARTMENT - BATTALION CHIEF rolled slowly through the bus loop.

The driver rolled down his window and spoke to Principal Harrison, who was standing with a megaphone near the building entrance.

The large fans that the firefighters had set up at the doors were powered down. The silence was sudden.

Harrison raised the megaphone.

"Attention students and staff!"

The chatter died down.

"The Fire Department has cleared the building. We had a mechanical failure in an HVAC unit on the second floor. The smoke has been cleared, and the air quality is safe."

A cheer went up from the students—not because they loved school, but because they wanted to sit down.

"Please proceed in an orderly fashion back to your homerooms. Do not run."

The mass of students began to surge forward, a slow-moving tide of denim and backpacks heading across the asphalt.

Patrick didn't move yet. He looked at the distance. It was a hundred yards across the bus loop to the door. Then up the stairs.

"Okay," Patrick said to Nichole. "Phase Two."

Mr. Henderson walked over. He looked relieved that the "teaching moment" was over.

"Bennett," Henderson said. "Let's go. We need to get back to the room."

He looked at Nichole, still sitting on the curb. He looked at the empty space where a wheelchair should be.

"Wait," Henderson realized. "Her chair is upstairs."

"Yep," Patrick said.

"Well... stay here," Henderson ordered, looking around for an administrator. "I'll go tell the office. They can send a custodian to bring the chair down to you. Or maybe the nurse has a loaner."

Patrick looked at the crowd bottlenecking at the doors. He looked at Nichole. She was shivering now. She had been sitting on concrete for an hour. Her hip flexors were tight.

If they waited for a custodian to navigate the crowd, find the chair, figure out how to unlock the brakes, and bring it down... they would be out here for another twenty minutes.

And a "loaner" chair from the nurse? Those were ancient, vinyl disasters that offered zero support for her specific needs.

"No," Patrick said.

"Patrick, you cannot carry her across the lot," Henderson said, his voice rising. "It's a liability. If you drop her..."

Patrick stood up. He dusted off his jeans.

He looked down at Nichole. "Ready to fly?"

Nichole looked at the teacher, then at her brother. She reached up.
