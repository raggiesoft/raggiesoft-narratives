# Part 5: The Escort

## Date: September 1999 Time: 10:58 AM Location: Hallway / Men’s Locker Room

Principal Harrison stood up from his desk and looked at Patrick. The boy was still wearing his gym kit—shorts and a t-shirt—standing in the middle of the administrative office while the secretaries typed and phones rang.

"You can't go to Latin like that," Harrison said, grabbing his walkie-talkie. "Come on."

Harrison didn't send him with a hall pass. He walked around the desk and opened the office door himself.

They walked out into the main corridor. The bell had already rung, so the halls were eerily empty. Usually, being walked through an empty hall by the Principal meant you were about to be expelled. But Harrison didn't walk like an executioner; he walked like a bodyguard.

"We’re going to the locker room first," Harrison stated, his voice echoing slightly off the lockers. "Get your clothes. I’ll wait outside."

They reached the double doors of the gym wing. Harrison held the door open.

Patrick jogged into the empty locker room. The air smelled of damp towels and deodorant. He moved fast, terrified that if he took too long, the truce would break and Mrs. Vance would come back with the police. He ripped off his gym shirt, threw on his jeans and his flannel overshirt, and shoved his feet into his sneakers. He grabbed his backpack from his locker.

He came out two minutes later, breathless.

Harrison was standing exactly where he had left him, arms crossed, staring down a passing sophomore who had been lingering in the hall. The sophomore scrambled away.

"Ready?" Harrison asked.

"Yes, sir."

"Good. Let's get you to Mrs. Dalloway."

They began the long walk to the Language Arts wing.

"You realize," Harrison said quietly, looking straight ahead, "that Mrs. Vance is technically right. The paper says you stay away."

"I know," Patrick said, clutching his backpack straps.

"But papers don't scream," Harrison said.

He glanced down at Patrick.

"You did a good thing, Son. But be careful. If you get hurt helping her, the District will use it to separate you two permanently. They are looking for an excuse."

"I won't get hurt," Patrick said.

"See that you don't."
