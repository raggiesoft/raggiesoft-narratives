# Part 4: The Fuel Stop

## Date: July 3, 2003 Time: 6:30 PM Location: Hotel, Brattleboro, Vermont

The drive back to Brattleboro was a blur of gray rain and windshield wipers.

By the time Tom pulled the van under the covered portico of the hotel, Nichole had stopped crying. The deep, jagged sobs had faded into a low, exhausted *hummm* in the back of her throat. She was completely spent. The emotional whiplash of the day—the soaring hope followed by the crushing rejection—had drained every ounce of energy from her body.

Patrick unbuckled her from the bench seat. He didn't ask her to transfer herself. He just scooped her up and placed her gently into the purple Titan AeroMotion that Tom had rolled out from the back.

"Bed," Nichole tapped weakly on her lap tray, not even bothering to turn the VocaLink screen on. She made a soft, pleading whine.

"I know, Nikki," Patrick said, locking her feet into the footrests. "But you need calories. You didn't eat lunch."

Nichole frowned. Her stomach gave a hollow, aching rumble that betrayed her.

"Just a quick pit stop," Tom said, pointing across the rain-slicked parking lot.

Through the mist, the bright red and yellow neon sign of a Rocket Burger glowed like a beacon in the gloom. It was in the same commercial plaza as the hotel. No driving required.

"Five minutes," Patrick promised her. "In and out."

Patrick grabbed a hotel umbrella from the lobby. Ellen held it over the two of them as Patrick pushed the chair through the puddles of the parking lot. The rain drummed against the umbrella canopy.

Inside the Rocket Burger, the air smelled intensely of hot grease and salt—a universal, comforting smell that existed in Virginia just as much as it did in Vermont. It was brightly lit, loud, and entirely unsophisticated.

Nobody here cared about "student autonomy." Nobody was looking at her chair as a liability. They just wanted to know if she wanted fries.

Patrick parked her at a booth near the window. He grabbed a laminated menu from the counter and brought it over.

"Point and shoot, Nikki," Patrick said gently. "What's the fuel?"

Nichole was too tired for words. She tapped the picture of the double cheeseburger. Then she tapped the large fries. Finally, she pointed to the chocolate milkshake.

"Heavy loadout. Good choice," Patrick said, patting her shoulder.

They ate in near silence. Nichole chewed mechanically. The hot, salty food was a grounder, pulling her out of the sensory overload of the day. Patrick fed her the fries, dipping them in ketchup and holding them to her lips so she didn't have to expend the energy of lifting her heavy, tired arms.

"So," Tom said quietly, dipping a fry of his own. "The backup plan. Raritan State."

Ellen nodded, looking out the window at the rain. "Aunt Carol said it's totally flat. And it's huge. A state school doesn't have the time to micromanage living arrangements like a tiny private college does. They just want you in and out."

"We can tour it tomorrow on the drive home," Patrick said, taking a sip of his soda. He looked at Nichole, who was finishing her milkshake. "If we like it, we apply. If we don't, we keep looking. But we're not compromising on the machine."

Nichole looked at him with heavy, tired eyes. She made a soft, agreeable *click* with her tongue.

"Alright," Patrick said, wiping a dab of chocolate from her chin with a napkin. "Let's get you horizontal."

They returned from the burger joint through the rain. The hot, salty food had stopped Nichole’s shaking, but as Patrick rolled her into the accessible hotel room, a different kind of weight settled over her.

Tom and Ellen claimed the bed by the window, immediately turning on the television to give the twins their space.

Patrick locked the brakes of the Titan AeroMotion near the bathroom door. He reached for Nichole’s pajamas. "Okay. Quick transfer, straight to bed."

Nichole shook her head. She didn't want the bed yet. She reached out and grabbed his wrist, squeezing hard. She pointed to the bathroom door. She pointed to herself, and made a scrubbing motion on her arm.

"A bath?" Patrick asked gently. "Nikki, you're exhausted. You can barely keep your eyes open."

Nichole’s eyes filled with fresh tears. She made a low, distressed sound in her throat. She didn't just feel tired. She felt dirty. The words of the Disability Director—*impropriety, safeguarding, abuse*—were clinging to her skin like a film of grease. Putney College had taken the purest thing in her life—her brother's absolute, unconditional protection—and twisted it into something grotesque. They had accused Patrick of using her vulnerability against her.

She needed to wash it off.

Patrick looked into her eyes and understood instantly. His own jaw tightened with that same residual disgust. "Okay," Patrick said softly. "We wash it off. Let's go."

He rolled her into the accessible bathroom and closed the door, sealing out the noise of the television.

He started the water in the tub, making it hot and deep. Then, he turned to Nichole. He began to unbutton her shirt.

Nichole reached out and grabbed the hem of Patrick's t-shirt. She tugged on it, looking at him with a fierce, absolute insistence. *You too.*

She needed to know his defenses were down, just as hers were about to be. She needed the playing field leveled.

Patrick paused. He understood what she was asking. He pulled his t-shirt over his head and tossed it into the corner. He unbuckled his belt and stepped out of his shorts and underwear, kicking off his heavy plastic ankle brace.

He stood before her, just as bare and vulnerable as she was.

He lifted her from the wheelchair and lowered her gently into the hot water. Nichole let out a long, shuddering sigh as the heat enveloped her. She leaned her head back against the porcelain rim, tears leaking silently from the corners of her eyes.

Patrick sat on the edge of the tub. He took the washcloth, lathered it with soap, and began to wash her, just as he had done a million times before.

He washed her arms. He washed her stomach.

As he rubbed the soap across her skin, he paused. He looked at her. He really *looked* at her.

He examined her body with the practiced, critical eye of a nurse. He checked her hips for pressure sores from the thirteen-hour van ride. He checked her ribs for red marks from the chest harness. He checked the tone of her muscles.

It hit him then, harder than it ever had before, just how completely dependent she was on him. She couldn't walk. She couldn't speak. She couldn't even wash herself. She had placed the entirety of her physical safety into his hands.

"I'm here, Nikki," Patrick whispered, his voice thick with emotion. He reached out and brushed a wet strand of hair from her forehead. "I'm never going to stop doing this. For the rest of our lives. I promise you. I will never, ever drop this duty."

Nichole looked up at him through the steam. She saw the absolute, dead-serious conviction in his eyes. He wasn't just a brother. He was her shield.

Patrick continued to wash her, giving her a deep, thorough scrub. He was meticulous, working the soap into her skin to wash the day away.

But when he reached for a fresh washcloth to clean her intimate areas—a routine step of personal hygiene they had performed countless times without a second thought—Patrick stopped.

The weight of Putney's accusation hung heavy in the humid room.

Patrick looked into her eyes. He held up the washcloth.

"Is it okay if I wash you now?" Patrick asked, his voice a quiet, respectful whisper.

Nichole’s breath hitched. He had never asked before. He had never needed to. But today was different. Today, he was reclaiming the narrative. He was explicitly acknowledging her bodily autonomy, proving that the trust between them was earned, not assumed.

Nichole looked at her brother. She let out a soft, affirmative grunt. *Yes.*

Only then did Patrick proceed, washing her with the utmost delicacy and care.

When he finished, he sat back. Nichole noticed the look on his face—a dark, brooding pain that mirrored her own. She made a questioning, high-pitched cry. *Are you okay?*

Patrick shook his head, looking down at the bathwater. "I feel sick," he admitted, his voice rough. "What they said to us today... it makes me sick to my stomach. I would never hurt you, Nikki. Never. When I hold you at night, it's to keep your nervous system from crashing. It's to keep you safe. That's all it will ever be."

Nichole reached out a wet hand and cupped his cheek. She didn't need the VocaLink. She knew.

"Alright," Patrick said, taking a deep breath and clearing his throat. "Bath time is over. Let's get to bed."

He spread a thick, dry towel over the seat of her wheelchair. He lifted her from the tub, transferring her seamlessly. He dried her off quickly but thoroughly, making sure she was warm, and dressed her in her softest pajamas.

He pulled on his own sleep shorts and t-shirt, restoring the armor.

He wheeled her out of the bathroom into the bedroom.

"You guys okay?" Ellen asked, sitting up in the other bed, her face etched with maternal worry. "That was a long bath."

Nichole didn't look at her mother. She rolled up to the desk where Patrick had left her VocaLink.

She turned the screen on. She typed furiously with one finger.

"DIRTY. PLACE." Digital Dan’s voice announced. "HATE. VERMONT. LEAVE. NOW."

Ellen looked at Tom. Tom nodded immediately.

"Done," Tom said. "We're done with Vermont. We pack up first thing in the morning. We'll head straight back to Aunt Carol's in New Jersey."

Nichole let out a long, relieved sigh.

Patrick lifted her into the queen bed closest to the bathroom. He climbed in next to her, pulling the heavy comforter over them both. Nichole rolled onto her side, pressing her spine against his chest. Patrick wrapped his arm around her waist, locking her in place.

The lights went out. The rain continued to fall in the Vermont dark, but inside the bed, the Bennett machine was secure.
