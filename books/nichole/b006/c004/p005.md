# Part 5: The Hopeful Morning

Date: July 2, 2003

Time: 6:00 AM

Location: Hotel, New Brunswick, New Jersey

The red digits on the hotel alarm clock clicked over to 6:00 AM, and the buzzer blared—a harsh, rhythmic noise that cut through the silence of the accessible king suite.

Patrick didn't groan. He moved with the instant alertness of someone who had a mission. He reached out, slapping the snooze button to kill the noise, then turned to the other side of the bed.

"Morning, Nikki," he whispered, shaking her shoulder gently. "Game day. Vermont."

Nichole stirred, her eyes fluttering open. She didn't have the usual grogginess of a school morning. A spark of genuine excitement lit up her face. Today was the day they were going to see the "sanctuary." A real college, with a real quad, made for people like them.

Patrick threw back the heavy hotel comforter. "Pit stop first."

He stood up, testing his stiff left ankle on the carpet, then leaned over the bed. He scooped Nichole up in a fluid motion, carrying her into the bathroom. He set her down on the toilet, helping her balance, waiting while she did her business with the practiced efficiency of a lifetime routine.

When she was done, he transferred her back to the shower bench temporarily so he could use the toilet himself. The door remained open. Modesty had long ago been traded for logistics.

"Wardrobe," Patrick announced, washing his hands and carrying her back to the bed.

He opened their duffel bag. It was a warm July day, so the "uniform" was standard.

He helped her first. Underwear. Bra—a quick, practiced clasping of the hooks. Then he wrestled her legs into her denim shorts, massaging her calves to loosen the morning stiffness so her feet could slide through. He pulled a crisp, clean t-shirt over her head, smoothing it down, and finished with her socks and sneakers.

Then it was his turn. He pulled on his own underwear and matching denim shorts. He sat on the edge of the bed to pull on his socks. Then came the plastic AFO brace. He slid it behind his left calf, tightening the velcro straps with a loud rippp, before jamming his foot into his sneaker and tying it tight.

He stood up and pulled his t-shirt on. They were a matched set. Ready for campus.

Patrick walked over to the connecting door and rapped his knuckles against the wood three times. Knock-knock-knock.

"We're up!" Tom’s voice called back instantly. "Meet you at the trough in ten."

Patrick grabbed the room key and unlocked the brakes on the Titan AeroMotion. "Let's go get some fuel."

They rolled out into the hallway and down to the lobby. The "Continental Breakfast" was in full swing—a spread of mini-muffins, cereals, and a toaster that smelled like burning crumbs.

Patrick parked Nichole right in front of the buffet line. He grabbed a plastic plate.

"Alright, Chef," Patrick said, hovering the plate over the options. "What's the play? Bagel?"

Nichole shook her head. She pointed a finger at the tray of scrambled eggs.

"Protein. Smart," Patrick said, scooping a generous portion. "Bacon?"

Nichole tapped her armrest. Yes.

"Fruit?"

She pointed to the bowl of melon, but then sharply corrected her aim to the danishes.

Patrick grinned. "Carbo-loading. I like it." He grabbed a cherry danish for her and one for himself.

He carried the loaded plate to a table where Tom and Ellen were already sipping coffee. Patrick set the food down in front of Nichole, putting a fork in her hand and opening her milk carton.

"You two look bright-eyed," Ellen smiled, buttering a piece of toast.

"We're psyched," Patrick said, sitting down and digging into his own eggs. "I was reading the brochure again last night. They have a student radio station. And a literary magazine. It sounds... real."

Nichole chewed her bacon, humming happily. She could picture it. Her and Patrick, rolling across a green quad in Vermont, heading to the radio station to host a show. No aides. No separate schools. Just them, in a place that finally understood.

"It really does seem like the best of both worlds," Tom agreed, checking his watch. "Traditional campus feel, but built for accessibility. If the tour is as good as the brochure, this might be the one."

"It's going to be the one," Patrick said confidently. "I can feel it."

Nichole tapped her VocaLink screen. "COLLEGE. KIDS."

"Exactly," Patrick high-fived her.

"Alright, college kids," Tom said, standing up and crumpling his napkin. "We have two hundred and thirty miles to go. New Jersey Turnpike to the Garden State Parkway. Let's beat the traffic."

Patrick wiped a crumb from Nichole’s chin and stood up. He grabbed the push handles, feeling a surge of energy. They weren't just driving to another tour. They were driving toward their future.

"Armor up," Patrick said, spinning the chair toward the exit. "Vermont or bust."
