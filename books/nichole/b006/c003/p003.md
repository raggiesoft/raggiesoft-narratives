# Part 3: The Roll Call

##  Location: The Welcome Center Lobby, Vanguard Convocation Center Time: 1:15 PM

The lobby of the Welcome Center was an atrium of glass and school spirit. Banners hung from the ceiling in blue and silver. A massive bronze statue of the school mascot—a Lion wearing a crown—stood guard near the elevators.

The group of prospective students gathered in a loose semi-circle. There were about twelve of them—a mix of bored teenagers, anxious parents, and one very enthusiastic tour guide standing on a small riser.

"Alright everyone!" the guide clapped her hands. "I’m Megan, I’m a Junior majoring in Communications, and I’ll be your guide today. Welcome to Virginia Dominion University!"

She smiled, a high-wattage expression that seemed immune to the Norfolk humidity.

"We have a lot of ground to cover today," Megan continued. "VDU is a big place—over 18,000 students and growing. So, before we head out into the heat, I want to do a quick 'Roll Call of Majors.' Shout out what you’re interested in studying so I can make sure we point out the right buildings."

She started on the left with a lanky boy in a surfing t-shirt.

"Marine Biology," he mumbled.

"Awesome," Megan beamed. "We’re literally right on the water. You’ll want to see the Oceanography & Physics Building. We have our own research vessel docked at the pier."

She moved to the next girl, who was adjusting her glasses.

"Psychology," she said.

"Very popular," Megan nodded. "You’ll be spending a lot of time in the Mills Godwin Building—that's the life sciences hub right in the middle of campus. It's got great labs."

She went down the line. Business (Constant Hall). Sports Management (The Fieldhouse).

Then, she got to the Bennetts.

Megan looked at Patrick. She didn't look at the wheelchair. She didn't look at the parents hovering protectively behind them. She just looked at the prospective student.

"How about you?" Megan asked.

"Computer Engineering," Patrick said.

"Ooh, tough major," Megan grinned. "You’re going to be living in the E&OST Building—that's Engineering & Ocean Science Technology. It’s brand new. Lots of toys in there."

Then, Megan turned her gaze to Nichole.

She didn't skip her. She didn't ask the parents, "And what is she interested in?" She waited.

Nichole felt a flash of gratitude. At Roosevelt, they had talked about her. Here, they were waiting for her.

She typed quickly on her VocaLink. The synthesized voice cut through the lobby chatter, clear and robotic.

"CREATIVE. WRITING."

Nobody gasped. Nobody looked horrified. This was a tech-heavy campus; half the engineering students walked around talking to their PDAs anyway.

"Creative Writing!" Megan said, not missing a beat. "That means you belong to The BAL—the Batten Arts & Letters building. It’s that huge nine-story tower you probably saw driving in. It houses the English Department and the MFA program. You’ll love the view from the eighth floor; it’s where all the writers hang out to brood."

The group chuckled.

"Okay!" Megan clapped again. "So we’re hitting E&OST, The BAL, and we'll swing by the new Maglev station since everyone asks about it. Follow me!"

She pushed open the double doors.

Patrick leaned down as they started rolling.

"Did you see that?" he whispered.

"See what?" Nichole typed.

"She didn't ask about your 'limitations,'" Patrick said, popping the casters over the threshold. "She just told you where the writers hang out."

Nichole watched the back of the blue polo shirt marching confidently ahead.

Writers hang out on the eighth floor.

Not "wheelchair users go to the first floor." Writers.

She tapped her armrest.

NORMAL.

"Let's roll," Patrick said.
