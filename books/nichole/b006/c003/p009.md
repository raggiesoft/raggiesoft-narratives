# Part 9: The Dealbreaker

## Location: Webb University Center (Back Exit) to Dominion Hall Time: 3:15 PM

"Okay, last stop!" Megan chirped. "Freshman Housing."

She led them to the rear exit of Webb Center. Unlike the wide, automatic sliding doors at the front, this was a heavy metal door that opened onto a concrete ramp that looked like it had been poured in 1975 and forgotten.

"Watch your knuckles," Patrick muttered, lining up the Titan AeroMotion.

The ramp was technically compliant, but barely. It was narrow—maybe thirty-two inches wide—with rusted metal railings that felt too close for comfort. It had a single intermediate landing halfway down, but even that felt tight, forcing Patrick to execute a sharp, precise ninety-degree turn to continue the descent without scraping the rims against the concrete wall.

"Grandfather clause," Patrick diagnosed, expertly steering the heavy power chair through the tight corner of the landing. "This passes code, but just by the skin of its teeth."

They survived the descent and crossed a patch of hot pavement to Dominion Hall, a mid-rise brick building that housed the incoming freshman class.

## The Model Room

They gathered in the lobby, which smelled of industrial cleaner and stale popcorn. Megan used her master key to open the door to Room 104—the "Model Suite."

"Welcome to your new home!" Megan announced, stepping aside to let them peek in.

Patrick rolled Nichole into the room.

It was tight.

Two Twin XL beds were lofted high to fit wooden dressers underneath. Two desks were crammed against the far wall. The floor space between the beds was maybe three feet—just enough for the wheelchair to drive in, but not enough to turn around.

"It's... cozy," Tom said, trying to be polite.

"It's a Suite Style," Megan explained. "So you have two students in this room, two students in the room next door, and you share a connecting bathroom."

She pointed to the door between the dressers.

Patrick opened it. The bathroom was the size of a phone booth. A standard tub, a toilet, and two sinks. To use it, you had to step inside and close one door to open the other.

Patrick looked at the tub. No transfer space. He looked at the floor. No room for a lift. He looked at the beds. Too high for a safe transfer.

"Megan," Patrick asked, backing out of the bathroom. "I know this is the standard room. But what about the accessible rooms? Do you have roll-in showers?"

"Oh, absolutely!" Megan nodded vigorously. "We have ADA-compliant rooms on the first floor. They have wider doors, roll-in showers, and lower beds."

"Okay," Patrick said. "And what's the policy on roommates? My sister needs overnight assistance. I am her primary caregiver."

Megan’s smile faltered for the first time. She blinked.

"Oh. Well, freshman dorms are single-gender by suite. So... boys with boys, girls with girls."

"Right," Patrick said calmly. "But we're siblings. And for her safety, I need to be in the room."

Megan bit her lip. "I don't think Housing allows co-ed suites for freshmen. Even for siblings. Liability issues. You'd probably have to hire a female PCA to stay with her, or she'd be assigned a female roommate who... well, random roommates aren't trained for care."

## The Verdict

The air in the room went still. The other parents looked awkward, staring at the linoleum.

Usually, this was the moment where Nichole would shrink. This was the moment where the "System" told her she didn't fit, where the "Rules" said she was too complicated for their square boxes. At Roosevelt, this would have sent her into "Turtle Mode"—chin down, shoulders hunched, trying to disappear.

But today?

Nichole looked at the tiny, lofted bed. She looked at the bathroom she couldn't use. She looked at the well-meaning tour guide citing the rulebook.

She didn't shrink. She sat up straighter.

She looked at Patrick. Her eyes weren't sad. They were triumphant.

See? her look said. I told you.

The dorms were just another institution. Another place with rules about who could sleep where. Another place where she would be "managed" by strangers.

She tapped her armrest. Strong. Rhythmic.

APARTMENT.

Patrick smiled. He didn't argue with Megan. He didn't ask to see the Dean. He just nodded.

"Understood," Patrick said to Megan. "Thanks for the info."

He turned the chair around—a three-point turn in the cramped space—and faced his parents.

"We're done here," Patrick said. "We've seen enough."

"Plan B it is," Tom agreed, looking at the tiny room with relief. "I'd rather pay rent than deal with a roommate agreement anyway."

They rolled out of Dominion Hall, back into the sunshine.

They had their answer. VDU was perfect—the academics, the campus, the Maglev. But the housing? The housing was going to be on their terms.

Nichole took a deep breath of the hot, humid air.

Apartment. Key. Front door. Freedom.

She was ready for Vermont. Because now she knew exactly what "Normal" looked like, and she knew she could build it herself if she had to.
