# Part 6: The Life Sciences Hub

## Location: Mills Godwin Life Sciences Building (MGSB) Time: 2:10 PM

"Okay, brave the heat one more time!" Megan called out, pushing open the library doors.

They stepped back into the blast furnace of the afternoon sun. Thankfully, the walk was short. Just to the left of the library stood the Mills Godwin Life Sciences Building—a massive, concrete structure that screamed "1970s Science."

"For our Psychology enthusiast," Megan gestured to the girl with the glasses, "and anyone taking Biology 101, this is your home."

They approached the main entrance. It was a raised plaza design.

"Five steps up!" Megan announced, hopping up them effortlessly.

Patrick scanned the approach. Five wide concrete steps separated the sidewalk from the doors. But to the left, hugging the side of the planter box, was a concrete ramp.

"Detour," Patrick said to his parents.

He steered Nichole to the left. The ramp was long and had a switchback, but it was wide and the grade was shallow. It wasn't an afterthought bolted onto the back; it was part of the architecture.

"Good grip," Patrick noted as his shoes held traction on the textured concrete. He pushed her up the incline, meeting the rest of the group at the top landing just as Megan held the heavy glass door open.

## The Lecture Hall

They spilled into the main lobby. It was a cavernous space with terrazzo floors and brick walls, smelling faintly of formaldehyde and floor cleaner.

Directly in front of them were the double doors to Room 101.

"This," Megan said, patting the wooden doors, "is the Big Show. This is the main lecture hall. It seats about 300 students."

She opened one of the doors so they could peek inside.

The room was vast, sloping down toward a stage with a massive chalkboard and a projector screen. It looked like a movie theater for science.

"Don't be intimidated by the size," Megan said, looking at the Psychology student. "Intro to Psych and Bio 101 are huge classes, but the professors wear microphones, and the acoustics are great. Plus, there are breakout labs for the hands-on stuff."

Patrick rolled Nichole up to the doorway. He looked at the seating.

The auditorium had stadium seating, but right at the top, in the back row, there were cutouts in the desks. Spaces specifically designed for wheelchairs.

"Top of the world," Patrick whispered. "Great view of the screen. Easy exit."

Nichole looked at the rows of seats. At Roosevelt, "education" happened in a small therapy room with three other people. Here, she would be one of 300. Anonymous. Just a student taking notes in the dark.

"Psychology," the girl next to them murmured, looking excited. "This is it."

"And for the rest of you," Megan added, "you'll probably have at least one class in here to fulfill your Gen Ed science requirement. So get comfortable with those seats!"

Megan checked her watch.

"Alright, moving on! We're going to cut across the greenspace to the Webb University Center—our Student Union. And on the way, we are going to see the construction site that everyone is talking about."

Patrick’s ears perked up.

"The Maglev," he said.

Nichole straightened in her chair. The train.
