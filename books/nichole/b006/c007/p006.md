# Part 6: The Library Commons

## Date: July 6, 2003 Time: 10:45 AM Location: The University Library, Braxton State University

They reached the end of *Sub-University Avenue*.

A massive bank of four stainless steel elevators stood at the terminus of the tunnel. There were no stairs. The architecture dictated that everyone—able-bodied or on wheels—entered the library the exact same way.

"Going up to the surface," Patrick said, pressing the button.

The elevator cab was huge—easily big enough to fit Nichole’s chair, Patrick, and a study group. They rode up two floors and the doors opened into the heart of the BSU Library Commons.

The transition from the subterranean concrete to the library was breathtaking. The space was flooded with natural light from a four-story glass atrium overlooking the expansive blue waters of Narragansett Bay. It smelled of old paper, warm electronics, and fresh espresso from a café in the corner.

It was quiet, but it was a working quiet. Thousands of students were moving through the space, studying, typing, and talking in low voices.

Patrick pushed Nichole out of the elevator. He didn't have to scan the room for obstacles. The aisles between the massive wooden tables and the bookshelves were a standard six feet wide.

He rolled her up to an empty study pod facing the water.

At Putney College, Patrick had been forced to pull a heavy wooden chair out of the way to make room for her at the table, creating an awkward scene. Here, the study pods were designed with "Universal Design" in mind. Half of the desks were permanent standing-height, and half were open, pedestal-free tables.

Patrick rolled Nichole straight under the desk. Her lap tray slid perfectly over the tabletop with two inches of clearance to spare.

Patrick pulled up a rolling chair and sat down next to her. Nobody came over to tell him to sit in an "Observation Zone." Nobody stared. To the rest of the room, they were just two more students cramming for a summer session.

Nichole looked out the window at the sailboats on the bay. She looked down at the smooth wooden desk.

She could see herself here. She could see herself sitting at this exact table on a Tuesday afternoon in October, writing a short story while Patrick studied engineering schematics next to her.

She turned to Patrick. She made a soft, high-pitched *keening* sound of absolute joy. She reached out and grabbed his hand, squeezing it tight.

"I know," Patrick whispered, looking out at the water, feeling the immense weight of the last four days finally lift off his shoulders. "It's perfect, Nikki."

## Time: 12:45 PM Location: Aboard the Newport Flyer (Return Trip)

Two hours later, they were back on the ferry, cutting across the bay toward Newport.

The commuter loop was complete. They had navigated two buses, a ferry, a massive transit station, and a subterranean tunnel network, all without their parents and without hitting a single barrier.

Nichole was exhausted, but it was a good exhaustion. She watched the white wake of the boat churn behind them.

Patrick stood next to her chair, looking out the window, his hand resting on his back pocket.

He could feel the heavy, folded cardstock of the *Foundry Lofts* brochure pressing against his jeans. He hadn't stopped thinking about it since they left the tunnel.

The plan was to commute from Uncle Mark's. It was a good plan. But as he watched the freezing spray of Narragansett Bay hit the ferry window, he realized the ultimate flaw. A boat is a boat. A bus is a bus. In a Rhode Island January, the bay would freeze over, and the roads would close.

"Hey Nikki," Patrick said, leaning down so she could hear him over the engine roar. "Tonight, we get Uncle Mark to fire up his desktop computer. We have some reading to do."

Nichole looked up at him and smiled. She tapped her screen. "HOMEWORK."

"Exactly," Patrick grinned. "Homework."
