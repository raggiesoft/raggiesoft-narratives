# Part 1: The Commute

## Date: November 15, 2003 Time: 6:15 AM Location: 95 West Main Road, Portsmouth, Rhode Island

The Bennett Machine was officially out of the testing phase. It was time for a live production run.

The heavy, automated interior door of the Bennett Wing swung open. Patrick pushed the Titan AeroMotion down the main hallway, past the glowing, closed elevator doors, and out the front door into the crisp, freezing November air.

They didn't have to navigate a single step. The front porch ramp led directly to a paved walkway that extended all the way to the curb of West Main Road.

"Bus is three miles out," Patrick said, checking his watch. He blew into his hands, his breath visible in the morning air.

Nichole was bundled in a heavy winter coat, a custom thermal blanket tucked around her legs. On her lap tray, the Apex ConvertiBook screen glowed in the pre-dawn darkness. Attached to a retractable lanyard on her chest was her lifeline: a plastic sleeve containing her OSTA Monthly Pass and her BTA Zone 15 RegionLink smart card.

They reached the corner of Willow Lane and West Main Road.

Uncle Mark hadn't just modified the house; he had modified the town's infrastructure. In front of the house stood a custom-built, plexiglass-enclosed bus shelter. It was deep enough for Nichole's chair to turn around in and completely blocked the biting northern wind.

Directly across the street, a brand new pedestrian traffic signal blinked a steady red hand. Mark had somehow convinced the Rhode Island Department of Transportation that Willow Lane, a quiet residential side street, absolutely required a push-button crossing signal so his niece could safely cross West Main Road to catch the northbound bus to Providence.

But today, they were heading south.

At 6:28 AM, the headlights of the massive white-and-blue OSTA Route 60 bus cut through the dark. It hissed to a stop directly in front of Mark's shelter. The bus kneeled, and the front ramp folded out onto the sidewalk.

Nichole rolled forward. She didn't need Patrick to talk to the driver. As she reached the top of the ramp, she lifted her chest, proudly displaying the OSTA pass in the lanyard sleeve.

"Morning, Nichole! Morning, Patrick!" the driver, a regular named Sal, called out cheerfully. "Heading to the mainland?"

Nichole clicked her mouse.

"BSU. OPEN. HOUSE."

"Go get 'em," Sal smiled.

Patrick locked her chair into the front tie-downs, swiped his own pass, and sat in the folding jump-seat next to her.

The thirty-minute ride down the spine of Aquidneck Island was smooth and quiet. Patrick watched the dark landscape roll by, checking the timeline in his head. Check-in at the Braxton State University student union was at 8:00 AM. The math was tight, but they had practiced this.

At 7:00 AM, the bus pulled into the Gateway Center transit hub in downtown Newport.

They unloaded quickly into the bustling morning crowd. They were no longer tourists trying to find their way; they were commuters. Patrick pushed Nichole through the plaza, and rolled one block down to Perrotti Park.

The BTA *Newport Flyer*—a sleek, high-speed catamaran ferry—was already docked, its massive engines humming, churning the dark harbor water.

They joined the line of business commuters boarding the gangway. This was the transfer point between the old transit system and the new one. No more flash passes.

At the boarding gate, Nichole rolled up to the electronic turnstile. She reached out with her spastic hand, gripping her BTA RegionLink card, and pressed it against the glowing blue reader.

*BEEP-boop.*

The screen flashed ZONE 15 - VALID. The accessible gate swung open. Patrick tapped his own card and followed her onto the boat.

Inside the heated lower deck of the ferry, Patrick locked the Titan AeroMotion’s manual wheel brakes into the dedicated bay near the massive windows.

"Twenty-eight minutes to Narragansett," Patrick said, unzipping his coat as the ferry’s horn blasted. He looked down at his sister. "How are we doing on battery?"

Nichole looked at the bottom corner of her Apex ConvertiBook screen. She tapped the power icon.

88%.

She clicked her mouse.

"PLENTY. OF. JUICE."

Patrick nodded, stretching his arms. "Good. Because my battery is at 100%, and we have a lot of campus to cover."

The ferry pulled away from the dock, accelerating into Narragansett Bay. As the sun began to rise, the skyline of Braxton City appeared on the horizon. Nichole stared out the window. She wasn't looking at Braxton as an insurmountable obstacle anymore. She was looking at it as a destination on her grid.

At 7:42 AM, the ferry throttled down and smoothly docked at the Narragansett Esplanade Ferry Terminal.

The ramp dropped. The commuters surged forward.

Patrick grabbed the push-handles and rolled Nichole off the boat, directly onto the Esplanade. Fifty yards away stood the BTA Metro station, and right next to it, the sprawling, historic brick buildings of Braxton State University.

It was 7:45 AM. They were fifteen minutes early. No parents, no accessible taxis, no paratransit vans. Just a bus, a boat, and the Bennett Machine.

Nichole stopped her chair at the campus entrance. A massive banner hung across the wrought-iron gates: WELCOME FUTURE BRAXTON BULLS.

She looked up at Patrick. He was smiling, his hands resting easily on the grips of her chair.

She tapped her screen.

"WE. ARE. INEVITABLE."

"That we are, Nikki," Patrick laughed. "Let's go show them what the Gap Year bought us."
