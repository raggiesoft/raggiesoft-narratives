# Part 4: The Return

## Date: June 15, 2043 Location: 95 West Main Road, Portsmouth, RI

They didn't hire movers to pack. They hired movers to *purge*. They sold the loft furniture. They donated the office equipment. Bennett Dynamics was officially closed for new business. They were keeping the patent royalties from the *Haptic Interface*, but the consulting firm was retiring.

They drove the new van (a 2042 hybrid transport) across the Newport Bridge. They pulled into the driveway of 95 West Main. The house looked exactly the same. The brick was timeless. The ramp Uncle Mark had poured in 2003 was still smooth and solid, free of cracks.

Patrick lowered the lift. Nichole rolled out. The air smelled of salt and pine. It was quieter here. The roar of the city was gone, replaced by the sound of wind in the trees and the distant hum of the Route 60 bus.

They rolled up the ramp. Patrick unlocked the front door. They entered the hallway. It was empty, but it wasn't lonely. It felt like the house had been holding its breath, waiting for them to come back.

Patrick pushed her down the wide hallway to the Bennett Wing. He opened the double doors. The mini-apartment was exactly as they had left it after the funeral. The kitchenette. The study desks. And through the bathroom door, the gleaming white hydrotherapy tub.

"Home," Patrick whispered.

He parked her chair. He sat down on the edge of the bed—the same King bed they had bought with their "Bridge Fund" money forty years ago. He looked at Nichole. "We're the old people now, Nikki. We're Grandma Helen."

Nichole smiled. She tapped her screen. SHE. WAS. HAPPY. HERE. WE. WILL. BE. TOO.

Patrick lay back on the bed, staring at the ceiling. He didn't have to worry about the subway schedule. He didn't have to worry about the elevator breaking. He just had to be here.

"Okay," Patrick said, closing his eyes. "Fortress Mode engaged."
