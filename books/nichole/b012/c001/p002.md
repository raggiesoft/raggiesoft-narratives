# Part 2: The City Problem

## Date: February 2043 Location: The Foundry Lofts, Braxton

The winter of '43 was brutal. For thirty years, The Foundry had been their stronghold. They loved the industrial windows, the polished concrete, and the direct subway access. But the city was changing.

Braxton wasn't the gritty, manageable city of 2004 anymore. It was a "Smart City." The subway stations were crowded with autonomous delivery drones. The pace was frantic. And the infrastructure was aging.

The Incident: It happened on a Tuesday. Patrick and Nichole were coming back from a doctor's appointment. They took the Blue Line to Foundry Square. They rolled to the elevator to go up to the street level. OUT OF SERVICE.

Patrick stared at the red sign. "Okay," he muttered. "Backup plan. We use the freight elevator in the adjacent building." They rolled down the connector tunnel. OUT OF SERVICE.

Panic, cold and sharp, spiked in Patrick’s chest. Thirty years ago, he would have been angry. He would have found a security guard, demanded a key override, or even carried her up the escalator if he had to. But he was 57. His back hurt. His knees clicked. He couldn't carry her up three flights of stairs. Not anymore.

They were trapped underground.

They had to ride the train two stops back to *State Center*, transfer to a bus, ride the bus back to Foundry Square, and then navigate the icy sidewalks to their building's front door. The "20-minute trip" took three hours.

By the time they got inside Unit 404, Patrick was trembling with exhaustion. He collapsed on the sofa, rubbing his left knee—the "bad" one. "The grid is failing us, Nikki," he wheezed. "Or maybe... maybe we're failing the grid."

Nichole looked at him. She saw the gray pallor of his skin. She realized that the "Mole Lifestyle"—the endless walking, the subway transfers, the urban hustle—was a young person's game. The city demanded energy they no longer had.

She tapped her screen. WE. NEED. A. RANCH.
