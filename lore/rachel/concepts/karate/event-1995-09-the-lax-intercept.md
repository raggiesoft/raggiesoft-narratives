# The LAX Intercept (September 1995)

## I. The Defense Strategy
*   **The Panic:** During the criminal discovery phase, Master Ray's high-priced corporate defense attorneys realize that Master April has compiled a massive, damning disciplinary dossier regarding the events of August 17th. 
*   **The Subpoena:** Knowing this dossier contains translated police reports, medical evidence, and a formal petition to excommunicate the facility's leadership, the defense lawyers panic. They secure an emergency, ex parte subpoena *duces tecum* from a Virginia judge to legally seize her documents before they can be submitted to any governing body.
*   **The Ignorance:** The lawyers view April merely as a disgruntled former employee and a twenty-two-year-old civilian. They are completely unaware of her geopolitical registration as Lee Hae-jin, a State Agent of the Republic of Korea.

## II. The Airport Choke Point
*   **The Route:** April opts to maintain the chain of custody herself. She departs Norfolk International Airport (ORF) to fly cross-country to Los Angeles International Airport (LAX), intending to board a transpacific flight directly to Incheon International (ICN) in Seoul. 
*   **The Ambush:** The defense attorneys track her flight manifest. They arrange for U.S. Marshals, armed with the Virginia court order, to intercept her at the gate during her layover at LAX. Their goal is to force her to hand over the physical paperwork before she leaves American airspace.

## III. The Diplomatic Guillotine
*   **The Confrontation:** The federal marshals approach April in the LAX international terminal. They present the subpoena and demand she surrender the files in her possession.
*   **The Valise Diplomatique:** April calmly gestures to the heavy, wax-sealed leather portfolio resting securely at her side, which is officially stamped with the seal of the Republic of Korea.
*   **The State Department Credential:** She does not argue with the marshals. She simply produces her U.S. Department of State Diplomatic Identification Card, featuring the bright, solid blue border that grants full, unquestionable criminal and civil immunity. 
*   **The Override:** The marshals flip the card over and read the explicit federal warning. They realize that under international law, the portfolio is a Diplomatic Pouch; it cannot be opened, x-rayed, detained, or searched by U.S. Customs or any law enforcement agency under any circumstances.

## IV. The Aftermath
*   **The Stand Down:** The U.S. Marshals immediately retreat, legally paralyzed by the Vienna Convention. They call the defense attorneys back in Virginia to inform them that their subpoena is effectively worthless toilet paper against a sovereign nation.
*   **The Departure:** April boards her flight to ICN, entirely unbothered, carrying the *Pamyun* dossier safely across the Pacific to place directly onto the table of the Supreme Council.
