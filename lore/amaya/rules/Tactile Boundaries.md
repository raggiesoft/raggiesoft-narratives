# Archive: Tactile Boundaries, Caregiving & The Guardrail Protocol

## I. The Foundational Rule (The Sibling Firewall)
While [[Justin Hayes]] is part of a closed intimacy pact with the women of the [[Wall of 20]] (managed by his primary partner, [[Amaya Rios]]), there is an absolute, unbreakable firewall regarding his sister, [[Meredith Hayes]]. 

Their physical relationship is intensely close, heavily tactile, and fiercely protective, but it is **strictly and permanently platonic**. Because both siblings have AuDHD and Justin has No Light Perception (NLP), their physical contact is utilitarian, grounding, and driven by accessibility and survival rather than romance.

## II. The "Guardrail" Sleep Protocol
Within the Duplex (and later, the NRVRC suites), the standard sleep arrangement for the Core Trio is a shared bed. Justin sleeps perfectly in the center, flanked by Amaya on one side and Meredith on the other. While bed-sharing among adults is often socially coded as purely romantic, the trio's arrangement is built on strict accessibility requirements.

*   **The Tactile Logic:** Because Justin is totally blind, he lacks the visual spatial awareness required to know how close he is to the edge of a mattress while asleep or waking up. A massive mattress can feel like a spatial void, causing subconscious anxiety about spatial drift.
*   **The Living Guardrails:** By placing Amaya and Meredith on either side of him, they act as living, tactile "guardrails." 
*   **Circadian Safety:** If Justin rolls too far in either direction during the night, he immediately hits a soft, familiar boundary, completely eliminating the risk of falling. It provides him with 360-degree tactile security and allows his nervous system to fully power down.

## III. Physical Affection & Strict Boundaries
The physical affection within the Core Trio is highly communicative, frequent, and completely transparent, operating under strict rules of intent.

*   **Deep Pressure & Cuddling:** Hugging and cuddling are constant in the shared sleeping space and around the house. It serves as deep pressure therapy, allowing all three of them to mutually regulate their nervous systems.
*   **The Baseline of Kissing:** Gentle kissing—such as pecks on the cheek, the forehead, or the lips—is standard among the three of them. It is used as a quick, tactile confirmation of safety and affection.
*   **The Intent Boundary:** The defining rule of this closeness is *intent*. While Amaya and Justin share an active sexual relationship, any physical touch (including lip pecks or cuddling) between Justin and Meredith is fiercely, strictly platonic. It is the physical manifestation of absolute familial trust, possessing zero romantic undertones.

## IV. Activities of Daily Living (ADLs) & Absolute Dignity
Because of his CP and visual impairment, Justin requires assistance with basic Activities of Daily Living (ADLs), including bathing, getting dressed, toileting, and eating.

*   **The "No Shame" Baseline:** In this dynamic, there is absolutely zero shame or stigma attached to disabled bodies or caregiving. Meredith, Amaya, and the fully vetted members of the [[Wall of 20]] seamlessly transition into caregiving roles whenever needed. 
*   **Separation of Care and Intimacy:** Caregiving is treated as a clinical, dignified, and strictly utilitarian protocol. When Meredith assists him with toileting, or when a Tier-2 pack member helps him bathe, the physical vulnerability is met with absolute routine. It is completely disconnected from the sexual intimacy pact, reinforcing that physical reliance is a normal, healthy part of their collective survival.

## V. Deep Pressure & Grounding (Non-Romantic Intimacy)
Because Justin relies on tactile input, deep pressure is a mechanical necessity for his nervous system.
*   **The Weighted Lean:** If Justin is overstimulated in public, Meredith will sit next to him and lean her entire body weight against his side or shoulder. It provides the deep compression his nervous system craves without being a formal embrace.
*   **The Shoulder Anchor:** Because Meredith uses his transport chair for her own CP stability, she is constantly connected to him. Resting her hands heavily on his shoulders from behind while they are stationary serves as a constant, reassuring physical tether.

## VI. The Forehead Rest (The Sensory Bubble)
This is one of the most profound, non-sexual forms of physical intimacy between the siblings, usually utilized after high-stress events (like navigating a massive crowd or experiencing an accessibility failure).
*   **The Mechanic:** Meredith stands in front of his chair, leans down, and presses her forehead firmly against his. 
*   **The Effect:** For Justin, feeling her steady breathing and the solid pressure of her forehead instantly shrinks his massive, chaotic auditory world down to just the two of them. It is the physical embodiment of a safe harbor.

## VII. Tactile Mapping & Fidgeting
Because both siblings have AuDHD, they utilize shared physical routines to regulate their nervous systems and manage anxiety (stimming).
*   **Hand Tracing:** In a waiting room or quiet space, Justin frequently traces the seams of Meredith's sleeve or the knuckles of her hand. It is not romantic hand-holding; it is tactile mapping.
*   **The Interlocking Grip:** If a sudden, massive noise startles Justin, Meredith will deliver a hard, white-knuckle hand squeeze. To Justin's logic-driven brain, this specific pressure instantly translates as: *I am here, the threat is external, do not panic.*

## VIII. Shared Resting (The Couch Lean)
Physical proximity is how the Core Trio confirms environmental safety at the Duplex or the NRVRC suites.
*   **The Baseline:** It is a daily routine for the siblings to crash on the same couch. Meredith might sit sideways with her legs draped over Justin's lap, or Justin might lean his head against her shoulder while listening to his screen reader. 
*   **Absolute Trust:** They do not enforce standard "personal bubbles" because they are each other's baseline for reality. This shared resting space is a pure demonstration of familial trust and exhaustion recovery.
