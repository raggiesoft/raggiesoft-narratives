# Archive: Activities of Daily Living (ADL) Protocols

## I. The Intersection of CP and NLP
[[Justin Hayes]] requires assistance with all Activities of Daily Living (ADLs). This dependency is not caused by a single diagnosis, but by the compounding intersection of his Cerebral Palsy (CP) and his No Light Perception (NLP). 

If he only had CP, he could use visual feedback to painstakingly manage fine motor tasks. If he were only blind, he could use unimpaired tactile motor control to navigate the physical world. However, because he lacks both visual spatial awareness and fine motor execution, his physical reality requires direct, manual intervention from his trusted pack. 

## II. The Shared Burden (The 20-Woman Protocol)
While [[Meredith Hayes]] and [[Amaya Rios]] are his primary anchors, executing ADLs is a shared responsibility across the entire [[Wall of 20]]. 
*   **Preventing Caregiver Burnout:** By ensuring that all 18 Tier-2 women are fully trained and vetted to assist Justin in the bathroom, shower, and at the dining table, the physical and emotional load is distributed. 
*   **Routine Standardization:** Because of his AuDHD, Justin requires strict routines. The pack executes his ADL protocols identically, meaning Justin feels completely safe and regulated regardless of whether Meredith or a Flanker like [[Rachel Davis]] is assisting him.

## III. Specific ADL Mechanics

### 1. Dressing and Wardrobe
Despite his wardrobe consisting entirely of sensory-friendly, tagless, and seamless clothing, Justin cannot dress himself.
*   **Motor Limitations:** His CP causes spasticity and limits his range of motion. He lacks the fine motor skills to manipulate collars, pull fabric over his head evenly, or manage elastic waistbands.
*   **The Caregiver Mechanic:** The assisting woman must gently maneuver his limbs into the clothing, ensuring his joints are not strained or pulled against his CP spasticity. Because he cannot see, the caregiver ensures clothing is oriented correctly and free of sensory-triggering wrinkles or bunching.

### 2. Eating and Hydration
Justin cannot self-feed safely or efficiently.
*   **Spatial Void:** Because he has NLP, he cannot locate the food on his plate. 
*   **Motor Deficits:** His CP prevents him from maintaining a stable, controlled grip on utensils. If he attempted to feed himself blindly with a spastic grip, he would risk injury or constant spills, causing severe AuDHD frustration.
*   **The Caregiver Mechanic:** The assisting pack member sits closely beside him, preparing his food into manageable bites. They either physically feed him or utilize hand-over-hand tactile guidance, tapping his lip with the utensil to provide a predictable sensory cue before the bite.

### 3. Bathing and Hygiene
The shower is an inherently dangerous environment for Justin.
*   **Core Instability:** Justin lacks the core strength to sit unsupported on a slippery shower bench. 
*   **Sensory Regulation:** For his AuDHD, unexpected changes in water temperature or pressure are physically painful. 
*   **The Caregiver Mechanic:** The assisting woman manages the environmental controls, ensuring the water temperature is perfectly calibrated to his sensory baseline. She provides physical stabilization to keep him upright on the bench, handles all soap and scrubbing (since he lacks the fine motor skills to lather), and ensures his skin is completely clean, checking visually for any skin breakdown or CP-related pressure sores.

### 4. Toileting and Transfers
Bathroom logistics require absolute physical trust and significant physical effort from the caregiver.
*   **The Transfer:** Justin cannot transfer himself from his transport chair to the toilet. The caregiver must unlatch his lap tray (which holds his QBraille XL) and execute a manual transfer (often the "deadlift carry" popularized by [[Jessica Barnes]]) to safely move him.
*   **Stability and Hygiene:** Once on the toilet, he requires physical support to maintain a seated balance. Because he lacks both the fine motor control to wipe and the visual ability to check for cleanliness, the caregiver manages his personal hygiene with clinical, zero-shame efficiency, ensuring he remains healthy and dignified.
