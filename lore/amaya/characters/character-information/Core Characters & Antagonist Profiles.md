## Justin Hayes (The Anchor)

*   **Demographics:** Age 20 (Older brother). He started kindergarten a year late due to missing the birthday cutoff, placing him in the same graduating class as [[Amaya Rios|Amaya]] and [[Meredith Hayes|Meredith]].
*   **Medical/Neurological Profile:** [[No Light Perception|Optic Nerve Hypoplasia]] (No Light Perception), Non-verbal [[AuDHD|Autism]], [[Cerebral Palsy]], [[Non-obstructive Azoospermia]].
*   **Physicality & Presence:** At 5'4", Justin has a compact, grounded center of gravity. Because he is seated in the transport chair and relies on tactile mapping, his world is strictly immediate and horizontal.
*   **Sensory Wardrobe:** To eliminate sensory friction for his [[AuDHD]], his clothing is heavily vetted—seamless socks, tagless shirts, and soft, predictable fabrics.
*   **Hardware/AAC & Tech Philosophy:** His hardware setup is aggressively minimalist. A QBraille XL is mounted directly to his transport chair's lap tray, acting as both his voice and his environmental interface, pairing locally with a Windows laptop running a screen reader. He rejects bloated software environments, relying entirely on raw, efficient text processing and native file parsing that his system can execute instantly without lag.
*   **Role in the Dynamic:** The Anchor. He operates purely on logic, routines, and verified facts. Because his AuDHD brain rejects emotional manipulation and he is entirely immune to visual cues, he acts as an unbreakable baseline of truth. He is at absolute peace with his biological reality and relies entirely on tactile fluency and the physical presence of his trusted circle.

## Amaya Rios (The Grounding Force)

*   **Demographics:** Age 19 (Best friend). The standard age for her grade level, she integrated into the siblings' lives when her family moved into the other half of the duplex during the summer before 5th grade.
*   **Medical/Neurological Profile:** Hearing Impaired (profoundly deaf without hardware).
*   **Physicality & Presence:** At 5'6", she physically bridges the gap between Justin and the rest of the world.
*   **Hardware/AAC:** Bilateral external cochlear implants. She wears the processors and cables openly, fully embracing her accessibility hardware as a natural, unapologetic extension of her body.
*   **Role in the Dynamic:** The Grounding Force. Amaya has shared a duplex, a bed, and an entire life with the Hayes siblings for almost a decade. She shares a completely safe, intensely communicative, and fully legal sexual relationship with [[Justin Hayes|Justin]].
*   **The Intimacy Translator & Threat Assessor:** Because Justin requires explicit verbal consent, Amaya is the master of direct, unambiguous communication and never relies on "hints." Acting as the ultimate external firewall, she immediately neutralizes abusers who try to weaponize false hope, isolation, or medical gaslighting against the trio.

## Meredith Hayes (The Navigator and Shield)

*   **Demographics:** Age 18 (Youngest sister). She skipped a grade due to being gifted, which perfectly aligned her to take all her classes alongside [[Justin Hayes|Justin]] and [[Amaya Rios|Amaya]].
*   **Medical/Neurological Profile:** [[AuDHD]], [[Cerebral Palsy]].
*   **Physicality & Biomechanics:** At 5'8", Meredith possesses a distinct height advantage. This is mechanically perfect for her role: pushing a 5'4" seated adult requires significant leverage, and her height allows her to maintain excellent posture and momentum while using the transport chair's push-handles to stabilize her own CP gait.
*   **Hardware & The Crutch Compromise:** She despises her forearm crutches because they force her to break her momentum and occupy her hands. She stows them in a custom holder on the back of Justin's chair, turning his transport chair into her primary, fluid mobility aid.
*   **Situational Mutism & AAC Integration:** Her [[AuDHD]] causes her spontaneous speech to lock up under stress. She utilizes a dynamic display tablet outfitted with a rigid plastic keyguard, mounted on a swing-away arm between the push-handles. This brilliant placement allows her to "speak" without ever breaking her physical stride, letting go of her stability anchor, or dropping eye contact with the environment.
*   **Role in the Dynamic:** The Navigator and Shield. She is the utilitarian processor of the visual world, translating the chaotic environment into safety for Justin. She is clinically detached from social drama, fiercely protective of her brother, and quietly excuses herself from the room to allow Justin and Amaya their privacy, deeply respecting the boundaries of their adult relationship.

## Vera Kowalski (The Predator)

*   **Demographics:** Age 23 (during the primary 2003 timeline). Her mother is [[Robyn Kowalski]], and her aunt is Carol, who resides in Kent, Washington. She eventually has a daughter named [[Lily Kowalski]].
*   **Physical Disadvantage:** At 5'5", Vera attempts to use physical intimidation and visual dominance to control her targets. However, she is literally looking up to both [[Amaya Rios|Amaya]] and [[Meredith Hayes|Meredith]], rendering her attempts to physically bully her way into Justin's space completely ineffective.
*   **Modus Operandi (The "Savior" Trap):** Vera deliberately targets young, vulnerable men with disabilities, specifically seeking out those with autism and Cerebral Palsy. She initially presents herself as a supportive "ally" to gain their trust.
*   **Tactics of Abuse and Control:**
    *   She isolates her victims by strictly monitoring their phone calls and attempting to sever their ties with their female best friends and family members.
    *   She utilizes medical and physical neglect as a form of control. This is brutally demonstrated when she forces [[Alex Miller|Alex]] onto a disaster of a cross-country bus trip to Washington state without his essential medications (which was the only time he was ever unmedicated). During this unmedicated trip, she starves him and allows him to develop severe pressure sores.
    *   She leverages extreme power imbalances, exploiting her victims' youth and disabilities to coerce them into compliance.
*   **The Strategic Error (Plan A vs. Plan B):** Vera operates on a flowchart of exploitation. [[Alex Miller]] was her primary target (Plan A) because he was easier to isolate. [[Justin Hayes]] was strictly Plan B because the [[Wall of Twenty|Wall of 20]] made him a logistical nightmare to access.
*   **The Pregnancy Grift:** While in a relationship with [[Alex Miller|Alex]], Vera cheats on him with [[David Fuller]]. David is killed in a violent bar fight roughly an hour after Vera conceives her daughter, Lily. Desperate for money and a provider, Vera lies and attempts to pin the pregnancy on Alex.
*   **The Medical Brick Wall:** Her extortion attempts fail spectacularly in sequence. She crashes into reality when she discovers her victims (both Alex and Justin) possess medically documented infertility, making her claims biologically impossible.
*   **The Ultimate Downfall:**
    *   Alex's family secures a strict Cease and Desist order, legally barring Vera from ever contacting him or his loved ones again.
    *   Vera spends the next two decades bitter, shifting the blame for her ruined life onto Alex.
    *   Her final loss of control occurs when she violently slaps her 17-year-old daughter, Lily. This physical abuse causes her own mother, Robyn, to permanently evict Vera from their apartment, forcing her to apply for public housing and severing all family ties.
