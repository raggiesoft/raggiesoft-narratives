# Archive: Character Profile – Meredith Hayes

## I. Core Demographics & Overview

*   **Full Name:** Meredith Hayes
*   **Age:** 18 (during the primary 2003 timeline).
*   **Family Role:** [[Justin Hayes]]'s younger sister.
*   **Academic Status:** Enrolled at the Community College of Hampton Roads (CCHR). Identified as gifted early on, she skipped a grade, which perfectly aligned her to take all her college classes alongside Justin (20) and [[Amaya Rios]] (19).
*   **Role in the Dynamic:** The Navigator and Shield. She acts as the utilitarian processor of the visual world, translating chaotic environments into a safe, navigable path for her brother while acting as the literal "engine" of his mobility.

## II. Physical Presentation

*   **Height:** 5'8".
*   **Build & Biomechanics:** She possesses a distinct height advantage over her older brother (who is 5'4"). This height inversion is mechanically perfect for her role. Pushing a seated adult requires significant leverage, and her 5'8" frame allows her to maintain excellent posture and forward momentum while utilizing the push-handles of his transport chair to stabilize her own gait.
*   **Demeanor:** Clinically detached from petty social drama. She observes the world with a highly analytical, protective gaze, constantly scanning the environment for physical or social threats to her brother.

## III. Comprehensive Medical Notes

Meredith’s medical profile is the exact reason her symbiotic navigational dynamic with Justin is so flawless.

**1. Cerebral Palsy (CP)**

*   *Mobility:* Meredith is ambulatory but requires a dedicated stability anchor to navigate safely, especially in crowds or over long distances.
*   *The Crutch Compromise:* She is prescribed forearm crutches, but she absolutely despises them. They force her to break her momentum and occupy her hands, limiting her ability to interact with the world. She stows them in a custom holder on the back of Justin's chair, completely repurposing his transport chair as her primary, fluid mobility aid.

**2. Autism Spectrum Disorder & ADHD (AuDHD)**

*   *Situational Mutism:* While Meredith is fully verbal in safe, low-stress environments, her spontaneous speech frequently locks up during high-stress situations, sensory overload, or moments of intense frustration.
*   *Social Processing:* Like Justin, she possesses an AuDHD brain that values logic and directness, making her highly resilient against emotional manipulation.

## IV. Hardware & Technological Framework

Meredith’s tech setup is entirely integrated into Justin's transport chair, turning the chair into a shared, centralized command station for both siblings.

*   **The AAC Interface:** To combat her situational mutism, she utilizes a dynamic display tablet.
*   **The Keyguard:** The tablet is outfitted with a rigid, plastic keyguard over the screen, allowing her to rely on muscle memory and tactile feedback to hit the correct icons without having to stare down at the screen.
*   **The Swing-Away Mount:** The tablet is mounted on a heavy-duty swing-away arm positioned directly between the push-handles on the back of Justin's transport chair. This brilliant placement allows her to type and "speak" via the tablet's external speakers without ever breaking her physical stride, letting go of her stability anchor, or dropping her eye contact with the environment.

## V. Navigational & Social Mechanics

Meredith is the architect of the trio's physical boundaries and the commander of their public movements.

*   **The Symbiotic Engine:** The relationship between Meredith and Justin's hardware is a perfect closed loop. Justin needs a visual navigator to push his transport chair; Meredith needs a heavy, wheeled stability point to manage her CP gait. By pushing him, they solve each other's mobility requirements simultaneously.
*   **Architect of the Arm-Bridge Protocol:** Meredith is the original engineer of Justin's mandatory physical boundary. When they were younger and Justin accidentally touched her chest while reaching out blindly for a hug, she recognized it as a spatial miscalculation. To ensure he never felt anxious about seeking affection, she designed the [[Arm-Bridge Hug|Arm-Bridge Protocol]]: intercepting his hand with her forearm to create a "guide rail" (the bridge) up to her shoulder.
*   **The Respect of Adult Boundaries:** Despite her fierce protectiveness, Meredith recognizes and deeply respects the boundary between her role as a sister and Amaya’s role as Justin's partner. When they return to the duplex or their secure suite, Meredith quietly and routinely excuses herself from the room, proactively granting Justin and Amaya the privacy they need for their adult relationship.
